  • Human Body What changes in a child's digestive system at around one year of age?

    NEW
    Q:

    There's lots of advice/rules in regards to feeding a baby. For example no honey because of the risk of botulism, no added sugar/salt etc. Most rules last until 1 year of age.

    What changes after the one year mark? Is it just the body mass that's now enough? Or does the digestive system mature? Or do babies need to "assemble" a certain amount of bacteria to handle grown-up food?

    brawnkowsky A:

    The immune system matures, not the GI tract. It takes a year before babies have a full population of T cells, which are needed to combat infection. They also lack a memory immune response, making them susceptible to first-time infections

    There are a couple things that change prior to 1 year. As mentioned, both the gut microbiome and theimmune system are relevant to the homey one, but this isn't AT 1 year, but BY one year. In the first weeks you have a very large increase in stomache size, allowingbabies to consume larger meals less frequently. This slows down, but continues even past 1 year. Of course, teeth become a factor allowing for more foods, as does the size of the throat in regards to choking.

    As far as I know the gut microbiome might play an important role in our immune system, a view that is getting increased support. However, the full development of the gut microbiome is only complete 2-3 years following birth, which is significantly more than the 1 year cutoff that you describe.
