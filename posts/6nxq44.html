    Q:

    Some table and floor lamps click on and off. Other lamps click twice in the on position, and twice more in the off position. Is there an advantage to different switch configurations?

    Cr4nkY4nk3r A:

    The other top level explanations are correct, but I wanted to share this animation that I've found helpful when explaining it to others...

    Pic

    Note that there are two different parts involved:

    • The bulb has two different filaments, with four different possibilities: No filaments lit, filament A lit, filament B lit, filaments A & B both lit.
    • The wiring in the switch is such that different positions of the switch produce the four different lighting conditions of the bulb.

    afcagroo A:

    The ones that click twice in each position are made for a "3-way bulb". That's a bulb with two filaments, low and high. They are made to do low, high, low+high, off.

    But if you put a regular single filament bulb in that socket, you get off, on, on, off. (It's only on when the "high" filament is supposed to be.)
