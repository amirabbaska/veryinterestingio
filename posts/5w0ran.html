    Stilev A:

    Coma is generally defined as a loss of consciousness (arousal, attention and awareness) as well as being unable to be aroused out of the unconsciousness. Which is one aspect that separates its from sleep. So in order to be unconscious you have to have loss of coritcal function bilaterally either due to a direct insult like anoxia OR by indirectly damaging parts of the brain stem or mid brain that send "be awake" signals. (One important signals that is released is histamine, and is why anti-histamines make your drowsy). Its important to remember that consciousness is a spectrum as well. So unless the underlying cause is fixed the patient wont awake from the coma EDIT: TL:DR directly by loss of bilateral cortical function or indirectly by loss of activating systems in the mid or brainstem
