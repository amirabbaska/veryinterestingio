    ELI5: Why do humans have to have water much more regularly than other mammals or other organisms?

    SYLOH A:

    Mainly because humans don't eat like most other animals.
    The stuff animals eat usually has water in it.
    It could be blood from flesh or juices contained in leafs or fruit.
    Humans eat alot of dried, preserved, salted and/or cooked food, that doesn't have that much water in it.

    Secondary to this is the fact humans sweat more than other animals. We're adapted for running down prey by having an more effective means of eliminating heat through sweat, it also means we lose more water that way.
