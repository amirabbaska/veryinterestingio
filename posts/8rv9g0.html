    rshanks A:

    There are some studies that have shown indoor air quality can be like 5-10x worse than outdoor air, but they might also be suggesting you go outside just for a change of scenery.

    https://www.cnbc.com/2016/04/22/indoor-air-can-be-deadlier-than-outdoor-air-research-shows.html

    Air conditioning doesn’t really do much to remove pollutants afaik. The filter will remove dust, and the removal of humidity may make it feel less “stuffy”, however I would imagine many smaller particles will go right through the filter, so it’s mostly just a comfort thing.

    I recall reading that Britain added a rule to their building code recently to require that houses exchange their air with the outside every so often. It used to not be a problem, but as houses started being better sealed to save energy, it was also trapping pollutants inside and causing other issues.
