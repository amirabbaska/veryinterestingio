    VoilaVoilaWashington A:

    A bit of history: smoking pipes and cigars used to be common. You use a decent to excellent tobacco leaf, process it carefully, and sell it for a high price. Eventually, some enterprising soul thought "we throw out tons of shitty trimmings and subpar leaves. Why don't we sell them?"

    Very quickly, the waste product became the major source of revenue. These days, over 90% of tobacco is used in cigarettes, and cigars and pipe tobacco have become a premium product.

    Cigarettes are dried, and chemicals are added to keep them burning longer once lit, which means that it takes almost no skill, and there's limited flavour. It's just burning leaves.

    Pipes and cigars are very different - the leaves are a higher grade, with tobaccos selected based on flavour, like wine. Some leaves are richer, others milder, and each type will be blended to be similar over time. Sometimes flavours are added (common with pipe tobacco more so than cigars).

    Proper storage is crucial. You want the right amount of humidity, not too much or too little, or it will spoil. But the key is that it takes a certain amount of wealth and effort to smoke these, because they're the premium product. If you don't want to put in that effort, you smoke cigarettes.
