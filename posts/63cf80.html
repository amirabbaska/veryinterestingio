    crimble_crumble A:

    Killer whales use echolocation to find their food. This tells them where the food is, how big the food is, and what it is made of. They can tell the difference between copper and lead balls - this sense is really sophisticated.

    There are different groups of Killer whales, called 'ecotypes', which vary in size, colour and location. They also eat different things -some eat fish, some go for mammals. Whatever they usually eat, they can tell the difference between their normal food and a person with their echolocation. We don't look the same to them as a seal, or whatever else they might be eating. We are also not a very nutritious food, nor do we usually hang out where Killer whales do, so there hasn't been a need to adapt to make us part of their diet.

    They have bigger brains than humans, so they're a lot more intelligent than most people think!
