  • Engineering How are my headphones playing a "ghost station"?

    NEW
    Q:

    • I'm wearing a pair of over-ear headphones. They have good sound isolation and I've quadruple checked that the sound is not coming from outside the headphones.

    • The headphones are connected to my 52" LCD TV that's about 8 feet away. They are plugged into a head phone jack using a 6 foot 3.5mm jack male to female extension.

    • The TV is on the "PC channel" (VGA input). The computer's volume is off. The TV's volume is at 0. In fact turning the TV volume up adds noise which hides this "ghost station" sound.

    • My physical position affects the quality of the signal.

    • The sound is extremely faint. Quieter than my heartbeat.

    ELI5, The sound is clearly people conversing and then what I can pick out to be music, what is happening?

    Edit - it's definately music, so it's a radio station, I checked all the TV chanels (I don't have cable, I use an antenna)

    2) the cable is acting as an antenna and picking up a radio station (but what is the receiver?)

    3) I'm acting as an antenna and picking up a radio station (how would my headphones pick that up, and where's the receiver?)

    whitcwa A:

    A nearby AM radio station is being picked up and rectified by the TV.

    A simple AM receiver can be made with a junction of two dissimilar metals or a metal needle point on a crystal. I suspect the electronics in the TV are performing the rectification and the headphone cable is the antenna.
