    Q:

    Since Voyager 1 is the farthest human made probe from earth - if it looks at the earth the light reflecting off us right now wouldn't have reached it yet, but what light would've?

    serious-zap A:

    The light that left Earth about 18 hours and 6 minutes ago.

    Distance to Voyager 1 is 130.62 Astronomical Units and it takes light 8 minutes and 19 seconds to travel 1 Astronomical Unit (mean distance between the Sun and Earth)

    The0ldMan A:

    Voyager 1 is currently about 19.5 billion Km away from Earth. Converted to light years, thats 0.00206607877 ly. Multiply by 365.242199 light days in a light year to get 0.75461915326 light days. Which means it could take a picture of Earth as it was about 18 hours ago. For us to view that image it would take the same time to transmit back to us so if we viewed a picture it would be 36 hours old. Since its before noon here, the date of the Earth in the pic would be the 7th. But the picture was taken on the 8th, and received on the 9th.

    lmxbftw A:

    Voyager I actually HAS taken one of Earth after it left Saturn. This picture is often called the Pale Blue Dot, and the Earth is less than a pixel in apparent size at this point. It can't take another picture of Earth, but already in this image the scale of the Solar System is dwarfing our ability to comprehend it.

    The light reaching it is whatever was happening 6 18 hours ago. Just not much of the light reflecting off of Earth hits Voyager I since it's so far away.
