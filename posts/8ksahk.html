Lots of times the whales and dolphins that end up beached are already dying and exhausted from something else.

    kurburux A:

    Their own weight is crushing them. Their bodies aren't made to sustain their structure on land. It's like suddenly something very heavy is sitting on you so you have trouble breathing and your organs and ribs might get damaged.

    Also, their skin is surprisingly sensitive to sunlight. They can easily get sunburns. They also dry out if they stay on land. This is why people try to at least put wet blankets on them until it's possible to drag them back into the ocean. On a hot day whales on land might die from heatstrokes. Their skin isn't able to sweat.

    Some whales (smaller ones) might also be unable to prevent falling over to the side when they are in shallow water. Which means their breathing hole might get under water and they drown.

    100jad A:

    In order for them to be able to breathe, their ribcage would have to expand (as normally), but with it, it would have to lift their bodyweight. Their muscles in the ribcage are not made for this.

    In the water, this wouldn't be a problem, because the bodyweight isn't pushing down on their lungs anymore.
