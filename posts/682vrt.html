    FuckingClassAct A:

    Okay, so I was victim of identity fraud.

    Here's how it went down, I'm Dutch though so it maybe different wherever you're from.

    About a year after I moved from The Hague to a smaller town, I got a letter in the mail from a debt collectors office stating I hadn't paid bills for an iPhone 6 by a certain provider. Two things: I did not own nor have I ever owned an iPhone 6, and I had never been with that particular provider. So I called the debt collectors and told them about this. I also asked for any information they could give me, but they couldn't release an info at that time. So then I called that provider and explained what had happened. They couldn't give me very much info, but they were able to tell me that my passport had been used to sign the contract, no payments had ever been made, and the adress given was an adress I've never lived at. They couldn't tell me what the exact adress was but they were allowed to disclose the street name to confirm I did not live there. The provider was going to start an investigation and adviced me to file a report with the police additionally.

    I looked up the street on Google maps and found out it was very close to the building I used to live. I lived in student accommodation then and I highly suspect it was an employee in the facility. I had never shown my passport to anyone, I wasn't social in the building at all, I had also never lost it. I did however, have to leave a copy at the front desk. So yeah.

    I filed a report with the police and any charges from the provider against me were dropped. I never found out who it was.

    Dabum17 A:

    You should be checking your credit report 3 times per year (Once from each bureau) via www.annualcreditreport.com.

    Whenever you apply for a loan, a CC, an apartment, they check your credit, and those things show up.

    So if you see ABC Bank pulled your credit, and you're like 'who the F are they?' you know somebody has your info.

    Alternatively, you never check that stuff, and you get sued for the debt they rack up.
