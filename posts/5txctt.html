    szpaceSZ A:

    What does perfect pitch mean?

    It means, that the person can name the frequency on the absolute scale (via a tone name, e.g. A, c''-flat) vs. on a relative scale (do-mi).

    In that sense, we all have "perfect pitch" vision: our resolution of hues would be different, but we all sense the actual frequency repeatedly. You don't perceive "this colour is to blue, as yellow is to green", but you perceive "this is green". And the next time it's green to you again.

    Perfect pitch is basically the ability to memorize pitches and their associated names (A, F#, ECT). You can train yourself to at least relative pitch (memorizing one pitch and adjusting to the interval that you hear to figure the pitch out), but it does take work.
