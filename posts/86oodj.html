    Q:

    How do single called organisms decide what to do?

    albasri A:

    I strongly recommend Braitenberg's Vehicles: Experiments in Synthetic Psychology. It's a series of thought experiments about how complex behaviors can arise from very simple circuits. For more intense reading, you could try Optima for Animals by Robert McNeill Alexander. Both authors emphasize that we should be careful to avoid anthropomorphizing non-human entities by ascribing mental states like desires, beliefs, and goals. It's all well and good to talk about those things colloquially as when we say that a thermostat "wants" or "tries" to get the temperature to be at some goal, but when pressed we wouldn't say that a thermostat actually has intentions or desires in the same way that we do. Likewise for all other organisms, whether they be single-celled or very complex, we should be extremely careful in talking about such things.
