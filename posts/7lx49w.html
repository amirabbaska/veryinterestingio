    Q:

    ELI5: Why do leather belts start out straight, but after being worn a lot, get curved?

    blipsman A:

    When one bends over or even sits, the back belt loop pulls down on the belt where the pants would want to gap. Over time, this stretches it out and causes it to curve.

    A straight flat belt fits around a cylinder perfectly. Chances are you're not cylinder, because you have a bum, and maybe some belly. Your girth is bigger at the bottom of your belt than at the top of the belt.
