    therestruth A:

    Once your circadian rhythm has been disrupted for about 8-10 minutes, your body begins to slowly release endorphins as an emergency response to remain awake. This is mainly due to the same mechanism that governs "fight or flight" and is intended to provide awareness against predators or other threats. The endorphins are released slowly compared to a situation where you are immediately in danger, and the supply lasts longer. As you remain awake, the sensation of awareness is heightened at the expense of a larger crash later, when the supply runs out.
