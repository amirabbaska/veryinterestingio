    ADDeviant A:

    Much of what is being discussed is correct and true, but the original question and many of the answers are trying to answer in metaphor or comparisons to himans, and what human athletes vs regular humans do etc. but I want to throw out there that a jaguar is built like a jaguar because that's how jaguars are built. Bear with me.

    A jaguar has a certain genetic makeup, a certain environment that it both evolved and then grew to maturity in. It has a certain physiology, a life cycle, certain microbiome, diet, etc...

    A wild jaguar doesn't have to lift weights to be powerful and muscular: it just has to be born to otherwise fit jaguar parents and then live the life of a healthy, well fed jaguar. It grows up, tussle with siblings, roams a territory, hunts, climbs trees, swims, etc. and it's evolutionary history ensures that that is enough to maintain it's physiological capabilities. Deer are fleet, and wolves are dogged, and they have shaped each other that way. But, deer and wolves are not the same, and neither are people and jaguars.

    If you look at a zoo lion, you are looking at a very different lion than a wild lion, too, because the zoo lion doesn't get to do what a wild lion does, eat what they eat, etc. A wild lion roams miles and miles daily, fights on a pretty regular basis, chases off hyenas, and kills things to eat. A zoo lion may live longer, but is body won't react well to the sedentary life of a zoo. He will be less muscular, even if he is not fat, etc...

    Modern people in the western world are more like a zoo lion. Watch a documantsrtly about humans living a normal, pre-industrial life, like our ancestors did, whether that is hunter-gatherer, subsistence farming, or following your cattle all over. Look at guys from Papua New Guinea, or Africa, or SE Asia, guys who do normal hard work, lots of walking, hoeing and planting, shooting bows or whatever other cultural pursuits, hunting, maybe make a little war, etc... that is more or less what a NORMAL human physique looks like. Sure the Amazonians are stockier than most Africans, or whatever, but those broad chested tribesmen with thick legs and arms from Trobriand Islands don't lift weights, they just live life. Likewise, the San Bushmen, the guys who run antelope to death, they don't train, don't run intervals, or do progressive distance training, etc. They just walk a lot, run as part of daily life, and don't gain that extra 30 lbs, sitting behind a desk.

    That's how jaguars do it. Evolution and lifestyle.

    A:

    Exercise isn't necessary to build muscle. The human body is programmed to only build muscle if you seem to need it. Exercise tricks the body into building more muscle. Animals that are pre-programmed to build muscle by default can build muscle without exercising. As an extreme example of this, there are mutant cattle that lack the gene that makes the body get rid of muscle: http://media.npr.org/assets/img/2013/08/09/belgian-blue_wide-8307ef42e43d9c6614c810aecf2422e8454b2ee9-s900-c85.jpg Humans are evolved to prioritize having low energy needs over being super strong. In our evolutionary history, food was scarce, and didn't require a lot of strength to acquire. Growing bigger muscles made us need more calories so our bodies became programmed to only grow muscle if we seemed to really be using them a lot.
