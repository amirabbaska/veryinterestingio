    Q:

    ELI5: Why do you often have less of an appetite when the weather is hot?

    JLP2005 A:

    In heat, your blood moves from your larger blood vessels to your smaller ones that are closer to your skin. Your skin sweats, and decreases your heat. You cool off.

    When you eat, your blood carries nutrients away from your GI system.

    When you eat, you lose a portion of your available blood that could be useful for cooling down, so your body releases hormones to suppress your appetite.

    It's a lot easier to die of heat stroke on a hot day than starve.

    Its not just that it needs less energy, its that eating and digesting food produces a ton of heat. Digesting protein and fat, for example, produces a lot more heat than carbs or just fiber. That said, the body's goal is to maintain temperature balance for obvious reasons and so it aims to decrease digestion when already overheated. Thats why you never crave a really heavy fatty meal when its burning hot outside.
