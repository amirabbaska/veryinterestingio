  • Biology When I don't get enough sleep, why does a 15 minute nap in the afternoon help so much more than an extra 15 minutes in the morning?

    NEW
    Q:

    I just woke up from a nap and feel great, after feeling terrible all day. What is it about an afternoon nap that helps so much, vs the same amount of extra sleep in the morning?

    Majora272 A:

    I believe it has something to do with R.E.M. Sleep Cycles. Essentially, you're waking up during the lighter stage of your sleep cycle, which means you don't feel the groggy effects of waking during deep sleep.

    I'm sure someone smarter can expand on hormone release during the cycle that also has an effect.

    TLDR: You're not in as much of a deep sleep as you would be following a full nights sleep.

    langel1986 A:

    Your body goes through sleep cycles that last average around 90 minutes each. More or less depending on how long you've been asleep. I started setting my alarm to wake me up at exactly the end of a cycle and you'd be surprised to see hoe much easier it is to get up. Besides lack of sleep, most people feel froggy in the morning because they wake up in the middle of a cycle.

    So for example if you go to bed at 10 (if you are a person tbat normally falls asleep within a few minutes) you would have to get up at either 5:30, 7 or 8:30. Waking up at 6 would interrupt a cycle and disrupt your morning.

    I use a sleep app that tells me when is the best time to wake up based on what time Im going to bed.
