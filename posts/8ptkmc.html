    Q:

    ELI5: Why is it that when you hit the mouth of a beer bottle from above with another bottle it foams up like crazy?

    RearEchelon A:

    When you knock the bottle it sends a shock down through the glass. When it hits the bottom it rebounds up through the beer, forcing gas out of solution. The gas wants to leave the bottle, the beer wants to hold on to it, so the gas forces a bunch of beer out.

    This is a better explanation but is a little more advanced than ELI5

    Ustaf A:

    I few explainations here are close, but my understanding of why it happens is a little different.

    When you hit the bottle quickly it is forced downwards suddenly. Because the liquid in the bottle is not 'attached' to the bottle it is momentarily suspended (floating) inside the bottle until gravity accelerates it enough for in to catch back up with the bottles downward motion.when this happens, it is causing a gap to open up between the bottom of the bottle and the bottom of the liquid. Because there is no way for any air to get into this gap it is a vacuum. The pressure difference between the liquid and the vacuum then causes it to 'suck' all the CO2 gas out of the beer, filling the vacuum. Finally as the beer falls to refill this gap (gravity catching up) it forces all the CO2 back through the beer, creating a mentric f***tonne of bubbles.

    All of this happens within a fraction of a second and is almost impossible to see, but is much more clear in slow motion
