    Q:

    Hey guys, I'm terrible with numbers and never really sharpened my skills... I'm trying to be a bit more knowledgeable, and I noticed sometimes a number has an exclamation point after it, and I've never learnt what exactly this means.

    WinterWight A:

    The exclamation point means factorial. A factorial is when you multiply a positive integer by all the positive integers below it. For instance, 5! = 5*4*3*2*1 = 120

    Edit: pointed out that this is for positive integers, also known as natural numbers

    RearEchelon A:

    Factorials (n! = n * n-1 * n-2 * ... * 1) can be used to express very large numbers very simply. For example, 52! (the number of ways you can express the order of a deck of cards) looks like a small number, but it is equal to 80,658,175,170,943,878,571,660,636,856,403,766,975,289,505,440,883,277,824,000,000,000,000. (80.658 unvigintillion).

    That's 80,658,000,000,000,000 times as many atoms as there are on Earth (roughly 10^(50)).
