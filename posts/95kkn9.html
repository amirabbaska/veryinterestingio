    MyNameIsRay A:

    Most animals, humans included, are naturally defensive and violent. It's an evolutionary advantage to fight for your own safety.

    Sense and reason prevents them from fighting everything they see. Even at the top of the food chain, they know there's still a risk. A lion knows a gazelle can impale them. A dog knows that kitten can scratch the hell out of it's eyes. They avoid the conflict because the risk of personal injury outweighs the gain.

    Add some alcohol into the mix, and natural inhibitions are lowered (less concern for personal safety/consequences) while decision making is impaired (overestimate personal ability, underestimate theirs, disregard risks). Between the two, animals are just plain more likely to respond with violence.
