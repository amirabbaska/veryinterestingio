  • Why does the 72 hour hold for a voluntary psychiatric commitment exclude weekends and holidays?

    NEW
    Q:

    Or does it? My girlfriend checked herself in via ER on Saturday. I was told today, Sunday, that the 72 hour period would begin once she was seen by a psychiatrist, which had not yet occurred. I have been trying to very little avail to find information about voluntary holds. Thank you in advance for any wisdom and knowledge that you can spare.

    Edit: The situation now is that she had a plan to hurt herself that she had not decided to go through with. Years ago, before we even met, she did attempt suicide. She told me today that this was the reason that she was instantly placed on hold. There are no recent charges, no reason for her to be there against her will.

    Trulyacynic A:

    Chances are, even if your girlfriend went in voluntarily, if she is placed on a hold, then she will be held involuntarily for 72 hours. If she readily admits to being a danger to herself or others, she will very likely be put on a hold and transferred to a facility for treatment. If she does not meet the criteria for a hold, she should be released, as far as I'm aware. I'm certain recommendations will be made for her to follow and resources will be made known to her.

    You are correct, those 72 hours will not begin until she is given a Psych eval at the emergency room by whoever is on at that time. The Psychiatrist will write his recommendations, whether that is a hold or otherwise and she will be either released outright or released to a facility for processing and treatment.

    This is a tough situation to be in, hang in there, both of you.

    Source(s): Was an EMT, family who work in hospitals.

    Rainbow_Aura A:

    I can tell you it depends on the State, the Hospital and how 'busy' they are. If she went to the ER and said she has thoughts of hurting herself or others but DID NOT act on them but was still admitted to the psych unit she has to request a form of release. This takes 24-48 hrs from when it is submitted, signed by the patient and a Nurse Practitioner or Doctor. (There SHOULD always be a NP or Doctor on staff seeing patients 24/7 but again due to the location and lack of staffing this could be the following day or it seems in her case the following business day which is a large issue in the Mental Health Care field) You cannot be at risk of hurting yourself or others. If you are, the Hospital will put you on a 72 hour mandatory hold. This does unfortunately mean 72 hours from her medical and psychological exams. This is for the safety of the patient and society. Its like a cool down period where you are forced to think about any future bad decisions you are planning. Most of these start with you breaking the law and being brought in by police. If you are depressed or just losing your mind a little and need a time out from life to recoup, you are likely in the Psych Unit at will and learn to understand that staying for at least 72 hours will allow you to regulate your medication safely. Some people don't take medication but choose to take advantage of some helpful 'classes' during their stay. Depending on how busy the hospital is and how many people in the area are a high risk and need to be admitted they will be able to release highly stressed but non-dangerous individuals earlier than those looking to harm. Good Luck and hopefully she feels better when she's out!
