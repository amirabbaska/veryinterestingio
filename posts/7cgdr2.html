    Q:

    If a person is allergic to domestic cats, would they also be allergic to wild cats (lions, bobcats, etc.)?

    acatagorical2005 A:

    Yes. It’s been studied (back in 1990). There is some cross reactivity with the major cat allergic protein (Fel d 1). But the extent of cross reactivity is variable.

    “They tested 11 cat-allergic patients against Fel d 1-like structures in eight members of the Felidae family: ocelot, puma, serval, siberian tiger, lion, jaguar, snow leopard, and caracal. Hair from the big cats were collected, extracted, and used in a RAST system and histamine test. The results showed that all patients had cross-reacting IgE antibodies to seven of the Felidae tested. No IgE antibodies were reactive with the caracal. Eight of the 10 patient with IgG4 antibodies directed to cat dander also had IgG4 antibodies directed to several Felidae species, including the caracal.

    In general, those with cat allergies also showed an allergic response to big-cat dander, but it wasn’t as strong. Your cat allergies should not deter you from visiting a zoo or sanctuary. You will be kept a safe distance from the big cats. The study found that this mild big-cat allergy doesn’t usually affect the patients’ lives. “ Source/summary of original 1990 study - http://conservationcubclub.com/2016/02/allergic-to-cats-probably-allergic-to-lions-and-tigers-too/

    mdisred2 A:

    Allergies are reactions to specific proteins. There are some proteins that are common to all cats and there are some common to particular species. An example of this is persons with shrimp allergy. Some are allergic to both shrimp and prawns and some are not allergic to prawns because of one of the exact proteins that shrimp have and prawns do not. So the answer to your question depends on which exact proteins from domestic cats the person is allergic to.

    drmyk A:

    Yes. Fel d1, the dominant cat allergen, is conserved across cat species and similar proteins are present in big cats as well.

    So, if you're allergic to house cats, you're probably allergic to other members of the Felidae family.
