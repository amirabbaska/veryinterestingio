    Q:

    ELI5: Why do flies keep on landing on the same spot after scaring them away

    Deffdapp A:

    Because they have very limited mental capacity. They are like a very primitive computer programm.

    Find food source. Avoid fast moving thing on collision course. Breed.

    They can't process that there is an entity that doesn't want them to come to a certain spot.

    kd7uiy A:

    A fly's brain has a bag of tricks. One of them is to seek food when hungry. A second one is to avoid being hit. It does not have a trick to remember that food in a single spot might be dangerous. Insects do not reason.

    Source: I took a graduate class called "Reverse Engineering the Fly". Very interesting class.

    dopadelic A:

    It actually can remember food at a certain spot is dangerous. It's called fear conditioning and fruit flies (Drosophila) are extensively researched to understand the mechanisms for that. It involves a wide range of protein synthesis to form long-term plastic changes in the nervous system.

    They can learn to associate a stimulus with fear and avoid it. https://www.jove.com/video/50107/drosophila-adult-olfactory-shock-learning

    It might just not perceive your slow human reflexes to be dangerous.

    JohnGillnitz A:

    Flies, like ants, leave a scent trail with them. It is like a night club: the more people who are there the more people are drawn to it. That is why jar traps work so well. So many flies have climbed in to die other flies think it is a party.
