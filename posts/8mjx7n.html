    im_thatoneguy A:

    Depends on the definition of "feel sick". If you mean exhausted, tired and 'depressed' then there is a study on rats which demonstrated that proteins called Interferons appeared to be responsible for most of their "feeling sick" behavior. Interferons are released by cells under attack to alert the immune system to an infection. They're like a smoke signal to let the city know that an invading army is coming and rally the troops. The side effect is that the interferons impair brain function.

    So brain impairment from lack of sleep and brain impairment from immune system proteins both result in you feeling bad through different causes.

    dopadelic A:

    When you feel sick in those cases, it's probably you eliciting a stress response to force yourself to stay awake. This activates your body's sympathetic nervous system (fight or flight). Part of this response is to shut down your digestive and immune system which can manifest in feelings of sickness.
