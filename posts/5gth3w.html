    Q:

    ELI5: Is there any evidence that suggests covering your webcam is anything other than an act of paranoia? Can a webcam transmit video without the indicator light being on?

    SamTheGeek A:

    Yes. There's actually a specific example of this happening in the real world â€” the Lower Merion School District outside of Philadelphia. Back in 2010, the school issued hundreds of MacBooks to their students with security software installed. It turns out that this software could record from the camera while disabling the indicator light.

    EDIT: It appears that the software in question didn't actually have the capability of turning off the green light, but researchers inspired by this incident actually proved that it could be done.

    Most laptop webcams use software to control the state of the light, since they are almost always 'powered on' at some level, regardless of whether the camera is taking images or not. Modern Macs, and many PCs, use the same camera module to collect ambient light information for screen/keyboard brightness control. For this reason, it is possible for software installed deeply within the computer to control the light separately from the camera.

    There are also (unconfirmed) rumors that Apple's new MacBook Pro with Touch Bar includes security features specifically designed to prevent this kind of low-level access to the camera. EDIT 2: A quick explanation of how this works. The keyboard-screen-thing on the new MacBook Pro is actually an Apple Watch under the hood. The camera is connected through this, rather than directly to the computer.

    It can happen, but how often it actually happens is up for debate. In general, most people who are using computers for illegal spy-ish type things are trying to make money doing so. Targeting and watching one person is not a very efficient way to do so, it's much more profitable to send a few thousand scam emails and wait for someone to be dumb enough to tell you their credit card.

    If you have a job that's a few rungs up the ladder (ie the kind of person who's closing their office door from time to time for important business conversations), or you're a celebrity or other particularly interesting person... that might be cause for concern.
