    Q:

    ELI5: If my pen runs out of ink when writing a letter, why is it so difficult to try and write over the patchy bit of the word, even after the ink has resumed?

    s0v3r1gn A:

    A ballpoint pen needs the friction of the paper to roll the ball, inking it.

    It leaves an indent in the paper after being drug across it that smooths the paper out and reduces the friction available to roll the ball a second time. No rolling, no new ink on the ball.

    Also oils can reduce the friction on the paper preventing proper inking.

    SquaggleWaggle A:

    When you press down with a pen on paper, you create a small trough where the pen was moved. This trough has then been created, and makes it very hard for the ball on a tip of the pen to press against the paper, as instead of pressing on the paper it is just forming a bridge across the gap. This then means that the tip of the pen, where the ink is, cannot touch the paper and thus cannot draw a line.
