    NEW
    Q:

    For example: Person A almost always has two short sneezes in rapid succession, while person B usually has three sneezes with longer gaps in between. Any biological reason for this, or is it just a habit people get into?

    woundup A:

    A sneeze is a nervous reaction , like a reflex , when something tickles your nose.

    You don't really control reflex actions , so I doubt it is habit driven but more a function of how your nerves send signals and your nervous system reacts when something makes you sneeze.
