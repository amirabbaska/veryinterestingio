    Q:

    What have we learned from Cassini's dive into Saturn so far?

    Gargatua13013 A:

    The data will not be fully released until the research is written & published, but you can understand what was being measured fom the JPL site:

    "even as the spacecraft makes its fateful dive into the planet's atmosphere, it will be sending home new data in real time. Key measurements will come from its mass spectrometer, which will sample Saturn's atmosphere, telling us about its composition until contact is lost."

    So ... to answer your question: direct measurements and characterization of Saturn's upper atmosphere through mass spectrometry.
