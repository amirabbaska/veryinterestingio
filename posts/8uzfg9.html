  • Biology How does rubbing two slices of cucumber together remove its starch?

    NEW
    Q:

    I know this is an old trick to make the cucumber less bitter. When you cut off the ends and rub one in a circular motion on the remaining flesh, a white froth develops along the edges. When you wash that off the cucumber is quite sweet and tastes great. How does this work?

    7LBoots A:

    That white fluid is in the skin. The skin has capillaries, or microscopic tunnels, for liquid to flow through. Much the same way that trees get water from the roots up to the leaves, so that when you tap a Maple tree, sap flows out.

    When you put the ends together, you create a seal between the flat parts. When you slide them, water tension causes the fluid to stick to itself and to the cucumber solid. This pulls a tiny bit of fluid out of the capillary, which sucks against the rest of the fluid in the capillaries like sucking through a straw.

    The foaming is just an effect of the fluid being slightly sticky. It's not water, it's plant juice.

    The bitter flavor is in that juice, you're removing it. I know you know that, but I have to include it.
