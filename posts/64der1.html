    As I sit in a highrise condo I see a bumble bee fly in front of the window. It makes me think, how high could a bee (or bird) fly if it just went straight up. I'm sure oxygen or temperature have some factor here.

    Bees can apparently fly over Mount everest. http://voices.nationalgeographic.com/2014/02/04/bumblebees-can-fly-higher-than-mount-everest-scientists-find/

    Dillon and his team of researchers found that the bees they collected could fly at air pressure equivalents exceeding 24,275 feet (7,400 meters) above sea level, equivalent to some of the lower peaks in Nepal’s Annapurna mountain range. Two bumblebees were able to fly at more than 29,525 feet (9,000 meters)—higher than Mount Everest.

    Higher than any building, that's for sure.

    Bees exist in alpine regions, thousands of feet about sea level. In terms of oxygen, pressure, and temperature, those are exactly the same as flying that high in the air.

    The only real issue would be fatigue, no place to stop and rest going straight up. I couldn't find an information about bees, but a housefly can travel about two miles without rest. It would be a little harder to fly straight up, but a mile seems doable.
