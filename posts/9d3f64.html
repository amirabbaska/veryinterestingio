  • Culture Why is the national anthem sung at sporting events?

    NEW
    Q:

    I mean, we don't sing it at church. We don't sing it at the grocery store. We don't sing it before we watch concerts. We don't sing it before each episode of Battlebots. We don't sing it while in line at the DMV. We don't sing it before all sports - just some. We don't sing it before big chess matches, or while we're camping... What's the connection that makes it "important" to sing at some sporting events but not at other events?

    Awkward_moments A:

    International sporting events:

    It first started in rugby when the Welsh captain lead the Welsh fans in a song (a country famous for it's singing ability) in a response to the All Black's doing the Haka.

    The Kiwi captain later recalled that he have never experienced anything like that in his life. New Zealand went into that game with a winning streak of 27 games on tour but Wales beat them that day.

    Mordern recording before a game [Hen Wlad Fy Nhadau] (https://youtu.be/AM4mIlYKG9s)

    Non International games: I don't know, just American's being American I guess.
