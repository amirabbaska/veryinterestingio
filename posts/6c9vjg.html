    Q:

    ELI5: What is the blue diamond pattern inside envelopes for?

    Zemedelphos A:

    One risk of sending letters through paper envelopes is that someone could hold the letter to a bright light to decipher what is written on the letter inside. By introducing a pattern of darker lines (the diamonds), most of the light that would pass through is absorbed, and helps obscure the contents of the envelope, meaning it can't be snooped on without some indication that the envelope was opened. Besides just obscuring lettering, it can help obscure the contents, such as if there is cash or some important card inside.
