    Okay.

    Lots of pseudoscience going on in the ELI5 here.

    It's really simple:

    1% of the population has had a nut allergy (which includes peanuts because the allergen is in both) for as long as they've been measuring it. There's no data, whatsoever, to show an increase in the nut allergy.

    Nor is this 1% those who have potentially fatal allergies. This includes rashes, etc.

    What has happened is two-fold.

    1- People have grown more considerate of food allergies in general. If you had a deadly allergy you didn't eat out because no one gave a shit about crosscontamination. As the food industry changed, concerns for crosscontamination increased, partially to avoid harming customers, and partially to avoid legal liability.

    2-- Hypochondriacs. As more awareness of a thing increases, self diagnosis increases. Many people who claim a nut allergy do not test on an irritation test. You see a similar phenomenon with gluten where 98% of people who claim a reaction to gluten react to gluten-free products if told it has gluten and don't react to products with gluten if they are told it doesn't (the other 2% have fucking celiac or some shit so don't fuck around and play the odds)

    desertsidewalks A:

    Well meaning pediatricians told parents to delay feeding babies certain foods because they were known to cause severe allergic reactions in some people. Unfortunately, this made the problem worse because it turns out early exposure probably DECREASES the likelihood of developing those allergies.

    The American Academy of Pediatrics is now suggesting early introduction of peanuts (with possible medical supervision for babies at high risk of allergies), but it's too late for a generation of people who now have life threatening peanut allergies.

    An easy to read discussion of the issue: http://www.israel21c.org/israels-top-snack-bamba-prevents-allergy/
