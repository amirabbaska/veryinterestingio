    lagerbaer A:

    From a brain perspective I don't know, but from a cognitive perspective:

    When you first learn to read, you take a long time even just deciphering the letters (think back to preschool). But after much practice, your brain instead recognizes entire words or phrases at a quick glance.

    This is similar to how it feels with music. A beginner would read the score note by note. An advanced player does a quick glance and knows "Okay, next up is an ascending broken C major chord from C4 to C5, then a C major scale down from C5 to G".

    I'm sure you could study and measure this sort of "chunking".

    EverythingIsFlotsam A:

    Think about what you need to do to fluently read text aloud. You actually need to make sense of their phrases and even sentences to be able to produce the sounds and intonations of the sentence in a flowing way.

    I don't see why it would be any different for a skilled musician. They would be reading ahead and making sense of what's coming up before they produce it after a delay.
