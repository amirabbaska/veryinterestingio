    bwc6 A:

    Generally speaking, those symptoms are due to the activity of your immune system. There are lots of different types of viruses, but your body only has a few methods for trying to get rid of them. For example: fever, inflammation (sore throat and joints), and physical removal via mucus (congestion and sneezing).

    unia_7 A:

    Because the symptoms you are experiencing are caused by the immune response (immune system activation), not the virus itself. These are symptoms like body aches, fever and malaise.

    Sore throat and congestion are not general symptoms for a virus though. It indicates that the virus is affecting the mucous membranes of the upper respiratory tract.

    alanmagid A:

    Those are the cardinal signs of the release of an anti-viral protein called 'interferon', a family of proteins. Epithelial cells in airways, gi system, other cells release it in response to viral infection. It blocks invasion and slows infection in a non-specific way, giving the defense time to rally more specific and more potent defenses. Those can bring their own miseries.
