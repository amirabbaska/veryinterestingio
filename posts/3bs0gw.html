    CmplmntryHamSandwich A:

    Because they are different!

    In essence, texture is a major part of taste (along with flavor, aroma, etc). Since the thinly-sliced turkey shifts differently when exposed to the forces of your tongue, you can sense a different texture. That makes your brain interpret them differently, so you get a different taste sensation!

    You can equate it to other food form differences (corn on the cob vs corn kernels vs creamed corn) or, more abstractly, other aesthetic differences (people feel different levels of attraction to thick men and women than thin ones, even if it's the same person).
