    creeper_swan A:

    That’s called a globus sensation. Sometimes this is from food/pills/whatever getting stuck in a tiny pocket at the “base” of our tongue. Other times, if there was something there and then you get it out, the sensation can remain. Best strategy is to tuck your chin and swallow. This allows for the tongue base to basically move more and wash out anything that may have gotten caught in that tiny pocket. If it happens a lot, talk to a doctor! (Source: I’m a speech-language pathologist who works with swallowing patients)

    It's to prevent possible choking your body reacts like that. They are 99% of the time never stuck it feels like they are when they hit the back of throat usually when swallowed dry.

    Your esophagus has muscles that pull them downwards as you swallow greatly reducing the chance of a tablet being stuck.
