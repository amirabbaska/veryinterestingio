    Sluttynoms A:

    So your brain has lots of nerves all smashed together in the center of you brain called the suprachiasmatic nucleus. It's a very accurate clock inside your brain! We naturally look for patterns all the time and so does the nucleus. So when you get in a pattern of sleeping and waking up at the same time the nucleus at those times release a chemical called cortisol and a few others to wake you up! An alarm clock actually causes a lot of stress, so your brain pushes those chemicals out before it thinks it will go off!

    DudeWithAHighKD A:

    This is a simple case of confirmation bias. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Confirmation_bias

    Basically, you only notice when it happens and ignore all the times it doesn't happen to strengthen your preconceived belief. For instance, I am sure there have been times where you woke up in the middle of the night for no real reason, but because these events are meaningless to you, you ignore/ forget about them. When you wake up and it's 5 minutes till you wanted to wake up, you tend to remember that more because it's more interesting and appears to be rarer. Same goes for bus stops and what not.

    This is also the reason why it may seem like stores never have your size of clothes. You only really notice when they don't and never when they do.

    Hope this helps.
