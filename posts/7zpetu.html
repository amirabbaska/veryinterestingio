    Q:

    ELI5: What is Germany doing differently then America where America is trillions of dollars in debt while Germany has a several billion dollar surplus?

    MajesticGoats A:

    Debt and deficit/surplus are not the same thing.

    Germany has a sizable amount of debt that is comparable to the US in terms of GDP: https://tradingeconomics.com/germany/government-debt-to-gdp

    Nations can post a surplus every once in awhile, but in the long run nations all have debt.

    10ebbor10 A:

    In the EU, there is a rule that requires every country to either keep it's debt below 60% of gdp, or actively on decreasing it.

    Now, enforcement here has been generally lax, but it tightened up with the financial crisis. Since Germany is above the debt limit (a result of the reunification), they're working on reducing the deficit.

    https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stability_and_Growth_Pact
