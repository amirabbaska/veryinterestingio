    Q:

    ELI5: Why do humans enjoy patting/ scratching animals?

    tallyho_palico A:

    It’s a social interaction that is fairly common in a lot of mammalian animals. Great apes will groom one another by picking fleas and dirt out of their hair. This strengthens bonds within a group and reinforces their ‘family’ ties.

    Dogs and cats lick their young to make sure they’re clean and healthy.

    It seems to be ingrained within a lot of mammals to form bonds wherever we can - scratching, grabbing, holding etc... seems to be a fairly common trait shared by many species.

    We are after all an animal, albeit a highly developed animal but at the same time highly social and rely on social interaction.

    And also it could be seen as a way of playing, like when young animals roll around on another. Play is incredibly important for the development of young animals (and humans) even forming social bonds and developing habitual skills.
