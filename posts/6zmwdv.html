  • How come the US wasn't hit with sanctions for developing weapons such as the Cobalt bomb?

    NEW
    Q:

    If the whole point of sanctions is to prevent nations from acquiring and potentially using dangerous weapons, why was the US not officially reprimanded for developing such an inhumane bomb designed specifically to deny areas by irradiation?

    If the argument is that the "US would never use it" then why was it developed in the first place?

    rhomboidus A:

    Sanctions are a political tool.

    Who was going to sanction the largest economy in the world and political and military head of NATO during the cold war? Who would benefit from that? Why would anyone politically aligned with the US want to risk that relationship over a hypothetical bomb?

    mtranda A:

    There are two parts to this: first one is the fact that it was developed back when sanctions for such things weren't, well, a thing. Back then everyone was going crazy about becoming nuclear.

    Second, it wasn't developed in the sense that "it would never be used" but, rather, in the sense that they wouldn't use it first. It's there as a deterrent for other nations that would attempt to pull such a stunt in the first place. Or, more precisely, Russia at the time.

    Now, I'm not american. And I think such weapons are a terrible, terrible idea. However, just because YOU play by the rules, doesn't mean others will.

    So it's all quite nuanced.
