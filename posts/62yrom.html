    Grape-Nutz A:

    Don't forget that the different neurotransmitters serve different "messenger" purposes. Endorphins, for example, are opioids (painkillers) made inside the body, and can help you heal after hard work. Serotonin helps regulate body temperature, mood, appetite and sleep. Dopamine seems to, among other functions, reinforce certain types of behavior with a psychological reward.

    All three are related to "happiness," but none are simply "happy juice."

    dirty_monotreme A:

    Dopamine reinforces behavior, after your motor system realizes an external goal. The thrill of success if mediated by dopamine release from the ventral tegmental area and a motor sequence is reinforced by dopamine release from the nucleus accumbens, increasing the chance that it is repeated in the future. Serotonin mediates overall wakefulness and drive to act towards goals, so works over longer/earlier time scales. Adrenaline sharpens attention and prepares the body for action by dilating pupils and blood vessels, increasing heart rate, etc., which is the somatic aspect of arousal. All these facets together underlie the experience of pleasure, motivation and arousal. Source: I teach at a medical school.
