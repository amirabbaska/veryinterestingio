    Q:

    What would happen if two black holes came into contact with each other?

    fishify A:

    They would merge and become one larger black hole. This happens once their event horizons overlap. This combined black hole goes through a a process called ringdown, during which the object rearranges itself into a roughly spherical shape, becoming a canonical rotating black hole (a "Kerr black hole").

    You can read a bit more about black hole collisions here and what about the stages in a black hole merger here (PDF).

    We've saw this happen before. They merge into one big black hole, and if they're large enough or moving fast enough, they can create gravitational waves. This was detected very recently, and proves a lot about Einsteins theory of relativity that we weren't quite sure about.

    knowledgeispowerx A:

    Most answers until now focus on a general relativity description of the problem. However during a black hole merge energies / distances are reached comparable to the Planck length and thus quantum gravity becomes relevant. This is not so much relevant for the description of the GW emitted and measured by LIGO which can be described by the approximation we know as general relativity, still we should be careful to create the impression that we really understand what happens during a BH merge without a complete theory of quantum gravity.
