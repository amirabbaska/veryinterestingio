    ColeSloth A:

    I'm one of those people that partake in the super spicy. Part of it is genetic. Some people simply have less of a response trigger to capsaicin than others. The other part is something you can get used to with practice (to a point). If you have a want for handling spicy, eat a bit of hot as you can stand stuff every day. You become accustomed to it, and may learn to really enjoy it, but like a muscle, if you stop training it, you will start to lose it. Going a week without eating anything really spicy will leave you unable to handle super spicy food again.

    Also, as an added note: when you try one of those record breaking hot peppers, like the 1.4 million shu ghost peppers, you aren't eating a pepper with 1.4 million shu's. Record breaking peppers are grown in very special conditions and selectively bred to get that hot. Your ghost pepper you got a hold of is likely closer to just 600k shu. Jalapenos can range anywhere from 6k to 300k shu's.

    A good sauce to start with is Da Bomb: Ground Zero. 321,000 shu's .
