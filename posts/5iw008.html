    dale_glass A:

    You always compare things to what you're used to. Eventually the new incredible shiny thing becomes the new baseline.

    I remember when I went from learning on a 286 with no hard disk to a 386 with one. That was amazing.

    I remember when I went from 4 MB RAM to 16 MB. That was amazing too.

    I remember getting on an airplane for the first time. It was an exciting experience. Now it's just boring.

    I remember back when I used the internet at 56Kbps. Now at 100Mbps sometimes things are still too slow, despite it being a mind boggling 1700 times faster. Formerly downloading a DVD image would have meant taking up a phone line for more than a week of uninterrupted downloading -- it would have been more like a month in reality if I dedicated all the time I could for it, did nothing else with it, and my parents would have hated my guts for it. Today it's a coffee break, and I still get impatient.
