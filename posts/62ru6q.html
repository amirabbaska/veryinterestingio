    psych_professor A:

    Chimpanzees have been taught sign language, but their abilities only rise to the level of a human 2-3 year old child. At that age, children produce what is called "telegraphic speech" which are simple 2-3 word sentences that lack grammatical complexity and variety. They do not use function words (and, if, or, but, for, etc) and use limited verb forms.
    Psycholinguists interpret this to mean that chimpanzees are capable of symbolic thinking (to understand how symbols refer to things and actions) but are not capable of grammar, which is the crucial part of language that gives it "generativity" or the ability to say the same thing in a variety of different ways, and to produce novel/original ideas.
    One of the main theorists on this topic is Noam Chomsky, whose claim is that that grammatical part of language is instinctual and unique to the human brain, embedded into what he calls the Language Acquisition Device.
