    jemmylegs A:

    Acid is a solution of water with extra hydrogen ions, or protons, in it (a normal hydrogen atom is just one proton and one electron; a H+ ion has the electron removed, so is just a bare proton.)

    Your stomach is lined with cells called parietal cells, which pump protons into the stomach cavity in exchange for potassium ions. The proton pump itself, known as H+/K+ ATPase, takes a lot of energy to operate, because it is pumping the protons against a huge concentration gradient, forming a highly concentrated H+ solution (i.e. highly acidic) in the stomach cavity.

    mighty_paulo A:

    There are two types of glands in the body, endocrine that secrete stuff directly to your bodily fluids (such as hormones to your bloodstream) and exocrine glands that secret stuff to your body cavities (such as saliva in your mouth). Stomach acid is an example of the work of exocrine glands in your digestive system, and in order to protect your body from the harm of the acid, the stomach also produces a protective layer around itself. The acid itself is basically HCl (hydrochloric acid), which is the combination of only two elements, hydrogen and chlorine, which your body has more than the ability to produce at a fast enough rate.
