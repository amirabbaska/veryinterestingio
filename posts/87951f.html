    junzip A:

    So there are several ways that the severing of diplomatic ties is a sanction. On the individual level, the diplomats themselves will not be happy - a posting in the US is generally very prestigious. This may not seem important, but you have to remember that diplomats are members of a quite elite club where people struggle for status and ostracised is never good.

    More importantly, however, diplomats serve important functions. Firstly, diplomats are important for promoting and protecting Russian interests in the US, and useful to Russian businesses who operate, who are looking to invest, or are looking for investment from the US. Secondly, they serve an intelligence gathering tool able to keep a finger on the pulse of goings-on in a country. Though this is perhaps less important than it once was given the internet and easy access to media, there is no substitute for having someone on the ground interacting with other diplomats and government officials (as well as perhaps managing covert intelligence gathering). Finally, it’s a punishment because it is intended to demonstrate that the US and the UK are taking a hard line on Russian actions. Reducing diplomatic capacity necessarily reduces Russian influence in forums in the US, for example the UN, and also makes settlement of future disputes through peaceful diplomatic channels. In this sense, it’s as much a warning, as a punishment, which is why people tend to refer to these measures as ‘sanctions’ that incorporate elements of both.
