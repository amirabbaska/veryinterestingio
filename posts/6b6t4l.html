    deathaddict A:

    If by steal your identity you mean that someone used your identity to get a new phone, installment loan on a product and pretty much purchases that require a credit inquiry; then you wont be held liable.

    Credit scores cannot be sabotaged permanently if you find out someone has been making purchases without your permission or knowledge.

    Once you tell the credit union/police that you're a victim of identity theft, they do the rest and your credit score rolls back to what it was before all the inquires and purchases by the thief.

    Credit unions can't and WONT penalize you for purchases that were made under your name without your knowledge after you tell them what happened. Same thing with Credit Cards, most/all credit card companies hold 0 liability protection for all its customers in the case someone steals their card and tries to use it online/in-stores.
