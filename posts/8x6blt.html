    ELI5: Why when lying on my side, my brain prefers to watch parallel to the floor instead of parallel to my eyes?

    entropyNull A:

    Your brain is a very complicated machine that does a lot of things you're not aware of, especially with regard to processing images. Your brain figures out what's up and down based on visual cues and a special little gravity sensor inside your ear.

    Fun fact, in an [experiment](https://www.theguardian.com/education/2012/nov/12/improbable-research-seeing-upside-down), a guy was able to completely adjust to seeing the world upside-down, through the use of mirrored glasses. It took the guy about a week, but when they took the goggles off, the "normal world" was as confusing and disorienting as the "upside-down" world had initially been.
