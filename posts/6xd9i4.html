    I get 8+ hours sleep a night, and I always wake up feeling tired. Whereas my SO can get from 5-8 hours sleep and will always wake up feeling refreshed. Why?

    006fix A:

    You mentioned below that you wanted an explanation of the genetic factor, so I can try and provide one for you. I'm something of an insomniac, ditto my father and sister. We all share similar tolerances to lack of sleep, to staying up, and to waking up without much effort.

    There are a variety of endogenous hormones in your body that mediate sleepiness, and the sleep cycle. One of the most common is called "adenosine". It promotes sleepiness, and suppresses arousal (sciency term for how awake you are). It's the receptor subtype thats influenced relatively well by a cup of coffee, or some other caffeinated substance.

    Variations in your bodies natural adenosine receptors can make you more or less receptive to the effects of this substance, in much the same way as some people have a greater or lower tolerance to alcohol or weed or even just basic dopamine / seretonin. Your bodies receptors aren't all identical to those of other people, and small variances in the genetic code can influence the effect of these receptors. These don't neccessarily have to all be based on the receptor - they could also relate to more/less efficient post-synaptic cleanup of neurotransmitters etc, but the end effect is the same : you can vary from other people in terms of how you react to the same input of neurotransmitter.

    Me + my family all appear to have a variation in this receptor (amongst other things we're not very fond of caffeine), and as a result seem to be much more "awake" on average than most people. I think of it as being naturally two coffees up on the average person. Some variation like this (or others, there are a lot of these chemicals that influence sleep) could account for the variation between you + your SO's sleep patterns.
