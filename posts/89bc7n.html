    Q:

    How can a bolt from a crossbow have a higher penetration power but less range than an arrow from a bow?

    PresumedSapient A:

    Arrow range is very much dependent on aerodynamic stability. A longer arrow with stabilisation fins (fletching) goes a lot further than a stubby one without any fletching.

    Crossbow bolts are generally shorter, and require different (sturdier, less aerodynamic) fletching that doesn't mind following a solid groove. This combination of shorter shaft length and heavier/smaller fletching reduces their long distance stability.

    Penetration power is a function of the weight and speed of the projectile (and shape of the head, but let's assume that's equal for arrow and bolt).

    Crossbows can use all kinds of mechanics for cocking the string (levers, ratchets etc.), and are potentially much stronger than any bow can ever be (the bow being dependent on the direct strength of its user). This allows crossbows to fire heavier and/or faster projectiles, providing more penetration power.

    DanielTheDank A:

    From the top of my head, bolts are usually released at MUCH higher speeds but are less aerodynamic. More wind resistance means it slows down faster over time. High penetration because more energy up close.

    Arrows being more aerodynamic means that the will slow down less over time, but the initial energy is relatively low, so less penetration.

    ceristo A:

    This may be more of an /r/AskHistory answer, but I think a lot of it has to do with specific bow and arrow vs the specific crossbow. The English longbow had superior range and penetration to the French crossbows of the time. The results of which can be seen from the battles of Crecy, Poitiers, and Agincourt. This was due to the insane draw weight plus the use of a bodkin point, specially designed to pierce armor.

    Recurves today don't have anything close to the draw weight of an English longbow because, unless you literally trained your whole life, nobody would be strong enough to pull it back. Conversly, crossbows made of composite fiberglass with complex pulley systems outmatch anything a human could ever draw. I would argue the modern crossbow has a range and penetration superior to any bow and arrow.
