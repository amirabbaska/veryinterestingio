    Q:

    How does physical exercise reduce the risk of getting cancer?

    Awk_Hawk A:

    Actually studying something relevant to this for an exam. First, we need to know that one major cause of cancer is reactive oxygen species (ROS). These are usually peroxides (like H2O2) that form free radicals (highly reactive molecules). Most free radicals can pass membranes easily and wreak havoc on DNA. DNA alterations (mutations) can cause cancer. This is an oversimplification, but is all we need to know at the moment.

    There is a lot involved with ROSs, but one major process our body needs to get rid of them is the pentose phosphate pathway. This pathway helps make reactive glutathione. Glutathione is a molecule that reacts with peroxides to convert them to water (via reduction reaction with enzymes like glutathione peroxidase).

    So how does this tie in to exercise? Exercise stabilizes the amount of insulin we produce by stabilizing blood glucose levels, decreasing fat storage, decreasing stress, and probably other ways we don't fully understand yet. Insulin is a stimulator of glucose 6-phosphate dehydrogenase, the enzyme responsible for the first step of the pentose phosphate pathway. So when insulin is being released properly, this pathway works more effectively to ultimately reduce ROS and thus reduce cancer.

    TLDR: Exercise = better insulin regulation.

    Better insulin regulation = more pentose phosphate pathway activation.

    More pentose phosphate pathway = more active glutathione.

    More glutathione = LESS reactive oxygen species.

    Less reactive oxygen species = less cancer

    There are lots of other factors and pathways that probably contribute to exercise preventing cancer, but this is the one I am studying now and probably one of the most important.

    EDIT: Thanks for asking this question. Typing this up actually helped me review the concept a lot.
