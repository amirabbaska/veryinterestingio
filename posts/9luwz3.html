    Q:

    Gympie Gympie stings last an awful long time, with reports saying even after years you can still experience pain. How does this work ?

    I was tempted to put this in ELI5, but I'm good with the scientific explanation as it gives me something to deep-dive into Wikipedia to kill a few hours.

    RedPandaRage A:

    Dr Marina Hurley, an expert in Dendrocnide moroides, wrote an article about this topic just last week - http://theconversation.com/the-worst-kind-of-pain-you-can-imagine-what-its-like-to-be-stung-by-a-stinging-tree-103220

    Apparently you don’t even need to touch the plant to be affected. Just being in close proximity can cause respiratory impacts.

    I’ve seen this plant in the rainforest before. It’s looks so innocuous! I’m very glad I knew what I was looking at.
