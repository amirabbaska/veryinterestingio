    MultiFazed A:

    Why does Lego not take down knock-off brands like Lepin?

    LEGO can't take them down, because Lepin isn't doing anything illegal. LEGO's relevant patents have expired, so making knock-off brands is perfectly legal. This is exactly how patents are supposed to work: Give a company time-limited exclusivity for a novel invention that will eventually expire and allow anyone to make the item. It encourages innovation without locking that innovation away from competitors forever.

    JournalofFailure A:

    For years, Lego did fight competitors/knock-offs like Mega Bloks in court. But Lego's patents have since expired in most countries, meaning that there is nothing Lego can legally do to have them taken off store shelves.

    That's why so many Lego sets now are based on Star Wars, Batman and other franchises. They can't stop people from making copies of generic Lego sets, but they can ensure that no one else can sell, say, a Harry Potter Lego-style set.
