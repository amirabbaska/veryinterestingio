    Q:

    During the winter, humans are known to track animals via their footprints in the snow, as we do not posses the same olfactory capabilities as say a wolf. Are there any other animals which have been observed tracking animals by means of visual cues?

    Jewnadian A:

    A Norwegian study found that kestrels at least seem to be able to track the paths of small animals. They preferentially hunt over the established vole trails.. Which if I'm reading your question right is what you're asking, basically using 'historical' sensory data to track prey as opposed to direct sight and chase style hunting.
