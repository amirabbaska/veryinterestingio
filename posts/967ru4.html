    Q:

    What are the moles on our skin for?

    Dubanx A:

    Moles are just benign tumors of melanocytes. They don't serve a purpose so much as they are harmless growths of skin pigment containing cells. Luckily, moles are typically self limiting but sometimes they are not, and that's how you end up with melanoma.

    JPSYCHC A:

    Not every trait is “for” something. Some traits came about as a totally random side effect of another trait that was selected for.

    For example, we didn’t “evolve” to have less body hair, that trait wasn’t selected for. We just evolved to have less testosterone, and/or other hormones, because that helped us be less aggressive and thrive in communities. Just so happens a side effect of those hormonal changes was having less hair.

    Likewise, moles were probably not selected for due to their usefulness. Rather, they are just a side effect of the overall makeup of our skin/pigment.
