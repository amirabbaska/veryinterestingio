    Q:

    ELI5: Is there an advantage of any particular blood group over the other?

    Chamale A:

    O-type blood provides a bit of resistance to malaria. Also, some blood types are much more valuable for blood donation. O- blood can be donated to 100% of the U.S. population, and O+ blood can be donated to 84%, so people with those blood types are encouraged to donate blood as much as they can. On the other hand, people with AB- or AB+ blood are universal plasma donors, so they're encouraged to donate plasma instead of blood.

    kaostriker A:

    There are genetic advantages for certain blood subtypes to confer protection from diseases, most notably malarial infection. But that's as far as I know. There is no other tangible or intangible (such as physical prowess or heightened mental acuity) advantages from having a different blood type to others. It's just an evolutionary variation.
