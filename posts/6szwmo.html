  • Why do initially attractive people look more meh over time and initially ugly people look more appealing over time?

    NEW
    Q:

    ELI5: Why do initially attractive people look more meh over time and initially ugly people look more appealing over time?

    GepardenK A:

    Consider your feeling of a persons "attractiveness" as a unconscious evaluation of that persons health, success, ability to take care of him/her self, potential as a mate etc etc.

    These are really only relevant in the initial stages of a relationship. As you get to know them further you get more information from other sources than looks and your relationship (whatever it is) becomes more established. The relevancy of your attractiveness rating becomes diminished and it is therefore no longer benificial for you to judge them as harsly in either direction.

    For example: "Attractiveness" can be a good tool for figuring out which potential mate it is best to invest time & resources in on limited information. But once you're in a commited relationship judging the attractiveness of your partner as harsly is less relevant.

    kyledcsantos A:

    When you meet a really really good looking person for the first time, the only thing you know about them is their looks. Since that's all you know about them, you will first think, "Wow, they're really really good looking. Every other part of them must be just as good looking." But, after a while, you start talking to them and getting to know who they are. And maybe from those interactions, you begin to find out, "Oh, maybe they're not as great after all."

    On the other hand, when you meet a very ugly person for the first time, you will first think, "Ew, this person is ugly! Gross! Every other part of them must be ugly too!" But, after a while, you might start talking to them and getting to know them too. And maybe from those interactions, you begin to find out, "I was wrong, this person is actually pretty cool!"

    Now, if you weren't five, I'd say it's all about first impressions and either holding high or low expectations of a person purely based on looks. Getting to know people over time changes those impressions and depending where you first hold them, it's easier to go to the opposite side.

    However, this phenomenon isn't always true. You'll meet people in life who can be both attractive on the outside and inside as well as ugly on the outside and inside. Everyone's different.
