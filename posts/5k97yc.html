    stillworkin A:

    Dreaming involves using our current knowledge to truly generate scenes and situations. We can defy those things we know (e.g., flying in dreams, which isn't possible in real life), but because we are constructing them ourselves, it would make sense for us to have a lower chance of questioning our own creations.

    Related, does anyone know how and why creativity is still possible in dreams, whereas logic/reasoning is allegedly "turned off"? That is, about once every few months I dream of freestyle rapping (which I love to do in real life), and upon waking up, I can usually remember 2-4 of the rhymes, which are things truly pertinent to stuff in the dream.

    Also, many years ago, I actually made a creative, stupid dad-joke (I dreamt I was badly falling asleep in school, and I told a friend "I'm about to crash like Windows 98". I had never thought of that beforehand). What is going on here?

    cthulhubert A:

    It's the same answer as to why a dream can seem to encompass years in a single night. You aren't really "thinking" while dreaming; it's fundamentally similar to remembering, except with much less structure, so it comes out all mashed together, weird and haphazard. In fact, in those odd instances when you do question things or realize it's a dream, it's because you're remembering thinking about questioning something, or thinking about your dreams (can also be because you're starting to wake up).
