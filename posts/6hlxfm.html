    ELI5: Why do some people feel sleepier at 2 or 3pm than 10 or 11pm?

    felicialexis A:

    Circadian rhythms

    It's your brains way of interpreting the environment and regulating your internal "clock". In most people, this is regulated by the general brightness as sensed through your vision. Brighter lights = brain thinking it is daytime and awake time. Blind people can regulate through habits like meal times. I had something in mind before but I'm sleepy. I know that 2pm and 2am are when we are most susceptible to sleepy attacks.

    Also from a historical perspective, the early afternoon is the hottest time of day and working outside at this time could be inefficient due to the heat.

    bclagge A:

    As an addendum to other answers:

    A lot of people eat nutritionally poor foods for breakfast and/or lunch that are full of simple sugars, like donuts or breakfast cereals. The sugars enter the blood stream quickly and your body immediately tries to bring the blood sugar under control with insulin.

    Now the energy necessary for body functions has come, and gone, leaving you with a feeling of "crashing" and a desire to take a nap.

    In addition many people drink something with caffeine in the morning. This also runs out around the same time contributing to the crash.
