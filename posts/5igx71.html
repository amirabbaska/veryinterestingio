    TheMadridBaleOut A:

    This answers are all really looking at this from the wrong perspective, and assuming the US is using exclusively digital methods and network analysis. That's almost certainly not the case.

    The US has observed Russian interference before. In eastern Europe, as well as the United States. They've become familiar with some of the tools which Russia has used. So when these tools appear in DNC/RNC networks, that's a telling sign.

    The US, and it's NATO allies, also likely have HUMINT (Traditional Spies) in Russia's intelligence services. It's possible these sources reported details confirming Russia had intent to attack US assets.

    It's possible (very likely) the US had hacked into Russia servers to gain access to the same info HUMINT might have gathered.

    It's possible the US is constantly analyzing traffic which they believe to originate from Russia services. When they observed outgoing/in-going traffic from these sources to the DNC/RNC, that would confirm it. It's fairly common practice for them to knowingly let the attack continue for some time while they're monitoring it, either to identify tactics, or to intentionally feed false information.

    It's possible the DNC/RNC hack involved not only Cyber, but HUMINT assets from Russia. The DNC reported to the FBI they believed their HQ was bugged. That would require a person physically entering the facility. If the FBI/CIA could identify this person, it's possible they could find evidence they communicated with Russia in some way.

    There's a lot of ways the US government might have reached this conclusion that a IP spoof wouldn't protect.
