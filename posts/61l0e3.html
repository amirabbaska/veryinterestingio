    Q:

    ELI5: Can a child be conditioned to think insects are the cutest thing ever and be instinctively afraid of kittens?

    awesomeJS A:

    When studying ethics in psychology at uni I read about "Little Albert". The short answer to your question is yes, a child can learn a phobia of something not usually thought of as phobia-material.

    https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Little_Albert_experiment

    In short, they used the techniques of Pavlov with classical and operant conditioning, only Pavlov famously used it on dogs with food. He rang a bell when feeding the dogs so they connected food with the sound of the bell and later they started expecting food as soon as the bell was rung.

    Watson and Rayner did the same thing but with a child and fear. It's a good read and very scary thinking about what the lack of clear ethics codes will give.

    Kuromimi505 A:

    Instinctively is already programmed in. They are born that way.

    As mammals we react to other mammals is a somewhat positive manner on average, particularly ones with "baby" features.

    Most mammals also have an instinctual fear of spiders or snakes. Just watch a cat encounter an unexpected belt if you need evidence. Even if they have never seen a snake before, they are "pre-programmed" to fear or be wary of the shape.

    As far as socially conditioning a child to fear other things, and exposing them heavily to others so they do not? Sure you can do that. Cause enough trauma and a person could fear anything.

    But it won't be an instinct.

    Edit: Scientific backup for spider/snake thing as instictual in humans.

    https://phys.org/news/2015-04-human-spiders-scientific-focus.html

    skookskook A:

    Yup. Conditioning is where it's at.

    25 years ago in Greece, dogs were considered a nuisance. There were despised strays everywhere (a massive euthanization prior to the 2004 Olympics ended this).

    There was then a public education program to foster positive feelings towards dogs. They brought dogs into schools, where many kids initially reacted as if you brought in vermin.

    The program worked, and the number of dogs kept as pets exploded.
