  • Planetary Sci. What is the average distance between Trojan asteroids at Jupiter's L4 and L5?

    NEW
    Q:

    Just read NASA's press release about Lucy and Psyche, the former of which will explore six of Jupiter's Trojan asteroids. Got curious as to how densely packed these swarms of asteroids are at L4 and L5.

    For the curious: https://www.nasa.gov/press-release/nasa-selects-two-missions-to-explore-the-early-solar-system/

    edman007-work A:

    Lets do some math, per the wiki there are 1 million that are over 1km in size. They orbit at 5.2 ± 0.15 AU, there are two swarms that are 26° long, and they have orbital inclinations of up to 40° with a width of 0.6AU.

    Anyways, those numbers don't agree too tightly to me (orbits should match swarm size, but they don't seem too close, I think the 40 degree number is an extreme and the orbital radius is a small number. So assume each swarm is a cylinder, 26 degrees long (2.4 AU), and 0.3 AU in radius (half the width). That makes each swarm 0.68 AU^3 in volume, and the density of asteroids is 1 million/1.4 AU^3 or 714 thousand asteroids per AU^3.

    To get an average distance, using this I get 0.874*(3/(4*pi*714000))^(1/3) = 0.006 AU (someone double check me). Anyways, that works out to just under 900,000km between asteroids which sounds right to me.
