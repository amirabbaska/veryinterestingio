  • Why are police uniforms so unathletic/restrictive?

    NEW
    Q:

    Now, I'm not from the States, but in my country (and from what I have gathered from videos and pictures of police uniforms from policemen from different countries) the police uniform looks so restrictive, by that I mean they don't look stretchy or like you could run in them. Why? Surely those pants tear/sag when you're chasing down a criminal, and those shoes look far too bulky, they're probably uncomfortable or too heavy. Even the US secret service have to wait a suit and tie and stuff, wouldn't it be more suitable to wear something more athletic (like track pants and a knitted shirt). I know they're meant to look professional, but should practicality really be sacrificed for it?

    kouhoutek A:

    Despite what you see on TV, police spend very little of their time chasing suspects on foot. Distinctive clothing and comfortable walking shoes are far more important than being able to run. Plus, they are wearing a belt with 10-20 lbs. of equipment on it, that's going to be a bigger impediment when running than tight pants.

    Also, many suspects are young and fit and will be able to outrun police anyway, relying on group tactics and communication is much more effective than footspeed.

    DarkAlman A:

    It's like the difference between a military dress uniform and a combat uniform.

    Combat uniforms are meant to be comfortable, light weight, and practical.

    Dress uniforms are meant to project conformity and authority.

    It's the same with the police, it's not about being practical, it's about what message the uniform is sending. "I'm in charge, respect my authority"

    stairway2evan A:

    90% of what police do is projecting the appearance of authority, safety, power, whatever. Actually chasing down the bad guys is a last resort - most crimes are managed or prevented altogether just by the presence of police, rather than them actually having to do anything.

    So the goal of a police uniform is to be distinctive, uniform, and to give the appearance of power. After that's accomplished, functionality becomes important. It's most important overall that people see a police officer walking down the street, or driving around town, and can instantly identify him, because that's what will make the average person say "It's not worth doing this, I'll get caught."
