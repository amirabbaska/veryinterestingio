    Q:

    ELI5: Would the Attack on Titan 3D Maneuver gear work on Earth? If not, anywhere else?

    kinyutaka A:

    No. 3DMG relies on rapid changes to momentum that would wreak havoc on a human body that weighs 150-200 lbs, especially since most of the force would be applied to one section of the body.

    Your second question is the more important.

    At 1.35 m/s^2 , there is a planetary body where the average human of the same dimensions would weigh only around 30 lbs, and furthermore large-scale monsters like the Titans could theoretically move around. Plus, it can be theoretically terraformed for human life.

    That location is, of course, Saturn's moon, Titan.

    SemiproAtLife A:

    Others have answered the gear physics well. Lethal forces across the board. It's the same type of issue that ruins FTL travel fantasies.

    To expand a bit more on the animal side of the equation though, the mass of most creatures follows a cubic scaling with size, while their skeletal structures only increase squarely. The largest creatures are all in the ocean due to the water essentially supporting their masses against gravity. The largest titans would hardly be able to move, and their size itself might kill them. Breathing and cardiac issues would wreck them, especially as bipedal mammals. In fact, the small ones, which are blocked by the wall, would be the only actual issue as predators.

    So you would need either everything to be underwater, or go to a planetary body that allows both sides of the equation to be viable, and still the humans still might be too fragile to operate the gear properly. Think of how bungie cords work, then realize that the 3D cables won't be doing much stretching. They'd be more successful with crushing, falling, or tripping traps on Earth if not these celestial bodies, which won't have the oxygen levels to support huge beings.
