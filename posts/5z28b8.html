    Q:

    Eli5: Why does the alcohol in alcoholic beverages smell different from the alcohol in beauty/hygiene products?

    Zerowantuthri A:

    There are different types of alcohol. Primarily:

    • Isopropyl Alcohol (aka rubbing alcohol...what a nurse rubs on your arm before drawing blood)
    • Methyl Alcohol (aka methanol...usually used as a solvent)
    • Ethyl Alcohol (aka grain alcohol...what people drink)

    You definitely do not want to drink the first two. What they use in hygiene products is usually isopropyl alcohol.

    FreneticPlatypus A:

    There's different kinds of alcohol. Ethyl alcohol (C2H5OH) is the kind you can drink. It's in everything beer, wine, spirits. It is the same compound no matter what drink it's in. Isopropyl alcohol (C3H8O) is rubbing alcohol and is used in cosmetics, lotions and on your arm before you get a needle. Methyl Alcohol (CH3OH), aka methanol or wood alcohol, is an industrial solvent in things like paint remover.

    kodack10 A:

    From a chemistry definition, an alcohol is any similar group of atoms forming hydrogen, oxygen, and carbon bonds in an alcohol arrangement. There can be ethyl alcohol, which is ethanol found in gasoline and alcoholic beverages, and there can also be methyl alcohol which has a similar evaporation point and weight as ethyl alcohol, but it is poisonous to humans.

    The alcohol in your first aid kit is usually ethyl alcohol with just a little bit of methyl alcohol added to make it un-drinkable. This is done in order to properly tax alcoholic beverages. If you sell alcohol for anything other than consumption, it's inexpensive and not taxed as drinking alcohol. However anyone could sell straight grain alcohol as 'medicine' and people could get drunk off it, bypassing the alcohol taxes. So the methyl alcohol is added to it to make it poisonous and unfit for drinking. Because the boiling temperatures are so similar, it's not viable to distill or separate the two alcohols.

    Because there are different types of alcohol groups, not all alcohols look, smell, and taste, the same.
