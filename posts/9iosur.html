    HonkHonkBeepKapow A:

    I am not a biologist, but I do not think having two heads provides a significant advantage to survivability. The only case where having a "backup" head would save your life is if your other head was irreparably damaged (cut off, impaled, etc.) and in such cases you are likely to die of blood-loss or infection regardless.

    In all other circumstances, a second head is more of a liability. It requires you to consume more calories than normal to support a second brain. A body with two heads may require a stronger heart or lungs to ensure that both brains are adequately supplied with blood and oxygen. And as /u/MrPrevenge points out having a second head significantly increases your biological complexity. Simply having two heads isn't enough; you also need a system to control how they work together.

    If having your head cut off is a threat to your survivability it is far simpler to evolve defenses against that danger (e.g. strong legs to run away from whatever is trying to decapitate you) than to evolve a redundant head.
