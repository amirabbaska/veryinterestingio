  • Why are barrel bombs so bad?

    NEW
    Q:

    In Syria we hear new reports about barrel bombs being used by helicopters and how horrible they are. Bombing of civilians aside, what makes them inherently worse and more frowned upon than a 'standard' HE bomb dropped from a plane?

    rahrah47 A:

    Explosives are categorized by burn rate. High explosives (high burn rate explosives) provide a quick “punch” that are good for shattering rock etc but don’t scatter a lot of material. Burying high explosives in the sand would do almost nothing when detonated. Low burn rate explosives have a large gas expansion which tends to move lots of material. Setting these off underground would lift the dirt and create a large crater. Setting off a low burn rate explosive in rock would tend to scatter large chunks instead of shattering the rock into small pieces. High explosives are good for surgical strikes where the destruction need to be contained in a small area. Slow burn explosives are more likely to damage surrounding buildings and cause more collateral damage. The bombs to which you are referring use ANFO explosives which are of the slow burning type. This is a common explosive in construction and excavation where large amounts of material need to be moved.

    Rimfax A:

    In short, barrel bombs are only really good at killing noncombatants in cities. They work well on military bases and in confined combat areas, too, but in cities they mostly kill civilians.

    They don't penetrate armor or break defenses. They just make everything burn and make the area uninhabitable for anyone not in a military vehicle or a bunker.

    agate_ A:

    It's not that the technology is especially horrible, it's the number of civilians they kill. Which is partly due to the technology, there's no good way to aim them, and partly due to deliberately targeting civilians.

    But the reason you hear the phrase "barrel bomb" so much in Western media is at least partly propaganda. It suggests that the Syrian government is so desperate and isolated that the only bombs they've got are jury-rigged barrels, and yet they're still managing to slaughter people. The implication is that if we got more seriously involved we could settle this thing before lunchtime.

    I am not going to comment on the wisdom of serious military action in Syria, my point is just that the phrase "barrel bomb" comes with an agenda.
