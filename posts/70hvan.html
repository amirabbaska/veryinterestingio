    Synnet A:

    There are a lot of factors that come into play when it comes down to coffee and caffeine. Question to you is, are you buying the same type of coffee every time you go or is it a different type? Caffeine levels in coffee vary from brewing method all the way down to the type of beans, ground level, temperature, brew time, etc. TLDR; There are a ton of ways caffeine content inside coffee can vary based on the way it is prepared.

    msk1974 A:

    Brain chemistry and hormone fluctuations. Think of this - some days you are just in a good mood. Some days you might be crabby for no apparent reason. Our bodies are not perfect at maintaining perfect and exact chemical and hormonal harmony. They tend to fluctuate from all kinds of factors: sleep, exercise, diet, etc. And in many cases, and with many people, these fluctuations can occur without much outside influence and even require medication to assist maintaining normal or consistent levels. In any case, the introduction of something like caffeine can greatly affect those sometime subtle fluctuation levels and seem to affect us differently base on where our bodies chemistry or hormonal levels currently are.
