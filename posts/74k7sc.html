    Q:

    When and what made humans realize that light speed isn't instant?

    LeChevalierMal-Fait A:

    The danish astronomer Romer was the first to suggest this, when observing the moons of Jupiter their orbits he noticed that they deviated from what Newtonian motion predicted. However they did not deviate randomly when the earth was close to Jupiter they were more precise, but became increasing divergent when earth was on the opposite side of its orbit.

    Either Newtonian motion was flawed, or there was another factor at play namely that light was not instantaneous.

    Working from the distance between the earth and Jupiter and the size of the divergence Romer calculated the speed of light.

    Edit: I looked up Romers calculation and he was 30% off modern calculations for c
