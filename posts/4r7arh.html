    Q:

    ELI5: Why can the feeling of hunger "pass" after being hungry for a few hours?

    KaDeCh A:

    Basically, think of it like this: You have a pantry and a refrigerator. The pantry has shelves, representing your muscles, canned food, representing your stored fat, and a refrigerator full of food, as your glucose.

    You eat the food in the refrigerator until you can't replenish it fast enough, so you (your body) moves on to the canned foods (your fat) until you can get more food in the refrigerator (glucose).

    Now, if you were not able to get more food in the refrigerator after you ate all the canned food, your body will start to use the shelves as energy (let's just pretend you can digest wooden shelves).

    When your muscles are being used for energy, it's at that point that your body is dying.

    So, that's basically how your body works.

    know_my_judo_well A:

    When the body is in a starved state, such as when you are feeling hungry, the body searches for sources and stores of glucose to chew up for energy. However, after a time, the body uses up all its stores of glucose and enters a state of 'ketosis'. This is when the body enters preservation mode, and starts burning off fat and protein stores for energy instead (glucose is preserved to be used as energy for the brain). At the same time, the enzymes in the stomach and pancreas which trigger the sensation of hunger are turned off to further preserve energy.
