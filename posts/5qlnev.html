    The whole idea of IQ is pretty outdated at this point.     SIrFluffsalot35 A:

    As it has already been said we don't know a lot about why this is. However, in the mid 1980's after a scientist studied Einstein's brain. What he found was different than what was expected. Instead of Einstein having more neurons or synapses than the average brain, he had nearly twice as many glial cells. What is known about glial cells is that they provide support and nourishment for neurons, and they play an important role in synapse formation. While the meaning of this difference is still unclear it seems that Einstein's brain had much more neural support than many others, which could have assisted in his higher learning.
