  • Biology Is there any correlation between eating less and sleeping more?

    NEW
    Q:

    As of the past 3 weeks or so I have been in an unhealthy rut of not eating more than once a day. Even the food I do eat would not be considered a balanced meal in the slightest. With working and going to college I became irresponsible with my eating habits, eating out mostly due to the convenience factor and low-cost. However I have been noticing that I get way more sleep at odd hours, (14 hours on off days) and it has become a nuisance when it comes to waking up for class on time. I'm wondering if a sub-par diet has any relation to sleeping more and imbalance of my circadian rhythm?

    amourphousblob A:

    Bad diets can definitely have an effect on your sleep schedule. I was in the same position as you in November, I didn't understand why I was sleeping all the time or why, after a full night's sleep, I was waking up exhausted. As I worked irregular hours I would never eat proper meals or eat well at all. The lack of healthy food in my diet was essentially starving my body of iron, leading to constant fatigue. The best thing you can do is try to eat more red meat (I.e. Steak) or get more dark, leafy greens in your diet (ideal would be kale, but spinach is also great!). Try to get more protein in your diet as well, so maybe take a bag of mixed nuts as a snack if you're on the go. It's difficult changing your diet, especially if you're busy, but you'll start to feel much better for it pretty much immediately, and it can help get your sleep schedule back on track too!
