    Q:

    Why is bagel dough boiled first before baking?

    cdb03b A:

    Boiling it in the lye water, sugar water, or salt water depending on the type of bagel is what gives it the unique skin that you find in no other kind of bread. Without doing so it would just be a hoop of normal bread and would have few of the characteristics that define a bagel.

    Corumir_Duvantar A:

    It's ALL about that smooth, chewy crust. Boiling sets the exterior before baking and it keeps the bagel from rising too much while in the oven so you wind up getting that dense inner bread texture. They're only boiled for about 30-60 seconds before they get time in the oven. The longer the boil, the more dense and chewy it gets.

    No boiling, and like it's been said, you get a ring of bread without all that chewy texture you get with a bagel.

    It makes it shiny and makes the texture nice. Just baking makes it tough and chewy and dense. Boiling it gives it the texture u really like

    longtimegoneMTGO A:

    Boiling the bagel changes the surface of the dough, mostly through gelatinization.

    The heat of the boiling water breaks down the starch and allows it to dissolve and reform into a gel. This gelled surface acts reacts differently to the heat of the oven, resulting in a smoother, chewier outside of your bagel.
