    Q:

    ELI5: Why does your body get the tingly good feeling when you stretch?

    Hellcowz A:

    When our muscles and ligaments aren't moved often, they tend to cramp up. We don't move as much in our sleep as we do when we are awake. When we stretch, it helps our bodies release waste and stress from sleeping and helps rejuvenate our ligaments and minds. Stretching helps us wake up more and prepare us for our days.

    When you sleep, your muscles lose tone and fluid tends to pool along your back. Stretching helps to massage fluid gently back into the normal position. Also, your muscles protect themselves from over-extension by inhibiting the nerve impulses as they approach their limit.

    NotaRobotKyle A:

    I work in sports medicine with college athletes- the "tingly" you might be referring to is the stretching of a nerve. When stretching muscles, you should feel a stretching sensation and that shouldn't tingle or cause pain or discomfort. Nerves, on the other hand, don't like to be stretched and when you're stretching a nerve you'll feel more of the burning, tingling, and numbness feelings. Nerves don't stretch, muscles should be stretched. Static and active stretching should be incorporated into a proper exercise program to help lengthen muscles and ease the pressure on nerves. TLDR: Muscle stretching is good, stretching of nerves is not necessarily good and could be causing the tingly feeling
