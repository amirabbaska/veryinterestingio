    Gramious A:

    Fingerprints start at a junction layer of skin called the Papillary Junction. It's where the dermis and epidermis meet. The friction caused by different growth rates of these layers of skin causes tension and, in turn, the fingerprint ridges.

    Strangely, the above is just a leading theory. There's really no agreed upon it fully researched answer, mostly because who cares? And why care?

    So, as others have said, damage past that junction and you've damaged the "source" of your fingerprint.

    Source: extracted fingerprints from internal 3D scans of the fingertip for my Research.
