    ELI5: Why is the European wildlife relatively so benign, compared to the one in the other parts of the world?

    Loki-L A:

    We killed all the dangerous mega-fauna.

    There used to be wolves and bears and even lions around to prey on people. There also used to be wild aurochs roaming around and all sorts of smaller critters.

    Humans ended up killing them all.

    The combination of being densely settled and having much of the wilderness destroyed to make room for agriculture means that there is not much room for wild animals that don't get along with humans.

    ZachMatthews A:

    Europe is the original home^^1 of the following large animals that will kill you, in order of likelihood if you mess with them:

    Woolly Mammoth

    Aurochs (wild cattle)

    Neanderthal man

    Wooly Rhino

    Short-faced bear Cave bear (basically a massive grizzly)

    Brown bear (grizzly)

    Straight-tusked elephant

    Wisent (European bison)

    Saber-toothed cat

    Cave lion

    Cave hyena

    Horse

    Timber wolf

    Wild boar

    Moose (elch)

    Giant deer

    Elk (Red stag)

    Of those, only a handful of mostly game species remain, like red stag, moose and wild boar. Timber wolves have mostly been extirpated except in European Russia and maybe the Pyrenees. All of the truly dangerous game was either wiped out (mammoths, their predators like cave lions and cave bears) or, actually, domesticated. The aurochs in particular was famously dangerous in Roman periods; its last wild descendant was killed in Poland if I remember right in the 18th century. You can see its domestic descendants in any cattleyard. Likewise horses and Neanderthal man were domesticated, with the least placid individuals wiped out. In the case of Neanderthal man we inbred with some and killed the rest.

    Europe is basically a garden. It has been as heavily modified by man at the height of his abilities as anywhere in the world, AND its climate has changed radically since we got there, which put additional pressure on the local megafuna in a way inconsistent with, say, India or Africa.

    Edit: Fair point about brown bears (grizz). For whatever reason, European brown bears don't have the fearsome reputation of North American grizzlies, but I still do not advise you to poke one with a stick if you see one in Russia, the Balkans, or Finland.

    ^^1 i.e. Europe was home to these animals when modern man (Homo sapiens sapiens) arrived. Many evolved elsewhere and migrated to Europe over the years before Man arrived.
