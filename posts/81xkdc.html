    So essentially it's your body's reaction to not having food, the early signs of "starvation". When you're hungry your body signals to your brain saying "Hey, feed me you buffoon!" But when you don't respond quick enough your body takes matters into it's own hands and begins to break down fat into sugars so your body can continue it's processes. This is the same idea of going to bed hungry and waking up not hungry. It's not that you aren't necessarily hungry anymore, but the actual needs for your body are temporarily being met and that's good enough for it to stop sending hunger signals to you.

    Agreeable_Dragon A:

    The ELI5 response is just your body needs food so it creates ghrelin, the "hunger hormone" so that you have an increased motivation to find something to eat. After not eating for a while you stop making the hunger hormone because it's not seemingly helpful and will return later.

    More explanation is:

    After a while your body begins breaking down fat and or muscle to fill the energy needs of your body.

    If you're fat adapted body fat will be used primarily but if you're on a high sugar and carbohydrate diet your body will convert muscle into glucose in a process known as gluconeogenisis.
