    Q:

    Why does my hearing go muffled when I get a headrush from standing up?

    Daannii A:

    Because this phenomenon is caused by a sudden drop in blood pressure and oxygenated blood is not reaching your brain, so you kinda loose a little bit of all functioning including muscle control. Vision, speech, and hearing.

    https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orthostatic_hypotension

    nickcagefan2 A:

    Your blood vessels are pipes filled with fluid, like a rain stick. When you’re sitting, it’s like the rain stick is on its side. When you stand up, you’re suddenly turning the rain stick on its end, and all the blood rushes to the floor (away from your brain, which makes you feel dizzy and muffled). Your blood vessels are supposed to tighten up while you’re standing so that you don’t feel dizzy/muffled, but for a variety of reasons, some people’s vessels are a little slower to tighten up. It’s called Orthostatic Hypotension
