There are several types of OTC pain relievers. Advil (ibuprofen) belongs to a class known as NSAIDs. Non-Steroidal Anti Inflammatory drugs. Some other well known NSAIDS are naproxen sodium (Aleve) and Ketorolac (Toradol). NSAIDs work by stopping the body's inflammation response. These drugs inhibit certain enzymes that produce inflammation/pain at the injury site, as well as fever. Essentially, NSAIDs lessen the body's physical response to an injury. Acetaminophen (Tylenol) on the other hand, works differently. It's not entirely understood how Tylenol works. It doesn't reduce inflammation in injuries and appears to only inhibit enzymes in the brain. Tylenol isn't actually doing anything physical to the injury site. So, while NSAIDs stop swelling and pain at the injury site, Tylenol appears to stop your brain from recognizing the pain.

Another interesting fact, both NSAIDs and Tylenol are metabolized by the liver, but excessive Tylenol intake can damage your liver and kidneys.

Source: Medical School
