AGiantNun A:

There's an old line that says that you can be at war with France but never at war with Paris. In Paris they had a very interesting set up called the salon. It was basically the first restaurant culture. Before this you would go home to eat or if you were traveling would basically stay at a bed and breakfast and eat whatever was served to you. In the salons they offered more than one option for your lunch or dinner. If you didn't like a particular Salon you could walk across the street and go to a different one. Quite literally the first time in history anybody had real options for lunch. This led to a competitive nature between the different salons to attract clients based on their food. So chefs would try to outdo each other and create better and better and better recipes. The rest of the world picked up on it later, copying the French model, but they were years behind in actually working out recipes.
