I found this paper to be quite readable on the subject. Here's the tl;dr version:

  • You have to directly image the planet in order to do it because you need to look at the spectrum of light coming from it.

  • O2 is twice as hard to detect compared to H2O

  • chlorophyll will be 6 times more difficult to detect compared to O2. Factors like cloud cover on the exoplanet factor heavily into this.

  • It's possible that level of resolution will only be achieved when the Advanced Technology Large-Aperture Space Telescope (ATLAST) telescope is launched sometime after 2025.
