The receptors on our body that detect temperature changes are called Thermoreceptors. There are two basic categories of thermoreceptors: hot and cold receptors. They are found all over the body, but cold receptors are found in greater density than heat receptors. The highest concentration of thermoreceptors can be found in the face and ears (hence why your nose and ears always get colder faster than the rest of your body on a chilly winter day). Temperature perception is very relative. Maybe you remember this experiment from biology classes in middle school.
