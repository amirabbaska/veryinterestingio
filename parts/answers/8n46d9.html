joesii A:

The main thing is that the air that is introduced contains small amounts of microorganisms, which will infect the contents.

In some rarer situations there's also at the the case of oxygen being introduced when previously it was vacuum sealed or filled with something generally inert like nitrogen. Theoretically (I'm not sure how common it occurs in reality with regards to sealed food; probably irrelevant compared to issues like proper cooking/pasteurization and contamination) it's possible that a microorganism in spore form could return to life now that it has oxygen to breathe (for the microorganisms that need oxygen in the first place).

In addition, introducing oxygen/air to a previously oxygen-free environment could cause other spoilage as well such as oxidation, even if there wasn't any microorganisms to worry about.
