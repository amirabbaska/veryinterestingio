tchomptchomp A:

Basically all vertebrates have both.

Our endoskeleton is made up of the cartilage-derived bones of the spine, limbs, ribs, and cranial base. The rest of our bones are intramembraneously-derived and are effectively exoskeletal. This includes the face and cranial vault as well as parts of the shoulder. Reptiles, amphibians, and fish tend to have much more exoskeleton. In sharks, the exoskeleton is secondarily reduced are replaced with an array of tiny teeth called denticles.

So yes, most vertebrates have both.
