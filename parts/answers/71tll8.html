BenificusAngorio A:

Toddlers have a lot of energy that drives them to fidget and do things involving locomotion. It's a way of making sure they use & exercise their musculoskeletal system, which requires it for nerve pathing, muscle memory, etc. Any simple stimulus is enough to trigger a desire for a movement. Fear, happiness, boredom, etc. They need to learn their bodies and grow them. Constant movement is helpful for that.

When you're older you don't have this energy. Presumably you need it to accomplish your work or tasks of the day. Dancing and singing as an adult is also disruptive and obstructive to most eating areas and activities of daily living. Flailing arms and jumping around when you're 6 ft tall is pretty hazardous. Your body is grown and your body doesn't need to develop nerve pathways or muscle memory anymore.
