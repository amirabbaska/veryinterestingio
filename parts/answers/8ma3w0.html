In short, a lack of proper breath pressure (support) and proper glottal (that is, vocal fold) closure. In this case, the folds either slam together, causing swelling, or come together incompletely, causing irritation and possible homorrhaging.

Opera singers sing unamplified over orchestras of up to 100 players without feeling pain, but if they shout without proper breath pressure and glottal closure, they experience the same pain you do.
