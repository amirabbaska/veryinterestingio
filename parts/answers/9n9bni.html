Out of that number, 340.3 W/m\^2 is reflected back by the atmosphere (greenhouse gases, clouds, etc). The total amount absorbed by the surface (163.3 + 340.3) should be approximately the same as the total amount emitted by the surface (398.2 + 18.4 + 86.4) as various forms of energy. Basically there are three layers in the graphic, the surface, the atmosphere, and space, and various arrows of the amount of energy going between them.

themykonian A:

Your graph has 3 layers: space, atmosphere and ground. So your ground balance is made up out of absorbed solar rays, emission, back radiation, thermals (wind) and evaporation (cloud forming), and interacts with the atmosphere layer. So for example solar energy creates clouds which in effect throws energy into the atmosphere (because rain will have to fall again, releasing it's heat partially in the form of infrared radiation).

You can apply your law of conservation of energy to each of these 3 layers.
