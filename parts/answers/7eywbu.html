just-gaming A:

The original scientific article about the Allan-Hills-84001 meteorite by McKay et al. 1996 is listed with >1000 citations on scopus, which means that the research is very well known. Bill Clinton's speech on the matter was broadly covered as well.

The main findings about the meteorite are:

  1. origin from mars (determined via gas composition of enclosed gas bubbles in comparison to gas analysis from the Viking missions on the mars surface)
  2. presense of carbonates (proof for flowing water at 0-80 Â°C, determined via O2 isotope analysis)
  3. presense of polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (marker for decayed biomass on earth; yet abiotic origins are known as well)
  4. 'nanofossiles' (rod-shaped, 50-200 nm; potential fossiles yet the size is very small; earth-based life would not be possible at this scale; erosion is now considered as an explanation for the 'nanofossiles')

From my understanding the scientific consensus is that these observations are not enough to proof life on mars because the observations can also be explained by abiotic processes.

The conditions for life (as we know it) were better in the past when the climate on Mars was warmer and there was flowing water on the surface. However, no conclusive evidence was found for past life on Mars. For example, no higher organic molecules have been detected by the Mars landers which carried gas chromatographs (e.g. Curiosity

The most promising lead for present life on mars is the detection of methane in the marsian atmosphere [1][2]. The methane might be of microbiological origin yet abiotic origins are possible as well (i.e. a reaction of olivins (minerals) with hot water and subsequent Fischer-Tropsch synthesis or slow release of methane from methane clathrates formed in the Marsian history). An isotopic analysis of this methane, which is only present in trace amounts (<40 ppm) in the atmosphere and constantly degraded by chemical reactions, would indicate whether microorganisms are the source. For a better perspective - the detected methane amount could be released by about 2000 earthian cows.

Mars is far away and mission are expensive. So far, the results of all missions that searched for life on Mars have been negative or inconclusive. Future missions (e.g. drilling and sample return [3][4]) could bring new observations.
