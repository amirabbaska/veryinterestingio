First of all: water

You loose water while breathing (the air you breathe out is always more humid)

You also sweat. The amount of sweat is different for each individual, but it can easily be a hundred mililiters or more per night.

last but not least. You breathe in O2 and breath out CO2. The C from the CO2 actually comes from sugars, proteins or fat that your body turns into energy to run itself. The excess C that is no longer needed in the body is removed by breathing.

super_ag A:

Veritasium did a video on this. You lose weight in water, lost through urine excretion with less intake, sweat and water lost through respiration.

Then there's metabolism that converts sugar and O2 into CO2. So you're converting solid into gas, which gets expelled through expiration. Atmospheric air consists of 78.9% nitrogen, 20.95% oxygen, 0.93% argon and 0.04% carbon dioxide. Exhaled air consists of 78.04% nitrogen, ~16% oxygen, and 4% carbon dioxide.

So the weight of ambient air is (0.789 x 14.0067 + 0.2095 x 15.9994 + 0.0092 x 39.948 + 0.0004 x 44.01) grams / 22.4 liters. This comes to 0.655 grams per liter. The average tidal volume of one breath is around 500mL, so each breath of inhaled air weighs 0.328 grams.

The weight of exhaled air (now with more CO2) is (0.7804 x 14.0067 + 0.16 x 15.9994 + 0.0092 x 39.948 + 0.04 x 44.01) grams / 22.4 liters. This comes to 0.717 grams per liter and a weight of 0.359 grams per breath.

So you lose 0.328 - 0.359 = 0.031 grams per breath due to gas exchange and metabolism. The average breathing rate is between 12 - 20, so let's say 16 RPM. You sleep on average about 8 hours a night. So you're losing 8 x 60 x 16 x 0.031 = 238.1 grams simply through the difference in inhaled vs. exhaled air. That's about 0.52 pounds. So this isn't the entirety of weight lost, but it contributes.
