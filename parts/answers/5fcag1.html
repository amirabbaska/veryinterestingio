wine-o-saur A:

Many reasons. Heat releases more volatile compounds from the cheese which you will detect in your retronasal cavity much more strongly when you eat the cheese (more ELI5 - you smell the food from inside your mouth, so you taste it more). The surface area of the cheese will also increase, causing you to pick up more flavours as well. The creamy texture of the cheese sends a stronger 'I'm eating fat' signal to your brain. Certain enzymes, sugars, and proteins will be altered by the heating process, so the flavour of the cheese is actually physically altered as with anything that gets cooked. Oil is also released from the cheese, so it coats your mouth more completely than solid cheese. Sweetness and bitterness tend to be intensified by higher temperature, and since most melting cheeses don't have a lot of bitter compounds, melted cheese tends to taste sweeter. Your brain likes this too. Depending on how hot you have the cheese, certain flavours can be muted as well, so the overall flavour of the cheese probably becomes subordinate to your physiological reaction to eating something that feels fatty and seems sweet. So it's comforting.
