In this paper, the dude describes the "floatation feeding process." All crabs have little appendages called maxillipeds in front of their mouths that they use to handle food, but the Sand Bubby evolved spoon-shaped maxillipeds that are really good at holding on to sand particles. So, they hold the sand with their mouth spoons and then shoot water out of their gill chambers across the sand and into their mouth. All the bacteria, algae, etc that were clinging to the sand get power-washed into the belly of the Sand Bubby.
