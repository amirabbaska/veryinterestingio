DanHeidel A:

So, it's not really a proper citation but Isaac Arthur did an excellent video on colonizing the sun.

TL;DW: we can probably colonize down into the upper photosphere with known materials and physics.

We can deal with heat pretty effectively but gravity starts to become a big issue. At the top of the photosphere, the gravitational attraction is about 28 times Earth standard. By using rotating orbital rings, you can negate that gravity down to 1G. By having nested rings, you can transfer heat from the lower rings up to shadowed radiators further up.
