From this pre-pub ms draft:

“Fetal behavioral states, also called sleep states, emerge during the third trimester of pregnancy and involve multiple interconnected neuronal networks. Functional (re)organization of sleep cycling likely occurs around 28-30 weeks postmenstrual age (PMA), 36 weeks PMA, and 48 weeks PMA (i.e. 2 months after birth). From 36 weeks’ gestation onward, the low-risk fetus exhibits two states of sleep and two states of wakefulness. [...] Fetuses normally pass through sleep cycles of non-REM (quiet) sleep and REM (active) sleep, which last about 70-90 minutes (Visser et al., 1992). The time spent in wakefulness is usually less than 10%.“
