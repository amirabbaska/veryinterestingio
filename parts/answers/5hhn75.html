The fact that black holes have a temperature was first discovered by Hawking. The temperature of a black hole is inversely proportional to its mass. The bigger the black hole, the lower its temperature.

But it takes a really small black hole to get a temperature you'd consider warm. If the mass of the Sun were compressed into a black hole, it would be about .0000001 degrees above absolute zero. To get to a black hole whose temperature was around room temperature on the Earth, you'd be looking at a black hole with about half a percent of the mass of the Moon (it would be about a micrometer across!)

When we talk about black holes, and especially the temperature of a black hole, we are generally talking about the event horizon, and not the singularity, as the later can't be observed, and thus in a sense don't have those kinds of traits.
