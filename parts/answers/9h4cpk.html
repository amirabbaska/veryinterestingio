Another_Penguin A:

There are several known strains of HIV. Two are closely related to SIV from particular groups of chimpanzees, and a third is related to SIV found in Gorillas. The NIH has a a summary of HIV’s origins with citations.

Chimps seems to have acquired it from other monkeys. It appears to be endemic to African monkeys.

It probably spreads to human hunters via blood-blood contact.

HIV doesn’t tolerate being out in the open air for long. It’s very unlikely that it would become “airborne”, but I’m no expert.

Just imagine all those researchers wandering around Africa collecting samples of chimpanzee poop so we can study HIV. What a job.

allthehiv A:

It is estimated that HIV crossed over to humans sometime in the early thirties, but there is no true way of confirming that. Patient zero is assumed to have been a hunter butchering a monkey.

It continued on for 50 years killing everyone that contracted it before it was first isolated in 1983. It took more than ten years before the first effective antiretroviral treatment arose.
