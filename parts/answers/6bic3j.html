Since at least the early 90s, companies have been using "slumping sales" as a reason to lay off employees in the USA (especially), but also other countries. Of course, the actual reason for this is automation, but saying you are laying off thousands of Americans to save money doesn't help your brand, so the press release blames slumping sales.

Recently, Coca Cola said they were laying off employees because people were drinking less sugary drinks, despite Coca Cola actually having increasing sales, because "sugary drinks" doesn't include their other markets like energy drinks.

Foxconn Replaces 60,000 employees with Robots Foxconn is the manufacturer of Apple iPhones and many other electronics you or a family member own.

USA Today, Automation is the Greatest Threat to the Economy

This is also a reason that things like Universal Basic Income or shortening the work week are brought up. When a very small percentage of the population is needed to manufacture the entire world's demand of goods, you can really only fix the problem a few ways:

1) Accept the problem, eliminating the middle class, and having the vast majority of the population live in poverty. Of course this means less people buying things and is a long-term terrible result for even the wealthy.

2) Decrease labor hours for everyone. If the work week was decreased to 4 days, with over-time for salary employees, many jobs would be created.

3) Universal Basic Income or other "wealth redistribution" plans.
