To paint a picture that might make more sense in your head, imagine to attractive particles close together, and then separate them. To separate them, you have to apply work (moving them against their mutual force). Because work is required for the separated configuration, it is of higher energy.

NutriaSystem A:

They (particles attracted to each other) have more energy when separated because the act of separating them requires energy - the force required to pull them apart times the distance they have traveled. The energy you apply adds to the potential energy of the two-particle system.

Binding Energy per Nucleon (BE/A) peaks at an atomic mass of 62. U-235 has a BE/A of approximately 7.6 MeV/amu. If you split that into 2 approximately equal fragments with 118 mass number, the BE/A of a 118 mass number is 8.5 Mev/amu. The binding energy is the energy required to break a nucleus up into its nucleons, where as a BE/A curve shows that you that when nuclei have an abundance of neutrons as heavy elements do, they are less stable having a lower binding energy per nucleon.
