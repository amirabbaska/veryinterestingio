It is possible to pull out IV's, however it also depends how well they are secured. The IV tube is wrapped around the hand/thumb and taped so if it gets caught, it doesn't yank it out of the vein.

The IV catheter is also usually taped at the hub and then further secured with a sticky transparent film (Tegaderm is a brand-name product for example). See this picture: https://thephotochik.files.wordpress.com/2013/03/img_4019.jpg

If they're yanked out, there is a chance for bleeding which will be controllable with simple direct pressure for a few minutes. The bigger risk would be infection and bruising from improper removal and not keeping the site clean.

When I'm removing the IV catheter from someone, I'll gently pull it from the hub, not from the tubing because there's a lot more control that way. I'll also have gauze ready to control bleeding.

For breathing tubes/intubation, you'd have a very hard time simply yanking them out as these both have inflatable bulbs on the tube which help create an airtight seal and to help stabilize the airway: Here is an example of a "King LT" advanced airway. https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Christopher_Buresh/publication/24346804/figure/fig1/AS:202640542703627@1425324678674/King-LT-reproduced-with-permission-from-King-Systems-Corporation.png

If the bulb/cuff was not deflated prior to pulling it out, it could cause very severe airway trauma.

Finally, Foley catheters (for collecting urine) are similar to the airway adjuncts in the sense they have an inflatable cuff that MUST be deflated prior to being removed. If it is not deflated and simply yanked out, it will cause severe trauma.

As far as the cardiac monitors, these are simply just stuck on with electrodes and will either come off from the clip to the electrode or the sticker will just pull off. These are no big deal, however these wires are stupid expensive and I would advise to not simply yank on them. If they are disconnected, then there may or may not be an alarm at the monitor/nurses station indicating there is a problem.
