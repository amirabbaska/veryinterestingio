Beau_Nash A:

The front centimetre or so of your eyes are lined by a membrane called the conjunctiva that folds back on itself and lines the insides of your eyelids too.

So there is a "conjunctival sac" that surrounds your eyeballs entirely and stops stuff "going behind your eye".

Blinking is an effective pump for shifting stuff from the base of the sac to the eyelid edge (see other answers for sleepy dust and gunk).
