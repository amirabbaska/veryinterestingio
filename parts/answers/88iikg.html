BlazeOrangeDeer A:

The field lines surrounding the particle get stretched and spread out around the whole horizon, before it falls all the way in. As it approaches the horizon, its electric and magnetic fields as seen from far away merge with the fields from the black hole itself.

Strangely enough, an observer falling with the charge wouldn't see any of this. The charge would look and act normal to them even as they cross the horizon, at least until they get far enough into the black hole that tidal forces rip them apart.
