Just like a black hole's gravitational field, a black hole's electromagnetic field is not something that is communicated from within the event horizon. There's nothing traveling from inside the horizon to outside to give some sort of message "this is what the field is". The field is already there.

(Also, virtual particles are not real things. It's just an unfortunately chosen misnomer for a mathematical tool.)

One way to convey information about black hole geometries is with a Penrose (or conformal) diagram. You can see the diagrams for a Schwarzschild (non-charged) and Reissner-Nordstrom (charged) black hole in the second picture on Wikipedia.

They are quite different in many ways, including singularity and event horizon structure. As far as I know, there is no clear explanation for how the Schwarzschild diagram would become the RN or Kerr diagram. Such a thing might happen in real life when a Schwarzschild black hole absorbs a radially infalling charged mass.
