The universe can be imagined as a sphere (like a balloon). The question of it's center has come up before, and that's when talking about how the universe expands. Observations showed that everything was expanding away from our exact position. In reality, it looks like that from every point in the universe. This can be imagined by thinking of the expanding universe as an inflating balloon. We exist on its surface. You can't really say where the "center" of the surface of a balloon is, every point is the same.

The short answer (at least as far as we currently know) is: No.

The slightly longer answer is: There is no "center" of the universe, because ALL points in the universe are the center. So no, there can be no 'center of mass.' Reason for this is that (..again, as far as we know..) the universe resembles the surface-area of an ever-expanding balloon. It's not a perfect analogy, but it's the closest I've found that does the subject justice.
