It gets the hottest. In summer, it has record high temperatures. In winter, even the nearby Sonoran desert will be warmer than Death Valley.

The high temperature is due to a combination of the low elevation (higher atmospheric pressure) and extremely dry conditions allowing for high insolation ("insolation" being sunlight).

Benign_rubbish A:

Night time radiative cooling is the process where heat that has been absorbed by land is radiated into the atmosphere and ultimately space.

Deserts have very low humidity so faster radiative cooling. In contrast, a swampy area with high humidity will retain much heat during the night because the photons (heat) have a harder time moving up. They physically get absorbed and re-emitted countless times basically bouncing and heating up the water vapor. This doesn't happen in dry areas so that heat easily gets drawn toward space.
