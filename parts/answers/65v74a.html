I'll do the math the other guy failed at doing.

An M16 fires a 5.7 mm diameter bullet. The weight of the round is about 4 g.

The drag coefficient for a bullet is approximately 0.295.

The equation for terminal velocity is

https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/ee680de6632767977a602cfbed943853e3854cdc

where

Vt is the terminal velocity

m is the mass of the bullet, 0.004 kg

g is the acceleration due to gravity (9.81 m/s^(2))

rho is the density of air (1 kg/m^(3))

A is the area of the bullet (pi/4*(5.7 mm)^2 = 25.5 mm^2)

Cd is the drag coefficient (0.295)

So the terminal velocity of a bullet fired straight up from an M16 is 102 m/s.

According to here, the minimum velocity of a bullet to penetrate skin is 58 m/s. This bullet well exceeds that.

Don't shoot guns in the air.

Edit: it's been suggested that I point out that the muzzle velocity is 960 m/s, so the bullet is far, far slower on the way down than if it were pointed at you. Also, a gun shooting at 102 m/s wouldn't count as a firearm under Canadian law.

ReyTheRed A:

It depends on the bullet. It will be coming down significantly slower than it goes up, especially if it is shot straight up, because if it is shot straight up, it is much more likely to tumble on its way down, drastically increasing drag. If it is shot at an angle, it is much more likely to maintain its spin and stability, so it will have a much faster terminal velocity, and therefore be much more dangerous.

With big bullets, even tumbling it can be lethal, but small bullets will likely be survivable if shot straight up.

Regardless, getting hit by a bullet that was shot straight up is never going to be better than not getting hit by a bullet it all, so don't be firing guns into the air.
