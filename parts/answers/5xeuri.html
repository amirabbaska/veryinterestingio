The realistic reason is because of how they hire. Most ethnic restaurants hire through networks they have and in most cases the owners are immigrants. In addition, they often hire family members which also happen to be homogeneous in the ethnic spectrum. It's not like most owners are actively trying to discriminate but rather their methods of hiring has a side effect of discriminating.

kouhoutek A:

Many restaurants, particularly ethnic restaurants, are family run. The people who work there are related to or are friends with the owners, and that means they are likely to be of the same ethnicity.

Apart from that, people of the same ethnicity are likely to be more qualified, as they know the cuisine better. They also might prefer to work in an environment that supports the ethnicity, and preferentially seek jobs there.

Finally, a lot of ethnic restaurants are run by immigrants, who will actively seek employees within their immigrant communities.

Legally speaking, in most cases they are not allowed to specifically discriminate on the basis of race.
