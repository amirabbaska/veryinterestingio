Pohatu_ A:

In my experience, games stay in early access because they're trying to see what works. Subnautica is a good example of this: it's an underwater survival game that has been in early access for a long time now. Its release date has been pushed back several times, and for good reason. While the game has a great concept and good gameplay/mechanics, it is plagued by many bugs, glitches and flat-out errors. Its notorious for running slowly and shakily, even on some higher-end computers. Thanks to early access, they've been able to prioritize these errors from community feedback, figuring out what to do and how to do it while keeping the fans happy. For example, they've been able to squash bugs quickly because the community finds them for them.

TL;DR- It's so devs can work on the game to make it better and eventually prepare it for release.
