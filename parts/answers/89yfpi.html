shawnwasim A:

Like others have said, its because the hot/cold air mixture distorts the image by bending transmitted light.

Fun side fact, mirages are also caused because of this phenomenon. Except that in mirages, air gets hotter and hotter as you get closer to the ground so the incoming light from a distance straight in front of you keeps bending as it gets closer to the ground until it becomes parabolic so you get the illusion of a reflection.
