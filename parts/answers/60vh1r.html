Cocaine gets absorbed by the mucus membranes in your nostrils, and crosses over directly into the blood stream. This is why cocaine acts so much faster than say, alcohol. Alcohol has to reach the small intestine before being absorbed in large amounts, which can take 15-30 minutes depending on your stomach contents.

Cocaine bypasses all of that by having a molecular structure that allows it to cross directly into the very small blood vessels (capillaries) in your nose, and then distribute into your body at a very rapid pace.

Anything that gets you "high", "drunk", etc. has to go into the blood stream at some point, where the heart pumps it throughout the body. So it goes "everywhere" including your brain.

Some things can't get to the brain though. The brain has a protective layer known as the "blood brain barrier" (BBB). Only certain things can get through. Cocaine is one that can which is why you get euphoric from it.

Immodium, an over-the-counter diarrhea medicine is actually not much different from heroin or other strong opiates. The reason it doesn't get you high is because it can't cross the BBB.
