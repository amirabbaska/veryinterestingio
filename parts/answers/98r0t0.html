The smallest fire is defined by the quenching distance. If a flame is smaller than this distance, then heat loss to the environment will be faster than heat generation by the flame. This is because heat loss it's related to the perimeter of a flat flame, and heat generation is related to the surface area. The quenching distance is usually on the order of a millimeter.

Incidentally, this is how flame arrestors work. If you're worried about a flame traveling "backwards" in a tube to the fuel/air supply, you put in a flame arrestors or flashback arrestor. These have narrow openings smaller than the quenching diameter so that a flame cannot pass through.

Edit: From some Wikipedia searching, the smallest gap is 0.25 mm for acetylene
