Satsuz A:

My understanding is that when something "tastes metallic", that what you're tasting is based on odor alone (due to retronasal olfaction, which is basically smelling something as you swallow it) and not anything your tongue is picking up. Humans don't have any taste receptors for elemental iron or carbon. So technically it's more of a "flavor" than a "taste". Metallic odors come from reactions on the surface, though I couldn't tell you precisely what reactions are happening in this case.

As for the safety: Steel is an alloy made from iron and carbon, along with a variety of trace elements depending on what purpose the steel is meant for. Iron is perfectly fine, it has nutritional use in your body. Carbon is also quite safe, and is not very reactive at all in this form. Regardless, most of your body is made of carbon so even if it was absorbed it would not be disrupting any of your chemical processes. Too much of anything is not great for you, of course, but that's probably not going to happen from a metal container.

The only possible risk is from the other trace metals used in the steel. However, since presumably you are using a container that's designed to come into contact with food/drink, the alloy would not be designed to contain any dangerous additives.
