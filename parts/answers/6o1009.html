WRSaunders A:

"Bored" is a human term, and it's associated with a set of negative behaviors. The "cure for boredom" in humans is additional interaction.

When an octopus is in a closed area, after a while they start to take on some of these same behaviors, and they stop when they have more interaction. Thus the term "bored" gets applied by the humans observing them.

In nature, they probably just go a little farther to explore an area beyond their normal area. Just like humans who decide to explore frontiers. We're applying human emotional state labels to their observed state because it makes good predictions.

RhynoD A:

Animals develop the same kind of stereotypical behaviors that humans do when they're bored. They do repetitive motions that don't accomplish anything, they get destructive of themselves and their environments. Birds, for instance, will pluck their feathers. Animals can also get abnormally aggressive, especially social animals that should normally get along well.

Octopi are no exception. They get aggressive, they look obviously agitated, etc.
