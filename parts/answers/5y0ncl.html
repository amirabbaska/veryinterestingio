DoctorKynes A:

Rehab doctor here.

In the vast majority of people with Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis, there's no appreciable damage to internal organs. The organs are well able to adapt to curvatures of the spine without serious compromise. For this patient population, the major sequelae of their scoliosis if untreated is that it may progress, lead to deformity, poor cosmesis, and back pain.

This is in contrast to people with what's called Neuromuscular Scoliosis. This occurs in patients with weakness -- such as patients with Muscular Dystrophy or SMA(Spinal Muscular Atrophy). In these populations, the scoliosis will invariable continue to progress, and if untreated can lead to organ damage -- most notably impaired lung function(due to difficulty with air movement), and neurological damage.
