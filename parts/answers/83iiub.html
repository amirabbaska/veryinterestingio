gotham77 A:

This is an interesting question.

Tea has tannins. They are released by the hot water. A little gets in your drink but most stays in the soggy tea leaves. When you squeeze the tea leaves, you release more tannins into the tea.

Extra tannins, give the tea a bitter flavor. Most tea drinkers find this unpleasant. That’s why it’s not recommended.

But it’s interesting because if you’ve done it your whole life, you must like it that way. The right way to drink it is the way you like it. So keep squeezing the tea bag if you prefer it that way.

Source: I drink an absurd amount of tea

Edit: I’ve been informed of the difference between tannins and tannic acid. Tea has tannins, not tannic acid. But yeah, it makes the tea bitter.
