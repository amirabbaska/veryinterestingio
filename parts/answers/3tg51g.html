WolfDoc A:

I have a hunch it is due to two or three main factors.

(1) Income distribution is a lot more uneven than in the 60's, 70's and 80's. Essentially fewer and fewer people take a larger and larger share of the wealth, to a degree it is threatening the existence of the US middle class as a financially secure demographic.

(2) A larger and larger part of the workforce and working time is "non-productive", i.e. produces a work output that is not useful to anyone else. Thus, the amount of goods and services produced is less, contributing to inflation, outscoring and simply less goods and services to go around. The most blatant (but far from only) example of this is the US health insurance industry. For each dollar spent towards health care through the insurance system, only a fraction goes to the useful output of actual nurses, doctors and supplies. The rest goes to feeding the paper mill of people pushing claims around and distributing costs and profits (most of which do not end up in the middle class but with stock owners -see point 1 above.)
