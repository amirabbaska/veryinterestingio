It's due to scattering caused by suspended particles in the air. There is currently a higher concentration of such particles due to dust from the Sahara blown in by Hurricane Ophelia as well as byproducts of the fires in Portugal.

Many of these particles are small enough that they mostly scatter light using a mechanism called Rayleigh scattering. This type of scattering affects light of shorter wavelengths (e.g. blue light) more strongly than longer wavelengths (red light). As a result, blue light is effectively filtered, causing the Sun to look red-orange. Conceptually the same effect explains why the sky looks red during sunset or sunrise.
