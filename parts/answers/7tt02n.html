kouhoutek A:

Because physics follows consistent rules. No matter what they are, so long as they are consistent, they can be described by math. Usually, the math comes from the physics, an empirical description of what is happening. Occasionally, the math fits the physics so well that it can be used to predict previously unobserved physical events.
