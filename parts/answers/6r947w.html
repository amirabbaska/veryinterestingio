crookedsmoker A:

They are able to distinguish their own individual 'calls', even in noisy environments. They can only use their own call for echolocation, because the bat itself needs to be the source of sound for echolocation to work.

Moreover, bats can only keep track of one call at a time. If they make a new call, they stop listening for their previous one.

Source:

Wilson W. W., Moss C. F. (2004). Sensory-motor behavior of free-flying FM bats during target capture, in Echolocation in Bats and Dolphins
