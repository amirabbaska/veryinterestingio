This is actually a much-debated question in physics, and unlike what people here are saying, it's not due to pressure or friction melting the ice. The pressure of a human being is not enough to meaningfully alter the melting point of the ice below.

Instead, I believe that the current mainstream theory is that the ice molecules in the topmost layer of the crystal lattice act like liquids. They are not locked in place from all sides, so they have some freedom of movement which gives ice its slippery characteristics.
