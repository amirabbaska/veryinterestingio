XaioPuppy A:

There's resistive and capacitive touch screens. Resistive is where you press in a bit and you can use pretty much anything to interact with it. Capacitive is what most smart devices use and it detects an electrical signature on the screen. Some screens are more sensitive to the electricity than others and can pick it up easier. The leather glove restricts the electricity from transferring.

nobbyv A:

Many touchscreens DO work with leather gloves. It all depends on what they were optimized for when they were designed. It's possible to increase the sensitivity of the touchscreen by increasing the gain of the drive that "powers" the screen. However, this comes with tradeoffs, including the possibility of false touches from extraneous noise. In some touchscreen applications (industrial markets and casino gaming, for example) the potential impact from false touches is FAR more serious than someone not being able to produce a touch while wearing gloves.
