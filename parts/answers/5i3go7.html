  1. The Greenland shark lives in cold water which must contribute to their metabolism. zykezero A:

There are a bunch of reasons why some animals live longer than other. It's not so much a rule but a guideline for all animals.

Some basic rules are: the lower on the food chain the animal is the more offspring they will have to compensate for being prey.

The higher on the food chain the less offspring because their survival is more likely.

In areas of extreme condition / little prey available the species in question likely has little offspring.

The amount of offspring animals have has a high correlation with longevity and sexual maturity. Ex: mice have more children and reach maturity quickly, elephants and whales have one child over many months every couple years and take years to reach maturity.

While we do not know the actual reason the Sharks live so long, we really do not know for sure why any living thing lives as long as they do (mostly), we do have guidelines that have proven to be very accurate as I described.

It lives in a very cold, relatively nutrient poor area, which means it's lifestyle tends towards slow growth, low activity. But it also has relatively little competition, so there's no need to replace losses rapidly. Thus, the best strategy evolution wise is to live long, reproduce rarely.

If they lived shorter lives/reproduced faster, they might over populate and exhaust their food supply.
