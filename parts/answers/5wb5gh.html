All chemical bonding is fundamentally based on quantum-mechanical phenomena. DNA can maintain its structure precisely because of energy minimization which of course is, at the most fundamental level, in your "Quantum Realm." Does quantum tunneling occur, well yes, there is always a small probability this will occur, and could conceivably cause deleterious effects if for example you altered a hydrogen bond and changed the genetic sequence. Conspiracy313 A:

Interestingly, DNA in your body is constantly being repaired. There's a general misconception that any damage to your DNA is irreversible, but usually several different things have to go wrong with the reconstructive part of the genome before real damage will occur, IE cancer. So even if certain sections did fall apart, your body has mechanisms in place to replace most missing pieces.
