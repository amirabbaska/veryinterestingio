LCD monitors are called LCDs because they are Liquid Crystal Displays. When you touch the screen, you're compacting a part of that thin layer of liquid held up on the panel. So, similar to making ripples in water, you are making ripples in the liquid crystals: the light coming from the back of the display is refracted slightly by the now not-so-uniform layer of liquid, making it look like a ripple.

See here for some more specifics: http://electronics.howstuffworks.com/lcd2.htm
