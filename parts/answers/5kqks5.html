flooey A:

Small amounts of alcohol aren't harmful to a baby, but large amounts are. The problem is that nuanced positions like that are hard to get across in a public health context, since what's small depends on physiological features of the mother, and people naturally have differing opinions of how much is a small amount of alcohol. It's a lot easier to get across the message that you shouldn't drink at all, which has the downside that women don't get to enjoy a drink now and then, but the upside of far higher compliance and many more healthy babies.

First off, in olden days beer and wine were weaker and water more dangerous. Spirits were largely unheard of through much of human experience. But there were notes about distilled spirits causing children to be born with problems.
