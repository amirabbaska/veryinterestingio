You can find this from conservation of energy. Let rg be the radius of a geosynchronous orbit and R be the radius of the Earth. Conservation of energy says:

-GM/rg = -GM/r + v^(2)/2,

where v is the speed of the object when it is at radius r. Rearranging:

dr/dt = sqrt[2GM(1/r - 1/rg)]

dt = [2GM(1/r - 1/rg)]^(-1/2) dr.

Now we just have to integrate this equation, from 0 to T on the left side, and from rg to R on the right side.

I integrated it numerically using WolframAlpha and got about 4 hours.
