MedicSteve09 A:

Paramedic here. In the unfortunate event this happens, we are trained to handle it.

Firstly, remember what most people call "an ambulance driver" is actually a trained medical professional, and in some districts, as trained as the Paramedic in the back of the ambulance.

Secondly, we are trained for the care of more than one critical patient at a time (Mass Casualties, First on scene, etc...). So we can prioritize, multi-task, while waiting on additional resources.

Thirdly, "Protocol" is what tells us how to handle situations. Some services, its strict as hell and says "If , then you have to " and some services it will say along the lines of "If , consider <this, this, this, or this 'within your scope of practice>"

Now, if I was transporting a critical patient, and we hit a pedestrian while transporting "hot", we would immediately notify dispatch and local 911 dispatch. If neither my partner, patient, or me were injured... then my partner would initiate care to the wounded pedestrian while I made sure the original patient remained stable, we would have also requested mores units to turn care over to.

10SE A:

Two additional ambulances are dispatched, one for each patient. The original ambulance and crew are taken out of service and remain on the scene of the accident pending a police investigation.

Of note... (in most US states) they are also responsible for treating both patients until they are relieved by other arriving medical personnel.
