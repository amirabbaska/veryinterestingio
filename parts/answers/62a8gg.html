Although Tyrannosaurs (and other large therapods) had small arms, fossil evidence shows that they were well-muscled and quite strong. This isn't really consistent with the idea that the arms were useless or vestigial.

To my knowledge, the two three main theories as to what T-rex's arms were used for were either A: for holding struggling prey before it was killed by the jaws, B: for helping to rise from a prone position, or C: something to do with mating.

For my money I'd say B sounds the most likely but I'm not a paleontologist.
