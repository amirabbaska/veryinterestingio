It's called presque vu and, like so many thing relating to the inner workings of the brain, we probably don't fully understand this.

Basically you're remembering things about the thing that you're trying to remember, but without actually recalling it. This might be because another memory that is very similar is "blocking" you from remember the thing fully, so you only get bits of it (known as state).

geydirabi A:

This may be due to Serial-position effect. Our brains are inclined to remember the first few and the last few items in a series the best. Also, in many languages the first syllable has more syllabic stress -new words are derived from the first syllable of an existing word giving more importance to the first letters. This is purely anecdotal but usually when friends and I experience this phenomenon, the word we're looking for will not be beginning with the remembered letter more often than not.

For me, I am better at remembering the shape of a word, or any patterns (without remembering specifically the letters). For example, if I couldn’t remember the word “yellow”, I would recall that in the middle were two tall letters, and the start would have a hanging letter.
