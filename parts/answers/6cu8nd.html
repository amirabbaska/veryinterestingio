Hi there. All paranasal sinus cavities are continuous with the outside world (via ostia) and should not hold pressure unless in a pathological state (sinus infection, tumor growth, sinusitus, etc).

Animals also have paranasal sinuses, and in some species they are very large and elaborate. This picture gives a good representation of horse sinuses, and this one describes how a bull's horns actually contain extensions of the caudal frontal sinuses within them (so, theoretically, a bug could fly into a bull's nose all the way up into its horn).
