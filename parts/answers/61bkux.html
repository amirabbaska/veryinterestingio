It is larger in size, but similar in form, to southeast Alaska. In Alaska, a mountainous area was glaciated, and every weak spot became a deep valley. Many of the weak spots are old (or still-active) fault lines making long narrow paths. In between are masses of solid rock that form each island.

You would see similarly complicated coastline if (for instance) you flooded British Columbia a few thousand feet deep.
