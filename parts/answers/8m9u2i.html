Yep. Specifically, on the nunataks, exposed rocky tips of mountains that protrude above the glaciers. These are home to a few species of charismatic microfauna like rotifers, nematodes, and (surprise surprise) tardigrades.

sciencemercenary A:

There's nothing. Penguins aren't very bright, and they'll go wandering inland sometimes. Seals too. They sometimes make it 20-50 miles before they croak.

We have seen lost skuas at the geographic South Pole. They're the only animal known to make it there on their own, and they probably die shortly after arrival. Tough birds.
