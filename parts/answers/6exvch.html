Simple experience. First, humans have long known that, at very high altitudes, we have a harder time breathing; therefore, oxygen equipment would be needed. We also know that extremely low pressure can be damaging to unprotected flesh, as a result of experiments, especially during the World War II era. Again, it was obvious that suits would be needed. Therefore, when planning for Space, which would see people exposed to extreme conditions, it was obvious that special equipment, such as suits, would be needed.

alanmagid A:

The experiments of Lavoisier in the 1700s showed that oxygen was necessary for life and human physiology studies established the lowest partial pressure animals can survive. Atmospheric science had long known that atmospheric pressure drops with ascent and at what rate. So the need for an oxygen support was recognized long before high-altitude flight was undertaken.
