The popularity of the white flag of truce or surrender arose independently during the Eastern Han Dynasty (25-220AD) and in Rome as recorded by Cornelius Tacitus in his Histories in 109AD. Specifically he mentions The Second Battle of Cremona, fought between the Vitellians and the Vespasians in A.D. 69.
