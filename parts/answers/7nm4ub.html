To echo what others are saying: Yes, although it's been colder than usual in the Eastern US, it's been even hotter in Alaska, the Western US and Siberia. Here's a map of today's temperature anomalies, which shows how much deviation from usual temperatures there's been for various places around the planet. Overall, the planet is +0.5Â° C warmer than usual.

Some more background, though: It's no coincidence that the Eastern US is cold while the Western US & Alaska are hot. This is likely part of a build-up to another North American dipole, also known as the Ridiculously Resilient Ridge - you can read more about it here.

The summary is that a warm body of water sets up off the coast of British Columbia / south of Alaska. This creates an upper level ridge of high pressure, and the jetstream comes in from the west and has to steer around the high, creating a standing wave. The result is that Alaska gets much warmer weather than usual on the jetstream's upswing, while the Eastern US gets much colder polar air from the jetstream's downswing. The fact that the jetstream misses California in the process means less moisture is coming in, which has been causing the drought there, leading to forest fires, etc.

Exactly why this standing wave has been set up in a recent years - and why it's so resilient - has been a open question that we don't fully understand yet. Maybe it's related to climate change.
