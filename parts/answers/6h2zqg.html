somedave A:

Anti hydrogen is formed experimentally by combining trapped anti protons with positrons from a radioactive source which are slowed to a reasonable level.

The problem with this is it produced anti atoms in highly excited rydberg states which makes them hard to confine. Hence we only end up with <100 anti atoms. At this density the probability of a three body collision (required to form a molecule generally because of energy and momentum conservation) is very low so almost no molecules form. Edit: It seems we are up to 300 anti-atoms!
