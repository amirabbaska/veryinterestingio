Check out photosensitive ganglion cells located in the eye which affect circadian rhythm which would come into play with SAD and bipolar disorder.

I have sad light glasses which shine light onto my eyes to stimulate these cells to trigger my brain to produce serotonin in winter when it's darker.

If those uv blocking contact lenses also blocked other wavelengths of light from stimulating those cells then you have found the source of your problem.

The only UV-blocking contact lens that is FDA approved to wear continuously in the US is Acuvue Oasys, but should be replaced once a week.

You may have dry or irritated eyes from overwear. Irritation may make your eyes feel tired and removing them may make you feel more comfortable and in turn 'energetic'
