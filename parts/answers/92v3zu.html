ghoulbug A:

Most species reach a point where if they got any bigger it would actually be a bad thing - they wouldn’t be able to eat enough food to metabolize and stay alive as long as possible, so it’s actually possible to become too big in terms of evolution standards. Animals evolve to the sweet spot size and then stay there as long as their environment allows - sometimes they (in general) get smaller when smaller is better, and sometimes they get bigger when there’s more resources and bigger prey.

Dinosaurs, insects, and mammals all used to be much bigger when our world was different pre-ice age (and even way further back) when there was more oxygen in the air and more prey readily available to them. Since evolution now favors smaller animals, we see modern species are much smaller than ancient species. (Sometimes. Usually. It all depends on the species and their environment.)
