DrSarno A:

Doctor here. You may not hear about them, but they sure do exist. I saw two in one week on my stroke service, a rare one called papillary fibroelastoma where the tumor sticks to the valve of your heart and when your heart pumps, it just flicks off little pieces of itself that go to your brain and block blood flow to a specific area causing a stroke.

The most common heart tumor is called an atrial myxoma, which grows in one of the chambers of your heart like a little ball on a string. If it gets big enough, it can disrupt blood flow from exiting that chamber and you die.

So really, they do exist.

amirs318 A:

Cardiologist here. Cardiac tumors ("heart cancers") do exist, but they're extremely rare. When they do occur, the majority (~75%) are benign; they are typically found incidentally during lab tests/imaging looking for other things, and they often don't cause symptoms. The remainder are malignant which, loosely speaking, means dangerous.

Those above are all considered "primary" tumors, meaning tumors/cancers that arise from the heart itself. Again, those are very rare. Much more commonly found in and around the heart are "metastases", which are cancers that arise elsewhere and travel to the heart.
