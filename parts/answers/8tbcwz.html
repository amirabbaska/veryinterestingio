MissBirdNerd A:

It depends on the species, as different birds build their nests in different places which could be easier or harder.

Some birds nest inside things, like an old tree, that hole in your siding, ect. These are called cavity nesters! They just have to find a cavity, did it out a bit, and start stuffing it with whatever nesting materials they want, typically small sticks or grasses, fur, other soft materials.

Some birds build what is called a cup nest, this is probably what you think of when you think of a bird nest in a tree or bush, and is often primarily made of grass. Birds will often gather spiderwebs to help stick the grasses together, helping prevent things from just blowing away.

Still other birds use mud to make nests along cliffs, or the side of buildings. I don't know a lot about this kind of nest building, but they often are built with a combination of mud and sticks or grass.

Other birds nest right on the ground! Some take the time to build a nest of grasses or sticks, while others literally just scratch out a small depression and lay their eggs in that!

TLDR: birds are crazy talented and build all kinds of different nests. Spiderwebs are like the duct tape of the forest for building nests.
