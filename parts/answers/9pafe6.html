Terr_ A:

how are we able to clearly visualize images

Wait, how do you know that's what's really happening in the first place? Human brains are excellent at lying to ourselves.

For example, suppose I ask you to "fully visualize" a pirate. Done?

Okay, was the pirate's right index finger regular, scarred, with a ring, or missing? Was his palm towards you or away from you?

I think you'll realize you probably didn't "visualize" (like a camera) those "in plain sight" details of the pirate at all until something triggered your attention towards the gap. At best it was filled in with an idea about a hand (or hook.)

We don't create pictures in our heads, we choose impressions, emotions, requirements, and stories. When you are drawing a real picture (or being asked questions) you use those to invent details as needed and to cross-check shapes you re-notice.

Most of the time it's all automatic, and the work of choosing fresh details feels like remembering old details we "saw earlier." This is appropriate, because experiments prove a huge portion of what humans "remember" is actually just our brain making shit up too.

TLDR: You probably don't, you just think you do, because humans are crazy meatbags.
