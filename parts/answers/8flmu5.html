One minor correction in your thinking: it's not that pollen and other foreign objects are antigens. It's that their antigens do not match your own antigens.

An antigen is any molecule that is capable of causing an immune response. Your own cells are covered in antigens. In fact, your cells are deliberately covered in unique antigens, coded in your DNA so that your cells produce antigen proteins that do any number of things to signal to your immune system. For instance, there are some are like dead-man switches, telling your immune system that they aren't cancerous. If the cell's DNA gets damaged in a way that could cause it to become cancerous, hopefully the sequence creating that antigen will also be damaged, so it won't make that antigen anymore. Your white blood cells will then attack that cell and kill it before it can cause cancer.

Your immune system is accustomed to seeing the antigens in your body. Anything that doesn't match what should be in your body is attacked as being foreign. Normally for things like pollen, your body simply cleans them up and removes them, no big deal. Allergies happen when your body freaks out and declares total war on the invader, ramping up parts of your immune system that are dangerous to yourself. It's like if some Canadian tourists lost their passports, and instead of putting them in jail the government sent in the national guard and starting attacking the tourists with tanks.

IIRC - and not being a medical professional - that's one of the reasons titanium is used for medical implants. It forms a protective oxidized layer around the metal that doesn't allow any other chemical reactions to the titanium. Because it isn't reactive, the chemical sensors used by your immune system can't be activated, so your body can't recognize it as being foreign. As a result, your immune system doesn't really attack it.
