rurudotorg A:

There are a sensoric (ultra-short), a short-term and a long term memory. The problem with the pizza within the oven is a problem of the short term memory. A "chunk" is lost, your pizza burns within the oven. Your brain thought, the information wasn't important enough to "store" it within long term memory.

Explaination

1st you have the sensoric memory - it is ultra fast and ultra short (e. g. image recognition in apperception, etc.) 2nd there is the short term memory that is limited to "chunks" - limited logical chains and - if it is really important (according to your brain) it's 3rd stored within the long term memory by creating new neuronal (dendritical) connections within your brain.

You can read a lot about it here: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Memory https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apperception https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chunking_%28psychology%29 etc.
