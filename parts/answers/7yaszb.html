bazmonkey A:

In technical/mathematical writing, we wouldn’t usually even use the separators. Long strings of numbers would have a space in between every three digits instead, if anything, and we also generally try to avoid needing to write a number like that unless necessary.

In other contexts, needing more than 2-3 digits after the decimal point is rare altogether. So, a convention for separating them never really formed.

lurk_n_throw A:

It's a form of subitising, which allows the reader to glance at the size of the number easily. In most contexts it is not meaningful to subitise decimals as each successive digit is less and less meaningful, however sometimes spaces are used when the display of decimals is useful such as when using scientific notation.

Interestingly it's not restricted to just the visual representations of numbers, we also break apart large numbers in speech, and there are even perception benefits by tactile groupings.
