The Yellowstone super volcano is the world's largest caldera. The last eruption tossed 1,000 cubic kilometers of debris into the air.

Over its life time, the Kilauea volcano has tossed less than 100 cubic meters of debris into the air in its largest of explosions.

The Yellowstone volcano is put in the highest category of volcanoes, ranked by their explosive potential. Only 40 such explosions are known to have occurred on Earth in the past 132 million years.

Explosions like the most intense of those from the Kilauea volcano happen on average every 18 months.

ianindy A:

Volcanoes have an explosivity rating called the Volcanoes Explosivity Index or VEI. It ranks volcanoes on a scale of 0-8 for explosive power. Kilauea is a 0 on the index and Yellowstone is an 8. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volcanic_Explosivity_Index
