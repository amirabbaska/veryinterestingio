TheLakeAndTheGlass A:

I would say that we're NOT very focused on it overall, given NASA's relative budget. But a few reasons:

  • What if the Earth is destroyed or rendered uninhabitable?

  • Learning for the sake of learning! Don't you want to know if life existed outside earth? Don't you want to see what it's like to step onto another goddamn planet? Not everyone feels this way but I think that human curiosity should be spoken to.

  • We could achieve other goals in the process, even in the short term. Many, many everyday technologies were developed in the process of working toward space exploration, and a task as daunting as Mars colonization would demand quite a few man-made miracles.
