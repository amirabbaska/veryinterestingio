BigDickMcThickCock A:

The Bernoulli principle describes the inverse relationship between pressure and flow speed of a fluid. As air flows past the open window, a low-pressure zone is created outside the window which causes the air inside the car to try to flow out until equilibrium is reached. Because the airflow is not maintaining a constant pressure, however, the point of equilibrium is constantly changing causing the air pressure to oscillate as it chases where it should be. By opening another window, it's no longer a closed system and any vacuum left by air exiting one window is immediately filled by air from the other. You can still detect the oscillating air pressure by the way open car windows sound, but the fluctuations are far smaller.
