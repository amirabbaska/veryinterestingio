Additionally to the answer you already have:

A game might have more than one way that you can hack in it. The way a developer can detect whether a player has hacked or not depends on the developer's ability to monitor the player. Let's say the developer have just found out a new way their game can be hacked - they develop a new method to catch the hackers, and then implement the method in the next patch. Once people start playing, they are found out, and are banned. A new 'wave' of bannings happened.

Not letting the hackers know you're on to them is one reason but another is just resources

A developer may not have a patch for their auto banning system yet (if they even have an auto ban system) and want to ban the cheaters. So the process goes like this:

Devs detect a cheat, comb logs to see who cheated, give list to people with access to the banning tool. Sometimes, due to server strain, the banning tool only runs at certain times, like off-peak hours
