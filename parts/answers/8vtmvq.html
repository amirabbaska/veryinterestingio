cantab314 A:

I found another article discussing some of the challenges. In particular making sure the iceberg doesn't break apart could be difficult. If a chunk of it snaps off then besides losing some of your water, the loose berg becomes a hazard for shipping. That said smaller icebergs have been towed for other purposes.

https://www.theguardian.com/environment/2017/may/05/could-towing-icebergs-to-hot-places-solve-the-worlds-water-shortage

The other question is whether it's the most economic method. Liquid water can be transported by flexible barge or by tanker ship. Desalination is well established. It's not clear iceberg towing would be cheaper. On the other hand there seems to be an element of "fairground attraction" to the UAE's plans in which case cost is less of a concern.

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flexible_barge
