Probably. The maximal mass of neutron stars is poorly known. The minimal mass is somewhere around 1.1 solar masses, although white dwarfs can exist up to 1.4 solar masses. The upper limit is somewhere around 2-3 solar masses. Wikipedia discusses this in detail with many references.

1.1 + 1.1 minus a bit of gravitational waves would generate an object with just a bit over 2 solar masses, probably low enough for a neutron star. 1.4 + 1.4 minus gravitational waves is more difficult already, as it is further away from the most massive observed neutron star and much closer to the theoretical limit.
