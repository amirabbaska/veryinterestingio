madboater A:

It would be easier to consider what is meant by expires? Typically it's when something doesn't do what it is supposed to do. If that doesn't happen then it will not 'expire'. So if your poison is intended to make someone ill or dead, no matter how, your poison will only expire if it stops doing that. In reality very few people create a poison without wanting other constraints, such as "humanely kill" in which case it could be the humanly bit the stops working and the poison gets more deadly.
