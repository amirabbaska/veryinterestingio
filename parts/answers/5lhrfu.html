The regulations forbid embedding non-edible things inside food. While banning bits of plastic in chocolate bars is a good idea in general, it also catches kinder eggs. The legal versions have a ridge on the plastic capsule which sticks out of the chocolate, like this. Legally, it's now two pieces of chocolate on the sides of a piece of plastic, not a piece of plastic embedded in a piece of chocolate.

PerlenketteFurDich A:

The knockoffs circumvent the rule against a non-food prize entirely enclosed in chocolate. One version featured two chocolate halves surrounding a plastic core. The seam where the halves met was the plastic outer rim of the inner shell.

Just today I saw that Kinder has finally solved the problem--sort of. They're now offering the Kinder "Fun" egg, which is two half-egg plastic pods. One side holds the toy prize, and the other holds Kinder chocolate paste and two crunchy candy balls. There's a small scoop between the halves you can use to eat the chocolate paste.

Edit: seems I remembered the name wrong. Yes, it's "Joy" not "Fun."
