There are mutations, called sex ratio distorters, that cause an organism (and all of its descendants) to only have children of one particular gender. If I remember correctly there are a few species breeding populations of mice that managed to get themselves extinct that way (Edit: this might not be accurate, see below).

I don't know if this is possible in humans though. And even if it is, it seems more likely that your family just happened to get 14 boys in a row. 1 in 16348 sounds like a lot, but it really isn't that unlikely in the grand scheme of things (how many families are there on the planet?).

tadrinth A:

Sort of: androgen insensitivity syndrome for example can cause those affected to appear female, regardless of XX or XY. This would throw off the boy/girl ratio for those families. However, they don't properly develop female internal reproductive organs, so they don't have periods and can't have children.

Given that the odds are only 1/16400 and there are probably more families than that in the population, it's not unusual for there to be families out there that are as skewed as yours.
