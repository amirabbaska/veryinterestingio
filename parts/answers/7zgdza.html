broloelcuando A:

Former state food and drug auditor. In the US there are almost no regulations against dietary supplements. As long as they don't make any direct claims to treat or prevent disease they're ok to sell. Normally there's a disclaimer that the item is not meant to diagnose, treat, cure or prevent any disease. Homeopathic remedies are sold in this way.
