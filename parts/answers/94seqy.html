The speed at which the cells will move will significantly depend on a range of factors, such as the local environment. But to give you a sense of the speed, there was one study where researchers used 3D microscopy to track neutrophils moving through a forest of collagen fibers. They found a speed of ~10um/minute. This is about the same size as the diameter of the cell, meaning that they travel their own size about once per minute.
