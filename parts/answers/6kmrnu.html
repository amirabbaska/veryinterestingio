ArchmageIlmryn A:

Since Ethanol(the type of alcohol that we drink) itself is a liquid, any solid food or powder containing it needs to absorb or contain it somehow, which limits what you can do with alcoholic consumables. You can for instance, as another commenter mentioned make cakes or similar foods containing alcohol, but such foods will either be sort of liquid(such as a pudding or sauce) and/or have fairly low alcohol content(as most alcohol will evaporate when say, baking a cake).

It's also notable that alcohol is consumed in far greater doses than most other drugs, which is why ethanol being liquid matters. If you compare marijuana for example, the THC(the active substance in marijuana, i.e. the stuff that actually makes you high) is 5-10% of the mass of a joint, which are usually only a few grams to begin with, whereas a beer with a similar % alcohol content is 330 ml, and if you actually want to feel drunk you'd probably drink quite a few of those.

TL;DR: Alcohol is consumed as a liquid because it is liquid, and unlike other drugs you consume a lot of it.

why there aren't, for example, alcoholic pills

Most drugs that you'd find in pills are potent enough that you only need 10s or 100s of milligrams of the drug to see an effect. This can be stuffed into a pill easily.

Let's say it takes you 3 beers to get drunk. Using a standard can (12oz = 355ml) and assuming a strength of 5% ABV, that's 50ml of pure alcohol - a whole shotglass worth. You'd either have a lot of pills or a very large pill.

...this ignores the fact that pure alcohol is not gentle on your system and having all the extra water in your beverages helps prevent damage. It also ignores the fact that anything with 100% alcohol is dangerously flamable.

BTW, if you ever start wondering "why can't we just have some pills for food", the same logic applies. Fat is the densest form of calories available. Butter is pretty much straight fat and a stick of butter only has 800 calories. These "food pills" would amount to eating 2+ sticks of butter per day.
