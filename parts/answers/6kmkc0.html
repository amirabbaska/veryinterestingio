palcatraz A:

Back in those days, the world was far less connected, which made it a lot easier for serial killers to strike and continue killing without people connecting their crimes. DNA was not available yet. We had fingerprints, but there were no connected databases.

In fact, without computers, it was a lot harder for law enforcement to share information between different districts, so it was harder to figure out which crimes were connected to each other. Nowadays, you collect fingerprints at a crime scene, you upload them IAFIS and within half an hour, you know if those fingerprints have ever been entered by any other law enforcement agency in the country either related to an unsolved crime or to a known perpetrator. That sort of thing just... didn't exist at all back in those days.

Well, first of all There ARE serial killers around America nowadays but the media tends to be hush about them. The most recent I can think of is LISK/Gilgo Beach killer (Long Island Serial Killer) who operates in the Long Island/New York area, there hasn't been anything discovered in a few years but police found 10+ bodies in the same dumping grounds killed with similar MO's, he targeted prostitutes in the area.

The other reason is that the world has simply changed. With the internet and telecommunications, gps tracking and forensics and vast databases of criminals it's simply harder to get away with it for long periods of time, which is why we are seeing this turn from "serial killing" to mass shootings, people that may have had their homicidal urges satiated by killing a single person and getting away with in the past now realize the chances of them getting away with even a single murder aren't great, rather than rotting away in prison they commit a mass shooting, lots of serial killers in the past were often obsessed with their own public media image and nowadays a single mass shooting will take up 24 hour news stations for days, making the shooter forever infamous, while a killing of a few prositutes or taxi drivers over the course of months most likely won't even make local news.

There has also been a shift in culture and thinking where "serial killer" has become a bit of an older vague term, is a gang member or drug dealer who kills rivals a serial killer? How about a mujahideen who slaughters villagers and millitary personnel? Or a rapist or burglar who kills victims so they can't speak to the police? Cartel hitmen? People with mental disorders? Paranoid schizophrenics who snap? Religious terrorists? The word "serial killer" has become sort of an outdated term after the whole 60's-80's "serial killer" fad where every news organization wanted their own villian, and even a lot of serial killers of the past would probably lumped into rapists or terrorists nowadays.
