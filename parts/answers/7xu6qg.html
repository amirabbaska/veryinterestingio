LostVegas A:

For a small amount, every molecule would continue to move unimpeded along whatever trajectory it has when you release it, so it would dissipate in all directions. Each molecule would be subject to the gravitational effect of whatever celestial mass it encounters. If you release a planet-full amount of it, then its total mass would cause it to exert enough gravitational pull to stay together as a blob. The center of the blob may register enough pressure to liquefy. You may be able to breathe if you happen to be at the position where pressure is close to Earth's atmospheric pressure, if you're lucky enough that this position coincides with the center of the blob. If it is on the outskirts instead then it means that the blob's mass is enough that you will continue to fall towards the center, with progressive increase in pressure, which would likely kill you, especially if the center is liquid oxygen.
