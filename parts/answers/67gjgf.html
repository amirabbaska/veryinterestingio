E = mc^(2) does not represent your total energy. It's the total energy of a free particle (or system of particles) in a reference frame where the total momentum of the system is zero.

If you're at rest in a Newtonian gravitational potential well V, your total energy is E = mc^(2) + V.

Changing V does not change your mass at all. However V is not a property of you, it's a property of the entire Earth-you system. And changing your location relative to the source of the potential changes the potential energy of the system as a whole.

If you were to write down the mass of the entire system, that's simply equivalent to the total energy in a reference frame where the total momentum is zero. So the total mass of the system is given by

Mtot^(2) = (Mplanet + myou + V)^(2), (c = 1).

The potential energy of interaction between you and the planet contributes to the mass of the system as a whole. Although if you just consider the system to be you, and the planet to be external, then V is treated like an external potential, and it doesn't affect your mass, but you're not a free particle in this system.

craazyy1 A:

E_0 = mc^2 represents your rest energy, the energy stored in rest mass (the mass you have when completely still). It does NOT include any kinetic energy.

The full formula for massed and massless particles, with motion, would be:

E^2 = ( mc^2 )^2 + ( pc )^2, where p is your momentum.

The formula for momentum is p = mv for objects with mass, or more accurately p = γmv. γ here is the lorentz factor: 1/sqrt(1- v^2 / c^2 ), which only becomes relevant at very high speeds. There's also p = h / λ, where h is the planck constant and λ is wavelength, which also counts for massless particles.

Here's also another formula for massed particles that our physics curriculum preferred:

E = γmc^2

If we're not dealing with relativistic speeds, you could probably use the classical formula for kinetic energy instead, and get:

E = mc^2 + 0.5mv^2

Which seems to be giving me approx. equal answers until we get to v>0.4c, which is when it's about 1% off. v = 0.68c is when it gets to be 10% off, and v=0.94c is when it gets 50% off target.

Ninja edit:

TL;DR the total energy is the rest energy, E_0, plus the kinetic energy, E_k

The system which is gaining potential energy isn't merely you, but you and the Earth. Assuming the elevator is using terrestrial power it's just potential energy changing from the form of chemical or nuclear energy to gravatational potential energy so the energy and mass of the system remain constant.

If, on the other hand, you were made of rubber and stretched out so as to gain potential energy contained entirely in your body, your mass would actually increase as a result.
