MightyLemur A:

It comes down to evolution.

It's not hard to understand that being eternally filth-ridden will harm your chances at living a healthy and full life. Naturally species that clean themselves, or more specifically genes that made their host animal clean themselves (a gene that makes you feel good when you are clean), were going to be more successful in living, breeding and thus making it through the process of natural selection.

It became a very successful, practically unanimous primal behaviour in animals because cleanliness is such a factor for survival that any gene promoting cleanliness will have a major impact on that animal's survival.

So here we are, with a primal comfort and reward from cleaning, because it drives animals to keep themselves healthy even if they don't realise it. Further years of evolution bound the act of grooming into a social interaction, but at its core it comes down to the fact that a clean animal is less likely to die, so we evolved to deeply appreciate and yearn for cleanliness.
