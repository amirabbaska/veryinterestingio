restricteddata A:

There are many ways to take photographs of a nuclear explosion without damaging the camera.

You can, at a minimum, use a zoom lens, with your camera at an appropriate distance. The "manned" cameras involved this approach, of course.

Should you need a camera closer, you can put them into concrete bunkers. At the Trinity test, they had cameras in concrete bunkers, pointing up, with mirrors on top. So even if the mirrors/glass got messed up, the blast wave would be moving at an angle perpendicular to the well-shielded camera. Mirrors; overall setup (the cameras were set up to be pulled out on a "sled" by a tank after the fact); a photo shelter 10,000 yards North of the Trinity test.
