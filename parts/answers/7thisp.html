ProprioceptiveOnager A:

4G is the name given to a group of technical standards which are able of operating at a specific speed. At the time that the naming was developed, this was 1 Gbps (1000 Mbps) at the cell tower. Signals designed to achieve that speed could be called 4G. The main example is LTE-Advacned (LTEA).

In practice, what has happened is that a lot of cell networks are using 4G as branding or as a buzzword.

The first 3G technology has gone through a lot of revisions, gaining performance as it has been upgraded. It started with UMTS, then got HSDPA data, which was upgraded to HSPA, and then HSPA+.

However, LTE was a big step forward compared to these technologies, so a lot of networks and phone manufacturers started branding it 4G, even though technically it is not a 4G technology.

These days, some carriers are even branding HSPA+ as 4G, even though it is a big step back from LTE.
