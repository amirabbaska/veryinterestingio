First you must understand the incidence of mutations is about 3% in children of unrelated parents. With inbreeding, of full siblings, the incidence increases to 6%. So it is not actually a huge change. Second, the chance of donor siblings meeting is very low. If you have one unknown half-sibling, which is not unusual in the general population, you have a 1 in 300 million chance to meet, as there are about 300 million people in the USA. Let us suppose you have 100 half siblings. So now you have a 1 in 3 million chance to meet. A 1 in 3 million chance that your chance to have a child with mutations will go from 3% to 6%. Finally, incest is full siblings having a child. Sperm donation isn't going to result in full siblings, it results in half siblings. So now you have a 1 in 2 chance of a 1 in 3 million chance your incidence of mutation goes from 3% to 4.5%??? It's just not worth worrying about.
