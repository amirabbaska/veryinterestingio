Something that's worth understanding is people will always find ways to be miserable. Imagine if you were really rich but had no friends and felt empty in life. Then you lost everything and you're living on the streets. You will look back on your days of being rich and think "how was I so miserable, I'd give up a limb to have those problems again". You never really have perspective on things until they're gone.

It's also easy to look back on the past because typically the problems you've faced are ones you've dealt with. Like not being able to talk to members of the opposite sex in highschool, or not being able to manage your time well in college. It's easy to forget how hard it was to learn to do the things you're good at now.

I was actually just looking through pictures of me and my girlfriend on her old camera from 2009. At the time I felt so isolated and depressed, and constantly had thoughts about how much I hated myself, and yet looking back at those moments I noticed that I looked really good, and was doing interesting things.

Sometimes with hindsight we can gain perspective on an event. Maybe you cried all day at a fair because you dropped your ice-cream cone as a kid, but looking back you realize that the day was also very magical and fun. Maybe you had acne in High School and obsessed about it at the time, but looking back you realize that you were surrounded by friends and were learning new things every day.

There are things that I look back on and am glad that they are not happening now: like the time I had a psychotic episode. It was stressful and painful and not very fun. Nostalgia can only go so far, but at least getting older and wiser helps with putting negative events into context, and can also make minor annoyances not seem as bad.

Overall I would say that memories seem better when you can appreciate the good things about an event, particularly because people can often get swayed by little negative things that won't matter in a year, or even a month.

IkonikK A:

Valuable moments are like oxygen. When you have a sufficient supply of them around, you don't even think about a desire for them.

When you have a shortage of emotional moments, then you really cherish the emotional moments you have had before.

So it's supply-and-demand equation, just like oxygen or water, which you never think about when you have it presently, but yearn for when you lack it.
