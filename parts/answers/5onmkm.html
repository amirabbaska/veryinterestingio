pyr666 A:

everything in the world is squishy. you apply force, and things bend or stretch. not much, for hard things. the amount you bend a block of concrete by standing on it would take specialized instruments to measure.

a sphere and a plane meet at a single point. because of this, even a tiny ball exerts enormous amounts of pressure on the table it's sitting on. as a result, both the ball and the table deform. just a little bit, and not permanently. this bending and unbending of both the ball and the table causes noise even with the best materials.

in practice, of course, balls aren't perfectly round and tables are not perfectly flat. the ball will click and clatter as it's flat parts hit the table all at once. the table will creak and groan as the divots and wood grain strike the ball. etc.
