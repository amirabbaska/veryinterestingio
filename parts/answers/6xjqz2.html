whythecynic A:

Explosives meant for use on humans kill in 2 main ways- the blast wave, which typically kills only within a very short range, and shrapnel, which maims out to a much larger distance.

In numbers: it takes about 45 psi of pressure in the blast wave to kill a person. For 1kg of TNT, the pressure wave at 1m is 100 psi, but rapidly peters out at 5m to 3 psi.

So by hugging a suicide bomber and immobilizing them, you both cushion the blast wave and hold them in place, letting others get to a safe distance. Even 1 meter makes a huge difference.

Shrapnel maims out to a much larger distance because it is essentially jagged metal bits flying through the air, and they lose energy by air resistance and when hitting something.

By hugging a suicide bomber, your body absorbs most of the shrapnel in a particular direction, protecting anyone behind it.

All this still depends on the relative power of the bomb. There are man-portable nuclear devices out there, and no amount of human sacrifice will stop a 10-20 ton TNT equivalent detonation.

tbfromny A:

The bombs that suicide bombers use can have shrapnel (like nails), to maximize the casualties - it's not just the shockwaves, it's the high speed bits of metal that gets you.

If one person gets in close, they absorb that shrapnel that would otherwise have gone out in an ever-increasing radius of destruction. They get super-dead, but the benefit is the lives of others.

If you look through Medal of Honor winners, you'll find soldiers that have thrown themselves on grenades, sacrificing themselves for the good of others. Same basic idea.
