GTFErinyes A:

They don't switch just because a new president arrives.

Air Force One is piloted by Air Force pilots (typically O-6, O-5 or O-4 - very senior guys - it's a high profile gig) and each tour of duty (like the rest in the military) typically lasts 3-5 years, so they will naturally rotate out over time during a presidency and overlap with multiple Presidents depending on timing in their career.

edit:

Here's a great National Geographic Documentary on this and what goes into planning each mission: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yKRQyDsKODE

edit2: /u/since_ever_since mentioned that only full bird (O-6) colonel's could be Aircraft Commander of AF1. The other pilots/navigators could be lower ranks. It is a prestigious gig, so pilots are typically selected for it as a good deal tour of duty
