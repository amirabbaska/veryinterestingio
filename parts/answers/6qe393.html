The cells used by the body to detect blue light in order to regulate the production of melatonin are separate from the cones that we use for our vision. These light detecting cells are called intrinsically photosensitive retinal ganglion cells and in some cases these cells can regulate circadian rhythms even when a person is completely blind... so being color blind wouldn't prevent blue light from affecting sleep patterns.
