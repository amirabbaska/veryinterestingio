So first lets start off with what happens for a conventional explosive. Ill choose gun powder since its simple and people come into contact with it on a regular basis. It is composed of a mixture of carbon sulfur potasium nitrogen and oxygen all of which is in a solid form. When lit it rapidly decomposes from its former structure into most notably carbon dioxide, molecular Nitrogen and lots of heat. These are gases thay are very hot and would create a bubble underwater.

For a nuclear bomb you start with uranium 235, a neutron comes along and attaches to u235 and makes u236 which is very unstable. u236 decays into several things differnt elements including Xenon gas plus Strontium and 2 neutrons. As Xenon is a gas it will form a bubble. How stable this bubble is im not sure, but since both gas and heat are produced a bubble will exist.
