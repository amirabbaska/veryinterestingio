SHU (Scoville Heat Units) can be determined by organoleptic (taste testing) or by means using a HPLC,

HPLC uses a sample that has been extracted in methanol and ran against known standards, and can be accurate to 1 shu, from 100 - <1,000,000 - however it is expensive.

Organoleptic also uses extracted capsaicin in ethanol, then serial diluted so a technician can taste test each sample until they "feel the heat" in a sample. - it is cheaper but a lot less accurate but can be scaled to over 15,000,000 SHU. The technician must not smoke, have a cold, or eaten any spice for 24 hours.

Source, I'm a technician working in this field, AMA.
