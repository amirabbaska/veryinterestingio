Wertsir A:

In nature edible food is always hard to find. You're foraging for plants or hunting animals, either of which requires time and effort before a pay off.

But water? water has always been plentiful in the areas that host species like humans. Be it a river or a lake or whatever. and while there might be some danger associated with it, it is mostly safe and predictable.

You need water => Walk to a river and drink.

You need food => Either look around for the 1/1000 plants that you can eat easily, or you go find an animal and chase it down for a few hours before it collapses, then you eat.

The time delay between starting to look for food and acquiring food is much larger normally. So hunger starts out a much stronger feeling.

But, it does not end that way. Thirst builds, and builds and builds, until it is overwhelming. Just as bad as any hunger pain.

So, thirst isn't a weaker or less distinct sensation, it just takes longer to build up to the same level hunger starts at, because water is normally easier to acquire.
