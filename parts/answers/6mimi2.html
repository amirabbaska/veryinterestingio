Infants are naturally curious. They often make faces that adults perceive as disgust when trying new foods, but it can just be a reaction to something they've never had before. It's new! There is a repeated exposures principle that suggests an infant or child should be exposed to a food 5-20 times before concluding that they do not like it. As kids get older they often become neophobic (afraid of new foods) and parents often pressure the kids to eat. Someone else mentioned that toddlers are at the stage where they are learning to exercise free will and will often rebel against eating the food the parent is pressuring them to eat. Often times parents will get exasperated and default to kid friendly foods like Mac and cheese, chicken nuggets, and French fries.

If you'd really like to know more about child and infant feeding check out the Ellyn Satter Institute especially the Division of Responsibilities in Feeding

Edit: someone also mentioned that kids don't like bitter foods. This can be true and some people actually have a genetic variant that amplifies the taste of bitter foods making it more challenging for them to accept vegetables. I still think acceptance greatly depends how the vegetable is prepared. Do you have the gene that makes you hate broccoli

Raebragus A:

The way I understand it, small children are biologically designed to not like bitter foods because bitter=poisonous. So it's quite natural for them to have to learn to like some things. Most of us outgrow that as we get older.

Sometimes it is just a power struggle, plain and simple. The little one figures out that if they refuse what they are offered, they will get what they want. I've even heard of kiddos being so hungry that they would cry all night from refusing to eat what was offered. That is too much for most parents when the solution is simply giving in.

I was a pretty picky eater when I was young. I just found something I liked and that's what I always wanted. It wasn't the same thing all day every day, but the lunch I carried to school was the very same thing 5 days a week, for 5 years. I don't remember not wanting other things, and my mom offered to make me other stuff, but I had my favorite and it was simple. I was in my late 20s before I started to try "new" things. My sister was shocked the first time I mentioned having Thai food. I hadn't even tried rice until I was nearly 30!
