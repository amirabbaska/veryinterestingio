  • justagadfly

    I like the latter half of your answer but disagree with the philosophical assumptions and implications of the first half.

    At one point you say "there is no category of things to be repeated", and then later you say:

    To the extent that we are right that one thing is like another thing, abstraction and counting save us a lot of brainpower.

    I don't believe numbers would be a useful tool unless we were right about that, and "to the extent" that we are right is all that matters. It always comes back to the "extent" that there is an objective reality that we perceive. Let's suppose the "extent" is very small indeed, limited within the boundaries of our subjective perceptions. Would it change anything? Would it matter? The extent is all that matters. There is number, because there are categories of things to be repeated.
