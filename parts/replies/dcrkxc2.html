    Ok so please correct me if I'm wrong. I'm a little confused. So, the shittiness we're feeling isn't directly from the flu but rather the body fighting the flu off? If our bodies didn't do these things to fight it off would we even notice the flu? Or would it kill us?

    • Levangeline

      Yes, it's mostly a result of our body reacting to the flu infecting our cells. It's not perfect by any means, it's just the body's best way of dealing with a virus it hasn't encountered before. When a foreign infection (bacterial or viral) enters your body, it's first response is a very general "purge and destroy" reaction; it generates mucus, raises your temperature, and tries to expel the invader by any means necessary. Unfortunately there's a lot of collateral damage because the body doesn't quite know what it's up against yet, so the immune response is very widespread. Once the body is familiar with a particular strain of virus, it can adapt to combat it by producing antibodies specific to that virus and attacking it very precisely. Think of carpet bombing a town of suspected terrorists vs carrying out targeted assassinations. Anyways this is why a second infection from the same virus is rare, or even impossible to get, and why vaccines are effective at protecting us from diseases we've never encountered.

      To more specifically answer your question, if our body didn't take these measures the virus would slowly hijack our cells bit by bit until our bodily functions eventually stopped working and we died. It has to start strong with a very broad and powerful immune response because it doesn't know what it's dealing with.

  • Zephyr93

    And how effective is a rising body temp, against flu viruses (virii?)

    • Levangeline

      Depends on how resilient the virus is. Most viruses have a fairly limited temperature tolerance, beyond which their proteins and nucleic acids begin to degenerate. Your own body's cells are a little more resilient but still sensitive to increased temperature, so the hope when you get a fever is that the heat will kill off all or most of the viral cells before it damages your own cells too badly.
