    If a cop sets up "bait" for any crime and they mess up even a minor detail then a lawyer can usually get the case thrown out under 'entrapment' rules. Entrapment is when police coerce or trick a non-criminal into committing a crime.

    If a cop is driving down the highway behind you, that is legal. If they start ridding your bumper (driving really close to your car), that is unsafe but still legal. If you feel uncomfortable about how close they are to your car and you speed up (past the speed limit), then you have broken the law. If the cop then decides to arrest/ticket you for speeding, that is called entrapment and it is illegal because they coerced you into committing the crime.

    If a drug buyer approaches an undercover cop that is 'selling drugs' and starts a conversation, the cop is not allowed to use any "pushy sales tactics" or offer free trials. Likewise, if an undercover cop gives a person money and requests that they go buy drugs the case is likely to be thrown out unless they also arrest the supplier.

    It is also worth noting that some very common police traps can fall under entrapment. If a cop parks their car so that it can't be seen from the highway, they are not allowed to use their speed-gun or ticket speeders - they have to remain 'clearly visible' from the roadway. Likewise, the cameras that photograph intersections are very frequently thrown out under 'entrapment' or 'privacy' rules if you appeal the ticket in court.

    FBI, CIA, and 'sting operations' have a bit more wiggle-room on what is considered acceptable practice, though.
