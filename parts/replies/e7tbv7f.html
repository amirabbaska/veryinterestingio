    One HERV is responsible for producing the protein that locally inactivates the immune system in the vicinity of the uterus during pregnancy. Retroviruses could very well be responsible or have played a role in the creation of mammals. Another caveat is that we find sequences from non-retroviruses in animal genomes all the time, just not to the extent of the RVs.

    Viruses have FAR more unique genes than all organisms on the planet combined. When one of the genes in a genome matches a highly conserved gene in a virus(quite a few databases for this) it can be inferred to have originated from an ancient infection. When that gene is in the same locus as other organisms of that species, we know it isn’t from an infection in that organism’s life.
