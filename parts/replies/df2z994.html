    • HeilBrendan

      Not OP, but you can think of your sympathetic nervous system and parasympathetic nervous system as "linked": when one is at 80% activity, the other will be at 20%, etc.

      Now, each of these systems controls various parts of the body, and function differently, but you can think of them generally as "makes you excited" (sympathetic) and "makes you relaxed" (parasympathetic).

      When you exercise, you active your sympathetic system. But remember, the two systems are linked. When you're running away from a hungry predator, your sympathetic system will draw energy from the parasympathetic system. More energy for your muscles, but less energy for your bladder (which is controlled primarily by the parasympathetic system). Because of this decrease, the bladder's urge is lessened, and you can easily ignore the urge to urinate.
