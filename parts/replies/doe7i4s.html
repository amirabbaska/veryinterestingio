    • Chemiczny_Bogdan

      Temperature is a measure of a material's internal energy (for each degree od freedom), so higher temperature means the gas molecules/atoms are more likely to already be ionized. Even if they're not, there's more energy in other degrees of freedom that can be redirected to ionization under external electric field.

    • Stucer02

      Primarily it's an issue of having enough energy in the system to overcome the thermodynamic/kinetic barriers. Hotter materials contain more heat and thereby more energy is already in the system
