    Additionally, genes that code for proteins that are necessary for an organism's survival are far less likely to experience a successful mutation than a non-essential gene.

    For instance, the series of genes coding for the small ribosomal sub-unit is less likely to experience a functional mutation than say the series of genes coding for eye colour. The reason for this is that the small ribosomal sub-unit performs a highly specific function in the body (translating RNA into proteins), and if mutated it is unlikely that it will be able to "do its job" properly any longer. The genes coding for eye colour don't really perform a critical function within the body, and as a result a mutation in these genes is less likely to impact the organism's ability to survive.
