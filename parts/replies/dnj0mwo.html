    There are some genetic disorders such as Waardenburg Syndrome which can be inherited as autosomal dominant traits (varying in expression) which may manifest in visually similar pigmentation issues (such as family members having a white streak)...

    "Mutations in the EDN3, EDNRB, MITF, PAX3, SNAI2, and SOX10 genes can cause Waardenburg syndrome. These genes are involved in the formation and development of several types of cells, including pigment-producing cells called melanocytes. Melanocytes make a pigment called melanin, which contributes to skin, hair, and eye color and plays an essential role in the normal function of the inner ear. Mutations in any of these genes disrupt the normal development of melanocytes, leading to abnormal pigmentation of the skin, hair, and eyes and problems with hearing." -NIH https://ghr.nlm.nih.gov/condition/waardenburg-syndrome#genes

    See also https://rarediseases.org/rare-diseases/waardenburg-syndrome/
