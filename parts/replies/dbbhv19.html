• quantumwell

    Unfortunately, this is not right: KATRIN will be measuring single beta decay, and SNO+ won't be measuring double beta decay for a few years. Experiments looking at double beta decay are CUORE, GERDA, EXO-200, and KamLAND-Zen.

    But either way, you can't get the neutrino mass by measuring the endpoint of the two-neutrino double-beta decay spectrum, since the process is really, really rare, so no experiments have anywhere near enough events near the endpoint nor the resolution.

    KATRIN will attempt to measure the endpoint energy of the spectum with single beta decay, which is very common in some isotopes.
