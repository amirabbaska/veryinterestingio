    The next B is at 123.47Hz, so really you're hearing something that is very much in the middle of B and B-flat.

    In music theory, a note is flat when it is slightly lower in pitch than the closest natural. So even though B♭ is 116.54 Hz in equal-tempered tuning, 120 Hz is still B♭ because the closest natural is B^† , at 123.47 Hz.

    †In a key where B is one of the notes, like C-major. In other keys and tunings, it may even be written in sheet music as B♯ (e.g. in B♭-minor key with equal-tempered tuning) or A♯ (e.g. in F♯-major key with equal-tempered tuning).
