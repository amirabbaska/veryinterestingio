  • Verrk

    I think the answer lies somewhere in how much you can imitate the visual impression of a real life scene by painting it, versus what you can achieve chiseling something out of a block of marble.

    For paintings, your comparison is practically what you see with your naked eye. When it comes to sculptures, you don't really expect some lifelike stone figure - you rather expect the artist bringing everything possible out of the medium. However lifelike it is, it's still grey stone with no chance to make you believe it's human skin.

    Moreover I reckon it is different standing before a life sized marble figure, walking around it and seeing it from different angles, as opposed to seeing a flat painting, however realistic it may seem.
