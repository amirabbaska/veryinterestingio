    • aeroblaster

      Megafauna are the first ones to die out when the food chain is disrupted. It's a bit weird, because they're not top of the food chain while at the same time requiring an enormous working food chain below them. Top of the food chain animals and smaller creatures can survive because their diets are so generalized they can live off anything. Turtles, Crocs, Birds, Sharks, etc. all have something very in common with humans: they're not picky. They can and will eat anything if their survival depends on it, and their bodies support that "anything" diet. Compare this with elephants/whales or mosasaurs/plesiosaurs, they're fucked if their one food source is not as plentiful as before. It doesn't even have to be extinct, the simple fact that megafauna evolve to survive in times of abundance, dooms them for survival in times of hardship.
