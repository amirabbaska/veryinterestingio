    Actually, some species beyond the dripline do live for quite a long time, even orders of magnitude longer than the timescale necessary to form an atom (~ 10^(-14) seconds).

    A few long-lived unbound nuclides on the proton-rich side have been found. It's harder to do on the neutron-rich side, but some candidates have been identified.

    For example, iron-45, a two-proton emitter, has a half-life of over 2 milliseconds. That's comparable to the lifetime of a bound particle inside the dripline, which decays by beta emission. Many orders of magnitude larger than what you'd expect for an unbound nuclide.
