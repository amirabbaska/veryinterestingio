    Something I haven't seen mentioned is the age of the beach/land mass. More time to be weathered can equal smaller sand grains. (But not always, there are other factors)

    For example Australia is a VERY old land mass, parts of which are 3+billion years old - and it's beaches have the softest sand I've ever experienced. Whereas Hawaii's big island is not even 1 million years old... and it has coarse, butt chaffing grains!
