      • chuckymcgee

        CFLs definitely were cheaper, but CFLs have a lot of downsides LEDs do not.

        CFLs have dramatically lower lifespans if they're rapidly cycled on an off. In a place like a hallway or bathroom or closet or garage where a light may often be on for only 15-30 seconds, CFLs may last for less time than an old incandescent bulb and effectively require more energy to provide the same light once you count the manufacturing energy requirements. LEDs don't show nearly as dramatic loss of life from rapid-on-off cycles.

        The light quality for any run of the mill CFL bulb is also inferior compared to LEDs and incandescents. They do not provide immediate light and take a while to power one and reach their full brightness. The cheaper bulbs have a very poor blueish light. LEDs in contrast provide instant high-quality light that exceeds incandescent. You can get them in whatever color you want.

        CFLs also have a lot of mercury. Nothing for a typical home user to be worried about as long as they don't break or eat the bulbs, but if we're thinking about the net environmental consequences of manufacturing and disposing a lot of them, it is a big deal.

        Thankfully decent LEDs have fallen drastically. Better light, less energy and a longer lifespan? Sign me up.
