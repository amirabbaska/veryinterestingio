      Oh! And to tack on to your original post, I remembered a comment that came from a native Louisianan: "you could have an entire dinner party full of people in your home, and a gigantic palmetto bug crawls across the floor and no one bats an eye. But if a little one walks into the room, that's when people stop eating."

      The thought there is that "palmetto bugs" (which, along with "water bugs," is a nice name southerners call them to pretend in their head that they're not in fact cockroaches) are commonplace in the south where it's humid, warm, and the climate is agreeable. Cockroaches are like any other bug. They go where they please. But when you see little ones, that means they're breeding. Laying eggs. In your house.

      That's put a lot into perspective for me when I see adult ones in the city.
