    • AWildBugHasAppeared

      Well you expect to 'see' gravitons at any scale, because otherwise Newton would've never gotten bonked on the head by that apple. However, at those high energy scales we expect to see traces of higher-order interactions between gravity and the other fundamental fields of nature, i.e. we can no longer say 'this electron interacts with that graviton and we can neglect the rest of the fields. More correctly said, our current models predict that at those kind of energy scales the interaction between gravitons and other fields becomes infinite. The fact that we don't live in a black hole means those predictions are false but we don't know how or why they're false and we would very much like to find out.
