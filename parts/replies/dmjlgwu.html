    And it depends what you're trying to achieve.

    For instance, Monster University averaged at 29hours per frame to render. 24fps, 104mins runtime, you do the math.

    That was partly because they had a lot of characters on screen at any time (complex scenes), but also because Sully in particular had 5.4million individual hairs in his fur. Rendering that is a nightmare (it's also why Lotsa was cut from Toy Story 1 - they couldn't do fur well back then - note all the TS1 toys have smooth surfaces - plastic like Woody, Buzz and Potato Head, or Porcelain like Bo Peep - rendering smooth surfaces is relatively easy).

    So if your animation uses relatively few textures/assets or rigged models, the animation is more straightforward. If you want realistic fur, water or fire then you're going to get a big bill from the render farm because you're buying more hours of their compute time.
