    • pineappledan

      Spiders use hydraulic pressure to extend their legs, but they don't use it to contract. To put it another way, the 'power stroke' against the ground is muscle-powered

      Edit: I should also say that insects DO in fact use hydraulic pressure in their bodies, just not for movement. Without a proper circulatory system as we think of it, insects often use "pumping" of their abdomen to shift around their hemolymph, to help circulate nutrients or cool the body.
