• Fieuryy

    I think it has to do with the fact that Eastern culture is a collective culture, with a large emphasis on family and being one unit. The children's decisions can reflect poorly on the whole family, and the parents freeing their adult children can be seen as neglect. In western cultures it's primarily individualization that is exhibited, a cultural view that maintains the individuality of every person. The family is a support unit, but every person is responsible for their own lives and actions.

    It's precisely that difference in culture that explains the drive for westerners to make their lives their own, hence the moving out/independence.
