      How does the muscle scar affect the muscle's future performance? -

      Eventually the scar tissue breaks down. The scar tissue "remodels" and embeds into the pre-existing muscle or soft tissue. The muscle does not technically fire or function as it once did, but functionally or strength wise, you generally do not miss it. What happens is, initially, the scar tissue is a "mix mash mesh" of fibers. After use of the muscle, the striations of the scar/scar tissue eventually align and embed into the striated (lines) of the muscle fibers. Exercises (eccentric muscle exercises, or even just regular muscle use will "re-align" the scar tissue fibers into the muscle) Think about a scar on your skin. At first it is bumpy, crusty and raised. Eventually the scar softens, embeds into the skin and forms a small scar. Eventually, over time, that scar lightens and becomes a white mark, or in some cases dissapears - the same thing will occur in the muscle.
