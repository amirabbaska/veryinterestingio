      • emmess14

        Correct - drugs like succinylcholine and rocuronium (known as paralytic agents) prevent the body’s muscles from moving while under anesthetic.

        In the case of other procedures where patients are awake but sedated, often they’ll be given a spinal anesthetic in addition to sedation. The spinal blocks all signals below the level of the spinal cord where it takes place (usually ~L4/5) - most importantly sensation but also motor activity, preventing any reflex.

        As an aside, before starting surgery, anesthetists will often brush a patients eyelashes (or something similar) in order to try and elicit a motor reflex. If the reflex is present, surgery won’t start until it is no longer there. I imagine the same thing would be true of the knee jerk reflex (just harder to test in an OR once the sterile field is set up etc)
