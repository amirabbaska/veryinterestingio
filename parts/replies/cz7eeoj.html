    • bossk123

      Special forces is obviously a lot different, and Im sure they act a lot more autonomously. However, our snipers generally won't be operating just by themselves. Yes they operate in spotter/sniper teams. They definitely can be sent out by themselves to do traditional sniper missions. However, it really does depend on the situation. My scout snipers platoon is essentially a battalion asset they will use as they see fit. Generally they will just want reconnaissance from us. We would work in small teams and set up observation points. Our snipers alone could be requested to other missions. I think it's one of those things about the military where it really depends on your unit, and what theater you fight in. One sniper could spend an entire deployment merely on observation with a scout team. Another could be tasked out on a large variety of missions.
