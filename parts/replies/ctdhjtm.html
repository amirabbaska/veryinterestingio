    • AngryEEng

      If you put the prints of 5 year old you and 30 year old you in front of a latent fingerprint expert, they would most likely be able to match the prints together (assuming the latents were of good quality).

      However, if using an automated fingerprint identification system (computer program for matching fingerprints) the correlation between 5 year old you and 30 year old you's prints would likely be very low due to the changed distance between minutiae points from growth.

      • killerguppy101

        But they aren't looking for the distance between points, they are looking for the relative positioning. That would only change with a significant change in shape (shape change due to growth can be accounted for, especially if you know race). If they just checked distance that could easily change with quality and resolution of the imager and the quality of the print, which depends on a huge number of factors.
