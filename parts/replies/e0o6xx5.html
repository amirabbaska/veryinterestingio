    Fatigue can occur from lactic acid production as a result of short-duration, high-intensity physical activity. If lactic acid is not cleared from the muscle tissue it disassociates into lactate and H+. The increased H+ concentration lowers pH and increases acidity. Muscle acidosis negatively affects energy production and sarcomere contraction.

    Fatigue can also occur from PCr and glycogen depletion, decreased blood glucose levels, excess Pi (as you said), ambient heat and muscle temperatures, and failure of neuromuscular systems.
