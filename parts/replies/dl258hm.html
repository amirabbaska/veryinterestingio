      • bulksalty

        An provision in the corporate charter (the document that creates a corporation and sets its rules) that allows management to issue a huge number of shares to certain holders under certain conditions.

        As an example when, Gordon Gecko buys 20% of a company, and threatens a vote to sell the whole thing to him, the company gives the 80% owners new shares, reducing his ownership of the company to 11% and greatly increasing the cost to takeover the firm.
