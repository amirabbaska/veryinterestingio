  • mycleverusername

    For the simple reason that in many people's opinion there's no such thing as a "no kill" shelter

    Well, there are a few true "no-kill" shelters.

    There are basically 3 types of shelters. The standard animal control/animal shelter that has a high kill rate and no funds to take care of animals. They typically either kill the animals or transfer them to other shelters.

    Then there is your restricted access no kill shelter. These are the fancy shelters that have great dogs and love to tout that they don't have the facilities to even put dogs to sleep. Yeah, because they only take in adoptable dogs and let the other shelters do their dirty work. They turn away dogs with medical and discipline problems, mostly.

    There are a small amount of open access no kill shelters. They take in any and all dogs, rehabilitate them, and treat them before adopting them out. To keep the open access no kill designation, they must kill less than like 2% of the animals they take in.

    So, yes, it's technically a kill shelter, but sometimes it is medically necessary to put the animals down, no matter what. I can't say you are a monster for putting down a dog with stage 4 cancer that can't walk.
