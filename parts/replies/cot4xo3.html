    In Brazilian Portuguese the diminutive is used all the time and actually alters the meaning of words. Changing "só" to "sozinho" changes the meaning from "alone" to "lonely".

      Actually, "sozinho" isn't a diminutive, even if it uses -inho.

      Also, "só" and "sozinho" both translate to "alone". "Solitário" is what translates to "lonely".

      But yes, diminutive changes the meaning of words too. It usually adds a negative meaning to the object. If I said "Ele tem uma guitarrazinha" ("he has a little guitar"), it would mean that his guitar isn't very good.
