    • myexisproblematic

      Well, no matter what, we do know that memories are linked in our brains. I hear mamma mia by ABBA, and I always have involuntary memories of playing a level in sonic 2 because I was listening to the music while playing that level once. We don't exactly need studies to know that memory contains links.

      But, is everything linked? Are two things linked just because you think about them? Are links basic building blocks, or something that sometimes happens? When we forget or lose access to memories, is it because the memories are gone, or because the links are gone? Are links elementary blocks? Are memories made only of links?

      We know that there are at least extra rules to the linked data model, because I don't have a terrible amount of links associated with the word 'the' that I can consciously summon, but there must be a lot of them.

      We know also that there are schema involved, do schema modify how links form? Are schema the basic building blocks, and a link a type of schema that occurs? Or are schema built themselves of links?

      Is there one universal type of link, as many of these questions implicitly suppose, or are there different links with different rules?

      Does priming work because it refreshes these type of links, or because it reinforces physical pathways in the brain? (Are they separate things?)

      A lot of implicit things in the model are pretty hard to actually test, especially since it was built to follow what we know the mind does do.
