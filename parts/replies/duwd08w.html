      Well, a lot of hackers have been caught through the years, and many more will be caught. However, when talking hacks of the magnitude where, say, an election is manipulated, or sensitive (like national security level sensitive) data is stolen, hackers usually aren't teens in mom's basement.

      Let's use the alleged hack against the US election as an example, and let's assume that it actually was Russia who initiated it. In this case the hackers most likely knew of every potential hazard, and took every precaution many times over to avoid detection/capture. Making it pretty much impossible to find out 100% who did it, not to mention actually proving it.

      Also, hacking is much more than just running some exploit tool against a target. The actual exploit is preceded by months of intel gathering and mapping of the target system, and often any neighboring or surrounding systems/IT-environments.

      Source: Work in cyber security and penetration testing.
