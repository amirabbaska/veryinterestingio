    • Sexpistolz

      The best way I think to look at it is like a car. A script/screenplay is like the guts, the engine, chassy, drivetrain. A screenwriter decides whether it gets a 4 cylinder or 6, AWD or rear wheel. It's where it all begins. It's what separates an american muscle car, and a honda civic. The director is the body designer. The curves, the colors, overall look and design. All the features, the way the doors open, the interior. It's what initially catches your eye when you see that hot rod drive by, even though underneath it might not be all that different than what's under your hood. And finally the actors, they're the drivers, they're the ones moving the whole thing along. Where should the credit go? Well all of them really. A great driver can only do so much with a rust bucket, and you can toss a spoiler on a civic and it's still going to be a civic. In general, terrific acting is done with the enabling of a good director, and good directing is done with a well written foundation to build upon.
