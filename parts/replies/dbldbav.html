      For me, the problem with constantly being around people, whether it's a group or even just most individuals, is that it takes effort to not come across as passive-aggressive. I don't know why that matters so much, but I'm aware that short comments can be interpreted as being hostile.

      When I'm alone though, I don't have to worry about any of that.

      It's not all the time either, and I think that's a common misconception about introverts. We can be social and even every once in awhile, surprisingly enjoy being the welcomed center of attention. Personally, I just get anxious when I can't take a break from it, or especially when being in that situation is something I'm forced to do (out of obligation or otherwise). That state of being on edge non-stop for too long is mentally taxing.
