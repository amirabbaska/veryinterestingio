    Technically, just pointing it in the opposite direction of the spacecraft wouldn't always get you back. It depends on where you are. If you are in orbit your local reference frame is rotating and accelerating with respect to the orbit inertial frame and you need to take that into account.

    Another way to think about it is that your maneuvers in your local frame of reference are also changing your overall orbit, so as you and your spacecraft move in your orbits of the central body, you will drift apart.

    You need to use something like the Clohessy-Wiltshire equations to determine how you want to maneuver in your local frame.
