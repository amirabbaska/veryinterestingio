    • MsRhuby

      Well yes, you are wrong. Placebo pills do not cause ovulation - if they did, there would be a lot of disappointed people around the world.

      After taking 21 consecutive pills, you will not ovulate during the placebo week. The pills prevent ovulation. Take less than 21 pills, and there's no guarantee.

      Of course, the placebo week is not needed, but it's included as an option. Some of my pill brands have not included them at all, but simply given the option to not take any pills for 7 days.

      POP, also known as the minipill, has to be taken every day and there is no break. It also doesn't always prevent ovulation, but protects against pregnancy in several ways.
