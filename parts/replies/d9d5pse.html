    Great history and all that shit, but I think its also important to mention the significance of the number 60, with 60 minutes in an hour and 60 seconds in a minute, and also the importance of 12 with the day being 2 12 hour halves. It might make more intuitive sense to someone to break a full day into 10 or 20 hours. This seems smart at first since we think of 10 as a nice round number, but 10 is bad for creating smaller units out of.

    Half of 10 is easy, 1/3 of 10 creates a repeating decimal, 1/4 of 10 isn't so bad but still creates a decimal. 10 simply does not have that many factors, just 1, 2, 5 and 10.

    12 has the factors 1, 2, 3 ,4, 6 and 12 so 1/2, 1/3, 1/4, and 1/6 of 12 are all simple whole numbers to compute.

    60 is even better, not only does 60 have the factors 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 10, 12, 15, 20, 30, 60 but it even has the consecutive factors 1-6 so its easy to compute 1/2, 1/3, 1/4, 1/5 and 1/6 of 60 without any decimals.
