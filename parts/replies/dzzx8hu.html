    Lots of extremely specialized equipment

    This. IDK about the regulations in the US, but in Germany (possibly whole EU) there exist export restrictions for a broad list of items that are "dual use" (i.e. are not weapons, but could be used to manufacture weapons). Examples are high precision ball bearings or magnetic bearings that can be used to build centrifuges (which again are used to enrich nuclear fuel).

    So if someone would like to build a plant with a lot of centrifuges they would have to source even "simple" parts like their precision bearings on the black market while the intelligence services of several countries are on the lookout for just such transactions.

    • wyseguy7

      This is correct. There is an international organization known as the Nuclear Suppliers Group that has a list (the Dual Use List http://www.nuclearsuppliersgroup.org/images/NSG_Part_2_June-2017.pdf) which details. The controls deal with almost all parts of the process involved in making a nuclear weapon, but many of them are specifically targeted towards stopping someone from illicitly building a centrifuge plant.

      The earlier responses are also correct - the Oak Ridge plant used nuclear diffusion, which uses a mind boggling amount of power. Centrifuge plants need less. But you still have to figure out how to make/import a bunch of highly specialized industrial products, like vacuum pumps, high strength steel/carbon fiber, and magnets.
