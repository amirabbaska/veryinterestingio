    The sad thing is a lot of employers incentivize people too through matching contributions. Most employers at minimum will match 3% of what you pay in. So if you contribute 10%, its actually 13% (free money) with the company match. And yet people still don't invest. I used to audit retirement plans at a Big 4 firm and I can't tell you how many 50 and 60 somethings have literally $0 in their 401K. And the younger crowd isn't doing much better. Obviously they have more time to change this, but a lot of young people opt to not invest in their plans too in return for a higher paycheck. That's how you have no retirement savings and end up in a crappy assisted living home when you retire. So kids, please invest. The recommended annual savings rate is 10-15% of your paycheck to your 401K alone.
