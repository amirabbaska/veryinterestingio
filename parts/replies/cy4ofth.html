    Both of these statements are wrong. First off, all of the energy in the universe would still not be enough to accelerate something to the speed of light since no matter how much energy was put into the system, more of that energy would be converted to mass the closer and closer you got to the speed of light.

    Secondly, time would not run backwards at the speed of light. Relative time slows the closer and closer you get to luminal speeds but even at the speed of light it wouldn't run backwards. There is some conjecture on reverse time travel at supra-luminal speeds, you can read about it here http://www.pbs.org/wgbh/nova/blogs/physics/2015/08/can-you-really-go-back-in-time-by-breaking-the-speed-of-light/, but this is all conjecture and completely impossible with anything made of mass.
