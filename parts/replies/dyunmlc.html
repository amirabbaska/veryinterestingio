    • Overtime_Lurker

      I actually changed "terrorist that hides among innocent civilians" to "an enemy that hides among innocent civilians", so yes, ISIS would be one example, a pretty good one at that.

      Cancer is like any other organism on the planet. Everything wants to reproduce itself whatever way it can. Malignant cancerous cells are really good at that, by definition. They've slipped past their one predator, your immune system, and have developed a "beneficial" mutation that causes them to multiply out of control. Unfortunately for them, just like any other organism that multiplies too quickly, they outproduce their resource, your body, and kill you along with themselves. In the wild, cancer would probably weaken you until you get killed by some other disease or predator, so it's a pretty good analogue to religious extremists (cancerous cells) that weaken their own home country (home organism) and make it vulnerable to other countries (other organisms).
