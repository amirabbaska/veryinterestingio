    • HappyFailure

      The fun thing with CFCs here is that the reason it's a problem is very tightly tied to why we thought it was safe. We wanted a chemical that would be very stable and non-reactive--CFCs manage that by having very tight bonds between the carbon and the fluorine and chlorine atoms, because fluorine and chlorine are very electronegative.

      But anything will break if you hit it with enough energy, such as you find if the molecules drift up to where they get zapped by UV above the ozone layer...and now those loose chlorine and fluorine atoms are hyperreactive due to that same electronegativity.
