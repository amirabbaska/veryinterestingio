    • ThudnerChunky

      It has to do with the structure of academic research which results in a glut of phd students and postdocs. Basically the money goes to creating job seekers instead of jobs. It's pretty complex (people have written books about it and there was even an nih workgroup on the topic), but I will explain a few contributing factors.

      1) The best scientists can run very large research programs with lots and lots of grants (one professor can take up a lot of the NIH money before it's worth spending some on a less brilliant professor).

      2) The research in these programs is mostly carried out by the phd students and postdocs. So you end up with one permanent professor taking a large amount of the money and producing a lot of job seekers (a pyramid like structure).

      3) The visa programs are very lax in academia. Lots of postdocs from overseas (mostly china and india) come here, further adding to the glut. There are some bigs labs that are run entirely on chinese or indian postdocs.
