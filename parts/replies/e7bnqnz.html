    • curlyben

      Because we are very possibly pushing things too fast.

      Imagine the climate is a swing that is swinging back and forth. Natural processes keep it going at the natural frequency, but human effects are giving it a surprisingly effective push to speed it up. Either we push it at the right time and make the swings more extreme, or we push it at the wrong time and dampen them. We could also never stop pushing and stop the swing entirely.

      The natural assumption is that the Earth and space and history is too damn big for us to affect it, which is why that's such a compelling argument, but the data seems to contradict that assumption or at least cast very real doubt that warrants extensive reevaluation. Originally these findings were very surprising, but it took people challenging their assumptions to look into it further so that we know as much as we do. It is still easy to fall back to seemingly obvious assumptions.
