        I think you're misinterpreting the fact that there are no surviving neanderthal Y chromosomes. At least, other people have made a similar conjecture on that basis.

        It means that there is no unbroken lineage of males without an intervening female descending from a neanderthal father. But, there is also no neanderthal mitochondrial DNA, so there is no continuous female line descending from neanderthals either.

        I asked about that here on this subreddit a couple years ago, the replies were very informative. The replies to my question cited statistical models that suggest that losing the neanderthal mitochonrion and hte neaderthal Y chromosome was the most likely statistical outcome, even if the mating ratio was equal and yielded fertile offspring of both genders.
