    *Energy can be lost to phenomena besides heat, e.g. thermal radiation at frequencies besides infrared (heat), the emitting of sound etc.

    Thermal radiation at any frequency will heat up objects that absorb it. There's nothing that equates infrared to heat. The only reason we associate infrared radiation with heat is because objects at commonly available temperatures will emit the majority of their thermal radiation in the infrared spectrum. But 1 W of visible light will heat stuff up just as much as 1 W of infrared (assuming both are absorbed 100%).

    Additionally, sound is absorbed by walls and other objects, at which point it is turned into heat.

    Assuming all of the emissions (EM radiation or sound) remain inside the house, they'll all end up heating up whatever it was that absorbed them.
