    • sailfaster

      A better term might be cosmetically unsaleable.

      There is nothing unsafe about a steak that's been in a retail case for three days and has a brown spot. Many grocery stores choose to grind instead of discount as a steak because it cannibalizes the sale of unblemished steaks.

      You legally have seven days to retail beef once its out of cryovac. Most large chains retail as a steak for three to four days, grind, then have another three days to sell as ground before they claim a loss and toss it.

      Beef as a steak or roast is remarkably hearty and takes over a month at refrigeration temps to become truly rancid, well beyond what you would ever retail something at.

      Lastly no-one is that sketchy in the retail industry to intentionally risk customer health, its an instant death sentence, look how much money chipotle has lost in the past two years for small norovirus and ecoli outbreaks.
