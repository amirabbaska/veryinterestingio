  • Omnifarious0

    There is the idea of 'taxpayer standing'. That you, as a taxpayer, have an interest in your government not enforcing unconstitutional laws. It has been used in 4th amendment challenges before. But usually the courts only accept it for 1st amendment challenges.

    One case in particular was in Seattle. Taxpayer standing was used to challenge city government hiring practices that had a blanket requirement for a drug test for all new hires instead of restricting it to just new hires in certain safety critical jobs.
