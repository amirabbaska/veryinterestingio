      • UScossie

        The schwarzschild radius of the observable universe is smaller than the observable universe, though only slightly. What this means is that the observable universe doesn't have sufficient mass/energy relative to its volume to become a black hole. So no we don't live inside a black hole per se. That said as you pointed out according to the big bang theory the universe burst forth from a singularity. Aside from containing many orders of magnitude more energy than the black holes within our universe, this singularity would be indistinguishable from the singularities they contain. (IE infinitely small and infinitely dense). All of our current physics break down when dealing with these infinities, so we can't say for sure what happens in a singularity, perhaps it explodes outwards through new dimensions that don't intersect with those our universe experiences, a big bang in its own right. This is a possible explanation for the origin of our universe. However, Hawking radiation, (highly probable phenomenon but has not been observed to a satisfactory degree to treat it as a certainty) degrades black holes over time via annihilation, eventually erasing the black hole and returning its energy to our universe. This would seem to be a hurdle to the idea that a black hole is the furnace of creation, but there is so much we don't know when it comes to this part of physics that it is impossible to say for sure. Perhaps a singularity is just a catalyst that allows unimaginably great amounts of energy to leak out of another universe or the m-theory postulated hyper plane into a new universe.

        TL:DR: No we don't live inside a black hole, but within the bounds of our current understanding of physics one may speculate that our universe may have been forged from a singularity in another universe without breaking any known laws..
