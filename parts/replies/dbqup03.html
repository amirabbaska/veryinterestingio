    It's a mild headache, and it goes away pretty quickly once the music is changed. For me, it usually only happens if the music has lots of percussion and is on while I'm trying to multitask, so I get annoyed at the noise that's disrupting my thought process and feel much happier when it's off. It's more of a "there's too much unpleasant noise" thing rather than a "too loud" thing. I do usually feel like turning it down will help, but honestly turning it off is ideal, and changing to a more enjoyable musical genre is a close second.

    I think the headache is from subtle muscle tension, which most people get when they're annoyed or stressed. I get pretty annoyed when things interrupt my thoughts over and over again and make me do my work 10x slower, and music does that for me. If it's music I enjoy listening to, the interruption is pleasant rather than stressful, so I don't get a tension headache. If you enjoy all music, it would make sense that you haven't experienced this phenomenon.
