    These kinds of intrusive thoughts definitely came to a head after the birth of my daughter.

    I thought I was secretly and silently a monster waiting to happen but once the exhaustion and chaos of the first few months passed I realized the thoughts served a survival purpose for my child.

    They made me super safe and vigilant about her safety and wellbeing.

    As I bathed her I imagined what would happen if I suddenly died and she was left to drown; if I was ironing clothes I panicked thinking of the toddler years where she would be crawling around in prime falling iron territory; if I was slicing vegetables I imagined her little arm and hand searching for something and accidentally being hacked off; if I was holding her I imagined if I had a heart attack and dropped her or fell on top of her, oh god the many days of finding the worst case scenario in any situation!

    My husband calls me final destination mom.
