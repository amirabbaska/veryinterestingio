    • Jegory

      It's basic economics.

      The exchange rate (the value of the £ compared to other currencies) determines how expensive exports and imports will be.

      A weakened £ means exports will be cheaper, and imports will be more expensive. As a result, more people will be buying UK exports, and fewer UK citizens will be buying imports from abroad.

      As a result, more money is flowing into the UK economy (in the form of exports) than is leaving it (in the form of imports). More money coming into the economy means businesses that rely on international trade will flourish.

      In short, the falling value of the £ can be said to be a good thing for the UK economy, and the businesses represented in the FTSE100 have had an exceptional year because of it.
