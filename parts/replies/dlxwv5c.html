    Hi! Controls are for when you are perturbing a subset of the population. You actually don't need a control group if you're just trying to prove two (or more) populations are different. You just choose one population as the "baseline" and use different statistical metrics to support or reject the hypothesis that the other group acts the same as the baseline (variations likely due to random chance).

    • hollth1

      People who are interested, look up quasi-experimental design or experiment design.

      In some cases we can't do a control. Imagine a scenario with cancer. We can't intentionally give people cancer, so we have to design around that. We lose out on the power that a control gives, but gain the ability to apply tools to a more varied set of instances.
