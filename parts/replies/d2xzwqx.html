    The idea of an antenna is to convert free space electrical fields into measurable electrical signal. When you are working with rf frequencies it is easy to measure as a Ghz sampler can easily process the lower MHz signal.

    What? Since when did antennas need to produce a signal that can be sampled by a digital system? Sampling has nothing to do with antennas - all antennas need to do is convert an EM signal into a voltage signal on a wire, and vice versa.

    In communications sampling happens after the signal is downconverted to baseband (the spectrum is then centered around 0Hz instead of around the carrier frequency). Satellite communication systems do not sample a 30Ghz signal (as you said, analog-to-digital converters cannot sample that quickly). Instead: 1) The signal comes in through the antenna and is converted to a voltage signal, then 2) it is downconverted to baseband (by analog circuits) so its spectrum is centered at 0Hz, and then 3) it is sampled.
