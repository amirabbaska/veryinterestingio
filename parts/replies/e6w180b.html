    • xboxpants

      You can change this, but it will take effort and most importantly, time. Your brain is very used to games, which are already heavily designed to give you a direct dopamine hit. That's great for business. The fun, rewarding parts of games are heavily highlighted with big, flashing lights and bells and whistles. Literally.

      But the rewards are there with other activities, too. They're just not as obvious. Let's imagine drawing - If you spend an hour working on a sketch, you won't get an on-screen notification every time you level up a skill or get an achievement or unlock a new feature, but all of those things are still happening in the background. You just haven't ever been taught to notice them.

      So you have to teach yourself. You're gonna have to make a conscious effort to look for the rewarding things in life. Look up mindfulness, and practice that every day, so you will notice when your shading is a little better than it was the day before. Write down a list every day of your accomplishments and struggles, so you start to see that fingers are a specific obstacle for you, and when you finally get good at them, it's essentially like defeating a boss, as well as unlocking a new skill.

      Eventually, thinking like this will start to become more natural, but for now, you've been trained by games not to be able to do it on your own. You'll need to learn. It took me until I was about 30, and I'm still working on it, but the rewards are fantastic.
