    That logic doesn't hold.

    Your argument would only make sense if companies were concerned with retaining otherwise unemployable persons. Persons who can find employment elsewhere will get healthcare at their new job.

    Actual reason: tax cost, and legitimate concerns about monopsonistic systems. "Monopsonies" are reverse monopolies (one buyer, who can dictate all pricing) -- and they can create huge problems via a number of means: lack of diversity in care and focus means that expensive experiments equipment, etc are less likely to be funded impacting medical research and buyer-seller relationships with one buyer can also hurt competition and thus innovation and quality.

    There are definitely legitimate issues on both sides and trying to reduce either view point to malicious/selfish intent is beneath us.
