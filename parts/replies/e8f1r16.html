      You can balance on a bike while you're moving, right?

      But what about when it's not? You fall over.

      The human body is exactly the same - it's constantly making minute adjustments. If you stood completely still and someone came over and turned you into stone with a magic wand, you'd fall over.

      When you're in deep thought, your brain has to shut out other processes - it has to stop the mind from wandering, it has to stop you from getting distracted, so it's making constant tiny adjustments to balance the mental load. There's constant sensory input. If you're jiggling your leg, then you're getting sensory input that isn't distracting while letting the body "vent" by doing its thing. It's like handing a dog a chew toy to stop it shredding up your shoes. Its letting the body follow natural urges in a way that doesn't distract you from the task you've set it.
