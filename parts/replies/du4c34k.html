  • jessegammons

    Additionally, I think you have to consider how saturated the fatty acid is. For instance, something like chocolate has a lot of saturated fatty acid (palmitate) in it, so it's more difficult to remove with detergents because it's already hydrolyzed. It's going to be slightly more difficult to get those things to bind to water in the wash, so they'll just stick around. Some agents that increase the acidity can help also, because this will kind of dilute the hydroxide in the overall solution, freeing up those areas to react more readily with water (i.e., why people suggest cleaning with vinegar or lemon juice, etc).
