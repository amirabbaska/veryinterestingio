    My sense is that these thoughts are more likely to happen because we watch movies. On a lower level, below conscious thought, movies are real to us. Sure we can separate ourselves from it, but our subconcious doesn't. So we end up somewhat traumatized by events such as murders and we have sort of a light PTSD, but it's not as bad because we consciously know it wasn't real. But it still affects the thoughts that bubble up from our subconscious, and since it is such an intense and stimulating thought, and a thought that our subconcious thinks is very important for us to be prepared for, we have a tough time not thinking about it.

    The people it's bad for are schizophrenics, because they have such a tough time differentiating between what their subconcious is sending to their conscious. A symptom of that is that they literally hear and see things that aren't there, which is to say they literally see and hear things from their subconscious. From what I've read on Reddit, and take of this what you will, but schizophrenics from regions without modern entertainment don't have the same occurrence of evil voices and visions.

    But my sense is that perhaps we're all a bit like that. The difference is that people who aren't schizophrenics can much more easily tell the difference between thoughts that are real or not.

    This is just what I think based on life experience and observations, I don't necessarily believe it. ;-)
