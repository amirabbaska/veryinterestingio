    This is more or less the correct answer. Tattoo ink particles are too large to be removed by the body's normal waste system.

    Tattoo removal lasers breakup the ink using UV into smaller particles that can be flushed away.

    Fading that people often see over time is most often caused by exposure to sunlight (i.e. lower power UV) that breaks up the ink much more slowly.

    Tattoos that never gets exposed to sunlight have much more minimal fading and muddling provide that the skin itself doesn't get damaged/change that dramatically from injury, weight gain/loss, pregnancy, etc.
