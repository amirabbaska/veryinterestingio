  • ZylonBane

    Ever see a tree come out of nowhere in the distance as you walk across a map? Somewhere behind you another one disappeared, freeing that memory space.

    Err, you're conflating two different things-- draw distance, and dynamic loading.

    In games where the entire map is loaded at once, a tree popping into view just means you got close enough for the engine to decide to start drawing it. Nothing behind you, or anywhere, gets unloaded.

    In open-world games engines like GameBryo, the world content is split up into cells that get loaded around the player as he walks around. But again, a tree appearing in front of you may mean a new cell has been loaded, but the trees behind you may still be there too, depending on how aggressively the engine unloads cells.

    Really, the only situation in which one tree replaces another tree would be when you're hitting the onscreen object render limit, and that's going to be happening to objects in front of you, not behind you.
