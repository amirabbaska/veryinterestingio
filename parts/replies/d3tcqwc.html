  • hartmd

    I am a physician. That usually is not true. Many viruses release substances that have very specific effects on the cells in our body to cause these symptoms. The degree of specificity is actually quite amazing. They often act on certain receptors in cells to cause symptoms.

    Many stomach bugs that cause diarrhea directly do this by releasing substances that affect the cell lining specifically causing those cells to not reabsorb fluid back into your body or to increase the amount of fluid released from your body into the GI tract. It has nothing to due with an immune response. This is advantageous to the bacteria because it makes it more likely you will pass it to another host.

    The same can be said about many viruses that cause colds. If my memory serves correctly, the rhinovirus is a good example of this. By making you produce copious amounts of mucous and sneezing, you are much more likely to pass it on.
