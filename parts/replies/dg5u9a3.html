      TLDR: It's just the name of a place in the brain that is roughly the size of a walnut.

      Hippocampus is the Latin version of the Greek name for seahorse. When surgeons take the brain apart in big visible chunks, the structure resembles a seahorse.

      Just looking at the big chunks of the brain is "gross anatomy", but in fact, the hippocampus consists of multiple smaller areas. To study these smaller areas would require surgeons to make many small cuts to the hippocampus itself and to use a microscope. This is what we call "microanatomy".

      We actually have two of these "hippocampi", one to the left and one to the right of the brain.
