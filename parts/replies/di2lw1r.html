    A couple of corrections about the Running Machine.

    The very first version of the bicycle had no handlebars, no seat, and wasn't steerable. It was invented in 1790 and was called the Celerifere. While not a bicycle by modern standards, it is the very first vehicle which moved under the rider's power, and also consisted of two wheels arranged linearly. It wasn't until 20 years later that we saw the Running Machine.

    The Running Machine, a.k.a Draisienne, Laufmaschine, or Hobby Horse was invented around 1816. It improved upon the original design by adding a steerable front wheel and handlebars.
