    • Cheben

      If you are standing next to the fire, no. Hot air will rise as another commenter has said. In your normal everyday life, convection is the dominant way heat is transfered (your radiators, food that cools, your house etc) but as soon as the "hot" temperature goes up an becomes large enough, radiation will quickly start to dominate, especially in an object such as a wood fueled fire. A flame is orange mainly from particles (usually coal) that radiates away energy as they glow, they are hot enough to glow in the visible spectrum. In devices such as coal, oil or biomass fueled boilers, radiant heat is the dominant heat transfer mechanism in the firebox. The big flame is cooled by water cooled walls without touching them. A certain degree of transfer is convection, but radiation will be the dominant force.

      The rate of transfer is proportional to the temperature to the fourth power(~T_high^4-T_low^4) which is the reason an object such as a vacuum thermos is very efficient at isolating, while a fireball at 2000K is very efficiently cooled by radiation alone.
