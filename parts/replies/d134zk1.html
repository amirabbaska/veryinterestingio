    • Koooooj

      It is very likely that what they're using for this is an artificial neural network. Google was at the absolute bleeding edge of neural networks in 2014 and is only outdone by a small, small number of researchers now. They won the most notable neural network competition for image recognition in 2014, although Microsoft took the victory late in 2015.

      One thing that neural networks are really good at is taking and image and reducing it from thousands or millions of bytes of raw data down to a few tens of bytes or less, where images that are semantically similar will be very close at the end, even if they're very different on a pixel level (e.g. a picture of an elephant from two perspectives. Both are elephants, but there's very poor agreement between the individual pixels).
