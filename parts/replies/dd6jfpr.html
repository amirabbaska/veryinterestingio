  • rdavidson24

    The Senate is required to confirm the President's nominee.

    Clarification: nominees to the Supreme Court must be confirmed by the Senate before they can take office. The Senate is not only under no constitutional obligation to confirm any particular nominee, it is under no constitutional obligation to even consider a nomination. It is generally expected that it will consider and confirm nominees, but this is a matter of historical custom and practice, not constitutional/legal requirement.
