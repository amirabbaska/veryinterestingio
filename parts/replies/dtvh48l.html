        My understanding of the macroscopic goings-on in a fusion reaction is that they're very well modeled by classical approaches that can rely on classical computing to predict. There's not a probabilistic "if the temperature on this hemisphere increases, there's a small chance the field might distort that way," but rather a "if the temperature goes up, you're definitely about to observe something happen, and you better do something really fast if you don't want it to destabilize."
