    • CuriousMetaphor

      A neutron star 1.4 times the mass of the Sun with a 20 km diameter has a gravitational pull of 1.87x10^12 m/s^(2) at the surface. To cancel out that pull it would need a rotational velocity of 1.37x10^8 m/s, or an angular rotation rate of 2200 revolutions per second. To experience 1g (10 m/s^(2)) at the equator, it would need a rotational velocity 0.03 mm/s lower than that.

      That would give you a 1g pull on the surface at the equator. However, if you went 1 mm to the north or south of the equator, you would now feel a force of 1.02g downward and 50,000 g towards the equator. If instead you went 1 mm above the surface, you would feel a force of 50,000 g upwards.

      (these are all Newtonian calculations not accounting for relativity)
