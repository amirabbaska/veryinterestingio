    You wouldn't get such a situation in the first place. Any collection of quarks and anti-quarks must have colours that add up to make white. Red, green and blue together make white, so if you've got three quarks then there'll be one of each colour. With two quarks, if one is red, then the other must be anti-red (anti-quarks have anti-red, anti-blue and anti-green as their colours, instead of red, blue and green).

    You can't isolate quarks of a single colour because the energy required to separate a quark from other quarks is also enough energy to create new quarks by pair production, so the quark you attempt to separate will just end up bound to one of the new quarks you've created.
