    Building onto what this guy said, the most basic way to determine genetic traits as taught in middle/high school is Punnett squares. Depending on whether the parents’ chromosomes are what combinations of dominant/recessive, the probability of the offspring’s trait can be determined.

    However in reality it’s far more than just combinations of DD, Dd, or dd in real life. Many traits like hair color requires far more than a single set to determine. You might end up with something like DDdDddDD for the mom and dDdDDddD for the dad, which a simple Punnett square just cannot illustrate.

    I do suggest you google Punnett squares though. I’m sure it’ll be able to give you a basic understanding.
