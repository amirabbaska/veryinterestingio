      It's because there are vast differences in heat capacity, density and thermal conductivity of water and air.

      You can experience the same differences just by touching different objects. If you touch a metal table, it will feel colder than a wood table, even though they are both at room temperature.

      The difference in heat capacity means the water molecules absorb more energy when they go from a lower temperature to the temperature of your body. The difference in density means there are more water molecules in direct contact with you. The difference in thermal conductivity means the energy absorbed from your body will spread faster in a bath than in a room full of air.
