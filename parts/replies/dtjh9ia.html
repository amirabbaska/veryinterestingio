      Not necessarily. We have had some success with similar diseases within isolated populations (see Jewish communities and Tay-Sachs rates) but it requires scrupulous record keeping and genetic testing to prevent genetic mixing which allows the carriers to drop out of the line.

    • OtterMaddness

      Noooooot quite.

      It's not like Natural Selection says 'OK, done with that! Now everyone with the carrier gene will just die'

      Gene carriers can pass the gene onto their offspring without a corresponding decrease in reproduction.

      And that's all natural selection is, at its heart. Traits that help an organism outcompete its romantic rivals tend to be passed on. Traits that prevent an organism from romantic competition tend to not get passed on. Traits that do not affect survival/reproduction one way or the other get passed on because there's no pressure to deselect for them.
