  • green_meklar

    As far as fish go, the weirdest ones would probably be some of the early jawless armored fish. Some of the ancestors of lampreys, or their relatives, looked like this. You might mistake it for a horseshoe crab before realizing it's actually a fish.

    However, that's nothing compared to the invertebrates you could catch. Go back to the Cambrian and you could find the opabinia, a free-swimming predatory invertebrate with five eyes and a mouth shaped like an elephant's trunk. We still don't know where this thing fits into the evolutionary tree, only that it and all its descendants died out more than 400 million years ago. They were small creatures, only a few inches long, but jump forward a hundred million years or so and you could find eurypterids, giant clawed sea scorpions that could grow as long as a human is tall.
