    • walksalot_talksalot

      This area of study became a little contentious in our lab. A new postdoc was studying how 300 hz sound would cause stress responses in mice.

      But, I studied hearing acuity in mice for my dissertation and mice don't hear below 1 kHz unless the sound is very loud. Even then they only go down to about 500 Hz. And my new colleague was claiming subtle 300 Hz sounds would distress mice.

      It's weird, we both have evidence to support our claims. I think the key takeaway is that the sound is doing something to the mice. I would wager that pressure sensors in the skin and internal organs are detecting the 300 Hz sound. As others have noted there is a growing body of literature showing that daily noise is having deleterious effects on our health. I'm curious to find out what the trigger/cofactor is. I don't think it's as simple as increased cortisol.
