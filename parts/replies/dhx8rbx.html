    An excellent answer. Too many people out there don't realize that sometimes these are legal terms explicitly used to trigger certain procedures that must be followed.

    For example, in the US one will sometimes hear that something is declared as a "state of emergency". That is done to make sure certain things are done in accordance with specific legal requirements, such as allowing federal resources to be used, securing funding for emergency operations, etc.

    Sure, "terrorism" has a definition people generally agree means "makes people afraid" or some such, but that has nothing to do with legally defining what the next steps will be.
