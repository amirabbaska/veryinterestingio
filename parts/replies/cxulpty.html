    • the_original_kermit

      A lot depends on the material, but I'm going to say no in general. It's important to point out if the max load is 100 lbs, that would be equivalent to carefully setting a 100 lbs ball bearing on the plate. 1 lbs @ 100 cycles would be the equivalent to carefully setting 1 lbs ball 100 times on the plate. This is called a cyclical failure, which does happen in materials, just not linearly. So if something falls at 100 lbs at 1 cycle it may do 99 lbs at 10 cycles and 90 lbs at 10,000 cycles. Eventually you will hit a load where even at infinite cycles there would be no failure. Most materials could handle a 1% load an intimate amount of times without failure.
