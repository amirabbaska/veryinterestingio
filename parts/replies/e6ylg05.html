    • PyroDesu

      Specifically pointing out any particular wavelength or band of EM radiation would be wrong.

      How a specific wavelength of light heats an object depends on the wavelength in question (shorter wavelengths carry more energy), the intensity of the source (how many photons it's putting out), and the absorption spectrum of what it's striking.

      Infrared doesn't carry all that much energy per photon, but the Sun is an extremely intense infrared source (over the entire IR band, mind - if you look at specific wavelengths, then solar intensity peaks in the blue/violet wavelength). Infrared is also strongly absorbed by water.

      Ultraviolet carries more energy per photon, but the Sun's intensity in the UV band isn't as high as the infrared band. Near ultraviolet is also not strongly absorbed by water.
