    • Legospyro131

      Light actually travels at speeds lower than c (speed of light in a vacuum) very often. This is what causes refraction. As the light moves between mediums of different densities, such as air and glass, it will speed up (moving from glass to air) or slow down (moving from air to glass). This change in velocity causes the light to 'bend' which distorts the image that we see of the object. This is why a straw seems to bend when looked at through the side of a glass of water.
