  • The_camperdave

    The advent of the machine gun came along either right before or during WW1

    Machine guns date back to at least as early as the civil war (eg. Gatling gun). The development of the submachine gun dates to WW1. The Tommy Gun (made famous by prohibition era gangsters) was originally intended as a "trench broom". You got up to the trench, and swept it to and fro, killing everyone in the trench. It was not a weapon of gallantry, but of merciless slaughter.

      Machineguns firing black powder were pretty underwhelming outside of niche roles like close in defense for gunboats or whatever. The invention of a practical smokeless powder in the 1880s transformed the machinegun from a gimmick into a serious military weapon.
