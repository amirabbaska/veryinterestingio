  • Muse015

    What happens if you get tachycardia? Like, I can get it sometimes without doing anything, am I at risk of cardiac arrest for this reason?

    • paracelsus23

      The exact risk depends on the cause. Tachycardia that emanates from outside of the heart (sinus tachycardia) is low risk. It's occurring in response to normal signals - although those signals may have abnormal origins (other medical or psychological problems). It's important to at least discuss it with a physician because long-term (over the course of decades) there can be structural changes occur as a result of unusual heart rate and they may want to put you on medication to reduce the chances of that.

      The arrhythmias that create the greatest risk for sudden cardiac death are "reentrant arrhythmias" where the heart rate is elevated due to incorrect signaling within the heart. These are typically the result of the electrical system in the heart not working properly. Sometimes it results from genetics, other times from damage to the heart. In either event, it is possible for an arrhythmia to emerge where the heart does not effectively pump blood. The chances of this are very low, but it is possible. Again, see your doctor.
