  • jcforbes

    Gas turbines are the most fuel efficient combustion engine known to exist. They have this perception of using lots of fuel because they make lots of power and are typically used in applications that need it. Because they are so compact people grossly underestimate their power output and assume that this little engine is using tons of fuel when it shouldn't be because it's so much smaller than the equivalent diesel, but a 1000hp gas turbine uses less fuel than a 1000hp diesel and both use less fuel than a 1000hp Otto engine.

    Edit: of course this is at peak. Gas turbines at 50% output are way less efficient than other engines at 50% output, especially diesels which shine in this area. Gas turbines are great for hybrid electric systems where a battery can do the work when low power is needed then you can fire up the noisy engine and run it at its peak when you need lots of power (and divert any excess to charging the battery).

    • 38t4uhbf98a

      Non-combined cycle gas turbines are definitely not the most efficient.

      Both gas turbines and diesels gain efficiency as they scale up but diesels beat out gas turbines at every stage simply because of their higher heat of combustion.

      This is because a turbine engine's combustion chambers have to tolerate combustion constantly whereas a reciprocating engine does combustion in pulses. This makes the piston engine less powerful for a given size but allows the combustion to be hotter without melting things, and the greater the temperature difference between the combustion chamber and the outside the more efficient a heat engine is.
