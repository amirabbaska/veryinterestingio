    • mb34i

      You can reduce or increase the speed of the droplet, by lowering or raising the faucet (so gravity accelerates the droplets less, or more, prior to impact), and you'll see thresholds where the impact energy is not sufficient to overcome the surface tension even for the level surface, and also where the impact energy breaks apart (and spreads out) the droplet even for the slanted surface.

      You can also eliminate surface tension and see just the effect of impact forces by using something that has no surface tension: a teaspoon of dry sand, poured on a level surface vs. a slanted surface. The way the sand grains spread on impact is similar to what the water molecules try to do on impact.
