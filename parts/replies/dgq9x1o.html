    That is unlikely. MRI's do not detect blow flow, only blood vessels. MRA's and fMRI's use mostly machinery but also do things like add contrast to your blood then take timed photos to see how it moves. Generally speaking that is difficult to do because of the blood brain barrier and because of the rapid circulation of through the brain, but a contrast that relies on the oxidation level of your blood was discovered, allowing for fMRI images. And fMRI and a regular MRI are not really the same thing though, just because they both use magnets to produce the image.
