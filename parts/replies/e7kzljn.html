  • torpedoguy

    Important to note that those very abilities are where there's the highest risk of abuse.

    Dictatorships often declare such emergencies and never revoke them, using them as the excuse for why whatever they're doing is legitimate.

    Over in the US, the National Emergencies Act had to be passed in the 70s in order to end at least 4 never-revoked national emergencies still in force, and to reign in some 400+ special powers, some fairly sweeping, delegated to the president and other branches of the government under those conditions. Much of those protections were overridden by PATRIOT.

    The Soviet Union dissolved due to abuse of this as well: The hardline coup leaders in '91 declared a state of emergency in order to try and pull it off, using measures and powers set up a year earlier "in case an emergency were to happen"
