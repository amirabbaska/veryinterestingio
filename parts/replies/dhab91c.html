    • mojoman913

      I probably go through 8 - 12 cards per year, sometimes less, sometimes more.

      As far as credit score, my score has increased since I started using rewards this way. If you look at how credit scores work, and how credit card use affects your score, and you use your cards correctly, you'll not have to worry about lowering your score.

      Basically, you have pay your full balance every month, don't use more than about 20 percent of your available credit at any time, and don't apply for too many cards at once. The negatives associated with opening a lot of cards (the drop because of new accounts, average age of account, and new inquiries) is less than the positives of using your cards wisely (the rise because of paying your bills on time and not having a high credit utilization ratio.)
