    And usually they are suited to filter organic compounds. We call the preference of a material to catch other materials 'selectivity'. This is why cooker hoods usually have activated carbon filters. So evaporated fats and such remain in the filter. Since the attachment sites / pores will eventually be filled up, the filter has to be replaced.

    For further reading, the mechanism behind this is called 'adsorption'.
