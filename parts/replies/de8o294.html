    • UWwolfman

      Generally single fluid MHD is described by 8 equations. if want to model electron flow specifically you really want to use an extended MHD or two fluid model. In extended MHD we use a generalized Ohm's law to more accurately model the electron dynamics. You can still model extended MHD with 8 equations.

      There are many equilibrium solutions for single fluid MHD. However there are few known, if any, nontrivial extended MHD equilibrium solutions.

  • swapwaspspaw

    There is a crucial difference between electrons in a metal and plasma in free space though. In the former, the electron momentum is not conserved due to the scattering between electrons and phonons / defects, which implies that the momentum relaxes rather than diffuses. Of course, in an ultra-clean system at extremely low temperature (e.g. graphene), where one may neglect the electron-phonon or electron-defect scattering, electrons do behave like a fluid, which gives rise to striking phenomena such as negative resistance.
