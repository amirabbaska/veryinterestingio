      Radiation oncologist here. You can't ignore free radicals, unfortunately. Most (at least 60%, probably closer to 80%) of the DNA double-strand break damage caused by ionizing radiation is due to free-radical interaction with DNA. Think of it this way: ionizing radiation --> kicks an electron off a water molecule, causing an unstable hydroxyl radical --> this unstable chemical group attacks DNA molecules, causing strand breaks, etc. This all happens VERY, VERY quickly, at VERY short distances.
