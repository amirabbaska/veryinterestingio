  • jonnytsunami66

    To piggyback off of this, melting points usually aren't at a specific temperature and occur over a range of temperatures. This is due to the stronger intermolecular forces (IMF) in solids as opposed to liquids. Since IMFs are so much weaker in liquids, it's a lot easier to break intermolecular bonds resulting in much more precise boiling points. The point I'm getting at is the phase change from solid to liquid is very different than going from liquid to gas. Liquid to solid transitions occur much more slowly because the molecules aren't moving as fast. (Source third year biochem student)
