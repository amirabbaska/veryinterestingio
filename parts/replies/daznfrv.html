      This guy is wrong. When he says "take it out of your estate" he is usually talking about ways to get it out of your probate estate (aka it does not pass under your will) but not your taxable estate. TOD accounts, 401ks, IRAs, and even FLPs are in your taxable estate if you own them.

      To answer your questions, there are lots of ways to do this in small amounts. Having life insurance policies owned by irrevocable trusts is a common one for anyone who will pay an estate tax. Putting assets into into an FLP, an LLC, or a Corporation and then transferring small amounts of it is another one. Also, people can choose to make taxable gifts during their life. Gifts are taxed at 15%, estates are taxed at 40%. I can go into this a lot more (I'm an estate planning attorney), but there are many ways to do it in bits and pieces.

      • centsoffreedom

        Your information on the gift tax is incorrect. Gifts are non taxable to the recipient and non taxable to the giver as long as it is less than $14,000 to a person per year. If you give more than the $14,000 it goes against your lifetime exclusion which is 5.43 million in 2016. The 5.43 million is what is excluded from your estate before calculating the estate tax. The gift tax is calculated on the current estate tax. There is far more detail to this but this is an ELI5
