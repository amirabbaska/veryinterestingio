  • koghrun

    Well-written. The only point I take issue with is your first school point. "Grades are based on doing the things assigned quickly and well."

    In all my years of education, I never came across a single assignment in which being done quickly was advantageous. Homework could usually be done in an hour or two at most. Big projects were given with a week or more to work on them. In college at least, people who finish the test within 30 mins got to leave early, but those who took longer than the 2-hour class were usually given extra time. Being 100% accurate was more important than how long it took to accomplish the task.

    I'm one of the people who usually finishes tests first. this has been really helpful in the workplace. I'm awarded based on the volume of work I get done, and none of it is really hard enough that accuracy matters. Getting a project done in 4 hours, then having to spend 30 mins fixing a mistake is preferable to spending 8 hours on it and getting it right the first time. At least that's the attitude of the company I work for.

    In school you are given an exorbitant amount of time to get things done as accurately as possible in one try. In the workplace, you are rewarded for getting things done quickly, accuracy only matters if it affects the bottom line.

    ELI5 TL;DR In school, they grade you on building one toy as well as possible. In work they grade on volume of toys completed.100 toys per day with 90% of them working is better than 50 toys per day with 100% of them working.
