  • Amazingawesomator

    Regarding Nitrogen, this will not "carbonate" (i know its not carbonating, but rather making it bubbly with nitrogen) drinks. When used in nitro beer/coffee/whatever, there is also CO2 in the nitro mixture. The reason they use nitro is because it allows the beverage tank to be at a higher pressure but still have the same amount of carbonation (vols of co2).

    When utilizing Nitro taps, there is a metal disc in the tap that has tiny holes in it to break up the CO2 bubbles, and the beverage tank needs a lot more pressure to push it through those tiny holes. :)

    source: i am a homebrewer.

    for Nitro not "carbonating" beverages, i have only tested this at home manageable pressures. I am not sure if it will make the drink bubbly at something ridiculous like 5000 PSI
