      1% by income for the nation is $429,000/year for household income. Given the average effective tax rate for the 1% of 23.5%, a 1% household would be left with $328,000 a year in take-home pay. A common guideline for what one should spend on housing is ~30% of take-home pay. That would mean a household in the 1% could spend up $98,500/year on housing. At current mortgage rates and with 20% down, that equals a house valued at around $2.1M.

      There are only FIVE cities in America where spending that much on housing would not get you a >5,000 square-foot house (what you used below to define "mansion): San Francisco, Boston, Washington, New York, and LA.

      So what should our 1% family do with that extra $229,000 in take-home income?

      How about a trip to Europe? Our family of 4 will take non-stop flights from NYC to Paris and stay at the Mandarin Oriental for two weeks. Each family member also gets $100/day for shopping, food, etc. That's $28,000.

      Now they're left with $201,000, so let's give them that second Euro-trip you said they couldn't afford.

      Now let's send both kids to board at Deerfield Academy (one of the best prep schools in the nation) and then on to some Ivy League university. That's $100k/year in combined tuition.

      Mom and dad have to get around, so how about they each get a lease for a brand-new Mercedes-Benz S550. For both cars, that's $29,000/year (and they get new ones every three years!!).

      Mom and dad need food, so let's give them a $1000/month food budget...each. (The average American spends $600/month on food).

      After a generous food budget, a mansion (by your definition), two brand-new Mercedes S550 sedans, tuition and board at the nicest prep schools and colleges in America, and two spare-no-expense Eurotrips, our 1% family has $21,000 left to spend on whatever they want.

      That sounds like a pretty fine lifestyle to me.
