      • cabbagemeister

        Weird effects happen as you get bigger, smaller, or faster. Big and fast things are affected more by relativity, and small things are affected more by quantum effects.

        Quantum effects are noticable when you are looking at individual atoms or molecules, although with some materials you can get quantum effects at a larger scale. The reason they arent noticable at a medium scale is that the effects cancel out and sort of blur into what we percieve as normal behavior. They are physically too small to notice.

        On the other hand, relativistic effects are noticable at any scale depending on the situation, such as a sattelite in orbit measuring time different from an object on the surface. As far as the more famous relativistic effects go, gravitational lensing is only noticable on large objects.

        I would say relativistic effects appear more gradually than quantum effects.

        All of these effects are explained by the same laws of physics everywhere in the universe, and like the commenter above me said, these laws never change. In this case, laws mean mathematical equations. These equations simply have more or less of an effect on the behavior of an object dependint on the scale.
