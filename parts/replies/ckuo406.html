  • Davecasa

    Small correction: In deep water, "normal" (ie. surface gravity) waves move water particles in circles, which get exponentially smaller with depth, mostly fading out by the time you get to about one wavelength in depth. As you move into shallower water these circles stretch into ellipses which get flatter as you approach the bottom (at the bottom movement is only horizontal), and as you get near breaking, the water particles no longer travel in closed trajectories, instead gradually creeping in the direction of wave motion near the surface, and opposed to it near the bottom.
