      This one is complicated because we don't know if dogs actually feel bad about the things they do wrong. It's possible that they have guilty mannerisms because of positive reinforcement. They simply understand that we do not like certain behaviors and/or find them acceptable, which is understandable in a social animal.

      For example: My dog once brought me something of mine she had destroyed and dropped it, then put her head down and flopped her ears back like she does when she's in trouble.

      I would like to believe:

      • She understands she has done something bad.
      • She regrets having done the bad thing and is now apologizing for having lost control.
      • She is a good dog.

      What I also except may be reality:

      • She wanted attention and/or affection and has formed the connection between behaving that way and me giving her attention after, usually involving some sort of a hug or pet and an, "it's ok. I can buy another."
      • She has made the association between behaving guilty early and getting caught and has realized that if she owns up to what she's done wrong, her punishment is milder and I get less upset.
      • She is still a good dog.

      P.S. I do not abuse my dog. Her punishments mostly involve timeouts of various lengths where she lays in a corner away from other people and animals, sometimes while I emit a string of vulgarities over whatever she's done into the air.
