      • Ben716

        It could be different country to country, but where I am from (Australia) the term 'Hay Fever' refers to allergy to various pollens, especially grass pollen, wheras 'Dust Mite Allergy' is where someone is allergic to certain proteins in the waste left by dust mites (dead bodies and poo).

        Allergic Rhinitis is just a fancy name for a runny nose, a common symptom of many allergy types. Allergic Conjunctivitis is another nice fancy word, it means red eyes.

        All allergies are caused by excess Histamine release due to an overreaction of the immune system to a foreign substance. Anti-histamines, to a certain extent, block type one Histamine receptors and can mitigate some of the problems caused by this excess Histamine release.
