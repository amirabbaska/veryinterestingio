  • scary_truth

    Cost is not the only thing stopping us from sending the waste into space! There is also an inherent danger involved.

    Think about what happened with the tragic challenger mission. Although it is very unlikely a catastrophic failure like that one will happen again, think about what would happen if instead of a crew of humans, that shuttle or rocket had contained a full shipment of highly radioactive nuclear waste.

    The fallout from this type of explosion would be devastating. The radioactivity would be spread around the atmosphere depending on the hight of the explosion.

    Although the chances of this happening are slim, it would take many trips to get all of our high-level nuclear waste to space, and each trip increases the odds of a failure happening.
