    • rewindselector

      "On all the time" is not necessarily accurate. Many modern tubes have intermittent imaging. You can choose how many frames per second the machine images. The radiographer and radiologist should not be using flouro during the times they are not actively watching the screen.

      Also, these tubes have 'image intensifiers' that basically allow you to use fractions of the amount of radiation you would use for a diagnostic xray film.

      The xray film needs much more detail to rule out bony abnormalities, whereas flouro is generally during a procedure and you don't need extremely fine detail. Also, dose is inversely proportional to the kVp employed. The higher the kVp, the less dose the tissue absorbs.
