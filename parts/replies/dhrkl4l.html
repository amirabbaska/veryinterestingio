      • straightgineer

        The leading 0 is not used in a 12 digit UPC.

        The leading 0 is used in a 13 or 14 digit UPC.

        A UPC Company Prefix is not the same thing as a GS1 Company Prefix.

        Example:

        UPC Company Prefix

        123456

        GS1 Company Prefix

        0123456

        If you use a GS1 Company Prefix on a 12 digit UPC, you're actually saying your UPC Company Prefix is 012345 and your Item Reference Number is 6xxxx.

  • HowIsntBabbyFormed

    Other than the country code, it's kinda funny how closely this tracks to how IP addresses were assigned, including:

    • some companies/organizations - kind of arbitrarily - getting large blocks (GE, IBM, AT&T, Ford, Apple, and the US postal service all got "Class A" blocks, each of which contains over 16 million individual IP addresses. HP has two class A blocks through their purchase of DEC.)
    • And later companies getting smaller blocks.
    • variable length prefixes (The IETF introduced Classless Inter-Domain Routing which allows for variable length prefixes)
    • too many people needing barcodes (IPv4 address exhaustion)
    • prefixes that are for internal use only (192.168.*.* and 10.*.*.* are probably the most well known private network ranges)
    • Heck, even country code has kind of an equivalent in that there are 5 regional Internet registries for handing out IP addresses in the various regions in the world. If you're in the US, you get your IP address from ARIN. If you're in China, you get yours from APNIC. So this is kinda like continent specific prefixes.
