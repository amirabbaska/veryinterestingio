      Yes, and one pathological situation in which you see this is where the fetus is severely brain damaged and therefore unable to process the "cramped" signals.

      When this happens, the fetus never initiates the cascade that leads to labor. At that point, one of three things will happen:

      • Fetus dies, and maternal immune response triggers labor
      • Fetus dies, and maternal immune response calcifies the fetus, which can then remain inside the uterus for decades
      • Fetus continues to grow until stretch sensors in uterus trigger labor
