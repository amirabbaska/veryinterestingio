      Coming back to a long past thread, but to throw this out there:

      Regardless of the active/inactive ingredient distinction, the alcohol in mouthwash is not effective at killing germs. Below 50% concentration, ethanol is not effective at killing bacteria. It may kill some (reduce the bacterial load) but is not the primary killing mechanism in mouthwash. This source, although primarily dealing with sterilization (which is not what mouthwash is designed to do), covers the topic nicely.

      Rubbing alcohol (which is, of course, typically isopropyl alcohol) has concentrations far in excess of 50%.
