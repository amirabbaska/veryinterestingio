    Are there other systems of entanglement? In the case of Helium electrons, the system is extremely local. Are there systems not local? For example, systems light years apart?

    • QuantumCakeIsALie

      Nobody can say for sure that there are no such systems. As an example, a pair of photons can be entangled and sent in opposition directions in Interstellar space. If the two photons are lucky enough not to interact with anything, just cruising through the void for X billion years, they'd still be entangled even if they're 2X billion years apart.

      It's not hard in a lab to create such pairs that are a few meters apart, that's still ~ 10^15 , 1million times a billion, times farther apart than the protons in an helium atom.
