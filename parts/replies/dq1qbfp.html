  • questfor17

    To expand on this a bit. Nitrous Oxide, N2O, is stored as a liquid under pressure. When you release the pressure by injecting it into your air intake, the liquid rapidly boils to a very cold gas. This increases the density of the gas going into your air intake.

    Once in the engine, the nitrous decomposes, 2 N2O -> 2 N2 + O2 + heat. This is the exothermic part. Just freeing up the oxygen generates a non-trivial amount of power. Also, note that in order to get 1 molecule of O2 in your car, the car had to suck in 2 molecules of N2O. However, if you are breathing air, you have to suck in 5 molecules of air to get one of oxygen. So for a given volume or weight of gas taken into the engine you get more oxygen.
