    All different things.

    OP is asking about the cold shock response, which is the odd and seemingly-counterproductive reflex that causes people to inhale when their whole body is suddenly submerged in cold water. It's a common cause of drowning.

    The mammalian dive reflex is the more-understandable opposing reflex that causes people's respiration and heart rate to slow when their face is submerged in water. It acts to prevent drowning.

    The gasp reflex is the reflex that eventually forces people to inhale when they've been holding their breath for a long time, which is what can cause people to drown in warm water.
