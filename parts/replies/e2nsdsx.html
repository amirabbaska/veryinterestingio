    • TitaniumDragon

      Fatal insomnia takes months to years to kill you. Average survival time is 18 months. However, it isn't entirely clear whether the death is a direct effect of lack of sleep, or a side effect of the neurodegenerative process which causes the development of insomnia in the first place.

      We know that rats will die after about a month without sleep from an experiment done back in the 1980s, but it still isn't clear why they died, just that they all did.

      No human outside of a disease state is known to have died directly from sleep deprivation as far as I know, but it is likely that if anyone did, it wasn't really well monitored.
