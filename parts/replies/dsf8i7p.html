  • Kriegenstein

    Then is the bandwidth that stops bass below 40Hz and anything useful above 15k, forcing a bit "tighter" mix.

    Those are not constraints of vinyl. Some albums may be mastered that way but it is in no way a limitation of the format.

    For instance, the Telarc recording of the 1812 Overture has bass as low as 6Hz that can be played on vinyl, although you need a well setup turntable to not have it throw the needle.

    On the high end, 23-24khz is quite common on good recordings.

    This doesn't change the debate much, but a limited frequency response of vinyl isn't a point at all.
