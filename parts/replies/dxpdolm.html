    • dman4835

      For the most part the animal tests are simply to make sure the drug doesn't have any horrible side effects. But they can be used to help discover whether something is an antidepressant. You're right, you can't ask a mouse if it's feeling sad. But you can watch the mouse to see if it behaves in an anxious manner.

      Scientists who have spent far too much time staring at mice figured out that the mice change their behavior when something scary or stressful happens (such as being roughly handled, or seeing a predator, or smelling a cat, or getting into a fight with another mouse). This change in behavior sometimes persists for a very long time after the stressful situation.

      Whether or not this is "mousey depression" (or to be scientifical about it, "murine depression"), is up for debate. But, we can also just give the mice human antidepressant drugs, and watch them relax back to their original behavioral patterns. So for all intents and purposes, we just call this the "mouse model of depression/anxiety", and some scientists make a career out of scaring the piss out of mice, then giving them drugs to see if they stop being antsy afterwards. https://www.nature.com/articles/nrd1825

      • SanAntonioHero

        One of the model systems for testing “depression” in mice is the hanging tail test- where a normal mouse will struggle for a prolonged period when hung my its tail and can even climb onto the suspension stand to escape hanging. Whereas Drugs treatments or mice with gene knockouts that result in mice struggling less and giving up- are interpreted as depressed. Models of depression can then be used to screen for interventions. I’m not an expert on this field, but learned about this technique during my PhD. I share it for general interest and because i thought it was a funny model system.

      In short and easy words it’s like this mostly:

      Monitor disease -> look for target (receptor, enzyme...) -> design stuff in PC that might work -> make the stuff that might work IRL -> does it work real life to the target?(test it on isolated target in some experiments) -> can I put it in living things without causing harm? -> does it make something in living things? -> and at some point you try it on humans but that’s a long way to go. And an expensive one as well.

      OR

      Find something interesting in clinical trial -> test it further. You can skip a lot of steps by this. A bit like viagra.
