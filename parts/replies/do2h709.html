      The whole "virtual particles popping in and out of existence" mumbo-jumbo is bad pop-science. There are calculations you can do which are representing by cute little pictures (Feynman diagrams) which the mnemonic of virtual particles comes from, but they don't represent what's happening. (And actually, these calculations cannot be used to describe nucleons.) Krauss has done good science in the past, but the "Universe From Nothing" book got mostly bad reviews from scientists.
