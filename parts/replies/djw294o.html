    Your source does not back up your statement.

    Unlike the broom example, where you have to think in order to push the handle under the center of gravity, the bicycle does this for you automatically. If the bicycle starts to fall over to the left, the front wheel steers left and the bicycle drives its wheels under the overall center of gravity. The bicycle returns to upright all by itself, even when you have no hands on the handlebars.

    The reason that the bike is stable is that when it leans left it steers left which applies a force that rights the bike and causes it to be stable. The wheel turns because the center of mass of the front assembly is lower than the center of mass of the rear assembly, this causes the front to fall a bit faster than the back which adds a force to the front wheel in the direction it's falling, turning it that direction.
