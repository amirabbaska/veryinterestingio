    Etymologists think this comes from the word nemy, or “mute,” and that the ancient Slavs called the neighboring Germanic tribes mutes because they couldn’t understand their language.

    This theory is underscored by the term "slovo" (from which the name Slav is derived) means "word" (or "letter") in most Slavic languages. Slavs are the "people with words," while the Germans were "mutes."
