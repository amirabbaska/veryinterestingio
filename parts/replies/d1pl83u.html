    • Daktush

      Relative pressure changes. Going from 0 to 10m deep doubles the pressure you feel and halves gas volume. Going from 20 to 30 increases your pressure by a 3rd (3 to 4 ATM). And the gas in our bodies goes from being 1/3rd of its volume in the surface to 1/4th , the need to compensate is not nearly as great and it doesn't affect your floatability nearly as much. Anything that is not a gas doesn't get compressed and so doesn't care about the pressure

      • zebediah49

    • The_camperdave

      When you are scuba diving, the regulator pushes the air into your lungs at the same pressure as the water around you. There is no pressure difference, so you wouldn't feel abnormal.

      When you are free diving, that's when you would feel the pressure.
