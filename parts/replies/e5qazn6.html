  • ChazR

    1) Overall, a hurricane turns latent heat of vapour into actual heat, so it's actually an overall heating effect. It's transfering heat from the ocean into the air. The heat flows are complicated.

    2) Hurricanes form when the surface sea water is over 26°C (79°F). A low can form at lower temperatures, then move to an area with warmer water and develop into a tropical storm or hurricane, but unless you have a lot of water above 26°, a hurricane wil not form.
