    In this instances, often the damage hasn’t been fixed. When the brain is damaged, it heals like a bad cut on your arm - with a scar forming. Unfortunately this scar can’t function like the original healthy brain it is replacing.

    Patients with long term comas (or even other, less dramatic types of brain injury) basically have long-term irreparable brain damage.

    Source: ICU doctor

    • lowtoiletsitter

      Is research being done to repair physical brain function (no "scars"?)

      • Wordpotatosalad

        I’m sure it is, particularly with regard to spinal cord injuries. Stem cell therapy would be one potential avenue to explore here.

        At the moment, though, we don’t have a meaningful treatment which can reverse established brain injuries.

        Our care for these patients is based around 1.) treating the cause as quickly as possible (like removing a blood clot causing a stroke), 2.) preventing additional brain injuries cascading from the initial problem (like big swings in blood pressure causing bleeds in the brain), and 3.) aggressive rehabilitation.

        Although the brain can’t repair damaged tissue, it can find new pathways which allow function to be regained - like traffic taking a different route to the same destination when a road is closed. The younger you are, the more your brain is able to do this. Rehab helps push this on - this is the ‘learning to walk again’ process.
