  • ArmyTiger

    That's not quite true. That dried crusty stuff is actually the tears and protein that normally lubricates your eyes. When you're awake, and your eye is moving, all of that lubricant goes across the eye from the lacrimal gland in the upper outer part of the eye to small ducts that take it from the eye to the nose. When you're asleep, not only does gravity pull that lubricant out of your eye before it can exit the proper way, but your eye also doesn't move, which keeps it from traveling appropriately. Instead, it comes out of the eye and dries up and boom! Morning eye gunk.
