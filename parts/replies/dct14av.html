      If you mean a cloth with a tartan pattern woven into it, that's not Welsh: it's Scottish, from the Scottish Gaelic "plaide", which I believe (although I may be mistaken -- Scottish Gaelic phonology isn't something I've studied closely) is pronounced something like "plat-uh". (Scottish Gaelic speakers, feel free to correct me.)

      In Scottish Gaelic, "ai" is pronounced somewhere between "a" (as in "car") and "e" (as in "bet") Although it's written with two letters, it's one sound. If you're pronouncing the word "plad", that's actually roughly correct. "D" is usually pronounced a bit like a "t", but sometimes like a "d".

      The Welsh word "plaid" is a different word, and it means "political party", as in the name of the Welsh national party Plaid Cymru, "the party of Wales". In Welsh, "ai" is pronounced like the English word "eye", so "plaid" is pronounced "plide".
