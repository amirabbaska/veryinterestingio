    It's dual damascene; the via and trench are filled at the same time

    It's not silicon dioxide, it's a carbon-doped silicon oxide, sometimes porous

    Lithography is the process of drawing the lines in photoresist using light, masks, and lenses

    The etching is a direct plasma reactive ion etch

    It isn't a buffer layer, it's barrier/seed - the barrier is usually tantalum nitride, not TiN. TiN is a barrier for WF6-based W deposition though.

    In the plating bath, the wafer is the working electrode, it's the cathode. It's hooked up to either a potentiostatic or galvanostatic (or both) power supply to enable plating via ions usually supplied by the anode
