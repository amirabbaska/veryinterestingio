      • Accipia

        Yes, we do. A lot of a lightning bolt's charge dissipates through the ground close through the strike. For us, that means the current moves up the leg closest to the strike, and down the other. Not exactly comfortable, but far preferable to the four-legged alternative, where the current will move from the closest pair of legs to the furthest, through all the vitals organs including the heart. Our hearts are largely out of the path of a lightning strike's current, so we are far more resistant than other animals.
