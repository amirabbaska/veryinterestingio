    • kkfvjk

      Yeah, I agree with u/morningwoodwilson and just want to add a point about your cotton vs cashmere sweater argument. There is food and bad cotton and there is even good and bad cashmere. I've also heard that by weight, wool is stronger than cotton. Cashmere is certainly more resistant to pilling because of its extra long fiber length.

      Fiber comparison aside, you're definitely right in that there's an inverse relationship between a fabric's comfort and its durability when considering thread fineness. The tightness of a weave is a whole other discussion, but a coarser fabric will be more durable than a finer fabric and a finer fabric will be more comfortable than a coarser fabric when woven (or knit, if we're going with the sweater example) using the same type of fiber.

      The reason a cotton sweater might last longer than a cashmere sweater is that the coarser cotton yarn resists damage better than the much finer cashmere yarn. However, if you were to care and handle them the same way over a long period of time, I think the cashmere one might actually win out. So the outcome of that scenario depends on your definition of "durable".

      Also, even the cheapest cashmere from uniqlo feels like wearing clouds, so I'm happy with it.
