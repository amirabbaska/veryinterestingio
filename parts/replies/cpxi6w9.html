    • PerilousGourd

      The interference pattern is caused by the electron behaving as a wave; it's not the accumulation of all the different positions the electron would hit were it behaving as a particle. As a classical particle, there are only two bands the electron could hit the screen along. (Imagine orientating the slits parallel to the ground and dropping marbles through them; they'll fall straight down in line with the slit they dropped through.) Interference patterns happen when waves flow through two slits. There are a lot more bands, most in positions a classical particle moving straight towards the screen wouldn't be able to reach. When the electron passes through unobserved, it behaves as a wave, flowing through both slits simultaneously, interfering with itself and creating the many interference pattern bands. When it is observed, it behaves as a classical particle and goes through one of the slits, hitting the screen at a single point each time.
