  • mikitric

    This is partially right. Food is a fuel source yes, but the body doesnt kill appetite because it doesnt need fuel to stay warm, its because the digestive process itself produces heat. Its called the standard energy of digestion. Protein and fat digestion produces more heat than carbs (this is not the same as heat generated when the calories are burned as fuel). So when you are already hot the body tries to maintain temperature regulation by suppressing your appetite. Source: graduate degree in clinical/biochemical nutrition
