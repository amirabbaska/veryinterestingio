  • AVALANCHE_CHUTES

    I think the biggest reason is that there is no current use for speeds that fast. Remember that the US lagged WAY behind Europe/Japan before the iPhone was released. When Apple came out with its new phone and proved there was demand for mobile web browsing (which required a lot more bandwidth), AT&T moved extremely quickly to upgrade its networks, and far surpassed what was available in Europe. Now the developed world has mostly caught up again. However, what's offered now is "good" enough for what people use their phones for. I get 20-50mbps with my carrier, and everything loads quite quickly (videos, pictures, audio, etc...). It's rare that I think "shit this internet is slow, I wish it would load faster." In other words, carriers don't really have incentives to offer 200mbps internet. Realistically, what are you going to use it for? If next year a killer feature/app came out that required internet speeds orders of magnitude faster, I think carriers would roll out the upgrades.
