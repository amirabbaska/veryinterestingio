    No, they don't. Here's Kirill Sarychev, current bench press world record holder. Blaine Sumner is a bit softer than Kirill, and he's no where near extremely fat.

    And, when you get into lower weight classes, it's even more unlikely that a successful competitor is going to be fat. They have to have the most favorable body composition for strength in their weight range, meaning excess fat is a liability.

    Leverages have less to do with extreme cases of ROM limitation--which is a fairly uneducated take on the matter--and more to do with where muscles tie into bones in relation to limb length. Extra fat isn't doing anyone any favors in raw powerlifting.
