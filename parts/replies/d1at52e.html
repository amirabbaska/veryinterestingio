    Two things- there is ice above sea level, and water is more dense than ice. Do these factors cancel each other out, resulting in little or no change in sea level?

    • charonpdx

      Buoyancy - if something is floating on water, it is displacing its mass of water, not just what is under water.

      You can test this yourself with a measuring cup. Put water in, then put ice in and mark the level of the water. Wait for the ice to melt and look again.

      There is a SLIGHT difference, but "all the world's floating ice melting" wouldn't make a huge difference considering the size of the oceans.
