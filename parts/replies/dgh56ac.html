    Med student here. There are many reasons for the abundance of anxiolytics and anti-depressants. This is because of many factors: Pharmaceutical investment/payout and historical research are the big ones. There is a lot of fascinating history behind research into psychological drugs, that I suggest you look into if interested. An interesting one to start with is the use of lithium in bipolar disorder: No one really knows exactly how it works, but it has been proven to be effective. This sub reddit prevents us from giving medical advice; However, the best way to find which medication works best for you is to establish consistent treatment with a psychiatrist. These doctors can help you reach your goal while slowly altering your regimen and lifestyle, without making you feel like you are just popping pills. Plus, venting usually helps anyone feel better almost right away.

      Additionally, everyone reacts differently to different levels of different drugs. You might have two people who are experiencing the exact same symptoms from the exact same neurochemical triggers, but their metabolisms could be different, which causes one patient to break down the medication into base metabolites and just poo it out, never experiencing a change in neuro chemistry. A change in primary or even secondary drug action might yield different results. Then, there's similar symptomology stemming from different triggers, but we won't get into that.
