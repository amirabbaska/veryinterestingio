      Edit: Looked it up myself. There have apparently only been failed attempted reproductions that I can find, and imo they were not particularly good. They seem to have tweaked a lot of variables to try to restrict the study not to just semi-isolation vs. group living conditions.

      Even then, one found that, at least for heroine, rats that lived together WERE less prone to heroine use and addiction, or at least developed any addiction much more slowly, while cocaine use was high among both groups from the word go, which is an interesting addendum indicating that the rat park results might only apply to certain classes of drug.

      The second study found reduced drug use for all the rats in the study, so I'm not sure how useful that one actually is.
