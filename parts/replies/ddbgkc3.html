    • Maout

      All EMR is a spectrum from radio frequency, infrared, visible light, ultraviolet, X-ray, and gamma. After UV-C and into X-ray, at around 10 keV, it becomes ionizing.

      Most real time or active dosimeters use a gas filled tube under a voltage potential to detect this radiation. Depending on the casing surrounding the detection medium and the type of gas used in the detector determine the type of radiation detected. A standard active dosimeter in a light metal case using a gas like argon would readily detect X-ray and gamma radiation.

      A passive dosimeter like a film badge or thermal pen use a material that changes color under a radiation field and is sent to a lab to be analyzed. Many film badges have an open window to allow penetration by beta radiation. Thus you film badge accounts for beta, X-ray, gamma, and possibly neutron radiation
