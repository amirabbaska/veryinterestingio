      • jhunte29

        Current describes the net flow of charge, not the instaneous flow of one charged particle. Electrons are always bouncing around, and they absolutely do move. If there is no current, the electrons move about but the net flow is zero. If there is D.C. current, the electrons move about, some in the direction of current flow and some against the direction. But the net flow of electrons is against the current. AC current is the same way but the net flow oscillates. The net flow of all the electrons is what current describes, not one single electron. Electrons are constantly moving
