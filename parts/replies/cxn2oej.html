    Parent bodies wobble as their planets or moons orbit them. As the moon goes around, the planet is pulled towards it. The water on the far side surges a bit as the earth is pulled away from it.

    As some additional fun info: the tides should slightly lag behind the moon due to inertia. But, the planet spins faster than the moon's orbit, so it carries the tides forward. This causes a gravitational gradient that is slightly stronger in front of the moon, pulling it along and picking up speed, and is slowly getting further away. Conversely, the earths rotation is slowing down.
