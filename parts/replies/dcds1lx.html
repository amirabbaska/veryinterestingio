    Dont think of an electron as orbiting around an atom but being "stuck" in an orbital.

    Not really stuck, but more that it's in the whole orbital at the same time. Rather than thinking of electrons as moons orbiting a planet, think of them as layers of atmosphere around the nucleus.

    Search "atomic orbitals" and you will get some pretty neat pictures of where the electrons end up.

    Neat looking, but mostly confusing until you get more into quantum mechanics and understand why everything past P looks like space flowers.

    • edguy99

      Certainly the S orbitals can be thought of as layers of atmostphere with 2 electrons in each layer, but the P orbitals are on opposite sides to each other with one electron in each lobe. One aspect of thinking of the electrons as "trapped" or "stuck" in an orbital helps the thought process of electrons "jumping" from one orbital to another and emitting or absorbing photons of a specific energy.
