      Continuous CPR is done during ventilation if there's a device in place to ensure the air goes in the lungs (endotracheal tube, supraglottic such as a King, iGel, Combitube, etc) because it's pretty easy to blow the stomach up instead, especially when you're compressing. Normal rescue breaths won't break this unless you blow like a cheap hooker, but normal breaths plus super forceful chest compression will. In theory, your idea works.     • TDNN

      It's actually very beneficial to do CPR with at least 2 people for that exact reason.

      One does compressions while the other ventilates. This reduces the time not compressing because person 1 does not need to move as long to do the ventilation. However you do stop while the ventilation takes place, as you want the oxygen into the lungs.

      Current CPR rythm is 30:2 at 100 bpm. 30 compressions, 2 ventilations, repeat.
