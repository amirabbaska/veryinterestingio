    To add to the bit about bad and good mutations: There is not always a clear cut between a good and a bad mutation. For example there is a gene variant that seems to be heightening the rate of dementia in old age, a trait that would heavily wheigh on a social group as an individual ages but not influence the reproductive ability of the same individual with the trait. Lately studies showed that the same gene variant may actually give resistance to certain parasitic diseases and thus actually be selected for. In this way the gene variant is both, "good" and "bad".
