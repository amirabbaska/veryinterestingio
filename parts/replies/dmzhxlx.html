  • HeyHeyHayden

    After a bit more research on the topic i've come to the conclusion that it basically isn't possible to have both an endo and exoskeleton, mainly because:

    1. The scientific definitions of endo and exoskeletons basically mean that if any of the exoskeleton is connected to the endoskeleton, it can no longer be classified an exoskeleton

    2. Its simply not viable to have both. You only need one to fulfil the structural role and having both does not offer any worthwhile evolutionary advantages. The extra weight just isn't worth it.

    Hope this (kinda) helps answer the question.
