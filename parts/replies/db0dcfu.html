      • Mazetron

        More specifically, the wavelength of the radiation. All objects emit black body radiation depending on their temperature. Iron glows red when hot because it's temperature increases to a point where the black body radiation is in the visible spectrum. Humans produce infrared black body radiation (which is why infrared cameras can see humans glowing in the dark).

        You can describe the "temperature" of a black hole using black body radiation.

        Note that it can be difficult to define the temperature of a black hole.
