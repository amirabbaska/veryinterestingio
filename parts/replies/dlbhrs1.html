    • Mokshah

      The term regolith combines two Greek words: rhegos (ῥῆγος), "blanket", and lithos (λίθος), "rock".

      The greek 'γ' should correspond to a "voiced velar stop", e.g. in English: gaggle [ˈɡæɡɫ̩] 'gaggle'

    • paolog

      In English, there is only a choice between "hard" g (as in "get") and "soft" g (as in "gem") before the letters "e", "ae", "i" and "y"*, so both g's in "megaregolith" are hard.

      * There are a very small number of exceptions to this rule: "margarine" (which was originally pronounced with a hard "g"), "gaol" (which is now almost always spelled "jail"), and words such as "judgment" which can also be spelled with an "e" after the "g".

      EDIT: "margarine" originally had a hard "g"
