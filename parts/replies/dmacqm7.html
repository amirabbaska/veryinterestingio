    Everything you said is spot on, but I will add one more.

    5). Overhead. Everything takes 2x or 3x as long when you are dealing with a government agency. If you'd normally be able to procure hardware in two weeks, assume it will take six weeks to buy the same server if you are working with a government procurement office.

    If normally you expect it to take a week to get a signoff on a design, assume it will take a month.

    I work as a consultant for state and county government agencies. I routinely double or triple all estimates for these contracts before they go out. Sometimes we multiply by 4x or 5x if we know the stakeholders are slow to respond and won't make decisions.

    Anything you do in a government context just takes longer and costs more. I'm sure the healthcare website took an eternity and zillions of man hours. Most of those hours were billed for just waiting for requirements, or having pointless meetings with people who like to have meetings in lieu of working.
