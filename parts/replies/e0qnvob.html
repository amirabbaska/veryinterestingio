  • TheBeardedSingleMalt

    Electronic Medical Record systems exist but tend to be customized for each hospital.

    Interface programmer reporting in. Some would think it'd be easy but every system has their own method of reporting and storing data...gender, race, ethnicity, religion. Even with all the standards (CMS for example) it's one thing when dealing with DRG or ICD10 codes, but it's another when trying to adhere to visit types, patient classifications, abstracting, etc because those are often very unique to the hospital.

    There are also reason for records about who you are.

    A lot of this can deal with legal requirements or billing requirements, but they do need to know if you are on any new medications or have any new allergies that may not be in their system. If you recently started a prescription of benzodiazepines from a different doctor's office, it'd be very dangerous if this one had prescribed you phenothiazines or MAOI's. If you only recently noticed a latex or iodine allergy they'd need to know before treatment. Them asking the patient this stuff every time is partly about providing the best quality care as well as reducing potential for incidents as much as possible.
