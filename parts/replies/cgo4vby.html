    I liked your post a lot, and I'd like to give an example of where existing evidence gives us a view in what extinct animals may have looked like: cave paintings. It was previously unknown that leopard-spotted horses roamed France tens of thousands of years ago, but cave paintings at Pech Merle clearly showed them with other creatures we know are realistic. DNA evidence later obtained (in 2011) confirmed this wasn't an imaginary scene, there really were spotted pre-domesticated horses.

    With that in mind, there are a lot of statues that show hair, ear, and nose shapes... but if Mount Rushmore is the only evidence of our facial characteristics, ears become open to debate.
