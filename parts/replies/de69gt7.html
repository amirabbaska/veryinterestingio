      • kdrisck

        Al-Anon is not a way of dealing with Alcoholism, it is a way for families of alcoholics to go through the steps for their own personal recovery. The thought is that being around an alcoholic is a lot like being an alcoholic and their tendencies might rub off on you in a negative way. By going through the steps and having the peer to peer understanding, you can recover as a family member in much the same way.

  • kdrisck

    This is somewhat accurate in today's usage, but it really isn't the point. If you think about this shit in the 30's, there were way more active alcoholics than recovered alcoholics. The reality is, if more established members had shared their full names, they would be inundated with calls and letters. So much so that they would be unable to deal with them in a non-professional sense, the way that Bill had outlined it. So they encouraged people to go to and start local meetings using the books, instead of making it a cult of the individual and allowing the guys who were recovering early on to go out and live normal lives. This is obviously not necessary anymore, because there are thousands of people with legit long term sobriety. The point about "outing" people is just not accurate. Some people would obviously prefer not to have certain business associates or friends know about their situation, but that isn't the point of AA. The point is that no person and their success/failure can speak for AA. That means that you can certainly talk to other alcoholics outside AA, and I would venture to guess that most people do, but you can't speak for AA.
