      To quote again from the paper:

      Words with the same form but a different concept. Words of this category have changed in meaning under the influence of sociopolitical factors in North Korea:

      Word SS PS
      /eobeoi/ 'father or parents' 'symbolic title for Kim Il-sung'
      /gajofjueui/ 'family system' 'sect formed by a group of people seeking their own interest'
      /pparcisan/ 'communist guerrilla' 'revolutionary hero'
      /agassi/ 'girl/miss' 'slave of feudalism'

      Words with new form and concept. Words in this category have all been formed in North Korea:

      Word Direct Translation Explication
      /babgongjang/ 'meal-factory' referring to a place where housewives get cooked rice with ration tickets
      /noruma/ 'standard amount of labor required of laborer'
      /gongminjeung/ 'a kind of identification card carried by people over 18 years of age'
      /inminbaeu/ 'people's actor' referring to a first class actor

      These words, which are all characteristic of the sociocultural structure of a socialist country, are simply nonexistent in SS.
