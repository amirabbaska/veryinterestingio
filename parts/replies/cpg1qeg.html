    • Mixels

      The nature of accidents will differ by country for reasons unrelated to healthcare. For example, you're much more likely to be shot in America than you are in, say, France because of the relative difference in gun ownership. Similarly, you're more likely to be injured in a car accident in America than you are in many other countries due to the lower relative cost of petrol in America and a higher prevalence of road systems which support vehicle travel.

      Now, if you focused the discussion on the quality of treatment for specific injuries, it would be fair to factor accidental injuries of that type. However, the question of the nature of accidental injuries themselves isn't normalized between countries, so using it to judge the quality of healthcare will give you a perspective that's influenced by a factor that has nothing at all to do with the actual healthcare.
