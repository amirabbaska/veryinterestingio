    • Wrathchilde

      One idea is that it is similar to how animals are larger in colder environments see Bergman's Principle.

      This is not demonstrated conclusively. Too little is known of deep sea biology to say for certain. It could be the "feast or famine" nature of food falls in the deep ocean, and the larger animals can withstand longer periods without food. It may be the nature of reproduction, where millions of tiny larvae are required (as opposed to thousands) for reasonable fecundity. Or, as Dr. Craig Young is studying, it could be more directly related to the pressure effect.
