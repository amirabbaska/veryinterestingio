    Likewise, people aren't going to repeat numbers because they don't see that as random, whereas it would be expected if truly random.

    Just like with the coin flipping. People can tell the difference between random flips and person-generated "flips" (just picking heads and tails, not flipping). This is because person-generated flips won't have a long string of one outcome (7 heads in a row) because of the illusion that it isn't random. Meanwhile, the random flipping will contain longer strings, and it is what people look for when comparing the two sets.
