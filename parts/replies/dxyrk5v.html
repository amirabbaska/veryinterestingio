      I lived pretty close to the Navajo reservation, and it honestly depends. In some areas, where they would farm primarily had more silt, however only a couples miles down in "Chokecherry Canyon" it's 100% sand to clay, and it's pretty deep sand everywhere. To keep in mind this was in a valley area, where on top of of the mesa's/plateau's there'd be more farming areas, while down in the valley it was almost all sand. If you look at sattelite images of the 4 corners area from Shiprock NM to Aztec NM you can actually tell by the color of the sand on top. Areas where it goes from sand to clay it's a lighter more khaki color, while the darker more coyote brown would be the sand/silt/clay. It's pretty neat.
