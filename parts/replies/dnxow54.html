      • Mic_Check_One_Two

        Yep. For those who don't understand why it would be an issue: Imagine a grid of atoms. If a grid node has an electron attached to it, it's read as a 1. If there's no electron attached to it, it's read as a 0. This would ideally work... Except that quantum tunneling means things can randomly switch from 1's to 0's, or 0's to 1's. This throws the entire system into question, because now there's no way (as far as we know) to make a reliable grid of atom transistors.
