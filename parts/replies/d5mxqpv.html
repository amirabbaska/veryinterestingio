  • Pr0veIt

    To add some research news about the role of hormones. This study found that "an increased intake of protein, and specifically animal protein, as well as an increase in reported food cravings, during the luteal phase [ovulation to start of period] of the menstrual cycle independent of ovulatory status. These results highlight a plausible link between macronutrient intake and menstrual cycle phase." They noted that chocolate, sweet, and salty were all popular choices along with animal protein. (It would be interesting to know how much of that was culturally influenced.)

    Interestingly, this study found that a differentiated weight-loss plan that considered the impacts of the menstrual cycle on diet and exercise made a positive, statistically significant difference in weight-loss outcomes for women. (Note that the study size was small, 31, and included self reporting.)
