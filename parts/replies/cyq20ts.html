    • urtext

      I'm not an expert, but my understanding is moiré is caused by line binning, which is where the sensor can do more pixels than you're recording, and the camera just dumps the lines it's not recording. As a result, your sensor starts resembling a fly-screen rather than a neat grid.

      Same when you dub 4K down to HD. Do it properly, and you get a nice image. Do it through line binning, and once again your HD image is like it's been through a fly-screen. And if your original 4K image already had a bit of line binning, then the effect becomes more pronounced.
