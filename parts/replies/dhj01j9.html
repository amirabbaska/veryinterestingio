    Just to add to this point; because they grew after everyone had cars, these cities often sprawl across very large physical areas. Even the city core is not very dense for a city like Atlanta, for example. So mass transit isn't as practical, because you'd need hundreds of stops spanning dozens of miles. In an older, denser city (New York, for example), you can build one stop and serve thousands of people. That makes it much more cost efficient.
