  • Echo63_

    The mirror is part of it (actually a big part)

    But lighting, lens focal length and a number of other camera related bits and pieces can affect it too.

    When i worked for a newspaper, I had to shoot a subject a few times, and she was rather difficult with which pictures she would allow published.

    The last shoot i did with her i took a whole bunch of pics, enough that she wouldnt catch on with what i intended to do.

    She went off for her tour around the site we were at with her pr people and a few journalists, and i processed a few photos to send then approached her as she was leaving to double check she was happy with the pics i had selected (not really, but she bought it)

    What i had done was selected the best 4 pics, and then flipped them (and added R at the end of the filename)

    She picked the 4 mirror image pics as her favorite, and the PR people all selected the correct ones. After i showed them what i had done, they were all rather suprised.

    (All pics were taken with a 1DX, 70-200 and speedlite in a small softbox)
