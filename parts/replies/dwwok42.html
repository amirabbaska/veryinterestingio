    • ssaltmine

      Just so you know, "artificial gravity" is not really a thing. It's more like a science fiction trope that in space you can generate gravity so it feels like on Earth, but current human technology is not there yet. The other explanations of a bucket with water or car turning on a curve deal with centrifugal forces. You could interpret that as artificial gravity but I personally would just say centrifugal force.

      Any sort of attraction between two masses is "gravity" as defined in high school physics, but that is different from being able to stand and walk inside a spaceship, like it is typically depicted in movies.
