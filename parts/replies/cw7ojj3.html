    • [deleted]

      It doesn't actually escape -- the theory is that at quantum scales, a particle and its antiparticle can spontaneously appear. However virtually every single time they just smash into each other and disappear again. But, when that happens right along the event horizon, one particle will be in the inside of the event horizon and the other on the outside. So one particle will be able to escape, creating hawking radiation, while the other antiparticle falls into the black hole singularity, theoretically cancelling out some particle in the black hole thus reducing its mass.
