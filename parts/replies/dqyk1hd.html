      • Unnullifier

        Smoke (from cigarettes or other sources) are very fine solids, that are light enough to remain suspended in a gas, at least for a period of time. What's being referred to as "smells" are compounds that are in a gaseous form at atmospheric pressure.

        Basically smoke is very tiny floating solids, whereas most "smells" are true gases. Smoke particles are much larger than gaseous compounds, and when they stick to a surface, they often slowly break down and release gases that our noses pick up as "smells".
