    • ookluh

      I'm not quite sure if this is off-topic for r/askscience, so sent a PM too in case this gets deleted. Most residencies are 5 years, but some 7 year tracks exist for those who want a prolonged research experience. The only way to practice both ENT and Facial Plastics would be to train in ENT and then do a fellowship in Facial Plastic Surgery. Plastic Surgeons cannot do fellowship training in ENT, because the scope is just too different from the rest of their training.. I suppose you could return for a second residency, but I can't imagine why anyone would want to do that. If you're interested in Plastic Surgery, I suppose the question would be whether you also want to learn about wound care, other types of cosmetic surgery, and general surgery principles or if you would rather learn about ENT disorders and focus on facial plastics later on.

      As for publications- generally speaking the ENT publications will carry much heavier weight than non-ENT. I can't say that non-ENT publications count for nothing, but I have certainly heard that sentiment passed around before. It really depends on who you ask. Basically, something MAY be better than nothing, but not my much. ENT-related something is vastly better than non ENT-related. As for favorite parts of ENT - it's a very diverse specialty with both medical and surgical components that range from tubes and tonsils to massive airway and head/neck cancer reconstructions, depending on what you fancy. It is challenging and also affords the opportunity for a good lifestyle with not too many emergencies if you don't feel the need to do airway or cancer surgery.
