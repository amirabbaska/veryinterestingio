    • airborngrmp

      LSD is a very powerful psychoactive drug. You know how a sudden serious moment when you've been drinking or smoking weed will sort of snap your focus back to reality? It doesn't sober you up, but you can sort of 'get it together' for a short while.

      LSD is too strong for that. Your brain might send an adrenaline shot to try and snap you out of something that's incredibly intense and disturbing but it will not work, and can even make the experience more intense.

      Basically, you might want to be done with your trip, but your trip is likely not yet done with you. You're a bit of a captive audience, and that loss of control can be quite terrifying. Some people can calm themselves within the trip kind of like a nightmare you can't wake up from, but that you can tell yourself is a dream while still dreaming. It's hard to explain, but standing on the edge of consciousness and...sanity, and being able to look down is a deeply intense experience that can be truly terrifying.
