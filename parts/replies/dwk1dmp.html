    • darxide23

      A lot of things can. Sudden altitude changes, being in a plane as it pressurizes, or even weather systems moving through.

      Your ears can equalize the pressure on their own, but in day-to-day life the pressure differences are rather small so you never really notice them. When the pressure outside your ears changes rapidly, your ears can't react fast enough. Eventually they pop which is just a very sudden equalization. It can also happen the other way. If you have a cold, the ducts in your ears (which are physically linked to your sinuses and therefore your snotty nose) can get clogged up which means your ears can't do anything to equalize the pressure to the outside world. It also can increase decrease the pressure inside your ears too much which causes negative pressure inside. If your sinuses very suddenly drain (like after you've taken a decongestant) you might get the very same equalization "pop".
