    Shameless piggy-backing. Some people who have brain zap also liken them to Lhermitte's sign, as it would seem the brain zaps (like many seizures, too) are electrical/electrochemical in origin. *In addition, anecdotally both have been correlated to head/eye motion in some people. As to what this destabilization is due to specifically, obviously no one knows the answer to (perhaps the GABA theory as you mention, which may be why small doses of lamotrigine are reported to help some people in this situation). As you say, knowledge about how these drugs truly work is pretty lacking.

    A reminder for anyone in withdrawal/suffering from brain zaps. You will improve over time. It sucks, but the brain is surprisingly neuroplastic, and with enough rest, maybe a few supplements, and most importantly time, you'll see improvement.
