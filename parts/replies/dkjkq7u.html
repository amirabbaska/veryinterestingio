  • d-a-v-e-

    Looking from air into water at an angle, the light of the sky reflects in the water and overpowers the light coming from within the water. A polarizing filter helps, as the reflected light from the sky has a certain directionality in it's vibrations, while the light from the water has not.

    If you are in the water, things look very different.

    It's hardly a gradual effect when looking from in the water up. Only a small circle above you is transparent, then there is a hard edge and outside of this edge, the water reflects like metal.

    There are some rainbowy effects on the edge, which spell out that the size of the see-through circle differs per wave length.

    If you are 2 meters deep, the see through circle is still less than a meter wide.
