    We actually have a suite of ecological traits that make us peculiarly susceptible to STIs. We:

    -Copulate for non-reproductive purposes

    -Copulate frequently compared to many species

    -Copulate asynchronously (that is, we don't have a season in which everyone pairs off, and then stops mating for the next six months. Instead, we just wander around mating with whomever will have us.)

    -Copulate using various orifices, exposing our genitals to viruses and bacteria that would otherwise perhaps remain isolated in other body areas

    -Copulate with multiple partners in our lives (for some of us, copulate with multiple partners in a week, and for an even smaller group of us, multiple partners in a day!)

    -We migrate across the globe, spreading pathogens between groups

    -There are not at all infrequent reports of humans copulating with animals, which is great way to find a brand new STI.

    As a species, we're almost optimized for getting and spreading STIs.
