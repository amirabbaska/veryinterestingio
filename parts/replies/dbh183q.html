    • v1perz53

      Most (where I've worked) current work limits were set based on studies looking at patient morbidity/mortality. Unfortunately for medical workers, it was shown that more patients died/more mistakes were made by far with hand-offs between staff than a tired doctor (up to a point), so to increase patient safety, work hours are long. Of course, the reason for caring about patient safety on the administrative end is likely money and not wanting to get sued, but the fact remains, in majority of studies more mistakes were made with 8 hour shifts than 24 hour shifts, because it is hard to explain everything that is going on with a patient and needs to be done to an entirely new team every time shifts changes, and the less times you have to do that the better, until shifts hit around the 24 hour mark.
