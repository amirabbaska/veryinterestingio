  • mtaw

    It's often forgotten in these points that the 'disestablishment of Genesis' did not start with Darwin at all. These things - results from geology, fossils, the birth of geophysics and so on - started in the century before him.

    It was the discovery by others that the world was far, far older than previously thought which enabled Darwin's theory. Evolution doesn't work as an explanation if there isn't enough time to evolve the species we see. This had been known to geologists for decades before Darwin, since the late 18th century. Even if the exact age was a subject of fierce debate at that time.

    So there's a common misconception on the history of science when this comes up, but there's also a misconception on the history of Christianity as well. Because the fundamentalists want to pretend that their version of the religion is the original they act like nobody had ever doubted Genesis as a literal account of history before Darwin. Making him a target and scapegoat. When the truth is that there's been theologians throughout history - going back to early church fathers like Saint Augustine - who've held the creation story in Genesis to be allegorical.

    Yet another point here is that, even if Darwin met with plenty of resistance from many (but not all) in the religious community in his day, ultimately rationalism won. Darwin's side won over most Christians. The Anglican church is not creationist, nor is the Catholic, nor the Lutheran state churches of northern Europe, nor are many of the USA's 'mainline protestant' groups.

    The difference here is that America had a backlash against in the 20th century and turned to fundamentalist evangelical protestantism. The more moderate 'mainline' denominations are no longer the majority of American protestants. It's not just views on evolution that sets them apart. American protestants now subscribe to all manners of extreme ideas that most Christians in the West do not: demonic possession, speaking in tongues, the 'rapture', prosperity gospel, witchcraft, faith healing, scriptural support for the modern state of Israel, and that the end times are imminent.

    It's saddening to keep seeing Americans raised in this environment who think that this is all what Christianity is, despite the fact that it's not only not what most Christians in the world believe, it's not even what most Protestants in the USA believed some 70 years ago, before Billy Graham, Pat Robertson and so on.
