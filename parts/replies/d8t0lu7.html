    I wish there were errors bars on these numbers. I studied chemistry and have run most of these measurements at one point or another.

    Measuring sodium was notoriously unreliable. Working with sugars was just super messy and tedious. And the results were often poorly reproducible when double checked with an alternative method. Heck, even something as basic as a bomb calorimeter had surprising variations.

    We were required to measure with 1% accuracy, and labs often claim better than that. Funnily enough though, I have seen plenty of samples, where the lab results were wildly different from what we could measure, and everybody swore that their results were absolutely correct.
