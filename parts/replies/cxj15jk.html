    This is done for a number of products, including medical diagnostic dipsticks like pregnancy tests or rapid strep tests. We call it stability testing. Finished packaged products from every single lot are kept at a range of temperatures/humidity from cool/dry to hot/humid and the various hot/humid tests can mimic longer shelf life periods.

    Periodic testing of these lots at each condition is mandated for quality control and helps ensure expiration dates are accurate and kit materials work as specified.

      This is the correct answer. Stability of these, and many other, devices and products is tested at different temperature and humidity levels. Things degrade more quickly at higher temperatures, generally at twice the rate for each 10 degrees C increase because reaction rates increase at higher temperatures. This is known as the "Q10" effect.
