    The way I heard it, it was a Francophobe thing way back when. Lieutenant is a French word meaning someone who holds a position when the higher up is away. It was briefly replaced by Steadholder (basically just a literal translation) when we were at war with them, because who'd want to take orders from a Frenchman? Afterwards it reverted, but was still pronounced oddly to make it more 'English-y'.
