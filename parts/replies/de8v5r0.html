    • ViskerRatio

      It's that you have considerably more clout in your personal negotiations with the insurance company than the average citizen.

      As the average consumer, your 'negotiations' with your insurance company are largely just a matter of them telling you what they'll pay for and you dealing with it. The friendly voice on the other end of the phone could care less if you're happy with their service - their job is minimize outlays and follow the rules by the book.

      In contrast, small businesses have account service representatives who get paid - in part - on their ability to recruit and maintain relationships with those small business accounts. While you're still not able to call up the CEO of a multi-billion dollar insurance company and demand they dance to your tune, having that representative within the company who is automatically on your side is a big deal. It means that when you ask what the best possible outcome is, you get someone who actually tries their best to find that for you.

      You might not have encountered this situation. Maybe you haven't been put in that position. But if you are put in the position where someone close to you becomes seriously ill and you need to run up huge bills to keep them alive, you chance of getting a positive result from your insurance company is significantly better because you've got that inside track.
