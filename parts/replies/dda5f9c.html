    No. Most black holes are so-called "stellar mass" black holes. The result of a single star or a binary pair collapsing. They are only as massive as the stars that collapsed to form them. "Supermassive" black holes are many, many, many times more massive than a single star and were formed quite early in the life of the universe. Either from the coalescence of primordial gas clouds too massive to form stars or from the merger of very many stellar-mass black holes or, more likely, a combination of the two.
