    I got sucked into the unsafe to reheat rice discussion... But this is really the correct answer to the original question.

    The ready to heat up (precooked) meals are essentially sterile. There is nothing there to grow until you open them. So they can sit out at room temperature until other chemical processes (like oxidation) reduce the quality to below acceptable levels or until the packaging is compromised.

    If it's dry rice there is simply not enough water to support micro growth and anything that happens to be there will be killed when you cook it. It can still spoil via oxidation or absorbing odors but that really is more of a sensory quality issue than a food safety issue.
