      There is some variation in brightness at a given mass. As /u/wolf_on_the_fold mentioned, the relationship between star magnitude (brightness) and mass is very well documented and is summarized by the Hertzsprung-Russell diagram he linked. A star's mass actually uniquely determines its entire evolution, so just from its position on the diagram we can get a good idea of its mass. There are other ways of working out star masses from their orbits, which agree with these calculations, so we're reasonably sure they're correct.

      We are also looking at the brightness of galaxies as a whole, which contain billions of stars of different masses, stellar classes, and ages, so any variations in individual stars are "averaged" out.
