    Funny thing about this, it’s not that the commercial is louder, it’s that the commercial is set to the highest volume point of the show, so while the show changes volume to add drama by raising it for shouting scenes and lowering it for somber scenes, the commercials don’t change along with the context of the show. This is the real reason commercials seem louder than the show.

    it even happened in non-commercial situations.

    https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Loudness_war

    when vinyl singles records were being pressed for juke boxes as their primary outlet the artists and producers wanted theirs to be louder than the others so it had more chance of being a hit. the same loudness push helped them stand out for radio play.

    it happened again in the 1980s when tracks were made louder to stand out in compilation (various artists) discs that were common at the time. this became less common once players had auto leveling tech built in.
