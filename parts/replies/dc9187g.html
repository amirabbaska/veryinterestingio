    Not only would I hesitate, I would flat out refute the conflation. As someone who works in clinical psychology and particular in the forensic population, psychosis is absolutely not psychopathy, and the two do not present similarly at all. Psychosis represents a break from reality, where the individual has significant cognitive and perceptual deficits. A psychopath may not have any cognitive deficits typically no perceptual deficits, and typically presents as an unemotional individual whom uses others for gain as they would a tool or object. They don't view others as individuals, but rather as resources to be used and manipulated.
