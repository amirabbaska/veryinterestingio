    There's another aspect to it. Some light bulbs don't immediately go dark when the power is cut. Incandescent bulbs emit light by being very hot; when power is removed they cool down but will continue to emit light for a brief moment. LED bulbs have capacitors in their power supply circuitry that (if not properly dealt with) allow the bulbs to glow a bit after power is removed until the capacitors discharge.
