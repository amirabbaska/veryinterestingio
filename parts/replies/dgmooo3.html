    Just to piggyback on your great response, one of the most interesting hypotheses put forward in the past ten years is that we have it backwards: Depression doesn't cause suicidal thoughts. Suicidal thoughts cause depression.

    The idea being that

    “many mental illnesses (such as depression and anxiety) are evolved defenses against the special risk that human cognition presents: the ability to conceptualize ending all of one’s problems by ending one’s brain.”

    This answers a few questions we don't have solid answers to, such as why do anti-depressants “cause” suicide, why the rate of suicide has remained “extremely flat” in the past eighty years (despite overwhelming progress in treating mental illnesses) and it could also give an evolutionary explanation for something “too common not to exist for a reason.”

    Obviously with the built-in caveats that evo-psych is a tricky field to take seriously.
