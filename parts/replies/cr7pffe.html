• burnerthrown

    Adblock does add to a percentage of users who are getting web content without the return that the people who paid for it want - the attention to and possibly clicks on their ads. However the website provider has already been paid so its only the sponsor that suffers, marketing networks.
    Adblock can keep loud audio ads from playing, keep pop-ins from covering the content, and can even crowbar commercials out of videos. A secondary benefit is that pages load noticeably faster when using it.
    I recommend a second measure on top of Adblock: in your browser's settings, under Plugins, set them only to play once clicked. This affects most video/audio ads, and while it adds an extra click to any video, there's nothing like finishing a page ten seconds faster and seeing all the animated ads waiting to be clicked to play. Knowing that some of them might have been audio, or thrown animations over the main page body.
