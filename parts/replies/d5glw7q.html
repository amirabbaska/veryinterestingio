      Ive got a skin condition called eperdermolysis bullosia simplex, which basically means my skin will blister at the tiniest bit of friction so I've experienced a whole lot of blisters and in my experience popping them is always the best option for me. I find that having a blister will keep hurting for as long as you have to walk on it or have clothes over it, but popping it will only hurt for about 5 or 6 minuets. The trick is when you drain the fluid you have to make sure you get absolutely all of it out so that the blister isn't wet, because the wet blisters are the ones that hurt for days. What I do is I make a slight tear in the blister and then use a tissue to push down with reasonable pressure and then soak my feet in some water and then let them dry out and by the next morning I'll be able to walk on them with no pain at all. Also, there's been a few times that I've been hospitalised with my blisters and in those cases the doctors have always popped and drained the blisters, as I think it's much more sanitary to pop them in a sterile way and treat them like a cut, rather than let them pop accidentally and have to deal with the loose skin getting stuck and drying into the wet wound.
