        So who is asking the question and for what purpose?

        When using money, you agree to its rules, for example, you agree that the Federal Reserve has the power to print money when you use the dollar. You agree that no one else has the power to print money, that is called counterfeiting.

        Similarly in bitcoin, everything has rules and these rules are designed such that anyone can verify that a person using bitcoin is not cheating the system.

        By agreeing in using bitcoin, you also agree that the mining system grants the ability to create these bitcoin out of thin air. Since most people agree with this, that means that freshly made bitcoins are desired by others, therefore currency.

        The mining problem is also in the rules.

        The purpose of these rules are twofold, 1, to allow for distributed generation of funds based on effort put into the system, and 2, to secure the blockchain, which is what u/LEMERDE said.
