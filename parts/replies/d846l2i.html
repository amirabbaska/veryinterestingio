  • cal_student37

    Although the overall outcome of all 500+ elections for congress is as or more important than the presidential election, any one congressional race is not that important. A single legislator holds relatively little power. A root cause is basing members off of geographic districts rather than proportional representation.

    Same thing applies to state races with the governor vs the state legislature.

    This is compounded by the fact that in most states we elect an absurd amount of officials: Governor, two houses of state legislature, several state executives other than the Governor (Attorney General, Secretary of State, Treasurer, Education Commissioner, etc), state judges (electing judges is an absurdity in itself), county boards, county executives (county clerk, recorder, sheriff, auditor, etc), city mayor, city council, city executives other than the mayor (clerk, treasurer, etc), and a slew of special district boards (education, water, parks, transit, utilities, etc). There's no way even the most eager voter could educate themselves and keep track of all of these officials. I vote for around 50-60 different offices, most of which are so low key that there is not any information online and the candidates don't even bother posting statements in the voters guide. This system needs to be streamlined!
