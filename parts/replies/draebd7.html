  • BananaPartOfTheBrain

    Yes, I studied this phenomenon. It's called Retrieval-Induced Forgetting. The idea is that in retrieving a particular memory, the other related ones are suppressed- you can measure this with timing subsequent retrievals of the same memory item, and related items. Conceptually-related items are slower to recall if they have not been recalled previously.

    There's a related concept called Accelerated Relearning- this is the idea that items previously-suppressed are easier to relearn.
