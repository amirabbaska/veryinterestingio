      Vaccines work by giving your body small parts of the bacteria or by giving you a broken form of the bacteria. Your immune system recognizes these bacterial parts as foreign and makes antibodies specific to them. After vaccination, if your body encounters the same pathogen, it already has the cells and molecular machinery required to scale up and eliminate the pathogen from your system.
