  • NNoell

    Rosehips, the fruit of the rose after the big bloom is pollinated, are a source for vitamin C.

    Vitamin C can't be made by the human body but is necessary for the human body to live, so domesticating sources of vitamin C is wise.

    I don't know if roses were specifically domesticated for the pretty bloom or because the rosehips helped prevent scurvy (a quick google search doesn't give me any quick answers on the forces behind rose domestication), but they certainly can serve a "practical" purpose beyond the flower.
