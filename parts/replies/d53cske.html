    • Cherios_Are_My_Shit

      I think a lot of people have this problem regularly. I sure do. The problem itself is called tip of the tongue syndrome or presque vu. There are a bunch of ways to handle it, but the thing that works best for me is "pairing" a word with something else. For example, I can never remember the word "mom" when I need to. So I paired it with the letter m and the number 18. Now all I have to do is think M18 and I can remember the word I need. This only works for words or phrases that you use regularly and can never remember, though. It's good for people's names or words like "spoon," that, while simple, don't really have conversational synonyms.

  • jsejcksn

    You can build your own retrieval cues also. The more they relate to data, the better, though.

    I had a combination lock which I used at the gym multiple times a week. The combination was 1-27-11. When I was first memorizing it, I just focused on the fact that the last two rhymed. When I switched gyms, I used lockers with built-in locks for a while and my lock fell into disuse. When I wanted to use the lock months later, I couldn't remember the combination, only something about rhyming. I had to spend a few days thinking about it before I figured it out again, but it made me think to improve the quality of the cues I create for myself.
