    I'm a little confused by this. Can you explain more?

    Five shillings has never been 100 pence, not in Britain at least. A shilling was 12d (12 old pence, before decimalisation in 1971) with 20s (240d) to £1

    Post-decimalisation £1 became 100 new pence, the shilling became the 5p piece (20 of them to a pound remember) making 5 "shillings" equal to 25p.

    A crown, while called a "dollar" historically, and similar in weight to the Spanish dollar, was actually more akin to a British quarter.
