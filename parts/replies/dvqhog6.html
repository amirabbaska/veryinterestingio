    This is not really an accurate description of a leveraged buy out. In an LBO, investors first secure a controlling (50%+) share of a company. They then have the company itself borrow money to buy back the remainder of the outstanding shares, generally at a hefty markup. This leaves the investors owning 100% of the company, and the company with significant debt. Profitability is cut by the interest on that debt, which is what happened with Toys R Us.

      Their role varies.
