    Ex-opiate and benzo addict here. Seven years addicted, now six years fully clean.

    The "once an addict, always an addict" thing is COMPLETE. BULLSHIT.

    It is not based on solid science. It is based on the old pseudoscientific, mostly religious AA model of the "sinner saved by grace" whose inherent proclivities towards vice are held in check only by some sort of external Power (or in the modern secular framework, therapy).

    Yes, there are people who face lifelong struggles with drugs and alcohol. There is even scientific support for the fact that these individuals have a lasting neurological inclination to use. But there are also people who walk away from addiction and never touch another drop, hit, or puff, or even feel particularly tempted to do so.

    The vernacular "once an addict always an addict" thing is a vestige of an era in which addiction treatment was expressed in the idiom of Christianity, which teaches the inherent and irredeemable corruption of the soul apart from a saving Higher Power.
