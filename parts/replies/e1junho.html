    • seppukuzushi

      The GPS application is one example frequently mentioned that has the added attractiveness of demonstrating a technologically practical application.

      My personal favorites involve measurements of particles called muons. For simplicity's sake, imagine it's in most ways like an electron, but much heavier. When cosmic rays from the sun -- or other sources -- pass into the atmosphere, the high energy interactions sometimes create muons, at a predictable rate. The half life of a muon is on the order of 1.5 μs.

      Knowing at what rate they are created, how they behave, and how quickly they decay, you can estimate how many would reach the surface over a given time. As it turns out, far more are detected than are accounted for from that calculation.

      The discrepancy is a result of time dilation. Faster bodies experience time more slowly. This has been verified under laboratory conditions where muon half life can be multiplied by accelerating them to near light speed.
