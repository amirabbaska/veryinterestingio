• quartz-m

    I always understood that the swelling and itching was caused by a histamine response to whatever nasties the mosquito injects us with - is that not the case?

    • itsjakebradley

      Well the itching is caused by the anticoagulant protein itself, but yes technically you're correct about the swelling. At the site of the bite, your body releases histamines as an immune response to the foreign proteins and other nasties left by mosquito saliva. Histamines are an essential part of the local immune response, because they command nearby blood vessels to dilate, increasing their permeability for white blood cells, and supplying extra resources to combat the invasion. This increased blood flow at the site of injection is what results in the visible swelling and redness of a mosquito bite.
