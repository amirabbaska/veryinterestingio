    Shockingly no! This is one of the coolest things about cuttlefish. Cuttlefish are, as far as we know, colorblind! They can only see tones of green. They can, however, see polarized light, which we’re incapable of seeing, and some scientists think that it’s possible that they communicate with each other by putting on polarized light displays that other animals (e.g. humans, fish) can’t see!
