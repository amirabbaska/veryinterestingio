    • tehzayay

      Good question, and actually I got that wrong at first. I edited back in the correct expression which goes like k^2 . The reason is because k is precisely the ratio of the excess to the uncertainty - that is, N*x = k sqrt( N/10 ). It's the number of standard devs above the expected value that you observe. When you then isolate N, you end up with k^2 on the RHS.
