    It is most certainly a loss leader. The warehouse clubs sell rotisserie chickens for $5, which is far less than it costs them. It's just to get you in the door to shop.

    You can truly save a ton of money if you only shop for the loss leaders and sale items.

    Also, most state laws have very strict guidelines about bait and switch. If they do NOT advertise "limited quantities," then they must either order one for you or offer you something comparable at the same price.

      It's not just a loss leader, though. Often stores will take any uncooked whole chickens that they haven't been able to sell soon enough and would have to throw away tomorrow and cook them and sell them today instead.

      It's not expired, it's perfectly good chicken, but if they didn't sell it today they'd have to throw it away anyway, and not that many people will want to buy uncooked chicken that expires tomorrow.

      If they can sell it to you for a little less than what it cost them to buy it in the first place, that's still better for them than throwing it away.
