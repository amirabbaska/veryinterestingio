    • Lancaster61

      It stands for Home Owner's Association. In theory, they are a group of people made up of home owners in a certain areas. It's common with town homes but entire neighborhoods can be one too.

      Their purpose is the keep the value of the neighborhood up. So they usually have rules like preventing you from painting your house, or lawn services, or even community pool, gym, road repair, waste service, snow shoveling, etc... basically to keep everything nice and tidy.

      They get the money to do this by charging every home a certain amount of money to provide these services. For home owners, this is usually a good thing IF the HOA does their job right (which in many cases don't).

      If you're the kind of person that enjoy the freedom to do whatever you want to YOUR house, HOA is not for you. Though you're risking the devaluation of your house.
