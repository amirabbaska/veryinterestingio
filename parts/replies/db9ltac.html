    • udenizc

      Yes there is a movement of charged particles, however they do not travel along the length of the axon. They move across the cell membrane in and out of the cell. So there is a "wave of depolarization" moving down the length of the axon, but there are no charged particles moving in that direction. Instead, the electrical potential of the cell membrane increases in a wave. This is why I said that the action potential propagation is not an electrical current.

    • Kurtish

      One of the major concerns is not that the K+ channels will remain open, but that the cell will not be able to repolarize. Somatic neurons generally repolarize by opening potassium channels to allow potassium efflux from the cell. If the extracellular potassium concentration is too high to favor passive potassium efflux, the cell will not be able to repolarize, and will no longer be able to fire properly.
