    • Lyrle

      A person could get some oxygen that way, but there's just way less oxygen dissolved in water than is present in air. It would not be enough to maintain consciousness, and I'm unsure if it would be enough to prevent brain death.

      I think a common amount of dissolved oxygen in habitable water is 8 mg/L. Compare this to air, which has on the order of 284 mg/L oxygen.
