    I think the HUMINT angle is particularly important here. This assessment was reportedly from the CIA, which undoubtedly has digital assets but is primarily focused on human intelligence. They're always in the shadows and can use a number of inferences, around the type of attacks, the hacker groups involved, the Russian governments response to the hack itself and US media's reports of it, etc, to come to a conclusion.

    This differs from the FBI, which is focused on counterintelligence and a bit more legalistic (with a higher standard for "proof"), and the NSA which is primarily signals intelligence. Will be interesting to see if we hear more about their conclusions and how they compare to the CIA's.

    TL;DR - intelligence agencies have different focuses and different sources.
