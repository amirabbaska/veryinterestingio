  • Primnu

    The more common way of doing it is a 302 redirect.

    Google search results are not always up to date, a website may not directly link to a result that the search returned so you get redirected to something else. Then you realise that the search result you clicked doesn't have what you were searching for and try to go back, but get stuck in a loop of being redirected.

    This method isn't intentionally malicious, it's just the search results being out of date and the website providing a solution to a broken link.
