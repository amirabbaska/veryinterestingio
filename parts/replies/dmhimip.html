  • F0reverlad

    Just as an addendum, what /u/Concise_Pirate said is also true: Larger pots hold more moisture. If a given plant can only uptake x amount of water, any volume of fluid greater than x is wasted. Given a larger, deeper pot, that water can sit at the bottom, unused.

    Though the surface soil of the pot will air out and dry, the lower contents of the pot will remain soggy, progressively growing worse if you're not aware it's fully saturated.

    That wasted water, if not properly drained from the pot, can cause mildew and rot, possibly killing the plant.
