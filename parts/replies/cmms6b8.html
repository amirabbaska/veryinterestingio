      • Linearts

        adding water above an object would decrease the effective gravity as you got deeper, since the mass of the water above you would be pulling you up.

        No, that's not true. If you are at some point inside a sphere of constant density, adding more matter to the sphere at a greater distance from the center than your current location will have exactly zero effect on the gravitational field where you currently are (assuming the addition of mass keeps the whole thing spherical and doesn't change the density anywhere). This was proven by Gauss for electrical fields and is taught in every university-level electricity and magnetism physics class, and the result can be generalized to gravitational fields.
