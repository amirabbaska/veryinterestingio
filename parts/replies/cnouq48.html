    Nice reply, but can you throw in some time scales? How long does it take for a single cell to die from lack of supplies/build-up?

    • CremasterReflex

      Depends on the type of cell. Brain neurons and cardiac myocytes have extreme energy requirements and minimal energy storage, so they start suffering from reversible ischemic injury within seconds of inadequate supply. Irreversible injury takes roughly 4-5 minutes with cell death in less than 10 minutes, at least in the large cerebellar pyrimidal neurons, which generally are the cells most susceptible to injury.
