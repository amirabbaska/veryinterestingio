    You're failing to answer your own question.

    Conditional on the four genotypes being equally fit, allele frequency does not change across large populations because of a simple mathematical principle, not because of an extreme hypothetical where one is significantly less frequent than the other and may confer evolutionary disadvantages.

    If random matching holds (which carries the additional assumption that no geographic factor exists), then both the allele frequency and the genotype distribution are fixed forever after 1 period, regardless of starting distribution.
