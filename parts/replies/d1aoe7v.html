    Correct. Thermal expansion of sea water is one cause of sea level rise. However, the oceans are already melting the ice. In a steady state condition, the amount of ice being melted is equivalent to the amount added, typically as snow that is then compressed. The ocean doesn't necessarily need to warm in order to melt more ice. Changes to temperature distribution can have a similar effect without an increase in heat.

    Specifically for ice shelves, as the ocean melts the base of the ice shelf, if the bedrock slopes downwards towards the continent, pressure increases. Ocean water that was at freezing temperature at that salinity and pressure is now able to melt more ice, without a change in temperature.
