    • trophosphere

      The heart does get bigger in the form of hypertrophy. There are two main forms: concentric and eccentric hypertrophy. Concentric hypertrophy occurs when the stress is due to pressure overload and eccentric hypertrophy occurs when the stress is due to volume overload. Generally speaking, concentric hypertrophy results in a thicker heart but not necessarily larger while eccentric hypertrophy results in a larger heart. Both forms can occur with exercise (depending if aerobic or weight-lifting) and both can be maladaptive if taken to the extreme.
