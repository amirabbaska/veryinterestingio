    To add to this, one of the more common types of poison used was curare, a neurotoxin (it acts on cholinergic sites in synapses, not throughout the whole body's tissues to kill you), so you don't need much for it to be lethal. Just a trace of it would probably be sufficiently lethal if it pierced the skin. Add the fact that a stone arrowhead has lots of micro cracks and pores in it for it to seep into and adhere, and it becomes a reasonably effective delivery method/weapon.
