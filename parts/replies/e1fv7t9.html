    This ruling eliminates "shop" or "agency" fees for public sector unions. Because it is based in the premise that all public sector union activity is political, and thus compelling people to pay for that activity is a 1st amendment violation, it has no effect on private sector unions.

      • pufendorf2

        That's right. Unions representing people who work for any level of government are affected by this ruling. Unions representing people in non-government positions are not effected. I hesitate to separate it out by union org (UAW, IBT, etc) because it's really a shop-to-shop distinction. UAW, for example, has both public and private sector members (as they represent grad students at State Universities). The public sector members of UAW will be affected by this and the private sector members (at GM, for example) will not.
