    • UWwolfman

      I'm not an expert on the different current drive techniques, so I can't say too much about them. But both electron cyclotron current drive and lower hybrid current drive can be used to drive current. You can even use neutral beams to drive current. If you want more information I look at ITER physics basis (google it). The ITER physics basis is a great review of the science of magnetic confinement. Chapter 6 is a chapter that talks about the different forms of external heating and non-inductive current drive.

      The bootstrap current that arises naturally as you heat a toroidally confined plasma is a neoclassical (kinetic) effect. You would not see it in a pure MHD course. Are you familiar with the difference between trapped and passing particles? It turns out that the motion of the trapped ions in a torus is not symmetric as the ions bounce back and forth in their well. This asymmetry gives rise to banana orbits and can also give rise to a net current if there is a pressure gradient.

      An atom in plasma state lost all his electrons, and therefore has a positive charge. Movement of (positive) charges = current.

      • UWwolfman

        Plasmas are composed of both positively charged ions and negatively charged electrons. To a very good approximation plasmas have a net neutral charge. The net number of positive charges are balanced by an equal number of negative charges.

        The current in a plasma is due to the difference between the positively charged ion flow and the negatively charged electron flow. You can have ion flow without current if the electrons flow with the ions.

        In order to drive currents in a tokamak you have to create an imbalance between the electron and ion flows.

        Also not all the atoms in a plasma are completely ionized.
