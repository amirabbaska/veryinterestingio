    Londoner here. Usually what people think is just British slang is actually Cockney (that is, South East London) rhyming slang. This is slang where you say a word or phrase which rhymes with the word you mean, such as "Dog and bone" instead of phone, "Butcher's hook" (Usually shortened to "butcher's") instead of look, "Pig's ear" instead of beer, and so on. It's a fun slang to try and speak. As a better example than what my North London knowledge can provide, here's a brilliant scene from Lock Stock and Two Smoking Barrels.
