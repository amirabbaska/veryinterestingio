    • hatheaded

      Landing softly on Mars is actually one of the more difficult engineering and technology challenges, because there is enough atmosphere to cause objects to burn up if they are entering the atmosphere at orbital speeds, but the atmosphere is too thin for parachutes or ablative shields to shed enough kinetic energy.

      Gravity on the Earth's moon, for example, is less than Mars, and without an atmosphere, spacecraft landers do not have to worry about compressive heating.
