    • Absobloodylootely

      I will not claim to be an expert, but I enjoy reading about this and hence have read quite a few things.

      A key aspect scienists look at is the sexual dimorphism between the sexes. Low sexual dimorphism usually indicates a more monogamous species and a higher instance of mutual selection. The explanation is quite long, but is linked to the investment each parent makes in reproduction. Mammals take a higher investment due to both gestation and a period of lactation. Humans take a very high investment on the mother's part - nine months gestation, many months of lactation and years before the offspring is independent. She will therefore choose a mate who will support her and the child through that. And that entails a bigger investment on the part of the male, which results in him being more selective than males in species of lower investment. And humans have a low sexual dimorphism.

      This has also had a biological impact as human females are among the few species to have full breast also when not lactating. The enlarged buttocks of human females is another physical display caused by intra-female competition / male sexual selection.
