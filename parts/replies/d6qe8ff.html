      This is a common misunderstanding that comes from confusing pigments and wavelengths on the electromagnetic spectrum. The mixing of pigments will not result in the same colors as the mixing of light. You have to remember how light works, and how pigments work. A blue pigment is blue because it absorbs all light that is not blue. It only reflects the blue light. So, throwing a mess of pigments together results in dark brown or black because it is absorbing almost all of the light hitting it, not reflecting any one color substantially more than the others. However, light doesn't work that way. Mixing many different colors of light together results in white light.
