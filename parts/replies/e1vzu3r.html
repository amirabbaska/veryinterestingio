    • luminiferousethan_

      Not technically "necessary". It makes a physical difference in how the photons interact with your eye. And it isn't about magnification of the telescope, it is about how dim the object you are looking at is.

      During the day, everything is bright, even when the sun is behind clouds. That makes things easy to see and distinguish, when there is a lot of light entering the eye.

      Your direct vision is better at seeing color. Your peripheral vision is better at picking up very dim objects where there is little or no light, and that's just because of how the eye works.

      So, when you look at sometime directly, you'll still see it, but it will be blurry and you won't get much defining detail, where as if you look with your peripheral vision, your eye is more capable of discerning a small amount of light and therefor you get more detail.
