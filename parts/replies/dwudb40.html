      ADHD could be a big part of the issue, it has a tendency to interfere a lot with executive functioning (y'know the "get stuff done" part of your brain process).

      I've got ADHD and autism so executive dysfunction is a thing I'm more familiar with than I'd like.

      One thing I use to help get past that when I'm drawing or writing is to sketch out the different parts of the idea in my head without worrying about making it something I can finish. So say I want to draw a deer lounging by a pond. There are parts of that I can focus on that aren't the whole drawing; so I'll look up reference photos of deer lying down, different kinds of trees, lakes in a forest, etc. and I'll end up with two or three pages of completely random lakeshores, trees, and deer. Then once I'm confident enough that I can draw all the elements I want and have the picture in my head of what the completed piece would look like, I pretend the finished piece is already on the page and I'm just tracing it.

      If it's writing, well I have the whole story in my head but it never wants to come out in the right order, so whatever scene pops into my head at the time, I write that down and worry about stitching everything together later (that's what editing is for! lol).
