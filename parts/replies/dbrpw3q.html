    Let me add to that that Supersymmetry (SUSY), Supergravity (SUGRA) and String Theory are all somehow related.

    Supersymmetry theories are contained in Supergravity theories (In technical terms: 'Supergravity is just gauged supersymmetry').

    On the other hand, certain Supergravity theories arise at a low energy limit of String Theory. If you take a limit in String Theory where your string length goes to zero, you arrive at Supergravity theories.

    So in the end these theories are all not 'different', rather hierarchical: SUSY < SUGRA < String Theory

    • Lanza21

      Quantum field theory. Master it. In tiers of difficulty:

      • Klauber & Mandl/Shaw
      • Schwartz & Maggiore
      • Srednicki
      • Weinberg

      Peskin and Schroeder is a phenomenologist or experimentalists' book, I wouldn't suggest reading it if you are interested in theory.

      Also differential geometry a la "Gauge Fields, Knots and Gravity" or Nakahara is a necessity. And last, any of the millions of group theory books out there. I haven't read much of it yet, but Zee's "Group Theory in a Nutshell" looks fascinating.

      West or Becker/Becker/Swartz seem to be the new standard introductory string theory books.
