    • WattWattWatt

      Yes, but this may not be the only reason.

      The music will keep you from being distracted by other sounds in the room, much like brown noise, but the rhythm can also help keep you at a maximum APM.

      A very related example of why rhythm can improve APM is typing speed. A common mistake people make when trying to improve their speed is to have bursts of fast typing when they get to a common letter pattern, only to slow down considerably immediately after the burst. You will never see someone that types >120 wpm doing this. Having a constant pace - which can be reinforced by listening to something with a constant rhythm - can help with APM just as it does WPM.

      Anecdotally, focusing on keeping a regular rhythm brought my wpm from 80 to 110 in about a week on 10fastfingers.com. I don't have numbers, but it did good things for my starcraft game too.

      TL;DR Yes
