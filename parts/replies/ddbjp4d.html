      I am not very knowledgeable about narcolepsy, however, I do know that the rapid entering of REM sleep is due to sleep deprivation. When you are sleep deprived, you do not have as many of the REM cycles (90 min long), but you fulfill the 2 stage 3-4 NREM deep sleep cycles (also 90 min long). NREM sleep is to regenerate the body, while REM sleep is to regenerate the mind. When you cut off your sleep, you are missing out on the REM sleep, which comes after both the NREM cycles. What happens then is that the body compensates for this by speeding up past the NREM cycles straight into the REM cycles, and makes the REM cycles 50% longer. After extended sleep deprivation, the mind may not have fully recovered from whatever activities during the day, and you may remain sleepy as your mind craves that REM sleep.
