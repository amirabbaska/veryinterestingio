    This is the answer. It’s not technology. It’s about expectations and what we have been conditioned to think of as ‘realistic’. Consider the stories of audiences jumping out of the way of the very first moving footage of a train approaching the camera. Today, we would never think of that footage as ‘realistic’ but audiences in the distant past had a very different set of expectations and conditioning. This is the same phenomenon, I believe, that can be observed as video games improve. We can be absolutely be blown over by graphics (for me it was footage of Gran Turismo 2, those cars looked reeeeeaaaaaal) in one generation only to be unimpressed by the same thing a few years later.
