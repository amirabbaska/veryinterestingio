    • DrDengue

      That thoracic motion has nothing to do with gravity. In indirect flight, which you're talking about, paired muscles warp the exoskeleton to elongate it or flatten it, kinda (but not at all) like how when you squeeze playdoe it gets longer. The wings beat because of they way they're anchored into the skeleton. There'd be no problem generating flight. There might be problems controlling it, but they've had bee colonies in orbit and the colonies seem to function normally, though I don't think they were flying freely.
