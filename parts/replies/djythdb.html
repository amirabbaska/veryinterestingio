    Is there an exact "Ideal" ambient temperature, assuming no wind, 1 atm pressure, etc that perfectly counterbalances metabolic and other heat generation? I get that somewhere around 75F (24C) is great, but is there an exact number?

    • hairy_quadruped

      Not really. Too many variables. People's basal metabolic rare varies, you generate heat from digesting food and exercise. Differing amounts of clothing (insulation). Your sweat production constantly varies.

      Another way of thinking is that there is a very wide range of ideal ambient temperature, because our bodies have mechanisms to cope with sub-freezing to hotter than 37C, and still keep your important organs at s constant 37C.
