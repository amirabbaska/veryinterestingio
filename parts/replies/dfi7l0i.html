  • WorfRozhenko

    Just to be clear, supernovae are not the sole distributor of stellar material into space. Most stars do not die as a supernova, and instead release their material during a late red giant phase (AGB stars). This ejected material is later seen as a planetary nebula. Both supernova remnants (e.g. Crab nebula) and planetary nebulae (e.g. Helix nebula) are responsible for the distribution of material which eventually becomes part of new solar systems (including us).
