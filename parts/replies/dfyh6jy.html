      If you're interested in this topic I highly recommend reading Martha Crenshaw's The Causes of Terrorism. It's a little lengthy but a great starting point for anyone who wants to get more familiar with terrorism (why it occurs, how the process works, the social and political effects, etc.).

      Page 386: "The most basic reason for terrorism is to gain recognition or attention. [...] Violence and bloodshed always excite human curiosity, and the theatricality, suspense, and threat of danger inherent in terrorism enhance its attention-getting qualities. [...] Today, in an interdependent world, the need for international recognition encourages transnational terrorist activities...[a]s the audience grows larger, more diverse, and more accustomed to terrorism, terrorists must go to extreme lengths to shock". Attacking civilians in a country like Sweden would certainly garner a lot of international attention, and certainly comes across as a huge shock.
