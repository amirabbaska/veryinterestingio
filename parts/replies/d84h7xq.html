    • ida_tarbell

      Term limits have a bunch of unhappy consequences. If you're a new Member of Congress, no matter how savvy you are, you'll need someone to show you the ropes and pass on institutional knowledge. When there's a constant churn of seasoned politicians, that institutional knowledge is instead kept by lobbyists & unelected staffers.

      Basically, it can have a "throwing the baby out with the bathwater" effect-- yeah, some shitty politicians will get kicked out, but so will smart, effective legislators (whose successors are practically guaranteed to not be as productive, at least in the beginning).

      In theory, we have term limits that kick out ineffective, obstinate politicians-- they're called elections. We should focus on fixing some of the flaws with that system (voter turnout issues, information bias, etc.) rather than imposing term limits.
