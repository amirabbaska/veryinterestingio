    There's no free BPA in plastics, it's chemically bound to form a polycarbonate plastic, which is also mostly used as a bulk plastic, not as a coating. BPA is released from the plastic by hydrolysis of the polycarbonate, not by simple diffusion. (With the exception of particularly badly made plastics with unreacted BPA monomer.)

    This is also not just related to microwaves, and any other type of heating in the presence of water can cause breakdown of the plastic in the same manner. (So heating a polycarbonate baby bottle in hot water is just as bad as heating it in a microwave.)

    You're right about the toxicity of free BPA though, and phtalates are just dissolved in the plastic.
