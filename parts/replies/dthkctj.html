        This isn't strictly true. The reason we feel nausea after intense exercise is the ditrubution of blood in the body. We have a finite amount, and cannot supply blood to all organs, all the time. During exercise our muscles require greater blood flow for respiration to occur and waste products to be removed. Blood is diverted from less vital organs such as the gastrointestinal system, which causes it to void the contents of our stomach as it cannot be effectively processed.

        This is also true for sensation of nausea during times of stress, also referred to 'fight-or-flight'. The blood is being diverted away from non-vital processes.
