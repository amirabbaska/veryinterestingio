      It matters because avoiding pain may be a purely mechanical action. We know that is not the case for humans, but we don't know that for insects, lobsters, etc.

      Insects obviously don't enjoy pain, they react to harmful stimuli by trying to get away from it.

      Is that obvious? Newer cars on the market will predict collisions and move away to avoid potential harm. By all external observations we can make, this is the same avoidance behavior you describe. But cars don't experience pain the same way humans do--they don't experience pain at all. We anthropomorphize insect pain because we want to know whether their experience is closer to ours or closer to a car's pain.
