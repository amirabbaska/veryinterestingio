    Ah I see. So your ear has 3 compartments. Outter, middle and inner. The middle ear is connected to your nasopharynx through what is called the eustachian tube aka pharyngotympanic tube. The purpose of this tube is to equalise your middle ear with the atmospheric air pressure (blockages of this tube causes what is called glue ear)

    The outer ear is seperated from your middle ear by the ear drum (tympanic membrane). Hold your nose and blow, what you get happening here is the air pressure of your nasopharynx rise, and as a result the pressure in the middle ear (because its linked to the nasopharynx via the eustachian tube) rises. Because this air pressure rises more than intended you get that funny pop and "pressurised sound" (for several reasons I can think of, none which I am 100% sure of).

    Now if you have constant fluctuations in air pressure your middle ear is constantly equilibrium with the changing atmospheric air pressure.

    TLDR: middle ear changes air pressure along side atmospheric air pressure. So of atmospheric air pressure changes it also changes in your middle ear.
