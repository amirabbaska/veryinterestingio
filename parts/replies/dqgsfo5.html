    • iorgfeflkd

      Hawking radiation is blackbody radiation given off by a black hole and is determined by its temperature. Temperature in general has to do with the relationship between entropy and energy. The energy of a black hole is proportional to its mass, and the entropy of a black hole was shown by Hawking and Bekenstein to be proportional to its surface area, so from this it's possible to calculate a temperature. Black holes aren't degenerate matter.

    • humanino

      u/iorgfeflkd talked about two different temperatures. The first one, which is very cold, is the Hawking temperature. To a coarse approximation, the wavelength of the emitted radiation is comparable or larger than the black hole event horizon.

      The other temperature u/iorgfeflkd mentioned is that of the infalling matter accelerated towards the hole. That matter is outside and the temperature is defined as usual here for a gas.
