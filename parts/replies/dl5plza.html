    Yes, this^ Also it is important to understand how broad the term health is. Fitness, as you are referring to with weight gain/loss, is just one aspect of health. Example: identical twins with exactly the same body mass, fat-muscle-bone density (etc.) ratio. Both eat the same number of calories per day. Twin A eats mostly pop tarts and fast food. Twin B eats mostly veggies and natural produce. Although their body compositions remain nearly identical, the risk factors for diseases increase and energy levels decreases for Twin A. A will likely have difficulty with digestion and feel sluggish. Body composition impacts overall health, but does not determine it.
