    • ramennoodle

      The latter is a "perpetual solar eclipse", regardless of what the OP meant. Forget about rotation for a minute: if some body is perpetually between the sun and the earth then the earth is perpetually undergoing a solar eclipse. If the eclipsing body is casting its shadow on the Earth then the Earth is seeing an eclipse.

      EDIT: The rotation of the earth causes the sun to rise and set but the sun would still be eclipsed throughout the day at whatever angle relative to the earth that it appears to be for you, as the eclipsing object is still between the Earth and the sun.
