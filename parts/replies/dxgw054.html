    • AirborneRodent

      That's a different test. When they dilate the pupil, they're looking at the retina to see if it has any health problems: detachment, blind spots, etc.

      The scope that estimates your prescription does not require dilating the pupil. It's usually the first machine they make you look into after you leave the waiting room and go into the back. It's the one that has a little picture of a balloon or a cross or an X that whirrrs in and out of focus for a few seconds.

        What he is likely referring to is a retinoscope.
