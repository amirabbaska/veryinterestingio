      That's not a problem with the comparison so much as it's a problem with trying to extrapolate the comparison to every possible scenario - which would be a silly thing to do. There are a lot of reasons we can't go full renewable yet anyway - location is certainly one, storage is another, production capacity a third, transmission capacity a fourth, and so on.

      What the study shows is that solar is so much better than fossil fuels that we should use it wherever feasible - but that doesn't do much for the places where it isn't yet feasible.

      In theory, I'm also in favor of fission for electrical grid base load. But in practice, I'm a lot less sure. The technology is sound, with most engineering problems long solved - but the people problems are, as usual, the limiting factor.
