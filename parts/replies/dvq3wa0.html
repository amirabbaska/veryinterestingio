    • doreibo

      I tested whatever I could get my hands on in the dog park and tbh my testing wasn't rigorous. It was only a highschool experiment afterall. As to the experiments conducted, each instance taints the likelihood of a trained response of the next test so repeated testing often results in a trained paw preference, the same for if you test a preference based on 'shake'. I don't have the source for the experiment run in the 60's cuz it was a newspaper clipping but I remember they noticed a huge discrepancy for their Dalmatian population with 80ish percent being preferential to their left of the females and only 60 percent of the males. Other species had varying results but were noticeably skewed to the left preferential.
