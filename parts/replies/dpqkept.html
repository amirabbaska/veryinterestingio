      Nope, that's literally how it's done. I've never done the process myself, so there's most likely more that goes into it, but when graphene was first "discovered" that's how it was made: scotch tape.

      In a way, the R2R process is the much more complicated version. You've got the graphene on one surface (in my case, copper) and you want to essentially peel it off of the copper and onto some other, more useful surface.

    • Metzeten

      Novoselov and Geim specifically used Selotape the first time.

      In current research, exfoliation techniques are usually reference to sonication, chemical oxidation and / or mechanical shearing forces now. No-one works with tape!

      Fun fact: one of the main chemical exfoliation protocols, which has been iterated on to produce graphene oxide was written in 1954 - they were literally one step away from "discovering" monolayer carbon half a century early.
