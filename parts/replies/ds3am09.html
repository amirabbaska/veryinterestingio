  • reinchelien

    This is true in theory but often not in practice.

    The problem is figuring out when “long enough” is for some foods (like raw meats) because microwaves have a hard time heating evenly.

    Other foods aren’t strictly safe to cook endlessly because steam builds up inside them and they can explode.

    For example, you can heat water up to the point that it is superheated and it will flash boil when you move the container. Similarly, you can microwave raw chicken and have some parts cooked and others still raw. If you didn’t happen to put a thermometer in the right place you wouldn’t know it until you’ve cut into the chicken.
