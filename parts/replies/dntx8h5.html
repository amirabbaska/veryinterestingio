    • Charyou-Tree

      To offer another explanation:

      Think about what individual air molecules are doing. At any given moment, air molecules are hitting you from all directions and bouncing off. Each collision exerts a little bit of force on you, and added together across all of the molecules, the total force per area is what we call atmospheric pressure. There's no reason to expect that air molecules should hit you from any particular direction; they're just flying around randomly bouncing off of solid surfaces and each other, so the pressure on any side of you should be the same, because its getting hit by air molecules just as often from every side.
