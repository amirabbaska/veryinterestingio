      I don't know the details of the issue, but there's basically two possibilities.

      1. Something about chromosome 21 makes it more "sticky" so that it doesn't always split in two when sex cells are forming, leading to the 2 + 1 = 3 situation.
      2. When other trisomies occur, they're usually more immediately fatal or cause the embryo to not be able to progress further, so we never "meet" these people.

      Note that Trisomy 13 and Trisomy 18 are also known to occur, just not as often as Trisomy 21.
