    • Doctor_Oceanblue

      Before I was diagnosed with major depression, I had a very similar problem. Nausea and vomiting every single day for no apparent reason. Eating would make it stop, but I had no appetite and all food looked disgusting, so I'd force myself to eat fruit. Turns out that it was entirely attributable to my depression. It stopped after I started taking an SSRI (selective serotonin reuptake inhibitor). Serotonin controls a plethora of bodily functions, so not having enough of it (the most likely root cause of clinical depression) affects more than just your mood, including digestion and body temperature regulation (you may feel cold all the time.) Some people with depression such as myself feel the physiological effects of depression more severely.
