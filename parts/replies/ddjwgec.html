    Hmm, I'm not convinced. The page you linked makes no mention of spheres, and in a brief search it seems Archimedes is usually credited as the first person to give "the volume of a sphere" (though that phrase is badly vague). Really, the formula we have in mind is V=4/3 pi r^3, which relates the volume of a sphere to that of a cube. In this form it's as recent as Euler in the 1700's. Archimedes on the other hand just related the volume of a sphere to that of a cylinder. I've seen nothing to suggest that relation was already known before him.
