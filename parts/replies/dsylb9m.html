    Does the body attempt to grow new tissue on damaged organs? Can this lead to medical complications?

    • sulaymanf

      It does to a limited extent. It can regenerate nerves up to a certain length and it can rebuild tissue in the gut. Often it scars over though. One of the big side effects of any abdominal surgery is it formed “adhesions” which are basically scars that stick to other organs, that can be felt months or years afterwards and can cause obstructions and are sometimes serious enough to justify reopening a person to cut them out.
