      /u/SQLDave's point is that the actual result of a coin flip as well as the distribution of results in available gifs is unimportant here. Even if every single coin-flipping video on the internet revealed heads, if you don't know that fact then it doesn't affect the probability of your guess being correct. Without prior knowledge of the distribution, you should reasonably assume a flat distribution, which means your guesses should also be flatly distributed. So you'll have a 50/50 chance of selecting heads and being right. The same is true for every other potential distribution of coin-flipping gifs.
