  • Valid_Argument

    I feel like this is the inevitable solution to shear in the artificial units. A single pump has to push too fast and swirls the blood too much, but if you could get multiple pumps, say one to the head, one for the organs, one to each extremity, then they could move blood pretty slow and still suffice. That's basically what the heart does but it regulates by varying vessel size and stuff instead. They can all share a master exchange for oxygen and have some sort of failover where they can take over some work for each other in event of failure.
