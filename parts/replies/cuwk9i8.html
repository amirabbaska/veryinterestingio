    Since L-DOPA causes dopamine down-regulation with continuous usage and may be addictive , how come it's still sold as a supplement and not regulated ?

    • ElBalubaerMOFO

      Since I am not from the US, I am not informed about the specific regulations that apply there - in most european countries including the UK it is regulated.

      However, L-DOPA in itself is simply hydroxylized tyrosine, i.e., something naturally occurring in the body and closely related to an amino acid. Furthermore, simple ingestion of L-DOPA does not have strong effects on the central nervous system, as the vast majority of it will be metabolized (decarboxylized) before crossing the blood-brain barrier and leads to peripheral dopamine increases causing mostly unpleasant side effects. However, combining L-DOPA with a decarboxylation inhibitor (like benserazide or carbidopa) prevents this metabolization and leads to increases of L-DOPA in the brain. These combinations should be regulated in most countries, including the US, however the names of the combination drugs can be confusing, as one is called 'levodopa' - which could easily be confused with the actual, pure L-DOPA.
