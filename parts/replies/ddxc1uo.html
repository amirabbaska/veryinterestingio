      • BurningBrightly

        It's not quite that terrible for most people who take it.

        After a thrombosis it's a good idea to reduce coagulation for a bit to prevent another one. At that level people will notice that small wounds bleed far longer than before, and an accident would get ugly faster, but they don't usually just start bleeding at random.

        Some people need their coagulation reduced a lot further, f.ex. after getting a mechanical heart valve. It's not so much that the drug itself is dangerous, the patients are balancing on the narrow ledge between an embolus caused by the heart valve and spontaneous bleeding (mostly gastrointestinal, not always easy to notice before it's an emergency) . At that point there really isn't much room for error anymore.
