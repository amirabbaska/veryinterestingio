    • AlexandreZani

      Anosmia is the loss of the sense of smell. Smell is how you perceive flavor. Taste specifically refers to how salty, sweet, acidic or bitter something is. Taste is something you sense using sensors on your tongue.

      So someone with anosmia will be able to tell you food is salty, sweet, acidic or bitter, but that's it.

    • cewcewcaroo

      Anosmiacs (hi! congenital here) can only taste sweet, salty, bitter, sour, and umami. So we can distinguish between foods, an apple does not taste like a steak. We can't detect flavorings for the most part though. The best example I can give is that all Starburst flavors taste the same. Skittles are the same way, and we can't detect herbs at all. I've noticed that the flavours I can pick up on aren't close to what they're supposed to be at all, cherry and blackberry flavours don't taste like cherries and blackberries, they don't even taste fruity.

      Congenital anosmiacs are really good at picking up on tastes in food so we don't feel like we're really missing out on tasting. However, anosmiacs that lose their sense of smell later in life often feel like all food is bland and has no taste anymore as they are not as tuned in on tasting just flavourtaste.
