    There are some really great stories from the Cold War about Soviet reverse-engineering. They did great with big complex mechanical devices â€” they were able to dissect a B-29, figure out the key materials and tolerances and functions, then come up with their own version that they could adapt to their own manufacturing methods and constraints (the Tupolev Tu-4).

    But other things were really hard. Information technology was just not easy to deal with. Complex circuits don't require just knowing what was made (which you can get by just getting your hands on one), it requires having the know-how to operate the foundries in which they were made. And that was very tightly-held information, nothing that could be just smuggled out. So the Soviets would smuggle out computer chips, and could use those chips, but didn't have the ability to make their own. That was something you couldn't really "steal."
