      Typically when you're court ordered to AA/NA, you have to bring a paper that one of the homegroup members would sign or you have to describe in a note book / journal what took place during the meeting or what the meeting was about. Yes people can and do lie about their meeting attendance and they get away with it. It really has to do with how much a person wants to be sober. I'm not sure about other cities but the one I live in, skipping and lieing about meetings will get people jammed up if their court ordered since there's a pretty good chance the PO has other clients that attend the same meeting. No they won't say "oh yea so and so wasn't there" but if two clients come in saying they attended the same meetings but both describe the meeting differently, chances are one of them didn't go.
