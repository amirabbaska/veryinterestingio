    Technically, the "true" tarantula is a wolf spider (Lycosa tarantula) which is native to Mediterranean Europe. People started using the term "tarantula" to descibe any large spider they were unfamiliar with, especially when Europeans started colonising the New World, and it's now stuck as that group of spiders' (Theraphosidae) common name.

        The scientific name in this case is kind of interesting with the spider; the vernacular name for the species of spider was "tarantula/tarantola/tarantella", derived from a nearby city in Italy called Taranto, where apparently this spider was quite common (also the origin of the "tarantella" dance. Linnaeus just followed this with the species name when he described it in 1758.

        To add extra confusion, there was a genus of Amblypygid also called Tarantula - since absorbed into the genus Phrynus IIRC. Fun fact.
