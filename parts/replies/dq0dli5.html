    Also, if pure hydrogen is put in a canister of pure oxygen, then will are they able to form H2O over time, without burning?

    • Tookie2359

      Depends on your rate of reaction. Even at low temperatures, over long periods of time water will form from its constituent molecules. It’s just that formation of water gives out loads of energy, and the hydrogen atoms want to use that energy to form more bonds with oxygen, creating a chain reaction that outputs loads of energy that we call “combusting”.
