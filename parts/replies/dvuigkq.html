  • eksyneet

    actually, when people wake up naturally (i.e. without an alarm), it usually happens during or right after a REM stage. that's because in non-REM bodily functions all go down (BP, temperature, breathing rate etc.) and then in REM go up again, almost to awake levels. so the idea that waking during REM causes extra tiredness is a misconception. sleep inertia is greatest when awoken during slow wave sleep (deep non-REM sleep).
