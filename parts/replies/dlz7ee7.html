    Talc mines in various parts of the country have contained asbestos post-1970. Gouverneur Talc, Engelhard (Eastern Magnesia) to name a couple. I assume you got the 1970 date because of OSHA but they didn't really have a hand in talc exposure and it being contaminated with asbestos.

    J&J owned the Johnson Mine in Vermont and sold it to Eastern Magnesia in 1967. That mine has been confirmed to contain tremolite (asbestos). J&J continued to operate their Hammondsville Mine near Reading, VT, for years after 1967 as their Windsor Minerals subsidiary. It was sold to Cyprus in the late 1980s but they Cyprus agreed to keep selling talc from this mine for years after.

    The expert reports by geologists on the plaintiffs and defendants side do play a huge role in this litigation and affect the jury's perception of who's at fault. The main argument between experts is what constitutes an asbestos fiber when viewing it through a microscope using TEM. When you look at a suspected sample using TEM, is it an asbestiform particle (fibrous) or is it a cleavage fragment from a mass sample that just splintered off to resemble asbestiform particles?

    Cosmetic talc products have never been comprehensively inspected by the FDA. Just look at the recent report about cosmetic imports. It's increased exponentially over the last couple decades and they don't have the manpower to fully inspect or analyze what's in this stuff. And we're dabbing it on our face and everywhere else.
