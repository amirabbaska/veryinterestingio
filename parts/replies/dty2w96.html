        Carbon dioxide the substance is not inherently be a toxin, but in this process it stems from a biological process making it one.

        No. A toxin is something that interferes with biological processes. The toxin itself is produced by the biological processes of another creature. Your body does not produce things that are toxins in your own body.

        There are things - again, like feces - that can have ill effects if they are suddenly relocated from one place to another, but again, that's not the same thing as a "toxin." Feces has bacteria in it, and those bacteria do bad things in the wrong environment.

        But bacteria are living things that create an infection, not a simple substance that interferes with the biological processes in your cells like, say, HF.
