    The idea that scientists in the '70s and '80s thought there would be an ice-age is a myth. Like any good myth there is some truth to it, and it had been perpetuated by climate deniers. Sulphur particles from coal-fired power stations were having a cooling effect on the globe, while the CO2 was warming. Scientists knew of both these effects. I believe most considered CO2 to be the stronger effect, but the most unpredictable factor was what society would do about it. The sulphur particles had a secondary effect of causing acid rain - a very visible problem - and laws were passed to scrub the sulphur from coal stations. Here's a good article about it: https://arstechnica.com/science/2016/06/that-70s-myth-did-climate-science-really-call-for-a-coming-ice-age/ and here's a more comprehensive one http://journals.ametsoc.org/doi/pdf/10.1175/2008BAMS2370.1
