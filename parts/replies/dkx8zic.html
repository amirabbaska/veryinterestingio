      • gnorty

        I expect carbon fiber would be dangerous because it would not conduct.

        Carbon (in it's graphite form) is a good conductor of electricity- they ake resistors out of it for example. The resin that binds the fibres will insulate (so you wont see it with a multimeter) but once that is broken down and the current reaches the actual carbon fibres the car body will conduct pretty well. You cannot use it very easily for a chassis ground like you can in a metal car, but that is due to the resin, NOT the carbon. Carbon fishing rods can be extremely dangerous if used under power lines or in an electrical storm.

        In a lightning strike, a carbon body will protect you as well as a metal one (although the body might well get destroyed in the process).
