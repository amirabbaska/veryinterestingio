  • thelokester

    If the outermost nucleons are not bound to the nucleus in the case of large |N - Z|, in what manner do they occupy space? Do they exist as probabilistic clouds like electrons, even though they have much higher mass? What theory governs this?

    • RobusEtCeleritas

      All nucleons exist in probabilistic clouds like electrons. The difference is whether the particle is in a bound state or a resonant state. Bound states stay localized within the nucleus forever while resonant states will escape with time (the survival probability decays exponentially with time).

      Beyond the dripline, the outermost unbound nucleons are in resonant states.
