    Impurities do not "cause" a hangover. Ethanol is converted in the body to a toxic compound called acetaldehyde, then to acetic acid. When too much alcohol is consumed, the body experiences a buildup of acetaldehyde in the race to metabolize it despite more consumption of its metabolic precursor.

    Source: basic Wikipedia knowledge https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethanol_metabolism

    Had to be anal on the science of the matter; can't let misinformation spread unhindered. Although impurities may contribute to the effects (such as sugars and whatnot) in whiskey, it's surely not the main cause. The main cause is ethanol's inherent toxicity!

  • cdb03b

    Impurities have an effect on hangovers, but not much of one. The alcohol itself is first converted into acetaldehyde, then into acetic acid. Hangovers occur when your body is not able able to process the acetaldehyde fast enough and it builds up causing damage as it is toxic. So volume of alcohol is the primary issue for a hangover, not impurities.

    I've done the full Bourbon Trail twice and take occasional day trips to other bourbon distilleries. Distilling the whiskey twice is the minimum and 3 or more times is not uncommon. Brown liquors gain their color from the barrels they are aged in. Bourbon's "impurities" are the sugars it absorbs from the charred oak it is aged in and has nothing to do with the hangover. The hangover is caused by toxic metabolites alcohol is broken down into by the body.
