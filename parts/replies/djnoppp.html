    • LerrisHarrington

      The problem isn't the fine, a monetary disincentive is a fine idea.

      The problem is the fine going to the issuing agency directly.

      This sets up two problems, it gives them a source of income, and therefor an ability to spend not overseen by legislators. And it gives them an incentive to find a way to fine you, justified or not.

      Fines should be paid directly back to a municipal or state body and not directly earmarked for the department that issued them.

      This re-establishes oversight over the departments budget, and removes the incentive to hand out frivolous, or trumped up fines.
