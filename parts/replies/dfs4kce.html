      • edman007-work

        Functionally it won't actually take more space, it's already in a spot with a circular region reserved for the lighted area. The real reason is cost, it makes the electronics about 33% more expensive and there are other orientation dependent limitations that start coming into play (the pixel layout starts mattering, both the display and CCD actually have red, blue and green pixels, and the best picture is obtained when you match the order of the CCD to the order of the display, you'll get weird artifacts if you're mixing vertical and horizontal orders). Also CCDs are generally read in entire rows and read out sequentially like that, flipping them is an expensive operation and can drastically slow down the camera (can't even start processing the frame until the entire frame is done reading, then you can flip it, then process it).

        Overall the whole problem is just something that adds significant costs and would only provide a minor benefit, when rotating the phone with your hands works just fine.
