    You can train yourself to identify dreams. It can be difficult at first to "remember" the tells while you're dreaming but it's empowering.

    Classic ones include looking for an analog clock (time will be frozen or moving wildly) and pushing a couple fingers against the open palm of your other hand (pressure will feel very different or non existent). Looking for mirrors is another good tactic.

    It's a bit creepy when you feel your brain "fighting" you to keep the Matrix believable.

    • PencilvesterStallone

      I have really wild dreams so I tried to find ways to allow myself more autonomy when I am in them, lucid dreaming. The thing that worked for me was to stop in the hour every hour of the day and say out loud in my head, "Am I awake or am I sleeping."

      I did this for almost a week, and then every time I was dreaming this weird booming voice would echo in the background and all of a sudden I would stop look around and realize, fuck, I'm actually in a dream right now. For me it offers a nice easy transition into becoming aware of it. Might not work for everyone but it's worth a try if you want to experience it.
