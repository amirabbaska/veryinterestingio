    It's not just that the slab is refrigerated, because you wouldn't use, say, a refrigerated piece of wood. It matters that marble has a high specific heat, which means that it can store more heat (or coldness, in this case) and so it gets less warm when exposed to the air. This allows it to keep the ice cream colder when its in use.

    There are plenty of other materials that have high specific heat (e.g., anything that feels hot when you touch it on a hot day and cold when you touch it in medium weather), and any of these things which are also non-porous would work. Alternatively, something conductive which could have a refrigeration unit right underneath it (e.g., a metal casing) would work well too.

    But the point is, it isn't just the refrigeration, the material matters too.
