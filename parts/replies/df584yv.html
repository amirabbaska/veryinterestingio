      They do and don't...

      Doctorate in relevant sciences here: They don't in the way those terms are usually used. Usually when people say "underground river" they actually think "river in a subterranean tunnel". However most groundwater is just saturated soil or rock with enough holes (pores) for the water to flow through (sometimes very very slowly). In these cases the ground water often still flows to somewhere lower elevation, like to a surface river or the sea.

      There are a few actual underground streams and pools, especially in limestone terrain. However, they're in relatively few regions, and accounts for very little groundwater.
