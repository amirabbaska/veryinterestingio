    • morphinedreams

      Almost certainly.

      You get smaller, more isolated waves approaching the 10m height in regions of the world such as the southern ocean. Most of the larger sea-faring craft can handle those large waves, when they occur. Frequent large waves would likely cause some structural damage to the hull (every so often there's one large boat in the southern ocean that has to return home lest they risk sinking in another storm due to cracked hulls), but a tsunami is not usually a series of 10m waves.

      All of that is theoretical though, because waves of that height tend to only be reached as the wave approaches shore, I just think it's worth mentioning that ships of that size can withstand some rather brutal wave action.
