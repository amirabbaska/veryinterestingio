    • IcyKettle

      It's called Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery (FESS) and it's very common. They clean you out and then widen your sinus openings so they drain better. Several of my friends had it done before me and they all highly recommended it. Changed their lives. I don't actually know if it's really something they do just because of sneezing. It's for people who get chronic sinus infections and headaches and are always getting sick. But if you do have sinus problems, I'm guessing sneezing is a common symptom. It certainly was for me.

      Because of my deviated septum, I couldn't naturally breathe through my right nostril for 20 years. That ultimately let to a sinus infection in all my right side sinuses. Tons of polyps.

      It's outpatient surgery and most people only miss a single day of work. I can't personally say how bad it is, because I had it done in conjunction with rhinoplasty to correct my septum. So I was on my back for about 2 weeks.

      I also can't say how much the FESS has to do with my condition now, since I started getting allergy shots around the same time and they're definitely helping with other symptoms like itchy ears, nose, and throat.

      Yeah, I'd definitely talk to your doc. They'd send you to an ENT for a consult. Good luck!

      PS: I tried normal septum straightening right after college but it didn't take. The doc told me it wasn't a sure thing and if it didn't help much, I'd have to try full rhinoplasty down the road. Just didn't get around to it for several years.
