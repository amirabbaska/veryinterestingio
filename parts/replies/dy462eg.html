  • Slime0

    What is a diffraction pattern (it sounds like a greyscale image but it must be more than that) and why does it reflect different colors of light in different directions?

    • sxbennett

      That should actually have said interference pattern, I fixed my original comment. The interference pattern is the result of the reference beam and the beam that illuminated the object interfering with each other at the plate. Because the two beams of light have the same wavelength, the only difference between them is how long it took the light scattered off the object to reach the plate. Small differences in that time change the phase of the light relative to the reference beam, so the two either constructively or destructively interfere at different points on the plate. This causes there to be a pattern of dark and light areas all over the plate which aren't really an image of the object, but an encoding of how the reference beam interacted with the light coming from the object. After the plate is developed, the same reference beam incident on the plate will diffract through the small areas in between the pattern left on the plate, reconstructing the light that came off the object that was imaged.
