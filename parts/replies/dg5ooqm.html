      A compromise, of sorts, exists within the US where property rights are paramount. It depends on the state, but it isn't uncommon to own the land underneath you, but if the municipality needs it for a reason that benefits everybody they can seize it via eminent domain. Some utilities will even pay for the usage of land (especially in rural areas).

      Sometimes this ownership is limited though. For example, mineral rights can sometimes belong to the state so if you dig in your back yard and find a geothermal vent, oil, or (I suppose) a diamond mine then the state can lay claim to it.
