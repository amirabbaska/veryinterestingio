  • someoneinsignificant

    To piggy back on /u/jppianoguy 's correct response, once you leave the realm of "food" you don't really use the term "Calorie" anymore. In the realm of food, it's about how much energy your body absorbs, and not about how much energy an object has. In terms of dealing with the latter, people use "Joules" which is similarly a term to describe the amount of energy.

    For instance, you can find out how many "Calories" are in a liter of gasoline. The energy density of gasoline is 45 megajoules/kg, which translates to about 10,922 Calories/kg, which translates to about 8,410 Calories per liter of gasoline.

    To put this in perspective, one liter of gasoline has the same amount of energy as ~15 Big Macs. You can eat one or the other and get the same amount of "energy" from both if your body was able to perfectly absorb all of that energy. However, if you drank a liter of gasoline, you would not gain 8410 calories; you would instead die.
