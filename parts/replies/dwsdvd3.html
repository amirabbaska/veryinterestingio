    This is a great question and really relevant to the work that we do. Pollution and bacteria in the area (from combined sewage outflows) mean that shellfish in these water are not safe for human consumption. The oysters in our restoration program are not meant to be eaten, but these issues can still have heavy impacts on our work and can actually dictate where we are and are not allowed to put oysters. Restoration in the NY Harbor is restricted to closed waters (where fishing and extraction are not permitted) and permitting agencies can deny our applications in certain areas where they deem the water too hazardous.
