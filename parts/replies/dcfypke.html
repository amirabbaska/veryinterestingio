    • PsilocybersStone

      Because the space is already full of relatively cool air.

      When it contacts the cold milk, it does not contract as much, and does not create as much void space for the milk to fill.

      Presumably eventually the milk could soak in and displace the air, then even cold cookies would sink, but it would take longer than a fresh hot cookie, as observed.
