    To expand on this a little, an axial vector is also known as a pseudovector, because it's not really a vector.

    The result of a vector cross product is a pseudovector; the direction of the result is dependent on if you make the arbitrary choice of using a left or right-handed coordinate system. Since nature doesn't care about our arbitrary choices, psuedovectors aren't directly observable.

    Since the B field is the result of a vector cross product, it's actually a pseudovector field, and so isn't directly observable. What you can observe is the force due to the field, which is calculated by taking another cross product. Taking a cross product of a vector with a pseudovector produces a vector, so the resulting force vector is indeed a vector which is directly observable.
