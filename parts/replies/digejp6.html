        There are several methods for visualizing the vocal folds via laryngoscopy, including rigid and flexible scopes: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laryngoscopy

        NB: These videos show views of the inside of the human body; some people find viewing such material to be uncomfortable :

        The most common way to visualize the motion of the vocal folds is to use stroboscopic laryngoscopy. Here, a light is rapidly flashed to "freeze" the motion at various points along their open-close cycle, allowing the full cycle to be visualized in slow motion.

        Here is an example of stroboscopic laryngoscopy: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9Tlpkdq8a8c

        High-speed video is great because you can see the motion as it unfolds (haha) but it is much more data intensive. Here is a nice youtube video of a high speed video of the larynx in action: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9kHdhbEnhoA
