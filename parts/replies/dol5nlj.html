    The "wax" is silicone and it only fills the buffer scratches in the poly (the buffing compound is called mirror glaze, but that is a bit overzealous of a name). You are supposed to wipe the rest off.

    Edit: and it is only one very shiny layer of polyester for a black piano. Wood grain is a layer of clear on the stained wood. Since you don't need the strength of a car finish, there is no need to use multiple thin layers. Way cheaper and easier to fix just to do the one thick layer.
