    • promunbound

      Hi there. Sorry it's taken me a while to get back to you. Sounds very frustrating (and familiar) to hear people written off in that way using vague terms. It can be a very dismissing thing and equate to saying there's no hope. I think in particular "anti-social" (without the PD bit) can really just be a shorthand for "we find them difficult", and often that stops us thinking about the lives and experiences people have had that might make them distrustful, angry or alienated through no fault of their own. As far as treatment for ASPD, I had to do a quick double check of the Cochrane Library reviews to confirm my suspicion, but there doesn't seem to be a gold-standard, evidence based treatment that works for everyone. The NICE guidelines suggest targeted cognitive-behavioural group work addressing specific goals like anger management or managing impulsive behaviour. What is rarely mentioned is that there is an adaptation of Mentalization Based Therapy (MBT) for ASPD. MBT is an evidence based treatment for Bordeline PD, which aims to help people understand their own feelings and the feelings of others. It's mostly group based again. There are protocols for delivering it with ASPD, but to my knowledge this hasn't been evaluated very much. Overall, I'd say the lack of evidence for ASPD treatments has a lot more to do with not many people having tested and published any research on this subject rather than there being good evidence that it doesn't work, if you see what I mean. Absence of evidence is not evidence of absence.
