    Electronics engineer here. Missing part of above explanation goes as follows: magnetostriction. It's a property of conductive metal - during magnetization of such metal it is changing its shape. And magnetization in charger comes from alternating current from our sockets. Since frequency of current is around 50Hz, or 50 times per second if You will' it means that you should be able to hear a low tone hum. And indeed High voltage cables and transformers are humming as You probably know. But since in switch mode power supplies you are creating higher frequencies it's not low tone hum but high pitched noise. Pitch of this noise should depend on switching frequency. Not perfect and complete explanation but should be enough.
