    • Flying_madman

      From the west to the east. It wasn't in the same plane as the equator since it launched from Florida. That means even if it moved due east at launch it would still wind up at an angle relative to the equator. (If you ever see diagrams of orbits on TV or in movies that's why they look like sin waves across the map).

      Most things in orbit will go from west to east because that's the direction the Earth rotates so spacecraft have a bit of a 'natural boost' in that direction. To go truly polar they have to cancel it out, and to go retrograde (from east to west) they have to cancel it out and add it back in the opposite direction.
