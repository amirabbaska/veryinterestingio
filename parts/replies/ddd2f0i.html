    • TheSirusKing

      These people don't really know what the point of the planck units are.

      Planck units are a system of units that scale so that all the following constants are equal to 1:

      the speed of light in a vacuum, c, the gravitational constant, G, the reduced Planck constant, ħ, the Coulomb constant, 1/4(pi)ε_0 the Boltzmann constant, k_B

      The units themselves don't mean anything significant currently and are just used so that each of these can be nicely organised mathematically. In plank units, one planck time for example is equal to 1 plank length over the speed of light, where one plank length is equal to the root of ħG/c.

      This can be useful in many situations. For example, the gravitational force between two objects is the product of their masses, times G, over the distance: F = G M m /r^2. The gravitational force between two plank masses at one plank length is thus one plank force, 1x1x1/1^2 = 1.
