I would of course point out (what I suspect you know) that they are very small; in essence you'd need quite a few women's lifetime output for an... omelette.

      That said, I'm pretty sure (based on what I know about the human ovum) that it would lack the key properties of chicken eggs. Human eggs don't have albumin, a "yolk", lecithin, or anything you expect from eggs in the culinary sense. The human egg doesn't carry its own food with it it; that's provided by the uterine lining and placenta. Really, when we eat eggs, we're not mostly eating the little fleck of genetic info, but the large "food store" for growing the baby outside of the mother.
