  • Wiiums

    Your description of air brakes is wrong. The service brakes on a big truck work by using air pressure to apply the brakes and a large spring is what returns the brakes to their normal position. The parking brake, however, must be a mechanical brake that is released when there is sufficient air pressure. In most cases, a spring holds the parking brake on and requires 20-45 psi of air to release it. This spring is located in the brake chamber as opposed to the springs in the drum that pull the shoes off the drum.
