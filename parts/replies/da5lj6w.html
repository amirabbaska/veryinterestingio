• 14MinutesFromEarth

    From Jerry: Thank you! Me and the team had a great time making Brain Games and it was a LOT of work to make the episodes come together. I was a mediocre science student but a HUGE science hobbyist growing up, and when I created Brain Games I kept asking myself, what can I do to make a complicated and typically inaccessible subject like neuroscience exciting to a general audience? Once the three original one hour specials became some of the highest-rated shows ever on National Geographic Channel, they asked me to turn the show into a 1/2 hour series. Our first insight was that we'd have to go narrow and deep for the series, rather than shallow and wide like we did for the specials. Once we had a topic approved, we'd find every journal article, study, and experiment we could get our hands on, then myself, my producing partner Adam Davis, and our writing team would think about where this particular topic/concept came into play in our daily lives. From there, we'd reverse engineer many academic experiments into games or real-world experiments, see which ones worked best, and then reach out to the scientists or researchers who did the lab experiment to get their help on crafting our "gamified" version.
