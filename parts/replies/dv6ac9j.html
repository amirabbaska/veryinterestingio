    I can't believe I had to scroll down to find the actual correct answer.

    I buy both rigid aluminum and aluminum foil as a part of my job. Rigid aluminum is much thicker and can tolerate more impurities. They use tons and tons of UBC (used beverage cans) mixed with ingot to make rigid aluminum (depending on the alloy). Edit: they do this in the States and elsewhere.

    The foil I buy is converter foil, which is very thin. In fact, the thinnest gauges are simply not made in the US, because of the difficulty involved. Right now, there is no choice but to buy overseas. Converter foil has to be made from ingot, not UBC. This foil gets laminated (glued) to paper, film, and/or other materials and as such, cannot be recycled. There is not capacity or quality needed to source more than a small fraction of the foil my company needs domestically.
