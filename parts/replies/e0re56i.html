    Right direction, but you're far from being close.

    CH4 + 2O2 --> CO2 + 2H2O

    As you can see, you need 2 moles of oxygen for 1 mole of methane.

    Methane is 16g/mol; Oxygen is 32g/mol;

    Assuming everything burns up correctly, you need 64g of oxygen for every 16g of methane, which is 4 times more.

    TL;DR you need at least 4 times more oxygen (in mass) as there is methane.
