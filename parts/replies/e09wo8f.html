        That's because it's a maddeningly simple design. You basically just have a small uranium cylinder that fits into a larger uranium cylinder, and you shoot the large one at the small one really really fast, which causes a nuclear reaction. In the case of the Little Boy, there was a casing of tungsten carbide around it (really strong material that held the explosion for a few extra microseconds), as well as a neutron emitter IIRC. The main issue with it was that most of the uranium didn't even go critical. I think it was only a couple grams in total that went critical.
