      The common non-live vaccines are diptheria, pertussis, and pertussis. Immunity to those is fleeting. Not everyone loses protection but most do over time, 5-10 years or so.

      Tetanus can be treated by giving tetanus anti-toxin if you are not current on your vaccinations. Diptheria and pertussis are seldom fatal in adults. Un-immune adults do provide a reservoir for infections that can be spread to others, including those that are too young to be vaccinated (first 2 months of life) or are immunocompromised and unable to respond to vaccines.

      Every year in the hospital we get a less than 2 month old child that dies of pertussis. They essentially cough themselves to death.
