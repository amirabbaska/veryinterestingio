      Chimerism has been successfully tested for a few times, though fetomaternal chimerism is a little harder to test for because the amount of genetic material that passes through is far less than in other types of chimerism.

      I wrote a paper about this and its relation to personal identity and some other things. Granzen's paper The Human Chimeria: Legal Problems Arising From Individuals with Multiple Types of DNA does a good job ELI5 the few most noted types of chimerism and how they differ, as well as a few case studies that revolve around tetragametic chimerism.

      The short answer, FMC (fetomaternal microchimerism) possibly occurs in every pregnancy. So the surrogate very likely shares a small amount of DNA with the fetus and, in fact, the surrogate likely also has DNA within them from the fetus.
