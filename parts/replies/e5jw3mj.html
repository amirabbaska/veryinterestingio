    • police-ical

      You can certainly give people serotonin, which will give them diarrhea without any effect on the brain. Turns out a lot of neurotransmitters can't go from blood to brain. This allows them to work double duty, controlling body functions independent of what they do in the brain. An alternative is to give the substance that's one step short of being serotonin and can get converted, but it doesn't last very long.

      "Serotonin blocker" is less meaningful than you'd think. There are a number out subtypes of serotonin receptors which do different things in different places, sometimes interacting with other neurotransmitter systems. Blocking the 5HT2A serotonin receptor actually has some antidepressant potential. Blocking the 5HT3 receptor... helps with nausea, especially in chemo.

      What has been done is to prevent serotonin synthesis. These studies have been inconsistent, but it can definitely reverse the effect of antidepressants which work on serotonin, can likely lower mood significantly in at least some people with a history of major depression, and maybe mildly lower mood in other people. It's still not a clean technique and may affect other stuff.
