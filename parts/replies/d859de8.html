    It is not refractive index which creates a rainbow, but dispersion - the change of refractive index with wavelength.

    If the refractive index of a material for all wavelengths was the same (zero dispersion), all light would follow the same path through it, and would not split into colours.

    It is specifically the fact that water refracts blue light more than it refracts red light that splits the "white light" up into its constituent colours.
