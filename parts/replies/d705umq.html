      Yup same principles apply. It is well known that joints and other connective tissues demonstrate viscoelastic properties. Along with the muscles that surround the joint you also stretch the passive structures such as the ligaments and joint capsule in the area.

      Using your example a physical therapist may use this position on patients after a total knee replacement to help regain knee extension. It works on stretching out the tissue, regaining range of motion, and breaking up scar tissue.
