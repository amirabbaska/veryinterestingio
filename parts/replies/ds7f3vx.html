    • Dr_Professor_Robot

      Here's a link with some images and more info. The example they use is more of a tilted torus, but that's just one 'slice' of the bowl-shape I'm using.

      Imagine if you were doing this with a cylinder going straight down into the ground. You spin it fast enough people will stick to the walls like that fast spinny fair ride, right? But they're being acted on by two forces; the inertial force of the spinning, and the force of the planet's gravity, so they won't feel like the walls of the cylinder are a straight floor. People will be stumbling diagonally, like they were on a slope.

      So now take your cylinder, keep spinning it the same way, all the forces are the same... but start pinching the lower end of it down, curving the walls inward, turning it into more of a cone or bowl shape. It's still allll spinning at the same rate, all the forces are the same (well, less so at the center but that's an issue with all spinning habitats of non-uniform shape.) but because the walls/floors are tilting to match the net force people are experiencing, they're now all standing up straight and not stumbling around. The people feel like the ground is nice and level, and the gravity is 1g.

      Now just make an equally slanted ceiling so people don't see the weird way they're spinning around when they look up, and voila, nice stable comfy habitation space.
