      Yeah and it is mostly a big problem only in elephant populations that experience poaching. Normally, young male bull elephants are the most aggressive when they go into musth. In healthy populations, aggressive behavior and desire to fight of young bulls is inhibited by older male bull elephants who can beat the crap out of misbehaving adolescent males. Unfortunately since old male elephants have the largest tusks, they are often targeted the most by the poachers. This can remove all the older males from the population and cause adolescent males to run amok. This as two further damaging effects on elephants. First, such males who are not disciplined by older males when young, continue to be aggressive as adults. Second, these very aggressive males can injure females when they try to breed with them. So poaching of older males can screw up elephant populations for decades.
