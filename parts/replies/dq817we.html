    • GetHighAndTinker

      Using an air pump introduces unfiltered air and oxygen into a sealed environment that's fairly rich in nutrients and digestable sugars.

      My hunch is that while it's not skunking specifically, you're starting to see some forms of biological contamination. There are a variety of bacteria that can impart distinctive flavors to beer, and some varieties actually do that on purpose (i.e. lambics)

      Oxidization is a possibility, but I think the bacterial growth is probably more likely.

      Air pumps are fine for parties where you don't want to derp with air tanks and regulators and just want to drink some beer, but if you're going to be storing it, you really should use a CO2 tank (or nitrogen, if the beer style calls for it). That will keep the level of carbonation consistent and minimize contamination risks. They can be rented too, but you'll have to check with a beverage distributor or gas supplier. Most beer stores don't carry them, and if you get kegs regularly, it's worth buying your own. They're not too expensive, and will last forever if they're taken care of.

      • dino340

        It's 100% oxidation, if you're introducing bacterial contamination via a hand pump like that it'll take longer than a day to manifest. In addition it'll likely taste sour not skunky.

        The reason most draft systems use beer gas or co2/nitrogen is because neither will impact the flavor of the beer, oxygen will cause flavors to oxidise giving it a papery taste.
