    I guess further simplifying this, dosing can be complicated on a number of drugs (see above).

    If the drug itself has shown a variation of effectiveness when given at the same strength between patients of dissimilar weights, then it would be dosed on weight if the weight can be reasonably established as the cause for the variation.

    This (usually) occurs in drug testing during clinical trials, so once this general rule is established during testing, it's used as standard reference. I did drug development research for a bit, but I wasn't hands-on with the clinical trials so this might be a bit oversimplified.
