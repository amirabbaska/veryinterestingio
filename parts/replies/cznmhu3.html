  • symmetry81

    In terms of the energy saved by a higher starting position then you're entirely right that it's trivial. However there are two effects that make starting from higher up a good idea.

    First you don't have to worry about atmospheric drag on your ascent. The pressure of the atmosphere has to be overcome by the rocket. This takes fuel and the structural load means that the rocket has to be sturdier taking up more weight.

    Also rockets generally have higher performance in lower pressure. Looking at the Merline rocket engine used by SpaceX it has a thrust of 756 kN at sea leval and 825 kN in a vacuum. And the version with an extended bell specifically designed for vacuum use has a thrust of 934 kN. This is all for the same fuel flow so you would need 24% less fuel for the same impulse.
