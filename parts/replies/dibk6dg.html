  • sanjosanjo

    Does it really matter if the same $20k in sales comes from a large number of small purchases vs. a small number of large purchases?

    • CardFellow

      Yes.

      Every transaction has multiple components of cost, and one of them is a per-transaction fee.

      If you have 25 cent per transaction fee, that's a big bite out of a $5 transaction, and almost nothing out of a $100 transaction.

      If you had $20,000 in $5 transactions, that would be 4,000 transactions vs $20,000 in $100 transactions it would be 200 transactions. Without taking any of the other fees into account, you'd pay $50 in per-transaction fees on the 200 transactions (200 0.25) or $1,000 in per-transaction fees on the 4,000 transactions. (4,000 0.25.)

      This is also why it's crucial to be set up correctly for your average ticket size. You can negotiate with processors so that if you have a lot of small transactions, you can get a low per-transaction fee in exchange for a higher percentage. For example, PayPal's micropayments pricing is 5% of the transaction total and a per-transaction fee of 5 cents. That's actually much better for small transactions than a 2% fee and 30 cents per transaction.
