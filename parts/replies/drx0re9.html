      Tangental to your question is cleaning vs sanitizing. Cleaning is removing debris and dirt from a surface. Sanitizing is removal, destruction, and deactivation of biological hazards. You have to clean before sanitizing as biological hazards (e.g. bacteria, viruses) can "hide" from the sanitizer in the debris. This similar to how a cat can hide from the rain under a car. If you move all the cars, the cat will either leave or get wet.
