    It took many months after the declaration of war in WWI and WWII for the US to be fully ready for combat.

    In WWI at least, the US industry was not even ready when the troops were. The US troops used lots of French and British equipment for the first several months until their own gear finished production and shipping. Even in WWII a shit-ton of US industry had to be converted to a war-time footing.

    When I was sent to Iraq a decade ago, the M-2 .50 cal machine gun at my guard post had a Craftsman logo stamped on it. I heard of one that was built by Mattel. Word is, the Army is contracting out for a new HMG to replace the M-2, but if another no-shit world war ever breaks out, you would probably find even those new models stamped with a tool or toy company's logo.
