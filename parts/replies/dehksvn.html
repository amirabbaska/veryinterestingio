    There is actually an organized network of Chinese owned staffing agencies that places Chinese immigrants in restaurants around the country. A lot of these agencies are in the major urban centers where Chinese immigrants tend to congregate, New York, San Francisco, etc. They have connections with Chinese owned bus lines and housing and will place low skilled, sometimes undocumented job seekers at assignments around the country.

    Many people here are presuming that their local Chinese food place is family run. That may be the case but they may also utilize this network. That is part of the reason you'll see workers in the restaurant working the hours they do. They are recent immigrants with limited employment prospects who were placed in a random location they have no connection to.

    If a restaurant only placed hiring ads within these services, versus the local paper or a sign, they will de facto hire a Chinese person without overt discrimination.

    Edit to add a bit of a digression: interesting how most commentators, including myself, thought of Asian restaurants rather than Mexican restaurants or the fact that Latinos hold so many kitchen and construction jobs in general. However, the reasons for both patterns are very similar. Networks, some organized, some spontaneous, that people outside don't even have awareness of.
