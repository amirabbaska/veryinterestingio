    In most cases, they don't actually withstand pressure, as they are equalised. The pressure inside the animals is the same as outside, which is why they need to be kept in pressurised containers to bring them up to the surface.

      So what happens if the container is not pressurized? Are they going to explode?

      • SteampunkBorg

        If they have a bouyancy bladder, yes. If they don't, not, but there is a risk of injury, because although water is almost incompressible, at those depths, the effects are noticeable.

        In addition, at higher pressures, gas dissolves better in water, and will return to gas state if the pressure drops far enough.
