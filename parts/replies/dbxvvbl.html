      Post-cholecystectomy syndrome.

      Usually the gallbladder squeezes its stored bile into the gut when fat is detected after a meal. Without this storage compartment the bile just slowly drains into the bowel all the time. Some of the symptoms are due to a constant but smaller trickle not being enough to allow full digestion of those fats.

      Also relevant: steatorrhoea.
