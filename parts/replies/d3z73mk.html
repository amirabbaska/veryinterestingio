    • kagantx

      It's theoretically possible. In fact, that was the way Einstein originally wanted to deal with the fact that General Relativity predicted expansion or contraction but the universe appeared static, by setting the cosmological constant to a precise value. The thing is that this equilibrium is unstable -if the universe expands the gravity becomes smaller and the dark energy stronger, so it expands forever. If it gets smaller, the opposite happens. But there is nothing analogous to a star possible, because a star is far too dense to avoid collapse if it has a mass close to that of the universe.
