    Actually, the resonant frequency of water tuning is a myth. The resonant frequency of water is ~10 GHz while a typical microwave oven is at 2.45 GHz. If it were at the resonant frequency all the energy would be absorbed on the surface and the food wouldn't cook well. I used to believe this myself until I looked it up.

    • Celebration2000

      On top of that, the frequency at which water maximally absorbs microwaves changes with temperature. From 0C and 100C, this frequency will shift by about an order of magnitude. Furthermore, at 2.45 GHz, the dielectric loss factor of pure water at 80C is roughly 20% of that at 0C.

      Any fine tuning of the oven to match the dipole resonance of water would be a complete waste of time.

      Further reading on the interaction of microwaves with water can be done here. I also used the figures on the linked page to give the rough figures above.
