      Otters are definitely not primitive mammals. They are within the family Mustelidae (weasel family) within the order Carnivora, which didn't radiate from most of the other major placental mammal lineages until after the Cretaceous-Paleogene extinction. And while it did technically occur after the Cambrian period, we're talking about a very big gap in time between the Cambrian and the Paleogene, as in almost all of the Paleozoic era and all of the Mesozoic era (close to 400 million years). Mammals themselves appeared in the late Triassic period in the early Mesozoic, but that's still a gigantic time gap between both the Cambrian and the Paleogene.
