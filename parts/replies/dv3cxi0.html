    Normal bacterial growth happens more when you do eat, not when you don't eat. Most of your cavities are caused from the first 30 minutes after you eat. The more frequently you eat carbohydrates, the more fermentation occurs on your teeth, the more cavities you'll have.

    Not eating for a day may cause ketosis (it depends on how used to it you are), but it may take up to 3 days. You breathe out acetone when you're in ketosis. Some thinks it smells bad, others think it's sweet, others think it's not a big deal. I don't really notice it when I fast for multiple days without food.

    Sleeping and waking up with bad breath has more to do with a bacterial overgrowth of Streptococcus Mutans, combined with probably sleeping with your mouth open causing it to dry out and/or dehydration. They're the bacteria that more contribute to cavities and they really like carbohydrates and specifically sucrose. Interestingly, while sucrose is nearly chemically identical to fructose+glucose, sucrose is far more cariogenic (it's good at causing cavities) than fructose and glucose.
