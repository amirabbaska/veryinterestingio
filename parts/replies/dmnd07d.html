    2) Supercars are kinda big
    2a. Supercars are generally somewhat long with a wide wheelbase

    Not really. They do tend to be a little wide (for better handling and to accommodate significantly wider tires) but they are generally shorter than "normal cars" in both height and length.

    Just as one example, a 2017 Toyota Camry is 190.9" (L) x 71.7" (W) x 57.9" (H). In comparison, a 2017 Ferrari 458 Italia is 178.2" (L) x 76.3" (W) x 47.4" (H). By total volume, the Ferrari is only about 80% the size of the Camry.

    Anecdotally, I can say that my Nissan 300ZX TT (what I would consider a poor man's supercar in 1994) looks really small next to my Honda Civic.

      Couple things:

      1. Aggressive styling can be polarizing (see: Honda Civic Si 2018)
      2. Most car companies reserve more aggressive styling for their more expensive cars as a way to distinguish them (see: Honda Civic Si 2018)
      3. If you just change the parts that are easy to tweak: bumpers, grille, fenders, front quarter panels, side skirts, you're either going to have to act with restraint in order to match it to the other lines of the car that are determined by practicality (the shape of the cabin, the shape of the engine compartment, the shape of the trunk/hatch, the shape/location of the wheel wells).       4. No matter what, it's going to have some effect on either aerodynamics, cost, weight, sturdiness or repairability
