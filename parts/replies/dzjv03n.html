  • linkschode

    Part of this is wrong. Power lines are not designed to send electrons from one place to another, they’re designed to move energy from one place to another. With DC the electrons move very slowly through a wire:

    “... the drift speed through a copper wire of cross-sectional area 3.00 x 10-6 m2, with a current of 10 A will be approximately 2.5 x 10-4 m/s or about a quarter of a milimeter per second.”

    Besides, large scale power transmission is almost always done with AC. As there’s a back and forth going on there with the electron movement, the electrons don’t really go anywhere anyway. Think of it more like pushing and pulling marbles through a hose pipe to do work at the other end (crude analogy but you get the point)
