    • CompletePlague

      In most businesses, suppliers engage in price discovery -- that is, they figure out what price the market will bear (to a greater or lesser degree) and adjust their pricing.

      For whatever reason, so many kinds of venues/promoters just don't adjust their pricing very much between acts -- and really, really don't adjust their pricing much for location quality. (For example, most theater venues will charge one price for all "orchestra" level seats -- be they row 3 dead-center or row 26 way out to the side). This leaves a huge open market for someone to buy up all the good seats they can get, because the front few rows near the center will all sell for a multiple of face value, even at shows with only moderately high demand.

      If venues sold each ticket for something closer to its actual market value, there wouldn't be room for mass-operating ticket brokers.
