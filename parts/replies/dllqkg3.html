    tl;dr If you have an astronomy, astrophysics, or physics degree and do well you will have access to a wide variety of great jobs!

    This is a great question and one that we get frequently. Fortunately, graduating astronomy majors regularly have one of the lowest rates of unemployment according to several studies (1, 2)! Before we get into the jobs we all get into we should address why astronomy degrees are so sought after in the job market.

    An undergraduate degree in astronomy will give you training in problem solving, research skills, coding, data management, statistics, and how to work independently or with a team. This says to an potential employer that you could be given any problem and you would be able to figure out how to solve it, whether that means assembling a team to research the issue or coding a solution on your own.

    Since you will leave college with a wide array of skills in coding, research, and data you will be well suited for a number of jobs in business, software development, marketing, engineering, and of course academia.

    In the context of the job market an undergraduate degree in astronomy or astrophysics will give you the similar training to a physics degree and often can be substituted when applying for graduate positions (if you go to college that only has a physics degree you can go into astronomy as a graduate student). The American Institute of Physics looks at employment in the field each year, here is a link to their most recent analysis of students one year after their physics bachelors degree.

    A PhD in astronomy will give you more training to work independently, develop your computer skills, and hone your research ability. This makes you a prime candidate for research positions in academia or at NASA. You will have the option to become a university professor as well, though these jobs are very competitive.
