      The main effect on ultimate droplet size is Rayleigh-Taylor instability. In which gravity is a factor, because it sets the acceleration and velocity of the falling droplet. The velocity caused drag forces effectively pushing up on the liquid. Beyond a certain velocity the droplet breaks up into smaller droplets. So gravity and atmospheric density (not just pressure) are the two main factors in droplet size.
