    This is an excellent synopsis of the Common Law system in the US. I'd add that sentencing varies greatly depending on the Court (there are many court systems in the US: municipal, state, federal, military, etc.) and also the particular judicial officer hearing the case. Even the most evenhanded judges are still human beings with their own particular worldview. Unfortunately, this can sometimes mean that two defendants convicted of the exact same crime will receive very different sentences, simply based on the judge's impressions of them.
