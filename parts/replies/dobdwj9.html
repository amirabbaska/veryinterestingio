    • shingeling

      IIRC, wines do mature in the bottle, as the maturing process consists mostly of tannins polymerizing (which causes the wine to taste less bitter and astringent) and esterizing of acids, which reduces the perceived acidity. This all causes the wine to taste more smooth and certain other processes can also develop extra flavours in the wine.

      What stops happening in the bottle is the fermentation, as wines, unlike some craft beers, are filtered before being bottled.
