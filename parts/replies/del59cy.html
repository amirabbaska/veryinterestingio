    Hey cheese, thanks for a nice block quote (hooray for context).

    But I wanted to point out that there's a big difference between unreliable and inconclusive research findings. Which I think is often lost on the lay public.

    From your excerpt, the paper found, reliably, that reported positive effects were inconclusive. Generally, peer reviewed findings are taken to be reliable, but even a perfect study can find that there is no conclusive effect.

    ie: three observers and a video camera give a very reliable account of 100 coin flips. But that experiment will not give us very conclusive results about which side the coin lands on.
