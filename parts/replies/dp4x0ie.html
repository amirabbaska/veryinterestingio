    • LHKSC2

      its a simple answer that isn't easy to do in practice.

      make sure you recognize when you're doing it, and tell yourself that this isn't what you want to be doing, and stop doing it when you catch yourself doing it. Over time you will condition yourself to recognize it as bad behavior. But you have to recognize that you are in fact doing it and it is in fact bad behavior you dont want. Until you can accept that its a problem and being honest with yourself that its happening, there's not much you can do.

      Thats the simple answer. The more complex answer is why you do these things you do, and thats why people go to therapists. They delve into how these things manifested themselves and try to fix them at the root, and I am not a therapist so can't do much for you there. If you can't condition yourself or it feels too deep rooted to do on your own, you gotta talk to a professional.

    • DrDreAcula

      1. recognize your emotion or behaviour that is destructive

      2. name it , be really descriptive you might need to expand your vocabulary from just sad and lost , i did

      3. now ask yourself why do i feel this way , an ishikawa diagramm may help , its a really simple tool for finding the roots of a problem

      4. now logically argue why your feelings are unbased in reality, or your perspective on things is skewed ( this is the most important part )

      5. repeat , after a while healthy thinking will be the norm , dont expect too much progress in days

      for example : i feel sad and hated because i think a person (or people in general) hates me / counter argument : why should he hate me i have never done anything that would upset him , infact i have been always nice to him.

      EDIT: Also seeking professional help is never a bad idea
