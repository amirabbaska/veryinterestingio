    • cheesegenie

      culling the population is a form of breeding control

      Yes it's a form of breeding control, but it is not a method of domestication.

      Culling is used to eliminate specific undesirable animals (like those that are sick or unusually small or weak) or to maintain a desired number or gender ratio in a group of animals. Killing all the animals without the specific trait you want is impossible because you'd end up wiping out the species. Culling doesn't introduce new and desirable traits to animal populations, it simply eliminates undesirable ones.

      In essence, it is used to maintain the status quo of a group of animals, whereas the goal of selective breeding is to change the animals.

      I don't think the ants are actually culling the aphids either, because as far as I know they eat them only occasionally and not with a specific trait in mind.

      Culling involves the deliberate selection of individual animals for elimination based on the undesirable traits those individuals have, and ants don't seem to be doing that.

      Yes. Domestication is essentially genetic modification and dependence on the domesticating species arising from interference with the natural breeding cycle of a species over several generations. You let the ones you like breed, you care for them, and years later you have subservient, dependent little buddies.

      Taming is the process of making an individual animal tolerant to the presence of humans.

      If you want an interesting example look at foxes. Domesticated foxes exist in very small numbers and can be purchased for vast sums of money. The scientist who domesticated them spent decades doing it.
