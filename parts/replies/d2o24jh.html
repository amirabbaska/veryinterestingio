      Commonly we use "catheter" to mean a urinary catheter, but it actually just means a soft hollow tube we are using to connect to part of the body. An IVC is an intravenous catheter, also called a cannula, which is the one that goes into the blood stream. The urinary catheter is usually called an IDC or in-dwelling cathether, or more properly an in-dwelling urinary catheter. In-dwelling refers to it staying in place, as opposed to, for example, an in-out catheter which is when you place an "IDC" just for the time it takes to drain the bladder (usually to sample the urine) or to deliver a drug. We still call it an IDC when we do this though! It's just the pattern of use of the terms now.
