      • AnitaSnarkeysian

        I live on a quarter acre in a suburb that is literally a 20 minute bike ride from the Canadian boarder. On my quarter acre lot, I have 4 sugar maple trees that I just started tapping, and will yield me enough syrup for the rest of the year. I also have 3 bee hives, and produce over 100lbs of honey each year. I have 4 egg laying hens (No roosters, because it's a suburb, and I don't want to piss off my neighbors). To reduce the cost of the chickens, I grow meal worms in a composting bin in my basement, and I feed the meal worms with leftover food from my dinners. Finally, I have a 19 x 22 foot veggie garden. I use chicken droppings to enrich my garden soil. I had an apple tree too when I bought the property, but it was struck by lightning 3 years ago, and I had to cut it down (I have been giving the applewood away to friends, family, and neighbors since it's great for smoking meats).

        I turn 30 this year... I am amazed at how productive land can be. I give eggs, and honey, and surplus veggies/herbs away to my immediate neighbors. The kids all get a kick out of the chickens, as they are not a popular thing to have in my area. One of the boys likes the bees, but I yell at him to stay away because I don't want his mom hollering at me because her dumb kid stuck his nose too close to the hive.

        Anyway, the point is... 5 years ago, before I owned land, if you had asked me what I could do with a quarter acre of property, I would probably have guessed "nothing", you can actually do quite a bit. I was lucky to find a property with established maple trees and an apple tree. I put the bees, chickens and veggie garden in myself, and I specifically constructed the plot to be aesthetically pleasing, with the garden and hen house lined up with the home on my property. This resulted in slightly less perfect placement, about 1/3 of my garden is in morning shade and can only grow root veggies. But I think having a more beautiful property is worth it, and prevents neighbors who might not like having foul and bees in the area from complaining. I will say, no one has ever complained to my face, I get a lot of compliments, and I like it when other families out for an afternoon walk stop at my yard to see what I've got going on.
