    • PiZeta1481

      Maybe for some small subset of genes...part of the problem for many genes, though, is that once they aren't expressed, there's little or no selective pressure to keep them in their current state. The gene sequence, which includes the sequence that directs regulation of the gene, then drifts due to random mutation. A cursory glance at the human genome shows a large number of so-called 'pseudogenes' which resemble human or ancestral genes, but have clearly been subject to random mutations over millions of years.

      Also, there's no correlation between genome size and genetic complexity. Many plants have genomes much much bigger than humans, which would seem to suggest that minimizing genome size isn't potently selected for, and also doesn't dictate complexity.
