      So, I don't actually do any of this stuff myself. I'm an attorney. The only reason I know any of this is because one of my clients is a plastic recycler. I can talk about the large-scale process with some authority, but when you get down to the level of how recovery facilities deal with small pieces, I got nuthin'.

      Thinking practically about it though, anything too small to stay in a bale after compaction will probably end up getting disposed of. Not efficient to bother with it.

      As to sorting, I do know that a lot of recyclers rely heavily on pre-collection sorting, where consumers themselves separate out glass/metal/plastic/cardboard, etc.

      Those that don't use a series of sorting processes, each of which is intended to collect a single category of material from a mixed materials stream. Forced air (different pressures can pick out paper and plastic). Magnets. Rotating drums. Manual pickers. Etc.
