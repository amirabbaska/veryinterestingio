    • ooooomikeooooo

      At will is portrayed as a benefit to the worker but it is 100% not the case. Job security is vital for people to be able to plan. As long as you carry out the work you are contracted to do, why should your employer be able to drop you with no notice? The threat of losing your job on the spot means that the employer owns all the power especially when you have no safety net of free healthcare.

      Here in the UK you can be fired immediately only if you are in breach of contract (gross misconduct) or you have been issued warnings etc and gone through the process. You can also be made redundant if the work is no longer needed. That comes with a notice period and a severance package. Conversely, you can't just quit a job, you give notice to leave. This allows the company to find a replacement. It's beneficial for both parties.
