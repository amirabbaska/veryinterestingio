  • VonLoewe

    I'm just being nitpicky, but saying "the lens would be your eye and the sensor would be your brain" is incorrect. The lens is obviously the lens in your eye, and the sensor is your retina. A sensor is a photosensitive surface, i.e. converts light into an electric current, which is performed by the rods and cones in your retina.

    The brain in this analogy is the whole electronic circuit behind the sensor,, from the A/D converter, which allows that electric signal to be processed, to the screen showing the image.
