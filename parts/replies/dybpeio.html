    It seems to me in order to have any of these behaviors, insects first have to be shown to have some more basic but abstract level mental processes - ie. do they even have concious level thought or emotional responses, nevermind "play". In recent years I've seen a number of papers for experiments intended to determine whether insects even have "intentional thought"--ie is everything they do completely instinctual or do they have the ability to assesss and make a decision. Some of these were kind of weird, like wiring up flies nervous systems and then playing images of a closing predator or food object. In another they clipped half the mouth parts off of queen bees so they couldn't fight, then introduced them into a hive with another queen. In the latter case, the injured queens avoided fights with the other queen, implying they knew they were weaker and likely to lose any fight they engaged in. It doesn't answer the question of whether they actually play, but it does demonstrate they are capable of intentional behavior rather than being complete autonotoms
