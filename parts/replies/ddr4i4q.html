    • bbqroast

      Pedestrianobservations.wordpress.com is my favourite blog on this.

      In the US it comes down to a mix of poor project management whoch is forced by a bad legal climate.

      Both the US and UK have case law which increases legal costs and setbacks considerably. The US has Buy Americs rules which restricts access to working off the shelf solution from other countries.

      Stations and tunnels tend to be overdesigned resulting in huge expense. There's also a massive bias towards concrete over upgraded signalling or improved operations. Eg adding platforms at significant expense when existing ones are wasted by long turn around times.

      Legally transit agencies in the US have to be very paranoid with their contractors as courts tend to favour the latter. The result is very tight contracts with no wiggle room. That means even good contractors can't work well.

      I'm just summarising, the blog has more details.
