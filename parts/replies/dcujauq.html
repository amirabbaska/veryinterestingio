      • Valleyman1982

        Concrete is a fundamentally alkaline material, and if it's reinforced contains reinforcement. Acid causes pitting and spalling of concrete as it chemically reacts with it, and makes it more porus. Water gets to the rebar and it rusts and degrades.

        It a similar chemical reaction that occurs in marine structures, giving them a shorter life, and why road salts cause huge damage to building over the years.

        Acid and concrete is a bad mix.
