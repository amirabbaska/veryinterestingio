    In addition to this, a pressure wave builds up in front of your fingernail or you hand when flicking or swatting at insects that essentially cushions the initial impact. Imagine standing in a open field and suddenly a 30x30 wall comes at you around 30mph (some have measured a finger flick at around 8-9m/s if I remember right). That wall has built a huge pad of air directly in front of it like a pillow that, even if it does not push you out of the way, will seriously soften the impact of the wall. For an entire hand, the comparison is like a 150x500 foot wall coming at you and the wave is even larger. This is the reason flyswatters work...all the small holes do not allow any significant wave to build, the fly or insect gets the full impact, and death occurs.
