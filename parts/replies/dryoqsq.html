    Does fluid or gas density play a big role? Or is it more attributed to the absorptivity and reactivity of the partials (materials)?

    • overtheridge

      The density of a fluid is much higher than that of a gas, so it bends light more, but the principle is the same.

      Refractive indices of mediums tend to increase with something called permeability, which is the energy it takes to form electric and magnetic fields within the medium. I would assume that because the reactivity of a compound is related to how much stability each particle gains after some change to its internal electromagnetic field (like forming a bond), it would be related to its permeability, but I don't know enough about the topic to give you a definitive answer.
