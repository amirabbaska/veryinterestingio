    This. Note that 1.6 is quite close to 1.5 (ratio of 3 : 2) and 1.66... (ratio of 5 : 3) which is a pretty good estimate for things that are neither very square nor very stretched. Look at your tv screen, which is probably something like 16 : 9 ~ 1.7; or a piece of paper, which is sqrt(2) : 1 ~ 1.4. I'll bet you that with a bit of trial-and-error you'll fit a good golden ratio to it. To the human eye, those are pretty close, and you'd be surprised how easy it is to fit a golden ratio spiral onto an image with a bit of cheating. For example, check out either of these Google Image results: http://media02.hongkiat.com/golden-ratio-in-moden-designs/mona-lisa-golden%20ratio.jpg, https://b.fastcompany.net/multisite_files/fastcompany/imagecache/inline-large/inline/2015/04/3044877-inline-i-1-the-golden-ratio-designs-biggest-urban-legend.jpg - do you notice how many of the lines in the image actually don't really match up with anything substantial?
