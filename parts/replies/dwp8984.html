    This isn't the whole story, training is important, but the everyone is missing what ability is being trained.

    Neural networks are relational and operate on whole snapshots of data (such as a picture) to produce results based on their own self-generated weights. Humans don't actually do anything like this.

    What sets humans apart from computers and most every other animal is causal inference. Literally, the ability to infer a cause or predict for a specific event without directly experiencing it. Every animal has this ability to some extent, but us humies are on a completely different level. This is what makes people, people. This is what lets us learn and share information better than any other animal.

    When a neural network is fed a screenshot, it processes the literal information from the screenshot and compares it to memory. It digests every pixel and compares it to memories of billions of other pixels based on their relationships to other pixels past and present. This is not who people do. Our working memory is literally incapable of this.

    So human's say fuck it, I can build my own picture with blackjack and hookers. We store the identified concepts and then effectively rebuild them into a working model out of pre-fab parts borrowed from the sensory system. Human's aren't trained by the environment directly, its our greatest strength that we aren't.

    Want proof? What does the top of the alien's head feel like? I bet you had an answer despite never having actually touched it before. Humans don't do visual processing, we scrape an image quickly and then do the actual processing and reasoning on our internally constructed sensory model. You train sensory information to have more prefab parts and data to build more complete models for prediction out of.

    In short, neural networks train by finding relationships between concrete instances of pre-existing data, every single literal pixel of the literal image for example.

    Humans parse the original concrete data into abstract concepts which are effectively bundles of pre-fab instructions for sensory organs, and effectively run those instructions through those organs as if they were actual sensory information coming into your brain that you were directly experiencing to construct an internal sensory model that can then be controlled and manipulated by extrapolating from known experiences. Effectively, ever item in your memory is actually just you in a costume.

Because to your brain, that Alien is just you. Its running instructions through your proprioception sensory system as if you were literally making those movements to return existing sensory information of what that movement feels like to build that model.
