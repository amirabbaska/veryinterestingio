  • isleepinahammock

    Evolution and the biology of animals and plants are not all going to be oriented and centered around us and our species. We are not the pinnacle of evolution, we are merely another step in a never ending chain.

    On the other hand, we are ultimately the only hope for all life on this planet. Even independent of human actions, this world, as far as life is concerned, is already 90-95% spent. Life has existed on this planet for perhaps 4 billion years. In 100-500 million years, the Sun's natural long term increase in brightness will render this planet uninhabitable. The oceans will boil and the Earth will become a second Venus.

    Thankfully, we can absolutely prevent this. We could probably do so even with our current technology. For example, enough thin mirrors launched into orbit would completely negate this process. With current technology, we could extend this planet's lifespan right up until the Sun goes red giant in 5 billion years or so. If we ever figure out stellar engineering, we might even be able to extend the Sun's life into hundreds of trillions of years, though that is certainly well beyond our current technology. (Though not inconsistent with our current understanding of physics.)

    We are children of a dying world. If we go extinct, it is highly unlikely that any intelligent species will rise to take our place. Any event that wipes us out completely would also likely wipe out any possible successor species. (For example, if all human beings just dropped dead magically tomorrow, chimps might be able to evolve into something at our level in a few million years.) Any event that completely wipes us out would also likely wipe out most or all large land-dwelling mammals.

    If we went extinct, the most "advanced" organisms that survived would likely be things like mice or rats. True, civilization-producing intelligence appears to be a very rare thing. In the hundreds of millions of years of complex multicellular life, we are the only such species in our planet's history. For all our flaws, we are the single greatest light that our planet has ever produced. If we go, there likely simply isn't time for our equal to arrive via natural evolution.

    And again, the long term crisis faced by life is something that only intelligence can solve. Without advanced, technological civilization, this planet will be devoid of anything but bacteria within 200 million years and completely sterile within 500 million.

    We cannot relinquish ourselves to nihilism or the belief that our extinction is meaningless. We humans are truly unique in the entire history of this world. Yes, this uniqueness gives us the ability to recklessly devastate our planet. But it also gives us the ability to win for life a reprieve from absolute annihilation. Even with current technology, we have the ability to double the natural habitable lifespan of this world. There has not been a single species in the entire unfathomably ancient history of this planet that had such a capability. And there is not likely to be another before time has run out.
