    • blablahblah

      This is a pretty common misconception. Employment numbers don't come from people filing for unemployment. It comes from an extensive survey (~100,000 people) that the government runs every month. "Unemployed" according to the official unemployment number means "has not been paid for work in the last month, but has looked for paid work in the last month". It doesn't matter if they qualify for unemployment benefits, how long they've been employed, or if they've ever had a job, only that they're looking.

      The survey results also include numbers on people who would take a job but aren't looking, and on people working part time but want to work full time but that's not the number the newspapers quote.

      You can read more about the survey on the Bureau of Labor Statistics website.
