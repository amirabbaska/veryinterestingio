    • TychaBrahe

      Well, one reason is an electrolyte imbalance. This can be caused by a variety of things, including diet, disease, strenuous exercise, and medications. Deficiencies in various vitamin B compounds—especially B1, B5, and B6—and minerals like calcium, magnesium, and potassium can cause muscle cramps.

      You can get B-vitamin deficiencies through a vegetarian diet, so many vegetarians and vegans supplement with Brewers yeast. People who do strict low-carb diets sometimes lack magnesium, which is mainly found in whole grains, beans, and nuts; and potassium, which is mainly found in high-carb vegetables such as squash, potato, and sweet potato; which are banned during the induction phase of the Atkins diet.

      Diseases that interfere with digestion, such as Crohn's and celiac can cause these deficiencies. Having bariatric surgery is another risk factor.

      Exercise causes the body to sweat, and electrolytes are lost during this process. This is why it is so important to stay hydrated, and why Gatorade was invented.

      Some medications can interfere with electrolytes. Antacids and insulin block potassium and magnesium absorption. Some blood pressure medications and antibiotics interfere with calcium absorption.

      I have a personal connection to this at the moment. I donated platelets through apheresis this weekend. The anticoagulant used to prevent your blood from clogging the tubes and machine is a calcium antagonist. Usually I'm sensible about this, but for some reason I was taking vitamin C supplements instead of calcium supplements. As a result, I became moderately hypocalcemic—called in this case "citrate toxicity"—and had muscle cramps for hours yesterday, followed today by severe pain as if I'd done a long and intense workout.
