      Its more that the ethanol would not be metabolized at the normal rate rather than it building up (in that hypothetical example).

      The first figure on this page shows how blood ethanol concentration rapidly rises following the consumption of ethanol and then gradually reduces over time.

      https://pubs.niaaa.nih.gov/publications/aa35.htm

      Metabolism of ethanol by alcohol dehydrogenase (ADH) is the main (but not only) way that alcohol is eliminated from the body.

      If most of the liver's ADH activity was inhibited, the rate at which blood alcohol levels drop (around 10 mg% per hour from that graph) would be much slower and blood alcohol concentrations would remain elevated for longer.

      It would also lead to much higher peak blood ethanol if the person drank over a long period of time (say one drink per hour for 8 hours), so in that situation you would have more of a build up.
