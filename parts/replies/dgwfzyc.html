      A Carbohydrate molecule example:

      C^12 H^22 O^11 (45 atoms molecule)

      A Fat molecule example:

      C^18 H^34 O^2 (54 atoms molecule)

      Glucose molecule is:

      C^6 H^12 O^6 (24 atoms molecule)

      (Proteins are organs, cells, tissues, these can be huge and very different)

      The Fat inside our fat cells are tryglicides, which is (example)

      C^55 H^98 O^6 (159 atoms molecule)

       

      After we eat and our intestines digest our food, there are plenty of C^12 H^22 O^11 and C^18 H^34 O^2 molecules (carbs and fat respectively) which have to be turned into glucose so it can float in our blood and feed our cells.

       

      Keeping it super simple, you can see how breaking C^12 H^22 O^11 into C^6 H^12 O^6 is rather easy. Break the carb once in half and we almost have the glucose molecule.

       

      Fat which is C^18 H^34 O^2 to turn it into C^6 H^12 O^6, is more difficult, we can't break it in 3 because there is not enough oxygen atoms, so it needs to bring oxigen atoms from elsewhere. This process is much slower.

       

      This is why carbs make blood sugar spike, while fat give a more prolonged release. Fats are slower to metabolize (got to wait for those oxygen atoms).

       

      If there are too many glucose molecules in the blood, our bodies will begin to store them inside the fat cells, this is really important because if it accumulates too many glucose molecules in the blood we can die.

       

       

      The molecule in which we store energy inside the fat cells, or the "fat" inside the fat cells, is the tryglicides, which is really energetic with about 900 calories p/ 100 grams (This is also where the, you need to burn 9000 calories for each kilogram of fat / 4000 calories for each pound of fat).

       

      All of these chemical reactions are made on the liver and pancreas.

       

      One issue, how do the molecules move from outside the fat cell to inside. It does so through these "doors" that are on the fat cells. The problem here is that tryglicides at more than 100 atoms long are too big, and it doesn't fit through those doors. So the body has to break it in half, move it inside, then reassemble it. This is when moving inside and outside. This takes time.
