    Your comment about approximating a number with rationals meaning it is irrational or will hit the rational exactly is not true.

    Take the sequence (n-1)/n. This sequence approximates 1 to any degree for large enough n, and is a rational solution.

    • incompetent30

      No, this is an example of what I'm talking about. (Maybe I didn't phrase it clearly enough.) The sequence s_n = (n-1)/n does a bad job of approximating the number 1, because |1-s_n| only shrinks as fast as 1/q_n, where q_n is the denominator of s_n (in this case q_n = n). If you have a convergent sequence of rational numbers with 'small' denominators compared to how fast they converge, either the sequence is constant eventually (i.e. you hit the exact value), or the limit is irrational.
