    • Ragingwhirlpool

      Our capabilities are already quite absurd. The term that's used most regularly to determine an aircrafts ability to hide its self is its radar cross-section and the smaller the cross section, the better it is at avoiding detection.

      An F-18 has a cross-section of about 1m^2, while an F-35 has a cross-section of about 0.005m^2 (about the size of a golf ball) and a B-2 stealth bomber has a cross-section of about 0.0001m^2 (about the size of a bumble bee).

      Source to educate yourself: http://mil-embedded.com/guest-blogs/radar-cross-section-the-measure-of-stealth/

      • MongArmOfTheLaw

        Yes, but its very unlikely that they'll ever make something that works against all possible radar wavelengths.

        Most stealth coatings are aimed at modern high frequency radars, against which they can work fairly well under ideal circumstances ie no rain or snow, plane not using data links, radios, radar transmitters etc.

        But much older radars with lower frequency emmisions can still detect them quite well. Thats how that F-117 was shot down; stealthy to modern stuff but not to older Soviet sets.

        The Serbs were very clever, they also used microwave ovens with their safety switches jigged so they'd operate with the door open to bait US anti-radar missiles. Worked quite well apparently and a very good trade - swapping a £20 microwave for a £500k missile.
