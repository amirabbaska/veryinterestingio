      • fishnoguns

        It is rarely quite that clear.

        IQ is what we measure intelligence with

        IQ tests have a strong cultural component; if you grow up in Western society just by consuming media you can quite accurately predict how a real IQ test will look like. Not 100% accurate of course, but close enough to be mentally prepared. Giving that exact same test to an inland Chinese farmer will give you a completely different result that may have nothing to do with their actual intelligence.

        Besides that, the idea that we use IQ to determine intelligence is disputed and not at all commonly accepted scientifically. This is why you often see all kind of explanations about emotional intelligences, physical intelligences, creative intelligences etc.

        High IQ is a predictor of greater success in life, even when you control for other variables.

        What we consider 'successful' is also strongly culturally determined. If I recall correctly the study you are probably thinking of does correlate IQ to higher income in life. Whether that means this person is 'more successful' is up for debate.
