      Yup! What you're describing is a reperfusion injury, it's caused by the formation of reactive oxydative species after reinfusion of Oxygen (O2- is very toxic to cells). I think a lot of people don't know how dangerous reperfusion can be after MI's, it's a super interesting cause of death... Sp most MI's post 5 minutes will have this occur and can lead to VF within the first few days post reperfusion, or ventricular rupture after those few days due to actual necrosis. What I was explaining earlier isn't due to an MI, but transient ischemia over weeks/months/years commonly seen in angina patients. The myocardium survives, but shuts down a large amount of activity/synthesis of proteins etc.
