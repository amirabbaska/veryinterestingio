    • iwhitt567

      Imagine a '+' made out of five solar panel squares (one in the center, one on each side). Now fold each of the side panels in as if they were the sides of a box, but only fold them about 30 degrees. When the center panel is aligned with the sun, the top and bottom pair (as well as the left and right pair) will be at the same angle to the sun, and therefore produce the same voltage. If you wire these up to a motor in opposition (not sure of the correct term here), this means the motor receives no voltage, and doesn't turn. As the sun moves, one in each pair will receive slightly more power (because the sun faces it more directly) and this provides a net voltage to the motor, aligning it in real-time.

      Also the side solar panels can be really small, since they only need to power a low-speed motor.
