    Not OP, but I've got a relevant "car in the woods" story.

    On a canoe trip in Minnesota, I once found the remnants of a 1915 Oldsmobile (or maybe Cadillac, can't remember offhand) on a random, tiny-ass island in the middle of the Boundary Waters Wilderness. It was so old that the wheels had wooden spokes, which had mostly rotted away. The only way you could tell that it was originally a car was the fact that "1915 Oldsmobile" was stamped on the rusted-as-fuck engine block.

    I cannot stress how middle-of-nowhere this place was. There were no roads on the island. There were no bridges to the island, nor was there anything for them to connect to if there had been. We were miles and miles from anything recognizable as "civilization." To this day, I still have no idea what that old junker was doing there.
