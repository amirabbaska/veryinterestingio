    • udenizc

      There usually isn't any damage from transient paresthesia that happens when you stay in one position for too long. But transient paresthesia does not really explain prolonged weakness, the weakness should go away after the transient paresthesia is resolved. Many things could account for the weakness and it's impossible to say anything without a medical history evaluation and a neurological exam. It technically could be a peripheral neuropathy caused by prolonged pressure on a nerve or something altogether different. Since you mentioned that you were a big man, brachial plexus or radial nerve compression could definitely be a possibility. Radial nerve neuropathy (Also called honeymoon palsy and saturday night palsy) is a possibility since you mentioned you often slept with your head on your arm or your arm between your legs. The compression is often not caused simply by postural changes but also by anatomical structures next to the nerves compressing the nerves. I don't know if you have diabetes, but if you do and it's not controlled, it could be associated with that as well. It could potentially be associated with a cervical hernia or a bulging disc. But once again, it could also be something completely different. If there really is noticeable weakness in your arms I would very highly recommend a neurological exam ASAP because it's an important sign. Especially if the weakness is progressing don't ignore it. If it really is due to prolonged compression it's not a serious or permanent condition but the treatment could take a fairly long time and might be frustrating, so an early diagnosis may save you a lot of frustration. See your GP, list your complaints and ask for a referral to a neurologist or your GP could also probably help you. If you have further questions feel free to PM me and I can refer you to some sources.
