    • haterhipper

      A meteor flying around the solar system has a lot of kinetic energy. When it hits the moon it comes to a stop but that kinetic energy still exists and is effectively redirected back into the meteor. If that energy exceeds the energy binding the meteor together it breaks those bonds, causing it to explode. It is the same thing that happens to snowballs when you throw them into a wall.
