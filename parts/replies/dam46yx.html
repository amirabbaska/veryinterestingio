  • accountnumberseven

    Now, let's have BigCorp ISP give everyone FREE internet - especially for the poor who cannot afford Internet. Wait... only approved sites and services are free - if you go elsewhere, please provide credit card.

    For those who don't know, this is a real thing that Mark Zuckerberg has been doing in less developed countries. It's a partnered initiative called Internet.org/Free Basics by Facebook, which grants free access to a specifically chosen selection of sites to anyone who can get the signal and offers paid plans for full Internet access. It sounds great on paper (free access to Wikipedia and other general sources of knowledge in places that wouldn't otherwise get any Internet connectivity for potentially decades.) But India banned it for violating net neutrality, and it's clear why. When you teach hundreds of millions of people that the internet is Facebook, Wikipedia, ESPN, etc, you can start shaping the views of a large part of humanity. How can Indian weather services compete with the one foreign weather service that anyone can get freely? If Facebook's news feed favours a certain Indian political candidate and a large part of the population uses Facebook because the alternatives are expensive, Facebook can start influencing their elections.

    The Indian government understands what a unique and precarious situation this is, and that Internet.org has selfish and selfless reasosn for wanting to provide non-neutral internet service to their people. It's a really interesting issue that could determine a lot about India in the next 100 years.
