    • oaifheoiuhf

      Think about how physically hard it is to unbreak a window. First, you have to recollect all the energy lost to the environment (all sound, light, heat, etc) from bonds in the glass breaking and pieces falling. If you can do that, and put all the glass pieces back to were they were, and put the energy back into the bonds, the rest is trivial!

      Basically, if you tracked everything that happened, you can theoretically do the opposite, it just isn't possible for us to actually do.
