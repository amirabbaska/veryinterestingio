  • phidus

    There are two major factors at play for why this happens:

    First, oxygen is higher concentration. Oxygen is required for fire. Kinetics (loosely) says when a required component of a reaction is in greater concentration, the reaction goes faster.

    Second, other things that don't burn, like nitrogen, comprise a smaller fraction of air. Thermodynamics tells us that when you use the same amount of heat to warm less material, the temperature change will be greater.

    Fire is complicated and has a lot of feedback. The hotter it is, the faster reactions go, the hotter it gets. But these factors make it so that enriched oxygen makes a big difference.
