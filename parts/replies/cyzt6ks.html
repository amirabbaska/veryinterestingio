      So leaf temperature is important for plant health, once leaf temperature gets above 40oC the internal leaf cellular structure breaks down. The high oxygen content allows for the combustion of even very wet vegetation. A plant could survive being on fire for a small period of time, but once internal leaf temperature gets above 40oC the plant starts to die, quickly.

      Wet things don't "burn" but yes the dead vegetation would be burnt and decomposed, this is where we get the charcoal data from.
