    • angrytacoz

      Carbide tools are made from powdered tungsten carbide, so the holes are formed in the press before it gets sintered. No machining necessary I’ve never seen HSS drills with coolant holes so I’m not sure if/how those are made

    • monkeysossidge

      Some tools have a central coolant channel and then holes are spark eroded in to meet the channel as required. Others where the coolant channels follow the helix can be made as the powdered carbide is pressed through a die. There are wires with a type of ferrule crimped on the end that float in the centre of the die as the powder is pushed through.

      Imagine the process of creating pasta twirls to get an idea of what I mean.
