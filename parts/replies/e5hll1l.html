  • Mic_Check_One_Two

    To put it another way: Imagine dipping your hand in a bucket of cool water. After a little while, the water around your hand heats up from your body heat, and becomes warmer than the water in the rest of the bucket. So now you have an insulating bubble of water shielding your hand from the cooler water in the rest of the bucket. But now if you swirl your hand around in that bucket, the water instantly feels cooler again, as that insulating bubble of water is disrupted.

    A convection oven is like constantly stirring the bucket, so it can’t ever form that insulating bubble.
