    • myself248

      Because we're not waiting around ten billion years to care about one second. We're measuring picoseconds over a period of hours, to determine the location of space probes, for instance.

      A ton of location-based stuff depends on precise time, because the speed of light is a convenient thing to measure against. The more precisely you can measure time, the more precisely you can determine location.
