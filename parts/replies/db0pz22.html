• Lung_doc

    One lung or one kidney is more than sufficient for life without modern medicine stepping in, at least in certain cases.

    It's true that many of the traumatic ways to lose an organ would often be fatal without modern interventions, but there are plenty of other options.

    The most common reason to have only one kidney is to be born with only one.

    The lungs also have a thing where one lung lacks a functional pulmonary artery, it's main source of blood to be oxygenated, so it doesn't really function. Agenesis of an entire lung, on the other hand, is rare but does exist.

    Finally, a number of nonfatal things can also destroy one lung and not be fatal. This includes severe tuberculosis where it destroys the lung, or a blood clot obstructing an entire pulmonary artery that fails to dissolve. Both would cause symptoms, but not necessarily death.

      • Peter5930

        There's little fundamental difference between two normal-sized lungs or kidneys and one double-sized lung or kidney. We could have evolved to have one lung and one kidney and have them adequately sized to our needs; we just didn't evolve that way. We evolved to have two of each, not because two small organs are necessarily better than one large organ with the capacity of two small organs, but because having two of each is a side-effect of our bilateral symmetry.

      • Lung_doc

        Humans are typically cardiac limited - in normal individuals and athletes alike, the primary determinant of aerobic exercise capacity is the heart. We can therefore lose a little lung function and not be impaired much at all.

        However, beyond the first bit of lung function, lung capacity and exercise capacity do follow a roughly linear relationship (lung function by FEV1 or FVC vs peak VO2) ref

        Practically speaking - at 50% lung function most can walk quickly, but much more often gets difficult.

        One other issue though: a healthy young person born with only one lung is likely going to have a lung capacity that measures more than 50%, because the other lung will have grown larger.
