    • Chagrinnish

      Any gyroscopic forces from two separate engines would have separate centers of rotation. It doesn't have the cumulative effect that you're thinking of; it's actually less and decreases with the distance between the rotational centers.

    • lie2mee

      Counterrotatng props are common in twin engine airplanes, and offer signficant advantages over same rotating engines.

      The asymmetric thrust created from same direction engines while the plane is climbing or flying slowly is significant enough to require a great deal of rudder to overcome. This also means that a larger vertical stabilizer and rudder is needed to maintain control during single engine operations at low airspeeds. The "critical" engine is the engine that, if it were to fail, would create the most impact on control and raise the single engine minimum control airspeed the greatest.

      Counterrotating props eliminates this problem, saves structural weight, and improves low speed performance critical for engine failures on takeoffs, for example.
