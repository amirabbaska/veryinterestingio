    Pressure cookers heat water into pressurized steam, but don't really circulate it. As steam transfers heat better than air a pressure cooker is faster than an oven for many things.

    Technically pressure cooker still cooks food by heating water and let the water transfer heat. Since the food container is pressurized, water doesn't boil at 100°C/212°F and can be heated to a much higher temperature (while maintaining liquid state) as you increase pressure. Kind of like how oil fries food but without using oil. Steam is just by-product of heating up water, and is mainly used for increasing pressure, not heating the food.
