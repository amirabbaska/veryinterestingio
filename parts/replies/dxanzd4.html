      • safewordisouch

        You are correct!
        Here's the 4 stroke version. 4 stroke engines have valve overlap. At the start of the intake cycle, the piston is at or near top dead center, causing very little suck or push and both intake and exhaust valves are slightly open.
        All non boost engine exhaust are designed so all 4 pipes go into one, that's called the collector. So now, during the phase above, another cylinder in that bank is pushing exhaust out it's pipe, causing a vacuum in the pipe that is in overlap, pulling air/fuel from the intake into the cylinder, helping to pre-fill it.
        Race engines have large overlap cams, the lumpy idle is just poor vacuum at idle and some reversion back up the intake runner. Exhaust tuning is crucial. In a race header you try and get the runners as close to equal length as possible and then play with the length of the collector tube. Your normal engines have very little overlap (smooth idle) so this is not important.
