    A very nice explanation. And it includes the most important caveat:

    This goes so far that you can basically define a symmetry and some other stuff and determine all the physics of the system from that.

    This is actually the mathematical part. The physical part of physics is finding out which symmetries are important to the system. Of course you can take educated guesses and check where to look (Higgs boson as the most recent example), but without confirmation from the real world the prettiest hypothesis is no more than a search for an extension of theory.
