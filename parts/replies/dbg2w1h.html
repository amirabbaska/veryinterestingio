• homerghost

    There is no evidence that taking any vitamins unless you have a diagnosed disease has any benefit to you at all.

    This is incorrect and very misleading. For example, being post-menopausal is not a "disease", but it is associated with a greater risk of bone density loss. Subsequently, there is a wealth of research into the benefits of Vitamin D and Calcium supplementation both to treat osteoporosis as a disease-in-progress, but also to PREVENT it in populations who are by no means guaranteed to develop it, but might.

    • Ooms ME, Roos JC, Bezemer PD, Van der vijgh WJ, Bouter LM, Lips P. Prevention of bone loss by vitamin D supplementation in elderly women: a randomized double-blind trial. J Clin Endocrinol Metab. 1995;80(4):1052-8.
    • Macdonald HM, Wood AD, Aucott LS, et al. Hip bone loss is attenuated with 1000 IU but not 400 IU daily vitamin D3: a 1 year double-blind RCT in postmenopausal women. J Bone Miner Res. 2013;
    • Bischoff-ferrari HA. Which vitamin D oral supplement is best for postmenopausal women?. Curr Osteoporos Rep. 2012;10(4):251-7.

    This is just one example of vitamin supplementation being a scientifically proven preventative measure in an otherwise healthy/normal population.

    Scurvy is another example. It can be both treated and prevented by vitamin C supplementation, especially in populations who are at risk due to diet.

    • Léger D. Scurvy: Reemergence of nutritional deficiencies. Can Fam Physician. 2008 Oct; 54(10): 1403–1406.
    • Wijkmans RAA, Talsma K. Modern scurvy. J Surg Case Rep. 2016 Jan; 2016(1): rjv168.

    Saying that "a fully balanced diet would prevent most of these too" does not negate the fact that vitamin supplements do very much work, both as curative and preventative measures.

    It also does not negate the fact that many people do not and will not ever have adequate diets. Flogging this dead horse of an argument is comparable to dismissing discussion on lung cancer because people shouldn't be smoking.

    Saying that "supplements don't prevent cancer" doesn't negate the fact that there are other benefits to supplement use.

    Saying that "there is very little that can make healthy people healthier" not only incorrectly presumes that everyone who takes supplements is optimally "healthy", but also that supplements are taken with the purpose of "augmenting healthiness" rather than to help avoid/reverse deficiencies.

    And it is a major fallacy to promote the idea that unless a nutrient is delivered to your body by flesh or plant matter, it is of zero value.

    Armchair shotgun scepticism and calling everything you don't like "snake oil" is not a helpful contribution to science.

    • WordSalad11

      This is either a very confused or dishonest and certainly specious representation of the evidence for vitamin D supplementation in elderly women. For example, the Ooms study (which used 400 IU per day) is directly contradicted by the Macdonald study (which found no benefit associated with 400 IU per day). Both studies were conducted in women with a vitamin D deficiency; the mean 25-OH vitamin D level in the Macdonald study was less than 35 nmol/L (deficiency is < 50 nmol/L). The third citation you provided recommends only supplementation in women with an identified deficiency.

      I recommend the USPTF guidance on the subject as a good, evidence-based approach based on meta-analysis of these data (URL below). They found insufficient evidence for routine supplementation of Ca/vitamin D in community-dwelling elderly women, but evidence of moderate benefit in women with vitamin D deficiency or a history of falls (so not the normal/healthy population).

      Likewise, your comments about Scurvy are similarly specious as this is not a normal/healthy population.

      At no point did I argue that people should not be screened for disease, nor did I argue that people with a diagnosed condition should not be treated.

      USPTF recs: https://www.uspreventiveservicestaskforce.org/Page/Document/RecommendationStatementFinal/vitamin-d-and-calcium-to-prevent-fractures-preventive-medication#consider
