    Also, while HIV is screened for by blood tests, every test has false positives (says it's positive when it's not) and false negatives (says it's negative when it's not. If you test only blood that has a low likelyhood of being HIV positive, you reduce the likelyhood of a false negative (and also increase the risk of a false positive, but better safe than sorry in this case)

      Beyond that -- by the time that you have the results of a blood test, you have incurred all of the costs of collecting the blood. By excluding groups that are more likely to have blood ultimately rejected, you are able to spend your resources to collect more usable blood.
