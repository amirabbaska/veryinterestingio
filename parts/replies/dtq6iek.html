      • OutOfTheArchives

        Anyone who wants to check out what newspaper typesetting was like right before computerization should check out the short documentary, “Farewell, Etaoin Shrdlu.” It was filmed at the New York Times in 1978 at the very end of the “hot lead” Linotype era. It shows how type was set, articles laid out on a page, etc. through a completely mechanical process. It doesn’t have much on how they did illustrations, but the sheer physical effort and noise that went into printing each issue of a newspaper is fantastic to see. It’s free to watch on the Internet Archive, https://archive.org/details/FarewellEtaoinShrdlu
