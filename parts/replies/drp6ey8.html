      A: You are correct that the very hot (150 million degrees Celsius) plasma in JET is held away from the walls of the container using very powerful magnetic fields.

      However, if the plasma becomes unstable it can break free from this confinement and hit the walls of the container, in the process doing a bit of damage. In this case, the loss of heat to the walls causes the plasma to collapse. So why does it not melt the walls? This is because of the relative masses of the plasma and walls. The plasma weighs only ~0.01g whereas the container is hundreds of tonnes of metal.
