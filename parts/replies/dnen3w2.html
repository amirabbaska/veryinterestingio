      That is also not true. I have read that fats can absorb better. In general, A lot of what people say is microwave mythology. The real reasons things are heated in a microwave oven are: Dielectric losses, Hysteresis losses, and Ohmic Heating or combination's thereof. I wish people would get off the usual microwave & water kick and all the other simplistic reasons.

      In addition to dielectric losses there are Eddy current losses (Ohmic or I^2 R), the electric currents that are induced to flow in the material, and cause Ohmic heating. Think about the conductivity of an ionic water solution. Currents are flowing through basically a salt water resistor. Try microwaving deionized or distilled water versus adding some salt to it.

      Some microwave browning plates use magnetic hysteresis losses. They have ferrite embedded into the plate. http://www.tpub.com/neets/book5/15g.htm

      http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hysteresis

      In short each item has to be analyzed specifically for the loss mechanisms that are responsible for the heating of that material in a microwave field of X frequency.
