    • fewyun

      Tidal forces do slow down the Earth's rotation a very small, mostly consistent amount.

      But earthquakes and weather patterns (or something else) can cause the rotation to vary a bit. Computer time uses leap seconds (not leap days) to correct for this variability.

      Edit: Leap seconds also correct for the slow consistent amount of slow down. Any change to the rotation of the earth that causes the start of a day to be off by a second.
