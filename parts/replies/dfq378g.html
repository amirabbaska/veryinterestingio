    • Deuter0stome

      Tritium is dissolved into the water. It is an isotope of hydrogen (H3) so it is unfilterable and forms water molecules just like deuterium (H2), which forms "normal" water. Tritium can come out of solution just like any other gas, but in a pressurized water reactor like where I work, it stays in solution.

      The water we used is deionized water with low dissolved oxygen. Dissolved oxygen contributes to corrosion of the fuel cladding so we maintain oxygen at levels <5 ppb at power. This is a limit set for all PWRs.
