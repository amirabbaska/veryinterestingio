    I use the "clean and gentle" detergent, I had assumed they just cut back on the dye and fragrance. Where can I get detergent that is just plain good at cleaning without all the extra marketing wank?

    • daelrine

      Find a store that sells detergents to professionals (hotel / restaurant owners). P&G has a dedicated "pro" business line for those. They would typically sale +10 kg bags or +5l bottles of detergent. When buying those you will save on:

      1. Packaging. Less visually appealing (cheaper) less packaging per kg of product sold.

      2. Perfume. Professionals don't like it and it's a pretty expensive ingredient.

      Whitening variants have more whitener (e.g. for bedsheets to look like new). Stain removal components are pretty strong as well though more targeted. When using those you need to stick to the recommended dosage. Going above may destroy the fabric.

      Alternatively, go for the cheapest laundry powder you can find. If you notice that it cannot handle certain stains, make a one-off purchase of stain remover (Vanish or Ariel Professional / Stainbuster) to deal with those stains. Than proceed back to the cheap stuff.
