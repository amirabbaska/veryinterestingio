        Yeah but different genres in rock and pop is not really the same as listening to, say, jazz v classical v pop/rock.

        Especially with something like metal which just has lots of silly, vague partitions "Death metal, black metal, green ham metal, flange whipped rock, satanic double step heavy gauge sprung metal" and so on. These are just designed for partisan reasons rather than because there's any real or significant differences to the music.

        Rock and pop is pretty much all sharing the same underlying chord progressions and so on. So it's not that difficult to find something that clicks.

        It's when you first listen to things like, e.g, Captain Beefheart's Trout Mask Replica or some of Zappa's stuff that it often sounds very dissonant, possibly many think it's just noise.

        If you listen to it enough though you tend to find it becomes musical and sounds just as musical as anything else you enjoy - perhaps richer though because of the greater complexity in the compositions (as in classical music too)
