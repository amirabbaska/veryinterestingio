    Not completely correct on the New Coke thing. "New Coke" was introduced after repeatedly losing taste tests to Pepsi. Coke Company decided to slightly change their product to have a sweeter taste (similar to that of Pepsi). After this change, they dominated taste tests, but their sales took an enormous plunge and the public opinion of it was overall negative. How was this change negatively affecting their sales if it caused them to do so well in taste tests? The answer is because they forgot the most important aspect of their product. They forgot how the product was consumed in the real world,... Instead they focused on how it worked in a testing environment. In the testing environment, you drink a couple sips and rate it based on that. In the real world environment, you drink an entire can in the comfort of your home while you snack on some chips and eat a sandwich. Coke's original and "less sweet" version was much better at performing in the real world, which is where it mattered most.

You have to be a whole new level of stupid to think any company would ever do that. Some companies make some pretty amazing mistakes, but that is so next level stupid I don't think anyone could do that.
