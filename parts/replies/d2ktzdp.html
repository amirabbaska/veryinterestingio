      • Danger-Noodles

        I'm not certain what public good that law is supposed to be ensuring.

        It's not for the public good. 3 Tier laws were originally put in place as a way of separating the producer, distributor, and retailer in an effort to keep the mafia from taking over (or at least make it tougher), and also to prevent "tied houses" like in the UK that only serve one brand of beer.

        Now days, the distributors are often the largest players in a given state, and they use their lobbyists to keep the laws the same because if the brewery can sell directly to the bar down the road the distributor is losing out on their 30% cut for being the middleman.

  • maybelying

    Just to add in general terms, manufacturers in various markets will also have contracts with wholesalers/distributors that prohibit them from selling to end users, or even anyone that isn't an authorized dealer.

    They do this to protect their dealers, so they aren't undercut by a wholesaler buying in bulk and selling direct at low markup.
