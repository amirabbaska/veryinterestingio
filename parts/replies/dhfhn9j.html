  • Linoa06

    Exactly. Usually the best translation will be a mix of the two - you want to keep some accuracy, obviously, but a translation that literally only takes each word into account with no care as to the context is little better than Google Translate. That is also why many dubs and subs of foreign-language media sometimes sound so wrong - they're translations too focused on grammatical accuracy to properly render the entire meaning and subtle cues of the original text.

    These choices in translation, as well as the relatively linearity between versions and the systematic approach of the Christian authorities to translate in every language, is what makes the Bible such a treasure trove and such a massive reference for linguistics as a whole.
