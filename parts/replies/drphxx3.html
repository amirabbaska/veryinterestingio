  • Analog24

    Heuristic explanations like this don't actually apply on the quantum level. Electrons and other quantum objects do not have a well-defined position and thus they do not actually "wiggle". This explanation also fails to account for the fact the size of the absorbing particle in this situation. Particles that are of a comparable size to the wavelength of the incoming radiation will interact. Gamma rays are significantly smaller than a typical atom and end up passing right through. Atomic nuclei are much smaller and do interact with gamma rays, which is why dense nuclear matter (like Lead) are used to shield gamma rays.
