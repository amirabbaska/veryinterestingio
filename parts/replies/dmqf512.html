      • Geo-Dood

        That's exactly what it means, and it's also why waves are noticeable mostly in the coast: as the wave approaches the shore and depth decreases, the front of the wave slows down, while the back is in a slightly deeper area, causing it to move faster.

        This difference in speeds between front and back causes the rear end to reach up to the front, stacking the water up to form tall waves.
