      • erchamion

        This isn't really what's referred to today as polyphasic sleep, though. Today the term is typically used to describe a sleep schedule where a person sleeps less at night and then breaks the rest of the day up evenly. What's described in that article is sleeping for 3-4 hours, being up for 1-2 hours, then sleeping for 3-4 hours again. You're still sleeping for the recommended 7-9 hours with a short break in the middle unlike modern polyphasic sleep where the goal is to sleep for fewer total hours and spread them over the day.
