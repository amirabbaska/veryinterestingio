    Hey there. You certainly know what it takes.

    I threw knives and such for about 10 years (12-20 years old) For knives, I threw the most classic style bowie knifes, holding them from the blade. It's all about the exact distance and knowing how many times your particular knife will spin in those distances. Like throwing a football, your finger memory, muscle memory, and making fine adjustments on the fly in your throwing motion. I had a true smaller throwing knife made by Case that I threw from the handle. I could stick it from about 21 ft on occasion.

    I threw axes as well. Traditional double blade axes. Last time I threw one of those I was at a County fair and there was a challenge to come forth and try to win a prize. I just stepped up and knew you have to have a good back draw that goes pretty close to your butt. The back edge of one blade split my pants just under my nut sack. Last throw I ever took.
