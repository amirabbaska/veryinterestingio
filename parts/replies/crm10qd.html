  • bigfrinky

    Actually rolling wheels on the ground (assuming no slip) are always dealing with static friction. This is because the point on the wheel in contact with the ground is always stationary while it is contacting the ground.

    Assuming the car that isn't moving has it's e-brake engaged, the car might budge forward a little bit without the wheels moving. In this slip condition, the wheel and the ground are experiencing kinetic friction when the wheels cannot spin, but the ground is moving relative to the stationary wheel.

    Tl;dr Wheels experience their friction counterintuitively. Rolling wheels mean static friction and stationary wheels mean kinetic friction.
