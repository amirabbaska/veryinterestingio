  • rabbit-light

    Latin defender here. I think it's reputation is deserved if misunderstood.

    In lexicon its prestige may be historically accidental, as with law, taxonomy and logic. But it's elementary for those subjects nonetheless. It's literary heritage is equally historical yet inescapable, as with philosophy and theology.

    In syntax Latin stands on its own though. Even up to the modern era scholars who knew it, such as Hobbes and Luther, used it instead of vernacular in their drafts and journals for its neatness. Latin is grammatically efficient compared to modern languages---you can say more with less ink, and with less sentential procedure.
