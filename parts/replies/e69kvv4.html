    You’re right about HIV not having a chance of being airborne. To me that’s the one silver lining of HIV—it’s an extremely frail virus and only survives literally seconds after being exposed to air. So the chances of becoming infected with it in any other way besides sex or using infected needles are almost non-existent. And those are things one has control over.

    Even coming into contact with HIV infected blood is not that risky—not that I’m advising anyone to not take precautions—but I once read an HIV expert say that the only way you could potentially contract HIV by touching infected blood is that you would have to have an open wound of some kind and the infected blood would need to come directly out of someone’s body and right into the open wound in a very small window of time.

    But if you just touch blood, it’s almost certain that the virus is already dead.
