    • resetting_this_pc

      Sometimes I will get a brief description of the sample but I've never had a "this should be 24,000SHU" I've learned from experience what each sample typically is from looks, or I taste a little raw.

      I will do my testing knowing the values, and one I have come to a conclusion, I will ask a colleague to do a blind taste of my sample.

      "The hottest" comes in 3 categories, Raw chili was around 150,000SHU, Spice blend/sauce was a lot lower around 80,000. Extract was a dizzying 22,000,000SHU, but would only be used as a flavouring, never raw.

      Yes the method is standardised, so samples are broken off into heat categories, then the dilutions are listed per category.

      And I definitely have been caught out, the 22,000,000 was labelled as a sauce not an extract, so I prepped it assuming it would be max 100,000. I did not like that client for a few days after that.

  • jaytee00

    do you do blind tastings of known concentration solutions to, uh, check your calibration? How often?

    • resetting_this_pc

      Yes we do!

      I make a solution up that has no capsicum in, and one that has juuuust enough to be detected.

      If both are tested positive or negative, the test is delayed by a few days. Or a warm 5% sucrose solution can swilled round the mouth to cleanse the pallet.

      Reasons for negative are colds and eating a lot of dairy in the morning.

      Reasons for positive are if something spicy has been eaten that morning, or sometimes even the night before.

      I do get funny looks when I say I need to calibrate someone's tastebuds for a blind taste test.
