    Dual clutch transmissions are basically a sequential manual transmission

    Unless the DCT incorporates a sequential selector, which it doesn't have to, then they are not sequential.

    Nothing about the physical design of dual clutch transmissions makes them sequential. Gears can be skipped on a DCT just like a regular transmission, just not with a seamless transfer of power (also, like a regular transmission). DCTs are "basically" two manual transmissions in one.

    What makes a sequential gearbox is the selector (which forces sequential shifts) and typically as a necessity due to the way the gears are selectively coupled to the shafts (wide spaced dog teeth over conical synchros). Because a gearbox built this way will be damaged by skipping gears, they are given a sequential selector.

    DCTs typically have none of this, and calling them "basically this" can't be further from the truth.
