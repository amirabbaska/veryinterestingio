      The crown passes to "legitimate heirs of the body", which means only children of two married people. Whether or not a gay marriage would be recognised, a child through any kind of surrogacy or adoption could not currently inherit. Even if the monarch is the biological parent of the child, they'd also have to be married to the other biological parent.

    • spazturtle

      Gay marriage isn't legal for members of the Royal Family.

      The 'Marriage (Same Sex Couples) Act 2013' only makes civil weddings between same sex couples legal, Church of England weddings are still only between members of the opposite sex.

      The 'Marriage Act 1836' is what makes civil weddings legal but it "shall not extend to the marriage of any of the Royal Family".

      So members of the Royal Family can only be married by the Church of England, and the CoE don't recognise same sex marriages.
