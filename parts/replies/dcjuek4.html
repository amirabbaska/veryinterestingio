      • LetsGoGators23

        So much like accounts due to you that are old (receivables) that you have to write off as uncollectable you can recognize some revenue as cards age.

        As accounts age they are less and less likely to be realized, be them monies owed to you or performance you owe out. Corporations have a policy for these accounts written, and if they have ever undergone an independent audit for any reason (which any corporation with stakeholders does, even if small, just even for investors or bank lines of credit) these policies have to be reasonable and revisited annually

        So yes. Technically a company could say from day 1 a gift card is only 80% collectible and recognize the revenue, but it's highly unlikely since no auditor would accept a policy without an aging schedule. So I'm sure it's primarily calculated as a percentage of total balance by age. That's the industry standard.

        And even if you DO recognize revenue at 20% off the top, if the gift card is redeemed you are having to expense the purchase with no offsetting revenue, which hurts your bottom line, and is generally something you want to avoid.

        Source: am CPA
