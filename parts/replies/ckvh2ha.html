      This is a nice presentation, but their math seems to be off by a factor of 10. The "size" of a hydrogen atom is more like half an Angström to an Angström, not ten Angström. Source

      This means that Feynman's approximation is a rather rough approximation. In addition, most of that volume is empty space and the size of the nucleus is only in the range of 10^-15 metres. Which means that, in comparison to the Earth, the apple would actually be something 100 nanometres wide (approximately 1000 times the diameter of an atom), with an "electron" probably somewhere in that 10cm sphere.
