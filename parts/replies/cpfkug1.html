    • Trubridge

      Hey. Yeah. It's hard to explain in a paragraph. But for me - the out of body part made me think differently about God/spirit/space/purpose for living and all that heavy shit. So that's a big one. I was literally floating above the scene in my hospital gown, watching all of it.
      After all of it, through therapy I figured out i had PTSD. Just from the events. Mostly nightmares of the event and feeling like I was dying all over again, hundreds of times. Avoiding anything having to do with newborn babies (I still don't like holding babies). Being in a quiet space alone and still feeling the commotion of that time/panic. Depression. Serious depression. Which actually i am just getting fully better now (5 years later) Things that matter to you before a traumatic event - will not likely be things that matter after. Work. Schedules. Some relationships. Material things. Fitting back into what people expect of you. Not a whole lot bothers me anymore. I can have anger now that I can't control. Sometimes I snap and I scare myself. Impulse control. Etc etc... none of which I struggled with prior.

      And let me say - this isn't just coma victims. People go through shit all the time & have traumatic experiences that can alter their life. This was just What it was for me.
