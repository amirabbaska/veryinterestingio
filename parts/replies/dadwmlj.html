    • pirround

      There are two different measures: sodium and salt (sodium chloride).

      2300mg (2.3g) of sodium is about 5.75 g of salt. That's the US FDA recommendation.

      The average sodium consumed in the US or UK is 3.2-3.7mg, or about 8.5g of salt.

      The danger appears to start at about 5-7g of sodium or about 12-16g of salt, with most of the data pointing to the higher end of that range.

      The really fun part is that some research even shows worse cardiac outcomes for people who consume less than 2g of sodium per day.
