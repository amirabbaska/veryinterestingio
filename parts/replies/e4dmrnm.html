    Copied my post from further down.

    The actual identification of patient zero can be important in stopping the spread of a disease as it can allow medical staff to identify the source of an outbreak, whether it is a cooling tower (legionella), animal to human transmission (flu viruses, SARS/MERS and ebola), contaminated food (E.coli, listeria), etc. Knowing patient zero and their habits can help narrow down possible sources for the outbreak. A recent trip to Saudi Arabia could be the source of a MERS outbreak, or the commute to work could pass a contaminated steam outlet for example.

    These can also be used for a baseline in looking at how the pathogen evolves in a population over tieme, this helping create more effective treatments both during the outbreak and in possible future outbreaks.
