    • GaugeSym

      Time dilation has definitely an effect. For example: Without taking time dilation into account, atmospheric muons would (on average) not live long enough to reach the ground with a velocity of 0.99c. But one observes muons with such velocities in detectors on the ground. If one considers time dilations in the calculation, the amount of measured muons is consistent again. This is one of the classical (1940s) experiments which supports special relativity.

      Muon decay and atomic decay work essentially on the same principle. Therefore this argument applies to both.
