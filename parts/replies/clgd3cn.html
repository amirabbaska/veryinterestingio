    • Sources_and_Facts

      The recommendation was leaning towards economy. Those padding packages are strictly comfort based, and I would always recommend someone get something they will be comfortable sleeping on.... however, sometimes you will get mattresses identical in construction (same coil count, coil type, same box) that have a huge disparity in cost. An example, when I sold beds, we had three Stearns and Fosters. One came in "extra firm" one was "plush" one was pillowtop. The queen sets were sticker-priced at $899.99, 1099.99 and 1499.99. That's $600 more for what is basically three inches of fiberfill and sci-fi pads and foam. They were identical in every way except for what separated you from the coils. The Beautyrests I sold later were even more sharply divided, with an entry-level "firm" stickered at $799 with a "world-class" equivalent with no pillowtop stickered above 1500. The TL:DR padding packages are selling points, but do nothing for you that a smart investment at Bed Bath and Beyond could not also do.
