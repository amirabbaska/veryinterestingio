      I'll just add the Chatham Island Black Robin are currently up to 250 individuals. This article goes a bit more into detail about how they brought the numbers up and the behavioural issues.

      They're not the only bird from my part of the world which has come near extiniction and been brought back we also have the Takahe thought extinct for 50 years or so and the Kakapo thought functionally extinct till a population was found in the 70's.

      In all cases the rebounds are closely tied to predator reduction/elimination, sometimes entire islands are made predator free (Kakapo and the Robin) sometimes it's intensive trap lines to make inland islands (Takahe), coupled with some intensive management of breading populations though nest minding/fostering/supplemental feeding in some cases/artificial insemination and so on.
