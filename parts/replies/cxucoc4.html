    • nerdbomer

      I'm going to be a little nitpicky, because AFAIK it's the other way around.

      A chip/crack would be a stress concentrator. The same force applied to that point would create more stress than other places in the screen; this increased stress is more likely to cause a screen fracture. At least that's the way the stress/force relationship is taught to mechanical engineers.

      • WyMANderly

        Nah that's correct. It cracks at the same stress regardless (ideally, at least), stress concentrators just cause that stress to be reached at a lower force. Note also that stress concentration is really only a factor when considering brittle materials or brittle failure modes (like fatigue) - you don't have to worry about stress concentration as much when you're dealing with ductile behavior because the material will just deform plastically in a local area and then be fine.
