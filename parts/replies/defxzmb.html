    Just to add to this, Tony Hawk's longevity especially in the competitive world is truly remarkable. Most professional skateboarders, unfortunately, wash out over time. There is constant pressure to do bigger and more dangerous tricks, and unlike in a lot of other professional sports, being a professional skateboarder doesn't really pay that well on average as most of your income will come from sponsorships (that don't really pay that well) and winning contests (which a lot of professional skateboarders choose not to compete in for various reasons). The wear and tear skateboarding does to your body, coupled with the fact that you're not exactly rolling in it means a lot of pros call it quits after 5-10 years. Whereas Hawk was professionally active for well over two decades.
