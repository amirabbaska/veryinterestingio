• BOBauthor

    Cepheid variable stars to not " oscillate between 2 states of nuclear fusion as the balance between gravitational and fusion pressure forces are unstable." Cepheids oscillate because of what is called the "valve mechanism." In a partial ionization zone (where an element is partially ionized, relatively close to the star's surface), the opacity of the stellar material increases when the material is compressed. This "dams up" the outflow of radiation, which pushes the stars surface layers outward. Then the less dense stellar material becomes less opaque, and the radiation is released. The surface layers fall back down, more light is trapped, and the oscillation cycle repeats itself. In other parts of the star, outside the partial ionization zone, the oscillations are damped instead of driven. In Cepheid variable stars, the driving is stronger than the damping.
