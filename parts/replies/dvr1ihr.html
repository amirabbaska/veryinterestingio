    • intellifone

      Basically yes. Think of it as a half life. You have a minimum useful quantity of material and as the original product degrades you approach the point where it is too degraded to function. In the past it wasn’t possible to manufacture such small transistors and so the product would still function as it degraded because there was more material than strictly necessary. If it was halfway degraded, it might still function. Now, there is less material and even a small amount of degradation affects performance. Let’s simplify and say that the transistors are structurally equivalent to metal wire. If the wire in your walls is 1/10 inch wire, then a rust coating of 1/100th if an inch thick after a year is pretty small. But the metal rusts at a constant rate. 1/100th if an inch is nothing to a inch thick metal plate. It’s everything to a wire that is nanometers. 1/100th of an inch is much thicker than the transistors themselves.the materials used now are much more complex than in the past. They are crazy alloys that do much more to resist degradation. They’re encased in non reactive materials so they aren’t exposed to the outside environment under any circumstances. If they were constructed similarly to older components, just smaller, they’d never turn on at all.
