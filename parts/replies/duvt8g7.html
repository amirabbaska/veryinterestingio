  • tminus7700

    Sometimes a company will alter the chemical structure or composition slightly of a drug. This allows them to get around patents of others. But the changes usually don't effect the viability of the drug action. The most subtle one I found recently advertised was Xyzal. It is a chiral variant of Zertec. The only difference between the two is one molecule is folded one way and one is folded the other way. Otherwise they are the same molecule. Same carbons, hydrogens, nitrogens, oxygens all bonded in the same structure. But for purposes of patents, not the same.
