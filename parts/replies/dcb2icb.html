    And from a chemistry perspective, "octane" isn't even actual octane. Real octane is a parent chain of 8 carbon atoms; what's commonly called "octane" in fuel is really 2,2,4-trimethylpentane, with a parent chain of only 5 carbons. The reason why it's called "octane" is because the trimethylpentane molecule has 8 carbon atoms in it, but they're not in a chain
