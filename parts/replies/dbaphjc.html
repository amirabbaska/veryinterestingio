  • shadowman-9

    Really good answer, but your definitions are just a little bit off. Angiogenesis is the formation of new vessels sprouting from pre-existing ones, regardless of size. Vasculogenesis is the formation of brand new blood vessels not from pre-existing ones. For instance, when there is a wound as in the original question, the capillaries formed sprout from the surrounding vessels and infiltrate the tissue, this is angiogenesis. In contrast, during embryonic development endothelial vessels will form from blood islands on the outside of the developing organism, this is vasculogenesis.
