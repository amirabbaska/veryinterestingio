    This has improved a lot with time actually, not only with buying higher-end gear. Dead pixels are rare these days, and considered a pretty major flaw if you have one. That's even true for cheap brands, though you would have a harder time getting customer service/repair from those brands...

    10ish years ago, even good manufacturers had acceptable numbers of dead pixels below which the screen was "good enough"... like, 10 or something. And only if they were stuck on bright colors and extra-annoying, not stuck on black and mildly-annoying.
