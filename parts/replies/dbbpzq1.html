  • roguemango

    This is it right here. The tensing for impact reflex did not evolve to deal with situations involving the velocities and forces of a car crash.

    Tensing is good for taking a hit from something made of meat not so much when you're in a metal and glass cage that was going 100kph.

    You can see it in modern car design too. Old cars would survive a crash but turn the person in it into soup filed with broken bone bits where as modern cars have crumple zones and air bags that are all designed to distribute forces rather than resist them. Just like a limp body does vs a tense body.
