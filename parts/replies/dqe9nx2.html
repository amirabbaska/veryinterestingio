  • BlondeJesus

    This is not quite correct. Time is different than spatial dimensions as it transforms differently under Lorentz Transforms and differs by a minus sign in our spacetime metric.

    What you are describing would be our universe being 4 dimensions, x y z and t. However, the universe is actually 3 + 1 dimensions (unless you ask a string theorist). If you are looking at the length between two objects in spacetime, time actually behaves as a complex coordinate. That is, the arclenght of a path through spacetime is x^2 + y^2 + z^2 - t^2 (note you might see the minus sign on the space components and plus on time, it's all matter of convention). This value is invariant under any relativistic reference frame. So, to keep this invariance time can't just be a "4th" dimension.

    As for why we don't have more time dimensions, I don't know. It's just the way the universe works. Mathematically you can add extra time dimensions and see how physics will change.
