    • Fr0thBeard

      Not OP, but I work in a pediatric home health therapy company that employs Occupational therapists (works on fine motor skills) and my wife is a Speech therapist, who also works with OT's all the time on medically fragile kiddos. It's pretty well-established that females have a much more advanced brain development, specifically in cognitive and fine motor skills. It's one of the leading reasons why they believe males are more prone to be born on the autism spectrum than females, and why neonatal cases of females tend to have fewer mental disabilities when they survive.

      A few sources, The Encyclopedia of Children's Health, National Institute of Health, and US Library of National Medicine.

      On a side note: I'm a practicing calligrapher and I love concise handwriting. I'm also male. I cannot tell you how often I hear "your handwriting looks like a girl's!". I think the top comment hit it on the head when he said it's nature followed by nurture, as neat handwriting is generally perceived as feminine.
