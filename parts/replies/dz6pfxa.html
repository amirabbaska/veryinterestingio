    • Bob_Ross_was_an_OG

      Neuroscience PhD student here. Dopamine is a neurotransmitter and a neuromodulator (those two things are not mutually exclusive). It's a neuromodulator because it can't solely excite or inhibit by itself, it can only alter the ability of another signaling molecule to excite or inhibit (glutamate and GABA, respectively). Having dopamine onboard won't cause sufficient depolarization to cause an AP, but it will make it easier for glutamate to initiate an AP. Dopamine can bring the cell closer to the AP threshold but it can't get it over the hump. Same idea for a hyperpolarization and GABA.

      Other neurotransmitters can also be neuromodulators, like serotonin, or norepinephrine.
