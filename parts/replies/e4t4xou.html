    • Laminar_flo

      Today it’s basically the same on the nasdaq and NYSE. 99.99% of trading on both exchanges (and pretty much all other exchanges) is computerized matchmaking that takes place on servers in data centers in seacacus NJ.

      The NYSE (and AMEX) back in the day had what was called a ‘specialist system’ where each stock had a specialist on the floor that matched buyers and sellers (the people screaming and yelling on the trading floor) to facilitate transactions. In the 70s, the nasdaq developed and perfected a ‘specialist-less’ computer matching system, which was inferior at first but evolved enough to eventually exceed the specialist system by the late 90s.

      Since then, the NYSE has for the most part ditched the specialist system for computer matching. There are a few floor traders and specialists left, but they see minimal volume and are basically dinosaurs that have yet to die out.
