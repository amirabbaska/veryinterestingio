    • Gastronomicus

      Two things: firstly, water consumption is highly variable person to person and by circumstance. Someone living outdoors in a hot climate might need 4-8 litres per day consumed over 10-20 different instances, or even more if they're very active. Someone living in a climate controlled environment who eats lots of fresh foods and isn't physically active might need less than a litre, possibly taken in a single instance. So there really isn't really a precedent for humans needing to drink water multiple times per day, though that's probably typical on average.

      Secondly, I guess it depends on what we define as "human". If you mean Homo sapiens sapiens, then probably always. If you include earlier Homo or even Australopithecus ancestors, then it's hard to say - but multiple drinking episodes during the day were still probably typical if access to water was good. I think looking at the habits of our closest ancestors that are omnivorous might be very helpful (i.e. Pan sp.: bonobos, chimpanzees). It's not a topic I'm very familiar with, so I can't comment more on this.
