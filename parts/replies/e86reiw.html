      • elcarath

        So the Movile Caves are actually a neat example of what OP was talking about: a totally- or near-isolated cave ecosystem, independent of external input. Other cave systems depend on external inputs, like bat guano or prey washing down the river. In Movile the food chain is self-contained and based on mineral chemosynthesis, with grazers feeding on bacteria that themselves feed on minerals.

    • Stewart_Games

      There have been a few hypogean systems - that is, caves without any surface input - found to date. Most sustain themselves from a foundation of bacteria that digest limestone or take advantage of hydrogen sulfide deposits to power chemosynthesis. Movile Cave is one such example of a completely isolated hypogean cave that can still sustain rather complex life-forms.

    • Stewart_Games

      From the Greek, meaning "mouse-hole: to dive in". Basically means "cave dweller", which accurately describes the smelly reptiles, but it also referred to a Greek myth about hairy pygmies that lived in caves south of the region of Ethiopia - a tribe they called "troglodytes". Another fun fact: chimpanzees have the species name "Pan troglodytes" because it was thought that they were the inspiration behind the Greek legends.
