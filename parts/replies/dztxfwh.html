  • gwinerreniwg

    While generally correct, your commentary overlooks the contributions of Day-Glo Color Corp. who developed mass-produced, stable florescent paints for consumer goods and litho/printing. Their invention and production of these pigments for advertising in the 1950's was specifically responsible for the subsequent explosion of fluorescent colours in adverts and consumer goods. In fact Day-Glo's pigments are so stable, you can even tell looking at vintage toys and prints today which are Day-Glo and which were a knockoff or close-to pigment, which have faded over the years.
