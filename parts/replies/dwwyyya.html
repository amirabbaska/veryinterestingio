    A bit more of an ELI20 answer follows:

    Most of it is due to taxes and local, state, and federal regulations.

    MSW is municipal as described. Typically taxed by the ton. There are many other more specific kinds of waste though, typically ladden with massive regulations.

    RMW = Regulated Medical Waste. (think needles, used bedsheets, anything potentially contaminated with blood or body fluids, or potentially contagious)

    HHW = Household Hazardous Waste (typically exempt from certain hazardous waste rules until collected at a collection location (e.g. the household doesn't have to be registered, licenced, pack it a certain way, etc.).

    HW = Hazardous Waste (regulated by the EPA and by authorized states in many cases with more strict requirements)

    Typically these other types of waste require much more expensive treatment prior to disposal (treatment being a very speciifcally defined term including incineration, wastewater treatment, etc, and disposal meaning final deposit in a landfill if not reused, recycled, or otherwise used for something other than the originally intended purpose of the waste).

    Source: Safety/Compliance manager for a relatively large company that deals with said items.
