      • Eliot_Ferrer

        All skeletons have them. You need to know what you're looking for, but unfortunately I'm not knowledgeable enough to show you. What I can say is that in general, serving as origin and attachment points for muscle is one of the primary functions of bones. From the size of a bone, you can infer what sort of muscle it supports, and how much power it generates (it generally correlates with size) If you look at an elephant skull, you'll notice that the part corresponding to where the nose would go is absolutely massive. That suggests it supports a large muscular structure, which we know to be true.
