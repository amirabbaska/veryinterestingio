  • konaya

    It's still a bad idea to run your web site off the bare domain. You can't put a CNAME record on a bare domain, for instance, so using a content distribution network such as Akamai is right out. Cookie management does also benefit from using www; a stored cookie from www.example.com won't be sent to user.example.com, for instance, but a cookie meant for example.com will be sent to all subdomains. This is a security problem, and also a performance problem if you're big enough; Twitter has to host their static content on an entirely different domain (twimg.com) just because the marketing dweebs won over the people who actually run the show.

    Perhaps most importantly, though, is that running your web site without the www makes you look like the same kind of vacuous prick who sorts books on the shelves by colour.
