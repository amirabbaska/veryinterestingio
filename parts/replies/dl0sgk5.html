    This is possibly one of the clearest and strongest answers I've read on here.

    Currently I am a medic though I spent a large portion of my youth living and breathing mma.

    Anecdotally, I was very comfortable and became very adept at angling off on punches and essentially attacking them... with my face. Most people thought because I seemed unphased that I possessed some super human propensity to take blows to the head. When really it's just about timing and spatial awareness.

    Realistically it is just a calculation where you change the angle the attack is coming from by altering your own position.

    So by tucking and slightly turning your head you are stabilising your skull for impact and ideally mitigating transfer of kinetic energy to the target (your face). If performed properly you won't get that whiplash effect which is generally the most serious offender regarding generation of concussions.

    This doesn't mean that you don't still suffer trauma, it's just generally much less trauma. Lower levels of concussions on the scale and less of them overall over the course of a match or bout.
