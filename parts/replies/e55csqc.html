      • thephantom1492

        Chance is that it will put up the antenna, and start looking for the earth signal.

        The scheduled transmissions is most likelly not like a phone call, more like put up the antenna, aim the earth, transmit the data, put down, do whatever else it have to do, and put up at the scheduled time to await commands. If the signal is not found then it will most likelly wait for the full transmission window, then put it down until the next transmission window.

        A transmission window is the time at which a transmission is possible. Think of the moon. When can you transmit/receive data from a lunar base? When you see it. With some math you know when such time is possible. When it is out of that time then it will just put down the antenna to protect it and save power.

        If the rover lose time, it will most likelly put it up 24/7 and scan the sky all the time when power is available (i.e. the battery have some charge left).

        It is all a priority question: what is more important now: protect the antenna? Or reestablish the signal? Or save power for the night? That is what the rover will do.
