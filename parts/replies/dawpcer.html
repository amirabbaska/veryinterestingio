    • RedditorDoc

      Mhmm. That's a very good example of how the two types of pains meet. The appendix is a visceral organ, so pain sensation from here is poorly identified to the area around the bellybutton.

      However, if an inflamed appendix touches the inner lining of the abdomen, then it's picked up by the somatic pain nerves, in which case the pain becomes sharp and easy to pinpoint. In your situation though, he entire inner lining of your abdomen was inflamed, which is a pretty serious condition. The sensation you had when the PA lifted her fingers is called rebound tenderness, and it's a giveaway for peritonitis.
