    To add to this a little, antiseptics/disinfectants (disinfectants are like antiseptics but for not living surfaces and tend to be more abrasive) tend to operate via different mechanisms when compared to antibiotics.   • TheLakeAndTheGlass

    “Anti-bacterial” actually can mean “bactericidal” (kills bacteria outright) or “bacteriostatic” (inhibits bacterial growth). Triclosan, the (until recently) popular antibacterial soap active ingredient, is a bacteriostatic agent and only kills bacteria directly when in ridiculously high concentrations that aren’t seen in commercial products.
