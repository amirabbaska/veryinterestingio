  • SmatteringOfPoo

    Yes, what you describe is exactly what I experienced. I threw myself into the military life/ethos pretty dramatically. This resulted in a ridiculously difficult transition to civilian life when I got out. One second I was an American badass on a whole team of American badasses that I loved and respected, the next I was a random civilian with a DD214 that meant nothing to anyone. I had no connections in the outside world, and a list of qualifications that were suddenly useless. Everyone wanted employees with college educations and job experience. I couldn't get a job for months after I got out. Cue the depression and anxiety, and it all gets compounded. I did have a ton of terminal leave and some savings. Without that, I very well could've been homeless in a matter of weeks.

    Maybe I just wasn't paying attention while I was in, but I was woefully ignorant of the assistance that I was eligible for through the VA. I have since taken advantage of a ton of mental health services that, had I known, would've been indispensably helpful upon getting out. I suspect that this true for other vets as well.
