  • chem_mage

    You're on the right track, but clumping happens in other powders that aren't "water soluble"

    The phrasing you're looking for is "hydrophobicity/hydrophilicity"

    Coffee beans contain oils, and grinding them releases these oils which coat a good amount of the grounds, which in turn makes them less susceptible to absorbing moisture from the air.

    • Rubdybando

      The roasting process is what determines the oiliness of the bean even before it's ground.
