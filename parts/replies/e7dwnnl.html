    I am from New York State. I align myself with alot of laissez faire concepts, and hold a strong distrust for socialist implements. I believe that relying on a free-market society gives more control to a healthy middle-class and individualism while more public methods are arbitrary, invasive, and suppressive even for the poor to bring themselves up. I don't believe non-voluntary (non-local taxes, unions) are either ethical or effective. I am, in my personal experience, the massive minority in political belief of my generation.

    Yet, I do think America is tainted by an excessive work culture. To live by your own means you invest a good 40 hours of your life (That really is a lot of our life spent working) every week, usually only after spending 2-6 years in some form of higher education working even harder and putting yourself in debt for awhile. Most people even if they become well-off, have no time for travel, art, and are filled with stress. What is the point then?

    I think economically we suffer from both corporate and state dependence, the common man will have a hell of a time with a startup business, and no job short of technological specialist or high-end manager will pay for a house, or even an apartment really. Today it seems the popular policies of both Right and Left empower both the shady big business and invasive and controlling state.

    The current situation is complex, and I too do not know the answer, and it is clear that free-market without regulation is vulnerable to abuse, yet the alternative is a slew of highly-invasive operations that drain our populace in a different way.
