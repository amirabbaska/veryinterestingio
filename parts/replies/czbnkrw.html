      Is this saying that, past 14 billion light years (size of universe now), space (and the objects in it) are traveling faster than the speed of light so our gravity cannot "catch up" to it?

      The 14 billion light year figure isn't, necessarily, the size of the Universe overall. It's a very particular measurement of the size of the observable universe- to be precise, it's the distance to the cosmic event horizon, past which objects are receding faster than the speed of light and can never encounter us again.

      Other than that, yeah, that's the basic idea.
