    • throwaway_MZ3Ji8yc

      What you're calculating is the angular resolution needed to resolve features within the star, not the resolution to separate stars as distinct objects.

      If two stars are 10 light years apart (in the component tangential to the line of sight), their angular separation is (10 ly / 2.5 Mly) ~ 1 arcsecond. So you could resolve them in principle.

      You're right that you couldn't see details on a star at that distance: they'd be indistinguishable from points. But if they're bright enough, they're still visible points.
