      The fire hazard arises anywhere the Al makes a junction with a material of significantly different coefficient of thermal expansion. Over time the differing rates of expansion and contraction can lead to gaps forming between the two conductors, producing arcing which may serve as an igniton source. However, we now have a techniques to circumvent this problem or retrofit older homes that posses Al wiring.

      While not quite feasible today, ill speculate that carbon based transmission lines could arise. These might use conductive carbons or conductive polymers to transmit electricity. Additionally tight-beam energy transfer by microwaves could also be used. Finally I have seen research into calcium nanoparticles being used within transmission lines which could reduce the overall amount of Al necessary - I'm sure there are variants on this with other materials. Not quite sure if any of these approaches could scale to the level of our current grid.
