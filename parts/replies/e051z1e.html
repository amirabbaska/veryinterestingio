    • rabbitwonker

      Sort of. The speed of expansion of Space Time is faster between two points as those two points get farther away from each other. Eventually that expansion is faster than the speed of light, and this does correspond to light getting redshifted to infinity and thus undetectable. However it’s deeper than that, because this is actually the speed of causality we’re talking about, meaning that threes fundamentally no way for further information to pass between those two points; the frequency of light is just one aspect of this.

      Another factor is that in the early years of the universe, matter was still packed densely enough that virtually all light was re-absorbed very soon after it was emitted. So we can’t see light from, say, 1 second after the Big Bang, because all those photons were intercepted by other matter before they could get very far. As the universe expanded, the average distance a photon could travel increased, until finally it reached a point where that distance was virtually unbounded. The light from this moment is what we see as the Cosmic Microwave Background radiation. That’s the earliest light we can see.
