It also explains why you should NOT put salt on ice in VERY cold weather; it will tend to melt the top of the ice, which will then refreeze, probably to a slicker surface than it was originally. The point to remember is that the salt doesn't melt ice directly, it changes the temperature at which ice melts.

    For ice cream, of course, you want the cream to freeze faster, so lowering the freezing point will make it happen faster at a given temperature.
