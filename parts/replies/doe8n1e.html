    This is correct but a couple of disclaimers—overrun is not a bad thing. It is important to balance, taste, texture, and process.

    High overrun isn't necessarily indicative of cheap ice cream, either. The higher the fat the higher overrun that is usually necessary to avoid shear (where the fat separates from the mix and turns to butter), at least in certain mixes on certain equipment.

    Also, without some overrun, ice cream would be tasteless. Those tiny little bubbles of air explode like flavor bombs on our tongues. When you conduct the experiment suggested above, taste the flat melted stuff alongside a fresh pint. Despite having the same amount of fat, sugar, and everything else tasty, it lacks air and thus taste.

    There is one ice cream company with significantly more than 50% overrun that is far superior to any super premium or super super premium ice cream I've ever tasted. So the figures above should be taken with a grain of salt.
