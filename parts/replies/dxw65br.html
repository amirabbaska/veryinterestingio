    • VeryLittle

      So suppose the sun was 1 million Kelvin all throughout. Not a bad assumption, it's about 15 million in the core, and about 5,000 K at the surface.

      The kinetic energy of a proton in a 1 million Kelvin gas is found from 3/2 k T (k is the Boltzmann constant). That's 0.0001 MeV. Compare that to the mass-energy of a proton, 939 MeV/c^(2). That thermal energy is less than a millionth of the energy per particle.

      For all practical purposes, you never really need to consider this.

    • NonstandardDeviation

      You might be interested in reading about wave superposition, and the linearity of wave equations. The equations governing most waves - such as ripples in a pond, sound in the air, earthquakes in rock, string, and light or radio in the electromagnetic field - are linear (at least to first order for small enough magnitudes). As a result, waves pass through each other without affecting one another, simply adding up in the exact region of overlap. This is how intereference works.
