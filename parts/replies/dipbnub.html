  • aroundlsu

    As an added FYI bonus, the smoke/atmosphere effects are created by the special FX department. They handle wind, rain, smoke/fog. One of the best methods of creating smoke/fog are the "tubes of death" which are long black tubes run along the ground around a scene with tiny holes punched in them. The fog is pumped through the tubes continuously. The tubes can be hundreds of yards long and surround the entire set so no matter which way the wind is blowing you still have fog.

    Another good method are converted lawn mowers that pump thick layers of smoke out of their exhaust with a special fuel. They can be pushed all around the perimeter of a war scene to create thick clouds of battle scene smoke.

    Low budget features can use a mosquito fogger bought at wal mart and filled with special fluids.
