      Keep in mind that while this explanation is entirely correct, if a material existed that wasn't held together by atoms in this way it still wouldn't work. It's not a materials problem, it's still an energy problem.

      Spin a ruler in your hand, then spin a yardstick. It's harder to do right? More mass, more inertia to overcome. Any material with mass is going to require energy to move, and the faster you want it to go he more energy is required. As you approach C the energy required tends toward an infinite amount. You cannot put enough energy into the system, any system you can contrive with mass, to ever reach C. End of story.

      Not trying to be pedantic, but the materials explanation always seems to lead to some jackass speculating about a better material, not understanding that it breaks because of a fundamental property of atoms.
