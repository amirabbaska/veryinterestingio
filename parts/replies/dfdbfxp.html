      The orbitals for the Hydrogen atom are actually relatively simple (at least compared to heavier elements). If you're programming a 3D graph you could plot the first few orbitals -- just write the equation and make the opacity of each point in space proportional to the square of value of the function at that point. The parameters are:

      Z = charge of the nucleus. For Hydrogen this is equal to 1.
      a0 = atomic radius. You can just set this to 1 as well, since the scale of the diagram won't matter.
      e^(something): exponential function. Most programming packages will have this built in, just search for "exp()" or similar.
      r, θ, φ: spherical coordinates in 3D space. r is the distance from the centre, θ and φ are angles.
