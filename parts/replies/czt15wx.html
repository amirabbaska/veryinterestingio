  • FaceDeer

    You could perhaps argue that an assemblage of galaxies is part of the same "unit" if they are gravitationally bound to each other, ie, if they are not moving at escape velocity relative to each other.

    This actually does provide a fuzzy sort of "maximum size limit" thanks to the continuing expansion of space due to the big bang. It's possible for galaxies to be far enough apart that the space between them is expanding faster than their mutual gravity is pulling them toward each other, and thus they will eventually fall away from each other beyond their observable horizons.

    This maximum size is dependent on how much mass the cluster has, though, so it's not really a hard and fast number.
