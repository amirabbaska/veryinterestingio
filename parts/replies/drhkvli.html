    • russellomega

      Filtration is when the medium retains the impurities as the clean water passes through. Osmosis is different because the impurities are left on the other side of the membrane but not as much in the membrane itself. That means an osmosis membrane can be reused and can be used for longer than if it were actually filtering the impurities out of the water.

      The word "reverse" is used because water is forced against, or in the reverse direction of the gradient, requiring external pressure to overcome the osmotic pressure that would ordinarily prevent water from going from high to low salinity
