      Look, you keep saying this in multiple areas. A little knowledge can be dangerous. Whilst you are correct that for small boats anchors hold them in place via lateral forces, in any decent sized ship it is the cable that holds the ship in place. The anchor is only there to hold the end of the cable in place.

      When seeking an anchorage in a large vessel there are a number of calculations that must be performed depending on, the depth of water, wind/sea state, swing radius and the amount of time that you will be waiting. The output is always a length of cable, not number of anchors or weight of anchors.

      Given that your comments now respond to the top two answers I'd be grateful if you could amend your answers so as not to confuse people.

      TL:DR: in large ships the cable holds the ship in place.
