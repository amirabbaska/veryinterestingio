    • DankNastyAssMaster

      A complete protein is a food that contains all 20 amino acids your body uses to construct proteins. Some AA's can be manufactured by your body, others (the "essential" ones) you have to get directly from food. https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Essential_amino_acid

      If you're a vegetarian, beans, peanut butter, soy/tofu, nuts and seeds, eggs, cheese, and milk are good sources of proteins. Not all are complete proteins though, so you should try not to focus too much on any one source.

      If you aren't, fish, poultry, and meats are all complete proteins by definition.

      Source: grad student in biochemistry
