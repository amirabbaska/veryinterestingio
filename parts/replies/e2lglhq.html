    Those were the commonly used names for battery sizes when I was growing up in Germany in the 80s. Especially "Mono", "Baby", and "Mignon". These were the names/sizes printed on the packaging when buying them; or printed on the packaging of electronic items where they told you which batteries the product needed.

    And I never could remember which was which - I mean how could you. How is "Baby" not the smallest size?

    Anyway, I was so glad batteries were later simply labelled AA and AAA, because that made sense; and the other sizes were rarely used any longer.

    Today, I am back in an even worse position. More and more poducts require button cells these days, and I'll be damned if I can remember all the effing numbers. They are mostly CR-somethings, but I now have to write the numbers down because otherwise I am sure to buy the wrong one.
