    The term you're looking for is economies of scale, but it's not quite what you've described. It's where making more of something leads to reductions in cost per [some amount].

    Because of this effect, if you're making 100kg of baking soda, and the raw materials are $2 per kg produced and it costs $20 to run whatever you need to run to produce any amount up to the limits of your plant/machinery... then you have a product that costs $2.20 per kilo.

    If your upper limit is 500kg, then it will cost you $2.04 per kilo to make that much baking soda.
