    Orthostatic hypotension. Both involve a lowered blood pressure in the brain, but the cause is different. With orthostatic hypotension your heart doesn't respond quickly enough when you stand up and the blood drains from your brain.

    Vasovagal syncope ('fainting') is caused by your brain triggering a reflex that actively slows your heart rate.
