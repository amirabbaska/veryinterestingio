    Yes, but the reason this works is because thrust=acceleration, for all intents and purposes, when opposing forces (drag, friction, etc) approach zero, as in space.

    Sure, a millionth of a meter per second of acceleration is minimal, but when it's not counting tired by drag, it's entirely cumulative, and so by the time you've accelerated for months at that rate, you're moving along pretty good.
