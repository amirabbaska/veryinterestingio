      To piggyback: I used to work for a vending company that owned and managed thousands of jukeboxes for bars and restaurants, etc. Every box has to have a license that is renewed every year. A portion of the fee for that license goes to Ascap and/or BMI. These agencies are ruthless, but their purpose is perfectly understandable. If you have unlicensed karaoke or if your tv is on a music channel and you don't have a public listening license, a rep might sit in your bar all night long, counting violations without saying anything. I heard horror stories of fines in the tens of thousands of dollars at bars that might clear a hundred bucks a night in profit. These may have been boogimen though. Never saw that myself, but the message is clear: pay your license fee, avoid trouble. Once digital jukeboxes came along, and computers starting tracking plays automatically, everyone got a lot happier. Except the customers. Songs cost more to play now because the artists know what they're worth to us.
