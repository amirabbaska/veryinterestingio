• weicheheck

    Wouldn't it be a lot more effective to have a program designed for testing games that can clock way more hours and costs less than humans?

    • SpyderRyder_

      Great question. Short answer: Yes-ish.

      Detailed answer: We have some internal tools that are in development which pretty much do what you're talking about.

      Over the last 2-3 years, we've been attending large QA Conferences and know that some other studios (bigger ones, like Blizzard, EA) are a bit ahead of us in these regards, however, even programs can't get everything. Or at least, making a tool that could get everything would be an enormous task in itself. What we're aiming for is to make a sort of plug-and-play interface that could be used across all current and future projects.

      I believe the idea is to get QA involved with development a lot earlier, and to hook it up to the engines themselves to pull data. Our internal tools are being made to do some of the more mundane tasks, so that we can let testers focus on the creative side.

      Things such as buying 1,000,000 potions in the shop, selling them back, then doing it at every shop, with every item in each shop. Picking up and dropping each item in the game in every-single-location in-game. Stuff that can be easily done with broad terms, like "Spawn character at position X.Y.Z", kill each enemy in the game with every type of weapon in the game, stuff like that. Or just mashing all the buttons repeatedly. Or saving after taking a single step, times a billion. Things that are pretty much humanly impossible. I mean we COULD ask the testers to do that but they would probably kill themselves.
