    To expand on the electricity, etc. For electricity, the gap inside walls allows for the placement of cables and receptacles and switches and stuff so that the "works" are a) hidden from view for aesthetics and b) protected from accidents (spills, falls, crushing, etc.)

    Currently, the National Electric Code (U. S. A.) requires a receptacle every so many feet, so walls pretty much have to have them. You can divide rooms with just flat panels, but if they exceed a certain length, you have to figure out how to put power where the code tells you to put it. If the panels slide, or move in some way, I guess that would be a door, and would eliminate the need.
