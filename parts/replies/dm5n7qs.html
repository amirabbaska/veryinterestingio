      • brieoncrackers

        From what I know of my archaeology classes, even digging it out of the ground destroys a lot of information about the thing. The matrix it's deposited in can have a lot of other information important for understanding the context of the fossil/artifact, and you're just never going to get that information again once it's fully removed. Given that, destructive methods of inspection have to be weighed against non-destructive methods present now or in development and how common the find is. Like, if you have a shark tooth or an arrow head and you want to smash them with a hydraulic press, that's going to be less of a loss than taking the only known example of something and even very carefully splitting it open or vaporizing a tiny piece of it for chemical analysis.
