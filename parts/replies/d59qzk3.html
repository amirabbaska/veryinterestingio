      • GepardenK

        We can infer that the concept of 'free-will' is an illusion even without evidence from space-time theory.

        Having 'free-will' implies that you are able to make a choice about what to choose before you chose it, which is a paradox. I can ask you to think of a specific country and you may think that you have the free-will to think of any country you like, but you don't since you have no way to choose what country to think of before you though of it.

        Even the decision to change the country to another one once you thought of it is not a free choice but simply something that popped up in your head (before it popped up in your head you had no way of knowing that this was what you were going to choose to think about, hence the choice was not a free one).

        Do note that while the notion of free-will is basically impossible that does not make choices or the act of making choices unimportant from the perspective of a human existence.
