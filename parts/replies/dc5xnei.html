    Expanding on that point, there are neurodegenerative diseases that are directly linked to DSM diagnosable disorders.

    Neurocognitive disorder – (mild or major) don’t affect or affect daily functioning.

    Significant decline in at least one of cognitive functioning, such as memory and learning, attention, visual perception, planning and decision making, language ability, or social awareness; personality changes—may behave inappropriately—and their symptoms worsen

    Neurocognitive Disorder Due to Alzheimer’s Disease- Individual displays the features of major or mild neurocognitive disorder.

    . Memory impairment is a prominent feature.

    . Genetic indications or family history of Alzheimer disease underscore diagnosis, but are not essential to diagnosis.

    . Symptoms are not due to other types of disorders or medical problems.

    These are considered psychopathological disorders by the DSM.
