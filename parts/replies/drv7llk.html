  • Anjin

    Combine all that with the fact that residential usage in California is something like 2% of the total water consumption and you can see why there isn't a big push. People's lives aren't at risk in CA droughts, even in the last severe drought there wasn't a risk of people running out of drinking water except in places where the water table dropped and municipalities relied on well water. Instead business revenues in farming and industry are at risk, and those businesses aren't willing to pay the sky high costs for desal and long distance pumping.
