    I am a cellular bone researcher. If someone worked out how to deposit calcium compounds into microabrasions on teeth enamel completely chemically without using the body's cells, then any trademark would very quickly be removed. That kind of technology would completely revolutionise the way we treat teeth and bone disorders. What's far more likely is that there haven't been extensive tests beyond "this is safe for human consumption" and they're "postulating" its effects based on preliminary results. Skin care, hair care, and toothpaste companies are absolutely notorious for doing this, and the vast majority of their research is purely making sure the product is safe, and then finding a hook that'll get them a patent or trademark based on pilot study level results. Most of the time, no further results are ever seen, and the brand will eventually shift onto the next trend in the industry.
