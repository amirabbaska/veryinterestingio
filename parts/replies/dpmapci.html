    What are the uses/benefits of those ultra low field scanners?

    • opticalsciences

      You can put folks with larger metal plants (heart stents, deep brain stimulators, etc) that are (depending upon the exact model) incompatible with strong magnetic fields.

      Since they’re resistive and not superconducting, there is less maintenance and cost from maintaining liquid helium and liquid nitrogen.

      They’re also a lot cheaper to build. Superconducting wire is way more expensive to build than copper.

      Smaller field also means that they won’t have as large a ‘fringe field’ an area which magnetic items will get sucked in. This makes placing them in hospitals or clinics much easier.
