      • SanityContagion

        Not going to pretend I'm an expert, but I can tell you that learning to shoot stills or video with crappy lighting is just as instructional to anyone willing to review their own work with a very critical eye. I learned more about lighting trying to shoot in low light environments that I thought I would. Specifically, I tried to snap clubs and parties where hauling around flashes and lights completely destroyed opportunities for candid shots. I learned quickly that lighting color completely changes the composition. If you have the chance, shoot the same thing with different lights and really critically review the results. I'd further suggest you have someone else look too, because you might learn something about your own biases in your resulting images or video.

        And remember to have fun with it. Try some extremes(lighting) that should make no sense... the extremes can make the best learning experiences.
