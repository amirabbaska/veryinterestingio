      • Helt-Texas

        That said, Hollywood depictions of EMP are usually wrong about everything else...

        For example, a machine fried from an EMP won't start working again after a few minutes. If the EMP was transient and the affected electronics are no longer operational, they were probably disabled due to destructive short circuiting or overheating.

        This shouldn't be confused with a more persistent EM discharge causing interference in the circuitry of less sensitive electronics (especially equipment without microelectronics). Such electronics may only be nonfunctional/dysfunctional due to the presence of electrical noise.

        Hollywood also tends to ignore range based effects. Everything within some distance is simply shown to turn off.
