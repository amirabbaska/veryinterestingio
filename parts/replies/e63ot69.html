    Polar bodies are actually the process of meiosis. Most every cell division is equal in everything. Equal dna split equal organelle split equal cytoplasm split except for egg cells. The final egg cell is visible with the naked eye because it has so much cytoplasm. The remaining cells are called polar bodies. Each having DNA but not normally large enough for fertilization and are just there. But sometimes they may not separate from a fully mature egg cell. I graduated with twins that were initially thought to be identical but later found out to be polar twins. The DNA was vary close but slight differences but not enough to be fraternal.

    • donnersaurusrex

      The final step of female meiosis doesn't occur until fertilisation. Oogenesis is really split up, and starts with mitosis to create a primary oocyte, which enters prophase I at the end of the first trimester. Meiosis I happens at ovulation (creates first polar body) and meiosis II happens at fertilisation, creating a mature egg and the second polar body.
