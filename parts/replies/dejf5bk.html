  • CrivCL

    which means on the same test, an american barely passing grade of 70% would be a reasonable high test score in Finland.

    I think that's the thing. It's definitely a bit of a headscrew for US students to get but the tests aren't the same in these systems to the ones you're used to. Every mark is progressively harder to get than the previous one.

    So, for an example engineering exam, you could have 6 long form questions where you have to pick 4 to complete in the 3 hr exam.

    In each of those questions, you typically have 3 sections - one explicit basic knowledge/application (5 marks available), one intermediate "bread and butter" application (5 marks available), and one advanced "do you understand the nuances" application (15 marks available). So, to pass (40%), you need to nail all your basic and intermediate questions perfectly or have a moderate grasp of them and some ability to at least get some "valid attempt" marks on the advanced questions.

    Speaking for our own degree scale, a 70%+ would be a First Class Honours - you'd expect between 5 and 15% of a class to get one in an exam. For a sense of scale, typically a Second class first division (60%) at the end of your degree would be seen as adequate for entry to a PhD - in the US, the same entry would be a 3.0 GPA.
