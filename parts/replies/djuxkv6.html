    What decides how much of the equation is equalised by space shrinking and how much is equalised by distance contracting?

    • Fahlm

      Time dilation always occurs, and follows a pretty simple equation as long as acceleration is not involved; at which point the physics gets quite a bit more complicated (though time dilation still behaves predictably in every situation involving acceleration).

      Space "dilation" (length contraction) also occurs in a way. The actual distance between you and a moving object doesn't change, but anything moving appears to be shorter the faster it moves. This shortening of an object only happens in the direction of motion. Whether or not objects truly get shorter as they move faster is a matter for debate; the center of that debate being that proper length (the length of something with no velocity relative to you) doesn't change.
