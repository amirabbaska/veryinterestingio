  • Mdcastle

    The "Stokes shift" is the grown-up name for this phenomenon. Some things will absorb light and then re-radiate it at a lower wavelength. This is most dramatic when say a black light poster absorbs UV and re-radiates it in visable colors. But it's very important commercially. Fluorescent tubes naturally emit mostly UV, but phosphors shift the UV to green and orange (which we perceive as white). "White" LEDs are mainly blue, with phosphors to shift some of the blue to yellow so we see them as white.
