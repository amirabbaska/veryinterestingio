    As an afterthought on this synopsis, another frequently asked question is "Why is the speed of light such a strange number like 299,792,458 m / s?" When you realize that the meter was originally designed by the French to be one ten-millionth of the distance from Earth's north pole to equator, and the second was designed by Mesopotamians to be a sixtieth of a sixtieth of one twelfth of daylight, an accident of the rotation of the earth, then it should be no wonder that c is such an oddball number. And in fact, in "sensible" units, the speed of light is 1.
