  • IronOntap

    This explanation holds up on a socio-linguistic level as well. Learning a dialect and beginning to imitate it is an integral part of the language learning process that we as human beings are preprogrammed to do. Copying a speech pattern in your mother tongue follows similar steps as learning a foreign language does. The more time spent surrounded by the linguistic culture', the more apt one becomes at mimicking the speech patterns until it is a natural part of how we speak.

    What is particularly interesting is our ability to 'code-switch', which just means changing the speech patterns depending on the cultural situation you find yourself in. The simple explanation is that you don't necessarily speak to your close friends the same way you would your boss.

    The better you are at adapting your language to fit the recipients language, the better they will be able to perceive it the way you meant for it to be perceived.

    This is very well said and the reason why cultural literacy is such an important component in, well, learning to be human really.
