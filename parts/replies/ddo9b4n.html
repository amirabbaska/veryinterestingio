      A person gets a jobs saves for a house, spends decades paying it off, saves for retirement, stops working and then lives off their savings. So, ideally, the older you get the more money you have on hand. If you retire at 60 and hope to live to 80 then on the day you retire you have 20 years worth of money to replace your income.

      Very few businesses have more than 6 months worth of revenue available. Even big ones. This surprises a lot of people but if Wal-Mart stopped selling stuff tomorrow and had zero revenue they have about a weeks worth of revenue of cash in the bank. That's right. Wal-Mart has half a paycheck in cash savings. If they sold all their stores, warehouses, every truck etc they have less than 6 months worth of revenue. Why? Because they have better uses for the money.

      When a business makes money (after expenses i.e. profit) they spend it on things like:

      • investing back in the business to make more money next year.
      • paying down debt
      • giving money back to the people who own the company (shareholders) via share buyback or dividend

      Basically, unlike a person, companies don't retire so they don't really save up big nest eggs. They hope to live forever.

      They do buy houses though, i.e. massive purchases they can't afford to pay upfront so they borrow money. For example if Wal-Mart wanted to build 4 new warehouses this year they wouldn't have the money in cash so they'd have to borrow it.

      They don't go to Bank of America like you or I. They have complex lending needs to they need complex banks. That's where something like a Wall Street bank comes in. They do banking that isn't familiar to average people so it's hard to relate to.
