    • Frankobanko

      For metal not a big problem. For glass a huge problem. Also you have to think of it as a whole system with parole trying to make money. One reason residuals are bad is because the buyer who is going to process the material such as the glass doesn't want to pay for residuals. So the MRF operator says here's a ton of glass that has 10% residual. Well now the buyer doesn't want to pay 100% for 10% he can't use. So the price the MRF can get for the glass drops but the cost to separate that glass + residuals didn't drop so the MRF loses money. With single stream the residual in glass has increased so the cost for a MRF to process glass is more that what they can sell it for.

      Second no one wants to pay to truck around residual. It cost around 10 cents per mile per ton to more commodity efficiently. So a glass processor doesn't want to pay to move stuff other that clean glass.

      With metal it's easier to get rid of residual than glass, but the exact same principals I outlined above apply.
