    Listen to this response - if your system doesn't use credit scores the same way the US does, be very particular about which card you choose.

    I travel a lot of work, which I pay for with a travel rewards card and then gets reimbursed from my company, so I literally get free travel points which I can then use to travel for fun for free. But in addition to that, it comes with full insurance of all my travel - if my baggage is lost it reimburses me for any purchases I have to make for clothes, etc while waiting for my bag to arrive; if my flight is delayed it reimburses me for any purchases or hotel stay needed during the delay; it provides full coverage for any rental car so I don't have to use my personal car insurance. And so many more protections while traveling. There is a $450 fee, but they​ give you $300 towards travel off the bat and $100 towards global entry, TSA pre check, etc, so the fee comes out to $50 which far out weighs the free travel I get out of it.

    So even though this will be your first card, if there's no such thing as building credit for you, definitely look into the perks and pick out a card that will really work for you. Of course, don't over reach and apply for one you have a bad chance of getting​ because being denied for a card will look bad.
