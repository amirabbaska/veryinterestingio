    • Eauxcaigh

      In practice small mid-course correction burns may occur near anti-nodes, and in order to satisfy the rendezvous constraints it may utilize an out of plane component, yes.

      You are correct in that nodes are generally placed near major burns to be more efficient in eliminating the out-of-plane velocity through root-sum-squaring it to an already large in-plane deltaV. However, particularly for problems with constrained arrival times, some burns are not lined up with apogee or perigee. You burn when you need to burn to get there when you need to get there.
