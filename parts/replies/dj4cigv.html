    To add - the Atlantic is growing (at about the same rate as a human fingernail) because of seafloor spreading along mid-ocean ridges, and the Pacific is shrinking because of continental plates sliding beneath others in subduction zones.

    Edit: Also, India may still be plowing into China, Himilayifying the continent ahead of it, so that's another place to look for changes in the far future.

      I would think that the Himalayan Orogeny is mostly over as the two plates suture together (seen in the northernmost tectonic unit, the Indus Suture Zone).
