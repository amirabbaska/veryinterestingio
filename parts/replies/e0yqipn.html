    • AlkaliActivated

      If you were an atom on the edge of that disk, what would your velocity be?

      The nice thing about the unit system of "radians per second" is that velocity is just the product of that number and the radius. So an atom at a radius of 1m spinning at a rate of 350 radians per second would have a tangential velocity (relative to the center) of 350 m/s.

      How does that compare to the speed of sound in aluminum?

      About 5%. The speed of sound in aluminum is over 6300 m/s.

      Interestingly, 350 m/s is about the speed of sound in air, so with some minor improvements in material quality, you could build a disk that spins so fast that the edge breaks the speed of sound in air. I have no idea what kind of sound it would make, though. Sonic booms are always shown as being generated tangentially to an object, so I don't know if a sonic boom would be generated.
