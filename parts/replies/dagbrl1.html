    No! Your momentum as measured by Neil is the same as Neil's momentum measured by you. But the kinetic energy is different (unless you and Neil happen to have the same mass).

    This occurs in Newtonian physics as well. A charge at rest detonates, sending a 2kg block to the left at 1 m/s, and a 1kg block to the right at 2 m/s. They have the same momentum, but the second block has more kinetic energy (per KE = .5 m v^2)
