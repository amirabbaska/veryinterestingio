      A latex party balloon only takes 0.15 psi (~1 kPa) to inflate over atmosphere...

      The video is underwater, already much higher pressures involved. It's an apples to oranges comparison. Much different materials or scales would result in a different source for the majority of the sound energy.

      In a balloon the energy released by the pop can be shown to be a factor of the internal pressure, but the crack propagation in the skin of the ballon changes too.

      Higher pressures put the material into a higher stress state, when the skin ruptures that tear is releasing that energy.

      An average party balloon (Volume ≈ 14 L) at our 1 kPa is about 15 Joules of stored energy due to the compressed air.

      An estimate for the fracture energy in the latex ≈ 190 kJ/m^2

      A ballon skin is about 1.1 mm thick for a quality party balloon.

      So for an equivalent release of energy, a crack 71.77mm would be all that formed.

      Instead you can see that multiple tears with a much greater energy release occurs due to the rapid crack propagation once the initial failure occurs. It's discussed in that paper I linked above.
