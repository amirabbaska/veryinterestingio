  • blanchae

    Something to be aware of when on oxygen, there are rules as to the amount of O2 you use but nothing to set the humidity level added to your oxygen by your humidifier. Over a period of 2 weeks, I was taking my father by ambulance to emergency twice a day at the end because he was having trouble breathing. A couple of hours at emergency and he was fine. After that, he wasn't allowed to return home which broke our hearts.

    The yo-yo between home and hospital was totally baffling until I realized (unfortunately after he passed away) that his lungs were filling up with moisture from the humidifier on his oxygen line - it was drowning him. When he was in the ambulance and at emergency, they didn't use a humidifier on the O2 line and it was drying his lungs out so then he could breath again. If you have COPD and wet breathing/cough, check your humidifier. You might have too much moisture in the O2 line.
