    • bunnicula9000

      Brain death turns out to be more difficult to prove than we thought, but a lack of activity in the cerebellum is not recoverable. Neurons have a minimum, base level of activity even when they're not being stimulated, so zero activity strongly suggests that they are dead. If activity was missed because it was very faint it might still be possible for the person to partially recover, although actually waking up is deeply unlikely. A full recovery to their previous life only happens in fiction.
