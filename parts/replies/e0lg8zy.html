    • clayscarface

      There is such a thing as "viral load" which is a common measure for HIV infection. It refers to how much of a person's cells are infected with the virus. Someone with HIV can get to a point where their viral load is undetectable. Some recent studies are showing that having an undetectable viral load can significantly reduce the risk of HIV transmission.

      This seems related in that if there are "very few" cells in saliva, it would still be less likely to be transmitted. Even when a viral load is undetectable, it doesn't mean it is nonexistent.
