      No, there is no "override switch" that causes a person to go into cardiac arrest, if accidently flipped. "Heart attacks" happen when there is a blockage, within a vessel. Because of that blockage, oxygen is unable to reach the heart tissue beyond the blockage. If caught in time, the blockage can be removed or the vessel can be expanded/diverted. If not caught in time, the heart muscle will begin to die. However, it is important to note that while we cannot choose when or how efficiently our heart pumps, we can increase it or slow it down. By stimulating the vagus nerve (blowing hard through a straw can do this or straining to go #2) we can cause our heart rate to slow. Not enough to stop or reset, just slow down a bit, in healthy individuals. On the flip side, I'm sure we are all aware of how exercise can increase heart rates.

      The heart is an incredibly interesting part of the human body. It is the only type of muscle tissue to work autonomously. In other words, your brain can be "dead" and your heart will still do it's job. You can be paralyzed from the neck down, and your heart will still do it's job. It's pretty cool stuff.
