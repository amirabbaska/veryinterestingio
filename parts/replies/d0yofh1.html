    • Dhaeron

      Imagine temperature as the amount of thermal energy contained in matter. If you have very little energy stored in extremely small amounts of matter the temperature of that matter is very high. If you touch it, the energy gets distributed across your matter, which can absorb far more.

      Very simple practical example: A candle-flame has a temperature of a few hundred degrees. Yet, drop a lit candle in a bucket of cold water and you won't notice any temperature change in the bucket because the mass of flame is so low. But pour half a bucket of water at just one hundred degrees in, and you'll see a big change.
