• 14MinutesFromEarth

    My wife Kathy had a list of 13 things I had to do before he jump. Some were obvious, like getting my will and advanced directives in order, but some where much harder. They came under the general heading of "I don't want to have to pick up the pieces when this goes bad", so I needed to do everything before the jump to plan my own funeral. Which funeral home, where I was being buried, write my own obituary, designate a friend to the planning, have call trees is place to let people know. The last item on her list was the hardes. Do a person a video for each our my two children that explained why this jump was so important, to be only shown to them after I died. Try that someday! In the end, it was good to think about those issues, since it made me more safety conscious, and gave me a better understanding of what she and the family were going through. AE
