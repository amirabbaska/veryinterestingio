    • PuddleCrank

      Lakes form at low point in the topography precisely because the water table is above them. There is a lot of water surrounding lakes as well it's just under the ground. As for considered stagnant water. Most lakes are big enough so that wind will create waves these, aerate the water. This alone means they aren't standing water like a mucky beaver pond. In addition as the the other commenter said, almost all freash water lakes have an out flow. This river carries away the salts that would normally accumulate in the lake. It also provids a current so that even without wind the water is mixing.
