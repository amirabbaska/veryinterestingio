    • BayesicallyThomas

      The Shakhova et al. (2014) 17 Tg yr-1 methane release from the East Arctic Shelf is almost certainly overestimated. Most of the methane from the ocean is scavenged by bacteria in the water column before reaching the surface. Only methane sources in shallow waters (<100 m depth) will have appreciable fluxes to the atmosphere (cf. McGinnis et al., 2006). Recent (Pohlman et al., 2014) measurements from neighboring regions don't show nearly as much methane.
