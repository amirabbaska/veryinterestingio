    • AllMightyGuy

      We use a method called reverse osmosis.

      Normally liquids want to go from an area of high concentration to an area of low concentration until there is a perfect balance and the system has reached entropy, its lowest energy state.

      Reverse Osmosis puts high pressure on the low concentration side, and pushes it up against a membrane that has holes so small that basically only individual molecules, or very small clumps of molecules can get through.

      With this high pressure and only one place to go, the H2O molecule passes through with little to no problem, and everything else gets left behind.

      And yes, these filters, or membranes as we call them, are long yellow tubes. You might call them noodle-like.
