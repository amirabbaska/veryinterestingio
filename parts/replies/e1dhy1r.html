    • MagnusBruce

      As another user has said, "West Midlands" can refer to two entities that are roughly in the same place but are very different sizes. The one that you saw on the map is likely to be the county called "West Midlands" rather than the area that's to the Western part of the middle of England.

      Counties generally have a level of government (and sublevels for each city) to administer local services like rubbish collections, road repairs, voting registration, local tax and schools (to some extent). Some of these things are done on a county level and some are on a city level. I don't know enough to know exactly which ones do what, and it likely varies across the country. So every now and then we have County Council elections that generally don't seem to change much. I couldn't tell you who my local councillors are or what recent ones have done in the last few years. I know there are several in each area but I don't even know which party has the most in my area. This is separate from our member of parliament who is involved in law making for the whole country and is generally accountable to (or 'responsible for' depending on how you look at their role) ~100,000 people rather than perhaps a few thousand per councillor.
