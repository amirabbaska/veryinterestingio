    You may already be aware of this, but it seems like a lot of inappropriate conclusions are being drawn in this thread so I wanted to help try and clear some of it up. Just an FYI - the fetal blood/DNA that has crossed the placental barrier (free fetal DNA) does not integrate into the surrogate host.

    The top comment right now is misleading in that there may be some small fraction of mitochondrial chimerism in a host or child, but that would be exceptionally rare and essentially inconsequential in terms of genetic makeup of either individual. There may be some epigenetic differentiation (ie existing genes are expressed somehow differently), but in terms of explicit genetic makeup, a surrogate has no appreciable effect on the childâ€™s DNA and vice versa.
