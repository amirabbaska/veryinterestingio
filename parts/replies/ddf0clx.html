      fun fact: scientists removed leptin from rats (not sure how) and the rats would eat non stop and became literally too fat to function. i don't know of any experiments or research where they removed ghrelin, but it would be reasonable to assume that if the rats never experienced satiety then they would likely feel no hunger.

      i know that doesn't answer your question, but from what i've learned leptin and ghrelin are responsible for your appetite or lack thereof. cholecystokinin is mostly used to signal to your body stimulate digestion; it only kind of helps as a hunger suppressant.

      side note - it takes a long time to starve to death (assuming you have access to water). death by starvation depends on your body composition and mass. it takes well over a month to die that way.

      source: i have a degree in nutrition and dietetics.
