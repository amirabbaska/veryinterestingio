      I love this follow up question because at first I thought "smartass" when i read it and then it started to bug me and get me thinking about it.

      So what you need to remember is that angular momentum is based on the squared distance of mass from the center of rotation. So when we are talking about VERY small objects they have almost no angular momentum no matter how fast they are rotating.

      But what does that mean?

      It means that in this system other energy sources are going to dominate the physical properties of the system. What other physical properties? Temperature - which is, btw, what I think the real answer to this question is.

      Temperature is the vibration of atoms within their atomic bonds and, since absolute zero is impossible (especially in a system that is supposed to be rotating), we know that even a five atom system is not going to be perfectly symetical due to the vibration of the atoms in the object.

      More than that when you try and rotate something this small, quickly, and it's temperature becomes the dominant form of energy in the system, and its center of mass and internal ballance is dynamically changing, well you are going to find physically it just becomes unstable and tumbles uncontrollably until it cools. So, really, it won't rotate at all, it will just behave as though it were heated.
