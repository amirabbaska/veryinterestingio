  • MigratoryPhlebitis

    There is also the fact of alloreactivity. T-cells are positively selected to interact with MHC, but negatively selected so that they don't bind too tightly to your own MHC molecules.

    As a by-product of this system a huge percentage (~10%) of T-cells end up having a strong affinity for a given non-self MHC molecules since negative selection only tests their affinity to self-MHC. This is a far higher frequency than any pathogen responsive T-cell and it means a huge T-cell response is generated in response to foreign MHC molecules
