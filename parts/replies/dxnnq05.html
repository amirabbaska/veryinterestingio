Gadolinium is toxic, but it's formulated in a chelated, water soluble form that's designed to stay bound to the carrier molecule long enough to exit the body via the kidneys. However it can cross the blood brain barrier, so not all of it will be able to exit the body.

      As a free ion, gadolinium is reported often to be highly toxic, but MRI contrast agents are chelated compounds and are considered safe enough to be used in most persons.
