      If you are on the surface, gravity acceleration would point mostly 'vertically' (radially?) into the surface (depending where on the torus you are). Just imagine that increasing the major radius you can approximate the planet to a infinite cylinder.

      If you are far away, the planet is just a dot located in its center of mass and we can use Newton's gravitation law for a point mass .

      However, if you in space near the planet, you'd need to input your current position to know exactly where the acceleration of gravity is pointing (and how strong it is).

      So basically, the acceleration of gravity does not point to the center of mass, and if I understood your question: yes, to a point located on the surface of a big enough ring, the pull towards the center of mass of the ring would be tiny.

      EDIT: the calculations should be done with a modification of Newton's formulation that takes into accound a distribution of mass https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gauss%27s_law_for_gravity
