    Worker bees are not sterile. Their ovaries are underdeveloped and they have not mated. The pheromones released by the queen as well as their diet as a larva suppress their sexual development but in cases where those pheromones go away it is not uncommon for some workers to start laying unfertilized eggs.

    We also see worker laying in apis mellifera capensis the race of honey bees native to South Africa where female workers often turn into laying workers and can destroy hives of non capensis races by laying only drones and overwhelming the hive.

      You are correct.....if a queen dies without a replacement or without a fertile queen cell, a few workers will start laying eggs in her stead. Their eggs will produce only drones (male bees) and the hive will eventually die out. A good beekeeper can usually tell if the hive is not queen-right when he goes into the hive. There are subtle signs to seasoned beekeeper. Excessive amount of drone cells is a dead giveaway! You can replace the queen with a new queen if you catch it in time and all be well if they accept her, which they usually do 90% of the time.
