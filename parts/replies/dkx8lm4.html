    Why can't the brain of a blind person adapt to use the ganglion cells to actually see blue light?

    • skippy94

      Rods and cones are specifically adapted to capture light and transmit it to the vision centers of the brain and turn it into a perceived picture there. Cells that simply detect the presence or intensity of light don't have those capabilities, and don't send information to be processed in the same areas of the brain. So both the tool and the wiring are not correct for the photosensitive ganglion cells to turn blue light into a perceived picture.
