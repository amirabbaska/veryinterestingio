    • TheEtherealTony

      https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boundary_layer

      The theory is basically boundary layer theory.

      Fluids, such as air or water, have a viscosity, meaning they are 'sticky' to a degree. When fluids flow over a surface, they will tend to 'stick' to the surface. This has a chain effect where fluid molecules will tend to stick to the other fluid molecules that are sticking to the surface. This chain of sticking fluid molecules forms a 'boundary layer' where the flow of the fluid over the surface will be slower than those that aren't flowing over the surface. The closer you are to a surface, the slower the flow of a fluid will be. Fluid velocity will slow down until you reach the surface where the velocity of the fluid is essentially zero.

      This is where the dust resides. Since there is no air flowing over the fan blades relative to the fan, the dust is just laying on the surface, the same as if it were merely on a wall or a desk.

      Oil and other filth will surely add to dust being more sticky, but that would make it stick to any surface, fan blade or not.
