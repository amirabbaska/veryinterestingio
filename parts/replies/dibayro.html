    Stochastic models are really just statistics, though. Statistics with, typically, very large sample sizes, but still just statistics. The half-life is just the time period over which the probability of decay is 0.5. The probability that significantly more or less than half (say, more than 60% or less than 40%) of the particles will decay in that time, while negligible for any sample of more than two hundred or so particles, is still technically nonzero.
