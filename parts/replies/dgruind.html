    Black body radiation is calculated by using the absolute temperatures to the 4th power, not the differential.

    OP, the problem with your calculation is that you need the view factors to calculate the percentage of area that the radiating energy is hitting the other body, making the energy balance F1-2 A1 T1^(4) = F2-1 A2 T2^(4).

    If you apply reciprocity to the two bodies (defined as F1-2 A1 = F2-1 A2 for any two surfaces), then you will find that the temperatures will be equal in the absence of other energy sources, resulting in zero net energy transfer.

    A lot of people here seem to be missing the point and attempting to reconcile your example by pointing out flaws in the exact situation that you've considered.

    I hate this. The point of a Gedankenexperiment is to tease out the mechanics of a theory, not to attack the practical assumptions.

    The Stephen Boltzman law states that the energy emitted/absorbed by a body is proportional to its surface area times the difference in temperature to the fourth power.

    This isn't correct, the Stephan-Boltzmann law states that radiation is proportional to the fourth power of the thermodynamic temperature, not that it is proportional to some kind of difference.

    Indeed it seems in this scenario one body will be heated beyond the temperature of the other. From the second law however we know that our model must be incomplete, so what did we ignore?

    The situation actually consists of three bodies, namely the Sun, the Earth and the photon field, which has its own entropy. By just setting the emissions equal we ignore that there is a photon field present that takes up quite some heat. Note that this is the difference to the more common scenario of the sun heating up the earth and earth in turn radiating away until it is in some kind of equilibrium. There the universe acts as a heat bath, but here we intentionally isolate our system.

    So the more complete equation would be P_from_field_to_earth - P_to_field_from_earth = P_from_field_to_sun - P_to_field_from_sun. Unfortunately, I do not know the relevant equations, but introducing the photon field will resolve the dilemma.
