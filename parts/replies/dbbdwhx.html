      • Rob___M

        It will probably be less. Audio is a good example, AM, telephone, cassette tapes, etc. sound relatively low quality. But then we settled on CDs, and the standard is basically high enough for human perception. Better quality audio exists, but it's used for things like mastering, or multichannel audio, but most is mixed down to CD level for mass consumption.

        We may be getting near that level for video. 8k is enough to project on a movie screen. Once that's what we're streaming and consuming online, on our phones, etc. we may be near a final video resolution. Maybe we'll go one or two generations further, but we'll probably reach a point like CDs where the quality is good enough for most purposes.

        But what this doesn't include is the desire for things like 360 video, streaming VR experiences, or multiperspective videos. We'll likely be looking for better quality in those areas for years to come, and the ones from today will look old ten years from now.
