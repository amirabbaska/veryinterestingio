      The idea the OP is talking about is the idea that too much close work causes nearsightedness. This idea has been replaced pretty recently by evidence that it is actually a lack of sunlight exposure that is causing more nearsightedness. (Less sunlight = wrong shaped eyeballs.) Since you were getting more sunlight while your eyes finished growing in your later teen years, your eyeball shape got a little correction.
