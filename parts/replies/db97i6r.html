    Black Capped Chickadees are the primary visitor to my feeder/suet (pacific northwest). I was so excited to see my first Chestnut Backed Chickadee last winter stopping by every once in awhile. Last Saturday I counted 8-10 Chestnut Backed alone on top of the 15 or so Black-Capped that frequent it. While I can't directly correlate my feeder to an increase in population it makes me feel good thinking more offspring may be surviving because I am feeding them and the breeding population is increasing as a result.

    Of course I'm an ecologist so I obviously think of increased risk of predation, gene flow, inter and intraspecies competition, disease risk, foraging behavior, carrying capacity etc etc.

    I look forward to reading more about that study!
