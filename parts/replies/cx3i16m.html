      Possibly a ground loop situation. Try a 3 to 2 prong adapter on the mixer and see if that does it. The idea is that every piece of equipment should only have one path to ground. Of course it's not ideal to use a ground-removal adapter but it's useful to diagnose where the hum is coming from.

    • CapinWinky

      This is an excellent point that has everything to do with that audio mixer being plugged into the wall and your headphones aren't. Basically, what is happening is your laptop's ground and the ground in your house are not at the same voltage because reasons (bad grounding in the laptop because of sloppy board/circuit design) and the potential difference between the audio jack and the properly grounded audio equipment is interpreted as sound signals.
