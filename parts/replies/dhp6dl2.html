    When it's cold, blood vessels constrict to keep more of your heat around your central organs. Your normal skin has plenty of blood flow going to it, so a little constriction doesn't cut off all the blood. However, scars have very little blood flow since scar tissue is mostly a dead region of protein. When it's cold and you constrict what little blood flow they have, they turn purple or white since there's no red blood cells going there anymore.
