    This is not true. Per the Pink Book, all indicated vaccines should be given in one visit, with only a couple of very specific exceptions (e.g. pneumococcal and meningococcal for asplenic patients). If two live vaccines are indicated but are not administered on the same day, then the CDC recommends a four-week waiting period between the two. But they can absolutely be given on the same day.
