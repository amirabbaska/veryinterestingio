    • DavidRFZ

      Yes. The salt is eroded off of rocks by rainwater runoff which flows into rivers and then the ocean. The salt does not build up in the rivers because the water is constantly flowing. The salt accumulates in the ocean because there is nowhere else for it to go.

      You can get the same effect in dry basins with where there is no outlet. The Great Salt Lake & Dead Sea are salty because rainwater runoff gets stuck there. Death Valley has dry salt beds. Those salt beds are created by rainwater runoff which evaporates.

      Not entirely. I'm sure there's a lot of direct saltification, if I can invent a word, as ocean water interacts with seabeds and sea shores. The amounts of individual dissolved ions have changed in proportion to eachother over geological time, though this is more of a subtlety to do with changing rates of certain inputs/outputs and is not necessarily saying that total salinity has increased.
