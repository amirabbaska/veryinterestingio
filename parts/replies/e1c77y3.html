    • NeurosciGuy15

      NMDA is the receptor. Glutamate is the ligand that activates the receptor. Glutamate binding to an NMDA receptor induces a conformational change that opens a pore and allows Na+ and Ca2+ ions to flow through the newly created pore and into the cell, depolarizing it. NMDA Receptors are a bit unique because they’re normally blocked at resting membrane potentials by magnesium ions.
