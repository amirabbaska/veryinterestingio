    • ztherion

      The PS2 is particularly difficult to emulate because of the weirdness of the Emotion Engine hardware.

      A modern PC has a single chip that processes instructions, does math, draws graphics and accesses memory. (Sometimes the graphics part is split out.)

      The PS2 has:

      • A chip for processing, which uses a different set of instructions compared to a PC processor
      • Two separate chips specifically for the math used in 3D video games
      • A chip for 2D graphics and movie files/DVDs
      • A chip for accessing memory

      All of these are separate units but operate with very precise synchronization and timing. That timing is very hard to accurately emulate.
