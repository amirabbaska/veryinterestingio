    Every one of your examples is energy TRANSFER.

    He wants to store light energy as light energy, which...is perfectly feasible once you understand what light energy is.

    Light energy is photon energy moving as a wave. In order to store it, you would effectively have to keep it in motion until you use it to transfer it into useable energy. The issue with transfer is that for every transfer, you lose energy. Storing it as light, indefinitely, means it's always at full potential.

    To store it, you would need an internally mirrored vessel that would effectively allow the light to continue moving without warming or absorbing into the mirrored vessel. In other words, it would need to be a near perfect mirror to ensure the loss over time is very, very small.

    If you opened this device and looked inside, I would think it would be instant blindness.
