      • iceman0486

        Many things are based on norms. Arbitrary is the incorrect word to use here. 0 dBHL is a derived norm, therefore based upon a rational and procedural system. It can be described as normal human hearing because it is based off ANSI specifications which were largely based upon, and continually updated from the entry hearing exams of military personnel.

        dBHL makes an audiogram easy to read, and makes detection of hearing loss simple. Measuring hearing loss in dBSPL would make a normal audiogram a massive curve and we already deal with doctors failing to understand what normal hearing is with the simple system in place.

        Just because you don't like it, and it isn't based upon some physics based, nicely packaged system does not make it arbitrary. Hell, even the gram is continually updated because we learn new things.
