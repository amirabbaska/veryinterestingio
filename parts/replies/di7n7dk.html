Electronics are made mostly of sand, relatively easy to find metal, and plastics. The company is usually well into the red until millions of the consoles sell because the high NRE for building new electronics, which is why consoles are placed so high. That said

    For reasons that should be obvious, most electronics are fully automated in assembly from the get go, so this isn't a thing really. The reason for the price decrease later on is because years later, the company is finally making profit and they can afford to make adjustments.

    Likewise for video games. It doesn't take any physical materials to make software, so you're really paying for the NRE.
