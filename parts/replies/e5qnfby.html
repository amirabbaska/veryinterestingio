    Storm surge affects islands too. Here's what that looks like:

    https://coastal.er.usgs.gov/hurricanes/images/breachLG.jpg

    The height of a storm surge varies a lot depending on the details of the shape of the coastline, so "12 foot surge for a cat 4 hurricane" is only a rough guideline. It also depends a great deal on the track of the hurricane: since Florence is unlikely to hit at exactly the same spot as Hazel, the storm surge could be less in some areas, much worse in others. It's a really bad idea to use Hazel as a guide.

    However, I can tell you that Hazel did produce storm surge of 12 to 18 feet on the barrier islands of South Carolina, and that simulations of storm surge for a typical Category 4 storm striking Charleston predict flooding of 18-22 feet on the barrier islands.

    Once the forecast track is pinned down, detailed storm surge predictions will be sent to civil defense. Listen to them, and if you're told to evacuate, evacuate.

    It depends on the topography of the land. A broad, long beach will get a huge storm surge, because there's nowhere else for it to go. A rocky island surrounded by deeper water will fare better, because the surge can flow around the island.

    Shallow sand cays, like the Carolina coastline, get hammered.
