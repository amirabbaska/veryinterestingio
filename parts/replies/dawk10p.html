  • kevin_k

    Maybe more direct an answer: Elements heavier than Iron can undergo fusion, given enough pressure/temperature. The difference starting around Fe in the elements is that fusion of lighter elements is exothermic, meaning it releases more energy than is required to initiate the fusion. Heavier elements undergo an endothermic fusion, meaning it requires more energy than it releases. So a star made of lighter elements can undergo a self-sustaining fusion, while those made of heavier elements can't.

      Interesting fact: The creation of heavy elements by the fusion of elements heavier than iron only really happens in one specific time in a star's life cycle. The energy required to fuse these is so great that it requires an enormous instantaneous release of energy. This comes from a supernova.

      So when a star finally gives up the ghost, it releases energy to form massive elements, many of which are essential to our modern lives and are found in our bodies.

      So the self-sacrifice of these stellar giants creates life. Truly a wonder of the universe.
