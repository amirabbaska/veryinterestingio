    This is a great explanation. However, to my knowledge, in modern printers, it is actually the neutral or discharged areas of the drum (that have been hit by the laser) that pick up toner. Bias voltages are applied to various components of the system to make that happen. The areas that are still charged actually "repel" the toner (the drum and the toner have the same sign charge)
