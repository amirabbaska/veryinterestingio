    Something very important to note is that diplomatic immunity applies to a position, not a person.

    So if someone commits a crime in another country being a diplomat won't protect them automatically. It has to be confirmed by the diplomats country. They could obviously lie but no government is likely to claim immunity if an ambassador murdered someone while on vacation for example.

      The immunity applies only in the country where the diplomat is accredited, and on travels between that country and the diplomat's home country. On a vacation, you wouldn't have immunity, even though you carry a diplomatic passport.
