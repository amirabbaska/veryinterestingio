  • EricPostpischil

    I cannot agree that math is “nothing more” than a set of definitions. Definitions alone would be inert, lifeless. Math also has rules about deriving statements from other statements. You could argue that these rules are just definitions too, but they are not—they provide actions and bring the definitions to life.

    Beyond that, math is meaningful because it is hugely useful. It models many things—hugely many different things. It enables us to reason and to predict better than we could otherwise. It guides us to new discoveries about the physical world. I would allow that these things are extrinsic, that they are outside of mathematics rather than a part of it, so they do not technically speak to whether math is “nothing more” than a set of definitions, but we should not neglect them.
