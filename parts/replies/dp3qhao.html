    • WhatSonAndCrick

      You're right. Just because it dissolves does NOT mean it's sublingual. Dissolving tabs are great for quick onset (because they readily go into solution -- surprise, solid tabs aren't absorbed by the stomach) and don't require water to get down (great for nauseated patients). For example, Zofran ODT (orally disintegrating tab) is not a sublingual tab. I have to correct nurses (and pharmacists) all the time on this. "Absorption: Oral: 100%; nonlinear absorption occurs with increasing oral doses; Zofran ODT tablets are bioequivalent to Zofran tablets; absorption does not occur via oral mucosa." Source: UpToDate + am pharmacist.

    • cjluthy

      The sublingual lozenges (dissolve in your mouth) end up absorbing a large amount of the melatonin via your oral mucosa (gums/cheeks/tongue/roof/floor). This allows it to enter your bloodstream faster, which makes it take effect much faster. The longer you hold it in your mouth, the more will be absorbed.

      The stuff you end up swallowing does also get absorbed eventually.
