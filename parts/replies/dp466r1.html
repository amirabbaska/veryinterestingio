      • K_Furbs

        You absolutely can! They're really the most common everyday type of emulsion. Most salad dressings are oil/water emulsions, like vinaigrette (what you make). Mayonnaise and hollandaise are also oil in water emulsions, and since I imagine you haven't seen a jar of mayonnaise that's separated into its base constituents you can see how this problem can get complicated. Emulsifiers are commonly used to help stabilize an emulsion like mayo, but I imagine for this post we're not considering those.

        Emulsions can "break" in four different ways:
        Flocculation - the droplets attract each other and form larger agglomerates like a raspberry
        Coalescence - the droplets combine into larger single droplets
        Creaming - the droplets separate through buoyant forces
        Oswald Ripening - a thermodynamic process I don't understand well enough to explain easily
