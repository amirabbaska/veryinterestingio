    Usually when you burn wood or something other organic (as in carbon based) material, it combines with atmospheric oxygen to make CO2. However, when you heat it in a low or no oxygen environment, all the water evaporates off and the long chained molecules begin to break down into shorter-chained molecules. This is pyrolysis. Pyrolysing wood is how you get charcoal. It's useful because most of the "investment" of energy has been made into breaking the molecules without getting the energy out, so you have something that burns more easily and at a higher temperature.
