    If I could expand on what you've already written: Kenneth Burke says that all of our identity-giving memberships (I am a college student, I am a conservative, etc.) come with a set of vocabulary words, or terms, that we recognize and use in a certain way. Our "self" is made of those memberships, and the terms form "terministic screens" that allow us to filter out information that goes against our terms (that are supplied by our identity) or receive information that doesn't. That's why Republicans use and respond to "illegal alien" where Democrats prefer "undocumented immigrant," etc. I'm not sure if I explained that well, but you're absolutely right, we like information that (at least) sounds like what we already believe.

    Cognitive dissonance occurs when reality or what we perceive as real conflicts with our self-construal (who we think we are, and who we think others think we are). One way to fix it is to change our beliefs, but it's easier to reassure ourselves or seek out information that assures us that our self-construal was in fact right.
