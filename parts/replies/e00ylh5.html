    The labor participation rate fell by about 7 points from 2008-2016, and has since stabilized at 62.8% +/- 0.2%.

    There are several reasons why the labor force participation rate hasn't recovered. The biggest is that folks over about 55 probably had very poor prospects in getting a new job after multiple years of unemployment. (Especially since unemployment insurance was extended to a very long duration during the peak of the crisis -- which carried many folks along until Social Security age, at which point jumping back in the labor pool probably felt pointless)

    Another reason was actually the big push to bring people from different economic and ethnic backgrounds into college (thereby delaying their entry into the labor force). That isn't a new trend, but it grew significantly during the Obama administration, which coincides with the labor participation rate falling.

    There was also a significant expansion of welfare programs after the crisis, which may have caused some who were living on a part-time minimum-wage paycheck to choose to stop working.
