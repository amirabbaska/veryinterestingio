    The enamel wears down, but the dentin layer actual becomes larger at the expense of the pulp chamber over time. So the older you are, the deeper a cavity can be drilled before you'll need a root canal.

    It's also important to note that clinical bruxism isn't ubiquitous and that there is debate concerning its etiology. It's also not limited to night time. Attrition is ubiquitous with time, whereas bruxism is considered to be pathologic.
