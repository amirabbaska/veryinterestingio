  • 3vyn

    In short yes, the sun does spin, but not all at once.

    Confused? Depending on what part of the sun, some parts may rotate faster than others. Spots on the equator of the sun rotate once about every 23-25 days, and spots near the poles can take up to 38 days to make a full rotation. Just as interesting, the inner layers of the sun rotate at different speeds than the surface. The inner regions, the core and the radiative zone rotate like a single solid body, but the layers above (the convective zone and photosphere) rotate at different speeds.
