    Even countries with their own currency often have a 'peg' against the US Dollar. They'll say their currency is worth X Dollars and they'll buy or sell Dollars to make sure their currency remains at that approximate value. If the difference in value isn't too huge people won't be bothered to form a black market for Dollars.

    That said, sometimes the government can't afford to maintain it's peg, or set it at an absurd number. In which case it's even worse for the country because a large black market forms and/or people won't send money to your country legally.
