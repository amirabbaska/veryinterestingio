    • DoomFrog_

      The high frequency UV waves don't transmit heat very effectively. UV, X-ray, and gamma can cause a lot of damage to tissue and DNA (leading to cancer), but they don't make you warm up.

      IR is the most effective at transmitting heat. Microwave works as well on certain materials, which includes the human body. But it would come down much more to how far you are from the Sun, because the density of the waves is going to be very important. Assuming we are taking you are floating out in space around Earth, it would be pretty low. Definitely low enough that in the seconds you would live you wouldn't feel it. Now if you could survive longer, I don't know. A lot of it would come down to how you would survive because the human body regulates heat mostly through the sweat response. So after your body lost all its water in the vacuum you would start to overheat because your body couldn't lose the heat and probably at a faster rate than the Sun's radiant heat would warm you. If we discounted that, then it becomes an issue of whether you would be absorbing radiation faster than outputting it and what the equilibrium point was, but then we would have to agree on a temperature for you to be at.
