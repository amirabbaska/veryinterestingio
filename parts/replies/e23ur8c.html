  • WeaselsRipdMyFlesh

    As mentioned earlier, oscillometric blood pressure monitors measure the oscillations of the blood vessels. These signals are very small and need to be amplified. this means they are susceptible to noise from movement and EMI. They also estimate systolic and diastolic pressure based on an algorithm, which has its own accuracy issues.

      • WeaselsRipdMyFlesh

        Yes, but by noise I mean electrical noise as well as physical movement. Because the device measures oscillations it can pick up minute movement in the skin and muscles. Or, for instance electrical noise from ECG leads. Actual audio noise is the drawback of the auscultatory (sphygnomanometer) method. Or, your doc gets old and can't hear the Korotkoff sounds like they used to. From my understanding (Biomed Tech) both of these are fine for routine blood pressure tests. If you're in serious condition, or need continuous monitoring, then they will use an invasive blood pressure monitor.
