    One thing I haven't seen mentioned in this thread that is an intriguing facet of this question only just recently given some of the attention it deserves is the potential role of the microbiome:

    The vaginal microbiota of pregnant women who subsequently have spontaneous preterm labor and delivery and those with a normal delivery at term

    The placental microbiome is altered among subjects with spontaneous preterm birth with and without chorioamnionitis

    Diversity of the Vaginal Microbiome Correlates With Preterm Birth

    Exploring Preterm Birth as a Polymicrobial Disease: An Overview of the Uterine Microbiome

    This is connected to the work I do, so I'm always anxious to share. As with most connections between health/disease and the human microbiome, it almost certainly does not tell the full story. We've started to identify associations, not necessarily causal relationships. The microbiome is likely either a directly relevant facet of the biology of pre-term birth or serves as an indicator of more directly relevant biological factors...or both.
