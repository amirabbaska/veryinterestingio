    Most modern airliners don’t use a turboprop engine with a propeller. They’ll use a turbofan engine with a fan section. The turbine is at the back of the engine that turns the propellor or fan. A big difference between a turbofan and turboprop is that the fan section and the bypass air are shrouded by the engine cowling while a turboprop basically has the core and then the outside air being pushed by the propeller with no shroud. Each have different advantages.

    Both commercial aircraft and military fighters use turbofan engines, but one is a high bypass turbofan and the other is a low or medium bypass turbofan.
