    • MiffedMouse

      In special relativity the kinetic energy of a particle increases as (mc^(2)) / sqrt(1 - v^(2)/c^(2)). That equation can look a little intimidating, but the key point is that as the velocity approaches the speed of light, the kinetic energy diverges to infinity. Thus, anything with mass can never reach the speed of light.

      Objects without mass do not have infinite energy. The above equation becomes 0/0, which is indeterminate, so a different equation must be used to determine energy. Specifically, the equation becomes E = pc, where p is momentum. Why do objects have momentum if they don’t have mass? That is one of the predictions of special relativity that turns out to be correct.

      The result of the above equation for massless particles is that all massless particles must travel at exactly c, no more and no less.

      As a result, the current physical understanding is that nothing can ever travel faster than the speed of light, ever. There is no trick to get around the speed of light.

      (Okay, there are things involving bending time and space using wormholes, but that is the shape of space changing to change the distance between different points in space. All the particles involved are still limited by light speed.)
