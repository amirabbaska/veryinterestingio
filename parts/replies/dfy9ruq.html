  • jimmboilife

    I don't think sea level fluctuations are very important in this case. Sea level fluctuations are a lot more important when considering islands that would be connected to a larger mainland during low sea levels (e.g. Indonesia during low sea levels is a massive extension of the Asian mainland called "Sunda", allowing Leopards/Rhinos/Elephants to colonize these islands).

    Isolated oceanic volcanic islands are only marginally larger during low sea levels, as their gradient is very steep compared to continental shelves. It just doesn't make a huge difference. If two islands are 500 miles apart, and they gain about 2 miles in diameter, that makes little difference as far as aiding dispersion.

    That's the reason these isolated islands are overwhelmingly dominated by birds (stray migrants) and reptiles (good candidates for vegetation raft dispersal), with limited mammals.
