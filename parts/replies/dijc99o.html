    This isn't really done at waste plants. Generally the large masses are settled off then coagulants are added too allow flocculation and settling of the smaller particles. It can have biological treatment to get rid of some nutrients in the water, but mostly chlorine (US), Ozone (Europe), and UV (most places) disinfect the waters to kill the smaller guys. They definitely are not filtering at a bacterial level or using boiling, or reverse osmosis at a medium to large scale place. That would be incredibly inefficient and expensive.
