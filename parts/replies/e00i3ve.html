    Just to clarify, you're not counted as unemployed if you haven't looked for work in the last four weeks. As long as you're looking for work, you're counted as unemployed, even if you've been unemployed for longer than four weeks.

    You might be under one of those nasty non-competitive clauses in certain tech sectors that prevent you from finding anything else for a few months or years.

    These are rarely enforceable, and the situations in which they are typically involve taking specific trade secrets or client lists to a direct competitor. Even in cases where they may be theoretically enforceable, they are almost never practically enforceable.

      • BassoonHero

        California bans noncompetes outright, under all circumstances. In other states, noncompetes are often explicitly restricted. In all states, noncompetes are implicitly restricted.

        Noncompetes are not uncommon for stylists, and they are enforceable in some cases. Generally, the basis for a noncompete is the concern that a stylist will take the salon's clients with them to a direct competitor in the same geographic region.

        In Texas, a noncompete for a stylist may be enforceable if they are limited in scope, in duration, and in geography, and if they are reasonably necessary to prevent disclosure of the employer's proprietary information (i.e. client lists) to a direct competitor. This is a fairly narrow situation.
