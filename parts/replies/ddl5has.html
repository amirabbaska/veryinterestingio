    Adderall isn't an amphetamine derivative. It is just amphetamine (racemic mixture of d-amph and l-amph). Methamphetamine is the N-Methyl derivative of amphetamine (which is still prescribed btw - Desoxyn). Vyvanse is a completely different animal. It doesn't interact with dopamine transporters at all in its unmodified state, lisdexamfetamine dimesylate. Instead it is a pro-drug of dextroamphetamine (Dexedrine) and it must be cleaved into this active form by the enzyme trypsin in the blood. This is why it has such a long onset time (1.5-2 hours) compared to dextroamphetamine alone ~20 minutes).

    Just to hang some more detail off of your comment: Amphetamines are one of the best-understood compounds known to medical science. They have been in use for over a hundred years.
