    • Karnman

      Most likely is that the thermo receptors of the skin are not feeling the warmth yet because they are still deep in your skin.

      And because if your core body temp is still slighlty below normal this lowers blood flow to your skin and keeps your periphery a little cooler than it normally would be.

      Once your core body temp returns to normal it'll increase blood flow to your skin and the thermo receptors there will get more heat and you won't feel cold anymore.
