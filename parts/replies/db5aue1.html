    • PanTran420

      Correct me if I am wrong as I don't watch the combine, but I am assuming they don't really have to worry about carrying the football, choosing routes, avoiding tackles, and maybe not even wearing equipment.

      You are mostly correct, there are no obstacles, no change of direction, no football, and no pads when running the 40. However, a player who runs fast at the combine will still run fast in a game most of the time. I've seen Marquise Goodwin blow by skilled defenders just with his speed. The athletic prowess based drills don't measure for route running savvy or the ability to read a defense (i.e. if a cornerback is playing man or zone, inside technique or outside technique). That was what Jerry Rice had in spades and is what made him the player he was.

      Another good analogy is Peyton Manning, he wasn't the most physically gifted athlete. His arm strength was mediocre and he wasn't fast. Jamarcus Russell (famously a huge bust) was big, fast, and had a cannon for an arm. Based purely on their physical stats, you'd take Russell 10/10 times. But Manning was an incredible leader and incredibly diligent student of the game. That was what made him the amazing player that he was.

      You want to hire people who have those intangible qualities that will make them good employees, and the GPA is often an incomplete measure of that.
