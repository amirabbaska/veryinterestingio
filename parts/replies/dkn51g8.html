    As a CPA and an auditor, this sounds like 80% of my job...checking that these controls are actually in place and working as they're supposed to. They definitely include a huge automated component. The individual reviewing this is likely reviewing a report that includes certain suspicious transactions already flagged by the computer, and then the human signs off as having reviewed this report and then they save this as evidence for the auditors later. then the auditors come in and check that the individual reviewed the report and also that the report within the computer system was pulled correctly and didn't exclude any transactions. This is called "404 work" for us and is related to the passing of Sarbanes Oxley.
