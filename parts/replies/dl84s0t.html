      Now I'm wondering, does the blue light filter for phones actually aid an individual so they may sleep at night?

      Yes. Because the blue light spectrum is the one who that "deceives" the brain into thinking it's still daytime.

      You know, blue light makes you so awaken that strong blue light lamps are used to treat patients who suffer from narcolepsy.

      Or it it overall better to just cut off screen viewing a good hour before bed?

      Yes. Avoid using your smartphone, computers, tablets etc at night, about 1-2 hours before going to bed. It will make it easier for you to sleep.

      If you really need to read something at night, you may use blue screen filters, and if you don't have them, there are other strategies to diminish that amount of light that goes to your eyes: reversing the print so the page is dark and the text is light, or using e-readers such as Kindle Paperwhite (because it's not backlit, the light is directed to fall at an angle on the the screen, it's not coming from behind the screen straight at your corneas). I've been using Kindle Paperwhite to read in bed before going to sleep and it's not preventing me from falling asleep at all. It's almost the same experience as reading a paper book.
