    • Kozmuss

      I'm sorry I cannot know for sure what the problem is without looking at your eyes thoroughly! But considering the fact you've had it tested regularly I doubt this is a pathological problem.

      We test at 6m at this is considered "infinity" because any distance further than that is considered to affect the image in a way that is not noticeable to the human eye. That's not to say it is impossible, as vision is a subjective experience.

      You very well may be able to detect subtle changes in image quality. We all perceive blur or vision differently, I'm sorry to say that it sounds like we cannot correct your vision anymore with glasses. I would say perhaps trying contact lenses for more crisp vision would be a good idea. The most perfect visual correction we can give are rigid contact lenses, though we usually use soft contact lenses because they are much more comfortable so it's up to you!

      And as for the lens seemingly being different whilst still the same "value" of correction, it may be because of the distance the lens is held from your eye. Especially with prescriptions over 4D, simply changing the distance the lens is from your eye can change its power as well.

      Hope this helps a little bit!
