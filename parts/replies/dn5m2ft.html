    • Astromike23

      For example, a short but very intense headache on one side around the eyes may be a cluster headache

      You have to be a little careful here, since this could describe any of the autonomic trigeminal cephalagias. The key here is what's meant by "short" duration:

      • 5 seconds to 4 minutes: SUNCT syndrome

      • 5 to 30 minutes: Chronic Paroxysmal Hemicrania

      • 30 minutes to 4 hours: Cluster headache

      There are other symptoms, as well - notably the autonomic ones and headache cadence - but the duration can help determine the correct diagnosis and course of treatment.
