    The biggest thing to have changed is that there's now no regulatory oversight when it comes to privacy and ISPs. Before the FCC took over control through relabeling ISPs as common carriers, the FTC loosely regulated the industry's privacy practices. Now the FCC has been stripped of its power to regulate and the FTC can't step in and take over since ISPs are common carriers.

    Saying that they're able to sell all the same information is not quite right either. Verizon was fined by the FCC for inserting headers into customer's traffic to help track them more accurately. Now, Verizon can start doing that again with no opt-out option and the FCC can't put a stop to it.
