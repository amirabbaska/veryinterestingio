      Thanks! It blows my mind how much we don't know about the human body, nutrition, and the like. I was amazed to learn that we don't really know what muscle knots are, or what causes them. I can feel if there's a knot in your muscle, and I may be able to make it go away, but all the doctors in the world can't explain what's actually happening in the body.

      I'd put my money on a knot simply being a bundle of muscle fibers that's contracted more than the surrounding tissue. That's what I assume in my answer, but I'm not sure even that's actually known for certain. And the question of *why* it's contracted is still a mystery. Trigger point theory predicts that metabolic byproducts build up where the motor nerve meets the muscle, screwing up the signal, but research has thrown this into doubt.

      Note that this isn't one of those "it's weird, we don't know how it could happen" mysteries, but rather "it's a complex system with lots of parts, and there are so many reasons a 'knot' might form that we don't know which one it is!" sort of mystery.
