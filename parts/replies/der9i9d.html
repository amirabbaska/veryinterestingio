    • Rostin

      That's one view. A couple of months ago I went to a panel discussion about tactical nuclear weapons. Two of the four speakers were opposed for various reasons. The other two favored the US developing more tactical options.

      The main argument they made is that if (say) Russia and NATO get into a skirmish over the Baltic states, and things start to go poorly for Russia, one option on the table for them would be to use tactical NWs. The intent would be to get the US to back off due to unwillingness to escalate to strategic weapons and lack of good, more measured options. But if the US did have more tactical options, Russia would be less likely to try to call the US's bluff in that way, and ultimately less likely to get into a fight to begin with.

      So, while I think it's true that there's not a lot of appetite for developing more tactical options among decision makers who count, that's not necessarily the consensus view in the strategy community as a whole.
