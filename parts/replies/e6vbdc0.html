    You also need to take into account how much of that oxygen gets eaten up when the algae dies. Unlike trees and other plant materials (which you mention), phytoplankton (and macroalgae and bacterioplankton) are more easily decomposed, and depending on where in the ocean the decomposition happens, the oxygen it contributes can be easily negated. So yes, while they are a major source, if we're talking net production, it's not much.
