    • gnomieowns

      Ions are molecules or fragments of molecules that have an electric charge. You can make ions by "injecting" energy into a compound, causing it to break apart into charged ion fragments. Ions are commonly created by producing "rogue" electrons (e^(-)) that will collide with molecules in the sample to ionize them.

      I think X-rays would be overkill. Generally, there is an electron source (high voltage source that "sparks" to ionize air and create e^(-), radioactive metals that release electrons), but there may also be a thermal desorber that heats the sample. Heat can melt or boil volatile compounds (e.g. explosives) to get them to gas phase without ionizing them.
