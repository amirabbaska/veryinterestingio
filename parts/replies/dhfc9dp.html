      Currently doing a bachelor's in physics with an astrophysics module, so don't take this as the de facto explanation, but off the top of my head:

      Moons forming at the same time as their planet are analogous to planets forming around their star, the same conservation of angular momentum argument holds. However, our moon is thought to have been formed by a collision with another large (mars-sized?) body. Different mechanism, different restrictions.
