    I think this is more of an answer than all the people talking about black "playing to draw". Chess is very binary - it's not like Go where you're adding up a ton of points and you can win by 0.5 points. In chess, when you win, it's a relatively overwhelming win - you have a significant material or positional advantage at the end of the game, or a pretty enormous positional advantage in the mid-game. So you have to make a pretty significant mistake to lose the game - drawing is relatively easy by comparison.

    (Obviously, by "pretty significant" in a world-class chess game, I'm talking about mistakes that your average player wouldn't even notice - not just moving your queen into a position where it can be taken or something.)
