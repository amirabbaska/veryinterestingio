    One more potential criteria can be sort of a mix of point 1 and 2. Groups that faced some level of discrimination tended to form their own communities, either by force or by choice. Especially in cities where you tend to find more people identifying by heritage. Saying you're from new york may imply certain things of your upbringing. Saying you're irish/italian/Chinese/ect narrows that down in a different way. People who grew up in an "irish" neighborhood in nyc, boston, or philly have more of a shared identity than an irish and russian neighborhood in the same city.
