    • Cruseyd

      Kinda. In the case of solids like most metals, "plastic" (permanent) deformation occurs when little cracks called dislocations form and move around. You can visualize this by imagining a rug that isn't quite laying flat and has those long ripples running across it as a result. With no external force added, these cracks are held in place by the rest of the material (atoms bonding to each other).

      However, if you apply heat, those bonds can move around for much the same reason liquid water differs from ice. You can visualize this as the aforementioned carpet sitting on a really slippery tile floor rather than a floor with more friction (like another carpet). Without friction to hold it in it's non-flat state, it will smooth out.

      This process - again, in metallurgy - is called annealing. It's also worth noting that even though the material is "healed" it won't spring back into shape. Atomic bonds have no memory after all :)
