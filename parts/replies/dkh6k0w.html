    Just curious. If earth's orbit around the sun magically stopped, how long would it take to hit the sun?

    • xxx_yyy

      Keplers third law tells us that the square of the orbital period is proportional to the cube of the major axis of the elliptical orbit (the diameter, if it's a circle). If the Earth were dropped toward the Sun, its orbit would be a very narrow ellipse (basically, a straight line) with a major axis equal to half of the diameter of the Earth's nearly circular orbit. Thus, the time to get to the Sun would be half of the new orbital period = (1/2 year)*(1/2)^(3/2) = 0.176 year, about 64.5 days.
