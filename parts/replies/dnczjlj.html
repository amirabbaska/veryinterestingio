      I think most of the sources that say drug companies spend more on marketing are based around a misunderstanding of accounting as well as how many drug companies develop drugs from a business perspective. For example many times articles will take the whole SG&A expense and compare it to R&D.

      SG&A includes many not marketing expenses such as HR, accounting, finance, sales and office space for the corporate office. The amount spent on pure marketing is likely much lower than the quoted expense.

      Another aspect is that if company A spends 500 million developing a drug and company B buys company A for 1 billion this is not included as an R&D expense for B even though it effectively cost them 1 billion.
