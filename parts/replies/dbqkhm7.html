    What are the 'accessible states' of a particle in a microcanonical ensemble?

    • RobusEtCeleritas

      The state of a monatomic molecule in a classical gas is simply its position in phase space (3 components of position and 3 components of momentum). Any available phase space location is equally probable in a microcanonical ensemble at equilibrium. Of course there are constraints, like the gas molecule must be located within the walls of the container, and it cannot have more energy than the entirety of the gas, etc.

  • ComradePalpatine

    In the thermodynamic limit when the number of particles goes to infinity the microcanonical and canonical ensemble are equivalent. This is a well-known result. See, e.g., https://arxiv.org/abs/1403.6608 for a detailed overview.

    • RobusEtCeleritas

      If OP is talking about a finite box of gas, you cannot strictly take the thermodynamic limit, as it requires that you take both N and V to infinity.

      • ComradePalpatine

        :)

        The box will contain at least 10^24 atoms, which means that the fluctuations around the average energy will be suppressed by [; 1/ \sqrt{N} \approx 10^{-12} ;]. This is for all intents and purposes essentially 0.

        Taking the thermodynamic limit is standard procedure in statistical physics. Of course it is never completely true, but for a large enough system it will be a more than adequate approximation.
