    Just a note: obfuscating JavaScript is not where the security of ReCaptcha comes from; the security comes from its (ostensible) ability to distinguish human use patterns from programmatic use patterns. So it's not really "Security by Obscurity" in the usual sense. The obfuscation is just intended to raise the cost of analysis, not to be the source of the security in the first place.

    Of course in the end captchas will always be a race: the better they get at distinguishing human behavior from programmatic behavior, the better people will get at programming computers to behave like humans, and so they'll have to build a better captcha, and so on. The obfuscation doesn't change this fact, it just tries to slow down this inexorable process as much as it can.
