      Take the example of the tethered bead. At any moment in time, the bead is moving perpendicular to the string. In order to maintain a circular path and proper trajectory the bead must consistently accelerate toward the center of the circle.

      All right, think about what happens when the older kid spinning you around lets you go. Suddenly the force that is keeping you spinning in a circle isn't there anymore, and you go flying away from that kid.

      If you spin a circle around in space, the floor of that circle is the force that is keeping you from flying off in a straight direction.
