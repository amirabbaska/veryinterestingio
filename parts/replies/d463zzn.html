    More fun facts, because there's no room for error and it's hard to tell amongst all the jolts, when the pilot lands on a carrier they go straight to takeoff pre-AB power. If they hit the wire it'll stop them anyway and they can let off the power. If they missed the wire they need to be at full throttle pre-AB immediately in order to not roll off the deck and go in the drink.

    Also, because the catapult shoots you forward so hard, the pilots trim the elevator nose up but keep their hand OFF the joystick (on a hold bar) during takeoff so as to not pull up into a stall immediately after release.
