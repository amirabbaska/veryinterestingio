    • docod44

      Lithium and valproic acid are the gold standards and lithium has the added benefit of protecting against suicidal ideation (valproic acid has better evidence for mixed states and rapid cycling). Lithium is recommended first-line by most published guidelines but being an old drug with a host of awful side effects, most clinicians opt for an antipsychotic with mood stabilizing properties, especially where mania +/- psychosis are the presenting symptoms. Abilify, zyprexa, risperdal, latuda, and seroquel are the ones with the most evidence but they work on more of a localized level (targeting dopamine and serotonin transmission for symptomatic treatment as opposed to working at the gene expression level). Gabapentin blocks a2d subunits on calcium channels which directly inhibits the release of glutamate into the synapse. It works well for some but hasn't been studied extensively.
