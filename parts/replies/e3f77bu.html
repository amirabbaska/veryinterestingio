      Gotcha, I guess I was referring to typical US configurations. In the US it is quite uncommon to have 3 phase residential. Even our electric based appliances are 1 phase/220v (use the 2 sides of 110v that enter the home).

      I didn't go into this on my explanation above. Essentially the distribution transformer on the utility pole takes in one phase into a core inside the transformer, next to it there is a winding of wire that transforms this down to 220v, 110v on each end of the winding, these two wires are fed into the home's electric panel. These two 110v circuits are 180^(o) out of phase from each other. The two lines are fed down electric panel as buses and breakers are attached to these bus bars. Every other row of breakers are on the same bus. 2 pole breakers that span two rows are the 220v circuits that feed our high power consumption appliances like dryers, A/C compressors, electric water heaters, electric ranges, etc.
