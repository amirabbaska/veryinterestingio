    • HaydenSikh

      There was a thread not too long ago -- I think in r/askscience -- that stated they were likely not very effective. IIRC the arguments were:

      • most viruses and bacteria don't tend to spread through the air. Most of the time infections come from rubbing your eyes, mouth, or nose or from eating after getting the pathogens on your hands.
      • face masks are better at blocking incoming particles than outgoing, but the trend is to wear them while sick rather than trying to prevent illness.
      • the ability to block incoming particles by a meaningful amount depends on a certain specification of the mask, which most masks won't meet.
      • even if the mask is of the right type, if it's not the right fit or not worn correctly then it's unlikely to be effective.

      The recommendation was to adopt better hand washing habits as it would be much more effective.
