  • flug32

    FWIW actors and actresses we usually see on stage and on the screen--particularly those who have the leading man/leading lady type roles--are highly selected based on physical good looks and so represent a very, very small portion of the full range of human body shapes.

    Whereas singers tend to be highly selected based on their singing ability and so represent far more closely the full range of human body shapes as seen in the general population.

    I'm never really sure whether opera singers are supposed to be fat in comparison to other non-opera stars of stage and screen or in comparison with normal people.

    In comparison with movie stars, surely they are "fat". In comparison with the full range of normal people, I would guess they represent more a random sampling.

    But, we are not used to seeing "normal" people up on the stage or the screen acting like movie stars or actors. They "look wrong". This is more a problem of us & our expectations than of the singers & their body types.

    It would be interesting to do an actual study of singers vs. the general population based on BMI or whatever. I'm guessing opera singers would come out looking very normal--but then again I live in a state where somewhere north of 30% of the adult population is in the actual "obese" range. If anything, the opera singers I see on stage look thinner on average than our general population . . .

    TL;DR: Opera singers tend to look more like a cross-section of the normal population than a cross section of young, thin movie stars. Maybe thinking of them as "fat" is more our problem than theirs . . .
