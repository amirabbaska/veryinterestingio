    Sorry to be nitpicky but the Lévy distribution is a continuous probability distribution, rather than an algorithm. The paper the article seems to be based on - "Free-Flight Odor Tracking in Drosophila Is Consistent with an Optimal Intermittent Scale-Free Search", https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC1831497/ - actually refers to Lévy flight which the Lévy flight foraging hypothesis suggests as an accurate model for predatory foraging as opposed to Brownian motion.

    Sometimes popular science writing seems like they are playing a game of Chinese whispers in terms of the difference between what the actual science is about and what gets printed in the end.
