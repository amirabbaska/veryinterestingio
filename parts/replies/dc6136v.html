  • _krankenwagen_

    Great breakdown r/jfa_16

    I've been on the job for 10 years, 8 as a paramedic. I work an average of 56hr/wk on a modified Kelly schedule and make just under $60,000 a year with no extra shifts. I am lucky to have a 10% matching 457b retirement account that is baller. Health insurance is covered for myself as well. My agency is a third party ambulance trust and is not for profit. We cover around 1,300 square miles and serve about 150,000 people. (American Mid-West, suburban and rural, outside a larger city) We run 12,000 calls a year with 5 24 hour units and the occasional extra 12hr unit for week days (yeah... we stay busy). We collect only $250,000 a year as a water bill collected subsidy for one of our units in a remote and rural town (this unit only runs 1.5 calls per shift on average, thus the subsidy. It costs roughly $500,000 a year to run an ambulance with a paramedic and EMT on board.)

    We are super close to needing to collect a subsidy to help cover our operations for the rest of the agency... Cuts in Medicare and Medicaid have hurt us.

    Here is the issue:

    People are not ready to pay what it costs to run a decent ambulance service. Period. Whether we are talking about some tiny town or all the way up to federal subsidies. People just don't wanna! Police service is justifiable because we have been subsidizing it for hundreds of years. Fire Departments, not as long as Police but still quite some time. EMS is still considerably young compared to the other two arms of public safety/health. Herein lies the issue... slowly but surely, education levels, public knowledge of what EMS provides, and standardization will gradually increase the pay and benefits of EMS professionals. I believe in EMS as a career profession, not just some job. I LOVE my job, but that doesn't mean I haven't had some rough patches and questioned my career choices.

    Until we start viewing EMS services as a basic right and privilege, things will basically stay the same. A Medicare for all type system is what we need for emergency care and preventative care. IMHO there should never be profit in the emergency care arena... ever. Now if you are some badass surgeon or specialist then by all means, make some cash!

    Whew... thanks OP for posting this.

    I feel like my first reddit post was a ramble, feels good to voice this stuff though!

      You are absolutely right. EMS is the baby of emergency services and as such it will take EMS a while to catch up to police and fire. Most people don't think they will ever need EMS, yet most people have called the police for one reason or another in their lives. Most people have insurance on their house in case of a fire. People seem to believe that they will need police and fire at some point in their lives, but most don't even think of EMS, let alone think they'll need it.

      Hopefully some day EMS will be viewed/funded as a necessary public safety agency across the board in the same light as police and fire. Only then will we see things get better for EMS providers.
