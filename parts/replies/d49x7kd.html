      • MeFigaYoma

        why the hell did America carry on with the posting?

        The destination country doesn't collect postage due unless the origin country says it's postage due. Specifically, they put a T stamp with a fraction of the First-class letter postage paid, which is then converted to the destination country's currency. If there's no stamp on it and no T stamp, they don't collect because they don't know if it was prepaid or something. So if Australia doesn't bother T-stamping, it won't arrive postage due.
