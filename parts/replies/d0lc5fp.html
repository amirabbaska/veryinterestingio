      • akaieevee

        Potential energy is relative.

        For example gravitational potential energy on earth has a formula of mass * gravity * height

        Let's say you wanted to get the potential energy of a 1kg ball on a cliff 10 m above you.

        You could choose any reference point, if you chose your height as a reference point, it would have 10 * 1 * 9.8 = 98 Joules of gravitational potential energy. If you chose the ball's height at the cliff, it would have 0 Joules of gravitational potential energy.

        Basically gravitational potential energy is measured relative to other locations.
