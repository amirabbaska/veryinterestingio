    • Appaulingly

      I'd be careful with thinking about it from a surface tension point of view as strictly speaking that doesn't allow you to consideration the solid surface. Maybe I'm confused by it but I think you're muddling yourself with that analogy. Breaking of the water's surface is working against us just as gravity is. Among other ways of physically explaining the phenomenon, it's the reduction of the total surface energy for the system that gives us capillary action, i.e. the solid-water interface has a considerably smaller surface energy than that of the solid-gas. This is why liquids nearly always wet solids. Energetically speaking, solids have inherently unfavourable surfaces.
