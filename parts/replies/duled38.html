    The first thing to understand is that "i3" and "i7" are more about branding than technical specifications.

    I can attest to this. My first laptop was a gaming laptop and had an i7, quad core 2.0GHz. My current laptop, non-gaming, also has an "i7" but it's a dual core with hyperthreading which I consider to be more of a mid to high range i5.

    At least it was still an upgrade, because I just wanted something less heavy and bulky than a gaming laptop. The boost clock gets up to like 3.5GHz which gives me over 2x as much processing power per core while using less battery due to improvements over 5-6 years. Even the iGPU is like twice as powerful as my gaming laptop's discrete GPU was.
