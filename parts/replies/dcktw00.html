  • TBNecksnapper

    Rather than imperfections in the sphere I'd say it's mostly due to imperfections in the surface the sphere is rolling on, causing it to move up and down a bit so surface vibrates, producing a noise.

    It's a combination of both of course, but the more perfect both the surface and the ball is, the less noise you will hear. They can never be completely perfect because at the end they are made of atoms, but I believe a solid metal sphere rolling on a thick solid metal surface actually makes very little noise. The thinner the surface the more it can vibrate due to the rolling ball, producing more noise, even if the surface is just as flat.
