      There are a lot of variables that contribute when you need to change your oil. How old is the engine? Newer engines will have metal shavings in the oil because the engine is essentially getting its groove going and you are quite literally “breaking it in”. A bunch of metal shavings will cause more wear on the engine and works like sand wearing down the engine. That is why it is very important to change your oil more regularly on a new car. Older cars are already broken in and less likely to have these metal shavings. Instead, grooves have already been worn into the engine and this can let small particles into the engine which is why you may need to change it. Generally, higher mileage cars need less oil changes than newer ones but they still need them.

      How you drive and where you drive will affect your engine. Driving short distances and not letting your engine heat up? You’re going to get more buildup in your engine since a certain amount of heat is good for your engine. If you live in a very dusty area, like Lubbock, Texas particles can get inside your engine and cause wear (a good reason to have a clean air filter).

      Ultimately, the best way to know if you need to change your oil, is to just check your dipstick. Oil usually has color to it, but look if it is dark in color, or if you can see small particles. These would be indicators that you may need an oil change. If the oil is smooth and pretty clear, minus some color, your oil is pretty good. Please note, that oil does decay, so even though it may look clean, if you have not changed it in some time, it is generally good practice to just change your oil.
