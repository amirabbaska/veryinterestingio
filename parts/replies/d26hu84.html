    • hafetysazard

      The only thing that matters is the bullet's speed at the muzzle. Up until that point the gun, and even the expanding gases inside the bore, have 0 velocity relative to the car. From a perspective relative to the ground, the bullet would be slowing down as it travels down the bore.

      The most apt analogy I can think of is like in The Martian, when they needed to use the force of explosive decompression to slow down their ship.

      If we think of the bullet as the space ship in that situation, then the expanding gases inside the bore would be the equivalent of the explosive depcompression.

        The only thing that matters is the bullet's speed at the muzzle.

        But between the time of firing the gun and the point that the bullet reaches the end of the muzzle, the bullet has been going slower than the car. Otherwise it wouldn't travel to the end of the muzzle.

        But it's still travelling faster than zero relative to outside the car. When it leaves the muzzle, then it starts traveling at zero relative to outside the car.
