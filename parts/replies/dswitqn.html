    Yes - if I remember correctly, when the esophagus is irritated repeatedly by very hot foods, it defensively adapts to become more like stomach tissue, which has a thick protective mucus-producing layer. Unfortunately, all the irritation and cell turnover and maybe changes in gene expression make the cells more vulnerable to becoming cancerous. The phenomenon is called Barrett's esophagus. I don't know if it causes any increase in stomach cancer, but I think there might be other dietary factors responsible for higher rates of stomach cancer in east asia.
