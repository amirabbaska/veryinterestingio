    yea, .. a chess-computer is a very complex machine, made a strong chess opponent by many features adding to one another to its strength:

    • whole branch depth,

    • brute force inquiry into variants depending on e.g. strong change in position's rating,

    • hash tables

    • opening book, endgame-module,

    • different playing styles ("aggressive", "defensive", "strategical", "tactical", a.m.m.),

    • the (different) weighing of different attributes of a position to rate it and choose a move,

    • ..

    and many more - I'm actually lay in such things, just a player - and all of these make up the "engine" and last not least, such "engines" can work together.

    Any of these features that make the chess-computer strong can be handicapped, be it in matter of reducing thinking-time or by random values within a threshold or by setting them according to a chosen strength level or by simply switching a feature off or.. or .. or ..
