    • Slowhands12

      Not the only reason (as others have pointed out), but rectangular sensors also make sense for a purely economic level.

      Camera sensors are cut out from larger sheets of silicon. If one cuts circular shapes, there's a large amount of waste silicon left on each sheet — it isn't like baking where one can just roll the scraps together and have them become a new sheet (at least, not without involving an expensive process).

      The issue is compounded by the fact that due to the complex manufacturing process involved, sections of the sheet will be deemed unfit for camera technology (often resold/reused for different, less exact purposes). You can minimize waste again if you're able to section off the sheet.
