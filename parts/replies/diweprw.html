      A bigger concern with bacon and other cured meats is the role that nitrites metabolising to nitrosamines has in the absence of reducing agents. Eating bacon by itself is a lot more provably carcinogenic than most other foods.

      Good news, though, is that ascorbic acid is a wonderful reducing agent and ubiquitous with fruit and vegetables. Get a good, real serving of those in with your bacon and you've blunted that effect while reaping all the other benefits, too.

      Bad news is that "no nitrate" bacon is usually bull. If it says "only uses natural celery salt," that's akin to calling HFCS "sugar free--" said celery salt is basically just "natural" nitrate and functionally indistinguishable.
