    I always thought a helicopter without a tail rotor would be impossible to fly but there is actually a way to still fly it. The pilot will rock the helicopter as it spins, let's say north. Everytime the nose points north he rocks it forward more, this causes the helicopter to start moving north and eventually has enough airspeed that the air flowing past the body of the helicopter provides enough resistance to keep it pointing straight. It can then be flown relatively normally so long as you maintain your speed.

    • Qikslvr

      That only works if the vertical stabilizer on the tail is still in place. That's actually what it's for. Once the helicopter gets moving fast enough, the tail rotor flattens out and is no longer in use and the tail control surfaces take over. If the whole tail falls off, then there's not enough resistance for that to work.

      Source: aerospace engineer with 10 years at one of the big US Helo builders.
