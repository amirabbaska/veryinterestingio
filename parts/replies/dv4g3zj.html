    • hitstein

      Just to clarify, because the other answer wasn't quite correct: The windows are actually stronger than the metal hull. The ISS skin is aluminum. The station is basically a bunch of soda cans joined together in space. The entire thing (except windows) is covered in thick (10 cm/4 in) armor. This armor is a thermal shield for heat, as well as Kevlar/Ceramic fiber "bullet proof" armor. It uses Whipple shields which "catch" the incoming projectile and spread the energy out along the armor instead of into the hull. Because you can't really make windows out of Kevlar/Ceramic fibers, they need to be very strong and very thick. Each window has four panes of "glass" ranging from about 1/2' to 1 1/4" thick. The Cupola, for example, has an outer meteoriod/debris pane at 0.37 in (0.93 cm) thick, followed by two inner panes (The redundant and the primary pressure pane) at 1 in (2.54 cm) thick, and finally an inner scratch pane at 0.45 in (1.14 cm) thick for a total thickness (including gaps) of 5.625 in (14.3 cm). For comparison, most homes on Earth have two panes at 1/16" thick, though I believe 1/8 in is the new standard and not all homes have double pane. Even then, when not in use, all windows have a shielded cover that gets put over them for extra protection.
