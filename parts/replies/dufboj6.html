    • lurk_n_throw

      Choices in digit grouping has a history of variation. European languages tend to use three as this corresponds to groups of thousands, China/Japan/Korea also use this even though their language divisions are in myriads (10 000). India is easily the most complex with the first 3 digits closest to the decimal separator being a group, then grouping in two. (10 00 00 000.)

      In terms of more recent standards, there is a growing harmony to use groups of three with a thin space in between. This can be used on either side of the decimal separator, keep in mind that this is to serve one function: make the number easier to glance - there are a lot of common sense scenarios where you don't use groupings.
