    I did an "accidental hack" like this years ago; in 2005 I was setting up something in PHP that let people quickly generate HTML layouts and store the result on an SQL server. I was also brand new at PHP. There was no account verification or typing password twice, you did the layout by drag and drop & put in a username and password to lock the page so you could edit it later. I was very early in development & put it out for people to test as proof of concept, and as I was going through the data later I realized that there was an extremely high likelihood that the user names & passwords (which I didn't bother to encrypt since it was so early in development & I didn't know what I was doing) would probably work on other sites. Because I was providing a professional looking webpage with a user name & password, and most people don't have or use multiple passwords or unique password per site, something as innocuous as this is a great way to farm passwords.
