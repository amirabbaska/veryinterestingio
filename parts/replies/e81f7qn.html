    • SRBuchanan

      It would most likely run into issues from interacting gravitationally with the Sun's other natural satellites. Jupiter in particular is a notorious changer of orbits. Without constraining the start condition further than 'capture of an extrasolar body into a retrograde solar orbit,' it's hard to predict whether it would end up in a stable orbit or not, and if that stable orbit would still be retrograde. The most likely outcomes are that gravitational interactions with the planets end up yanking it into a prograde orbit, ejecting it from the solar system again, or causing it to collide with something (most likely Jupiter).
