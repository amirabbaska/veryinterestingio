    • omg_drd4_bbq

      Each wavelength of EM radiation is associated with some sort of interaction with matter. Long wavelengths like radio can only absorb when you have a "sea of electrons" (typically a metal) with a feature size roughly half the wavelength or smaller. See the screen on a microwave oven door. High energy (short wavelength) like X-Ray and Gamma mostly interact with the nucleus of atoms.

      Transparency of a material at a given wavelength occurs because that photon does not interact with that kind of matter. Because nuclei have a small cross-section, high-energy photons tend to pass through most low-atomic-weight materials (like humans). You need either high density materials (like lead) or a greater depth of material (like the Earth's atmosphere) to attenuate them.
