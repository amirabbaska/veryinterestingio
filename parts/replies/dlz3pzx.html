    Could you explain how they made the shell not explode in the barrel the moment they activated the triggering device? Because the barrel, and the ground, would both be in range of the radar, right? What was the mechanism to make sure they exploded only bafter being launched?

    • Garfield-1-23-23

      Excellent question. The VT fuse used centrifugal force (yes, I know there's no such thing) to turn off a safety after a short period of time. Firing the shell from a rifled barrel caused it to spin at high RPMs, which activated the fuse. A simple accelerometer safety would not have been sufficient, since it could be accidentally triggered by just dropping the shell from a sufficient height; there was no way to accidentally impart sufficient rotation to trigger the safety (this could only happen when it was fired).
