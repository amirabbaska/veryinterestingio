    • unsuitableshoes

      Sort of, maybe, no.

      Alcohol and alcohol based disinfectants work in two ways: firstly by breaking down (effectively dissolving) the phospholipid membrane around a bacterial cell leading to cell destruction, and then also by denaturing proteins such an enzymes so they won't work anymore. For this to happen, there needs to be contact between the alcohol and the bacteria. If the bacteria is protected from the environment by contamination in the mouth, then irrelevent of how long you keep the Listerine in your mouth, it will never reach the bacteria.

      It's also worth noting that the cell membrane of animal cells is exactly the same as bacteria, and the same process will happen to those cells. Not fun.
