    Don't forget that in practice, employers can just lie about why they fired someone, and fire for discriminatory/retaliatory reasons all they want. Best case scenario, the fired person sues, and maybe gets a settlement years later.

    One perk of union membership is that members can't be fired without the employer presenting evidence of the reason. The employee gets a representative to argue on their behalf. Falsified records, which employers sometimes make, are harder to make stick if the union has kept it's own records.
