    • Captain-Griffen

      Yes. Increased processing power or new mathematical techniques can render a previously believed uncrackable hash crackable.

      Additionally, not all hashes are designed to be uncrackable. MD5 is not usable for security purposes (as collisions can be created), but is perfectly viable for detecting random corruption, eg: in a download.
