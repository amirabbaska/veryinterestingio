    • agate_

      I really like /u/Throw_Away_Humans 's answer: let me add to it by saying that burning radial in/out is almost always a bad idea. Basically you're burning fuel to push inward or outward, which is the same thing gravity would do for you, if you gave it a little more/less time.

      As a general guideline, anything you'd like to do with a radial burn can be achieved with much smaller prograde/retrograde burns, plus a little patience.

      • CaptainRyn

        Note that sometimes radial is a good idea if you are in a time crunched Hohmann transfer and are wanting to adjust where you want to do orbital insertion (like not entering your burn point while in radio shadow)
