    • police-ical

      As you suspect, the active neurotransmitters will never get to the brain; even if they didn't get broken down by gut or liver enzymes, they wouldn't cross the blood-brain barrier. The weird part is that those neurotransmitters actually have totally different roles outside the brain, because they exist separately. Serotonin helps move the intestines along and does something with platelets I've never fully understood. Norepinephrine and dopamine help regulate blood pressure in different ways.

      Your second idea is already in use. Prodrugs are converted into the active ingredient in the body. L-DOPA is the molecule step before dopamine, but unlike dopamine, it can get into the brain (where it's turned into dopamine.) While it's one of the most important treatments for Parkinson's disease, it's never turned out to be much of an antidepressant. 5-HTP is similarly converted into serotonin, and it at least has some crappy evidence as an antidepressant, but... well, it's crappy evidence.

      What all this suggests is that the old model isn't good enough to explain what's going on with these drugs. Just increasing synthesis of these chemicals doesn't have the same effect as prolonging their effect. It seems there's more stuff going on, probably involving weird indirect effects we're only partly aware of. The same neurotransmitter can have very different effects in different parts of the brain--dopamine can influence movement in one area, reward in another, and breast milk in a third!

      (P.S.: We're also all worried about the potentially terrifying side effects of ketamine, which is part of why it's not more widespread.)

    • muscle405

      They already do this for people that want to self medicate without taking drugs. They're called pro-drugs, they get converted (ex l-dopa is the precursor that converts into dopamine)

      The difference with SSRIs and SNRIs is that they don't do that, they simply prevent the body from sucking the serotonin/norepinephrine (or even) back into the synapse. Think of it like this: Reuptake of neurotransmitters is like a Mom cleaning up the toys and putting them back in the toy bin (toy bin=synapse).

      Without the toys there's no fun any kids can have. If the mom didn't get a chance to clean it up The kid could have tons of fun whenever they wanted. The fun in this analogy is a synaptic potential that allows the signal to move across the synaptic Gap between the two neurons.

      • Jigglysquare

        Using a prodrug is using a drug. In fact, lots a drugs have pharmacologically active metabolites. The difference is that a prodrug is (largely) inactive until metabolized (by the body) into an active compound. There are a variety of reasons why you would design a drug to be a prodrug -- increase absorption or half-life, for instance.

        As an example, consider patients with Parkinson's. These patients have lost the dopamine producing neurons of the substantia nigra (a part of the brain the regulates the gating of movement). Perhaps the symptoms of Parkinson's could be alleviated if dopamine could somehow be resupplied to the brain? Unfortunately, dopamine itself is unable to cross the blood-brain barrier and reach the brain. Enter L-dopa. L-dopa can cross the blood brain barrier. L-dopa is precursor to catecholamines such as dopamine. L-dopa is a prodrug. L-dopa is a drug.

        L-dopa is not used to "self medicate without taking drugs".
