    However, this is culturally learned and can be culturally unlearned. Serendipitously, he wound up studying a troop where all the aggressive males died at once. The only males left were social and nice, both to one another and to the troop's females. This changed the culture of troop.

    Well, it didn't change the culture of the troop per se, it changed the dynamics of the remaining individuals. Aggressive males have higher serotonin levels, they're natural born leaders. It's not like some social worker came in, gave a seminar and everything was nicer. Sapolsky is quite clear he has no real solutions for reducing stress levels, he's merely describing the endocrinological mechanisms of stress responses. In fact in the last chapter he ironically says he's an atheist but believing in God seems to be the only sure fire way to protect oneself from stress.
