    That's a better average speed estimate. Now we could do the same for seconds.... milliseconds.... and as we approach the smallest time value (aproaching zero) you get a very good average speed estimate.

    That's a fairly roughshod intro to integral calculus.

    And when you say you're approaching zero it means that you repeated the step of choosing a smaller time slice over and over. In fact, if you did it manually you'll never actually reach zero. Calculus gives you a formula to simulate breaking the time slice down an infinite amount of times, to an infinitely small period of time, and then adding up an infinite number of data points.

    When you understand it and stare at what you've calculated you realize you captured infinity in a little formula... Three times over!

    You've done three impossible things and distilled it into a little formula.
