    It follows from: n^a n^b = n^(a+b)
    n^a     n^0 = n^(a+0) = n^a
    So n^0 = 1

    Another way to look at it is to follow the pattern:
    To get from n^5 to n^4 we divide by n
    To get from n^4 to n^3 we divide by n
    To get from n^3 to n^2 we divide by n
    To get from n^2 to n^1 we divide by n
    And finally to get from n^1 to n^0 we divide by n.
    But n^1 = n, and n/n = 1. So n^0 = 1
