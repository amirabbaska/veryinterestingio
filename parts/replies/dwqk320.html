    Mass of Bi-209 = 208.9803987 amu

    Mass of alpha = 4.00260325415 amu

    Mass of Tl-205 =204.9744275 amu (decay product)

    208.9803987 - (4.00260325415+204.9744275) = +0.0033679 amu

    Since the mass of the parent Bi-209 is heaver than the mass of the decay products, it is a reasonable assumption that the Bi-209 is radioactive. Also since the decay energy of the alpha is 3.1 MeV (take the mass excess and use E=mc^2 and convert the units), this is pretty low for alpha decays so we can be pretty certain it will have a very long half-life.

      • RobusEtCeleritas

        Geiger-Nuttall is an empirical method of approximating alpha decay half-lives. All you have to do is plug in the Z and the Q-value for the decay. As long as the Q-value is positive, the equation will give a physically reasonable answer (meaning not zero, infinity, imaginary, etc.).

        There are no assumptions necessary. All you need is a nuclide with a positive alpha decay Q-value.
