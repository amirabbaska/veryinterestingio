    • ThisIsDivi

      "Anything with fluoride is good for strengthening weak enamel, but you're wasting your time using anything with less than 1000ppm ( parts per million) as this is what most toothpastes have.

      Stannous fluoride is very good at strengthening weakened enamel, but it can stain teeth a dark colour. It is therefore best used not as a mouthwash but instead applied directly to problem areas by your dentist so it doesn't affect your smile!

      If you have weak enamel or are prone to caries (tooth decay), the best thing you can do is to use a toothpaste with a high level of fluoride - there are ones that have 2500ppm or 5000ppm, though the latter is chemist-only where I live. You get the best effect if you don't rinse after brushing, but just spit the excess and leave the residue to coat your teeth. "
