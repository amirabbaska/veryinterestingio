  • millijuna

    And if aliens were to look at our solar system in radio, they would see Earth as millions of times brighter than the Sun.

    Well, yes and no. At short range they are, but the inverse square law is a pretty devilish bastard in this respect. So yes, the transmissions we produce on earth are significantly more intense, they are a lot less luminous.

    In the satcom industry, twice a year (around the Equinoxes) we have to deal with the issue of Sun Outages. The geometry works out just so that from the point of view of an antenna on Earth, the sun passes directly behind its target satellite. During that period, the sun drowns out the satellite, causing the receiver connected to that dish to lose lock. Depending on the size of the dish, the frequency, and the point where you are in the cycle, the outages can range from 30 seconds all the way up to 12 minutes or so, and they happen every day, at the same time, for about 2 weeks.

    The Sun produces a vast amount of wideband RF noise.
