  • Pan0pticism

    Yeah. Taste buds are sensitized by heat.

    Also, flavours are volatile compounds that are present in foods. Some of these compounds are activated by heat. Some are destroyed by heat. More and different volatile compounds can be present when food is warmed, when it is too warm, when it is kept warm for too long, when it cools back down, and so on.

    Coffee is a good example. The key aromatic compounds that give coffee its characteristic flavour are altered or destroyed when the water used to brew it is too hot. They also break down when the brewed coffee is left on a heating element too long, to stop it from cooling. If you have a cup of hot coffee that just doesn't taste right, it is usually the way it was prepared rather than the quality of the bean.
