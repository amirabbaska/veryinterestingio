    • Heartbeats_

      There's also something called recruitment of muscle fibers. When you lift, a certain amount of your fibers, let's say 50% are actively contracting to lift. If you're struggling to achieve that motion, your body will 'recruit' the other fibers and the % of fibers activating increases until you can lift, or you near injury.

      Like some else said, holding your breath stabilizes you, which assists in support and recruitment. It's not unhealthy as long as you're getting sufficient oxygen.

    • thecptawesome

      Imagine the heavy back squat. The bar is on your traps or back. What js producing the force during the lift? The glutes, hamstrings, quads, adductors. That force has to be transferred through your body from those muscles up to the bar. Your torso needs to remain locked and rigid so you don't absorb any of the force generated by your legs. Breathing definitely hurts your ability to generate a rigid torso. That's why we take a deep breath, contract everything, perform the rep, then take another breath.
