    • Chimcan

      Different cells will look different due to hypoxia. The cells I mentioned (neurons) begin by looking shrunken and more pink than usual, with smudged nuclei.

      Hypoxic neurons

      Normal neurons

      At this stage, the neurons are damaged - depending on the degree of hypoxia, they could recover, but may also die, be cleared by immune cells (macrophages), and that area of brain becomes gliotic (brain's version of scarring).

      Edit: This is a microscopic appearance, seen under a microscope, on a slide, after special preparation. You would not see this after cracking open the skull and cutting into the brain.
