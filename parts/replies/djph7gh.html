    The thing about professors is a bit of an odd one.

    Very few professors at top tier universities are there because they want to teach kids fresh out of high school. They're almost all there to do research and publish papers - lessons and grading student papers are a major drain on their available time to write papers. The only marginal benefit to the professors is the possibility they will identify good research assistants.

    So there's this assumption that graduates from prestigious universities have recieved education from the best educators possible that may be a lot less true than people think.
