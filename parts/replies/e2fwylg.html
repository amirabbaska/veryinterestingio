    • mfb-

      What is unclear?

      In Newtonian mechanics they do, in special relativity they do not. Trying to accelerate something in its direction of motion is much "harder" than trying to accelerate it orthogonal to it. If you have a force e.g. pointing at a 45 degree angle relative to the velocity for an ultrarelativistic particle you mainly accelerate the particle sidewards in the lab frame.

      F = gamma^3 m a in the direction of motion, F = gamma m a orthogonal to it (where m is the (rest) mass). For fast particles, gamma is large.
