    Teacher here, and You're not technically wrong, or right, about that point. Though it might be different for university professors, teachers are supposed to use their emotions for the sake of the Student's development/classroom management. If a student is doing something that annoys you/ disrupts the class, you have to use a proper show of the right emotion to let that student know that his/her behavior is not okay. This isn't to put "authority" over the student, but it let's them know that as a human, you have boundries and that if they do X to a person, they will probably react with Y. (A little more to it than that, with things like setting base rules at the beginning of the year and modeling behavior you want to see in the class, setting up a class of equals, not teacher vs student, etc). So you do need to show emotions, but they are very controlled yet genuine emotions. Or sometimes a situation isnt getting the usual/appropriate emotional reaction as it should, but you need to act appropriately and consistantly for this too (example: maybe you had such a good evening the night before that nothing is gonna bother you today, but johny is throwing paper airplanes at another student. So you have to still act like this is bothering you for the sake of the class and johny's emotional development). As a teacher you really don't want to fly off the handle because if it's not appropriate for them to do it, it's certainly not appropriate for you to do it. And there are times when a student is going to come to difficult times in their life and you need to be able to show a really human side of yourself if you are going to help them through it.

    When I first started teaching I thought I had to only keep up a mask of pleasantness and try to not let anything bother me, even when things were eating at me hard. Students really liked me for being nice but even my best students walked all over me and accomplishing any work was so difficult. I also missed a lot of chances to really connect with my students and become more involved in the school community. Then again, other teachers at this school reacted in very inappropriate ways with their students, such as excessive yelling, threatening, etc. It made professional development very hard and I left after my year was up.

    Tl;dr: emotions are very important to a teaching environment, but they are intelligently managed genuine emotions, not off-the-handle emotions.
