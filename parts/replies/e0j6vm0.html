    • Milleuros

      That's how we test that a theory is correct (or "correct enough" I should say).

      Coming up with a model to describe phenomena that we observe is pretty good, but it does not tell anything behind the phenomena themselves. Instead, if you are able to observe some phenomena, come up with a grand theory that describes them and also describes phenomena we never observed, and then you observe them, you conclude that your base hypotheses are pretty good.

      There were plenty of other phenomena like that. General Relativity predicted a ton of stuff, and all of them were observed much later: gravitational waves, gravitational lensing, gravitational redshift, black holes, expansion of universe, and so on.

      The Higgs Boson was also first mathematically predicted, and only afterwards observed.
