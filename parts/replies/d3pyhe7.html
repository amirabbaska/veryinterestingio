    • thebellmaster1x

      And it may interest you even more to know that the standard way of ~keeping it open~ closing it is with indomethacin, which is basically just extra-strong ibuprofen.

      NSAIDs like ibuprofen, indomethacin, aspirin, and naproxen are prostaglandin inhibitors, and one of the prostaglandins (I've long forgotten exactly which one) is responsible for ~closing~ keeping the ductus open.

      NSAIDs have quite a lot of useful functions outside of people's usual thoughts of "oh, this is good for general aches." For example, indomethacin is essentially a cure for hemicrania continua, a rare type of headache related to cluster headaches, that is pretty much otherwise untreatable. But the fact that indomethacin works so well compared to a typical migraine/cluster headache makes it almost diagnostic of the condition (as well as a similar kind of headache known as paroxysmal hemicrania).
