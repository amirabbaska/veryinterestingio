      • cesium14

        Red+blue=purple has to do with the way the brain maps a spectrum to a perceived color, and is not exactly related to spatial interference. Basically we have three types photoreceptor rod cells, each of which has a distinct spectral response, peaking at red, green, and blue. And the excitation levels of the three determine the color we see. The combination of red and blue lights excite the rod cells in an identical way as the monochromatic violet light, so the brain can't tell them apart, interpreting both as the same color purple
