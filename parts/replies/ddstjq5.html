    The 8-bit processor would itself come with a ROM - read-only-memory, which would provide basic functionality. To the best of my knowledge, this was actually hardware. As in, the programs were written with logic gates.

    Just a small niggle: you don't write programs for "MS-DOS"; you write programs for "architecture X".

    Windows (and Apple) changed things up by offering not just an operating system that let you organize your computer, but also offered direct support to integrate your programs into that system. It's easy to forget that this was not a primary task of the operating system before GUI's started to take over.
