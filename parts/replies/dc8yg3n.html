    The "common" pathway is:

    Oppositional Defiant Disorder> Conduct Disorder> Antisocial Personality Disorder

    Many people with ODD and CD also are diagnosed with ADHD. ADHD does not cause sociopathic/psychopathic traits. The diagnosis criteria does not even cover those traits any more than most other psychiatric disorders.

    In sum, many people diagnosed with ODD/CD/ASPD also carry, or previously carried an ADHD diagnosis. In some cases it's mainly because their behavior was labelled as "impulsive" and "hyperactive" and someone missed out on the other traits. So, if you are freaking out that you, or your child, with ADHD is going to go totally psycho, it's unlikely.

      ADHD=Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (either predominantly inattentive, hyperactive, or combined)

      ASPD=Antisocial Personality Disorder (think sociopath/psychopath traits)

      CD=Conduct Disorder (junior version of Antisocial Personality Disorder)

      ODD=Oppositional Defiant Disorder (think severe problems with authority; super irritable, angry, vindictive kid)

      DSM-V=The Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders, Fifth Edition
