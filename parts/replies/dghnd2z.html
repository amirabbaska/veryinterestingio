    • Saint_Nitouche

      Yep, it can definitely be a breath of fresh air -- though it does have its own share of cynicism, since it's very much an Internet-era, post-9/11 kind of cultural moment. I tend to see postpostmodernism not as a blanket rejection of all the things postmodernism said; that'd be reactionary. It's more like... the postmodernists saw all these problems, and when they saw them, they just threw up their hands and said "well, shit! Guess the most we can do is make a joke out of it."

      The postpostmodernists acknowledge the same problems, but actually have a go at trying to tackle some of them. Even if it's ultimately impossible, the intention behind it is valuable, IMO.
