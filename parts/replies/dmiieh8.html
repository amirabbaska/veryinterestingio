    • adamxftl

      100-120 times a minute, push about 1/3 of the depth of the person's chest. Make sure you allow for full recoil. It's not easy like it looks on tv. You literally have to put a lot of your weight on the person's chest. If you are the first one starting cpr on an older person, you will more than likely break their ribs.

      Take a CPR class. You'll get plenty of opportunities to experience the technique. It's not exactly accurate (a plastic mannequin can't quite replicate the combination of bone, cartilage and tendons in the chest) You might never have to use it but you will help so much if you take it and know at least a little of what to do.
