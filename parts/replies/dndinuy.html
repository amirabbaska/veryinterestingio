    • Nowhere_Man_Forever

      I still think carbon is the natural choice. Nothing else can come close to the complexity that organic chemistry provides, and any sort of life would require this complexity. Any life would require some analogue of enzymes, because the formation of complex molecules will always be kinetically unfavored and it is difficult to imagine an inorganic chemical framework allowing for this. Self replication in biological systems always requires a macromolecule of some sort, and this is pretty much exclusively catalyzed by protein enzymes.
