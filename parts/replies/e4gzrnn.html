    • jklmnoq

      When you die your cells do not immediately die, but shortly afterwards as they cannot replenish nutrients from your blood. This is why organ donation is possible, but must be done quickly.

      So if a person is left out in the sun right after they die, then their skin would produce some (read: very little) melanin. How much depends on an enormous variety of factors but it's highly unlikely that enough melanin would be produced before cell death that it would create a tanning effect on the deceased.

      UV rays are still absorbed by the body even after somebody dies. The energy from these rays cooks the cells they come into contact with like they normally do while we are alive. While technically a sunburn, we wouldn't see the normal bright red swelling that we associate with sunburns because there's no blood flow in the deceased.
