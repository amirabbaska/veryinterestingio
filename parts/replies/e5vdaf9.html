        Yes. You could use a flexible bolt and it may have a flight path similar to what you'd expect from archer's paradox, especially as crossbows tend towards very short draw length and high draw weight.

        The thing to keep in mind is the archer's paradox was kind of useful, as it means you can aim by pointing your bow at something, rather than aiming by pointing your arrow at something. Presumably this is much more natural, especially for high draw strength like English longbows, where holding at full draw could be very difficult so a more natural aiming motion is probably best.

        However for crossbows you can easily aim down the bolt, and even if you hip fire, naturally tending to aim with the bow or stock, you will only be off on range, which is less likely to miss a human entirely, who are typically taller than they are wide. There was no reason to deal with a more wobbly flight path and potentially less penetration for the crossbow (which already required higher draw strength to compete with bows) so stiffer quarrels are used.
