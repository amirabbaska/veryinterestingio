  • loveinthemodernera

    In any type of power generation, you need power to make power.

    With large wind turbines you'll need enough to pitch the blades to capture energy and thus spin. Apart from that you'll need energy to power the various instruments and systems that are required for operation. It's not necessarily a motor that spins it. The wind solely does that, but if wind speeds are low the turbine looses money if it is on stand by mode.

    After reaching a certain rpm mark, you'll start making net positive energy and make money.
