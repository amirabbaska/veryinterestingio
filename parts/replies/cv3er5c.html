    Check out some of the other answers (Becca and I are doing this at the same time so we're jumping around a bit!) for more detail, but long and short of it:

    We currently believe that the most likely hypothesis is intentional disposal of bodies is most likely due to inaccessibility of the cave, as well as lack of other animal remains. Animals (including hominids) could sometimes get lost in caves, if they were seeking refuge or water, but this is quite a long way to go and hard to access. It doesn't seem as likely, to us, that multiple hominins/ groups would have all happened to get lost in the same remote, inaccessible chamber over and over again. And if it was someplace that they might have ended up, why didn't other animals also make their way into the chamber? Those are the questions we've asked ourselves and that now we're asking the world!
