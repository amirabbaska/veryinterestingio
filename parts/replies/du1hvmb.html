      • grispo

        Exactly. A properly insulated and ventilated attic should be the same temperature as outside.

        If insulation or ventilation is inadequate, the attic will heat up and melt snow on the roof. As the water travels down over the soffit, cold air comes from underneath and refreezes the water, forming icicles and more importantly, ice dams.

        Ice dams are huge collections of ice that form in and over your eavestroughs and they can literally tear your eavestroughs off of your house.

      • undercoveryankee

        Exactly. Icicles form when you have liquid water flowing into an area that's cold enough to freeze it. If the source of the water is snow melting on a warm roof, that's heat that you're paying for that you don't get to benefit from inside the house.
