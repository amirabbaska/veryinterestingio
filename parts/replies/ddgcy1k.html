      When there is a unanimous consensus of a "learned class" -- scientists, to use your example, about something we simply can't prove ourselves, we take them at their word and have for centuries.

      People have learned not to trust those establishments though. Lately, discoveries are flying in the face of beliefs that have been held since childhood, or beliefs that someone builds their whole life upon.

      More realistically, the way the news reports on scientific breakthrough has been doing a lot to weaken the trust in science overall. When a hack journalist puts out a headline that says "BREAKTHROUGH SUBSTANCE WILL REPLACE PLASTIC IN 4 YEARS!" but the actual science is that someone found Graphene and nobody knows how to anything with it yet, you and I know that the journalist is looking for clicks, and isn't representing the facts correctly, but someone else who doesn't know anything about the topic might just think that scientists were lying to the media to get attention and funding.

      The establishment has worn away a lot of its own credibility over the years, while at the same time, people are losing their ability to understand what's going on in science, and how the scientific method works. More and more of the world is made up by things that you can't see with your own eyes. When you lead a simple "touch based" lifestyle, you naturally distrust all of those other things, and feel like everyone is trying to pull one over on you.
