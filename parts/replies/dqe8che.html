      The second law of thermodynamics gives rise to the arrow of time. It states that, in a closed system, entropy will always increase. This means that as we experience systems moving from lower entropy (highly organized) to higher entropy (highly disorganized), we see the second law at work. You never see an egg yolk jump back into the egg and seal the crack, because that would be a decrease in entropy.
