    Indeed, this was a major reason why the Toyota F1 effort failed. First they had to pay an absolute fortune - the budget was $450m p.a. by the end, the largest in F1 - to hundreds of employees with any kind of F1 experience to move to Cologne in Germany. They recruited some guys from German sportscar teams, but a large chunk came from that British cluster and they paid over the odds.

    Second the ones that moved weren't the most experienced, just the most willing to relocate. They never had a really first class designer on the team for example.

    They made other mistakes, like driver selection, but the location was a key issue from day one. Red Bull learned from that.
