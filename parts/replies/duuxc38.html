      Yup.

      Jurisdictions have a cut off for when you can have a free lawyer. Most places it is like where you are: under six months in jail, and no free lawyer.

      Interestingly, for anyone out there facing a similar conundrum, there is a motion you can file in most jurisdictions that will get you a lawyer for a minor crime, even though you're not normally entitled to one. The motion basically says there are complicated legal issues in the case, and it just straight up wouldn't be fair to expect a lay person to handle it. Pro tip: one thing you can claim that almost always get you a lawyer, is that there is state evidence you would like to try to "suppress."
