      I'm sure there's a cool grammatical term for it, but "bimonthly", "semiannually" and the like are all adverbs, though they are commonly detatched from the verb and used as an adjective modifying the noun. Adverbs can occasionally do that, though its not commonly obvious. The "bimonthly report" is the report that happens (implied verb) bimonthly. Breaking it down by root and pre/suffixes, bi=two, month, ly=adverb modifier, so its more like "bimonth" is its own entity meaning "two months" then -ly modifying it into meaning "(verb) once in two months" If that makes sense...
