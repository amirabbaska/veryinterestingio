  • Teekno

    To add on to this excellent answer: the issue is the licenses. Streaming content companies need a license to provide that content. That's negotiated with the content owner (the studios).

    But with physical media, the content is bound to the media itself. When you purchase a DVD, it comes with a license to watch that DVD. Whoever possesses the DVD has the license to watch it. The license is valid for the useful life of the media itself.

    So when Redbox buys a DVD, they get that license. When you rent a DVD from Redbox, you get that license as long as you are in possession of the DVD. It's not something that is negotiable.

    This "bound license" is the core of the first-sale doctrine. Since you can't detach the license from the media, the content owner has no say in what happens after it's first sold. With digital content, there's no physical media, so there isn't a transferable license.
