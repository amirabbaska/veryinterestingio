    I did this years ago in college. Using a back-to-back prism pair for each eye, hot glued in a plastic mask. I was bicycling around (off street) a few hours later. Switching back and forth wasn't a problem. It felt like the spatial world was only narrowly coupled to visual appearance. There was a "appearing over there means its that way now" linkage. Which would start out being very conscious, with a hesitation, but then quickly became automatic, with occasional relapses. Physical skills mostly seemed to just use the unchanged spatial model. Hand-eye coordination worked fine once the hand was moving, but would have hesitation and false starts at the beginning of movement - "wait, which way should it start going?" My now fuzzy recollection is it was fun, but easier than I expected.
