  • _Jake_The_Snake_

    Good structural integrity, mostly. A seed's primary job is to house whatever "startup" nutrients the plant needs, as well as to protect the important stuff. That's basically it. There's not much that a microbe or another small organism could get to to decompose it.

    Furthermore, if there is very little oxygen and water, there aren't many more common & naturally occurring small molecules that can cause degradation. Oxygen and water would be the two major ones, and their presence would also indicate other potential hazards for the seed. If the seed isn't growing, it isn't in the presence of them.

    Increasingly, we are also finding that enzymes stabilize other structures to avoid degradation. For example, LEA proteins are produced in low water or high salt environments to stabilize other crucial proteins and enzymes.
