    How do countries know when a missile has been launched by another country?

    Generally it's infrared detection by satellite. ICBMs generate a TON of heat when they launch, and satellites (usually very high up in geosynchronous orbit) use highly sensitive infrared sensors to (hopefully) detect the massive heat bloom associated with a missile launch. Radar is not relied on as a primary detection method, at least by countries with a satellite network, simply because, as you said, radar can't cover everywhere.
