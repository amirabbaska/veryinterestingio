    they discovered they needed about 3 times more powerful lasers

    That's not entirely accurate. The main problems NIF runs into are the same ones physicists were pointing out at it's inception and that is the problem of Rayleigh-Taylor instabilities. The paper published not so long ago by Hurricane et al where they used a "high foot" to puff up the capsule and smooth out imperfections helped to clamp down on the instabilities and brought the experiments to within a factor of 2 on the rho-R, Lawson criterion for ignition. The result was that the energy produced by the fusion reaction in the capsule was more than the energy dumped into the capsule. This is not break-even yet since there are significant losses all along the chain from producing the laser up to the final optic before the capsule.

    http://www.nature.com/nature/journal/v506/n7488/abs/nature13008.html

    As far as laser energy goes, NIF is actually capable of putting out a lot more laser energy than it currently uses on experiments. The problem isn't with the laser energy but rather the damage threshold of the optics used to transport the beam to the target chamber (and possibly some optics earlier on in the chain).
