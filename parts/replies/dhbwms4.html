  • CalibanDrive

    there are two different phenomena here.

    The static blobs that never move except just as your eye ball moves are called 'floaters' or muscae volitantus, they are little bits of junk like detached tissue or red blood cells that are suspended in the fluid that fills up your eyeball, and they cast blurry shadows on your retina.

    There are also bright dots with dark tails darting around in squiggly lines in your vision when you stare at a bright blue sky. This is called the "blue field entoptic phenomenon". It is caused by white blood cells squeezing through the capillaries that sit in front of your retina.

    Normally your retina filters out the red color of the capillaries so 'red' is neutral, but when a white blood cell passes through the capillary, it created a little window in that 'red' and some extra light comes through which appears as a bright dot. behind the white blood cells there are some extra red blood cells that bunched up, appearing as dark tail that follows the bright dot.
