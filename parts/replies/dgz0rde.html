      • elonhunk

        Every trait that exists is not due to natural selection.

        Traits which are useless or even disadvantageous can evolve as natural byproducts of other traits. For example, say deer benefit from increased levels of a hormone that helps them with run faster , but the increased level of hormones also causes a great increase in size of their horn. Does this mean their larger horns evolved because natural selection created a pressure for bigger horns? No.

        Or they can just be byproducts of biological functions that did not cause enough of a negative pressure to cause the species to go extinct.

        For example, disease. Animals don't get diseases because it's evolutionary advantageous. They do simply because of natural causes of how biology works.
