    • whitcwa

      They are detected and added automatically each time an automatic black balance is done. The problem is that the circuit sometimes picks the wrong pixel to mask. It usually picks one to the right of the bad one, but it can be any of the eight adjacent pixels. Once marked as bad, it will not unmask and pick the correct one. I'm the poor soul who gets to find the incorrectly masked bad ones and mask the real bad guys. There are 3 CCDs in each camera. It takes me a couple of hours if I haven't seen the camera in a year. A 55 inch monitor helps a lot.
