    I worked tech support for Dish Network in the early 2000s for a few years. I'm not sure if they still do their channel authorizations the same, or if DirecTV used a similar system or still do, but...

    The signal that refreshes the authorizations on the card is for your receiver/smartcard combination. Multiple receivers will require multiple signals. We (as a customer service rep) can manually send the signal, which was called a "hit." But there is no real good reason to keep sending that signal once the change got made. The simple "hack" was (and may likely still be) to order the channels you want, wait until they come on, unplug your receivers, call up and cancel the channels. Wait at least 24 hours, 48 to be safe, and then plug them back in. You conveniently miss that "remove authorization" signal and will likely keep your programming for years. No hacking or special cards or voided warranties or even any laws broken.

      Cable systems use the same "hit" to tell the device it's authorized, and which channels it can display. The 2 way boxes would have to reply in order for the box to stay active. If no reply it was automatically sent a deauthorization hit after a certain time so those weren't really hackable to my knowledge. We would get trouble calls for non-responders often in houses with sub-band signal problems. It would cause the boxes to reboot and have no channels because they would randomly poll boxes from the head end. One way boxes didn't do this so I assume you could still do this.
