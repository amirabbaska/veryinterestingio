      • amaurea

        One of S2's neighbors, S14 (aka S0-16), is the one with the closest periastron distance (only 45 AU), and hence the highest maximum speed. At this point it moves at 11,700 km/s, or 4% of the speed of light. That's more than twice as fast as S2. However, due to its highly elliptical orbit, it spends most of its time moving much slower than S2. It is very difficult to observe stars in this region, so there may be stars on even closer orbits that we haven't detected yet.
