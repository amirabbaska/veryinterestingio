      Doctor's office no, hospital yes.

      Even before everything was digitized we'd had the central person registry for decades, so as long as a move from one doctor to another was planned the papers our parents filled out when we were born were manually faxed ahead (along with all updates).

      Only exception is if you make an unplanned visit to the hospital without being in immediate danger, in which case they might do the verbal equivalent, asking about relevant history, allergies, etc. Since insurance isn't relevant they don't need the lengthy written forms.
