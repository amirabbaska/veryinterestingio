      • wlesieutre

        Stereopsis aside, we still have depth perception beyond 1 km within the atmosphere. This is mainly provided by "atmospheric perspective." As there's more and more air between you and an object, it will appear desaturated, blueish (or reddish, brownish, depending on time of day, presence of smog, etc.), and lower contrast.

        For example: http://www.flickriver.com/photos/kmyelin/2058683220/

        This won't help you with the moon and sun since the amount of air in the way is the same in either case (one atmosphere worth), but it reaches much further than our stereo vision.
