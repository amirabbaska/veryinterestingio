    • silent_cat

      So blackbody radiation prevents permanent runaway warming from occurring as the planet will eventually reach an equilibrium point where energy in = energy out. It wouldn't be able to reverse a warming trend though, correct?

      Well, "permanent runaway" would amount to "eventually becoming hotter than the sun" so in that sense you're right, the laws of physics will prevent that. Eventually you will reach an equilibrium point, which in the case of Venus is 462 degrees C. Not exactly something you want to rely on.

      Are there any known negative feedback mechanisms for cooling events?

      Sure, if the planet was covered in ice it would reflect so much sunlight that it might never warm up.

      The basic thing is: the climate is quite complicated and we don't know about all the different things that effect it, but we have pretty good estimates. What we do know is that at the current temperature level we can survive fine and at a different temperature level, who knows? We're going to find out though. (Or rather, our children and grandchildren will).
