    • kolkolkokiri

      I can't speak for hiding the evidence but I can talk to cleaning up blood. The problem with bleach / peroxide is it's obvious something was there and probably left blood in the cracks of the subfloor. It also bleaches fabric.

      • Dab dry as much as possible.
      • Cold water only. Hot water will make it impossible. Ice water even.
      • Scrub with soap and cold water, rinse several times. Salt can be used as an abrasive.
      • 1/4 hydrogen peroxide and water soak. Rinse. Scrub with hydrogen peroxide soaked cloth.
      • If no hydrogen peroxide bleach can be used as well, about 1/4 bleach. You have a larger chance of damaging fabric.

  • PedroDaGr8

    Note that alcohol above around 70% is actually not a disinfectant. This is because you need some water in order to rupture membranes and viral coats. Also, alcohol is not sporicidal so it will not kill bacterial or fungal spores. Therefore, it's not a complete full spectrum disinfectant.

    Note, alcohol is great at getting permanent marker off of non-porous surfaces. We use it all the time in the lab. Write on glass with a sharpie, then wipe it off with a paper towel with alcohol on it.
