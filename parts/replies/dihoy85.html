    Just wanna say that I'm an opera singer as well and I would be what is called a "Bass-Baritone" and there are a couple of famous/badass roles that are quickly associated with that voice type; think Wotan in Wagner's Ring Cycle or Figaro in Mozart's Marriage of Figaro. In regards to weight with this particular voice type, most bass-baritones that have incredibly powerful voices are in fact rather tall (6 feet and up) and weigh anywhere between 200-260 lbs which depending on body composition and actual height is usually not that "fat." Most of the best bass-baritones--either colleagues or legends in the business--that I have looked up to in fact usually had very muscular builds (see George London's "Leb wohl" https://youtu.be/B24qmkKfK7w or Greer Grimsley's Flying Dutchman https://youtu.be/p6DkNfNPt3s). I'm not entirely sure if what voice part you sing has some type of "optimum body shape" that is in some way correlating to the necessary muscles that are required of the different resonating frequencies/harmonics, but there is a general or stereotypical kind of "body shape" attributed to many different voice types.

      Suzuki is mainly a violin/strings method of how to learn music.

      Lots of teachers will have you hold weight in front of you to help engage your intercostal and lower abdominal muscles, which are the primary muscles of breath in singing. It's mainly rooted in the Italian *bel canto*** approach to singing, though the Germans did a fair amount of it, too.
