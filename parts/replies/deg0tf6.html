    • AurorEowyn

      Nielsen has these devices (I can't remember what they're called) that hook up to your TV. This device will record what show you're watching and when you're watching it. So whenever you go to watch TV you'll have to signify which person(s) are watching. That's how they get age/sex demographics.

      They have two different ways to measure: ratings and shares. Ratings are the percent of people watching a program out of every person who owns a TV. Shares are the percent of people who are watching a certain program out of only the TVs that are on. (Hopefully that makes sense, haha).

      Nielsen takes a cross-section of families in the United States to select their samples.
