    Perfectly acceptable answers for: "Are you going to the store?"

    ' I'm not.' ' I'm driving.' ' I'm walking. '

    ' I am.'

    In the first three, we are waiting for something more after "I'm". We just expect it ("I'm") to reference something. The last one could be used as a prefix for the first three (in place of I'm), but it also stands fine on it's own. If we stop talking after "am", then the mind decides the sentence is finished. If we stop after "'m", the mind just keeps looking for the reference.

    Don't know why, it just does.
