    While individual polar bears may be able to successfully mate with individual grizzlies, the two groups mate at different times and places.

    In addition to this, genetic compatibility is not an either/or thing. Within a typical species, two healthy, fertile individuals can produce fertile offspring close to 100% of the time. As speciation occurs, genetic incompatibilities begin to arise that make it less likely, but not impossible, for those offspring to be produced.
