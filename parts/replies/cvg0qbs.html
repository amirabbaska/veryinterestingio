    • lachlanhunt

      Even with a second factor, you should still change your password if there's any chance it has been compromised. The second factor only increases the time before a successful attack. Most 2FAs are just 6 digit one time codes that change every 30s or 60s.

      It's not completely infeasible to correctly guess that. An attacker could simply pick a couple of random ones and then just repeatedly try those same codes over time. Chances are that any random 6 digit code will become valid sooner or later.
