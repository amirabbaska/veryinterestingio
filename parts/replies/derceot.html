    • davjet1

      The science is that naturally when you look at a bright light, your pupils contract accordingly in order to limit the amount of light entering them to preserve themselves. But when the pupil contracts, it also causes the light that enters to be more focused. The light from the sun is filled with very high energy UV rays which when focused can cause a lot of damage to the cells of your eyes. Normally side effects appear if someone stares at the sun for a long period of time, but its better to be safe so its not recommended to look at the sun for any period of time.
