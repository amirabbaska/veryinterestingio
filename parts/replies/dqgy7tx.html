• danby

    Science never really answer why questions. It just answers how questions.

    This clearly isn't the case. Whether science can answer both why and how questions is typically a function of the extent to which the system under analysis is sufficienctly accessible to observation/experiment.

    For instance, take "why does the sun rise in the east?", we are now in a position to answer both the why and how of this query. How: the earth rotates, why: gravity. In contrast "why is there gravity?" asks about processes we simply don't have sufficient access to to answer. Perhaps one day physics will progress to the point we can answer that question. I would certainly hope we are aiming that high.
