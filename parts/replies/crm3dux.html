      • rokthemonkey

        Triple car tandems, as I recall, were theoretically faster and more efficient than two car tandems, but I think it ended up being too hard for everyone to run together for it to be a viable option. A few teams tried it in practice sessions and IIRC the speed gained just wasn't worth the trouble. It's also important to note when you're tucked up behind another car like that, you can't see shit. It's kind of problemactic if you've got a train going at 200+ mph and the two cars pushing you can't see out their front window. The drivers actually had to directly communicate with each other in order for two car tandems to work.

        You also have to consider that most teams are either two-car teams or four-car teams. If everyone ran in three car tandems, the four car teams would have an odd man out and the two car teams would have to rely on a non-teammate to be competitive.

      • rokthemonkey

        Yes, they're all on teams. NASCAR allows teams to have up to 4 full time cars and 1 more part time car. Some teams even have sister teams and technical partnerships with other teams.For example, in that video you see the cars 5(Kasey Kahne), 24(Jeff Gordon), 48(Jimmie Johnson) and 88(Dale Earnhardt Jr). Those cars are all a part of the Hendrick Motorsports team. Hendrick Motorsports also provides engines and Chassis' to several other Chevy teams, like Stewart-Haas and I believe Ganassi. Those teams are essentially sister teams to Hendrick. Anyway, since the drivers in that video are working with their teammates, they're choosing to do what's best for both them and their teammates. The ones in that video who are working with a non-teammate are doing so simply because that's their best shot at winning.
