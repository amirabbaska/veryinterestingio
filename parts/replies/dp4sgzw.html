      Close but not quite. The way an apple phone knows a phone is connected ot the charger is that instead of the data lines giving data, theyre literally just receiving some combination of 2v and 2.7v static voltage on the data lines. Anyone can do this with resistors to make a voltage divider. 2v on both lines means the charger can provide 500 ma, though only legacy chargers really use this, the stock iphone 'sugar cube'charger has one line at 2.0v and one at 2.7v (i forgot which one is which), which signals that the phone can draw up to 1000ma. The stock ipad charge has the lines switched (the opposite lines have 2 and 2.7v on them) This tells the ipad that it can draw 2000ma out of it. Fun fact: while plugging in an iphone 5 or lower will only pull 1000ma, if you plug a iphone 6 or better into an ipad charger, itll charge faster, not at 2000ma but something like 1300 - 1600 ma. Theres also chargers with 2.7v on both lines meaning the charger can provide up to 2400ma, though apple doesnt sell any of these.

      Android phones have a different charging spec where the two data lines are just shorted together to mark the usb port as a charger. The way these regulate current is that when the phone starts pullling too much power theyll lower the voltage that theyre supplying(on the 5v lines, not the data lines). Note that this is the proper way to do a usb charger as this method is actually listed in the offical usb spec.

      Of course this all a mute point as any modern phone, apple ones included have a chip that lets them recognize both types of chargers. Also sometimes youll see 'smart chargers' that say they "identify your phone and supply the proper charging signal". In reality they have a chip in them that is actually just doing one of these first then the other to see what the phone will accept.

      also apple doesnt check if a charger is counterfeit via some fancy detection. All they do is do 2) of what u/aj67891 said, they say the charger is counterfeit if the voltage or amperage is too low OR the charger cuts out multiple times. You can test this by using a genuine apple charger and lightning cable, and plugging it in and out every few seconds, after 3 or 4 times you get the "accessory not supported" messege
