    • ArcFurnace

      Both, depending strongly on the metal and the temperature.

      Tungsten has an extremely low vapor pressure even at very high temperatures. On the other hand, zinc and cadmium will sublimate (go directly from solid to gas phase) at low pressures, and have low boiling points for metals even at standard atmospheric pressure. Finally, essentially every metal has very low vapor pressure if you cool it down towards absolute zero, as the available thermal energy drops below the strength of the metallic bonds.
