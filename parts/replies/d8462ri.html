    1) If your microwave is properly shielded this won't happen.

    Every microwave on the market "leaks": They have shields and seals, and so on, but a few watts will leak from any model on the market, short of outrageously irrational shielding. So no, your advice is wrong -- buy a new microwave and in the same situation you'll likely have the identical problem. As the top post mentioned, wifi operates at a very low power level, so even a tiny amount of radio noise will knock it out.

    The people who have this problem usually have a specific scenario where a small amount (e.g. normal) of microwave leakage causes issues. A microwave between the AP and the consumer, or with a consumer with an iffy signal where the load of the added noise disrupts it. Etc.

    And there is another post that says "If your microwave is knocking your wifi offline, you have leakage and shouldn't be using the microwave." - that's just ridiculous. There is no health impact of a microwave having a tiny, normal amount of leakage, and if you go and replace the microwave you are almost certainly going to have exactly the same outcome with the replacement.
