    • VBtheHun

      It is quite impossible for such a thing to happen now, as any organic compounds that are thus produced will probably consumed by other organisms, practically instantaneously.

      One can truly appreciate the lightning-striking-water thing, if we just grasp the time scales involved. The first organisms probably took hundreds of millions of years to form from this chemical soup in the ocean. There were so many molecules, and so many interactions, and so much time, that probabilistically molecules that could self-replicate were bound to form. Also, it helps to remember that these molecules wouldn't 'go bad' with time, because there were no agents that consumed them.

      Tl;dr: Yes nature does do step one of the process, but there isn't enough time or lack of disturbance to actually proceed with biogenesis.
