    They used to say similar things about the manganese nodules found all over the ocean floor, sometimes in high concentrations. ... The nodules are much easier to harvest than the mud this article refers to, but despite people having been working on it since the 1970s no economically feasible method has been developed.

    As it turns out, a lot of the hype about the undersea riches of manganese nodule mining in the 1970s was related to the Glomar Explorer, a ship that was supposedly going to study ways to mine manganese nodules, but which was actually a cover story for Project Azorian, a secret CIA mission to recover a sunken Russian submarine.

    Point being, this rare earth deposit is less practical to mine than a resource that was only useful as a Cold War cover story.
