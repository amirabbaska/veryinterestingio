      Due to my kitchen setup, I spend about 4-6 hours a week, in total, writing the next weeks menu, then prepping and cooking. I’ll prep things like cured meats, pickles, preserves, meat, bone and vegetable stocks on Sunday. In addition to, things that can go in the freezer. Like I’ll grind and potion hamburgers, then I’ll use my vacuum sealer and I’ll make packages of 4 patties and freeze them off, pulling them out as needed.

      I spend 1 hour, twice a week, prepping mise en place - things like diced garlic and shallots, cooked rice and pasta, blanched veg like asparagus and broccoli.

      Dinner then works like a restaurant, where any entrée on the menu can be cooked from start to finish in under 10 minutes, with no leftovers. One pan, one entrée

      Not sure if you could pull this off in a home kitchen, unless you, like me, built a commercial kitchen at home.

      When people come over and see our kitchen, the 1st thing they ask is if we cater. We don’t. It’s just for us :)
