    Does beta+ (positron) decay occur exclusively from antimatter, or are there cases where regular matter emits a positron when decaying?

    • xatham

      Actually, it comes mostly from regular matter. When an atom has “too many protons”, it emits a positron and becomes a neutron. Likewise, a neutron emits an electron to become a proton,

      Theres also the opposite, electron capture, where the proton “eats” an electron to become a neutron, but that doesnt emit any betas.
