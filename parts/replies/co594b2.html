        40fps

        I have done a lot of tests with blinking LEDs, and there is a very noticeable difference between a LED blinking 40 times a sec and one blinking 100 times a sec. The 40Hz one is more or less stable, but it looks a bit noisy and looks to be fluctuating a bit. The 100Hz one is rock-solid. No noise, no fluctuations, nothing. And IF 40Hz was enough, why then are CTRs 60Hz and up? For me, even a 60Hz CRT isn't a "stable" image, it's flickering ever so slightly.

        The average human being isn't going to be able to detect any differences in frame rates higher than 40fps

        The average human doesn't know there is a difference. Tell there there is a differance. Then ask them which is better in an ABX test.

        There are people that can't hear the difference between a $5 headset powered by a no-brand chine MP3 player and a HiFi system set up by an audio technician. Their brain is filling in for them. They aren't listening.

        Another problem is the notion of "FPS". A more interesting question is how quickly can in image flash before you before you can't make out what is was? Fighter-pilots have been able to identify the correct plane when shown an image for 1/4000th of a second, of 4000FPS if you'd like. But 30FPS is enough for most motion to be perceived as "smooth".

        TL;DR: There is a huge difference between what is passable as real, and what that is indistinguishable from real. 30FPS is passable as real, 200FPS+ is.
