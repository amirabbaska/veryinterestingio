    • nat_r

      There's typically language in the contract between the officer's union and the municipality that determines disciplinary procedures.

      Unless there was overriding legislation at the state or federal level to stipulate such an action it's going to come down to the negotiators for the municipality and the union. It's obviously not in the union's interest (ignoring PR issues) to have ultra strict disciplinary rules, and to an extent it's not always in the municipality's either. You don't want police so afraid to do their jobs that they don't actually do them.

      Changing that situation if the public believes officer's are given too much leniency requires political pressure on elected officials.
