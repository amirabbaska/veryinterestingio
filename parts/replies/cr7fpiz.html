    • triplehardvark

      Yep the simple way is to call advertisement.js - every adblocker will block it. advertisement.js can just write an invisible div then your page checks for the existence of that div id and if it's not there shows a message.

      I never used this technique properly but I did test sending a "Damaged sector 00FFA11 Failure Imminent" message just to gauge how many people used blockers. It was a lot.

      The other way is just to set a background image on your ad placement divs.
