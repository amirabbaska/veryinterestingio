  • Ryanflick010

    The electric pulses in reference are the same pulses you would receive from your own nerves. Your muscle can only contract in one way and that revolves around a complex psyiological and neurological process so the NMES or FES the PTs are using are not contracting the muscles, they're stimulating the nerves which then contract the muscles. The difference is, when you try to voluntarily contract a muscle with your mind, you stimulate the entire motor unit which would simultaneously contract the entire muscle. With electrical stimulation, you can just activate specific muscles by putting the electrodes over the target muscle and triggering those specific nerves which correspond with the muscle you intend to activate. It also allows for the PT to control the intensity of the muscle contraction which can allow you to contract your muscles without hurting yourself.

    As far muscle growth is concerned, in theory you can build muscle by damaging it and then allowing it to heal, whether that be through electrical stimulation, massage, trauma, etc etc. However, muscle growth is largely different from muscle hypertrophy, strength, or power. In order to gain muscle size, you have to stimulate the muscle in a certain way, the same with muscle strength and power. I've never tried massaging the muscles to force them to grow, but that would be an interesting study if someone wanted to conduct it and I'm sure there would be no lack of willing participants - at least at first, until they realize the amount of pressure and pain they would have to go through lol.
