    To make this answer a bit more advanced - I'm a microbiologist.

    The salt dissolves in solution (water) creating an osmotic gradient to the cells, in effect, water wants to leave the cells to balance the gradient.

    Animal cells have a lot of resources to draw upon to combat this chemical equilibrium where as individual bacterial cells do not, and this will cause them to "lyse".
