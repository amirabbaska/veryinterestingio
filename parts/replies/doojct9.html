    • Shaftway

      This can't possibly be an issue they encountered. The Panama canal is two separate channels with locks. The locks raise and lower the boats so they can get over the land in between. The water used to fill the locks comes from Lake Victoria (IIRC) which is in the middle. The canal isn't just a trench they dug from one end to the other. Dealing with moving boats up and down is literally the problem it solved, so it can't have been a problem if the oceans weren't level.

      What is that nontrivial degree?

      The tidal range is the difference between high and low tide, and depending on where you're talking about, it can be up to fifteen meters. In the open ocean, it's more like half a meter.

      I imagine water is slightly above zero because of surface tension.

      Not exactly. Surface tension affects the edges of the water to a limited degree. With angle of repose, you're looking for a consistent angle -- the same angle when you've got 1kg of material as when you've got 100kg. (Assuming that's enough of the material to form a pile.)
