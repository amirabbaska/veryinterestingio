      I think you have that flip-flopped.

      the left hemisphere does all the linear thinking like math and language. The right hemisphere does all the sort of holistic or foggy thinking like creative work.

      Early in the US Space Program they tested all of the Air Force and navy Pilots for their ability to adapt to new learning situations, to deal with completely unexpected changes. When they had culled the thousands of Pilots down to a few hundred based on this criteria alone they found that half of them were left-handed. They also found a much higher incidence of speech impediments and minor learning disabilities like dyslexia because the individuals that were best at creative problem-solving were worst at rigid, logical, linear thinking.

      It is important to note that hemisphere dominance and handedness are not 100% correlated. There is a small percentage of people who will be right hemisphere dominant but still right handed or left hemisphere dominant and still left handed. There's also a slightly larger number of people who have mixed dominance. The left eye, right hand and right foot is the most common configuration by far. Left-handed people generally have a dominant right eye and left foot but there is a small percentage that has mixed dominance meaning any configuration is possible.

      Then again there are some people, notably autistic but not exclusively so, whose "brain geography" does not comply to most or any of the norms. some people, usually but not always blind, process sound in their visual cortex.

      I have studied all this because I myself am a lefty with a minor abnormality.
