    • Treacherous_Peach

      Day lengths are always changing for all satellites, specifically slowing. Orbiting planets always want to tidal lock with whatever they're orbiting. This means that only one side ever faces what it's orbiting. The moon, for example, is tidally locked with the Earth. Eventually the Earth and Mars will both be tidally locked with the Sun. Mercury is near tidal lock with the Sun already. Where the Earth rotates about 365 times per revolution, Mercury only rotates 1.5 times.
