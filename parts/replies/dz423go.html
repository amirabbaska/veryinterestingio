      plus recycled material is not usually put into new products but repurposed for other things

      With plastics, that's definitely not the case. A ton of "new products" are made out of recycled plastics. Bottles are a great example of that. A lot of them are made from 100% recycled materials. You also see plastic used to make one thing (e.g., milk jugs) being melted down and used to make completely different things (e.g., lawn furniture). Most types of plastics can be melted down and re-molded basically indefinitely. So can almost all glass and metal. Even most paper products can be re-pulped and used to make new products.

      Still, you're right about tires. But that's because the rubber used to make tires is really hard to actually recycle. Chemistry is all wrong. It's only recently that chemical processes have been developed that make it economical to reclaim tire rubber. But most tires still wind up either sitting in landfills or being shredded for other uses.
