    Precisely. It's typically beneficial for plants to surround their seeds with nutrients, which improve the ability of a young seedling to grow. That's the original evolutionary benefit to proto-fruits. But the thing is, there's even more benefit, evolutionarily, to being able to widely disperse seeds and it turns out that animal poop is nearly as good a fertilizer for plants as is the fruit body. Seedlings need some amount of easily accessible calories to begin growing, but once they start photosynthesizing then they need nutrients more than calories, so fertilizer is often just as good as carbs. Being able to spread to more locations (due to seed transport by animals) makes plants much more successful than they would be otherwise, and that favors plants who produce fruits.
