    For those interested;

    Most filters 'should' have a coagulant that traps most the cyrpto bacteria before the sand. I put Aluminium sulphate in the strainer basket during a backwash (the process of cleaning the filter) which then disintergrates and settles a top the filter sand.

    Chemicals commonly used

    Cholrine - Calcium Hypocholrite - the cleaning agent for most pools

    Sodium Bicarbonate - Used to raise the alkalinity in pools, can also clean the pool, as in make tiles sparkle

    Calcium flake - Calcium cholride - Used to raise the calcium levels in the pool

    Co2 - raise acidity levels, maintain correct PH

    Theres loads more, like Bromine & Ozone, but the list above is what most modern pools will have in their plant rooms.

    Now a days pool plants are being fitted with a high powered UV lamp, the water passes over and cleans the water, killing 99% of bacteria. Cholrine is used in conjunction. The main advantages of UV are that you use less chemicals, save money long term & have super clean pool water. Our combined cholrine dropped from 0.6/7 to 0.1/2, sometimes lower.
