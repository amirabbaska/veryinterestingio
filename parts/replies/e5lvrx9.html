    • BitterPomegranate

      Even if you have smoked your whole life, it’s still possible to get a transplant. There was a famous case - a lady named Pam Laffin. She started smoking when she was 10, and had severe COPD by the time she was 26. She got a transplant (probably because she had quit smoking and was the single parent of two young girls), but sadly went into rejection. She was listed for a transplant a second time, but died before she was able to get it (at 31).

      She became famous for doing a lot of work with the CDC, anti-smoking PSAs, and she was featured in an MTV doco which can be viewed here: https://youtu.be/BTTBu59GDEg

      It’s a horrible disease, I am sorry your mum has to deal with it. I think you’re right in saying that she could technically be eligible for a transplant, but a lot of other factors come into play such as age, health, blood type, etc.
