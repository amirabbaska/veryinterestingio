    This is very important. Do you know why industrialization was slow going? We had slaves. As Manu Saadia just put it on Twitter:

    Hero of Alexandria invented a steam engine in 1st century AD. Didn't pick up b/c of slavery in the Roman Empire.

    And while for a long time, it was believed that Hero's engine never went past the "toy" phase, there's newer evidence that steam engines were used to operate the city gates. We could do real work with it. But it never caught on, because you could just make people do the work for you. Why invest in capital when you can literally own labor?
