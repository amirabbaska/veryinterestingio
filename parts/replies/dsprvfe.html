    • boredgamelad

      Debit card protections are not as good as credit card protections. Fraudulent credit transactions can be reversed almost instantly once reported, and fraud protection laws make you liable for much less. Fraudulent debit transactions will not only drain your account of funds before you're likely to notice (and therefore impact your ability to access money you should otherwise have) but it can take several weeks to investigate and reimburse you. You're also potentially liable for a higher portion of the fraudulent amount.

      Honestly you're better off getting a credit card and using it instead of your debit card and just paying the statement amount every month.

    • ReeseSlitherspoon

      Yes and no. Yes in that banks are also going to be monitoring for fradulent charges. Yes in that all the same confirmation numbers and rules appear. But, there's still a reason people say credit is safer: your debit card is a check card, so if something goes wrong, that money is actually taken right out of your account, wheras with credit you elect when to pay the bill. Debit fraud can actually bleed your bank account directly, resulting in overdraft charges or worse. Like, say you have rent due but your account has been emptied. Usually fraud's resolved quickly, but if for some reason there is a deeper investigation, or you have to argue the claim yourself, that money is gone for thar time, so you're shit out of luck on rent for a few days.

      That said, debit is still safeish. Most banks have their shit together on this. Just slightly less safe than credit.
