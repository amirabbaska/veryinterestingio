    • TridentSC2

      Correct! I have taught a lot of CPR courses and this is one of the main questions nowadays: why do we teach compression-only CPR?

      1. as u/Scrub_Lyfe has pointed out we have enough oxygen in our bodies to last the brain for around 10 minutes (+/- ofc)
      2. A cardiac arrest is a shocking situation for anyone not specifically trained on how to react. Having to do only CPR (100 beats/min) is much easier than alternating between CPR and mouth-to-mouth breathing. For anyone interested, you should do CPR 30beats -> 2x mouth-to-mouth -> CPR 30 beats etc. It's just additional stress that has little benefit, hence why we only teach compression-only CPR for bystanders.
