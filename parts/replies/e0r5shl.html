      You could remove nitrogen, but not methane and make it work, but that’s really hard, even for planetary engineering. There has to be a better way.

      Ligeia Mare, one of the great methane/ethane seas in the northern polar region is about 125,000 km^2, and 100 m deep. That’s about 7.5e15 kg of methane and ethane. There’s probably 3x that much in Kraken Mare. Titans atmospheric pressure is 1.5x Earth and it’s radius is 2575 km, with a gravity of 1.35 m/s^2. That gives an atmosphere of around 9e18 kg. So it would seem that evaporating the lakes wouldn’t be enough to increase the methane/ethane fraction. BUT, near the surface, methane concentration levels out at 4.9%.

      Sooo, if you evaporate the lakes with nuclear fire and dump enough oxygen into the atmosphere from hydrolyzing Enceladus, you might just be able to set off a global firestorm in the lower troposphere. Obviously, this would be a Bad Idea (TM).
