    When we post on Facebook on someone's wall or comment on their posts, we do so in a public forum (not totally unlike Reddit or other forums and aggregates).

    I've been thinking about it based on all your great responses, and there's a divide between friends and strangers. We all want to see friends' posts, and friends' comments on other friends' posts. We don't want to see strangers' posts that have no connection to us, and we don't want strangers to see posts of ours that have no connection to them.

    The issue is that "friend F comments on stranger G's post" crosses that friend/stranger boundary in a way that it's not obvious how to proceed. Some people prefer to see them and have them be seen, and some, like myself, don't. I understand now why facebook made the decision they did, I just wish there was an alternative or option about how to deal with those cases.
