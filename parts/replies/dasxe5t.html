    Gains take work.

    Going to the deep cuts of specific macros, science based lifting programs, etc, will probably get you better gains, but it's a very small difference. If you are just starting out, go to the gym, and make sure you walk out feeling sore (not from hurting youself, but from working hard)

    Increase the weight you work with every time you go in, not a lot but some. Eventually you will hit a weight that you fail at. Go back a few days worth and start upward again. Depending on your diet and body, you may need longer or shorter breaks in between workouts.

    If you don't get stronger or bigger, you are a) not working hard enough. b) not taking care of yourself of c) seriously ill.

    I've never actually seen c in person. It's almost always a.

    Hit it hard, keep hitting it, and never, never ever skip leg day.
