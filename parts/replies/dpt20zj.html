    • udenizc

      The diarrhea has nothing to do with gas or bloating. The type of diarrhea seen in lactose intolerance is called osmotic diarrhea and it's one of the most common types of malabsorption syndromes. Basically, lactose can't be absorbed by your gut unless it's digested into more basic sugars first. So if it's not digested by lactase, it just sits there in the lumen of the intestine. As with other water soluble molecules that are osmotically active (cause a shift of water to the compartment of the body they are in), lactose "pulls" or "retains" water inside the intestine. The result is diarrhea.
