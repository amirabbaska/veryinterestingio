  • mortalwombat-

    This is the answer the OP is probably looking for. To simplify it even further, draw a simple picture of a rocket, with the bottom being open (seriously, so it. It will make it so easy to understand). Inside the rocket draw arrows, one that points up, one down, one left, and one right. Those arrows represent force built up in the engine by the burning fuel. The arrows that point left and right are equal but opposite so they cancel each other out. The rocket does not move left or right. The arrow pointing up is not canceled by the arrow pointing down, because the downward force is able to escape. Since you have more upward force than you have downward force, the rocket moves up!
