• Semiresistor

    Yes, it is incorrect to say we see in 3D. We see two 2D images which produces a perception of depth. Seeing in 1D means you see in a continuum along one dimension. You can see its left and right side simultaneously. Seeing in 2D means we see in a continuum along two dimensions, as we do. You can see the left and right, top and bottom simultaneously. Seeing in 3D would mean seeing in a continuum along three dimensions. Seeing in 3D would mean you can see the front and back of an object simultaneously (along with left/right and top/bottom). Depth perception does not allow us to do this. It merley attributes a depth to 2D images projected onto a plane.
