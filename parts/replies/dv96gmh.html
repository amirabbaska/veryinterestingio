    • logggur

      Yes, ambient conditions affect the rate of degradation. As a general rule, heat, sunlight/UV, and time will all increase how quickly/how much will leach out of a plastic. So, for instance, a bottle holding water outside on a hot day will likely contain more degradation products than a similar bottle kept in the dark in a refrigerator. As far as washing plastic in the dishwasher, I’d imagine that’s fine since the bottle is rinsed as part of the process. In fact that’s usually my strategy for reusing plastic bottles: a quick rinse before use and try to avoid prolonged storage (like, days. Bringing a bottle to work with you is fine) of liquids. Sidenote: definitely never microwave disposable plastics, microwaves can ramp up degradation extremely quickly.

      Anyway, if you use some discretion in how you treat your plastic containers I’d imagine most of the risk is mitigated. I usually tell people that, statistically, time spent worrying about trace contaminants in food/drinks is probably better spent making sure your car’s brakes are in good working order.

      The disappointing answer is, we're not sure.

      You are definitely washing away BPA as the plastic goes through the dishwasher, yes. So overall the washed surfaces will have a lower BPA concentration to offer the water you pour into it.
