      Depends on the mass of the star. Some stars (0.8 - 2.0 solar masses) undergo a "helium flash" in which a lot of the helium is fused in just a few minutes, comparable to all of the energy released in the entire Milky Way galaxy. However, various mechanisms keep that energy from leaving the star, so they are hard to detect.
