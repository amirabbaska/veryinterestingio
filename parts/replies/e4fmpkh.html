    This is actually mostly wrong: very large capacitive inputs (i.e. the whole palm of your hand) are filtered out, but metals and things that are smaller will work if the size is not too large. The reason is that any capacitive object cannot be filtered out (the human body has a wide range of resistance and capacitance, depending on electrolytes on the skin surface). So if they filtered out certain capacitance values your touchpad might not work when you have been sweating after a workout.

    Metallic objects will work on your phone, but you have to expose them to the right amount of surface area for the right amount of team. Give it a try!
