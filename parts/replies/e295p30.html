      In typical cables signals travel at around 70% of the speed of light: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Velocity_factor#Typical_velocity_factors

      Typical digital sound exists at 44.1 kilohertz, or one amplitude sample every 22.7 microseconds, so to get a one-sample offset one path would have to travel a distance of around 4700 kilometers 4700 meters longer than the other path. You can imagine that the resistance along that path would inevitably be rather large compared to the shorter path.
