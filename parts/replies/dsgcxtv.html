    The reason it works better on water than on some other molecules

    It works better on fats. A really asymmetrical molecule. But your post is spot on. Ovens actually have frequency "jitter" of tens of megahertz. Not enough to move the standing waves much though. The stirrers even contribute to this. I have displayed the ovens on a spectrum analyzer.

    BTW, many commercial microwave ovens work at 915 MHz, another ISM band.

    https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microwave_oven#Principles

    Consumer ovens usually use 2.45 gigahertz (GHz)—a wavelength of 12.2 centimetres (4.80 in)—while large industrial/commercial ovens often use 915 megahertz (MHz)—32.8 centimetres (12.9 in).[22] Water, fat, and other substances in the food absorb energy from the microwaves in a process called dielectric heating.
