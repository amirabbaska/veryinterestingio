        Bats have calls used for echolocation, and more general calls for communication, etc. The former is very hard to hear with human ears; the latter is usually audible and often annoying. You might have heard a form of the latter.

        Also, like everything in the world of sounds, the call used for echolocation has a spectrum of wavelengths/frequencies. The lower end of this call is at 15-20 kHz, which humans can technically hear if the intensity is high enough. Practically, however, the peak intensity for bats is usually above 25 kHz, so unless you were in a very quiet environment (which the wild isn't), it will be almost impossible to hear it. Adults tend to lose hearing in the high frequencies to make things worse.

        Whispering bats do have echolocation calls in the audible range. However, they live in the tropics. Unless you were specifically in such regions, chances are that you were hearing something else and mistaking it for a bat call.
