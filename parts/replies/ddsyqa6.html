    You are mixing up different things here. No pun intended. Coke and other Softdrinks come in concentrate everywhere in the world when served at fastfood restaurants. They are mixed in so called "Multiplex" machines. This is done because the syrup or "base" yields much more mixed product so they cut down on mass to transport and to store. Also using local water and adding carbonation is cheaper, again because of transport and because it costs less to buy just the concentrate. It is still a "drink" because the concentrate obviously has more than one ingredient.

    Concentrated orange juice still is 100% juice because ther water was simply taken out, so mixing it back in still counts as juice. If anything else was added, sugar, carbonation, anything then it HAS to be called "drink" otherwise it still can be, and is considered 100% juice.
