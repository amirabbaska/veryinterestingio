    • restricteddata

      It was a one-point safety test, in which they took a nuke that ought to have been "safe" if you set off its high explosives at only one point, as opposed to the simultaneous detonations needed for a true nuclear explosion. Apparently the weapon was not one point safe — 55 tons, while quite a bit smaller than the "normal" yield, is still pretty huge (the Oklahoma City bombing was like 2.3 tons of TNT equivalent). The full-yield of the device, had it detonated "properly," would have been more like 10,000 tons of TNT (10 kt), so it is still better than that, but 55 tons is pretty extreme for a safety test — that bomb was clearly not one-point safe.

      Pascal-B, for what it is worth, was way worse. Same sort of setup, same sort of goal, same expected 2-3 lbs... but it was 300 tons of TNT equivalent. So not 50,000 times greater... 300,000 times greater!
