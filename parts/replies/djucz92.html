• VinnySauce

    Sound pressure level can be measured (often given in dB) and does a decent job of quantifying loudness of sounds. For humans, an A-weighting frequency filter is used, to amplify the perceived sound amplitude in dB according to our biological bias with frequency response, because the human ear is more sensitive to some frequencies of sound than others. This adjusted amplitude scale is called dB(A).

    https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A-weighting

    • salmjak

      In addition, the perception of loudness is modulated. Humans have built in defence mechanisms (m. Stapedius and tensor tympani) which will reduce the transmitted amplitude of loud noises. If an animal lacks these they would experience certain loud noises differently (like some humans with paresis of the facial nerve).
