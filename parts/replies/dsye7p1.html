    • orangegluon

      They do the same kinds of things that virtual photons do. The reason they only exist as virtual particles to us is because particles at these energy scales just don't have enough energy to produce real W or Z bosons. But a Z boson acts basically just like a photon except it only talks to left handed particles (particles with a certain chirality); hence it's like a very short-ranged electromagnetism if you'd like to think of it that way. Virtual W bosons can change particle flavors in interactions, such as in beta decay for example, where a down quark emits a virtual W boson and becomes an up quark, while the W boson decays into a real neutrino and real electron.

      Gluons can exist as real particles in principle since they are massless, but in practice they'll just hadronize and form jets instead for the most part.
