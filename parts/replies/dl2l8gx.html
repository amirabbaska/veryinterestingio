    • insertSpork

      Given sufficient time, skill, and motivation absolutely any man-made system can be compromised (and, generally, as we expand our collective knowledge we tend to have some luck with non-man-made systems as well). Effective security isn't actually perfectly secure, it simply drives the cost of compromising a given system into the realm of impracticality via those three factors. Encryption, for example, can always be defeated by brute force but, when implemented properly, should take so long to do so that it's nigh impossible for modern computers to do so in a remotely reasonable time period. Security practices and standards are continually updated and improved to try to drive the skill barrier out of reach of the VAST majority of would-be adversaries.

      As for everyone... humans have been pretty good at manipulating each other since WELL before computers were ever a thing.
