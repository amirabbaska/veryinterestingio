    Note that "perfect RNG brain" does not mean anything and I'd encourage you to define what you mean with it since you might discover a misunderstanding, which is what I suspect. Having a 50% chance to select the correct answer has nothing to do with our brains, it's an intrinsic property of a fair coin toss. Without human brains in the universe the coin (alien coin in this hypothesis) still has a 50% chance for each outcome, and any independent system that "predicts it" will have a 50% chance of being correct.

    For example instead of brains I can use coin tosses to predict the outcome of coin tosses. I'll choose my prediction of a future coin toss with the result of a coin toss, if it's heads/tails I'll predict heads/tails. Doing so makes me correct 50% of the time after infinite tosses.

    But I can also use another strategy. I can write "heads" in a sheet of paper. Then choose my preciction with whatever there's in the paper: always "heads". This strategy also has 50% accuracy. Or I can write "heads, heads, heads, tails", and choose each one sequentially and repeating. This also has 50% accuracy..
