    • some_kinda_guy

      Epinephrine is used heavily during cardiac arrest! paramedics in the field, and nurses/doctors in the hospital administer loads and loads of epinephrine during CPR. It's in our ACLS (advanced cardiovascular life support) algorithms, which are basically flowsheets to follow when someone goes into cardiac arrest.

      Epinephrine helps to increase blood pressure by constricting blood vessels, and can increase heart rate (a patient in cardiac arrest can still have electrical heart activity, it's just not in an organized rhythm which means blood isn't flowing anywhere)

      Source: am EMT, just finished ACLS training as part of a paramedic program
