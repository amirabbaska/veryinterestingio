      Molecular nitrogen has an enthalpy of formation of zero, so any heat generated by breaking the bonds of the reactants leaves as heat unless byproducts are produced as well. The problem is that many of the bonds in nitrogen containing compounds are fairly highly energy intensive to form in the first place so it liberates a lot of energy when they break
