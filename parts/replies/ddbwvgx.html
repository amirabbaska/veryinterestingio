    Glycogen should not be described as 'a bunch of glucose molecules loosely stuck together.' They are covalently bound together in neat smoothly branched structures. So they're very ordered, and also strongly bound. It requires an enzyme to build and remove glucose molecules from glycogen, and some serious diseases lead to abnormalities of those enzymes. Including von Gierke's disease, Pompe's disease, Forbes-Cori disease, Hers' disease, and more.
