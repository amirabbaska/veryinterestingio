      That's pretty much it; your references validate your existence. If I have a relationship with people at the company you're providing as a reference then I can usually find someone I know there and get a real reference. Beyond that it's a crap shoot.

      I've offered myself up as a reference loads of times and have only been called back a hand full of times. I'm called back perhaps 5-10% of the time even if my previous employee has used me as their primary reference.

      Take this with a grain of salt however. I live in Vancouver, BC and we're fairly progressive. In more traditional places I'm sure references are more important.

      Edit: In 15 years I've had 1 reference check that's resulted in a hire. The rest of the time it's had absolutely no positive or negative affects. "Did they work with you? Are they good? Why did they leave? Anything else?" Then they tick yes on a box marked "are they a real person".
