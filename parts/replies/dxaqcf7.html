    The hyperboloid shape is able to be constructed using only straight lines (i.e. the curve doesn't need to be built into the raw materials).

    That's not how they're built, though. When you say a hyperboloid "can be constructed using straight lines", I assume you mean diagonal lines like this:

    https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ruled_surface#/media/File:Ruled_hyperboloid.jpg

    But hyperboloid towers are not built from diagonal members. They're built a layer at a time by assembling a ring of concrete forms, pouring concrete, then moving the forms upward to pour the next layer. The forms have to be shaped to conform to the circular footprint and the sloping sides of the tower, and the same technology could be used to build a paraboloid, a sphere, or any other shape. There's nothing special about hyperboloids from an ease-of-construction perspective.
