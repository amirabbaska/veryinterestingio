    I disagree that the "post" refers to the majority in Parliament. I mean, it could, but the more relevant use of "post" is at the riding level. Most talk of electoral reform is focused on fixing that post. You could have a proportional system and you're still going to have to have a "post" in Parliament.

    The main issue is that in each riding, anyone who didn't vote for the winning candidate effectively has their vote thrown out, because it isn't used for anything other than determining who wins that single constituency.

    To reduce it simply and absurdly: there could be an election between two parties (for simplicity) where party A wins 326 constituencies with 50%+1 of the vote, and lose every other seat 99-1, and win a majority.
