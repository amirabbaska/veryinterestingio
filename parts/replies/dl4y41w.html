    • monicadiazzz

      No, blood is filtered daily through the kidneys, so you could say that you're blood is in constant renewed, for example the kidneys filter out waste of metabolism, regulate electrolytes in our body and even regulate the amount of water in our system, this is done constantly.

      The cells on the other hand are a different subject, red blood cells have an average lifespan of 120 days, while for example white blood cells have a shorter lifespan, so you could say that blood is "cleaned" constantly but the cells circulating through the bloodstream are the most long lived elements that conform blood itself.

    Our kidneys are also important for telling our bodies when more red blood cells are needed! As they age they are destroyed as you said, but your body doesn't exactly notice until kidneys do. In response to lower levels of oxygen in the blood (from either too few cells or other reasons) the kidneys release a hormone called erythropoietin (EPO) which signals your bone marrow to begin production anew.
