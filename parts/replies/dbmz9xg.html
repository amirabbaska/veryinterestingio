      That's why I question what someone above said about "have they tried lavishing praise/rewarding the kid when he DOESN'T act out?"

      When I was a kid I was the quiet, obedient kid (besides the one time I got ISS) and I got PISSED at how the usually "bad" kids would get rewards and praise for simply not being little shits for a few hours. I'd show up every day and do what I was supposed to do while Johnny clowned around and didn't do his work. Then one day he does one assignment without being asked and the teachers act like he cured cancer or something-- he gets ice cream and a special visit from the principal for a pat on the back and he gets student of the week and gets all this stuff simply for doing what the rest of us do day in and day out. As a quiet, obedient kid it pissed me off.

      The other kids notice it. I was volunteering in a school a few years ago that gave out wrist bands when kids got a certain amount of compliments from teachers and met certain citizenship goals. It didn't take the "regular joe" kids long to realize the kids who act up regularly were earning wrist bands twice as fast as the normally "good" kids. Teachers would go out of their way to reward the "bad" kids for any and all normalish behavior while the regular kids' good behavior was simply expected and would go unrewarded. It undermined the whole system and left a lot of good kids jaded and unmotivated.
