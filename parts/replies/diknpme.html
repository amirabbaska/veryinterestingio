    Also psychologist, good answer!

    I would add in that there are lots of other psychological processes common to both problems. (I did work on transdiagnostic variables in grad school). Rumination is one, as is intolerance of uncertainty (it is what it sounds like), as well as experiential avoidance (you mentioned avoidance coping style). Anxiety sensitivity mostly predicts anxiety but may have independent links to depression also.

    Additionally, we sometimes see temporal-causal links between the two. The most common direction is anxiety leading to depression, which we often conceptualize like this:

    1. Get anxious
    2. Do what anxious people do, which is avoid stuff in life
    3. End up avoiding enough of life to be cut off from important reinforcers, and/or cause psychosocial problems (lose job, relationships, school performance)
    4. Get depressed about #3
    • eng_Mirage

      What kind of things do you consider to be "important reinforcers"?

      • halfascientist

        Life itself! Physical activity, hobbies that give us pleasure, sunshine, food that doesn't make you feel gross, and most of all: social activity and connectedness.

        People often get depressed because something cuts them off from some part of the reinforcers that matter. Depression, in a cruel irony, involves maladaptive responses by which the sufferer cuts herself off from the rest.
