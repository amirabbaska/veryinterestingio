  • Saxon2060

    This is a good answer but doesn't quite tell the who story just in terms of the "cultural" angle.

    There is no mass gun culture to speak of in the UK, shooting sports are niche. As original commenter says, handguns are outright illegal and all other firearms heavily restricted. Firearms offences carry very heavy sentences.

    In the UK, civilians simply don't see firearms as part of every day life, we mostly associate them with the military. This is the case in other countries, too, even ones where gun ownership is less restricted. The restrictions themselves don't fully explain the lack of ownership. The 'appetite' to own a gun in the UK is tiny, even if it was easier.

    For these reasons, and the reasons original commenter lists, the police are vanishingly unlikely to face guns in most scenarios.

    Police in sensitive areas may be routinely armed (e.g. palace of Westminster. The recent knife attacker was shot by a routinely armed officer, not an armed response officer, I believe.)

    Police officers on foot do not routinely carry firearms but there are constantly armed and available officers in patrol cars.
