    not exactly. sometimes the body does make too many RBCs, which is a condition called polycythemia vera. the effects of this disease are mostly itchiness and arthritis, but sometimes it can cause blood clots (since the same marrow stem cells that make RBCs also make platelets, and so you've got too many of those). the treatment for polycythemia vera is simply therapeutic phlebotomy, which means you would go give blood once a month.

    your blood is a combination of plasma (mostly water & some proteins), RBCs, WBCs, and some platelets. the amount of plasma circulating in your system is more an indicator of how high your blood pressure is, rather than the amount of RBCs you have.
