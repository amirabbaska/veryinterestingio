    • half3clipse

      A paradox is not something that's inherently unexplainable.

      A paradox is a statement that despite being seemingly based on a true premise leads to a contradictory or otherwise unacceptable conclusion.

      The archers paradox is this: The arrow, when drawn is deflected to the left, and when released should be expected to continue in the direction it is pointed due to that deflection. Despite this the arrow travels forward from the bow and does not deflect to the side.

      The paradox arises because of the expectation that the arrow acts like a rigid body, and in fact for a bad arrow it will act as a rigid body and will deflect. You resolve the paradox by re doing the analysis for a non rigid body
