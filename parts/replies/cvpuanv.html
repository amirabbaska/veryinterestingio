    matter wave

    Just to be clear, we're talking about the propagation of mechanical disturbances here. Matter wave is sometimes used in the context of quantum physics (de Broglie waves) to mean something entirely different.

    released heat

    What would releasing heat in a crystal lattice look like? The nuclei in a lattice don't have the same translational degrees of freedom of a gas - you can't look at their average speeds to get the temperature. Most of the thermal energy in a crystal is held in vibrational degrees of freedom. So the shaking/waving back and forth of atoms in a lattice and the various modes of such vibration is the thermal energy in the lattice. Orderly, directional vibrations and wave packets will eventually scatter and devolve into random vibrations. The analogy in air is that a loud sound traveling through air will eventually dissipate and simply contribute to the thermal energy of air.

    no effect at the opposite end

    You can't usually add some energy into a system and expect that to not eventually propagate everywhere (unless you're doing some really funny business). The pole will eventually need to heat up or dump that energy somehow.
