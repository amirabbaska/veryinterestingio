    >The argument that people will choose easy-to-remember passwords falls over once you start looking at dumps of PINs, where the most common ones are '0000', '1111', '1234', etc.

    What would you expect the most common PINs to be, 7254? 2885?

    This applies to the "most common passwords" lists too... Unless everyone is literally perfect about choosing a random value, there will be some asymmetry to the distribution of PINs towards what we recognize as "easy" or "memorable" values. Even if 999 out of 1000 users pick a perfectly random PIN and the other 1 out of 1000 pick one of those "easy" values, when you do the analysis, you're going to find "hey, the most common PINs are really insecure!"
