    Concorde had afterburners. It was used for takeoff and for accelerating to the Mach 1.7 cruise speed, at which point the engines supercruised (supersonic flight without afterburner).

  • Big_Bernie_Chu

    Fuel is not the only cost added by afterburners. Using the afterburner causes accelerated coating wear/loss in the combustor and other areas. This of course means more maintenance and part replacements. No way commercial airlines want to deal with these extra costs and engine downtime.
