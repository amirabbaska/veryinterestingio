    Note: Gamecube/Wii (the internals are close enough that I'm not going to differentiate,) is difficult for its own reasons. It really just depends on how precisely a game relies on hardware, timings, etc. as to how hard it is to emulate. zFreeze, MMU, Cache Reliance, External Framebuffer usage, the starlet coprocessor on the Wii, and much more have been or still are stumbling blocks for Dolphin. There's still a lot of unsolved issues; so I wouldn't call the GameCube or Wii Normal.

    Dolphin blog (full disclosure, I'm a writer there!) has a lot of documentation on the various challenges that particular games have presented with how they use the hardware. The same goes for PS1, PS2, N64, SNES, and everything else. While we're ultimately trying to emulate the hardware, some behaviors that games do break our assumptions or require features that we simply cannot emulate (or emulate realistically to the satisfaction of performance needs.)

    The reason why Ratchet and Clank were unplayable due to performance issues have nothing to do with the emotion engine. The problem with lack of support for GPU mipmapping in the hardware GPU backends.

    That has been solved recently so the games are more or less playable.

    • LunarDelta

      Ah, that's good to know. I never really knew quite why the issues existed, but it's good to know they're better now.

      • JMC4789

        Yeah; the emotion engine and, hell, every CPU core on the PS2 is problematic due to various reasons. The most common reason is that the PS2 CPU cores will round differently than our processors; and emulating that is a pain. Many of the rounding behaviors are undocumented and are even nonsensical, and if they don't match our processor's rounding, then all of the sudden you have to do trickery which slows down your emulation to do it right.
