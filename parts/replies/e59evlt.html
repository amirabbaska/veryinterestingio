    It's probably worthwhile adding the reasons that some Europeans are light-skinned. This relates to there being strong selection for the genes for light skin due to the lack of pigmentation allowing for more efficient vitamin D synthesis.

    People living in hunter-gatherer cultures in northern latitudes (e.g. the innuit or native Siberians) get plenty of vitamin D in their diet. There is no selection pressure to evolve lighter skin. People living in agricultural cultures in northern latitudes often don’t get enough vitamin D in their diet. Humans can use UV light to synthesize vitamin D in their skin to make up for this, so natural selection has favoured light skinned genes for these cultures.
