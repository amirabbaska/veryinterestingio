  • ShelfordPrefect

    I'm 99% sure "to insure promptness" is a false etymology for tip. Aside from it having the characteristic flavour of a folk etymology, and words formed from acronyms being essentially nonexistent before the 20th century,

    • "insure" means "pay money to protect against the risk of loss"

    • "ensure" means "make certain something happens"

    so according to that explanation it should really be "tep".

    Snopes also deem it false, pointing out the word "tip" meaning "small monetary gift in exchange for service" has existed for centuries. While it might be entirely true to say "the New York times gave it as the origin".... they were wrong if they did so :)
