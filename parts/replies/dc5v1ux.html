    I'm replying to your comment because it stands out as "on point" and personally relevant. Anyway, amongst those starting a career in academia, i.e. grad students mental illness is endemic. This plog post cites and summarizes the results of 2 studies: https://www.psychologytoday.com/blog/in-love-and-war/201511/graduate-school-and-mental-illness-is-there-link. In the first study, 47% of doctoral students at UC Berkely were deemed to fit the clinical criterion for depression. Seems significant to me! Question is, were they like that during high school? hehe. There is so much more to look into here.

    Anyway, grad school attracts the achievement oriented "A" students. They've had a clear, mapped out plan for success and checked all the boxes on the route to accomplishment. Then in grad school they face failure for the first time: they have a publication rejected, provoking an existential crisis. Of course all those accolades from age 3 through age 21 may be to blame, but there are many other stresses of grade school: financial, social; the uncertainty of the future which generates anxieties of its own.
