    Good answer. You can (should) go one step further though and give the story context. I find it really helps for my development team to understand why we are doing what we are doing. Often when you understand the why, the solution that gets built really is a solution to the problem. Typically you do this in the story itself like this:

    As a... I want... So that...

    Sounds like a minor difference but on occasion it really helps.
