    • solarswordsman

      I think "concentrated" is a misleading way to describe it. You could just as easily say table sugar is "concentrated" sweet taste. It's not wrong, but the whole "concentrated" part conveys a connotation.

      The flavor of MSG (Umami) is tasted by specific receptors and is its own "pillar" of the five tastes. These are glutamate receptors, and there are many many foods which naturally contain a form of glutamate which triggers these receptors. MSG is just a convenient way to add that flavor to a given dish. It's delicious (subjective :P)!
