    • hihcadore

      I trained under some of the best grappling coaches in the SE United States and it's not even about injuries keeping these guys from being top level competitors. It's just a different set of skills.

      When you're competing, it's more reactionary than anything. You really don't... at least in combat sports... have the opportunity to really think about what's going on. It's said if you're thinking you're a step behind. Really hard to explain to someone whos not been there themselves but some people just have the ability to go into autopilot and be moved ahead of their opponent. These are the world class competitors.

      On the flip side there are people that understand the nuances of what they are seeing. They also have the interpersonal skills to be able to explain that to their students and push and drive them in a direction that will help them improve.

      The best competitors I've been around weren't the best coaches. And the best coaches had lack luster competition performance.
