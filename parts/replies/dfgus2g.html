    To add to that specific point: if you want to go into the trades in Germany, you apply for an apprenticeship (Lehre). This means that half of your two- or three-year apprenticeship is spent in the company where you're employed and the other half at school (Berufsschule, vocation school).
    In school, you have mandatory subjects like German, history, sports, ethics/religion, civics etc and some pertaining to your chosen profession, i.e. a hairdresser might learn some chemistry as applies to hairdressing, and if you want to become a car mechanic, you'll have lessons on engine physics.

    Of course, most students (like students anywhere) are interested in some subjects and not in others. The German system is no guarantee for turning out butcher shop employees (Fleischfachverkäufer) who are super-aware of history and civics. But it's a start.
