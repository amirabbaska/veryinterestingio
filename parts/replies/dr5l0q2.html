    Hey, I'm studying neurobiology and want to piggyback off of this.

    Self-harming behaviours like chewing, picking, hair-pulling, etc. can be triggered by high cortisol levels (stress) or low serotonin (depression, anxiety).

    Basically, if you have too much stress or emotion to effectively deal with, your body turns to seeking some kind of physical sensation (even if it's pain) to calm itself down.

    This is true for a lot of non-human animals, too - like our other primate relatives.
