    • cerker

      You heard a mixture of the flyback transformer and the horizontal deflection coils.

      The picture is written line for line, using 15625 (50Hz PAL) or 15750 (60 Hz) lines per second. The electron beam is moved across the screen using powerful coils around the neck of the picture tube. Since the electrons get quite fast (actually near 1/3 of the speed of light) you need a strong magnetic field to do that. You used to hear those coils.

      The current using to drive this coils (actually, the energy which is stored into the coils after you moved from left to right which you have to remove to get back to start, that's why it's called "flyback") is also used to drive a transformer which creates the high voltage for the picture tube. You heard that transformer, too.
