    • CalHollow

      I'll preface this by saying I'm only a medical student, but I believe that there are a number of factors (not all completely and well understood -- there is actually an ENORMOUS amount of info that we don't yet understand about brain functions) that could contribute to this feeling.

      For example, stress (including your body being tired) induces the release of cortisol to help fuel your body during stressful times. So if you're tired, but not ready for bed, cortisol would tell and help your body to stay awake. Additionally, stress could cause unconscious facial muscle contraction resulting in decreased ability to relax and close the eye. It may also be possible that if you have a headache, the stress from the headache could be keeping you more awake or your body may be able to deal with the headache better if the eyes are open and fixed on something rather than having no visual input when your eyes are closed.

      Again these are all just possibilities, but it's also possible that each of these may contribute a little bit resulting in the overall promotion of keeping your eyes open even when you're tired. I hope this helps a little bit.
