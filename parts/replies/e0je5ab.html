• TonboToh

    Styrene plastic actually contains a lot of energy in weak bonds- it is great fuel to burn in high temp incinerators, one reason why Sweden buys our trash. When it is blown into styrofoam (not at all the only use for styrene) it is not economical to ship it to incinerators.

    Luckily those high energy bonds break down easily in sunlight or exposure to ionic salts or ph fluctuations. So styrofoam breaks down in a few months in sunlight or ocean or landfill. Did you ever pick up a discarded styrofoam cup by the road and it looks oddly browned? Disintegrating.
