    ELI5: Coming from a computer science background, I suspect the brain is probably responsible for creating most of the actual imagery and patterns like what you were seeing. When we work with neural networks - systems we designed off of biologist's descriptions of how the brain functions - we can see similar sorts of effects if we feed random noise into image recognition networks. I suspect the pressure on those cells causes random signals, which the brain is then making patterns out of in an attempt to understand the signal, which the brain then "sees" but because it can't piece them into a "real" image it ends up confused and focused on the fragments of structure.

    In depth: So while the pressure stimulates the nerves (not my area of expertise) it's probably in a mostly random way, at which point the brain's image processing wetware (e.g. the part that probably functions similarly to our computer based image recognizing neural networks) attempts to reinforce structure (this image shows how this would normally function in a computer; note that the structuring is similar to many of the anecdotally reported grids) it is receiving so our higher functions can more easily discern what we are seeing. But because it's random, our higher functions become confused and just see the hints and pieces of random shapes and patterns the image processing section was trying to reinforce. This is probably why the patterns are unique-but-similar (e.g. some people see buildings, but most people see faces or geometric shapes) to each person: each person's brain was wired slightly differently as we learned how to recognize images, but we generally learned the same basic structures (geometric shapes, faces).

    Disclaimer: This is all speculation from a pure computer scientist, I have no clue how the brain or biology functions. I can only describe things from my understanding of the computer science systems that have been translated from biology by computational biologists. I am also assuming our discoveries on these subjects would hold true for the original biological systems we cribbed them from (probable, but not certain).
