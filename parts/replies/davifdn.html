      They aren't interacting much as individual systems.

      The distances involved are too large for most stars

      However, each one feels the combined effect of the rest.

      After some calculations, the radial acceleration of the Sun towards the center of the Milky Way:

      2.16 x 10^-10 m/s^2

      While the acceleration felt by the Sun from the Alpha Centauri system is:

      7.76 x 10^-14 m/s^2

      This acceleration due to the closest neighboring star system is 2780 times smaller than the combined effects of the Milky Way.

      In essence most stars' motion around the Milky Way will be under the influence of mainly the galaxy as a hole whole with a small adjustment for limited local interactions.

    • thefaptain

      Spiral galaxies have arms due to density waves in the plane of the galaxy. These density waves are due to coherent perturbation in the orbits of individual stars. Due to the coherence of the perturbation, they are assumed to be due to some object outside the galaxy, typically assumed to be a dwarf galaxy. Interactions between stars are not considered in the most basic form theory. Interactions between stars may be important as second order effects in areas of extreme density, meaning the galactic core, but to first order density wave theory does not depend on interactions between stars.
