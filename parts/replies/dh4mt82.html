    • rankor572

      This is a good question to have for understanding qualia. Qualia is an argument about external inconsistency; internal consistency is assumed. You see all red things as red; you might argue about which things are red as opposed to orange or yellow or even a dark purple or maroon. But if something is red, it's red. Ignoring those edge cases, if I point out something to you and say "that thing is red" and you say "yes it is" that doesn't answer the question of qualia. Even if we point out 10,000 things and we agree on which of those things are red and which are not, that still doesn't answer the question. The question of qualia doesn't actually matter (even less than usual) when we fight over whether a particular object is red or orange, yellow or green, blue and black or white and gold.

      And there are many potential reasons red doesn't change for you, even assuming qualia is a true problem. Arguably your memory is a perception (a perception of a past perception) or at least a reflection that you perceive, and thus the thing is red every time you look at it in your memory and now, but your concept of red is constantly changing despite containing the same exact list of objects.
