  • slightlyoffkilter_7

    Interestingly, the piano's original popularity was fairly limited to continental Europe and England, plus their subsequent colonies. In Celtic cultures, the harp has evolved to be the instrument of choice for playing polyharmonic sounds. Traditional Irish lever harps are small enough to easily transport but can play a wide range of sounds similar to a piano and can easily change keys mid-song. Additionally, one can easily sing along while playing the harp and it can be a solo or backing instrument, depending on the goal of the musician.
