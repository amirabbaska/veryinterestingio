      Do people that do hospice ever get over the feelings of uselessness?

      I can only speak for myself. I do it because some of these people have no one left. They're just so old, and they never married, or their spouse is dead, and their kids, if they have any, don't live nearby. There was one woman I used to go see who had had a sister in the same facility. The sister died, and the only family left lived a few hundred miles away. I used to let the remaining sister use my cell phone so she could call them.

      But anyway, I figure someone should go visit people like this, someone who isn't there as a medical person or a social worker or whatever. Just, here I am, you can talk to me about whatever, and if you don't want to talk to me, you can talk to the fuzzy dude.

      other than that I see life as so fragile.

I dunno, it sort of gives me hope that regardless of what goes on in the world, some basic humanity is preserved.
