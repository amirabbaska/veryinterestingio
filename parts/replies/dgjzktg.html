    • McFly6661

      As OP said, muscle contraction is dictated by a balance of sodium and calcium in the muscle cells. To contract, calcium is let into the cell by a calcium transport channel. To relax, calcium is then pumped out of the cell by separate type of active (consumes energy) transport channel. When a muscle knots, something is stopping the Ca from clearing out. When the knot is released, pressure forces a relaxation reflex which allows the old blood (with the elevated Ca levels) to be forced out and new blood to enter, restoring resting levels of Ca in the muscle fibers.

      TLDR: It's carried out by the bloodstream. Other muscles won't then knot as they have to allow the Ca to enter for initial contraction

      Source: Exercise science degree
