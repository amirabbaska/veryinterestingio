      Radar refracting off the atmosphere is an aspect that helps radar detectors of police speed guns more effective.

      You want your car radar detector to detect the police gun as soon as possible and before the office has a clear line of sight. When the radar pulse refracts around the corner, your radar detector can notice there's a speed trap before you take the turn or go over the hill.

      Police know this and use their gun conservatively. When you see an police use the gun, notice how they'll look for a car they suspect is already speeding before they target them and activate the gun.

      Each time the gun is used, little bits of the radar gets spread throughout the area, allowing cars approaching to detect the speed trap.

      Even though most of the radar is focused on a target car, it still refract before it reaches the car. That's enough to light up detectors around corners and over hills.

      Also, radar that bounces off the target car is reflected and refracted throughout the area, giving detectors more signal to detect.

      You can see this effect with a regular red laser. Shine one in a dark room and you'll see the entire room get a bit brighter. That's because the laser is refracting off the air in the room (and also reflecting off the wall) enough to brighten up the room.
