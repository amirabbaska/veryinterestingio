    This wasn't the best answer to upvote to the top. It implies that fake news is just another word for clickbait or yellow journalism without mentioning the biggest controversy: the overt fabrications and deceptive use of legitimate-sounding domain names. An example of fake news is when a website called seattletribune.com is claiming that Clinton's chief of staff has been assassinated. Of course there is no Seattle Tribune, but it looks legitimate in a social media feed.

    This whole secondary conversation about opinion and clickbait mostly comes from obfuscation from people who feel attacked by the implication that fake news helped one candidate more than another. Really, no matter which side you're on you should be opposed to websites that exist solely to spread objectively false information (not just opinions you might disagree with).
