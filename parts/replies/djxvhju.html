    Also, just to add something I find interesting... I always thought the winter sky (from NA) had brighter stars because of some "crisp winter air" phenomenon. Nope. It's because in summer the earth is between the sun and the center of the Milky Way so the night sky is basking in the light of all those stars, causing the ones we can pick out to seem more faded and making some invisible. In the winter the sun is between us and the center, so the night sky is pointed out towards the void where there is less light "pollution" from our galaxy. So the stars in the night of winter seems more crisp.
