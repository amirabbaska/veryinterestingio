      Probably it is the whole part. There's a Creationist argument that feathers couldn't evolve, because they're useless until flight is achieved.

      That argument ignores the fact that, until about 2000 A.D., the very finest insulation material available on planet Earth was baby bird down feathers.

      Feathers almost certainly evolved to conserve heat, not to enable flight.

  • aRoseBy

    Note that while the feather is growing, it has a blood supply. If your pet bird breaks a feather which is still vascular, it's time to get out the needle-nosed pliers and yank the broken feather so the bird doesn't lose too much blood.

    After the blood supply dries up, feathers are easier to detach. Which is why a cat trying to catch a bird often ends up with a paw full of feathers.
