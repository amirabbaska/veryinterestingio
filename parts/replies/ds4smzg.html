    The word "rhotacism" is weird. In this case it means "not able to pronounce the letter R (rho)".

    However, in most common usage it describes the accents of the same language where some speakers pronounce the R (rhotic), and those who do not (non rhotic). So for example, American English is typically rhotic when the R comes at the end of syllables and words, and British English is non rhotic in the same cases. For example, in the words "car", "ladder", "part", the R is usually heard in American English (rhotic), while in British English it's more similar to "cah", "ladda", and "paht" (non rhotic).

    So, it's difficult to compare languages based on their level of rhotacism. Languages like French or German don't have very strong Rs, while Spanish is very rhotic.

      The accent feature is rhoticism, whereas rhotacism is a speech impediment where the speaker is unable to pronounce r's. Very similar spelling but different things.
