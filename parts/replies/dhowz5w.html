    • zjt2846

      Depends what you mean by connected. Other than a small nerve and reflex, they don't really share much in the way of direct connection. The canal is distinctly divided from middle ear and rest of throat by the ear drum.

      But if you're just looking to connect the word ear with nose and throat, that's much easier as middle ear as a connection to the throat from the Eustachian tube. The nose also more obviously connects to the throat through the nostrils to the nasopharynx.

      But lastly, the territory that some specialties cover is often times not only due to anatomy, but also to common diseases that tend to effect all those systems (nephrologist treating with what would typically be thought of as heart meds), as well as somewhat arbitrary tradition from older times (there is often some turf wars between neurosurgery and orthopedics as well as between urology and gynecology).
