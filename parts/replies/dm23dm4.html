    It would be much to heavy and expensive to work on large scale wind turbines. There is also the fact that the second blade is only ~70% (rather optimistically) as efficient as the first blade in such an arrangement creating a lot of cost for not a lot of gains.

    Small scale ducted turbines though, such as the ones that power water pumps on farms and private homes, sometimes do use more exotic blade layouts in order to minimize their footprint and get the most power out of a single unit.

  • TheEngineer09

    I suspect it's because the air flow doesn't work the same way. With airplane props you're trying to drive as much air as possible, with less concern for the fact that you're making turbulent flow. Wind turbines are trying to extract energy from the wind. To do this you want to create very minimal turbulence to maximize extraction. Counter rotating blades aren't good for this.
