      • scotscott

        the way film works is that a photon strikes a molecule of photosensitive material, and that molecule breaks down. When you develop the film, the bits that didn't break down get washed away, leaving you with a negative. Since there are usually many photons, a lot of these molecules will get washed away. By adding more of them, you can get a more sensitive film. The problem is that one photon can only do one molecule. So adding sensitivity doesn't actually help here, as you're still limited by your ability to pick out one molecule that was broken apart out of many trillions.
