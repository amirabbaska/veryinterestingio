    This.

    There's basically three categories:

    -walking at a constant, sustainable pace
    -running at a constant, sustainable pace
    -sprinting at an unsustainable pace (close to max effort)

    Running requires a little bit more work than walking over a given distance, but the total calories burned is going to be similar, because both utilize mainly aerobic respiration.

    Sprinting the same total distance in discrete intervals is going to burn way more calories over a given distance than walking, because anaerobic respiration is much less efficient than aerobic respiration.
