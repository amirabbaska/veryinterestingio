  • Hopper_Sky

    I am not a medical professional, but I have the same exact problem as you and a medical professional told me that my melatonin process is reversed. Now, the medical professional who told me this never did any tests, and was a GP not a specialist, but she had also been my doctor for my entire life at that point (I was 19 and she was my pediatrician, it was right before I got an "adult" doctor). FWIW, it seems that there are people who are physiologically nocturnal - not "night owls," or people who just like being up at night, but people whose bodies send out sleep/wake hormones at the wrong signals. My body produces melatonin when the sun comes up, instead of when the sun goes down.

     

    As far as I know, there's no real fix. I take about 5 mg of over the counter melatonin every night before I go to bed (around 10 or 11), and it's made it so that I can at least be functional on a day schedule. My body still wants to sleep during the day, and I don't think I'll ever feel as rested or good as I do when I stick to a night schedule, but like I said, it does at least help. I've been taking melatonin every night for about four years now, and as long as I leave myself a solid 9 hours to sleep, I can get up in the morning and feel functional and okay throughout the day.
