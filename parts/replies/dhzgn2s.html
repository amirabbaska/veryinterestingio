      Good questions!

      Sheep, goat, and cow were all domesticated around the Fertile Crescent, so it's likely that we started drinking their milk in the same place and around roughly the same time, with sheep and goats first.

      Yak and camel (and reindeer, for that matter) live elsewhere, so logic would suggest they were separate, but it's possible that our consumption of their milk was catalyzed to some degree by the introduction of goats or sheep (which were already being milked) to their areas.
