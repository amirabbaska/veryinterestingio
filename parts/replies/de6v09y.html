  • SwiftTyphoon

    I'd like to add that the gas clouds that form solar systems generally tend to be very slowly rotating (far slower than pluto's orbit). As gas condenses and falls towards the center, conservation of angular momentum increases the speed of rotation of the disk. You can't really have another axis of rotation while the disk is still comparatively dense, particles that are moving in other directions will bump into particles in the disk. Eventually the disk condenses to form planets and the leftover gas (which is far less dense) is blown away by solar wind.

    I don't really like the marble analogy because by the time you have distinguishable objects instead of a gas cloud, (almost) everything's already rotating in the same direction.

    Source: took an astrophysics course
