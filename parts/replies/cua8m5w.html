• MacSev

    Increased demand in the summer actually doesn't affect prices much anymore - actual demand for gas is pretty constant year-round nowadays. The price goes up in the summer because refiners switch to a summer blend which is more expensive to make.

  • gaspah

    I think that petrol prices not used directly for commercial/farming purposes should be higher than what we pay at the pump. I think that far to much unnecessary driving is being done, there are so many alternate options for general purpose transportation. We're not really paying the full amount that our fuel consumption will cost us as a society when we have no choice but to reverse the impact it's having on our environment. Worse still is that this will have to be acomplished at a time when this very effective and mobile fuel source will have become more scarce and more costly to extract. This will make the job that much tougher for any construction required for any carbon capture solutions we may develop. Plus the more we release, the more we must remove.

    Unless this fuel is going in a tractor, a freighter or something else of vital importance to our daily lives we should be paying a few more dollars per gallon at the pump. You don't need to drive by yourself to work or down the road purely for the convenience. There are busses, trains, car pooling, even mopeds or pushbikes.

    I think if fuel cost more, then people would think twice before wasting it needlessly.
