    • blipsman

      They don't own their "fleet" so there is no cost to Uber w/ regard to vehicles.

      They have variable pricing that better matches realtime supply and demand -- if you've ever used taxis in big cities during a torrential thunderstorm or below zero temps, you could spend hours trying to get a taxi because drivers have no incentive to drive in bad conditions for same money as they do in good conditions, whereas Uber drivers making surge price payments do have incentive to hit the roads.

      Also, Uber isn't tied to a medallion system that limited the overall number of taxis on the roads... even as big cities populations and rides increased, places like Chicago and New York did little to increase supply of taxis.

      And Uber's rating system helps ensure drivers act in certain ways to ensure high ratings or they get kicked off service, While there isn't a good way to rate taxi drivers.

      Of course, many of these differences are things that COULD be built upon the existing taxi model, and may be at some point.
