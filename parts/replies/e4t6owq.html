  • Vuelhering

    With fewer breeding pairs you'll have more risk to fatal mutations but every mutation isn't fatal.

    I don't believe that's the issue. The lack of genetic diversity is alleles going to fixation, not mutations killing them off. Inbreeding can permanently breed in a "bad" trait in small inbred populations, which eventually takes over the entire population. This "bad" trait might not even be seen until it matters.

    The risk is that they are now all weakened as a species at being able to handle changes, due to lower diversity. A single thing like a new pathogen they didn't evolve to survive, or a changing climate can wipe them out. With more genetic diversity, the likelihood of some of them surviving would be higher, but if their genes are at fixation they have to depend on those random mutations to hopefully survive it.

    This happened at some point to the cheetahs, where only a few survived some sort of ancient catastrophe but they somehow repopulated. As a result there's very low diversity in them.
