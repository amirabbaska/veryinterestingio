    • trackerFF

      Yes, that has been one of the side effects. But interest rates have been at a historic low, which in turn has made lending money much more accessible, if you already have some money/collateral to start with. People have been able to take out very high loans, which has driven the price up much, much faster than salaries.

      They just recently implemented the 5 times annual salary limit, which effectively hammered down the last nail for young people, or poor people. Simply put: If you don't earn enough, you have to compensate by paying a larger down-payment. So instead of the 15% down-payment, some people have to save up 30% to 40% in down-payment, simply because the 5 x annual salary is much, much lower than the loan needed.

      Now, that rule was implemented to regulate the out-of-control prices, and hopefully prices will either calm down, or reverse a bit. That would sadly mean that people who overpaid are going to lose money, but it also means that regular people can get access to housing.
