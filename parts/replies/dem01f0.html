  • Astromike23

    Venus, unlike earth, does not have a strong magnetic field or a magnosphere. Without it, large quantities of hydrogen is lost to solar wind.

    This is incorrect reasoning.

    It turns out that Earth loses almost the exact same amount of hydrogen as Venus does. Our magnetic field does very little to prevent hydrogen loss, and in certain cases actually hastens it (polar outflow, charge exchange, and heated ionosphere).

    This is because hydrogen is such a light molecule that it can gain escape velocity from thermal energy alone via Jeans escape. There's no need for the extra energy from the solar wind for hydrogen escape; it can do it by itself just by virtue of being at the temperature of the upper atmosphere.
