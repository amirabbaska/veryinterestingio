• Leif_GW

    Solvated ions (read salt) are part of the solution. Reverse osmosis is forcing the ions to stay on one side while the water flows through to the other. If it helps to think about it that way, the solvated ions are bonded to the water. In reverse osmosis you have to break these bonds.

    Filtration is removing particles from the solution, but these particles are not solvated and therefore not part of the solution. Entirely mechanical process if you will because you're not breaking bonds.

    (Reverse) Osmosis is a distinctly different process and thus has it's own name.

      I am not an expert but in treatment plants that I have worked at, reverse osmosis processing doesn't flow the same way other filters do. There is a percentage of reject water that carries the contaminates away. The membranes do not "catch" anything like a filter does. Instead, you end up with some water that is purer than the original, and some that is dirtier than the original.
