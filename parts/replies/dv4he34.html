      I used 12 hour cycle as an example, it varies by latitude. Nothing grows at the pole though.

      But nowhere on earth do you have a growing season with 45 minute day/night cycles. This is important because plants do different chemistry in the light and in the dark. They need time to switch reactions, and switching costs energy.

      You may have seen flowers and leaves unfurl in the morning, close at night. Look more deeply and you'll microscopic pores called stomata open and close. If you have 45 minute cycles, plants are constantly switching between light and dark, they never get time to ramp up glucose production.

      Most plants will die with such short cycles. Some may survive, but they won't thrive. Since real estate for growing plants is so extremely expensive in space, why would you waste it on a suboptimal cycle? Specially when all it takes to fix it are some LED lights and a battery?
