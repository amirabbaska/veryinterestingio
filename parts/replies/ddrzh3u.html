      These are great! (Some potential spoilers ahead)

      The second one, using complex numbers, actually takes advantage of confusion about the domain of the square root. Even though people sometimes say i is the square root of -1, it's just a square root of -1. When taken as an operator, the square root does not have -1 in its domain so in truth "the square root of -1" doesn't really exist.

        in regards to the second one While that is for sure a problem, that can be resolved relatively easily by just picking root(-1)=i (we're extending the field so whether we pick +/- i they're both solutions to x^2 -1=0 so the extensions are isomorphic). The more subtle issue is the step before that one. The identity Sqrt(a/b)=Sqrt(a)/Sqrt(b) way back whenever you learned it was only proven to be true for a,b positive. The identity turns out to be false if either one is negative, even when those negative square roots are defined. Theres a nice discussion of it on the website: https://www.math.toronto.edu/mathnet/falseProofs/guess11.html
