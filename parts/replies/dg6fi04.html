      If we really want to stretch the analogy, place yourself in the shoes of the seller.

      Let's say you've paid $60 to put some items on your shelves. (and now have 'debt' because you've spent $60 to stock your store). A customer will take those debts from you if they purchase the items. In this case, they 'take the debt' from you by literally giving you $60.

      However, it's totally plausible that someone could 'take debts away' from you that you don't technically have (Resulting in positive gains). For example, if you steal a bunch of stuff, you have no monetary debts. When you sell the stolen goods, customers are 'taking away debts' from you by giving you $60. However you never had any debts! So you gain the $60.
