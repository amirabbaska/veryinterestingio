    +1 Many other answers seem to miss the point of the question. Just to reiterate:

    • P-value means the probability of getting your result or a more extreme result by random chance if the effect you're studying is not there (if the null hypothesis is true). So mathematically P(result|no effect).

    • That is not the probability that the effect is not there given your result, which would be P(no effect|result). This is what we would really want to know but p-values don't answer that question.

    So p-values give answers to questions you would not even want to ask if you knew a better way to ask what you really want to know.
