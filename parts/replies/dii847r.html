  • ImpartialPlague

    Jury nullification is a logical consequence of a legal system in which juries are triers of fact that also contains prohibitions on double jeopardy.

    If a jury believes that a defendant is guilty, but also believes that punishing them would be an injustice, they can return a not-guilty verdict despite having determined the defendant's guilt. The jurors can not be punished for doing so, and the defendant can not be tried again.

    If a judge asks you "Is there any reason you couldn't render a verdict according to the law?" and you are aware of jury nullification, it is unclear whether you can be truthful when saying "no."
