    Forces such as gravity directly affect the medium in which acoustic waves propagate, and a nonlinear acoustic wave (especially a shock) can modulate a medium to the extent that gravity becomes important in the dynamics (Example: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richtmyer–Meshkov_instability in gravity-stratified fluids, and Rayleigh-Taylor instability triggered by shocks). Then there is the astrophysical problem of propagation in self-gravitating media. So, it is possible to find cases where gravity directly affects acoustic waves, even if it is not so important for "sound waves" (if defining them as only audible acoustic waves at low amplitudes near Earth's surface).
