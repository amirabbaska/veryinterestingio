    Its proposed to be a thin halo 3x the diameter of the galaxy. When two galaxies collide it appears the halo has momentum and take longer to slow down because gravity drops off by the cube. Basically, if the sun froze in its orbit of the milky way today the kuiper belt would take longer to stop than mercury.

    Combine this with the fact very few stars collide in a galaxy merger means the halo could pass through the galaxy without interacting except via gravity and coalesce into a new halo.

    And, if you've taken physics, you may remember shell theory with fields. You can treat an object with gravity as if the particles are distributed evenly (cloud) or on the "shell" (halo) and the only thing that matters is mass and distance from center when outside of the halo.
