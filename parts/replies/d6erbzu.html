      Would it even be possible for a human to get close enough to a black hole to notice these effects?

      I'd guess it would take an extraordinary amount of delta V to park in a circular orbit about a black hole at 1.5 Schwarzchild radius. Plus the obstacle of traveling light years is likely insurmountable.

      For small to moderate sized black holes tidal forces would tear you apart. Spaghettification is the term sometimes used, if memory serves.

      For very large black holes the gravity gradient might be shallow enough to allow your body to stay intact. Even in this scenario, I believe the extremely energetic environment would create lots of heat, gamma rays, high energy ions, etc.. Not a good place for meat bodies.
