      The easiest way to understand why this happens is a simple explanation of what is called lorentz contraction. When you were moving at the speed of light or close to it space itself contracts around you, meaning that the distance that you perceive yourself traveling is actually shorter than the distance and observer sees you travel. This is why it might only take a week for you but a year for other people watching you. For you to travel a Year's worth of distance in a week might require that you travel faster than the speed of light, but from your frame of reference you didn't travel a light years distance at all you only travel a light week maybe because space itself contracted and the distance was shorter.

      If you take this to The Logical extreme, at the speed of light you actually experience zero distance. A photon emitted by the sun does not perceive itself taking eight minutes to get to Earth, it perceives it happening instantly, because the distance between the aun and the Earth is zero when moving at the speed of light.
