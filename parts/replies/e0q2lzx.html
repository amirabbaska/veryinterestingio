        Lets assume the hole was 5 feet in diameter. It looked closer to 6 feet, but I'm trying to help make it work. At 5 feet, that gives radius=30 inches and area=2827 sq inches. At 14.7 psi, that gives 41,563 lbs, or 20.8 tons of force. C=188.5 inches, so at the edge, the tape and plastic would need to support 220.5 lbs of force per inch of width. Looking at Table 1, column E in this study, five full layers of tape should hold that pressure. Assuming that the adhesive strength is greater or equal to the tensile strength, you would need five full widths of tape on each surface. That would require about 50 wraps of tape half lapped, or about 4.4 rolls of 180 ft each. According to this site, some plastics have a tensile strength as high as 150 MegaPascals or about 21,750 psi. Using 220.5 lb per inch of perimiter, it looks like 11 mil plastic would hold. I guess it is could work, but it would definitely not be flapping in the breeze.
