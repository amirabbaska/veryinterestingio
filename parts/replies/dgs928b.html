      The formula -GMm/r is the potential energy of a system of two masses m and M. The formula I used -1/2Gm^2 /r is the total potential energy of a gravitating shell of matter. Like a ball, but with all the matter compressed in the boundary. A derivation for a uniform sphere is on wikipedia

      But I guess your formula makes sense too, but you're neglecting the gravitational "self-energy" of both masses (and the rest-mass of the larger mass). Still, it shows that you cannot get a mass to have a total negative mass-energy without entering a black hole.
