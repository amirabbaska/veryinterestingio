  • pastyloudmouth

    I am piggybacking to explain what goes in to that terminal velocity.

    In general, any object shot upwards will have a moment at the top of its trajectory where it's vertical velocity goes to zero. A bullet shot from the ground is then equivalent to a bullet dropped from rest at the top of its trajectory.

    You might imagine dropping a bullet from a really high building. The bullet will reach its terminal velocity when the force of gravity (constant) is balanced by the drag force on the speeding bullet. In this sense, terminal velocity has nothing to do with how it was shot, only with how high it falls from. Even so, the bullet will reach its terminal velocity after only a short time. Dropping a bullet from a higher point would not result in a faster bullet.

    TLDR: Bullets are deadly.
