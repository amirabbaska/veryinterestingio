    This is only partially true.

    Capacitors don't increase voltage. To raise voltage you need to use a boost circuit (for DC) or windings (for AC).

    9v behind a 10Ah 1C cell still won't pass a lot of current because the voltage isn't enough to overcome human resistance.

    If your body is say ~4MOhm from one hand to another then a 9000mV cell will pass 0.00225mA of current which is why you don't feel it.

    edit: I just measured myself from thumb to thumb at 1.6MOhm. So feel free to multiply that current by 3x. It's still a tiny tiny amount of current.
