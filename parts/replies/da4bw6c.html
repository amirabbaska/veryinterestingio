    This is half-right, the true answer is, no, and that the glass is transparent to all animals. Agreed the butterfly sees some change in color, but we've all seen through colored glass just fine. To make it opaque, one would need to scatter the light, commonly achieved by roughing up a surface. In this case, however, the light would need to scatter only for particular wavelengths. Common glass is simply not remarkable enough to do this job. That being said, novel optical properties are being created every day in labs, and a material that blocks 100% transmission of a particular range of wavelengths could be invented that is selectively opaque/transparent to different animals.
