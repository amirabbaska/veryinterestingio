    The pointy bits act to "focus" the electric field.

    Conceptually, I can give an example. If you have a field between two parallel plates -- let's say it works out to two volts -- it will be uniform. If I now stick a nail from the 0V side half way into the middle, I mess with that. The center around where the nail is has been forced to also be 0V. So, around that tip, we have a much higher field, since it has to go up to 2V in a much shorter distance. Also, because charges repel each other, they will push each other to aggregate in pieces that stick out.
