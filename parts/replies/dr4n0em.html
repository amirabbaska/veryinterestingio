    Weight. You'd need to add a fan and a motor to power it. Then fuel for the motor. Then more propane for the burner to keep the heavier craft aloft for the same length of time, since the inside has to be kept hotter.

    And for all that you wouldn't get much control. The balloon does a great job of catching the air and moving with the currents. It would be tough to move that through the air.

  • shleppenwolf

    There are those that do, and they're another category of aircraft: airships, which includes blimps, dirigibles and zeppelins (which is another word for dirigible). The Goodyear Blimp is an airship.

    These operate on a lighter-than-air gas -- helium or hydrogen -- but in principle, there's no reason why one couldn't operate on hot air.
