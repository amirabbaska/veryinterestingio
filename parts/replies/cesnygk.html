    • Onetap1

      wells

      The water is usually drawn from a deep aquifer. All the bacteria, vegetable matter, faecal remains, animal remains, etc., are in the top layer of soil. There's nothing to for bacteria to feed on in the aquifer, so there are fewer bacteria. The higher layers of soil and sub-soils should filter out any nutrients from the water before it gets to the level of the aquifer. It SHOULD be clean, BUT ISN'T ALWAYS.

      Old wells were hand-dug, had to be wide enough for a man to work in, stopped as soon as they reached water and were open at the top; they are liable to contamination. Modern wells are drilled boreholes, with an electric pump at the bottom, are deeper and are sealed at the top and at the sides by the plastic well casing. They are more likely to be clean.

      Water from wells can be contaminated, by dumps, former industrial activity, dissolved chemicals or contamination of the aquifer, so the water should be tested and treated if necessary.
