The "Lost Cause" myth, from the handful of sources I skimmed/checked info on, sounds even worse than what you're describing.     Last note - As part of my googling spree, I found data both for and against slaveowning being a big part of Southern lives. Probably a third of the population was slaves, but only 6% of southerners owned slaves, but up to 49% of households owned slaves (by state, average around 25%). Presumably that means the 6% number is intentionally misleading, counting women and children who couldn't own property. There was also something about southerners who didn't own slaves still benefiting from having a punching bag/aspiring to be a slaveowner... I think overall the data makes it undeniable that slavery was in fact a huge part of southern life and economy, and we're definitely being bamboozled to a degree by our education system, but it's not quite a crazy conspiracy.
