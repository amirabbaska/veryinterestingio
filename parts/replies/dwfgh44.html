    • aezart

      There are so many people who know just enough to get their job done, without knowing all the neat tricks. I noticed the other day that one of my coworkers would completely quit vim whenever they made a change to a script and wanted to test it. I showed them how to do ctrl+Z to suspend the task, and then fg to resume it later. I also taught someone else about ctrl+S freezing the screen and ctrl+Q unfreezing it.

      It's called "satisficing", and it's an important concept in software usability testing. When a user finds a way to get something done, they stick with it. They almost never go back and try to find a more efficient method.
