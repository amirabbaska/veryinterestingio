    It's not a matter of an instantly fatal dosage obviously; it's a matter of cumulative dosage.

      If lead vests do anything for dental x-rays, it's an increase in dose.

      The beam is already collimated to just hit your jaw region. But some radiation will scatter though from your jaw into your body. Some of that radiation will scatter again. If you're wearing a lead vest then it has a chance to hit the vest from within and scatter back into your body, whereas if you wore nothing it would just exit your body.

      All in all, this extra dose is tiny and is absolutely nothing to worry about.
