    The health system is based upon incentives. For the assurance that a company turns over a profit, it spikes the premiums of the individual insurers. If my insurance is covering 80% of the costs after the satisfying deductible, I'm only responsible for the remaining balance. However, until that deductible is met, which could be $2000-5000 dollars, you're responsible for the costs prior.

    The financial goal is for you to spend thousands of dollars before any type of insurance kicks in. The fine print is usually the deceiving part of the transaction. Insurance companies only cover certain tests and deem others as unnecessary to the well-being of the individual. The written language is written specifically to confuse the client. By this method, a patient who encounters a medical emergency might be subjected to tests and procedures not covered under your plan, which could throw you in massive debt.

    The structure of healthcare is horrifically flawed. Insurance companies are in for the profit. The well-being of the established society is viewed as expendable. At some point - as you've aged, you're likely to be paying far more in costs due vulnerability of illness, deterioration in physical condition, etc. The higher the cost to keep you alive, the higher the premiums. It's a never-ending game to cut corners and expand the market for its enterprise.

    We need a system structured solely on doctor/patient relationships. The middle man cannot interfere with the transaction, and our tax dollars should be used to fund a healthcare system designed specially so upon a doctor visitation, it costs you nothing. The well-being of individuals should NEVER be a business model for people to profit it off. That's abhorrent and completely unethical.

      Getting appointments with your Family Physician is about the same. You might have to wait a couple more mins in the waiting room in Canada than the US....But I was told in both countries that that is due to patients asking "one more thing.." and taking up extra time...

      Hospitals and Specialists is where you notice the difference. Good insurance gets you preferential treatment at hospitals and faster bookings at specialists/follow-up appointments.
