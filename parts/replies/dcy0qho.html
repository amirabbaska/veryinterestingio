    One thing that complicated the 'warning system' was that the troops on the wall lived and worked on the frontier and were completely self-reliant. There weren't enough supplies or wages from the capitol to ensure loyalty to the Emperor.

    This led to the local communities establishing relations with the people they were supposed to keep out of China. AskHistorians can provide quicker sources than I can dig up this instant, but there are several instances where the local garrisons would open up the doors of the gates to raiding parties in return for trade.
