    cold spots in the water, superheated water that boils vigorously on contact with the tea

    Liquids have convection though. If the only goal is to make the water hot (and you don't have your tea in the water yet) you shouldn't have any issues with hot/cold spots. If it takes you 5 minutes to remember you boiled some water and it's cooled off before you add the bag, yeah you're gonna have problems with your brewing.

    If the tea is already in the water while you heat, it's going to produce a nucleation site for boiling, preventing the water from superheating.
