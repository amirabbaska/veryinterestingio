    • PedanticPendant

      It's hard to pin down precisely, because the technology to do this isn't even in its infancy - it's really far off (though that's not to say that we can't get to a place that's far off if we move fast enough in the right direction). Things that might be true of that kind of world:

      • You could have a bulk record of sensory information backed up to the cloud and relive a moment in perfect detail. This means any encounter with police, a sexual partner, or stranger in the street, could be recorded and shared for anyone to live through.

      • Rather than having instant access to Google to ask it a question with a thought, you could have cloud access to whole databases as easily accessible as your own memories. With a moment's thought, you would summon any fact from Wikipedia as easily as you could remember what you had for breakfast yesterday from your own personal memories.

      • Information security would be terrifyingly important, as your own mind could be hacked into and exploited. An attacker might make you wake up in the middle of the night with false memories that your spouse turned into a zombie and is trying to kill you, forcing you to kill them in self defence.

      • Similarly there may be people willing to have other people control them by livestreaming thoughts from person A's mind to person B's body. Couples could switch bodies with one another for fun. Armies may be populated with soldiers controlled in part by AI which can synchronise the movements of 20 men in a way that would be impossible for 20 individuals to achieve.

      The mind boggles to think of the ultimate possibilities of the kind of neural interface that Musk is hoping to bring about, but it should be remembered that the technology is very theoretical at the moment.
