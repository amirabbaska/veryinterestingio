      • harper_kentucky

        You can measure the speed of stars with spectrography. That's a lot to go into.

        But you can get mass from that velocity with Vc= Sqrt (G M/R)

        Where G is a constant, and R is measured (just distance from the center of the galaxy). The circular velocity at that R will tell you how much mass is inside R.

        This gives you the mass inside that radius.
