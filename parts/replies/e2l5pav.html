      • SweaterFish

        If you've ever visited a redwood grove, you may have noticed that the trees often grow in circles, called fairy rings. These are the result of an ancient tree that died, but a ring of stump sprouts formed around the old trunk from the still living root system and eventually several of the sprouts grew into huge trees in their own right. In the best groves, you can sometimes even find evidence of multiple generations of this kind of ring growth, tracing back possibly several thousand of years.
