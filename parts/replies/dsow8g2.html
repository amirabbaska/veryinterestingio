      This is could be explained by newton's first law: a body will keep its motion unless a force is applied and newton's second law: a body will change its velocity (speed) if a force is applied in the direction this force is applied.

Since there will be no other force acting on it it will keep its velocity. If we would apply a constant force to this object without another one to counteract it, the object will keep speeding up.

      Of course space is not a complete vacuum but close enough that friction can almost be neglected, only thing we need now is a force.
