      Stronger is not relative to the heart, its a container of blood and it needs to pump it. The bulkier it is due to size, decreases the amount of room for blood(it is 'strong enough' already).

      Its response to stress(stimulants) and constricted arteries the heart must work harder to pump the same amount of blood, so it does get larger.

      taken from /u/mmtree

      When you exercise, you increase heart rate but you also get a dilation of the arteries (aorta, brachial artery, femoral artery, etc) due to the build up of metabolites like lactic acid, increased CO2-- thus you get a decrease in systolic pressure and a slight increase/no change in diastolic. This means your heart can pump blood easily, albeit quickly, and become efficient but not get bulky; similar to how a gymnast or cyclist trains.

      The ideal heart is an elastic and springy one, not a larger and bulky one with thicker walls and less room for blood.
