      • rubseb

        A decent amount of thought seems to be done in the spinal cord.

        Not really. The spinal cord is mostly a big highway of cables from the central to the peripheral nervous system. Almost no computation whatsoever takes place there, except for a very small amount of in the form of reflexes, where a sensory neuron connects pretty much directly onto a motor neuron, thus computing a very simple "if X then Y" operation. But to call that "thought" would be a huge stretch of terminology. Indeed, a definition of "reflex" might well include the condition "occurs without thought".

        Decisions, by and large, are made in the central nervous system, while the spinal cord just carries the result of those decisions to the periphery.
