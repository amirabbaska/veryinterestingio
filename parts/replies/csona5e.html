        No; distant things aren't dimmer than close things. e.g. a building two blocks away from you isn't 1/4 times as bright than a building one block away from you, because the area that the light (which is 1/4 as much) falls on in your retina is also 1/4 the size, so you're getting the same number of photons per area, so the brightness is the same.

        So the problem wouldn't be that you'd be much darker due to inverse square. But your image would be tiny (your image in the million mirrors would be the same size as somebody standing 3 x 10^8 m away). So you'd need a really good telescope.
