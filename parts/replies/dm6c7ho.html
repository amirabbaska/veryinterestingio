    Of course it varies from deaf person to deaf person but, generally, completely deaf people visualize sign language in their heads or feel themselves signing in their head. Congenitally deaf people think more visually. You do it all the time without really noticing it also. On the flip side most deaf people usually have some exposure to "words" as they are present on the lips of people around them all the time or were exposed to it when they weren't deaf and thus it's rare for these people to not have "words" in their head or some kind of "thought container" reassembling words. Imagine reading a book with a word or the name of someone you can't pronounce. You might not be able to pronounce it but you know exactly what the author is talking about any time that word comes up. Kind of the same thing.
