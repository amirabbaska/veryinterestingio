    I agree with this description (I assume you are talking about the relationship between stem cells and differentiated cells).

    It's unfortunate that people hear about telomeres in some high school or undergraduate biology class and assume that's all there is to aging. If that was the case, we would have cured aging already! But numerous experiments have shown that you don't get say, immortal mice if you give them the ability to extend telomeres indefinitely. It's only a small part of a bigger problem.

    The bigger problem is an accumulation of damage, either as damage to the DNA, or too much junk protein, or whatever else. Eventually it overwhelms the body's ability to regenerate itself, as you described with the failing stem cells.

    And it absolutely is a problem for reproduction, that's where all the genetic/congenital disorders come from!
