      Left over sludge at the end of the wastewater treatment process is typically sent to landfill or is used as fertilizer. The waste sludge from the secondary clarifier that isn't recycled is thickened and sent to anaerobic digestion. This is where the solids are destroyed and stabilized and eventually produce gases (~65% methane and ~35% carbon dioxide) . These gases can then be utilized to generate electricity or used as other fuel. This is where the inactivation of pathogens takes place. Check out this and this for a bit more depth.

      After anaerobic digestion the remaining sludge is dewatered and then sent to disposal. Dewatering is typically used to reduce the volume of sludge that must be transported to final disposal.
