    The Vasa sank in Stockholm harbour, it was only in a hundred feet of water. What preserved it mainly was the sheer pollution that went into the water in that time, which prevented a particular parasite that would ordinarily feast on wood from a ship from doing its thing. That and Stockholm harbour is deep in a 50+ mile archipelago, so there isn't a lot in the way of tidal action by that point, and the salinity compared to open seawater is very low.

    But yeah typically depth and cold help a lot.
