    One small correction, the air doesnt actually move with sound. Its a pressure wave tha travels trough the air, thats a common misconception.
    To illustrate: If there is a speaker 20 meters away from you, it doesnt "shoot" air at you, it presses the air in front of it together. This creates pressure in the air.
    As the air doesnt stay compressed without something to hold it, it expands again and in turn compresses the air in front of it and so on.
    This goes untill your ears, which can feel this pressure difference.

    This is how sound moves through air. For any kind of music, this happens rhythmically depending on how high the note is: high note means lots of changes per second, low note means fewer changes.
    Also, in a medium all sound moves pretty much the same speed, the rate of compression (how often its pushed and pulled per second) determines the pitch TL:DR Soundwaves work me pushing air together and apart (compressing and decompressing), NOT by pushing air to you.

    Didnt want to sound overly correct, its just a problem for a lot of people i teach, so i thought id explain.
