      When a building goes up, the steel is never perfect. As a glazier, when we run the panels of glass and aluminum up, we plumb them using a laser floor to floor, which is aligned parallel to gravity at that point. But that means each wall of the building will be different from the opposite wall. So all buildings flare a little at the top vs the bottom? Also once you hit about 20-30 stories, your laser level is no longer still, it moves back and forth with the building so you shoot for average middle.

      Assuming the accuracy of the level, we were never out by a single mm from bottom to top. Thing is with glass, you can see when it is out as you move past the building as the mirrors will reflect different things.
