    • BurningBrightly

      In evolution the organism wins that can access a resource better than others. Being complacent/content with the way things are means the more competitive species will probably invade your space and push you out. There are some species who have been solidly lodged in their niche for millions of years, but those niches often aren't all that appealing to begin with. For the nicer spots in nature, the competition is fierce.

      Humans are wimpy, lanky creatures, no scales, no claws, tiny teeth, the only thing we're physically good at is running long distances, and simulating different realities in our brains. The joke is, at that stage, we anticipate things to be much more enjoyable, and the joy to last much longer than it actually does. Our brains pull mean tricks to keep us going in more than one spot.
