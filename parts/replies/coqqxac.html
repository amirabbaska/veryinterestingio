    I like all the points you made except letting your car idle for a few minutes after start. Your begins to prime itself as soon as the key is switched to the on position. The most wear from starts happen because the engine is not initially running at its suitable operating temperature. You should start driving the car about 10 seconds after you start it so the engine will get to its optimal temperature faster. Of course before you reach this point you dont want to rev the car too high either. When I first start driving I keep it below 3000 rpm until it is at the optimal temperature. If you sit idle for however long and wait for it to warm up you are just burning excess fuel because the car has to work harder to warm up idle.
