      You're partly right. Light emitted by electrons in ordinary matter is indeed quantized, because electrons can only exist in discrete states. But light itself is not actually quantized. A single photon can have any energy you want it to have. All you have to do is move either toward or away from the source of the light at the right speed, and you can make the light have any energy you want.

      Put another way, E = hν, yes, but ν is not confined to being an integer. It can be any number.
