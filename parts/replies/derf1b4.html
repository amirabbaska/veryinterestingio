    • Trudzilllla

      Yes

      Gravitational force falls of with the square of distance. So if you double the distance to the center, you cut gravitational force to 1/4 it's previous level.

      Edit: someone can check my numbers but I think, for a cube, the Gravitational Force at the corners is 1/2 that at the faces.

      F=GM/r^2

      if r=1 at the center, then r=sqrt2 at the corner.

      (Sqrt2)^2 =2

      So G stays that same, M stays the same, r^2 doubles, so F is cut in half.
