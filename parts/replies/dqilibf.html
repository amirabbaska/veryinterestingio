  • Wakebrite

    The first energy source for muscles is ATP, this lasts a few seconds. The second source is phosphocreatine. It lasts a few minutes. After that, you have muscle glycogen, and then glucose and things that can be made into glucose. If your muscles are working really hard, they’ll use up the readily available energy sources quickly and begin anaerobic respiration in the absence of sufficient oxygen.

    As you exercise more, your blood volume, capillary number, and muscles will improve. You’ll get more glycogen storage capacity, which will reduce the fatigue.

    The utter fatigue feeling is caused by depletion of glycogen.
