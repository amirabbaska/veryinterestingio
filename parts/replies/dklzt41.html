    Agreed, no one knows the whole story. Speaking for some of the nasa projects, fellow engineers are not told what project they are working on or how their part fits with every thing. Only the PhD knows what the big picture is. This is an efficient form of secrecy because if only one guy knows that part you know who leaked it. If the whole project is leaked it's the PhD if only part of it is leaked it's the engineer in charge of that piece. I've actually seen projects where no one at the facility even knows what it's for not the engineer or management, some of those actually caused quite a bit of fuss as to where they are going or what to do with it in the meantime until some general comes to pick it up six months later.

    Tldr: if you break it up small enough, the pieces don't even need to be classified, only how they fit together
