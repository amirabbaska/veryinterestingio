    • NamityName

      It really depends on the area. Where i grew up (Alabama), your scam would work 99% of the time. There were laws placing fault on the driver that did the rear ending and police were allowed to make a initial assessment about fault at the scene. You could challenge that in court, but it was an uphill battle going against the officer's initial assessment.

      However, where i live now (maryland), the scam would be much harder. there is no automatic blame placed on the car doing the rear ending. And police are normally not allowed to make any assessments of fault. All fault for an accident is shared between all parties equally unless there is solid evidence which normally entails two eye witnesses or video evidence.

      That all being said. I think the prospect of insurance scams is still high enough to warrant inctivising dash cams. But what do i know. I'm not in the insurance business
