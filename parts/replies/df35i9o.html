• camerongale

    Getting student loans discharged in cases of permanent disability is a real bitch, too. My MIL is in the process of that now.

    First, you have to get social security disability. Only about 17 percent of people are approved on their first application.

    Then you file an appeal. When that appeal is denied, despite a stack of paperwork from your physician detailing your medical issues and usually forms from family and friends detailing how much assistance you need in daily life, you get scheduled for a court date. The process of application and appeal took 2 years in my MIL's case. Luckily she still was getting long term disability insurance payments from her former job, or she'd be screwed. The entire time, she's making payments on her student loans.

    Getting the court date can take up to a year. When you finally go, it can still take months for you to get a decision from them. You have about a 50/50 shot of getting approved at a court date.

    And then after that, if you're approved, you still get to make payments on your student loan debt! You have to be on social security disability and pass your first case review saying that, yeah, you're still disabled and can't work, before you can apply for student loan discharge. This is 3 years or so after you're approved.
