    • BanesHenchman

      It's all about angle of the rush. Holders are typically 7 yards behind the line of scrimmage. That puts the kicker in the "pocket", but far enough back to clear the middle lineman. The angle an edge blocker needs to take is very tight. That angle also goes right by the wing lineman. Usually, even a slight push by the wing is enough to throw the rusher off his direct path. If the holder moves back to 10 yards, the edge rusher can take a straighter and opposition free angle to block the kick.
