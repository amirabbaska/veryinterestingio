    • Altiloquent

      Atmosphere would tend to carry away a lot of heat so you'd probably need much more power to get the metal to the same temperature, and your chamber would have to be designed to accommodate it. Transport of the vapor would also no longer be ballistic and instead be determined by diffusion or convective motion of the gas. In practice, you would place the metal in close proximity to what is being coated so that the diffusion is more rapid (some materials are actually deposited this way; it's called close space sublimation)
