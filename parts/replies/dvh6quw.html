    To expand this further, consider that to massless particles traveling at the speed of light, time does not pass. Could a photon observe the passage of time, one emitted from a distant star 1.2 billion light years away would have zero time pass from when it was emitted until it hits our upper atmosphere (and slows a bit).

    Meanwhile of course other bits of the universe had other amounts of time pass; in this case, 1.2 billion years would have passed on earth.
