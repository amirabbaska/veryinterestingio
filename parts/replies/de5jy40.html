      TL; DR: anti-depressants aren't supposed to make depressed people happy.

      So depressed that happy pills just make you normal.

      I'm not sure if you meant this literally, but a lot of people misunderstand the goal of medication. All of this isn't aimed at you.

      Anti-depressants ("happy pills") aren't really intended to make depressed people "happy". They should be prescribed to relieve symptoms of depression, giving the person more normal moods, so they can feel happiness, sadness, fear, and anger.

      A lot of people, including people with mental illness, think happy is the opposite of depressed, but the opposite of depressed is actually manic. Being manic can feel great, but it's just as destructive to the person and those around them.

      So basically, if taking anti-depressants actually makes someone super happy and energetic, that could be a bad sign.

      Sorry for the lecture. It's a widespread myth that gets under my skin for two reasons.

      1. It gave me unrealistic expectations about my treatment, and I didn't understand this for over a decade because no one explained it well.

      2. It leads some people to rail against psychiatry and those with mental illness, arguing that it's not normal to be happy all the time. They're right about that, because it's not.
