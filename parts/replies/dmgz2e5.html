  • TuMadreTambien

    They were chasing megafauna like camels (really!), wooly mammoths, bison, caribou, muskox, as well as deer, sabre-tooth cats, dall sheep, saiga antelope, yak, moose, flying squirrels, lynx, lion, dhole, dogs, river otters, ferrets, bears, jaguars, lemmings, mice and voles, fox, rabbits, and wolverines. The land across the Bering Sea and along the coast of Alaska was dry, with very little snowfall at that time. There were massive glaciers that held a lot of water to the north and east, which is why the sea levels were so low. They most likely did not realize that they had crossed to another continent and that they would be trapped there once the ice melted over the next 10-15K years. They were already in Siberia, and were probably following migrating animals, or searching for game. Camels actually evolved in modern day Canada and the US, and migrated across the same Bering Sea land bridge into Mongolia and much warmer weather. The same adaptations inside their noses that allowed them to breathe cold air also allowed them to lose very little water caper during respiration, which helped them to live in dry areas.
