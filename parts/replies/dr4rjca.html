      • Wolham

        If you're taking it for pain relief, ibuprofen has far less side effects.

        Aspirin is used as an antiplatelet therapy to prevent strokes and the likes, but when used for this purpose, it's given as a "Low dose"; i.e. at a dosage far too low to cause analgesia (pain relief), but also so low that it doesn't really cause any notable side effects.

        I.e., at 400 mg (pain relief dose), aspirin has more side effects, but at 75-125 mg (too low dose to give pain relief), aspirin reduces platelets whilst ibuprofen does nothing.
