    • Melanopsine

      In both cases, all the information the brain seems to be getting is: "We're getting a nerve signal from the foot"

      It has to do with where the signals are sent in the brain, and to some extent how many signals are being sent. Very simply put you can view it as if there is a pain-center, pressure-center etc. If a pain-percieving neuron is activated, the entire row will terminate in part to a center for that body-part which is affected AND to the pain-center. However, if we instead stimulate say a pressure-neuron, it will also terminate at the body-part-center but won't terminate at the pain-center, instead it would terminate to a pressure-center.

      In reality, this is significantly more complex and there are still much we don't know yet.
