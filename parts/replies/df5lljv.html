  • dougger12321

    It is true that as space expands our "observable universe" becomes more limited, but this is not the only limit on our observations! After the Big Bang (~13.7 billion years ago) we believe all matter in the universe was in a plasma state, meaning that rather than being linked together by atomic bonds the universe was made up of free floating elementary particles in this hot "cosmic soup". The bitch about cosmic soup is that, like regular soup, it is opaque so no photons from this period were free to begin flying around the universe. "Recombination" happened about 300,000 years after the Big Bang so we still have a potential 0.4 billion more years of historical information to collect, but before that the universe had no free moving light particles so there is no light from that period to ever observe.
