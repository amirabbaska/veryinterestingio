  • tminus7700

    TNT is actually hard to set off. Just hitting it with a hammer will not do it. There is an inverse relation between the yield of an explosive and its sensitivity. With nitroglycerin as an exception. I have taken small pieces of RDX (another high explosive) and actually placed them on concrete and hit them with a hammer. Nothing. Likewise lighting them on fire will not set them off. We once disposed of old dynamite by burning it. Since dynamite has nitroglycerin, we hid behind a dirt berm and triggered the fire remotely.

    Now wiring up the blasting caps is the risky part. Those have gone off from static discharges.
