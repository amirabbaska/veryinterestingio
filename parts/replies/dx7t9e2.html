  • TenNeon

    Sorta. In one sense, things always mutually orbit one another. Both objects orbit a point between them where the point is weighted toward the more massive object. Usually this point is actually inside the larger object, but the closer they are in mass the closer this point is to the point exactly between the two. Pluto and its moon Charon are an example of such a system- both orbit a point in space between the two, with the point being closer to Pluto but still well above its surface.
