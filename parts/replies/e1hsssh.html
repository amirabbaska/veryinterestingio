  • HighDerp

    Is there any way to treat this without extensive therapy?

    • BurningBrightly

      Not that I know of.

      Those locked-away parts and memories are extremely scary and trauma patients need to learn how to approach them carefully without overloading their minds yet again. It's a skill, there are several techniques to get there (EMDR, imaginary screen, even forms of physical therapy, it's all about staying anchored in the safe place and moment and go look at the scary monster with enough distance). Once people know how to control their mental and emotional state, it can be pretty fast. Not every single traumatic experience needs to be carefully dissected, a few typical ones can be enough and the rest calms down too. The fear what could happen when the memories are brought up is MUCH worse than actually doing it. A good deal of the preparation work is establishing trust between patient and therapist. To approach your worst nightmares you want someone along who is trained, stable and reliable.

      Systems are also astonishingly resilient without therapy. As long as they're under stress they keep on functioning, not necessarily in a healthy way or happily, but they do, somehow. The big bang comes when the stress eases off. Their kids moved out, or they retired, or they found a nice, calm place to live and are finally relaxing. I just hope knowledge about complex trauma disorders keeps spreading, so people who think they're going crazy, life is good and still suddenly everything is going haywire, get a diagnosis and treatment faster. At the moment it's still over 5 years between the onset of noticeable symptoms and the correct diagnosis.
