    To add to this, renewable energy generation like solar, wind and nuclear has no real answer to peak demand. They are run at maximum output, in which they are not built for the largest, short lived spikes in demand on the grid.

    Coal is slow to do so, as heat output will slowly rise, but it can respond. Natural gas is even faster. If energy storage technologies reach a scale of affordability and capacity, this might change, but the current system is ad-hoc at best.
