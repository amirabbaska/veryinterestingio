    That definition used to be used. Since 2001, Mars datum is defined as the equipotential surface with the average Martian equatorial radius.

    • PhysicsBus

      Since planets bulge at their equator, does this make the altitude over most of the surface negative?

      • rapax

        No, because you don't use a sphere as reference. The bulge is due to the spin of the planet, and this is reflected in the equipotential surface. The fitted shape is more of an oblate spheroid (a squashed ball).
