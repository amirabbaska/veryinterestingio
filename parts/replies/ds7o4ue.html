        OK, I'm a biker and an EV driver just for context. My bike will do around 300km and frankly after just 150km I'm very ready for a break so while I could fill it up in a few mins and be on my way again, I typically can't ride the thing for more than a few hours before I have to get off and stretch my legs. I'm sure there are more comfortable bikes but really it makes sense to take a significant break. This is exactly when an EV would be charging. My current EV has a crazy small battery by modern standards so can only cover 140km on a single charge but with our roads that can still take nearly two hours to do. If the battery was say twice as big the charging time wouldn't be terrible and I could drive for more than three hours comfortably before needing a break. Also, a full charge even on a fast charger can take a long time because the charging rate slows as the battery gets close to full so it is more efficient to charge to 80% and then get back out on the road. My car will do 100km at 100kph on 80% (average speed is usually less though so range is better than that) and it takes 20 mins to get to that so hopping along routes populated with fast chargers can be done and I've done 475km runs in our car. Again, a bigger battery would help but the recharging isn't a massive issue and it is good to stop for a bio-break, stretch and to grab a coffee.

        The latest EVs are affordable and with Nissan now hitting 40kWh which is nearly double the size of my current battery the issue of range and recharging is largely gone for the vast majority of use cases. If a car (or bike) can travel for 3 hours+ and then needs 20 mins to recharge to do it again we're there except for people who absolutely must be able to fill up in a few mins. Heck, EVs have already been perfect cars for the last five years, people just need to get over the range thing and realise that they generally don't drive long distance and there are solutions (such as renting if range is needed) that work out much cheaper than insisting on owning a car that can do 500km+ on a single charge when you rarely actually need that. The thing with EVs is to buy the battery you need, not the one you want. Do that and they're very cheap to fuel, extremely reliable and great fun to drive. We plug ours in overnight when the battery gets low and it is ready to go again the following day so charging time is essentially zero anyway.
