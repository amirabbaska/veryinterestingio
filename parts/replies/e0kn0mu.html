      Hey there, thanks for the interest. I also had a auto correct error it seems, Dubai should be dubia, these lovely creatures. - https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blaptica_dubia

      As far as the roaches go, their normal diet is bread crusts, fruit peels (NON CITRUS) and a banana whole, as a treat once a month. You have to control their breeding so over feeding and including citrus will result in a population boom, useful short term, but this can have the effect of throwing your colony genetics off. The difference between a fine breeder and a by the Lb breeder you can find online.

      When im pulling from the colony for a feed they move into a special container with a lot of Buffalo beetles for a few days where they clean up and the weak, sick, pregnant and nymphs are culled by the beetles.

      Then they go on a crash glut to gain their gut load depending upon their destination. Right now i even have some fattening up on rose pollen for human consumption, so its funny you mention 'dessert roaches' because i breed those too.

      Its actually a hobby that pays for my expensive taste in cars, im not a drug dealer i breed insects, i keep anywhere between 20 - 25 50 Gallon totes running at any time with 5 feeding containers, maybe 1 - 2 million insects at $1 a piece is some hobby. Some of my master breeders are sold to other breeders and your talking $50 - $100 a roach
