    • jamincan

      • Steuard

        This is true, but it's also perfectly reasonable to talk about massless particles without involving quantum mechanics. Even leaving quantum physics aside, p=mv is only an approximation to the relativistic definition of momentum. The approximation is reliable when v << c, so it can't be used at all for objects moving at the speed of light.
