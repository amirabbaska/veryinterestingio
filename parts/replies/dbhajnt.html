    it's slightly different in Canada due to our healthcare system. Our resident salary is government funded and so the government determines how many residency spots they are willing to fund each year. This is in turn determined by things like patient loads and safety, job prospects (eg. neurosurgery jobs are tough to come by so there are fewer residents each year). So it's not as simple as just opening up more spots, because ultimately the money has to come from somewhere AND this person needs a job at the end of all of this 13 years of post secondary education.

    Actually, residency spots in America are funded by the government, too. Specifically by Medicare. Politicians are loathe to spend more money on Medicare. Residency positions don't keep up. Presto. Projected physician shortage of 90,000 by 2020.

        Supposedly the shortages is split evenly between primary care and specialty services, though the shortage of primary care is a large problem which has been getting more attention over the years.
