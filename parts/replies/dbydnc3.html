    • SenseiPoru

      In a former career I was a physician's assistant and today I review and audit medical records. (NOT INTENDED TO BE MEDICAL ADVICE). Chronic chest pain needs to be better defined. Is it superficial (i.e., costalchondritis), or within the chest i.e., pleurisy)? Massage probably wouldn't help either very much. A condition such as a torn intercostal muscle muscle can probably be ruled out, as this is usually acute and relatively short lasting.

      It could be a localized muscular inflammation. This can be caused by a number of things such as immune reaction, infection, calcium deposits, etc. They can become chronic due to continuous movement or mechanical stresses. This might very well respond temporarily to massage.
