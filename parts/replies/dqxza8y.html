    • wetnax

      Yep, it's called Acoustic reflex.

      In your ear there's the eardrum, connected to a bunch of bones, which then connect to the cochlear (the thing that converts sound to electrical signals). There's muscles attached to the bones, which can pull on them. When this happens, the eardrum gets pulled too, making it stiffer and less responsive to sound. The part of the cochlear that the bones touch also tightens, with the same result.
