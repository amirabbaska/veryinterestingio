  • DrunkenSpoonyBard

    That's the Amsler Grid, which tests for macular degeneration

    As someone who has to stare at this thing on the regular, may I chime in on what they're actually looking for when you look at the grid?

    Basically, you're looking for any sign of warping or bending in the lines. When you're looking at the center dot and there's distortion, where precisely it occurs on the grid can pinpoint exactly where the flaw causing the distortion is in your eye. I personally suffer fairly regular macular hemorrhages (exactly what it sounds like, bleeding in the eye) that push on the back of my retina, and this manifests as what I like to refer to as black holes in my field of vision - because everything bends in towards the large blind spot, like it's being sucked into a black hole. The first one I ever had was pretty much dead center and blocked out a good three or four squares in each direction; now I just tend to get small ones that are pretty far off to the side, but I'm so used to keeping an eye (sorry) out for them that I can spot them even before highly sensitive computerized scans can. If they're big enough, too, when my eye doctor looks in my eye with the slit lamp (the really fucking bright thing that then gets magnified into OH MY GOD WHY via going through a large handheld lens) I can actually see them on the...reflection, I suppose you'd call it, of the back of my eye. Giant spots that break up the monotony of what looks very much like some kind of demonic lava field. It's actually kind of fun.
