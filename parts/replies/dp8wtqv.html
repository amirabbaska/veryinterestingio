        it stops the release of the inhibitory neurotransmitters that prevent the release of dopamine.

        GABA is universally an inhibitory neurotransmitter, not only when it comes to dopamine circuitry. Hypothetically, if after activation you see a net increase in the concentration of dopamine, you will probably see a net increase in multiple neurotransmitters. The ratios of these neurotransmitters alone facilitate hundreds, thousands, if not hundreds of thousands of processes.

        am I wrong in saying that the effects of cannabis use can be mainly attributed to the release of dopamine?

        Probably. The downstream effects are complicated. If we look at THC alone, it also inhibits N-type calcium channels, implicated in neurotransmitter release and pain most importantly. THC also disinhibits A-type potassium channels, which are fairly poorly understood overall.

        We can factor in other oddities, such as CBD not being psychoactive despite agonizing the same receptors as THC. This is probably related to the fact that THC acts as a partial agonist instead of a full agonist.

        After factoring in whatever oddities the two 'primary' cannabinoids portray, factor in everything for the 100+ other cannabinoids.

        It gets fairly complicated, but it is fairly safe to claim that there are other mechanisms in play, and the experience is more of a synergy.
