    • myplacedk

      But those few 1's and 0's you miss in music (and yeah that's what I meant), is it such a small thing that we just don't register it as humans?

      Music is basically either s smooth curve (nice clean tones) or a big mess (think distorted electric guitars).

      On a CD, there's 44100 samples per second (per channel).

      Of one sample is wrong in s "messy", it doesn't matter. Nobody will ever notice.

      If one sample is missing in a smooth part, you can just interpolate it. Assume it's right in the middle between the previous sample and the next sample. Nobody will ever notice.

      This is why computer data needs more error correction than audio, which is why an audio cd has more storage than a data cd.

      One frame on an audio CD is 1/75 second. At 44100 samples, 2 channels, 2 bytes (16 bits) per sample, one frame of audio is 4410022/75 = 2352 bytes. One frame of data is 2048 bytes, the rest is error correction.

      2048/2352 ~= 650/750
