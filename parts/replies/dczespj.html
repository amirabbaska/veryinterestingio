    • pyrespirit

      Not quite, but the mechanics are essentially that - you're just missing a step.

      The sun emits a lot of visible light and UV light.

      Both these hit the Earth and warm it up, where it's re-emitted as IR light (this is blackbody radiation - thermal radiation emitted by all things in thermal equilibrium with their surrounding).

      Greenhouse gases are largely transparent to visible light and UV light, so they don't impede light from the sun.

      However, greenhouse gases are semi-opaque to IR light, so the emitted thermal radiation from the Earth is absorbed by them and can't be transmitted into space to cool the Earth off.
