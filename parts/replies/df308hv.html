  • Toilet2000

    There's a huge impact from input. Input is normally processed at the beginning of a game loop iteration. Frame swapping is normally done a the end. The lower the frame rate, the bigger the time lapse between those two.

    If you're expecting something to move, you're a lot more likely to get annoyed by low frame rate, because it's not only about your eyes' "capture rate", but your brain expectation of movement (which is most probably a lot faster).

  • hater_of_fun

    Yep. As long as the game's framerate is a factor of the monitor's framerate, it'll be fine. This is why SGIs used to run at 72Hz, because that allowed them to display 24Hz content without issues (72/3=24).

    The problem is that a 60Hz monitor can only reliably display content at the following frame rates:

    • 60Hz (every frame)
    • 30Hz (1 in 2 frames)
    • 20Hz (1 in 3 frames)
    • 15Hz (1 in 4 frames)
    • 12Hz (1 in 5 frames)
    • 10Hz (1 in 6 frames)
    • 8.6Hz (1 in 7 frames)
    • 7.5Hz (1 in 8 frames)
    • etc...

    So a game running at 20Hz is OK, a game running at 30Hz is OK. But a game can't actually run at 24Hz - instead it's constantly alternating between 20Hz and 30Hz. This gives a super-juddery effect.

    tl'dr; it doesn't matter that much what frame-rate you pick, as long as you hit it consistently and your monitor runs at the same frame rate.
