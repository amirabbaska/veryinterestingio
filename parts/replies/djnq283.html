    • Laminar_flo

      This seems like it should be a simple question, but it's actually incredibly complicated bc you are touching on why companies acquire each other, and there are millions of non-financial and non-economic reasons companies buy each other.

      Business school teaches you that you should use some sort of DCF/NOPaT/FCF/WACC analysis, but this doesn't match the reality of doing M&A in practice. I did tech/TMT M&A for about 4 years. In 9 out of 10 situations, company A wants company B for whatever reason and it's up to the bankers to find a multiple/valuation/theory/story that makes the deal work after the fact. M&A banking is a lot like high-touch real estate sales.
