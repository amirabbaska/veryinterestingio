• bencbartlett

    Possible: yes, likely: no. We don't know of any planet with a gas moon. Gas planets are typically quite large because gas isn't very dense so you have to have a large mass to gravitationally bind it, so a planet with a gas giant-sized moon must be very, very large. (Mutually orbiting gas giants could be more likely to form, but could also be orbitally unstable, depending on the configuration of the rest of the solar system.)

      Wouldnt something like a 20 jupiter mass exoplanet with a neptune mass moon be perfectly feasible?

      • bencbartlett

        Yes, it's feasible if that system were to form, but its formation is unlikely. A Neptune-mass moon is unlikely to form in orbit around a 20-Jupiter mass planet, since the planet will dominate the process of sweeping up available mass in the system, and it is unlikely to be gravitationally captured because a Neptune-mass object is likely to stabilize into its own orbit.
