    • Brudaks

      Well, for starters, it's worth noting that humans are much better at throwing stuff than chimpanzees or gorillas. Even an 11 year old little league pitcher outclasses them on throwing ability despite being much, much weaker. To get reach and accuracy, you're throwing rocks that are small and light compared to the weight of your arm. To do that well, you need arms that are long (to get leverage), skinny (if they're heavy, they'll be hard to swing really fast) and accurate. You need your ligaments to store energy elastically instead of powering through the movement; you need strong core body and shoulders relative to the weight of your arms, which are mostly providing just leverage. You need enough muscle, but after that huge strong arms don't help at all. Look at top baseball pitchers as an example. For throwing much heavier objects than mere rocks, you need more strength, but still - look at pictures of olympic javelin throwers, which have the best shape humanly possible for throwing things very far - they're extremely fit but their arms still are quite small compared to even an amateur bodybuilder; if more arm muscle would help, they would train until they'd look like olympic shot put guys, but they don't.

      Also, compare chimpanzee arms and gorilla arms - chimpanzees throw much better than gorillas despite being much weaker; gorillas can lob a heavy boulder, but a small, fast stone is more damaging and has more reach.

    • Vegalyp

      It's partially about dimishing returns. You can build as much muscle as possible, which will require tons of calories every day to maintain, but double the muscle mass doesn't equal to doubled spear throwing speed/distance. You might get 20% faster/further of a spear throw.

      You are also sort of hard capped by bones (joints), ligaments, and tendons. You can have insane amounts of muscle, but if the force you generate with that muscle breaks or dislocates your shoulder each time, it's sort of worthless.
