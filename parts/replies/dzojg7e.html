        People with vitamin D deficiency are routinely directed to take up to 10,000 IU per day or sometimes given a weekly megadose by prescription (50,000 IU in a single pill). Those at risk are also advised to supplement up to 2,000 IU per day. Most pharmacies do carry several different dosages over the counter. It is possible you live in a region with lower prevalence of vitamin D deficiency, which is why you don't see the higher doses at your pharmacy, but actually more than 1/3 of American adults are deficient to some degree.
