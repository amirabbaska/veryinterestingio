    • angels_egg

      The short length of the growing season and hard frosts out of season. In the time I takes to form new leaves, the growing season is almost already over, and late frosts could entirely kill of new leaf growth for an entire season.

      Also because of the slow growth rate and frequent glaciation extreme latitudes tend to have poorer soils.

      There are exceptions to this rule though. Birches and larches are both deciduos trees that thrive in taiga environs
