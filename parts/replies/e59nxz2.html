• phunkydroid

    This is the correct answer. People are giving me crap for saying "higher pressure under the lid" is wrong even though I did the math and the pressure increase is minuscule.

    ​

    With a lid on, there is only a small crack for air/steam to flow between the inside and outside of the container, and once it's boiling and generating steam, that flow is outward only. It will carry all of the air out over time leaving only steam filling the space above the water. With the lid off, the steam rises away and dry air flows in around the edge of the pot. Dry air vs steam being in contact with the bubbles is what makes the difference in how long they persist.
