      • mckulty

        For most liquids, no. In general liquids are several times less "compressible" than gases so pressure waves in the air see the liquid surface as essentially a rigid wall.

        The pitch will vary with the volume of air and the length of the "standing wave" that forms in the vessel. The purity of the tone, the overtones, come from the shape of the vessel and the resonance of the vessel.
