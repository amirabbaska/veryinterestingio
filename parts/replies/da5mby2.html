    I was promoted to VP of all Google Engineering in around 2005, and keep that position until a reorganization in 2012, when I was given the position of VP of Knowledge. Larry Page came up with the title, since he thought Google should not just depend on web sites to answer queries, but we should build a database that understood the relationship between all the entities on the web. For most of the time before I retired in 2015, Knowledge including Search, Maps, and Research. The inside joke was that if I did really well at the VP of Knowledge role, they'd promote me to VP of Wisdom :-) AE
