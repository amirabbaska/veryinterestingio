    To our best knowledge protons don't disintegrate into anything, in the first place.

    The reason a neutron can release a few light particles and turn into a proton is because it is very slightly heavier, not the other way around. The binding energy is not directly the reason for the mass difference between the proton and neutron.

    To my understanding, because the proton is charged, it has some small interactions with virtual photons that the neutron can't, which effectively lowers the mass. This in turn allows neutrons to decay into protons, since particles can only decay into lower-mass particles under normal circumstances.
