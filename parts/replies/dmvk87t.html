    The restoration point, at least, has, if not a solution, a mitigation - actually, my power company does a similar thing with the OH lines, to cut down on the amount of 'driving around until they see a pole down' (repairs are really freaking fast since they put it in - if the power goes out at all, since depending on the break location there might be a way to reroute power, which happens automatically).

    Put in the occasional sensor that relays status. Or, rather, stops talking if the line breaks. Then you know a lot better where the break is - between such and such poles, in the case of OH lines, between such and such stations for UG.

    Adds more cost, though. Not just for the sensors, but for the network lines they communicate on (my utility ran fiber for it... and has paid for it and more by allowing it to also carry internet service. They don't even charge as much as Comcast, either!).
