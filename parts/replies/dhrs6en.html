    Not for profit? - technically yes, although it's hard to see how they manage to somehow, spend all of this, if they are not paying massive salaries out to everyone involved.

    Quick technical note: there's absolutely nothing to stop a non-profit from paying any salary at all. The thing that makes it a non-profit is that it can't make a profit. Paying salaries is an expense, not the result of a profit. Paying dividends, conversely, is the result of a profit. The thing that makes a non-profit theoretically more-ethical than a for-profit company is that it's harder for people to make money from it by doing nothing (i.e. just being a shareholder): you actually need to be employed by the company. This has implications (which vary geographically) on taxation, transparency, etc.

    Furthermore, some kinds of non-profit have further restrictions. In most localities, charities (for example) are a specific type of non-profit that has other limitations, such as perhaps financial transparency and/or being able to demonstrate that they're acting towards a specific public good.

    GS1's clearly a little shady, though.
