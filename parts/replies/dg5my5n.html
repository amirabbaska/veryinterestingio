    To add to this, most games have to be finished a month or two before the actual release date, so that there's time to produce and ship the physical game discs/cartridges. That's why "day 1" patches are so common, because during those 1-2 months that the game is being manufactured and shipped, the developers are still working on ironing out those bugs and glitches, maybe finishing up some extra content, or what have you, and then those fixes are sent out on release day.
