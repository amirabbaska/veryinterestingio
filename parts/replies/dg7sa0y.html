    one way to think about this is that:

    Imagine a brick wall, made of bricks.

    The bricks themselves can regenerate if someone chips away at them, graffities them, or otherwise rain erodes away at them.

    But the shape of the wall itself, if something incredibly heavy leans on it - there's nothing to lean it back the other way. It doesn't matter how much the bricks themselves individually regenerate, that almost doesn't matter, the wall will be bent and mis-shapen and no amount of regenerating will fix the shifting, it'll be a shifted over wall with perfect bricks for all intents and purposes.

    for us to regenerate eyesight, you'll need a whole wall that can regenerate, not just the bricks. In other words, it's not good enough that the eye cells regen, we need the lens to regen as well.
