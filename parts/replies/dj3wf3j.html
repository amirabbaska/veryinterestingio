  • doepy

    I had a mild concussion eight years ago, followed by light and noise sensitivity, constant brain fog, mental tiredness and depersonalisation.

    My life has been turned upside down, doctors haven't been able to find anything (MRI, PET scan, CT, blood tests, etc.), and worst of all, none of my friends or family are all that supportive.

    I feel like one of those homeless drug addicts that needs years of re-adjustment and help to get back on their feet, but society looks at me like a normal person who just needs to get on with their life, so I have to do all the normal things, although I'm constantly suicidal, tired and depressed.

    I wish that I could, at the very least, get a diagnosis of some kind. Like "this guy has autism, therefore we need to help him in this way"...

    I agree with you that doctors don't know much about the brain, or at least about brain injuries. Many of the doctors I've gone to contradict the "latest" (1-10 years old) research.
