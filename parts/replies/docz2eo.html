      Well, you can’t collapse a wave function twice, but maybe you mean two separated experiments that interact with the same big wave function. But now the particle’s wave function is shared among causally connected observers as part of one overarching mega-wave function that both experiments are in, and the whole thing collapses to one state when they share results, which will agree. Causality is the constraint that fixes the paradox.

      The only way they could disagree would be to design the experiment that they were somehow unable to share data, say they are on opposite sides of the cosmic horizon. Then it just doesn’t matter, because causality is unbroken. To steal an idea from this PBS Space Time video, causality defines space and time, not the other way around, and tricks with wave functions never break causality. The weirdness of wave function collapse is part of that diabolical conspiracy that makes reality work.
