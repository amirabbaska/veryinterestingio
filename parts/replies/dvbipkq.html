    • Penkala89

      The axis they rotate on doesn't necessarily line up perfectly perpendicular with their orbital plane (why we have seasons on Earth, for instance.) Mercury has a low axial tilt of 0.03 degrees, while Uranus rotates on its side with an axial tilt of about 82 degrees.

    • nikstick22

      If you view the solar system from above (such that the orbits trace out circles), then from your perspective, they rotate on the Z axis. If you view the solar system from the side so that the orbits appear to go left and right, the planets are rotating around the Y axis. If you view the solar system so that the planets rotate up and down, they rotate on the X axis. When you rotate around an axis in 3D space (assuming you're rotating at a perfect angle), the axis you're rotating around is the one in which your coordinate remains the same. So if you view a car tire from the front, it rotates on the X axis because a point on the tire will move up and down and forward and backward but not left or right.
