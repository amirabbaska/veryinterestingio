• SyntheticAperture

    Any time you are trying to find something rare, the problem is not finding it, it is false positives. Think of a guy on a beach with a metal detector. There is a gold coin somewhere on the beach, but if there are a thousand pennies scattered about, and you have to spend five minutes digging each time you get a hit, all your effort goes into digging up pennies.

    Except here "digging up pennies" are actually expensive, painful, and possibly deadly operations. Better to search only when you are pretty sure of the location of the gold coin.
