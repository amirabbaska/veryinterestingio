      • jinxbob

        For reference, 12kg of TNT is about 48MJ of energy, which is just over 1kg of petrol (gasoline). The nature of the energy release is important, if its converted effectively into a rapidly expanding hot pressure wave, it would behave like TNT.

        A more likely scenario is that the plasma radiates (and conducts, convects) thermal energy to the shell of the reactor. If the energy transfer is slow enough, thermal conductivity of the shell is high enough and its adequately cooled, nothing will happen. If the energy transfer is much faster then the conduction of heat away from the shell, parts of the shell will ablate info the plasma cooling it.
