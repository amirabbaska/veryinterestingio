    Everything other than your last line is unsupported, and based on most of our understanding, outright wrong. Can you point to some evidence as to your theory of being fixed in place having that effect?

    As to the last line, fundamentally, plants have a much lower cost of genetic maintenance than animals do, because they are by definition, autotrophs, i.e. they make their own food. So essentially they can turn air, water, sun, and some soil nutrients into whatever they want. So having a larger genome has low cost to them. For animals, who have to acquire all nutrition by finding other organisms to devour, the cost of a bloated genetic base is high, and so from the very beginning of their lineages, there has been strong selection pressure to be more genetically efficient.
