    RCA - Recycled Concrete Aggregate, is basically broken up concrete used as a base for asphalt paving or sidewalk concrete. It is typically cheaper than bringing in new crushed stone or other stone mixes to a jobsite.

    Asphalt millings are what happens when you grind up an old roadway. Millings can be brought back to an asphalt plant to be recycled into a new asphalt mix where it is considered "RAP" or recycled asphalt pavement. RAP can be incorporated into a new asphalt mix, or the millings can be placed loose in an area where you want to fortify the soil, like a large gravel parking lot. You wont get the same quality asphalt surface you would normally get if everything is hot mixed and rolled, but it will bind up over time and give you a little better wearing surface than just dirt.
