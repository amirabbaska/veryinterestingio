        The top example is awful advice and partially the reason people have an issue. I mean it is commonly (albeit incorrectly) "it was me/her/him" to the question "who did this?" Thus breaking the device.

        You should absolutely use the substitution method, but only after you employ this rule first:

        "Who" (does the verb) to "whom"?

        Which is to also say: The object of the verb -> whom Subject if the verb -> who

        Much easier to remember and even a five year old can check easily if their usage is correct or not.

        Whom ate the sandwich? Wrong The sandwich ate whom? Correct.

        Finally, if you aren't sure, default to who, you'll sound pretentious using whom incorrectly.
