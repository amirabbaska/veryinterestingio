    • vwlsmssng

      Common in continental European crossings is continuous ticking sound that changes pace when it is safe to cross. If you can here the ticking you know you are at the crossing and the system is working; faster ticking and the signal is in your favour.

      In the UK they sometimes have a beeping sound that accompanies the green man safe to cross visual indicator, however this doesn't work for deaf-blind pedestrians and can confuse people if there are two nearby crossings. So on many crossings there is a small cone findable under the control box with the buttons to request to cross. This cone rotates when the crossing is indicating it should be safe to cross.
