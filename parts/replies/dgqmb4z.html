    Your standard moisturiser (from a bottle, jar or pump) is normally going to be an oil in water emulsion. You rub the lotion on the skin, and some water will reach the skin and be trapped by the oily layer.

    Ceramides are occlusive substances so will trap water under the skin. Hyaluronic acids are humectants and draw moisture from the deeper layers of the skin.

    A "good" moisturiser will usually have a mix of water, humectants and occlusive substances.
