    • jslsyl

      的 is used for possessives, but it behaves more like the English "'s" than the similarly-pronounced French "de" because the possessed word goes after 的. It can also be used for adjectives in the structure [Adjective] 的 [Noun].

      地 means "ground". It can also be used for adverbs in the format [adverb] 地 [verb]

      得 is often used to describe the effects of an action in the format [verb] 得 [adjective], as in "Subject verbed the object until it became adjective."

      Note: Noun-person, place, thing Verb-action Adjective-Describes noun Adverb-describes adjectives and verbs

      Edit: clarified adjective 的

    • akanosora

      的得地 are three propositions in Chinese all pronounced as 'de' but used to link different types of words. Today people don't distinguish them anymore and mostly use 的 in general.
