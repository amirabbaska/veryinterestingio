      A couple thing to keep in mind here.

      1. Taiwan has never claimed independence. They claim to be the government of all of China.

      2. Republicans were historically pro-Taiwan. Nixon shifted his position because he said we can't leave a billion people in angry isolation--i.e. the negative consequences of refusing to recognize the government of a billion people instead of the government of 15 million outweighed any positive benefit.

      3. Jimmy Carter normalized relations with China, not Nixon. Nixon formed the communiques that were the basis, but the details were not done under his administration. The U.S. did stop opposing China's entry into the United Nations under Nixon, though they did have poor George Bush there to try to argue that Taiwan should also get a seat that the world rejected pretty easily.

      4. Ronald Reagan criticized normalization with China, even though he accepted it upon taking office.

      5. I would say the U.S. opposes independence in most cases. The U.S. is not pro- Tibetan independence, Dagestan independence, Kurdistan independence, South Ossentian independence, Western Saharan independence, and so on. The U.S. supported Kosovar independence, which is another insanely complicated can of worms that I really don't want to get into.
