        That's kinda misinformation. Local anaesthetic can block all kinds of nerves, sensoric and motor neurons.

        Now these nerves have different thickness of insulation and the pain fibers, also called c-fibers, are completely naked.

        So a normal dose for pain reduction will not be sufficient to block the thicker nerves and thus kinda preventing total loss of sensory and motor function.

        It is however, possible to just use a more potent anaesthetic, or just more of it and block every nerve in the area resulting in a blockage of nerves of all qualities.

        It's not a matter of affinity, but a matter of dose.
