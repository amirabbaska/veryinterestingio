      But you can't ever remove a proton or add from/to a nucleus except by nuclear reaction, which involves tremendous energy, and usually nuclear waste.

      Proton conduction is motion of existing ionized hydrogen (H+) nuclei in solution of liquid or gas. Car batteries' reaction does move atoms around like that:

      PbO2 + 4H+ + SO4-- + 2e- -> PbSO4 + 2H2O and Pb + SO4-- -> PbSO4 + 2e-

      But it's not done in solid conductors. Actually electromigration does mean some metals' atoms (and thus their protons) creep along with electron flow, but it's a very minor, incidental effect.
