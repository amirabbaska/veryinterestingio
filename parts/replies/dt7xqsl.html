    Yes, it is. It's from the caesura.

    Play is a right facing arrow because we read right to left (derp!) left to right (thanks, /u/ShipBobbin ) in the West, it's a cultural thing. Other UX elements have right facing arrows to mean "next" or "progress" for similar reasons. Rewind is, of course, the opposite.

    Eject is an line and an up arrow from old top loading VCRs.

    Stop is a box because it's the only simple geometric shape which wasn't already used (a circle was, originally, power off, but then became record) and it looked like a "more complete" pause.
