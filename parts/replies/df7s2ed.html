    Adding to this, the separation of hot and cold is required thermodynamically for any system of this type to produce usable work. Whether it's a Stirling engine on your coffee mug, a solar cell under the hot sun, or a thermocouple radiating heat into cold space, a temperature difference is there.

  • TacoPi

    To elaborate on why a heat gradient is needed:

    Kinetic energy is easy to harvest when moving in any one direction but thermal energy is moving in every direction. For the same reason that water rapidly switching directions in a stream is bad for the function of a watermill, the particles' kinetic energies composing thermal energy is useless when random.

    With a heat gradient made, the thermal energy pushes outward from the hot area to the cold area and that energy can be used. This is true for Newtonian physics with a Carnot Engine but also true for RTG's using the Seebeck effect to have parts only moving at the atomic level.
