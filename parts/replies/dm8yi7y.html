    • Jammersy

      I haven't looked at this particular weather map, but - a cold front typically is a high pressure system. If the storm is mashed between yet another front, it's going to be squeezed and dump rain even faster.

      Collisions of warm and cold fronts generally cause storms to form, as it is the cooling of warm, wet (and low pressure) air that causes storms in the first place.
