• PatFromQc

    This was the worst moment of my life... the 1st month! Thinking it might grow louder. Actually, I was the problem... since I was focussing on the noise so much, my brain became really sensitive to the frequency. Gliding my finger over a sheet of paper was pearcing my ear. People talking felt like they were screaming to me. I was not suicidal, but I would have gladly welcomed death at that time... What saved me was to stop seeing this noise as a threat, but a friend... and to manage that, I started having conversation with it before going to sleep. The noise became a very talkative friend to whomI would speak about my day. Eventually, It dropped to the point where I had to really focus to hear it.

    Now, 20 years later, stress is the only thing that can make it worst for me, but knowing about it helps me to stop focusing on it. Sleeping with a fan helps and when the noise gets louder because of stress or alcohol, just breath heavily and steadily and focus on the sound of your breathing.

  • uk-ite

    Same. I had always dreaded getting tinnitus. I suffer quite badly from claustrophobia and the idea of having some permanent ringing in the ears that you couldn't escape from terrified me.

    I ended up having to have brain surgery for a tumour, a consequence of which was a went permanently deaf in one ear, but also developed tinnitus in that ear.

    It was awful at first, I didn't believe it would ever get better, and being deaf in that ear meant I couldn't even play something to try and mask it. I was in a constant state of panic, it was like I was trapped in a lift with no possibility of escape.

    It has gotten better over time - I still notice it but I can tune it out most of the time. Some days it is worse than others but the fear has gone.
