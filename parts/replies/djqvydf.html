  • earthwormjimwow

    You don't end up with increased color resolution, it's still a 4:2:0 image. You would have to re-encode, and try to interpolate the correct color information, at the downsampled resolution, based on your higher sampled 4k data. This requires error-diffusion and dithering. This is not done on the fly when you look at Youtube 4k videos on a 1080p screen.

    4K videos simply look better because the bit rate is way higher than 1080p is usually streamed at. Even after accounting for the extra resolution, the bitrate is a lot more.
