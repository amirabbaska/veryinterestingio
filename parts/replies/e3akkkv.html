    • BigBarnum

      The huge mass curves space-time and things 'roll' on top of it, light included. In the absence of this huge mass, space-time is 'flat' and 'things' propagate in a straight manner : their velocities remained unchanged along their trajectory, they don't experience acceleration, and we interpret this as an absence of gravity.

      As soon as you introduce any kind of energy (and mass is a form of energy), it curves space time, velocities are altered when 'things' enter this space and that's gravity.
