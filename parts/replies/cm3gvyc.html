    You are correct. "Moon" is not a proper astronomical term. Natural satellite is the correct term for what many consider a moon. Moon (Luna) is only the name of one of earth's natural satellites. While the earth has many natural satellites, most are too small to be of significance beyond ensuring they are not going to hit one of our artificial satelittes. The term "Earth's sole Moon" is correct not because it's earth's only satellite, but because the satellites name is moon. Now in order for a natural satellite to have a natural satellite of its own, it must have more gravitational influence on an object than its host planet. This is possible but would be very rare to naturally occur. We can see it artificially whenever we put something in lunar orbit.

        Earth doesn't have any other natural satellites, but some objects do appear to orbit it from the Earth's perspective because the orbits overlap so much.
