    • EternitySphere

      Your body will also increase other chemicals in what the body thinks of as "Ultimate Survivor" mode. Those chemicals, like adrenaline, epinephrine, dopamine, etc all play a role in giving you a sharp sense of focus, drive, and to some degree, "invincibility". Your pain threshold will rise, time will feel slower because you're able to make many more decisions in the same amount of time that you could only make a few before.

      Epinephrine and dopamine play levels in encoding memories. People also have a tendency of remembering tramatic events, specifically those where your body has dumped those chemicals into your body.

      I still remember the phone call I had with my parents on 9/11, they were in New York the morning of the attacks. I had tried to call them several times and was pacing the floor. I can recall the conversation and sounds like it was 10 mins ago. They were driving through the whole mess when I got the call from my mother that they were ok, while I heard my father in the background yelling at firefighters/cops asking if there was anything he could do to help or if anyone needed anything.

      There are some memories you will carry with you forever when those situations occur. A contributing factor is your bodies chemistry.
