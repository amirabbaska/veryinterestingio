    One tiny comment - not for your benefit (since you clearly understand this), but for the benefit of others.

    This sentence kind of makes it sound like you match your speed to your altitude, when the better (IMO) way to think about it is that your orbital speed is your orbital altitude. You go higher by accelerating, and lower by decelerating (forward is up, up is back, back is down, down is forward).

    Yeah, this is a very tiny, subtle distinction (and again, you're absolutely right in what you said), but it's something that I've seen a lot of people get hung up on (the idea that you get to orbit by going up), so I think it's useful to really emphasize that your angular velocity is your orbit. Altitude only matters insofar as being too low means you'll run into people, mountains, air, or the planet - which will knock you out of orbit.
