  • slackinfux

    Now the hard drive issue wasn't an electronics problem at all, rather it was mechanical. The issue was stiction in the motor that spins up the platters due to some shitty lube on the bearings that would dry up and cause the motor to stick.

    This was common in Seagate ST series MFM hard drives back in the mid to late 80's. I worked as a tech for ComputerLand back then, we used to get these in for service all the time. The usual procedure was whack it with a rubber mallet to get the drive to spin up, then we'd back up all the data and sell the customer a new hard drive with the data restored.
