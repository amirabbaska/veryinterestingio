    • _snu_snu

      Not who you were asking, but I don't think the hypothalamus is implicated in Raynuads Disease. In normal thermoregulatory responses, the peripheral NS (e.g. skin, extremities) receives sensory information such as the sensations of heat/cold and relays this information back to the central nervous system (i.e. brain and spinal cord).

      Raynauds disease is more related to issues with vasculature and/or peripheral nervous system dysfunction that results in excessive constriction of your blood vessels.
