    A lot of people claim this to be a density problem, rather this is mostly a frame of reference problem (thanks u/mkang96). Newtons first law states that an object at rest wants to stay at rest whereas an object in motion tends to stay at motion. If the drone is flying stationary and then the car moves, indeed the drone does move to the back. But again this is relative to the frame of reference. If we are in the car it does appear that the drone is moving back. However, not accounting for air resistance, if we were an observer standing outside, the car would be the one moving but the drone would appear to be stationary. This is the reason why air in the first place would pool to the back of the car, when the car accelerates, newtons first law indicates that the air naturally wants to stay at rest.

    Likewise, if the drone was sitting in the car, (not flying) and the car is moving at a constant velocity, all objects in the car are moving at the same velocity relative to each other including the air. So when the drone starts flying, it's already technically in motion in refence to an observer outside the vehicle, but stationary inside the vehicle. Since everything is constant, there is no force on the air or the drone, so they would continue to stay at the constant velocity with the car.

    Edit-> as pointed out general relativity was changed to frame of reference.

    • TheTriscut

      It does depend on density though, since there is air involved. If you had an object the same density as air, the air around it would force it to accelerate with the car. If you have an object that that is less dense than air, a balloon for example, it will actually move relative to the car in the direction of the cars acceleration. This is because the air in the car has more momentum than the object and will want to continue moving at the same velocity "more" than the object, having the air move away from the cars acceleration relative to the car. This is all assuming the windows are closed and everything in the car has to stay in the car.
