      This specific trial is only for stage 4 brain cancer called glioblastoma, and is even then only for patients where the cancer has started growing again after conventional treatment. Because they are testing it, they are only using it on the patients with the worst prognosis. Many of the patients ended up dying from the treatment because too much of the virus was injected, which resulted in too large of an immune response. In other parts of your body, you simply get swelling as a result of a large immune response. The skull, however, does not allow for any swelling whatsoever, so too large of an immune response creates so much pressure inside the skull that the patient dies.

      The phase 1 trial was a dose escalation study to determine the optimum dosage. They started at dose level 0 and were working their way up when they realized that the high dosages were causing death. They are now in phase 2 of their trials, and they are using a dosage that is smaller than even dosage 0. The phase 2 trial says that they are using dose level negative 2, so less than even the starting dosage from the phase 1 trial.
