      • wanna_be_doc

        It really depends on the chromosome. Chromosome 21 is the second smallest autosome, so there's fewer genes on it and the inactivation/overexpression of those gene products apparently don't cause spontaneous abortion. However, the only other two autosomal trisomies that commonly occur in humans are Trisomy 13 (Patau syndrome) and Trisomy 18 (Edward syndrome), which are right smack in the middle in terms of size.

        And then as far as the sex chromosomes go, if trisomies or polyploidy occurs, it can be virtually silent. XXX females or XYY males might be totally unaware of their genotype. Although if you take away a chromosome from a girl (XO) then you get Turner syndrome, and if you give a guy an extra X, then he's a Kilinefelter male (XXY). So the human body's weird.
