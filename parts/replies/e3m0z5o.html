    (correct me if I'm wrong...)

    The term here is called chopping. It's not unusual for players to negotiate the winnings available to lower their swing, the randomness, of the tournament outcome.

    For example, Adam, Betty and Charles are the final three players in a 30 person tourney. That's great, right? A, B and C are going to be in the money at the end the night. What happens is that they might agree to split a portion or all of the prize pool, normally roughly based on their chip counts. Ok, let's say top prize is $1000, second is $500 and third is $300. And let's say Adam has the most chips, 10000. Betty has 5000 and Charles has 4000. They agree for a full chop. Therefore Adam gets about half of the total sum winnings available, uh, around $850. Betty takes $425 and Charles gets $325. (Please excuse, on mobile, can't exact math).

    Why would they do this?

    It's to lower the swing of outcomes. All players benefit when they have a more predictable outcome. Adam should probably win but he's better off with a slightly smaller but more consistent take home. Same with Betty and even Charles.

    Why not just "go for it!"? It's poker, let's gamble!

    Well, if you can lower the swing of outcomes, you'll have a more consistent return. Better to have some money all of the time than lots of money a little of the time and little money most of the time. There's some fancy math behind but it has to do with managing your bankroll.

    TL DR:. Chopping for better EV. (Inside joke, poker peeps will get it)
