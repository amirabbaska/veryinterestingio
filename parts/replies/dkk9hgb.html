    They train you to smile while you speak so you sound more friendly and open

    I'm a VO actor and this is also how you sell 'commercial' brands. Think fast food brands, mid-level clothing, etc (as opposed to luxury brands which usually require either an "attitude" or a whisper depending on the product).

    Ultimately the answer to OP's question is because we're all acting. It's the same reason why people adopt hushed sullen tones at funerals. We put on the face (or voice) we think is most appropriate for the current situation, and we've understood that average interactions with strangers for customer service purposes require pleasant, friendly attitudes.
