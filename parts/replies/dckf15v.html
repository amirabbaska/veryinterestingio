    Here's a graph of the wavelength sensitivity of the three different types of cone cells in our eyes, overlapped with the wavelength sensitivity of rod cells

    Two of the types of cone cells and the rod cells all have high sensitivity to light with wavelength in the green part of the spectrum. Rod cells do not resolve color, but they still have wavelength-based sensitivity.

    This means that the least amount of energy can be used for the greatest ability to resolve images if those images focus on green light.
