    Adding to what others said about photons carrying momentum, the full (relativistic) form of the energy equation is E^2 = (pc)^2 + (mc^2 )^2 . The form more widely recognised, E=mc^2 is a low velocity approximation, as for massive bodies with low velocity pc<<mc^2 , so the pc term is ignored in the equation and the full version reduces to the better known version.

      • SynarXelote

        Actually to be more precise, for low velocity, E=mc^2 +p^2 /2m.

        mc^2 is the mass energy and p^2 /2m=(1/2)mv^2 is the classical formula for kinetic energy.

        For a massless particle such as a photon, E=pc, with p=h.lambda and E=h.nu where nu is the frequency and lambda=c/nu is the wavelength of the particle.
