    • jkoer

      If you start from the greater trochanter, you just make an incision in the skin and soft tissue a few centimeters above the greater trochanter and don't even have to get near the joint (you can feel the bump on the side of your leg at your hip...thats the trochanter, we go just above it). And for the knee, you do have to go into the knee joint, but you can just flex the knee a bit in order to get the right angle.
