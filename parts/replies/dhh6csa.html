    • zebediah49

      I'd say it's the other way around.

      Energy in the grid is fungible -- if 30% of your generation is renewable, and 5% of the consumers demand renewable, it's trivial to "allocate" them to the rewnewable pool and say they're getting it (by "allocating" the dirty producers to other people).

      If only 1% of the total generation is renewable, and 5% of the demand is for renewable, the only way to meet that demand is to build out renewables (or maybe import it? not sure if that's a thing that counts). You can't just say "yeah, sure" and reallocate resources, if those resources don't exist.
