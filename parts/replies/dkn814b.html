    I'm an American who lives in Germany. Moving here really showed me how antiquated the US banking system is in many respects. Two things really stand out:

    • I can pay anyone in Germany electronically and they will have the money in a few hours. Anyone outside of Germany will have the money the next day.
    • Credit card processing fees are half of what US merchants pay.

    Leaving aside the monopoly that is Visa/MC, why hasn't the US advanced further regarding electronic payments. I know we have the ACH system but it doesn't seem to be available for the average user, especially when they enforce the "2 small deposits" thing that makes it useless to send a single payment to someone.

    One of the more interesting things they have here are ATM-like machines that people who don't have computers can use to pay their bills. They fill out a form with the transaction they want, put their card in the ATM machine, feed in the paper and voila, payment sent.

    Do you see that changing and how long do you think it will take?
