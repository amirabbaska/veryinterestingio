    Well... I'm not going to read the whole thread, but in my opinion that's really just half of the answer. The lesser half.

    "Bitrate" is sort of the other half, but just saying "bitrate" gives an incomplete answer. Fact of the matter is that "video" always has artifacts. But if you take 4k-rendered artifacts and scale them down, that process carries the same kind of advantage as rendering a 4k game for your 1080p monitor. In the latter case, it's a kind of anti-aliasing. Similarly, in the case of compression artifacts, you're doing a pretty good job of smoothing those artifacts over - hiding them away.

    Compared to that, the improvements inherent in effective 4:4:4 color are downright subtle.
