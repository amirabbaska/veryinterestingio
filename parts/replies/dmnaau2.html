    • _This_Is_Stupid

      Likely not. The margins on the cheap cars are in the hundreds of dollars (a 17k car certainly nets them less than a grand; closer to 200 bucks), and they'd kill their high-end market entirely. The high-end market wants to look different than the cheap cars. If you take that from them, you'd immediately remove a major selling point of the high-end cars.

      Cheap cars that look like expensive ones aren't drawing buyers from the expensive pool to the cheap pool--they're driving people from the expensive pool, for a fleeting advantage in the small pool. If everyone has a car that looks cool, the appeal of them is much diminished.

      It's not at all worth it.

  • _This_Is_Stupid

    ith a $17,000 car that earns them probably closer to $1500 profit.

    Closer to 200-300 bucks. The margins on typical cars is pretty tight; the luxury end is much more profitable.

    What you're getting at, kind of, is that they'd kill their high-end market entirely by doing what OP asks: why buy a lambo if it looks like every other car on the road? The status of it is immediately removed, and the draw of the car is immensely removed. You can't exactly drive 120 mph on most roads without winding up with felony charges (actually happened in Minneapolis/St Paul last year), can you? But you can drive envy in all those who see your car.

    If you suddenly remove that envy, why buy an expensive car? No one's really able to tell the difference, so there's no draw.

    You'd tank the sales of the high-end market, and have no real increase in sales on the standard market.
