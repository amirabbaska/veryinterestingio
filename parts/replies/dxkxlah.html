    Scott McCloud’s book “Understanding Comics,” talks about the same subject in great depth. The brain mentally paints over the gaps in the image to create meaning. It’s a fascinating read.

    Looking at drawings of women is a bit like a Rorschach test. Your brain picks up the cues that this woman is supposed to be attractive and then fills in the ambiguity with an imaginary ideal.

    Add to this the emotional factor from empathy and pathos that others have talked about, and it’s not hard to figure out.
