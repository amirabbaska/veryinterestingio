    • Petrark

      Centrifugal force is not erroneous, but only really makes sense to talk about in a rotating reference frame.

      Let's say you are the Sun, and you are rotating in place such that the Earth is always directly in front of you. From this rotating frame it appears that the Earth does not move at all. Thus, there must be a net force of zero acting on the Earth. However, we know for a fact that the Earth is experiencing centripetal force due to gravity from the Sun. Thus, in a rotating reference frame centrifugal force acts outwardly and opposite centripetal force so that the resulting net force on the Earth is zero, and thus the Earth appears stationary.

      And again, all of that is only the case when you are in a rotating reference frame. In an inertial reference frame centrifugal force doesn't exist.
