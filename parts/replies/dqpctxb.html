    "ways to extract it efficiently" is the core of the issue. When people say "we only have X years of proven reserves of commodity Y" that has a very specific meaning: as I recall a proven reserve is an area demonstrated to contain the commodity in a form which is economical to extract (ie. Sale price is higher than costing extraction).

    Decades ago no-one would have considered sucking the oil out of sand when you could just make a hole in the ground in an oil field and get liquid oil out. As time goes by two things happen: new extraction techniques make it easier/cheaper to extract, and lowering supply increases the price meaning it's now economical to extract from somewhere that previously wasn't.

    These combine to mean the end of today's "proven reserves" is not the point at which the world has run out. That's not even counting new techniques for finding reserves we didn't know were present before.
