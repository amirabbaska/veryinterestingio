    Fun fact: I am growing graphene on a copper crystal for my bachelor thesis, ad I have assisted in growing it on gold foils as well. Even though the two require very different processes, something very similar happens.

    If a crystal surface is smooth on the atomic level, it will still have step edges where each layer of atoms ends. Imagine a stair case with a couple meters between each step, like on a park walkway with a small slope. The layer of graphene will always start on these step edges, because there is a significant energetic bonus for the carbon atoms if they connect to the corners. The next carbon atoms will connect to other carbon atoms, and soon, you will have a layer.
