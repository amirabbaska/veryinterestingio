    Excellent analysis.

    To kind of piggyback, creating your own morality, while indeed what nietzsche was a proponent of can be a slippery slope. He was also a proponent of overcoming his own Humanity. "The human being is a rope strung between a beast and the overman" as he put it in Thus Spoke Zarathustra. His idea of "the will to power" is directly related to the overman. He claimed to live his life "moraine free" or without intoxicants. Dude never drank coffee or smoked. His belief in the overman was partly overcoming some of the basic human urges that we have that sets us on the same level as beasts, like lust and gluttony. By overcoming these small every day battles with the self, you can feel your will power growing "What is good? All that heightens the feeling of power in man, the will to power, power itself. What is bad? All that is born of weakness. What is happiness? The feeling that power is growing, that resistance is overcome" as he put it in the Antichrist.

    I like to think of Nietzsche as a series of thought excercises. Between the eternal recurrance, will to power, and the overman, one can create their own interpretation of morality and their definition of "the good life"
