      The definition of the temperature is

      1/T = (dS/dE), where the derivative is a partial derivative, and the other extensive quantities are held fixed. S is the entropy and E is the internal energy.

      For a classical ideal gas, the equipartition theorem gives the relation

      = 3kT/2, which allows you to relate the temperature to the average kinetic energy of the gas molecules.

  • Puteh

    The same relationship between T and KE applies to atoms in solids, liquids and gases, and indeed, between T and any variable that appears quadratically in the Hamiltonian (e.g. x^2 in the harmonic oscillator). This is an elementary result of statistical mechanics.

    If you partition energies, the relationship need not apply to the individual partitions. This has often been a source of confusion here.
