      Actually, the word "electricity" came first, and the electron was named after it. It was a Latin word (electrum, a Classical Latin noun, and electricus, a New Latin adjective) from the Greek ἤλεκτρον (elektron), meaning "amber," since (static) electricity (and electrical attraction) was a curiosity in antiquity that was produced by rubbing amber.
