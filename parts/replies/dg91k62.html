    It is quite common to find evidence of few or no cavities in ancient humans. Many cultures had not developed dental hygiene until only the modern era, though some had rudimentary brushing.

    The plaque which develops on one's teeth hardens and becomes tartar, which acts as a protective barrier against bacteria which eat through the enamel. The teeth stay relatively protected in this manner.

    However, the gums can easily develop gingivitis and eventually periodontal disease. Destroy the connective tissue & structural bone holding those teeth in place, and you can't eat, with a high chance of acute (and lethal) infection. Also...stank breath.
