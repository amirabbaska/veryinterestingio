    This is correct. You also need to know a little about the distilling process to put this into context-- distillation is the separation of alcohols from water. This is acheived by raising the temperature of your alcohol/water mixture to above the boiling temperature of alcohol, but below the boiling temperature of water. The alcohol turns into vapor then is "caught" on a cool surface and run into a collection container. For example, ethanol boils at 173 F and water at 212. So to get the ethanol out you bring the mixture to 173 F and most of the vapor that comes off is ethanol.

    Here is the catch-- besides ethanol (the good stuff), there are a bunch of trace alcohols and other compounds in your batch natural to the fermentation process . When you are distilling, your still will dive off each compound as it approaches its respecive boiling temperature. The first to come off is Methanol at 149 F (mostly Methanol, you will have trace removal of a wide range of compounds at all temperatures). This is poisonous and must be separated and discarded. Your process will not want to pass 149 F until most of the Methanol is driven off.

    Around 160 F you will get mostly light compounds like aldehydes and acetone, with some smaller percent of ethanol (distillers call the the HEADS). Next ethanol (mostly) will strip off around 173 F. These are the purest "cuts" of the distillation-- distillers call this the HEARTs. As you creep towards 195 F, you get heavier alcohols and other compounds-- distillers call this TAILS. The HEADS and TAILS can be redistlled to try and further separate the non-ethanol compounds and get more HEARTS.

    All of these cuts will have ethanol. While you can drink the HEADS (roughly 40% ethanol), HEARTS (95ish% ethanol), and TAILS (60ish % ethanol) they are not all equivlent. Usually the HEADS is reguarded as the worst part and will have an "off flavor" and give you a nasty hangover. The TAILS can be used to blend to get a more complex flavor that is sometimes desired for barrel aged spirits, however it is reguarded to give you more a hang over.

    So good vodka is almost pure ethanol and will contain all HEARTS (and distilled water to make it 40% ethanol).

    Cheap vodka will have HEADS, HEARTS, and TAILS because the producer is trying to maximize profits by not discarding distillate-- and also this gives you a wicked hangover. This is true for most cheap alcohols. The off flavors can be covered with sugar or flavoring, but as others have mentioned, these also may contribute towards a hangover.
