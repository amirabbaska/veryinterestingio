      Yes. If there was such a thing as an absolute zero speed, it would also have absolute zero movement which would make that mass attain absolute zero.

      Of course, there is no way to have an absolute frame of reference so you'll never be moving at absolute zero speed, and the uncertainty principle means that you can't have an object at absolute zero temperature or energy because you could then predict its position with 100% accuracy, which is not permitted in quantum mechanics. In QM, those sorts of things are always probabilities, there is always some little bit of jitter or uncertainty as to where a particle actually is until the waveform collapses.
