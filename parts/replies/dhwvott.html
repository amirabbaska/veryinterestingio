• LullabyforElla

    This is the best answer. World class conductors are able to teach the finest detail and nuance of the music while high school conductors (myself being one) mainly have to focus on the nuts and bolts of technique. And discipline.

    My favorite saying to get my students to play more expressively is "Don't just play the notes, play the music." There's so much more there than what's printed; a great conductor focuses on this more than anything. When I was a student some of the best conductors I played for had spent hours and hours studying the music and researching past performances. They knew EVERY nuance of EVERY part and how to get the most excessive performance out of EVERY performer.

    High school conductors spend most of their time just getting kids to play the right notes. No joke. (Of course that depends on the ability of the group)

    What s/he said. Same with choir directors.

    I sing in a community choir (auditioned, though not paid), and our director has a doctorate in Conducting. In the years I've known him, I've seen him direct everything from elementary and middle-school kids, to festival choirs, to university choirs - which is his day job - and also some orchestras. Who he's working with makes a huge difference in how he directs.

    Incidentally, he's the most into nuance when directing the university choir. We grownups singing for fun don't practice as much and aren't nearly as disciplined.
