      • OpalBanana

        While plausible, due to the connotations that are associated with "chance", it can lead to strange conclusions.

        Lets play a card game called "Ace of Spades". We both get half a deck for a hand. We both have to play any one card from our hand (can choose). If you play ace of spades, you win. If neither player plays ace of spades, you swap hands, and continue play. In practice, this is a game of 100% chance, because you will just look through your hand, and if you got ace of spades you win.

        However, a random player versus perfect player: Random player will lose 50% of the games (when perfect player gets the ace). They will lose 25/26 (96%) of games where they have the ace, when they fail to use "skill" to play the ace of spades.

        With this, we can deduce a perfect player only has a 1.92% chance of losing. Implying this is a game largely of skill, and not chance.
