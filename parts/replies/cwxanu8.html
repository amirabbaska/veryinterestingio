    In addition to other things that people have been pointing out, another thing that you missed is that the job market is not static. Teenagers are entering the job market every day - how in the hell is someone who is 15 years old going to be able to get a job at $15/hour? My first job was bagging groceries - no way in hell the employer could have afforded to pay me $15/hr to do that. The minimum wage acts as a barrier to entry to the job market for young people. Once you have the minimum wage job, you can work your way up from there. Raising the minimum wage makes this much, much more difficult.
