  • PaleBluePuck

    It goes beyond antibodies. Some roundworms (parasites) that frequently infect humans (but are no longer found in developed countries) produce interleukins and other peptides that interact directly with the immune system. The "Old Friends Hypothesis" argues that our immune system evolved and developed with the constant "cross-talk" with roundworm immune signaling molecules. When we removed these parasites, it threw the system somewhat out of balance which produces a variety of undesirable immune responses, which manifest as autoimmune diseases in additional to allergies.
