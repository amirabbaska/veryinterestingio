    It's multiple things. Dirt or oil can be burned out that may be stuck in the nut and thread. Another thing is that even if a metal says "stainless steel" the purity levels of said steels are likely to be very different, and so different expansion rates come into play that allows for gap formation as well. Finally, when you heat the metal it makes it far softer. As a result, you can remove a bolt or screw and any previous resistance it would have had at room temp can be negated as the softer metal can be either compressed or bent to allow for it to be removed. All sorts of.forces come into play :)!
