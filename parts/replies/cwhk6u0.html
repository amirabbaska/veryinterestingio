    The dollar was weak, and Peugeots were expensive for what you got.

    that's probably the main point. cars are usually sold cheaper in the united states than in europe. a european car maker not only has to deal with the costs for shipping, changing cars for american regulations, additional advertising and the costs of using a competitor's dealer network, but in the end the cars are also facing lower priced competition than they have in europe. that usually doesn't leave much of a profit margin.

    if then the exchange rates shift for the worse, you make losses by default. consequently, most european car makers withdrew from the u.s. market during the 80s or early 90s.
