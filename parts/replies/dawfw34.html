    • Ximitar

      Misspelled words, subjective language and any other signs of a lack of editing makes credibility go down.

      This should be your first sign in any written piece, but be careful not to confuse technically good writing (where everything is in the right place, commas are well distributed, apostrophes are used properly, ditto capitalisation etc.) with objectively good writing. Lots of really shitty sources (Breitbart, Salon, Observer) tend to have well-written pieces.

      Generally, however, if something is competently written and/or edited then you ought to weight it a little more towards 'credible'. It's a very handy rule of thumb.

      Source: former editor.
