    • MaunoBrau

      I'm not entirely sure. This can go either way. If you are in a state of learning something new, then your brain will be alert, so that you don't miss something. This can cause you to take longer to settle down. I find that once I'm in the "brain full" state, doing something repetitive, monotonous, or not new-to-me can cause me to fall asleep quickly. So, I guess I would say that the more new stuff you learn in a day, the easier it will be to fall asleep, as long as it is not right before bed such that you are still in an exited, aware, ready state.
