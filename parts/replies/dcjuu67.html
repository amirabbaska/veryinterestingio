    This is correct. I wish people would stop up voting the other incorrect comments saying it's logarithmic.

    It's very simple. The time constant of an RC (resistor+capacitor) circuit is, well, RC (resistance times capacitance). This is the time it takes for the system to respond to a signal.

    The resonant frequency is just 1/(RC). Therefore when you change the resistance (or capacitance) with the dial, the frequency changes as the inverse.

    For a circuit with an inductor, it's a bit more complicated since the resonant frequency is sqrt(1/LC). Still the same concept though.
