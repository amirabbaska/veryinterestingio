    • Meron35

      Very unlikely. Japan's culture with regards to personal finance is drastically different to most of the anglosphere. They had a very deep recession caused by an asset bubble burst in the 90s, which means Japanese citizens/investors are very averse to risk. In particular, their real estate market went to absolute crap so most people don't consider home ownership as a very safe investment. In addition, Japanese people really like new homes, so the homes immediately start to depreciate as soon as you buy them - sort skin to the car market is western countries. A fair few banks also either went broke, restricted deposit withdrawals or simply defaulted on deposits during the aforementioned recession, so many Japanese people are quite wary of banks. Thus, many of them turned to the safest, yet still easily accessible investment: Japanese and US government bonds. It's a really difficult situation with no real solution.
