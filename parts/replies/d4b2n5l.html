        • featherfooted

          It's not a weird gimmick of base 10. Look at the first line of the equality again:

          (10x+y) - (10y+x) = 10x+y - 10y-x

          If we were in anything that wasn't base 10, (i.e. base B):

          (Bx+y) - (By+x) = Bx+y - By-x
          = Bx - x + y - By
          = (B - 1)x - (B - 1)y
          = (B - 1)(x-y)

          so the difference of any two-digit number and its transpose (reverse) in any base B is always a multiple of (B - 1).
