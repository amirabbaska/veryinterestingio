    • ididnoteatyourcat

      Sure if you could modulate the current with the frequencies of visible light, you would produce visible light. No mystery there. But if you are wondering why we don't produce visible light this way, it is because we don't know how to modulate currents with that high of a frequency. The frequency of visible light is extraordinarily high. In the hundreds of trillions of cycles per second. Even the cpu's in your computer don't oscillate any higher than a few billion cycles per second.

    • ididnoteatyourcat

      Virtual particles do not have a speed. What you might be referring to is the heuristic using the time-energy uncertainty relation to approximate the range of a force given the mass of the mediator. This was famously used by Yukawa to estimate the mass of the pion associated with the nuclear force.
