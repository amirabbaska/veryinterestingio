    • Unholyhair

      Depressive realism is not considered to be an illness in and of itself according to the DSM-V. As a result, there are technically no "symptoms" as such. A depressive realist may be someone who is depressed because of their realistic evaluation of the world around them, or it might be that they are depressed and therefore have a more realistic outlook on life. The reality is that, beyond being an observed phenomenon, depressive realism is not really well understood or classified.
