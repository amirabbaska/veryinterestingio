        This is very very wrong.

        Harvestman "spiders" are not true spiders and do not have venom glands at all. Obviously it's difficult to have "the most potent venom in the spider kingdom" when 1) they aren't spiders, and 2) they don't have venom. They are perfectly capable of biting humans. Harvestmen do not eat other spiders, they eat mostly decaying matter.

        Cellar spiders are actually spiders. However, they are also perfectly capable of biting a human, although they almost never do. Their venom is not any more potent than any other average spider - which is to say, it isn't very potent, and certainly not dangerous to humans. Mythbusters tested this by having one of them stick their arm into a container full of them and getting bit (it stung a bit; nothing else happened) Many species are known to hunt and eat other spiders.
