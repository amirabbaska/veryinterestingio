      • RobusEtCeleritas

        Are they physical?

        Yes, they're passing through you right now. At sea level, it's mostly muons.

        How do they travel through metres of 'earth' and get detected?

        They are moving very fast, and sparsely interact with matter. But there are a lot of them, and if you record data with a large array of detectors for long periods of time, you rack up a lot of cosmic ray events.
