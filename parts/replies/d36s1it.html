      Most people are replying to you on why 360 can be easily divided into whole numbers, but not really why it was important.

      Today we have calculators and division algorithms and modern fractions.

      Instead of using these, the Babylonian scribes would write out tables for others to use, consisting of of pieces of 60 that each division represented. In one column on the table would be a number and the other would have the corresponding part of 60. For example:

      Divisor Pieces
      2 30
      3 20
      4 15
      5 12
      6 10
      8 7,30

      It all appears to make sense until you get to 8. The thing here is that they were working in base 60, and the comma is kind of like a decimal place, but not. It means that 8×(7 + 30/60) = 60.

      It gets a bit more confusing with other numbers, where 27's reciprocal is 2,13,20, or in other words, 27×[2 + 13/60 + (20/60)/60] = 60.
