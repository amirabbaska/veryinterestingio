      It is possible to trace lineages of descent in various human groups through things like mitochondrial DNA and the Y-Chromosome. There are seven main Y-DNA haplogroups among Europeans and European-descended peoples, for instance, however, these don't line up with any 'races' as we understand them. While different phenotypic traits certainly do predominate in certain areas, there's frequently just as much (if not more) genetic variation WITHIN a 'race' than between races.
