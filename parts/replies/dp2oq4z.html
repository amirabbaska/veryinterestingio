        You can quote it all you like, but the physics of it doesn't make sense.

        Granted, I only know this from sous vide cooking, but if you look at this thickness chart, it takes a 5 mm thick steak 5 minutes to get up to the temperature of the bath its in. Given that the center is 2.5 mm away, that's how long it takes for the heat to penetrate that far.

        Similarly, the reverse is happening when you're cooling a burn. 2.5 mm is the depth of a typical second degree burn (partial penetration into the dermis), hence 5 minutes. If you need to get 20 minutes of cooling, i.e. 5 mm in, that's a third degree burn, bordering on a fourth degree burn. At that point, it's made it into fat layers. As I said:
