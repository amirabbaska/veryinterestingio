    • microli

      It's true that the probability would eventually hit zero for an isolated system, but in the real world there's no such thing as a truly isolated system. Also note, however, that the Boltzmann distribution is derived with the assumption that the system comes to equilibrium by being in contact with an effectively infinite heat reservoir, which is why it gives non-zero probabilities for all possible energies. In reality no system is at true Boltzmann equilibrium, but the differences are often far to small to be measurable.
