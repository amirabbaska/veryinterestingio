    For those looking for more information, learn up on "Single Sign On" - its essentially this. Websites that aren't facebook/google/apple can't possibly maintain a level of security and ubiquity that those big companies can. Virtually no one has an account to "joes forum" and virtually everyone has a facebook or google account. Offloading login to those services allows faster login times, easier collection of necessary data for said account, and basically allows them to totally ignore one of the hardest parts of creating a new service: authentication.

    Do google and facebook collect more information about the people using their services? 100% yes. If you aren't willing to make these connections for them, simply don't use whatever service wants you to do this. That's the trade off. "Joe's Forum" literally doesn't care about your moral stand against sharing your data with facebook or google - that's not their business. Many a time I have decided making the connection to facebook simply isn't worth trying to get access to whatever service needs it - other times I have. It just depends on how much you care in that moment.
