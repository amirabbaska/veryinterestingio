      Your wife was relatively fortunate that 'rough massage' stopped the bleeding.

      The next step is bimanual pressure — an obstetrician or midwife puts a fist up the vagina and compresses the uterus between that and a hand on the lower abdomen. Meanwhile, blood samples are taken for an urgent crossmatch, an operating theatre is organised and various drugs given to force the uterus to contract. If that's not working, it's off to theatre for a laparotomy to look for internal tears; wrapping of the uterus to try and make it contract; full suction bottles and blood product transfusions galore ... and if necessary to save a life, a hysterectomy. This is sometimes where it goes wrong — it's tempting to leave the hysterectomy until too late, hoping to preserve the ability to have another child.

      Pregnant women bleed like firehoses when they do bleed. We're talking splatter film levels of gore. Not to be taken lightly at all.
