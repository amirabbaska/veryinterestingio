      I believe that yall are talking about the same thing; "scratchy" actually sounds worse than "burning" to me when it comes to food.

      I posit that people who are bothered by the sensation are more likely to label it as a "burning" sensation, whereas people who are not bothered at all by it are still feeling the same sensation, but just never label it in such an unpleasant way.

      It's "tingly"; but you show me the human being who says that carbonated-water feels flat, instead of tingly, and I will show you a lizard-person in disguise.

      To make a slightly analogous observation, you know the feeling commonly described as "pins-and-needles", which some people describe using words like "unbearably painful"? Yeah I feel the same thing, but I am almost never even bothered by it. I have had it hurt before, and it's still the exact same feeling. The difference really does seem to be "all in your head", just down to the very subjective experience of pain.

      So rather than seeing much evidence for a difference between people, besides that which is already very subjective like pain, what I see here is simply evidence for a failure of people to communicate their common experiences.
