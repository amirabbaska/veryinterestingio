    My understanding is that Titans are two generations removed from chaos.

    At first there was nothing; chaos, the void and absence of all things. Then Gaia was formed. Gaia gave birth to Uranus. Uranus and Gaia got it on and had the Titans (among other things).

    But Uranus was pissy, and locked the Titans in Tartarus. This pissed off Gaia. She gave Cronus a sickle and he castrated Uranus.

    Now the Titans come to power. Rhea and Cronus have the Olympians, but Cronus is deservedly skeptical of children at this point. He eats all of the Olympians as they're born. Rhea gets pissy and tricks Cronus into eating a stone instead of Zeus. Zeus grows up and beats the crap out of Cronus, and he pukes up the other Olympians. Then the Olympians overthrow the Titans.

    Where do humans fight into the narrative? The mythology of humanity is split into five (or four) ages. The origin age is the Golden Age, when the Earth produced enough food for everyone. The Golden Age ends when Prometheus (a Titan) steals fire from Olympus to give to humanity. Zeus chains Prometheus to his rock and creates Pandora's Box to reduce humanity from their god-like state.

    So the Titans actually ruled over humanity during the Golden Age, not a chaotic period.
