    • fchnr

      Stars are essentially blackbody radiators ( http://hyperphysics.phy-astr.gsu.edu/hbase/wien.html#c1 ) so different coloured stars would likely always have energy within the visible spectrum.

      Additionally, if you were on the surface of a non-gaseous planet that had some refractive rainbow-causing particulates in the air, you wouldn't be able to visibly see a shift in colour of the rainbow. Only with scientific instrumentation could you see a non-smooth spectra. But that's already present here on earth ( http://earthobservatory.nasa.gov/Features/RemoteSensing/remote_04.php)
