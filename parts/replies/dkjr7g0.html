    A good place to see this 100% reflection is looking up at the surface of a pool while submerged. While looking straight up, you can easily see the sky but past a certain angle all you can see is the reflected bottom of the pool. Also, fiber optic cables use this principle. The core has a different refractive index than the cladding although both are glass. if light is shot into the core at the right angle, you get total internal reflection. You can actually allow the light to escape from the middle of a fiber optic cable by introducing a sharp bend (not breaking it!). The sharp bend will increase the angle that the light strikes the core/cladding boundary to the point where the reflection drops below 100% and the light will just fly right on outta there.
