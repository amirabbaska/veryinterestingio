  • Junkaccnt

    It is mostly due to the fact that we have a lot of rivers and bays around here emptying silt and organic matter into the Mississippi Sound. Also the barrier islands basically make this part of the gulf a large bay in itself and the lack of wave action keeps the water from being pulled out and replaced with "clean" water. The one advantage that comes with this is we have great fishing as the Mississippi Sound (called the Fertile Crescent for a reason) is a great breeding ground for sea life.
