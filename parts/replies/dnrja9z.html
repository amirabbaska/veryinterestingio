      Whose momentum has to be preserved?

      The total momentum needs to be preserved. Before the collision the total momentum is the momentum of the moving proton (when shooting at a stationary target). With all particles at rest the total momentum is zero.

      Is it because there is now more mass, that all the particles don't need to be traveling as fast to contain the same momentum?

      Not sure what to answer here. I have difficulty to reason about kinematics in special relativity intuitively myself. I just cling to the math :)

      When particles collide head on, does the conservation of momentum say the result can be stationary saving you that energy?

      Yeah, when they collide head on with equal but opposite momenta the total momentum before the collision is zero so it has to be zero after the collision as well.

      This can mean that all four particles are at rest. It also can mean that the momenta just add up to zero (for example the particles fly into four different directions at a 90Â° angle with the same speed).

      I think that they can be zero is not really interesting in general but is handy here because we are trying to figure out the threshold energy, so we are calculating the initial kinetic energy assuming that all particles are at rest after the collision.
