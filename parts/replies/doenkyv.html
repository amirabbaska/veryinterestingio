      This is something I hear about a lot when I help people move to a new house and there are stairs involved. The question is usually along the lines of "How can you carry so much up the stairs, and I so little?". (I'll leave the implications if this line of questions aside here...)

      Consider this: you weigh 70kg, I weigh 120 and we are supposed to lift 30kg up the stairs. You lift 50% extra weight up the stairs, I lift 25% extra. Yes, I carry 50kg more up the stairs than you do, I always do that, but that also mean that any added weight adds less for me compared to my normal.

      So far about capacity in kg, but in the moving scenario there is also the question of staying power. I lift 50kg more than you each time I go up the stairs, so it better be worth it! I can't do as many round trips as you can, so if 1 lamp needs to be hoisted, you go. Simply put: I can't go as many times as you can, so I better make each trip count. I normally lift more than you, because taking 1 extra round trip costs more to me. So in my every day life I lift heavier than you do and thus get more training with heavier weights.
