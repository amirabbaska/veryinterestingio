    • Dave37

      Our atmosphere consists of mostly nitrogen gas and oxygen gas. These are molecules, consisting of several atoms (2 atoms in this case). But each atom nitrogen atom/oxygen atom is also a lot heavier than a hydrogen atom (14 times & 16 times respectively).

      So it depends on how you count. If you compare the number of particles (molecules), then there is about 2.547x10^(19) particles/cm^(3). If we instead focus on the number of atoms, we would have to double that to 5.094x10^(19) atoms/cm^(3). Lastly, if we're interested in doing a mass comparison and convert all the atoms in the atmosphere to hydrogen atoms, then we end up with 7.351x10^(20) hydrogen atom mass equivalents per cm^(3).

      This is calculated for a global average temperature of 288.15K (15^(o)C) and sea level pressure of 101325Pa (1 Atm).
