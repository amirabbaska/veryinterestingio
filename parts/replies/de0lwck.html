    There is a difference between mens rea and just motive though.

    Someone can know something is illegal, but not feel it is immoral (stealing bread for your family to eat). At the same time something can be illegal regardless of motive.

    And I'm pretty sure you will never see the value on an item adjusted because of motive, you would have additional rewards for a civil case though. But the item in question is the same value either way, the additional costs are supposed to reflect actual losses (couldn't make it to work because you slit my tires, that's worth another day's pay on the recovery), not the defendants motives.
