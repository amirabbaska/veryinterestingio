  • Farnsworthson

    Not just that, but basic mechanics. It's extremely difficult to make a hinge bend by pulling straight through the joint itself; mostly what you achieve is to put pressure on the joint. You need some degree of sideways pull. Simplistically, the further away from the centre line you apply the force (i.e. the pull through the tendon), the more leverage you get, and the less stress you put directly on the joint. So having a basic design of fingers that are naturally slightly bent when relaxed makes good sense - the pull starts off-centre, it takes way less energy to start them bending further, and puts less direct pressure on/through the joints.
