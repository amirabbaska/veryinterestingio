    • Soranic

      Nope. Part of the problem with uranium is the poisons that build up. The daughter products of an induced fission actually shut down the chain reaction. Eventually it's too difficult to maintain criticality at end of life (and have it be a safe fuel assembly at beginning of life too) that the fuel is removed from the core.

      We could reprocess it, remove the poisons, but there are treaties and laws restricting that in some countries. Also, making fuel from fresh or may actually be more economical than recycling.

      Fusion doesn't really have the issue with daughter products stopping the reaction.
