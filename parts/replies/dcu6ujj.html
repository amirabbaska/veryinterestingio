  • OldGuyzRewl

    I think this is probably the most correct answer.

    Lead acid batteries have been known to leak sulfuric acid. This was especially true before sealed batteries came into wide use.

    Sulfuric acid attacks concrete. For the chemically inclined readers, it reacts with the calcium silicate, producing calcium sulfate, which is highly water soluble, and will leave craters in the floor where the concrete was eaten away.
