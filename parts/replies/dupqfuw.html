    • Defgarden

      Large debt can be manageable with low interest rates. But if rates start to go up, a country ends up spending way more to service that debt, which necessitates either more revenues (taxes), spending cuts, or more borrowing (at higher rates). If left unchecked, a country runs into a debt spiral, where the debt itself costs more than normal spending.
