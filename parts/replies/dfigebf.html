    Algorithms are slower than heuristics, they are more specific in scope

    ? Algorithms are way, way faster than human heuristics. The tradeoff is the second part, that they are specific in scope. They're "dumb", in the sense they cannot handle tasks that depart in the most minute ways from assumed parameters.

    So, coming up with an algorithm (a solution that works every time, and encompasses all reasonable use cases) is much harder than coming up with a heuristic.

    But an algorithm that works in most cases is much easier to come up with than an equally good heuristic. That's why computers use algorithms, it's easy to come up with algorithms that cover most usage cases, and then we try to restrict the task to what our algorithms can handle.
