    • 4nsicdude

      A witness can, a forensic examiner can only testify to the facts, evidence, procedures used in the lab, and their training. There are exceptions but they're uncommon.

      ex. I have 10 pictures of cats on a computer, one of which is a lion.

      Def: "According to your report there are 10 pictures of cats on this computer, is that correct"?

      me: "That is correct"

      Def: "Did the lion kill my client's dog"

      me: "I cannot answer that question, the evidence I examined did not pertain to the death of the deceased dog."

      Def: "Could a lion kill my client's dog"

      Pros: "Objection your honor, it is not Examiner XYZ's area of specialty and any answer would be speculative conjecture".

      Judge: "Sustained."

      And that's pretty much how testimony goes. I could have very well found an email that said "I'm going to feed that dog to my pet lion" but if nobody asks about it I can't forward that information to the jury.
