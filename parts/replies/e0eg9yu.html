  • gloria_monday

    Close, but I don't think that's quite right. After the gap forms, the bottom surface of the beer smacks into the glass bottom of the bottle. Water is very nearly incompressible, so all of that momentum is transferred in a very short amount of time. This is known as a 'water hammer' and is illustrated in this xkcd. The force can sometimes be high enough to blow out the bottom out of the bottle. If the bottle doesn't break, it will create a very intense pressure wave that propagates back through the beer. It's that pressure wave that catalyzes bubble formation.
