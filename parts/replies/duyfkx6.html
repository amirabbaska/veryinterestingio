    Yes, "age" doesn't make any sense when talking about what galaxies are primarily made of: dark matter and hydrogen gas. Although we don't know what dark matter is, we have no evidence that it "ages" and, barring the possibility of proton decay on much, much, much longer timescales than the age of the universe, hydrogen gas does not "age". Stars, however, being dynamic objects that fuse hydrogen into heavier elements and seed the surrounding medium with heavy elements via supernovae and neutron star collisions, do "age". The net effect of billions of stars forming and dying over time increases the amount of heavy elements in a galaxy and changes the galaxy's reserve of hydrogen gas, eventually affecting its morphology (gas-rich disk galaxies evolving into elliptical galaxies as their gas reserves are converted to stars).
