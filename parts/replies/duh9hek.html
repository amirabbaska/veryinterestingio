      • jeo123911

        All fruit/vegetables are made from cells. These cells contain water inside them and are held together as a shape by cellular walls.

        When water inside a cell freezes, it rips those cellular walls apart. Depending on the speed at which you freeze water, you can get lots of crystals forming on some parts which leads to tearing and tissue damage. Or if you gradually and slowly freeze that water, it will then expand and mostly fit inside that frozen cell, with much less damage.
