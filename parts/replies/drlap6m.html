      • pointyhead88

        Because nickel aids in corrosion resistance it just isn't the passive protective kind. If you want corosion resistance, generally, you start with a chromium alloy steel (stainless) not a nickel steel. Think of it like this. If you paint something it prevents rusting. The passive protective coating caused by chromium is like paint. Adding nickel makes the underlying material resistant as well so its less succeptable to anything that gets through the paint.

        Nickel increases strength with less effect on ductility than other elements which allows for a tougher steel. If you're forming silverware for example this is desirable. Also, nickel is what gives it that high shine.
