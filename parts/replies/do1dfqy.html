      What I assume you are referring to is the depletion of NAD+ via the metabolism of ethanol using alcohol dehydrogenase and subsequently acetaldehyde dehydrogenase. You bring up a good point that this can impair gluconeogenesis within the liver. I think the major extra piece is that this subsequently liberates fatty acids because of the downstream increase in glucagon. Through some complex mechanisms this leads to the resultant fatty liver over time that is seen in heavy drinkers. It certainly does contribute to hypoglycemia based on the biochemistry alone, however I will be honest in saying I do not know how much of an effect this has on the systemic circulation during a bout of drinking.
