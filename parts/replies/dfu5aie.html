      Man youbrits are weird... Jk, but is this system still common in Britain? Here in Canada (and most of the developed world as far as I know) we use hot water tanks. So cold water is fed from the city or town supply and brought into a sealed tank. The water in thattank is then heated using elements and kept under pressure. Because the tank is pressurized and sealed, it's just as safe as the supply water, unless you have a faulty tank.

      We are also seeing thankless tankless hot water systems more and more. Where water is fed directly from the supply, through a heater and to the tap. This is more efficient t as it does not need to hold hot water for long periods of time, constantly keeping it warm, it instead heats the water as it is needed.
