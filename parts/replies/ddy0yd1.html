    It's 25Mb/s (not MB), and that's under a perfectly optimal setup on Bluetooth 3. Bluetooth 2 only supported 2.1Mb/s, and you probably won't even get that when your headphones are on your head and your phone is in your pocket. At a range of 15-20' it's entirely possible that the connection will drop down to a couple hundred Kb/s, which is why your phone is compressing it.

    A2DP can support basically any codec (as long as the transmitter and receiver support it) but pretty much all you'll see in the wild is mp3/mp4.
