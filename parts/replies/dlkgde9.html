      • HurleyBurger

        Super volcanoes... little notice events...

        I think I agree, overall, but I do argue that super volcanoes give fairly significant warning. Volcanologists are able to track many variables that give clues to pending eruptions of volcanoes. Seismic activity in the form of seismic swarms indicate magma movement and hydrothermal activity. Using INSAR and GPS they can track land deformations; swelling and subsidence. GeoChem gives us further insight to possible future eruptions based on the content in fumeroles. There are many other methods, such as magnetic detections, gravity fluctuations, and tiltmeters. We can use all of these methods in concert to make pretty good predictions about possible eruptions. I will, however, also argue that it's still mostly guesswork and even the best predictions can be wrong. Also, evacuating millions of people around the Yellowstone supervolcano would be a tremendous undertaking.
