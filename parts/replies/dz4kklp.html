    Pretty close to what I described in my initial comment elsewhere in-thread, I'd imagine.

    1. Collect bottles.
    2. Process them into flake.
    3. Melt flake into plastic resin.
    4. Spin resin into thread.

    Thing is, anybody who recycles plastic bottles does steps 1 - 3. Those are just what plastic recycling is.

    As to step four, most plastic bottles are made out of PET/PETE (plastic resin type 1, for recycling purposes), and that's one of the plastics used to make polyester. I assume the way money was made here was by accomplishing steps 1 - 3 at a cost below that that most textile manufacturers spend for their raw materials.
