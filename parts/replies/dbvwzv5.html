    That's a very powerful sentence and I want to expand a bit on it:

    It's not that nature discovers and uses "magic" numbers like the golden ratio or pi or e. The universe is mathematically consistent and when you look at billions (of people) made of billions (of cells) made of billions of atoms you're bound to find some fundamental mathematical concepts propagate from the molecules in DNA all the way up to the organization of human society. This has two important consequences:

    1. These "magic" numbers are found in nature; and

    2. if you look hard enough, you will find them almost everywhere.

    There's no magic in finding the golden ratio in so many natural classes. It's like looking at round structures and finding pi or like looking at two apples and finding 1+1=2.
