    The compression on the chest squeezes the heart, pushing some blood out of it. When the compression stops the change in pressure allows the blood to fill the heart again, much as it does between heartbeats.

    Maybe it's obvious or implicit, but I think it's worth explicitly pointing that out- The (healthy) heart's function is to pump blood, which it does by contracting rhythmically. The point of CPR is to mimic this, by artificially/manually contracting the heart rhythmically.

    CPR is a way to imitate/approximate the normal behaviour of the heart.
