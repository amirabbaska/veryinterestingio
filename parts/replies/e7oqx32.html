    From what I can gather from reading: https://www.osapublishing.org/optica/abstract.cfm?uri=optica-5-9-1113

    It is indeed one pulse of light passing through multiple filters that are changed by the pulse depending on what they're measuring. Fig. 1 in the article shows 2 kinds of detection methods; active, and passive. I'm not sure what kind the researchers used, but both approximate the total image. To define it more clearly they used "...another camera that acquires a static image. Combined with the image acquired by the femtosecond streak camera, we can use what is called a Radon transformation to obtain high-quality images while recording ten trillion frames per second.” http://www.inrs.ca/english/actualites/worlds-fastest-camera-freezes-time-10-trillion-frames-second
