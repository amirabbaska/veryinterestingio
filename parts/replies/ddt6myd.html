    Yes!

    Increase in temperature produces a decrease in the concentration of dissolved gases at equilibrium. That's why you see bubbles form in water as you heat it even before it starts to boil.

    Solubility for gases goes down with increasing temperature because dissolving a gas releases heat. Many solids (like salt) absorb heat when dissolved, so they show the opposite trend in solubility.

    The effects of climate on ocean oxygenation are more complicated an not just an equilibrium effect; the recent news report suggested that 15% was from this effect but the majority was from less efficient mixing of ocean layers, I believe.
