    • djfl00d

      There was the argument made as to whether or not Congress could intervene in Obama's decisions regarding Gitmo detainees and whether or not this violated the separation of powers between the legislative and executive branch.
      The problem was that there were restrictions on where we could send detainees. People could not be released/transferred to countries with questionable security where their lives would be endangered, or where they may be tortured, or where their incarceration was not guaranteed. Those who were not slated for release had to be incarcerated elsewhere... and short of finding nations who would/could take these prisoners, the only other option would be to bring them to the US. And this is where Congress stepped in and said no.
