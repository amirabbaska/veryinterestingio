    • skate_enjoy

      Front foot placement is important for height of ollie. For high ollies you need to make sure that the board does not level out too early because that pretty much means you have most of the time reached your max height. A longer slide of your front foot allows for more control on higher ollies. That is not to say you cannot get a high ollie with your front foot closer to the nose, it just is not as controlled and takes much more effort to get it right and high. Rule of thumb is foot futhur back equals a higher ollie. I think in your analysis of this you might have overlooked that skater is putting weight on the board pretty much at all points of time. Back foot on pop and front foot to level out.
