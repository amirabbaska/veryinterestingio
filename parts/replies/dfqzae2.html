      I think you're thinking of this in a particle physics context versus an astrophysical context. If you pump a bunch of anti-protons into a region filled with gas/plasma (otherwise known as "most of the universe") then you'll get proton/anti-proton annihilations and you'll get production of positrons. Those positrons will eventually annihilate with electrons in the environment. And in general that will result in a peak of gamma rays around 511 KeV.
