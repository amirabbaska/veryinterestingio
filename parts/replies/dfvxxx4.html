    • HodlDwon

      Yes it gets bigger, but it's not really a practical problem yet or any time soon as storage is pretty cheap. Also there are light clients available too for phone / mobile users... which in the context of the above Bittorrent analogy are like perpetual leechers always downloading and watching, but never storing for long.

      In the future (probably 2 years), there will be some upgrades that mean you only really need keep track of the last 4-8 months of history. So the spreadsheet will grow (adding rows faster the more people use it), but at some point rows at the top will be deleted about as fast as they are appended to the bottom and the total rows at any given time will roughly stabilize. Archive nodes will maintain the whole history, should it be interesting to anyone... kinda like the Internet Archive / Wayback Machine, but they won't be strictly necessary.

      See also http://ethereum.stackexchange.com/q/143/311
