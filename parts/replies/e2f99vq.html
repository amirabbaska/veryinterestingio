  • VeteranBore

    Actually, what that page says is that it isn’t required to list potential cross-contaminants on the label; that’s left up to the discretion of the company, and many companies opt not to label for shared lines, shared facilities, etc. (believing, for example, that their cleaning is sufficient to remove any possible traces—although it's tricky, because these cleaning processes almost never has any transparency to them at all, so it's hard to find anything out about them, and if your allergies are severe enough that you have a chance of reacting anyway [and these people do exist], trying to figure out what's safe for you to eat can be... a crapshoot).

    What is required by the FDA is listing all allergens that the item explicitly contains—as the article says, either within the ingredients list or at the end (e.g., “contains: nuts”).
