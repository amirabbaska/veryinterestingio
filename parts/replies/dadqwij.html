    Why does the sodium stay in the blood forever? Wouldn't it be taken up by the cells more or less immediately?

    • RedditorDoc

      Sodium tends to remain in the extracellular space, that is, in the fluid that surrounds cells and tissues, and in the blood. Cells actually pump out more sodium than retain it, but that's an entire ELI5 in itself.

      And sodium doesn't remain in the blood forever. Your kidneys are capable of sensing how much sodium is present, and will adjust the reabsorption of sodium from the urine accordingly on a more or less minute to minute basis. Your heart can also detect sodium concentrations, though indirectly, (The only time this will happen is if the heart is failing, and can't pump properly) and increases Na+ excretion in the urine by secreting special peptides.
