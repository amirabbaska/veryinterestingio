    • elijahoakridge

      It will exist sure. For each of whatever three axes about which you have established nonzero rotation there will be a centripetal/centrifugal forces relative to those axes.

      It's just that it's not physically possible to set up a rotation that will make the magnitude of those relative forces equal in all three of your chosen orthogonal axes simultaneously. So if you average the vectors of those three forces, it will point it some particular direction. And if you adjust your coordinate system to make that direction one of your axes, you'll find that your net force in both of your other axes orthogonal to that axis equals zero.
