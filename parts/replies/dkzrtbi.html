    • NeuroBill

      Should we all be stone cold sober all the time for optimum brain health ?

      Probably. But there are certainly arguments the other way. There are a few studies that have shown that one or two drinks a day are associated with lower risks of Alzheimer's disease. But above that, you get worsened risk of all sorts of diseases. So it's a pretty fine line (i.e. one drink a day might be good, but three is def. bad).

      Does it matter what kind of alcohol we consume i.e. Wine or spirits etc ?

      Again, my understanding is the evidence is mixed. The red wine brigade has certainly got less noisy over the years. But I suspect that is probably the best, then beer/white wine, then spirits. But I'm not aware of a study that has systematically looked into this.
