    This is not correct. The longer the polymers are, and the stronger the intermolecular non-covalent bonds, the more you will experience intramolecular bond breakage as a consequence of the bulk disruption.

    When you cut with scissors, you are pulling apart individual polymers, and along the way many covalent bonds are broken due to this stress.

    Although your key point is correct that you're not cutting apart individual covalent bonds at an atomic level.
