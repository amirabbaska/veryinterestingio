      At OP: The other big thing aside from deerskin being warm for us (warmer than you'd think) is that while alive, deer can do the equivalent of goose bumps. They can slightly adjust the 'angle' of each hair from "all laying flat like a carpet" to "all slightly raised, creating more like bubble wrap". All those little air pockets (each hair has one around it, literally) can be 'held' at several degrees warmer than surrounding atmosphere and reduce the amount of energy a deer needs to produce to fight heat loss; something in the same way that wearing a knit cap can keep your ears warmer than simply pulling up a hood on a jacket/hoodie. Lots of small air pockets are much more effective than one big one!
