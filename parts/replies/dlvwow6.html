  • gnualmafuerte

    Dogs have more perception of time that people give them credit for, in fact, they are absolutely capable of planning ahead. In fact, that's how they hunt. Wolves will cooperate and hunt an animal for days sometimes.

    A quick example: I have two labs. They both used to insist on going upstairs and sleeping right next to my bedroom door. When the female was little she climbed the stairs and fell, breaking her leg. Since then, even though it's been 3 years and she fully recovered after a few months of having the leg immobilized and a small surgery, she's been scared of stairs. So, after all that happened I began telling them both to stay when the other one wanted to follow me upstairs. So, what he did was wait around 10 minutes, then come upstairs. So, it's been years, and I no longer tell them to stay downstairs, and yet he consistently waits around 10 minutes after I go to bed before coming upstairs. He just sits in the first step and waits a bit.

    They certainly don't follow time, so they can't tell if It's been 2 days or 3 since a certain event, they don't recollect or track time like that, but they certainly don't just live in the moment, and they do have a concept of time, and most importantly of future and that allows them to plan ahead .
