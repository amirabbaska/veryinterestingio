      Sucking in air is the result of the negative pressure you created. The poster was just being more technical. Think of the air as a spring, because it can be compressed or expanded. Creating a POSITIVE pressure would be like compressing both ends of a spring, when you release it would push outward. Creating a NEGATIVE pressure is the reverse, pulling on both ends of a spring, it wants to pull inwards. To suck in air, your lungs create an "expanded spring" that causes air to enter your lungs to allow this "spring" to contract to normal. Not a perfect analogy but hopefully gives a visual
