      All of the above are indeed very possible, but in the case of Bezos he actually sells c.$1bn worth of shares (into the public market) each year in order to fund his other projects e.g. Blue Origin (Source: FT article from yesterday on this topic)

      When CEO's have such low cash salaries, it's because the majority of their compensation comes in the form of (usually restricted) shares. These are shares that either a) the company purchases from the public market or b) creates anew (far less common with large, publicly traded companies due to the complexity of new share issuance) and gives to the CEO. These will often have a "lock-up" period where they cannot be sold for a period of time (can vary from 12 months to several years)

      This is also a very common form of payment for junior employees' bonuses as well, especially in start ups and the tech sector
