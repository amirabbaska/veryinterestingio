    Was surprised this explanation wasn't the top post, since it is literally one of the first examples used in university when learning about water absorption bythe body.

    If you drink just water, you will absorb water normally by osmosis. If you drink water with a certain ratio of glucose and sodium added, you will absorb water by both osmosis and these active transport gates, which requires a combination of all three molecules to function. Therefore hydrating you faster.

    This is perhaps less of an issue for sports, but is used extensively by health organisations like the WHO to treat diseases like cholera. Cholera deaths are mostly due to dehydration due to the incredibly dehabilitating diarrhoea, meaning any liquid you drink goes straight through you, with greatly reduced time for water to be absorbed by osmosis. Adding glucose and sodium means water can be absorbed by two methods, and much faster, saving a huge number of lives during epidemics.
