    • datarancher

      A few hospitals (literally...like 3 in the US) use neutron beams as a form of chemotherapy.

      Traditional radiation treatments don't actually kill the cells directly, but either cause DNA damage or generate free radicals, which then cause DNA damage. This is particularly bad for fast-dividing cells, so it hopefully affects the cancerous tissue (which divides quickly) more than the surrounding stuff.

      In contrast, the fast neutron therapy beams deliver a lot more energy and can directly affect the nuclear structure of the targeted tissue. This is much harder for the cells to survive, which would be good for cancer treatments, but bad for whomever's holding the sack of neutrons.
