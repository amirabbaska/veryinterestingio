    Whilst I really mean to not belittle any of your experiences, like a few other commentors, I too really doubt that there would be any basis behind this hypothesis. Wounds that pierce into your abdomen or digestive tract have not really been very survivable at all until fairly recently, so there couldn't be any meaningful evolutional pressure. Furthermore, the amount of people who would suffer these wounds and survive them, is so small that it can't be enough from the perspective of the whole population.

    I think it's been shown that adrenaline may induce an emetic response (i.e. vomiting) and it might be connected to our flight-or-fight mechanism (run faster on an empty stomach, all that) or it might just be an imperfection, a coincidence. Anxiety and stress on the other hand have been shown to put physical strain on our digestive systems, which can lead to diarrhea simply due to the failure of the system to properly process foodstuff. This, too, could have some evolutionary advantage such as redirecting our resources to muscles and brains.. or it could just be coincidental.

    None of that is wholly conclusive, but I find those much more plausible explanations nevertheless.
