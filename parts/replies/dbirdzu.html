      Because a high HR means your body is having to pump more times to get the same amount of blood to the periphery of your body as a normal HR. That's why Armstrong's heart was so strong because his HR was insanely low and he was doing the same work for almost half as much a normal heart rate. It's all about the efficiency of the each or the stroke volume.
