• Nyrin

    This is definitely true for power utilization, at least for lithium ion cells; as soon as you switch over to CV charging (somewhere above 70%), the waste fraction will start going up and the efficiency of what's "going into the battery" will start going down. You can probably even notice that a phone charging at 90% gets warmer than one charging at 40%.

    It's also worth keeping in mind that deep discharge is really hard on contemporary LiIon tech, though. You can literally trade half of your overall battery life (on a milliamp-hour basis) if you're regularly sucking the thing dry.

    Even when inefficient, the actual power used in recharging most portable electronics is very small and cheap, so optimizing for that is rarely the most prudent focus.
