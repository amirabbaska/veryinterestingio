      • crimeo

        As a fairly avid photographer who owns multiple film cameras including a view camera, I can attest that 10 years ago? Maybe. Today? No, there's pretty much zero advantage to film other than it being fun. Maybe if you want a film-like look, are shooting video, don't have digital training, AND have no budget (all 4 things at once), then MAYBE you can come out ahead by buying cheaper up front stuff and sending it to a lab instead of buying and learning software. But everything it does can be digitally replicated these days.

        It USED to have meaningful differences, not long ago. Better dynamic range (not anymore with high end digital), it has a sort of soft look (can be replicated now by algorithm), lasted a long time (nowadays digital storage will last long enough for better digital storage which will last long enough for better digital storage.....)
