    That’s not true. First principles includes mass balance and constitutive equations (relationship between flux and potential). This is an engineering problem, that engineers solve all the time.     Once you have the location of the phase boundary, the difference in density is the driving force, and this can be equated to a pressure difference, and then to flow potential. Diffusion is going to work against buoyancy do we need the diffusivity of oil-in-water and water-in-oil; basically a ratio of the viscosities if we ignore capillary effects.

    The time to separate is going to be proportional to the diffusivity times the square of distance based upon the transport equation (Laplace’s equation).
