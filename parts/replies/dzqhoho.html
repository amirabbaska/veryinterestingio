    • myotherpassword

      There are limits, but they are way larger than we can conceivably reproduce on Earth. A first approximation to the limit of the field strength could be calculated by pretending that all the electrons in the neutron star were moving around its equator at nearly the speed of light. This upper limit goes down as you require the electrons to spread out over the surface, or if you suppose the system rotates at more realistic speeds.
