    • jeppev

      Kind of. The problem ia, though, that often it isn't directly the bacteria that makes you sick, but the waste produced by live bacteria. When you heat food, you kill the many of the living bacteria, but the waste is still there. And everytime the bacteria are allowed to regrow, they produce more and more waste.

      Optimal solution is keeping the food above a certain temperature at all times. In Denmark, we have a law about this for restaurants, if they keep the food above 65°C, it can in theory stay out, for example in a buffet, indefinitely.
