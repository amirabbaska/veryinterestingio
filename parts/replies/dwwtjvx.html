• HollidayDDS

    Gray (or grey, if you prefer) is just the common term for "reflects most wavelengths of light evenly". So when you polish a gray material to get a clearer reflection (less diffusion), you are reflecting light evenly across the spectrum and don't get a tinted reflection. If your material is colored (like gold or copper), that means it is reflecting that color of light more strongly, so if you polish it up, you will still end up with a tinted reflection.

      • yslvenom

        No. From a spectrum perspective, white and black are both values of grey, as in, it's actually the same color. All 3 "shades" have the same flat wavelength spectrum, just at different intensities (True black being 0, true white being all your cones can handle). And if different veils have the same spectrum, the only thing that can change is the overall intensity. Therefore the only difference between the veils would be their overall transmission coefficient.

        If you took a neutral density filter, AKA a gray veil, and sent pure white light through it, it would look white. Stack a couple neutral density filters, and the light white would come out gray. Stack 1000 and the light comes out black, AKA there is no light. Basically the lighter gray the veil is the easier you will the shape because the lightness of gray is literally just how much of the light coming from the shape is making it through.
