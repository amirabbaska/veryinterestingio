      • unexpected_post

        First of all, if it's really cold, birds will try to cover as much of their feet as possible with feathers. Second, birds that deal with cold temperatures have a mechanism to prevent heat loss. Warm blood flows into feet through arteries, which run next to and are often surrounded by veins, which carry cold blood back into the body. The warm and cold blood flows opposite directions (countercurrent) to facilitate heat exchange. Therefore blood reaching feet is barely warmer than the surroundings, to prevent heat loss.Blood returning to the body is warm enough to prevent body temperature from dropping. This way the feet can be supplied with blood without losing much heat.
