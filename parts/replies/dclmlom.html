    • LunarDelta

      It's kind of hard to explain without getting deep into some serious maths. Our belief that the universe could be infinite is based on the fact that the math we use to model and describe spacetime allows for the possibility of infinite space if certain criteria are met; e.g. the energy density of the universe is such that it is either flat or open in geometry, isn't folded back in on or connected to itself in strange ways, etc.

      Personally, I find an infinite universe to make more intuitive sense than a universe with a definite edge, or that isn't simply connected. Of course, it's entirely possible the universe is finite and spherical, or even torus-shaped, but much of modern cosmology operates under the assumption that the universe is flat and infinite.

      (Note: Just to avoid confusion, "flat" in this context means that 3D space at any given moment has the same geometric properties as a flat 2D Euclidean plane, not that it is literally flat like Flatland.)
