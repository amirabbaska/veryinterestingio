      • jdthetransient

        I assure you that the force is not oriented away from the moon. Gravity doesn't become a repulsive force just because it is "behind" the earth with respect to the moon. Gravity is strictly a force of attraction. The wateron the "back side" is farther away from the moon, so it is pulled with the least force compared to the rest of the moon. The earth itself ("land" which is where we take measurements from) is pulled more strongly than the "backside water", so it is pulled in the direction of the moon as well, but with greater force, so it forms bulge #1. On the "near side" it is pulled strongest of all, accounting for bulge #2.
