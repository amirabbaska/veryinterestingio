      It's a different type of movie really. Michael Crichton made a career out of writing speculative fiction. Almost all of his novels take a scientific development that people worried about like nano technology, genetic engineering, lab created disease and turned it into a fanciful thriller extrapolating a nightmare scenario where science and nature clash.

      The original Jurassic Park is a faithful adaptation of that story (even if it is a bit more condensed than the novel). Regular people lost in a nightmare of science and nature gone mad. The sequels are mostly just action movies with monsters. The recent Jurassic World is closer to a godzilla movie of monsters vs monsters with people in the middle than Crichtons ideas.

      I think it would be really hard to make a sequel to Jurassic Park that captures the original. It banked so hard on the sense of wonder and achievement of what had been done before it all turned into a nightmare.

      After seeing the movie so many times since I was a kid, I think these days my favourite scene is John Hammond sitting in the ruins of his park, eating melting ice cream from a defunct fridge while telling Ellie about how he wanted to build something real and wondrous. And Ellie just chides him for the sheer arrogance of thinking it could be controlled.

  • CrazyMike366

    One scene that sticks out to me as being especially brilliant at combining life-like illusion with simple effects is the scene with the sick triceratops. It's just a puppet, but the context of illness confirms that it's a living, real creature because it's fallen ill like a real creature should, but it also provides built-in excuses for it just laying there statically, it's obviously labored, mechanical breathing, milky, blankly-staring eyes and a mucous-y rubber texture of its mouth and skin.

    And it just works so well because illness creates an instant empathy for the creature - we've all felt like just laying there, breathing difficultly, with blank eyes and that awful mucous-y mouth and sinus feeling - so we subconsciously expect, and therfore pay no attention to, all those deep visual flaws in the puppet.
