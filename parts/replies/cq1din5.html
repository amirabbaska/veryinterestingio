    Well, I think when people talk about the difference between an animal and a human interacting with speech, they're talking about having some understanding of the concepts behind the words versus a rote memorization or 1:1 association.

    The parrot may have made some association between "I'm leaving" and making the "Goodbye" sound however if you just put your shoes on and walk out the door it won't say "Goodbye" unless you specifically teach it that. It doesn't understand what the meaning or purpose of "goodbye" is. It doesn't understand why it's saying it.

    Which would put it on about the level of the most basic chat bot. It doesn't 'understand' what you're saying in any meaningful way. Its vocabulary is a one-to-one mapping from input to output.

    A dog, on the other hand, will make some associations between the words and the world. Saying "let's go for a walk" or "get your leash" will get them excited and running for the door because they understand that those words tie to a concept. Similarly, if you just pick up their leash and go to the door they'll probably react to the same. They have some understanding of how the world works, and their understanding of language ties back into that.

    You can teach your dog phrases and combine them and they can understand. If your dog knows how to "go find", teaching them the name of their "ball", "rope", "plushie", etc will allow you to tell them to "Go find your ball" or "go find your rope". You can teach them to "go find" and the names separately, and they're able to make the connection. If you have a really smart dog, you can say "go find " and even though they don't recognize part of the statement, they understand the concept of fetching something and can deduce that they're to bring you the unrecognized item.

    You can teach a dog what a ball is, and even if you replace their ball with a different ball, they'll still understand that you want them to fetch that thing you play with them with that you throw.

    When you tell them "go find ", you don't need to separately teach them that people can be found. They understand that to "go find" is to seek out a thing.

    To me, that shows a level of understanding of the concepts behind your words deeper than simply reacting in a 1:1 manner.

    So for your friend's parrot... It's on the "just recognizing sounds" end of the spectrum. It has no recognition of how goodbye is supposed to be used. It's nothing but a sound which prompts a direct reaction. It has no understanding of the context of your words.

    To a dog, the words work more similarly to humans and can actually be tied to a concept which can expand and evolve which they can use to make intelligent decisions and deductions.
