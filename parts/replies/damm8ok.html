  • jmalbo35

    While most of the mechanisms you mentioned are accurate for how things are killed by the immune system, several of them aren't usually done by T cells, but by other white blood cells. Antibodies are made by B cells (though T cells provide support for them to do so), reactive oxygen species are usually made by phagocytes or granulocytes (T cells mostly only use ROS for signaling, not as a weapon), and T cells don't participate in phagocytosis at all (that's more the realm of monocytes/macrophages, dendritic cells, mast cells, and neutrophils).

    You were correct on the pore forming proteins being a T cell function, though you actually meant perforins, not porins.
