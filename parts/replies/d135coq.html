  • lunamoon_girl

    Type 3 Diabetes has become pop-culture relevant, but it's not necessarily an ideal term. It's referring to the fact that you a) have increased risk of AD when you have type 2 diabetes, and b) there is evidence that insulin mis-regulation in the brain may play a role either upstream or downstream in the pathogenesis of AD. I think there is very little evidence to suggest that insulin and IGF abnormalities cause AD, but the jury is still out. There is far better genetic evidence for the protein misfolding theories (And their downstream repercussions) being the underlying mechanism. The only causative mutations we have found for AD create increased amounts of amyloid beta which deposits in AD brains.
