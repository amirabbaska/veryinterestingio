    often failed early was because anti-freeze is also a corrosion inhibitor which those engines need,

    Anti-freeze (ethylene glycol) alone isn't but it has a corrosion inhibitor (potassium disphoshate, ISTR) mixed in with it.

    Anti-freeze gradually oxidizes to form some acidic compound, so it is recommended that it is replaced every 3 years, before the alkaline corrosion inhibitor is used up. Once it has turned acidic, it starts bi-mettalic/galvanic corrosion which usually destroys the head gasket. Look up 'bad anti-freeze'.
