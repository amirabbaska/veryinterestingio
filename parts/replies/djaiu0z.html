    • Unique_username1

      Actually, there is more than a "risk" of overcharge or wear. Batteries will certainly be damaged if exposed to voltages above 4.2v (with some, but not much, wiggle room). Voltage is the "force" that makes current flow. So once you're putting (for example) 1 amp into the battery and 4.2v is required to achieve that, you cannot charge faster (or continue charging as fast for longer) because this would require (for example) 4.3v. Again, you physically could, but this is less of a "overcharge if you aren't careful" situation and more of a "guaranteed firey death" situation.
