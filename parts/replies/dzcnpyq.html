      By contrast, in the Tony Awards one it looks like they have many shots ready from pre-planning, but they haven't planned every single shot.

      There are co-directors that talk direct to cameras, they guide what shots to take, if the pic needs to tightening or opening up. Since they can see other cameras they can offer shots that are not on any other camera, compositions that are missing at the moment. If there is only cold camera wide shot and all other cameras offer a zoomed in-your-face shot... that is something that the camera man can't know. It sure is interesting work.
