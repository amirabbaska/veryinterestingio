    Some of Vermeer's paintings, which I believe came before the camera, have depth of field in them. That's something that not even the human eye sees. You don't see an in-focus foreground and a blurry background when your eyes dart back and forth between them, yet somehow Vermeer was able to paint blurry backgrounds.

  • CreeDorofl

    that depth of field and photo blur is especially key, it isn't something you'd notice before the advent of photos because when you flick your eyes to another part of the scene, they focus and the area that was previously blurred is now detailed and sharp. Photos make you acutely aware of this, but an old master may go his whole life and not notice the presence of DOF in a close up scene. Thus his detailed rendering of that looks realistic, but sharpness all the way through it ruins the effect of seeing the world through a lens/eyeball.
