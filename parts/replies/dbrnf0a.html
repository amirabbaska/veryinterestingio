  • Hup234

    I used to get severe sinus infections once or twice a year. Usually I could get through a working day with the pain in the background. But almost as soon as I got in the car to begin the 50 - mile trip back home, the pain would flare up to almost epic proportions. Once I got home things would settle down until I had to drive back to work the next day and it would start all over again.

    I eventually had the problem fixed with surgery but for years I could never understand why driving caused such agony. It finally dawned on me that it was the slight changes in air pressure as I traveled up and down the hills of northern NJ that were causing the already-inflamed tissue in the sinus cavities to become irritated even further.

    I used to doubt people that said changes in the weather affected their aches and pains but I don't any more.
