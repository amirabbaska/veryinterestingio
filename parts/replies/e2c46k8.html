        this is a harder concept to explain, but I'll throw my hat in to explain.

        basically, a system (like an atom) wants to be in its lowest state. this is just a general law of physics and you see it all over the place. low energy states are often called "stable".

        it turns out if you have an atom with a neutron, keeping the neutron as a neutron is the lowest energy state. however, if the neutron is by itself, there exists a lower energy state which is if the neutron splits up into a proton, an electron, and a neutrino.
