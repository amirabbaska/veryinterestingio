  • thedankbank1021

    I think a lot of the issue is that with the declining popularity of professional sports, will the cost of the subsidy be worth the economic boon of having a sports team.

    In 1990 a $16 million annual subsidy in sports infrastructure could yield $24 million in additional tax revenue.

    Today whether or not these programs break even is hit or miss. The big concern is that a lot of the time it's paid upfront in lump sums. So a government might have given $200 million to a team for stadium expecting $20 million in benefits each year for 10 years.

    BUT with the decline in sports they might only be getting $12 million, or $8 million the next year. Doesn't bode well for these investments.
