    Vet here. There's a lot of observational clinical improvements noted in dogs and cats getting acupuncture. I recently saw a middle aged Dachshund with bad chronic inflammatory rhinitis. It was difficult to manage even with steroidal therapy. Then started getting acupuncture, and the 1-3 days after acupuncture it would release the flood gates of cloudy mucoid nasal discharge, and feel great for a few weeks before needing another treatment. I've also personally seen positive gains in energy with dogs that have cancer. I'm also pretty sure the NIH concluded that acupuncture had benefits in pain management analogous to traditional therapies (i.e., pharmaceuticals). I absolutely hate medicine without evidence, and hate even more the alternative medicine racket and supplement bullshit. But acupuncture is on my list as something to always consider. Especially when it has no real harmful effects.
