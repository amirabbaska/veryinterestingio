      I think alot of time with questions like these, its best to tackle them by saying, "Ok, Could we do/observe/make X if we could account for everything else that would interfere with X besides the effect we're concerned about". In this case, "Could we observe duplicate images of stars wrapping around a hypertoroidal universe if we had a magical, infinitely powerful telescope to see it and infinite time to wait for it to happen". These kind of questions are important because, if we didn't ask them, we would never dream of things that require more than one breakthrough to be realized and no one would ever invent anything because after one innovation it would still seem impossible.
