    If you've ever flown through heavy clouds during the day, you'll see that clouds that appear gray from the ground look as fluffy and white as any other cloud from above. This is especially evident when flying past cumulonimbus clouds, since the sunlit side is shiny white, and the shaded side is dark gray.

      This is probably due to the fact that when you're flying above the cloud, you're seeing it from the same side as the light source. From the ground, the light has to pass through the cloud. Some of the light is absorbed, lost, refracted or whatever while passing through the cloud, which would explain the difference in apparent colour.

    The vast majority of the light is scattered back into space, while only a tiny amount is absorbed. Clouds are opaque because of their random structure, which causes multiple scattering. If you take all of the water molecules in a cloud and condense them into a cohesive liquid, it's clear.
