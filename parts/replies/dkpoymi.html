    Yes absolutely. The important thing here is the density of states, telling us essentially how available certain states are to electrons for a given energy/momentum. This changes as we change number of dimensions. The exact effect for quantum gases is finicky to calculate, but in the classical (high temperature/low density) limit, the equipartition theorem tells us that we get k/2 heat capacity for each quadratic term in the energy.

    So, in 3D, a free particle has 3 directions it can move it, so a heat capacity of (3/2)k, while in 2D, a free particle has only 2 independent directions, so the heat capacity will just be k.
