    Making more ATP after you have used all your ATP and creatine requires oxygen.

    Not in anaerobic glycolytic muscle fibers which typically don't have any myoglobin to begin with. Look at these fibers under a microscope and they'll be almost completely clear.

    The short duration that humans can sprint for has very little to do with ATP production, it has to do with the sarcomeres in your muscles physically breaking down and no longer being able to cross-bridge cycle and therefore can't generate tension. These muscle fibers don't use oxygen to begin with.
