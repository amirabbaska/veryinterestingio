    Calico cats aren't actually caused by a mutation. It's because their fur color gene is on the X chromosome. Before a female mammal is born, one of the two X chromosomes is inactivated (silenced/not expressed) in each somatic (non-egg) cell. The male cats have just one potential fur color, while females could have one of the two fur colors expressed depending on which of the X chormosomes was inactivated in each cell.

    Some male cats are calico, but those are rare since the male calicos have three sex chromosomes instead of two (XXY).
