    Is there any benefit to the enamel being in a mineralized state?

    • CuriousGeorgicus

      The teeth are normally mineralized with calcium when healthy. When they are exposed to an acid long-term, they develop chalky, white spots (decalcification spots) where the tooth structure is demineralized and has the calcium "removed." These areas can be remineralized (made healthy again) by the use of a fluoridated toothpaste and fluoride varnish treatment at your regular cleaning appointments.

      In addition, the microscopic structure created by fluoride remineralization (fluoroapatite) is strong and more resistant to acid erosion than the calcium-fortified structures (hydroxyapatite).
