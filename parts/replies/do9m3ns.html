    • Kauboi

      I'd replace it altogether. Go online and look around. Search "2010 Smart Fortwo OEM wheel" (or whatever year/model you have) and get an idea of the price. Then call a shop and see what they would charge you to order a new one and install. Then find out what they would charge to mount and balance your old tire on a new wheel that you brought in (one you found yourself online).

      Generally that's your cheapest option for replacing a wheel and I would recommend replacing the wheel over getting it repaired. It would be different if you were on 1000 dollar wheels, but I doubt a replacement smart car wheel is going to run you more than 100-150 USD.

      Edit: that 100-150 is for a new wheel, if you're comfortable buying used you could find one a lot cheaper. Then, like I said, you'd just be paying a shop to mount/balance your old tire and transfer over your TPMS sensors. Good luck!
