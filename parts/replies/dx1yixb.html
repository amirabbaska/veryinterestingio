    So I would counter that it's not. My in-laws and spouse are Chinese, and we've also travelled in China. There is good Chinese food in most large cities in the US. Plus much of the lousy Chinese places have some traditional dishes, they are just either (1) mediocre versions of real Chinese dishes, or (2) breaded and fried and sweetened, like general tsos or orange chicken, to better suit our tastes.

    In general, I would suggest you almost never should go to a buffet. Chinese food should be eaten hot, not reheated. Second, mostly avoid chains. Some of them are mediocre to good, but it's hard to rise higher. Finally, for the best food you have to ask around. Even a previously great place may drop to just average when a chef leaves. And these better places are usually in an Asian business area of town

    Last, if it tastes good, who cares (notably, sometimes the US creations do make it back to China)
