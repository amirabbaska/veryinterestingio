  • JustMeRC

    You are asking if we know how the animals feel relative to human emotions. Such answers are always difficult, if not impossible

    Neuroscientist Jaak Panksepp has done significant work in this arena. He says that we have feelings because they tell us what supports our survival, and what detracts from our survival. We can look at the similarities between our neurology and other animals’ neurology and draw inferences about similar feelings we share.

    There are plenty of videos with him lecturing on the topic, or one could read his book, Affective Neuroscience: The Foundations of Human and Animal Emotions. I’m not a scholar on his work, but I have enough familiarity that I could probably answer some questions if anyone has them.
