    • Bannedfromfun

      Yes. The ideal way to determine your sleep need is to see how long you sleep without waking up with an alarm.

      If you want to improve your sleep, it's more important to have a set Wake time then a set bed-time to regulate your circadian rythm.

      If you are concerned about the length of time needed to sleep and feel "rested" then there are some factors to consider. One is your circadian rythm. Night Owls / Delayed Sleep Phase Syndrome people who go to bed earlier, even if they manage to get to sleep, will have lighter and more fragmented sleep during the first part of the night until they get close to their "natural" bed time. Even with the use of melatonin. Regular Bright Light therapy in the morning can "push back" the circadian rythm however.

      As well avoiding electronic devices or strong light 2 hours before bed prevents melatonin suppression and consequent lighter fragmented sleep. This is preferable to trying to just take "more" melatonin OTC because your body just flushes it with your regular melatonin. Blue Light Blocking Glasses that fully cover your field of vision are the only really significant option if you must use electronic devices close to bed.

      A healthy sleep environment with the absolute least amount of light and possible environmental disruptions is a must. Even a brief arousal that doesn't cause you to wake up can disrupt your sleep cycle and result in you missing deeper restorative stages.

      Reducing stress associated with your sleep environment is huge. Three key points - Your bed is only for sleeping and sex, to prevent associating it with anything else.

      You only go to bed when you're sleepy, trying to sleep is contraindicated because it actually stimulates you when you focus on something. It's better to just go to bed later and wake at the same time as usual because then you should be tired at the appropriate time the next night.

      Finally since once again trying to sleep doesn't help, if you are awake in bed still after 30 minutes get up and out of bed and do a light non-stimulating activity until you feel sleepy again and then return to bed. This prevents associating frustration and wakefulness with your bed and also distracts your brain from focusing and agitating itself because of the failure to sleep, allowing it to relax and enter the sleep state.

      The last thing you can do is see a sleep doctor and be screened for primary sleep disorders. Some, like High Upper Airway Resistance or Periodic Limb Movement Disorder, aren't screen able by respiratory therapists or respirologists using at home sleep apnea screening studies and mat result in false positives for mild OSA. Other issues like hyper-vigilance or alpha intrusion require EEG screening. Finally chronic pain or mood disorders such as depression or GAD can affect pts sleep need. It's quite common for people to be in denial about these issues however they are one of the things sleep physicians are trained to screen for and they might offer something like Seroquel or more likely Trazodone which do have sedative effects but also can help regulate patients moods if they're treatment resistant regarding possible mood disorders.

      Essentially sleep is complicated and the only way to be able to reduce your sleep need is to ensure you are getting optimal quality contiguous sleep.
