  • illithidbane

    Presumably nothingness is outside the sphere. It's not as much some barrier at the edge. The universe is just all the stuff and energy that exists. If you moved past the furthest reaches, then you (part of the universe) have expanded into that void.

    Drop a salt shaker on the ground. All the grains of salt are all the stars. The "universe" is the circle of spilled salt. Now blow on the pile in the center, causing grains of salt to fly outward in all directions. The "universe" just expanded to be a larger circle of salt. What's outside the salt? Nothing. We draw an imaginary line to measure how far the salt has flown from when the shaker broke. The Big Bang flung matter far and wide, but it's just a growing radius of stuff being farther apart.
