    • petitbleu

      Making blue cheese at home is super tricky. I know some very enthusiastic home cheesemakers that have tried it, but you're better off sampling blue cheeses and experimenting. It would be very difficult to create the ideal conditions for aging blue cheese at home. Stilton and Valdeon are very strong blue cheeses, and Fourme d'Ambert and gorgonzola dolce are lovely sweeter (relatively speaking) blues. There are so many others, but those are the ones that come to mind that are readily available at a decent cheese counter.
