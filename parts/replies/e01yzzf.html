  • serpentjaguar

    While I absolutely respect Harari and genuinely enjoyed "Sapiens," I think he's demonstrably wrong about humans making the jump from prey to predator overnight. Everything from the fossil record and what we know about non-human primate behavior indicates that our ancestors were opportunistic omnivores for hundreds of thousands of years at the bare minimum, but probably much longer.

    Add to that the fact that every single large predator you mention would have been a prey species not much earlier than the late Pleistocene at latest --when there were much bigger versions of wolves, bears and lions-- and you can see Harari's argument doesn't really hold much water. (University of Montana Biologist/historian Dan Flores, for example, convincingly argues that North American black bears instinctively avoid unforested land not out of fear of modern grizzlies or wolves --which might chase them off, but hardly see them as prey-- but rather as a genetically encoded response to the presence of giant predators such as the short-faced bear or the saber-toothed cats.)
