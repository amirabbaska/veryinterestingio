    The only answer I've seen in this thread with any crediblity. Dimensional lumber is a grab bag. If you want a true 2x4 piece of lumber go to the sawmill and get 8/4 lumber and rip it down. Or you can get lumber by the board foot. Other than those options, you're paying for convenience in a rough-build situation. This is why some people choose 5/8" drywall. It helps hide the imperfections in different dimensions of the lumber that supports it.
