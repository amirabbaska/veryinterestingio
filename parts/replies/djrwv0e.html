      I did say a sizable hole. Sure if you have a very small leak then sure it could take a little while but if you have a hole large enough to develope a large amount of force the pressure differential is likely to dissipate extremely quickly. Quick calculations show that if you had a 20 in diameter hole like one large enough to rip large heavy objects into space then the initial volumetric flow rate would be roughly 2950 ft^3/ second. That flow rate would be enough to empty a 2000 square ft home of oxygen in roughly .7 seconds (if the initial pressure remained constant).

      Obviously the pressure wouldn't remain constant which would increase the length of the evacuation process but it would also significantly decrease force generated by such a pressure differential. The pressure should follow an exponential decrease meaning it will only have any really pulling power for a short period of time under most circumstances.
