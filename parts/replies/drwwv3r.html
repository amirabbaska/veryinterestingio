      • ssaltmine

        Depends on how much current, that is how much energy, you try to extract from it. You can still use a battery when it is 0 Celsius, but you may only extract a tiny amount of power, like a few milliwatts.

        Under ambient temperatures of 20 C to 30 C, you may extract watts. So, the order of magnitude changes by a hundred.

        Roughly speaking, lithium ion batteries work okay from 15 C to 60 C. Lower temperatures require lower energy extraction as I mentioned; and at 0 C it is rather difficult to extract any energy.

        Obviously, many devices work in cold weather, for example, there are many Tesla electric vehicles using batteries in Norway. These machines essentially preheat the battery to a working temperature, maybe 20 C, before using it. Then the electrochemical reaction also produces heat, so the battery temperature remains more or less stable.
