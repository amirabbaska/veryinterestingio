    • lucky_ducker

      But it can be done. More than 10 years ago, a 1.5 mile stretch of interstate in downtown Indianapolis needed to be completely rebuilt - the stretch where I-65 and I-70 are concurrent. It was decided the fix would be the "Hyperfix:" they completely closed the section. Both directions. Of two major interstate highways.

      It was inconvenient, to be sure. It added ten minutes to my commute - but just for three months. Estimates were the project would have taken nine months if they had tried to keep the highways open. And it would have cost twice as much.
