    You may have gotten this in your google search, but the gripping in water hypothesis has interesting biological support. Strangely, your fingers "pruning" in water is an active and intentional process. It seems to be controlled by the nervous system rather than a passive osmotic process as was previously thought. This was first noticed in people who have amputated and re-attached fingers. When they put their hand underwater, the re-attached finger doesn't prune up like the others. Re-attachment involves the repair of blood vessels, tendons, and muscles, but does not repair the nerves to your finger. So it seems like your central nervous system controls pruning underwater. While this doesn't prove that the point of fingerprints is underwater gripping, it seems like a pretty intricate system to just be a coincidence!
