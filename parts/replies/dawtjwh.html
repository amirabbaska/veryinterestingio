    Don’t jump to conclusions, don’t limit your sources to those you know will agree with you, and don’t be afraid to challenge yourself. It’s a bit more work, but the reward is worth it.

    I think it's really important to point out that these things take more work because they are NOT the default functioning of your brain. The grey matter in your head does not WANT to contradict itself, and it requires not just time but emotional fortitude to critique your news. This isn't just true about things you care about - once you've had a thought, that is the preferred thought. It's your "frame."

    If you're new to the "holy shit, I could be wrong about ANYTHING" phase of learning (this phase is recurring - you'll get it when you're 70 if you care to), pay attention when you start to fact-check your news. You will literally feel uncomfortable when reading contradictory facts, and will feel a sense of relief when you corroborate something that seemed like it may have been untrue.

    Bias isn't something that happens because we watch shit news, we watch shit news because we're biased - and bias is built into the fundamental mechanics of the human brain.

    EDIT: Hell, you could argue bias IS the fundamental mechanism of the human brain.
