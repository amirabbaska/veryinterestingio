  • Satsuz

    Warts are caused by a virus, not by blisters. If your blisters indeed remained unbroken, you shouldn't have been at any increased risk for developing warts, and even if they were opened up you'd still have to come into contact with a virus that results in a wart. Blisters do not automatically become warts. Chances are what you saw was either the early stages of that same viral infection and thus you already had warts (which, as Wiki says, can resemble a solid blister), or you contracted the virus in that area later on completely by coincidence. Your mother's advice to avoid popping them is not to blame.

    Fun fact, though: Many of the treatments to remove warts involve creating blisters.
