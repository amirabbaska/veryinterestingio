    Minor point: the phrase was just "per se [letter]", not "and per se [letter]"; the first "and" in the phrase which later became "ampersand" is because the character in question was the last one in the list, not because it was part of the stock phrase. Thus the letter i was "per se I", not "and per se I", and when referring to the epershand (why am I using the Scots name? I have no idea. I'm not Scottish) it would just have been "per se and" outside the context of reciting the alphabet.
