    • RobusEtCeleritas

      Running an accelerator just to measure foil thicknesses would be a waste, but knowing your target thickness is important for any nuclear physics experiment involving a target. It's a small, necessary part of the experiment, certainly not the whole thing. And for sufficiently thick targets, there's no need to do this, you can just mechanically measure them.

      As for measuring foil thicknesses outside of experiments, you can buy a machine which will produce monoenergetic alpha particles, collimate them, and measure their energy downstream from the foil.

      It's not a cheap machine, but it's certainly something one can afford to have if they run a lab which produces such thin targets.
