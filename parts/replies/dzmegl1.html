    • MrKittySavesTheWorld

      Metal detectors don’t detect the presence of metal in the way most people think; rather, they detect changes in the magnetic permeability of the field generated by the detector.
      So when you move a piece of metal within the field of the detector, it alters the detector’s magnetic field, and the detector is designed to react when it’s magnetic field is altered.
      That’s why metal detectors don’t detect themselves; the metal in them is static, unmoving, so it doesn’t alter the magnetic field.
