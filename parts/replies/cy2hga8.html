    • Snuggly_Person

      Yes, gravitational waves interact with each other, producing spacetime curvature of their own, though trying to split up a hierarchy of "waves" and "metawaves" is kind of artificial. The important takeaway is that gravity can in fact be self-sustaining, and that there are nontrivial solutions to Einstein's equations that have no matter in them whatsoever.
