  • NoodlesInAHayStack

    And even if they don't terminate themselves, the majority of mutations have no effect on the organism (suppose you mutate a protein in a section that isn't really required for enzymatic activity).

    An ELI5 example of this: pretend your car is a protein. It's normal function is to take a person from point A to point B. Let's say we mutate the car with a baseball bat with the words "radiation" on the side (classy). If we hit the wheel, the car might still work but it might constantly turn in one direction, making the journey from A to B difficult.

    Now let's hit the car with our radiation on the hood of the car. This will most likely have no noticeable effect on the journey to Charlie's chocolate factory, but it might have a larger effect on the car if the hood gets hit multiple times in the future, eventually reaching an important part of the car.
