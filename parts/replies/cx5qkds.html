    • Holy_City

      I don't think there is an ELI5. Like processors didn't just pop up one day, it took like 20 or so years of digital circuit design before people even had the idea of this kind of machine.

      I guess a better ELI5 is to think of a computer like a car. It's got a bunch of parts, but the most important is the engine. The engine itself is a very complex machine but does a simple thing, turn energy in gas into motion. Similarly, a processor is a very complex system that does some very simple things, move data from one place to another and do some math with it.

      And just like an engine, to understand how it works you have to break down the goal into several individual problems. You want to be able to do math, so you need a combinational logic circuit. You want it to do more than one thing, so you need a control logic circuit. You want to be able to hold onto data, so you need chunks of memory.
