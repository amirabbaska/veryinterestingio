      In terms of acquiring the material, based on BEST as in most environmentally friendly methods:

      Aluminum Glass Cardboard Plastic

      And here I’m ranking cardboard lower because we shouldn’t really be devoting forest products to cardboard, even if it’s “renewable”,

      Aluminum highest because of the high potential for recyclability.

      Glass, high potential for recycling but likely increased production negatives (SO2 gas production).

      Plastic, unsure if 100% “renewable” plastic exists yet, so can’t really think of any positives. Anything that’s derived from petroleum should be eliminated.
