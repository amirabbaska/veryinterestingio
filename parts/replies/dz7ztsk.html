  • hujo83

    It’s also a matter of most bread having a crust that’s relatively hard, and a center that’s soft and spongey. If you apply pressure to the crust, the center just gives. If you remove the crust, bread is easily cut by any knife. Tomatoes are kind of similar, but much less so. You only need a decently sharp blade to cut tomatoes. Some foods are hard to cut because of density and adhesion, like cheese, where a wire is often the best tool.
