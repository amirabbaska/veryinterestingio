    I don't know much about this in particular, but what you're saying doesn't really jive with what I know from physics.

    1. The planck time is the time it takes light in perfect vacuum to cover a planck length. The planck length is just the length where are modern understanding of physics breaks down. It's not really a theoretically important concept.

    2. Presumably the chronon is a particle that is needed to make certain theories work. It's fundamentally distinct from the planck length, and the OP is probably looking for a discussion of the nature of the theories that describe chronons, not this generic "it's a quantum unit of time" business.

    For the unit of time version, that's like asking what evidence there is that a second exists, or that a minute exists. It is an extremely short period of time, possibly the shortest discrete unit of time, much like the numbers 1, 2, 3, 4.

    This is a false equivalence. The evidence for any potential chronic is very different from the evidence of a minute or a second, and if it's real, it would also be distinct from a chronic because it would be a thing with very real physical significance, unlike the second. It would be very akin to the difference between degrees (seconds) and radians (chronons).
