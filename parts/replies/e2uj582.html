    The gill filaments, or lamellae, act like hair strands that separate in water, but stick together when pulled out. The reason fish suffocate on land is due to the lamellae ‘sticking together’ out of water and reducing the surface area so less O2 is diffused into the bloodstream, thus, suffocation
