    • CharlesOSmith

      To clarify, the Krebs cycle is not exclusively used to produced the NADH and FADH2 used by the ETC to pump protons. The intermediates of the Krebs cycle are also central to several pathways for amino acid and nucleotide synthesis. So depending on the cell type and energetic status, the Krebs cycle or some part of it could be turning substrate over very rapidly, but not transporting electrons to the ETC.

      Also, if the Krebs cycle is working faster than the ETC, the extra NADH is converted to NADPH by the NNT and used to help reduce the antioxidant proteins after they dismuate ROS (superoxide anions or hydrogen peroxide for example)

      Additionally if the proton gradient producing half of the ECT completely stops (such as would happen when there is no oxygen) complex V of the ECT (the ATP synthase) will work in reverse to consume ATP and pump protons to maintain the proton gradient.

      So classic energy generation does require the Krebs cycle and the ETC to work in tandem, the Krebs cycle has many functions other than sending electrons to the ETC, and the ETC can work without the Krebs cycle in an emergency.
