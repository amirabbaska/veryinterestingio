    • tavelkyosoba

      I add that because I'm an engineer and don't work in perfect physics land.

      The tires are not stationary with respect to the car and air frame of reference, they are spinning. They spin 25mph backwards as they sweep across the road, which is now 25mph into the wind. This would drag air back and kick it up to where passengers can feel it. It would not be significant, but it would be there.

      Same with the road, it is not stationary in that frame of reference and may produced interactions. Any bumps or obstacles would slow and disturb our perfect 25mph wind, which the passengers would feel as a airflow in the backwards direction.

      So in perfect physics land, they will feel no airflow. In the real world they will feel modest airflow from disturbing factors.
