• GregoPDX

    I worked at a movie theater in high school back in the mid-90s (including 1994, considered one of the best years ever in cinema) and we had really simple pricing for both pop and popcorn. Small, medium, and large popcorn were $4.00, $4.50, and $5.00, respectively. Small, medium, and large drinks were $3.50, $3.75, and $4, respectively. This made for the easy 'up sell' - if someone ordered a medium popcorn you'd as if they'd like a large for 'only 50 cents more', and they'd usually take it.

    People are very open to lots of up selling. After a shift one time my manager was going through counts and saw that we sold 50 Milk Duds more than usual for a Saturday afternoon, he thought it was a mis-count. What had happened is that the concession folks, just for fun, decided to always offer Milk Duds for every order. They were very surprised how many people said yes to the really overpriced candy.
