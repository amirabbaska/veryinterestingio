    • jaa101

      In WWI the pilot was required to manually operate the gun so it had to be near him; there could only be one gun and it had to fire through the propeller. In WWII fighters the pilots just pushed a button to fire so there was some electrically operated automatic mechanism for firing the guns. This meant they could have multiple guns and mount them in the wings, angled in to converge some distance ahead. Firing through the propeller was no longer an issue. I'm pretty sure that if there were any WWII single-seat, single-engine fighters that fired through the propeller, they would have been obsolete types.

      • thenebular

        It wasn't that the pilot had to manually operate the gun, many WW1 planes had a button on the stick to operate the trigger. It was that with the handling of WW1 planes, it was far easier to aim straight ahead of the plane rather than try and worry about aiming guns outside the propeller. Also the machine guns of the time need to be manually reloaded so keeping the gun close to the pilot made reloading easier.
