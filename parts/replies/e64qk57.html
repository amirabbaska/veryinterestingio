    How come when helium 4 becomes a boson it doesn't all occupy the same lowest energy state?

    • Sharlinator

      Helium-4 doesn't "become" a boson, it is a boson. And the answer is that in real world everything is above zero kelvin and thus has quite a bit of energy. Reaching very low energy states requires some ingenious cooling techniques. But if you do it, you'll find that helium-4 forms a Bose-Einstein condensate exactly because it's a boson.
