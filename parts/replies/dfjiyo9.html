  • Yuccaphile

    I would like to add to this that what is important with heat (the transfer of thermal energy) is the temperature difference between the two items.

    The interior of the oven will still be "warm" after you're done placing, say, creme brulee very carefully in the oven. So the food starts being cooked immediately. It only takes the oven a minute or two to warm up again, depending on how well your oven works. If you have a convection oven, it helps to turn the fan off a few seconds before opening the door.

    But it only takes a couple minutes to heat up again. Once the oven is above the boiling point of water, it heats up quite quickly. Ovens aren't exact science, they rarely hold the exact temperature you set them to, but flux a little above a below through heating cycles. This is all why all ovened recipes give a little leeway in cooking times.
