      • Elkazan

        You are describing a situation which is identical to the previous ones, except your initial distance is 1350 units, and you're using miles instead of kilometers. We can therefore use the modified solution

        x1(t) = -1350e^(-t) + 1350

        Your friend, meanwhile, is traveling akin to the simple linear equation

        x2(t) = 65t

        The simplest way to compare distances is to check how long it would take you to reach the destination, then check what distance your friend has traveled in that time and see if it is equal to or greater than 1350 (Spoiler: it is not.)

        Simply equate your distance equation to the point in position where you would get out of the car, and you get

        x1(t) = -1350e^(-t) + 1350 = 1350 - 3

        Solving for t, you have

        t = -ln(3/1350) = 6.109

        where ln is the natural logarithm, or logarithm with base e. You then travel 3 miles at a speed of 1 mile per 6 minutes, which is 0.1 hour, for a total of 0.3 hour. Therefore, your total travel time for the whole 1350 miles is 6.409 hours.

        However, in that time, your friend traveling at 65 miles per hour has only traveled

        x2(6.409) = 416.6 miles

        and is not even a third of the way there.
