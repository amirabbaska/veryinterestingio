      • niemandnirgendwer

        It's also important to remember that the sun is running on 'normal' hydrogen while fusion reactors usually use deuterium ("heavy") hydrogen. The latter is much, much more reactive. With normal hydrogen we'd not stand a chance to create a power plant whatsoever. Even replicating the conditions in the sun wouldn't be sufficient. The sun emits only about 5mw of energy per kg. It's obviously more in the core, but even with a ton of sun core you'd not reach the energy output of a candle.

        This is good thing btw. If the sun were more reactive it would just explode. And since it's so big the reaction rate is more than enough to keep us warm.
