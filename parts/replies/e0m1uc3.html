  • minnsoup

    I would also add along with who you talk to, it GREATLY depends on what you're studying and trying to prove different. For genetics and finding SNPs, advisors have told me a sample size of 1000 is a general rule of thumb but that's still a rule of thumb. For differently expressed genes, we can sometimes get the sample size per population down to as low as 4 or 5. Plant response to the application of something can be between 4 and 50.

    Generally speaking more is better, and sometimes no matter how many samples you take the response will NOT be normal prompting a nonparametric test, even after transforming data. I think 30 is an arbitrary number for those starting statistics.
