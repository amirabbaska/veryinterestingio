      I like this paper because it's simple but leads to so many questions - and there are certainly shortcomings in the experimental design that limits its applicability - choosing the same undifferentiated cell type to measure and making those measurements after growing the cells under the same culture conditions certainly predisposes the comparison to show similarity.

      There's definitely more allometric variation in differentiated cells than in stem cells - as cells specialize they take on more unique functional characteristics, hence more unique structural characteristics. Vertebrate blood cells are a great example - lots of variation in size, shape, nucleation (for erythrocytes), chemical composition (for leukocytes). There is some cool research on the factors which affect cell volume across species - here is a nice (if a bit old) article that addresses genomic composition in relation to cell size.
