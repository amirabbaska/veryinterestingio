    Click for Addiction Scale by Substance

    Some of the big drugs like Ecstacy, LSD and Cannabis are in fact lower on the addiction scale and lower for overall risk than the 2 legal and taxed substances.

    Drug addiction depends on the type of drug as well as it's effects on the perceptions by messing with our memory machines (our brains).

    When you really bring it down to it's core, the only connection the consciousness has with the world is what it can store in the brain. Without memory, thought does not exist. It's the amalgamation of the links we've made over time between our neurons that creates the memories we use to create logic based on experience to understand our surroundings.

    Addiction is mind-borne process. You can alter the memories in the brain by creating experiences with alcohol, but those experiences are ones that you created while on the drug. They can similarly be un-done through meditation, drug based psychotherapy (to allow faster remapping of the neurotransmitters), and self hypnotherapy.

    Source: Myself. I was diagnosed with major depressive disorder triggered by childhood PTSD, I self medicated with MDMA, self hypnotherapy, meditation and LSD. Both of these substances are used recreationally but they have huge value in therapeutic scenarios due to their ability to link areas of the brain and allow child-like states of impressionability. In some cases, complete loss of the ego resulting in a psychological rebirth.

    We need to be pushing the front lines of these treatments. They've already shown greater success than some traditional 'treatments' (if you can call mind-numbers like SSRIs treatments) and they make complete sense in terms of both logical psychology and anecdotal tests.
