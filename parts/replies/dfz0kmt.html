    Fun fact, the reason why ice is more slippery the warmer it is is purely because the pressure of you walking on it melts the ice a little bit. If it is colder out, you don't melt as much ice by stepping on it.

    It's also how ice skates work. That blade is pressing down on the ice, melting it ever so slightly and making it slippery.

    This isn't true. It's an old explanation that has since been rejected. For example, a 150lb person on ice skates decreases the melting point of ice by about 0.03 degrees F. The two prevailing theories are the intrinsic water layer of ice near the melting point (due to vibration, probably) and friction between feet/skates and the ice generating enough heat to melt it. Either way, pressure is not a significant factor.
