    • 4mpers4nd

      First, grape yields are much lower than cane. Especially in places like Brazil, where it's actually feasible to use cane as a source for biofuel. Very few other places in the world approach their productivity, and it's safer to have two options to sell r&d crops than just one.

      Second, grapes also make tasty, tasty wine that's more expensive than sugar and the plants take years to mature. Same with pears, apples, and many berries. Time is money, so producers will apply highest yield to their specialized crops. Sugar is definitely not the highest for non-cane/beer applications.

      A cane or beet sprout is chucked into a cleared field in a developing country's cheap land of non-grape friendly micro-climate, grown for a season using cheap labour, and then bulk shipped for processing.
