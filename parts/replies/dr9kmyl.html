    • wonkey_monkey

      How much of the shapes on the wires in that image is "real"? It looks like it's a little bit interpolated.

      • reallymostlyharmless

        Not intentionally interpolated, at least. There are two problems: secondary electrons are exiting the filament preferredly at the sharp edges, leading to higher brightness. This is partially what gives the image depth, and we need this to show topography, however it reduces the focus on fine detail. Problem two is that it was originally stored as bitmap, then converted to png. Then I reopened that png in GIMP, cropped parts off and saved it again. And I'm sure that does not actually help image quality :)

        The sharpness was adjusted on the melting striations on the top of the molten ball in the top quarter of the image. Anything outside of there is slightly out of focus anyway.
