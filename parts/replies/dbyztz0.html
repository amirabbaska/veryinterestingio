  • joebidensidepiece

    I work in product development for cosmetics and personal care. Some materials like petroleum jelly or butters (Shea, cocoa) do act as barriers and do not absorb into skin, they are a barrier that locks in existing moisture.

    However certain ingredients can penetrate the skin but it depends on the particle size, what they are mixed with and how they are applied.

    Nanoparticles have a better chance of permeating the skin but are more regulated on the chance that it presents a danger.

    The marketing angle is that thinner formulas such as serums have a lighter texture that easily absorb and contain more expensive active ingredients to benefit the skin. Then you lay thicker creams and lotions on top to lock it in.
