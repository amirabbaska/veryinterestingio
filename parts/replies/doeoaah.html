    This is true. Also, every time you drink or eat something, the residue stays in your mouth for thirty minutes. Drinking a carbonated and/or sugary beverage all in one go is much better, for your teeth, than constantly sipping on aforesaid drink for several hours. The bacteria in your mouth that cause caries (cavities) eats the sugar in the residue and excretes acid as a waste product, thereby compounding the damage. Source: am dental hygienist
