      Hey no prob. For the fever, i’m gonna make up a number, but i’d say >95% of all fevers are okay. Really. A lot of people come in saying they have a fever, but that’s just your body doing what it needs to do. For kids, if a fever is CONSTANT for >4 days we have to consider a few bad things. Also people with AIDS, people on chemotherapy, or people with immune deficiencies can have complications from just fever itself. And yes, there are things to do about it like immunoglobulin therapy, but seriously, for the average person, don’t even worry about that.

      Aspiration into the lungs unfortunately you just gotta sit these people up, put them on a nasal cannula for oxygen, and hope they don’t aspirate. If they are very high risk for aspirating or can’t “protect their airway” as we say, then we sedate and paralyze the patient and put them on a ventilator for their own safety. But of course, in very specific situations, usually in stroke parients who can’t control how they swallow.

      The immune system, anyone with an adequate immune system, like i said before, if you don’t have AIDS, are not on chemo, or don’t have genetic immunodeficienciesike agammaglobulinemia, then you will respond to the flu as predicted 99% of the time.

      Here’s the real ELI5. Your body turns up the temp as a gamble. It gambles that the virus is more likely to die under the inflammatory conditions rather than your good cells. So the fever itself is just a natural process that almost always works in the end.

      Unfortunately young babies and old people can’t regulate that inner body thermostat well and can fry too many of their own normal functioning instead.
