      Science is about telling the most accurate, most predictive stories we can about the world. We advance science when we find better and better stories, or develop new tools to tell better stories. But it is not finding "the truth". We just want to approximate the truth as well as we can, and so we're bound to be wrong in all sorts of ways.

      There isn't a better system for understanding the world than science though, so we have to take the faults as they come.

    • Krexington_III

      That's how we discover lots of new things, actually!

      The Neutrino was discovered kind of like this. Scientists measured the energy that comes out of certain radioactive processes (when atoms break into pieces) and decided that there was too little energy coming out.

      And then someone said "what if there's a tiny amount of energy coming out in the form of a particle travelling almost the speed of light and which behaves like this and like this and like this? That would be undetectable to us, but then the math works out entirely".

      Then scientists sat down and figured out how to detect this probably almost undetectable particle travelling at almost the speed of light. And they found it! It's there! And now the math works out.
