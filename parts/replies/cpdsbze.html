      So, basically in this instance a committee drafted a bill and voted internally if it should proceed to be tried by the Senate and this one guy said...

      Yeah, pretty much, although obviously he's got to get a majority of the committee members to agree with him. (Each committee has X members from each party, BTW, but the head of the committee is always from the majority party.)

      If you delegate drafting bills to a committee then why does it matter if they do it in secret or not?

      Because if word gets out that the majority party is letting popular bills languish in committee, the majority may end up as the minority after the next election.

      Can anyone interfere with the committee's work?

      The party leaders in the House and Senate could lean on the committee members. So could the President, or any of their constituents.

      This is why being head of a committee, or even being on it, is a big deal. You get to kill bills before they've even progressed from zygote to fetus.
