  • Farnsworthson

    Not just combat sports. There's a line in the old West Country song The Bell Ringing (also known as "The Men of North Lew" and a couple of other names), which is about a Change Ringing competition, that goes "They rang for a belt and a hat laced with gold". The song and lyrics were collected from at least two different Devon singers in the late 19th century.

    The Yetis did a really excellent version of the song in the 1960s or so that not only sounded excellent but really evoked the bells as well; sadly, I can't find it on the net, or I'd link it. This solo a cappella version is a little dragging, but probably the best I managed to find. Needs an instrument behind it, though.

    I wonder whether it started out as a low-tech thing, a poor man's trophy - get the local artisans to knock up a fancy belt with brass fittings and the like (akin to horse brasses, say) as a token prize to be competed over. Good craftsmen would be able to make something that would look pretty special, without it having to cost an arm and a leg.
