    You've got it backwards. Its the linear velocity that is constant at large radius, which means the angular velocity is changing. If the galaxy was entirely undergoing solid body rotation it would have a constant angular velocity and linear velocity proportional to radius. What we used to expect was solid body rotation (v proportional to r) and then a keplerian decline (v declining proportional to 1/sqrt(r)). Instead we go from the linear solid body rotation into constant linear velocity at larger radii which indicates there is extra matter further out from the centre which can't account for and differential rotation (a changing angular velocity).
