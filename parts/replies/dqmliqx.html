I read OP's question something like: "when a particle changes from its high energy state to a lower energy state, how quickly do the wavefunction and spatial probability function change?"     To expand a bit on what /u/robusetceleritas is describing with exponentially decaying excited states: if you have 1000 identical particles in excited states, you can trace the number of excited states as decaying exponentially in time.         The probability explanation makes sense on the collective, describing how often transitions occur over time.
