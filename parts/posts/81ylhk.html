    ELI5; why does the USA need to import 90 percent of the aluminum it consumes when there is so much aluminum recycling going on?

    TokyoJokeyo A:

    A lot of scrap aluminum from the U.S. is actually recycled in other countries. So it's being recycled, but in the trade statistics you'll see exports of aluminum scrap as well as imports of aluminum.

    ReaperZ28 A:

    Recycled aluminum also contains impurities that make it unusable for certain applications. For instance in aluminum engine blocks it can create a porous case and cause oil to leak directly through the block. A lot of aluminum needs to be imported to fill the need of non recycled aluminum.
