    Q:

    ELI5: What is the point of poisonous fruits? Aren’t them supposed to be edible so seeds can spread easier?

    ameoba A:

    They're generally not poisonous to all animals. The animals they "want" to eat them can eat them safely. For example, while it's not technically a poison, the chemicals in chile peppers that make them burn only affect mammals. Birds can safely eat them and birds tend to fly & travel longer distances, allowing the plant to spread further.

    In other cases, the plant might not always be poisonous. They might only be poisonous (or taste horrible) before they're ripe. This prevents them from being eaten before the seeds are ready to germinate.

    Some fruits that are poisonous to us are not to local wildlife. Some birds and animals have ways to cope with the toxicity of different fruits which allows them to still be spread.

    BurningBrightly A:

    Plants "want" to entice the best carrier/distributor for their fruit. An animal that is able to eat the fruit but can't digest the seeds. Which sometimes gets a bit too specific, as with avocado where now humans do the job of extinct giant sloths.

    Not all fruit need to be eaten. Some only become able to sprout after cycling through an animal, others aren't that picky and only use animals for transport. Tomato seeds sprout great when the fruit rotted around them. Strawberries mostly procreate with runners, the berries aren't all that important once a plant took hold but if ants spread seeds a bit further, it's faster than growing runners that far.

    Blackthorn fruit are gross until the first frost. They don't have much flesh to offer but because the plant is timed to ripen when there's barely anything else to eat, it gets away with low-effort fruit that still get reliably transported.

    You know how humans love to eat grapes but grapes are really poisonous to dogs? Well all fruits are like that, tasty to some species but poisonous to others.

    It’s because a plant wants to make its fruit really appealing to whatever species will spread the seeds but doesn’t want other species to eat it, so it makes it off putting to them.
