    I_Pariah A:

    CG is not required even today to do that. The effect can be achieved entirely via VFX techniques.

    FYI: VFX these days typically involve a computer but just because something uses a computer does not mean it is "CGI". "CG" is just one tool in creating modern VFX.

    The effect can be achieved this way (how I'd do it on a low budget in comparison with doing it in the 80s) explained in a way that hopefully the average person can understand:

    1. Shoot the scene normally with the Predator/actor in the shot (limit camera movement or make sure the camera is static to make things easier).

    2. Shoot the exact same shot but without the actor so you have footage of the background behind it.

    3. Rotoscope the actor (essentially "trace" and create a selection of the character for the entire shot. OR chroma key the actor (basically a less manual method of creating a selection) if they wore a colored suit. This will create what is known as a "matte".

    4. In the editing or VFX software use the matte as a stencil so that everything inside it is footage of the background behind the actor. Actor is gone. To get that repeating edge effect you could just nudge the edges of a duplicate of the footage multiple times with each duplicate offset by a certain number of pixels. You can then arrange that however you want and however many times you want. I would probably do it for the left, right, top, and bottom to give a more dimensional (3D) look. This entire effect will be contained within the shape of the matte, which is basically the shape of the actor so it looks like it is "invisible" or using the "optic camo" or whatever you wanna call it.
