• Biology why is it that when we put cold feet in hot water it feels like it's burning more, but when we put hot feet in cold water it's relieving?

    NEW
    Q:

    ELI5: why is it that when we put cold feet in hot water it feels like it's burning more, but when we put hot feet in cold water it's relieving?

    dragonx254 A:

    The direction of heat transfer.

    When you're putting your cold feet in hot water, the hot water is transferring its heat to your feet and trying to reach equilibrium. There's a huge difference in temperature between your feet and the water so a lot of heat is transferred, so it feels extra hot.

    The opposite is true when you put hot feet in cold water. The heat in your feet is being dissipated through the cold water.
