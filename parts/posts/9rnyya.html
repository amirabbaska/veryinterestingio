    KannonFawder A:

    It temporarily reduces inflammation through the application of pressure, which feels good. It also puts pressure on pain receptors, which feels bad.

    summit_slayer14 A:

    Gate Control Theory: sensory nerves are faster than pain nerves, so when you activate sensory nerves when you press down on the muscle, they block the pain nerves’ ability to tell your brain ouch. This pressing also causes “tissue damage” which is why it hurts a little, but ultimately feels better due to not as many pain signals getting to the brain.
