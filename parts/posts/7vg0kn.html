• Who vs Whom?

    NEW
    Q:

    The most I've ever been taught is "If you don't know for sure, just say 'Who' otherwise you'll look like a pretentious idiot.".
    -High School English teacher

    rednax1206 A:

    If you're asking who or whom, think about how the answer will be phrased. If "he" then ask who. If "him" then ask whom.

    • "To whom it may concern" - it concerns him.
    • "Who wanted the sushi?" - he wanted the sushi.
    • "Whom did you expect at this meeting?" - we expected him at the meeting.
    • "Who's going to clean this up?" - he will clean this up.

    _pure_supercool A:

    Just ask yourself a question using this formula by replacing some words with their corresponding pronoun:

    He/She = Who
    Him/Her = Whom

    Example: "Who/Whom did it?" --> He or she did it. "Who"

    Example: "To who/whom should I give this paper?" --> I should give it to him or her. "Whom"
