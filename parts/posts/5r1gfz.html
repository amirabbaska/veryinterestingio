    s37747 A:

    The locks actually help ships get above sea level for a stretch of the canal and then another set of locks lowers the ship back down to sea level. There's no real major difference in the sea levels. The suez canal in Egypt is lockless, meaning water can flow from the Indian ocean to the Mediterranean sea, but again this doesn't affect either bodies of water much.

    LouCheOne A:

    Locks are a combination between a dam and a ship elevator.

    In building the canal they constructed a set steps over the continental divide and used locks to hold enough water in place to both lift ships and allow them to float across each step.

    If the locks were not there, you'd simply have a sometimes shallow river on each side of the mountains that no one could navigate a ship all the way up.
