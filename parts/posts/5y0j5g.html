    ELI5: What happens that causes our voice to sound "old" when we're 70+ years old?

    iBleedWhenIpoop A:

    As we age our vocal cords and surrounding muscles start to wear out which results in a change of pitch and adds extra vibrations making our voices sound more rough. You can think of a new guitar string compared to an old stretched out, worn, or frayed guitar string.

    Bianchez A:

    Several things occur, the larynx also ages at about 30/40 years of age. Cartilages begin to ossify including the cricoiarytenoid joint (responsible for bringing the vocal folds together). So the vocal folds will not be able to come together as much as they used to, this may make the voice sound more breathy since air is escaping during speech.

    The thyroarytenoid is the muscle that makes up our vocal folds. It begins to atrophy, so the vocal folds get thinner and decrease in elasticity. Women experience vocal fold swelling, and the larger the vocal folds, the lower the frequency. That is why women's voices lower with age.

    Because of atrophy and losing muscle mass, men tend to have higher voices as they age. A lot of this is hormonal factors coming into play. For women, it has to do with less estrogen, for men, less testosterone.
