    Q:

    As the speed of two colliding particles increases, does the chance of quantum tunneling increase as well or remain consistent per head-on?

    RobusEtCeleritas A:

    The probability of tunneling increases rapidly with their relative energy.

    In this figure the green curve represents the probability of barrier penetration for two similarly-charged particles as a function of their relative energy.

    Once the relative energy exceeds the barrier height, tunneling is no longer necessary.
