    Hagenaar A:

    Pressure and time created the ice of earth's glaciers and ice sheets. Accumulation of snow over older snow applies the pressure. After a year of compression, a compacted snow called firn or névé is formed. More time and pressure squeezes the air from this and ice is formed.

    CrateDane A:

    Depending on temperature. It is entirely possible it will become liquid water instead. Increased pressure makes the liquid phase (which is denser than regular ice) more favorable.

    At lower temperatures, the snow/ice would just end up in other (denser) ice phases than the one we usually see on Earth.
