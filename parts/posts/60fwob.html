    Helaskold A:

    Two feet = four points of contact with the ground (heel and ball of foot x2).

    One foot = two points of contact with the ground.

    It's like getting a motorcycle to stay upright vs. a car.

    Theodore_the_python A:

    Alot of people are missing the fact that our legs balance our weight on the sides of our core. If you stand on one leg youre putting all your weight on one side, then you need to make the microadjustments in weigh distribution and foot placement to find balance.

    Obviously we dont do that alot and its hard, add that to our ears being a big part in balance and it gets tough. Athletes use balance boards and points of reference to train themselves to get a good balance on both and either feet.

    Muu-dzic A:

    Let's just say that you cut a human body vertically into two symmetrical(assuming) pieces and they are placed side to side. Now, what would happen if one of the legs is cut off? The upper part of that side of the body would fall down due to gravity right? This force was initially supported by that leg which was cut off. The torque due to this force is what causes the imbalance.

    Torque is the force that tends to cause rotation or twisting motion.
