• Why is a rice cooker considered a superior method of cooking rice?

    NEW
    Q:

    I was looking into buying a rice cooker but I am cheap. I was told rice cookers are the preferred way to cook rice, but they just look like crockpots to me. What makes them so superior at cooking rice compared to a crockpot, or just cooking rice on the stove?

    ameoba A:

    A rice cooker isn't necessarily superior, it's just vastly more convenient. If you eat rice on a regular basis, it's just one less thing to worry about - you can toss your rice in the rice cooker before you start making the meal and BAM it's ready to eat when you've finished everything else.

    I buy rice by the 25# bag so I eat a fair bit of it. A $20 appliance that makes cooking it a zero-thought part of meal prep is a good investment for me.

    khorbin A:

    The most obvious benefit is that it makes it impossible to screw up rice, and frees up an extra burner on your stove top when you're cooking other things. Basically, it makes rice an afterthought, a thing that just gets done while you do other things. You don't have to set a timer, or keep half an eye on your rice, ever again.

    But it's a lot more versatile than that. Because it will automatically switch from Cook to Warm when all the water evaporates, anything where you want it to stop cooking and stay warm after it reaches 212Â°F becomes trivial to cook. I've used it for every kind of grain from pearled barley to quinoa, lentils, couscous, polenta, etc.

    If you want to go really crazy, you can use it like a slow cooker, get one with a steamer basket and use it to steam vegetables (while your rice is cooking or just to steam the veggies by themselves), make oatmeal, make or reheat soup, or go really nuts and cook things like eggs or cheesecake or banana bread in it. I even used mine as a hacky sous vide cooker a few times before I bought an immersion circulator. It's definitely not a unitasker in my kitchen.

    It's also a really simple machine, so even a cheap one will likely work for years. I bought a cheapo rice cooker for like $20 over a decade ago, and it still works perfectly.
