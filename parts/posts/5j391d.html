• Engineering Are liquid abrasives (toothpaste, rubbing/buffing compound, etc) more effective in thinner layers?

    NEW
    Q:

    I have noticed while working on my car and hand-buffing scratches that liquid abrasive products seem to be more effective when less is used. Do liquid abrasives lose efficacy when excess material "cushions" the effective abrasive surface from the cloth?

    whowereyouexpecting A:

    Yes. Liquids form a boundary layer adjacent to their surface. Transfer of mechanical movement to the boundary layer maybe impeded by increased liquid depth or low viscosity liquids. This can be overcome by applying more force to displace the liquid or increasing the velocity of the liquid's movement.

    https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flow_separation

    In short, use less or you'll need to rub harder.
