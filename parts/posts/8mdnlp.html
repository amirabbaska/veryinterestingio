    Q:

    If the universe is (generally speaking) flat, how come, as seen from telescopes, nothing lies on a single plane? Images of the CMB aren't 3D models, so why do we see galaxies in every direction?

    Caolan_Cooper A:

    When we say the universe is flat, we don't mean it in the planar sense. We are referring to the shape of spacetime. Imagine you have two lasers that are perfectly parallel to each other. In a flat universe, they will behave as we expect and remain parallel forever. In a curved universe, on the other hand, the lasers would actually start to bend towards or away from each other.
