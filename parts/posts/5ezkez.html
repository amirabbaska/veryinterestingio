• How and why belly button lint collects in your belly button. Also, why does it not collect anywhere else on the body or in clothes?

    NEW
    Q:

    ELI5: How and why belly button lint collects in your belly button. Also, why does it not collect anywhere else on the body or in clothes?

    drink_your_tea A:

    short answer: clothing fibers + body hair that grows towards a sheltered depression in your skin = belly button lint, but not "any-other-body-part lint". you need both the hair and the place for fibers to collect in order for it to work!

    longer answer: tiny fibers from your clothing are rubbed off by the hair around your navel. the hair grows in such a fashion that it "points" into your belly button - meaning, hair above it points down, and hair below it points up - and this pattern of growth helps funnel these tiny fibers into your navel, a depression where they can collect.

    since other body hair typically doesn't "funnel" lint or fibers into a protected, shallow pocket - also, since there aren't any other real protected, shallow pockets in your skin - lint isn't collected elsewhere.

    so, if you have an "outie," or a really shallow belly button, or don't have (much) hair that grows around your navel, you are likely to experience much less (if any) belly button lint.
