    agate_ A:

    There are no global space cops. That is, no global regulatory body for outer space activities. The Outer Space Treaty says

    "the activities of non-governmental entities in outer space, including the Moon and other celestial bodies, shall require authorization and continuing supervision by the appropriate State Party to the Treaty"

    That means if you're an American company that wants to launch stuff, you have to get permission from the US government, if you're Chinese, from China, etc. Each nation is responsible for damage or harm caused by its citizens' stuff.

    In the US, that approval happens through the Federal Aviation Administration, which regulates the rocket launch, and the Federal Communications Commission, which regulates satellite radio communications, and orbits to the extent that they affect communications. To my knowledge, nobody regulates the actual "being in space" part of it.
