• Biology Why is it that a very warm room makes us sleepy during the day, but keeps us awake at night?

    NEW
    Q:

    A warm room makes us fall asleep easily during the day, but at night we are supposed to fall asleep more easily when we are a little cooler than usual. ELI5

    Slavetoeverything A:

    Your body cools down as it prepares for sleep, which is naturally in the evening (due to circadian rhythm). Being too warm interrupts the neurotransmitters that assist in falling and staying asleep. This is also why a warm bath or shower before bed isn’t recommended if you already have trouble sleeping. It raises your body temp unnaturally and against what it’s trying to do.

    My body warms up after I fall asleep (I’ve been compared to a furnace or space heater while I’m sleeping, haha!) and if I happen to wake up shortly after falling asleep I’m uncomfortably hot and getting back to sleep is impossible.

    Just for the sake of information, here’s another trick to help yourself fall asleep that works with neurotransmitters:

    When you’re in bed with your eyes closed, roll them back as far as you can (it’s kind of a weird feeling to me). Hold them like that for several seconds, relax, repeat. The reason something so simple works is because your eyes roll back naturally when you’re really asleep in this fashion. Doing it deliberately signals the brain to release melatonin, which is one of our “sleep hormones” (and popular as a supplement to take at bedtime to help sleep). So there you have it.
