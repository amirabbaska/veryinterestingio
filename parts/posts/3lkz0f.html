    mvw2 A:

    It's a MPG game.

    During testing, the car should run slightly rich of stoichiometric for good emissions. Slightly rich is because emissions aren't constant, so you always oscillate between lean and rich, slightly. Rich produces CO. Lean produces NOX. NOX is worse, so you err slightly towards rich, just a tiny bit. MPG isn't the best it can be.

    Because car buyers are so focused on mpg for buying cars, this is kind of a big deal and a massive gamble that sales outweighs the risks involved of cheating.

    What did VW do?

    Well, no one knows exactly without knowing the tune and coding. However, the high NOX content means the car is running lean and doing so very specifically to increase mpg. NOX is bad, worse than CO. From an environment standpoint, running lean is worse than running rich.

    This is virtually nothing to do with power output. You won't really notice much of a difference at all. In fact, the car would make less power running lean. Again, this is only a MPG game they were playing, and it paid off for a number of years.
