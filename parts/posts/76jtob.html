• Biology Why do my limbs sometimes try to automatically copy what I'm watching in an action scene?

    NEW
    Q:

    If I'm say watching a fight scene, the actor throws a right handed punch, my right hand would automatically try to copy the move but it ends up sort of like a twitch but different in a way I can't explain. It also sometimes occurs while watching sports.

    Pelusteriano A:

    Humans are a social species. Empathy is an important part of our lifes. When you observe babies or children interact with their parents or someone they feel empathy for, you can notice they will copy their movements and behaviour. That instinct doesn't really fade away when you grow up, you just learn to control it.

    When you're watching a movie or a game, you're not really focusing on controling those minor movements, thus, you begin copying what you see on the screen.

    threefourshutthedoor A:

    From the Wikipedia article on mirror neurons.

    Everyone first learns how to do something by watching someone else do it and then copying that. For some reason, (science isn't sure because it doesn't understand it well yet,) some people are more sensitive to this than others and when they see someone doing something it triggers their muscles to try to copy it before they realize what is going on. This can even make it hard for someone to stand still if they are being told a story about how someone else is moving.
