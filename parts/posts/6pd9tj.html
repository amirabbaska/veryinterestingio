• Biology How does a Sand Bubbler Crab sift food from sand?

    NEW
    Q:

    While watching The Hunt, I saw the segment where Sand Bubbler Crabs sift food from sand. The act seemed fluid to me as they brought the sand to their mouths and quickly sorted it away to form the inflated pellets they leave behind. I was curious as to how these crabs can seemingly sort small amounts of food and sand with ease, and how their mouths worked at a mechanical level. Are there any good diagrams or explanations as how this is done?

    dragonite_myFriendxx A:

    In this paper, the dude describes the "floatation feeding process." All crabs have little appendages called maxillipeds in front of their mouths that they use to handle food, but the Sand Bubby evolved spoon-shaped maxillipeds that are really good at holding on to sand particles. So, they hold the sand with their mouth spoons and then shoot water out of their gill chambers across the sand and into their mouth. All the bacteria, algae, etc that were clinging to the sand get power-washed into the belly of the Sand Bubby.
