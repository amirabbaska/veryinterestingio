    Q:

    So if I heat iron into molten iron. How much more would I have to heat it for it to become a gas?

    The_wazoo A:

    Yes it is possible to evaporate metal, however it is incredibly difficult due to metallic bonds being so strong.

    Iron melts at 1538 °C and turns into a gas at 2862 °C. Gold is somewhat similiar, melting at 1064 °C and boiling at 2856 °C. Mercury, the only liquid metal, requires "only" 356 °C to boil.

    imsowitty A:

    I used to do this all the time in Grad school. Melt metal in a tungsten trough and evaporate it onto some sample to make a thin film. Aluminum evaporated at ~880 F, Silver at ~600. Calcium just sublimes, never melts. All of this is done in a vacuum chamber, otherwise everything would just oxidize.
