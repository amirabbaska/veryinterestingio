    RobusEtCeleritas A:

    Yes, we can remove protons from a nucleus using various kinds of nuclear reactions.

    If you're asking whether there exists any nucleus with only neutrons in it (besides a single neutron), no such nucleus is bound. There are only resonances, like the dineutron and the tetraneutron, which decay with half-lives on the order of 10^(-22) seconds.

    frogjg2003 A:

    At the far extremes of the table of isotopes are the proton and neutron drip lines. They are called that because any nucleus past them are unstable to neutron/proton decay. They're mostly extremely short lived and none of them are long lived enough to form atoms.
