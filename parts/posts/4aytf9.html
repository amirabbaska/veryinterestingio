    yeahsciencesc A:

    This actually is a pretty complicated question because there are a number of different types of nausea, and not all directly and initially involve the stomach (motion sickness, stimulation of the area postrema from irritants in the blood supply, etc.)

    Upon stomach and GI irritation, cells in the body called enterochromaffin cells release serotonin. There are a plethora of serotonin receptors, but the 5HT3 subtypes are particularly important for sensing nausea. Afferent/sensory enteric neurons with these receptors tend to synapse near the chemoreceptive trigger zone and vomiting center of the brain, causing nausea sensations.

    Additionally, there are 5HT4 serotonin receptors which increase gastric motility when stimulated. This can cause an irritated stomach, which releases serotonin, to become more contractile. This can lead to a positive feedback loop of stimulating more nausea until you vomit.
