    ELI5: Are we at the battery capacity peak? Does the capacity only increase by volume?

    s0v3r1gn A:

    We are nowhere near peak battery capacity.

    There are multiple battery chemistries we have yet to figure out how to reasonably manufacture a battery out of but we know that they will work better.

    There are also new technologies in nanostructure manufacturing. The energy density of a battery is directly related to the surface area of the materials it's made from. But with increased surface area usually comes larger size or less durability, which we are working on solving using gel substrates instead of fluid.

    There are also multiple chemistry batteries and other hybrid styles of energy storage being worked on.

    There are still quite a few amazing developments in batteries being made.

    mfb- A:

    The capacity is still increasing. Other parameters are also relevant: charging speed, cycles before the battery degrades notably, safety, cost, ...

    Technology usually follows a single common trend: something new is introduced at a bad quality, then makes rapid initial improvements until the technology is mature enough to be used, over time improvements get smaller as the performance approaches its maximum. Meanwhile, new approaches are developed that lead to a higher performance later.
