    Icestar1186 A:

    They're actually low ultraviolet, which is just outside of the visible range and right above violet on the spectrum. They usually overlap into violet slightly.

    pez78guy A:

    Blacklight is actually ultraviolet light, slightly higher energy than visible light. When fluorescent surfaces absorb ultraviolet light, they give up that energy in two or more steps. One is in the form of visible light, which is what you see as purple, even when other luminous sources of light are turned off. The other step(s) are lower energy light that we cannot see, such as infrared.
