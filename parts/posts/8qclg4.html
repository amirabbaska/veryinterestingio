    Q:

    ELI5: Why does water left in a bottle taste weird and gross after a day or two. But soda can be put in a bottle for weeks and taste just fine?

    finchdad A:

    Soda is extremely acidic, which generally prevents the establishment of common bacteria, mold, and other funk-creating organisms. Water is pH neutral and has no qualms whatsoever about proliferating all the microbes in your backwash, on your lips, or floating around in the air.

    Water in bottles (as in store-bought bottled water) can and does stay fresh for long periods of time.

    bizitmap A:

    Exposure to air. Remember, bottled water sold at the store manages to stay just fine for weeks and weeks too.

    When water (or soda, which is just water plus carbonation plus flavors) gets packaged and sold, the packaging is airtight. You usually can't make it air-tight again yourself, even if you screw the lid back on really frigging hard. Exposure to air means dust can settle on top of the water, bacteria could in theory move in, stuff dissolved in the water or soda has somewhere it can leave to.
