• Biology Why is it that having a mint taste in your mouth causes water to feel like its ice.

    NEW
    Q:

    Sitting on Reddit in my first period study hall chewing some mint gum, took a swig of my water bottle and started tearing up. Everyone's looking at me funny now.

    nothingbutnoise A:

    Menthol, which is what you mean by mint flavor, binds to the temperature receptors on your tongue and tricks your brain into thinking that you're eating something cold.

    coolkitok A:

    We have a taste recepor on the tounge called CMR1 (cold and menthol receptor 1) or TRPM8. This receptor is expressed on sensory neurons which transmit temperature. Upon menthol bindning to the receptor, the probability of the neuron firing increases and thus increasing our perception of "cold". Fun fact: the same type of mechanism happens with Jalapeno, except the other way around!
