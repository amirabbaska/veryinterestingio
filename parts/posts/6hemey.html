    Q:

    If I'm flying by Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune, would I see them bright and colorful or would they be somewhat dark because they're so far away from the sun?

    loki130 A:

    You could see them pretty well, even though the outermost ones are receiving a fraction of a percent of the light that Earth does. This is because our perception of light is logarithmic; Large changes in light emission by bright objects look the same as small changes in dim objects. Think about how your computer screen looks inside at night compared to outside on a bright day; It looks brighter inside because your eyes adjusted to the lower ambient light, and the actual objective difference of light conditions is much bigger than you might expect.
