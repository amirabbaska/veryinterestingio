    Wizywig A:

    There are a few things that happen during sleep:

    • the brain generalizes knowledge from concrete knowledge into abstract knowledge
    • the brain prunes old memories
    • the brain recycles waste and heals
    • the heart gets to beat slower and giving it a chance to recuperate
    • the muscles are barely working so they get their excess waste removed

    To "feel sleepy" there's a neurotransmitter. If you stop sleeping normally eventually your brain just gives up feeling tired because it knows this is the norm and nothing can be done. The brain then starts to compensate. It'll have more of itself asleep at any given time to try to give neurons a break and not die. It'll not give you as much energy to try to recuperate your organs, etc. If you push it you are functioning outside of the normal human operating conditions and the body can only operate like that for so long.

    At best you will be at ~60% brain power. You will be dumber, learn slower, more tired, and unhealthy... but awake. You're basically going through insomnia... voluntarily.

    Edit: As you get older, you need less rest. An adult can function well on 8 hours of sleep, but a child needs that 10-12 hours of sleep. That's why early bed time for kids is so critical, because they really do need it.

    The ONLY exception are people with a special generic condition making them need maybe ~3 hours of sleep a night which is the equivalent to a normal person sleeping for 10 hours. These are about 1 in 100,000 or so.
