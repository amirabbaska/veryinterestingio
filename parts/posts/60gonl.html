    ELI5: Why hasn't anyone created cigarettes without all the toxic chemicals in it?

    RiotShields A:

    Among other reasons, tobacco companies suppress knowledge on the toxic stuff in cigarettes.

    Take polonium-210, an isotope in the radium decay chain. It's been known to be a major carcinogen in cigarette smoke since the 1960s. Tobacco growers failed to clean the stuff from the tobacco leaves, partially because the washing process removed the flavor and partially because it was not effective in general. They then tried to filter it out, but that, too, was ineffective and introduced its own problems. They then tried to genetically engineer plants that wouldn't absorb the Po-210, but funding kept being cut right before results were analyzed, so no data could ever be published. In the end, instead of continuing to try to remove the Po-210, tobacco companies stopped publication on the problem to avoid lawsuits.

    It's one of those things that seems like a crazy conspiracy theory, but here it is in the NIH archives: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC2509609/

    TL;DR: 3 Things Tobacco Companies Don't Want You to Know! Number 2 Will Give You Cancer!

    Cedira A:

    Do E-Cigarettes/Vaping not come close? - Most of the time the only chemical they retain from a standard cigarette is the active ingredient nicotine, which is the main source of the addiction.

    Nicotine is not entirely safe on its own, but without tar and CO, vaping is considered better for you than traditional cigarettes.

    There are still studies being performed to figure out the long term effects of vaping, and so far nothing is conclusive, based on a quick search.

    Edit: I'd also like to add, in order for a cigarette to be a cigarette, it has to maintain the addiction element for the user, nicotine. So as it stands a nicotine-free cigarette cannot really be described as a cigarette at all.
