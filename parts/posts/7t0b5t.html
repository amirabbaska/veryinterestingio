    Q:

    ELI5: If NASA knew Columbia was damaged, why could the shuttle not have docked at ISS while another shuttle was prepared and sent to rescue Columbia crew?

    a2soup A:

    Columbia was in a different orbital inclination than the ISS, orbiting at a 39 degree tilt relative to the equator vs. 51.5 degrees for the ISS. That doesn't sound like a lot, but the Shuttle did not have nearly enough fuel to rendezvous with the ISS, so that wasn't an option.

    After the disaster, future Shuttle missions not going to the ISS had a backup Shuttle prepared for a rescue mission while they were in space.

    warlocktx A:

    They knew there was a potential problem, but didn’t consider it a serious risk. And preparing a second shuttle takes weeks. They didn’t have time to wait on that. And they had space lab docked in the payload bay, so they would have had to eject that to use the airlock. And they didn’t have the robot arm on that mission, so even ejecting the lab would have been difficult. And they may not have had fuel to get to the ISS. And the ISS may not have had room or supplies. And I’m not sure the ISS could dock two shuttles at once. And the second shuttle couldn’t carry everyone.

    DevilRenegade A:

    I'm sure this has already been answered but Columbia had nowhere near enough fuel remaining on board to reach the ISS.

    There was a theoretical rescue plan to rescue the crew of Columbia using shuttle Atlantis but it was considered unrealistic due to the logistics involved.

    This is a brilliant article on that rescue plan that never was.

    As a side note, after Columbia, no shuttle launched without a rescue sortie on standby, and all subsequent missions carried enough extra fuel to reach the ISS if it was deemed necessary.
