• Economics Billion-dollar valuations for tech companies that have never turned a profit (!?)

    2016-12-23 NEW
    Q:

    I simply don't understand the sky-high valuations for companies that have not only never turned a profit, but who don't seem to even have a model in place to ever be profitable. Some examples:

    • Uber: valued at $28B - $62B despite reportedly being massively unprofitable

    • DraftKings: Valued around $1B despite being very unprofitable

    • ClassPass: Valued around $500M despite reportedly being quite unprofitable with some predicting an imminent demise

    • List goes on and on: Twitter, Snapchat, Box, etc

    I used to work for a startup, so I LIVED in that world...and I still struggle to explain it to other people. My default answer is usually: "Well, they're CURRENTLY losing a lot of money, but investors are gambling on the idea that one day they'll figure out how to turn a profit. Or failing that, they may prove to be an attractive acquisition target for a company who DOES have the money-making thing figured out, e.g. maybe Facebook or a profitable brick & mortar business, who might see them as a valuable new sales channel for their existing business." But even that answer seems unsatisfying to me. Maybe someone with a better handle can ELI5.
