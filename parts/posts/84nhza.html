• Engineering Does using a graphics card create "wear and tear" on the GPU?

    NEW
    Q:

    Take a graphics card that is run 24/7 for two years as part of a crypto mining operation. Assume that heat is never an issue because it's sufficiently cooled and that its undervolted like most miners do. What kind of "wear and tear" is applied to the GPU and ram chips through constant use?

    III-V A:

    Yes, especially modern ones, and it is becoming increasingly problematic. Modern transistors aren't very durable, and they degrade over time (they always have, but that degradation has accelerated). You'll find that a stable undervolted card likely won't stay stable at that voltage for long. How long is a matter of manufacturing variation. Could be months, could be years.

    itty53 A:

    Not a lot. GPUs are built to run at the temperatures they do. The greatest wear and tear on a GPU is done by the rapid temperature swing caused by turning off and on. You can cool it while it's in use, but when it turns off it's a lot cooler than when it's on. Can't avoid that, other than never or rarely turning it off.

    For that reason, GPS from miners are usually quite a good deal. Just make sure you replace the fan. That's the part that wears down the most.
