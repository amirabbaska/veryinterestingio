    Q:

    I was thinking that since we have more matter than anti matter, maybe there is also a possibility that there is either more positive or negative charge than the other.

    How easily would this be detectable?

    QuirksNquarkS A:

    Not an expert, but my understanding is that the two problems are inherently related. Effects that can produce a matter-anti-matter assymetry are usually classified into either Baryo- or Leptogenesis mechanisms. These are effects that can produce either a large overabundance of protons or electrons and a mechanism will usually produce only one or the other. More complicated analyses could considered a few in combination, but viable mechanisms are still so unconstrained that they are usually analyzed separately. With a completely charged universe you could then proceed to a neutral universe via the chiral anomaly, which is fairly well accepted.
