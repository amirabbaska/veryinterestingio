    Q:

    What are the reasons behind your eyes watering when you begin to choke on food or something?

    KCL40mVal A:

    it's a "vagal" response. the vagal nerve is often stimulated, when something irritates the trachea. the vagal nerve lets your trachea excrete more mucus and also the tear glands more tears.

    Your body has a defense mechanism that helps when we get dust or sand in our eyes and it'll start to cry. Much like how a dirty wound will bleed a lot, it helps clean it out.
