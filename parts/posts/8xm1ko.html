    ELI5: What is that sound you hear when you shut your eyes as hard as you can?

    lobsang_ludd A:

    You're activating the tensor tympani muscle, which pulls on the bones of the inner ear to damp sounds like chewing. The rumbling noise is just what muscle fibers sound like under tension, but stronger than you'll often otherwise encounter it because the muscle is inside your ear.
