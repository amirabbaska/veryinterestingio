• How is a nuclear missile flying over another sovereign nation's airspace not deemed an act of war?

    NEW
    Q:

    ELI5: How is a nuclear missile flying over another sovereign nation's airspace not deemed an act of war?

    unaskedforopinion A:

    Because the result of the act is what people will consider the most.

    It's kind of like this:

    Do you see a difference between me swinging a bat in your general direction, in a threatening manner, and me swinging a bat to hit your body? Sure you do. And on average, the reaction to the first example is liable to be more measured and proportionate, compared to the second.

    Declaring war is a very serious move, and one would hope world leaders would try to exhaust all avenues of remedy before taking that step.
