• Why do so many characters in movies and TV series have perfectly white teeth even if it does not fit their role at all? Is there a specific reason why teeth barely get any 'Makeup'?

    NEW
    Q:

    A good example is Game of Thrones. The plot is based in a medieval-themed world, however, characters have these shiny white Hollywood teeth. For me this is a small detail that distracts me a lot from the actual story and acting.

    To clarify: I do not expect that the teeth are actual rotting or missing at all, but a little bit dirt, making them more yellowish or added plaque would make this way more authentic. What bothers me is that the characters have just perfect and shiny white teeth.

    mynameipaul A:

    Because it would gross people out and they would be less inclined to watch. It sounds shallow, but is very likely to happen.

    I actually started watching a show called "Frontier" on Netflix: Ahistorical drama, set during the colonization of North America, all about the fur trade in the 1700s.

    Some of the main characters (Irish immigrants) are dirt poor and basically stealing to survive. These characters are interesting and endearing (and are portrayed by impossibly good looking actors) but - suitably for poverty stricken paupers in the 1700s - their teeth are yellow and rotten.

    Their teeth drew my attention a lot, and kind of made me uncomfortable, because I'm a sheltered bigot or whatever I guess.

    I watched an episode or two, then the next week, when I returned to watch some more, that was my overriding memory - how grossed out I would be by their teeth - and in the end, I didn't watch any more of the show.

    I would bet my house I'm not the only person who didn't continue with that show because of the historically accurate teeth 'make up'.
