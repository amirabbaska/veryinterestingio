    ELI5 Why do humans have different blood groups?

    jhojnac2 A:

    Currently there is no difinitive answer as to why. Scientists believe it is an environmental factor that led to the development of different blood groups such as disease resistance. Some blood groups are more resistant to malaria and so on... It just so happened that some of the blood groups became incompatible throughout evolution.
