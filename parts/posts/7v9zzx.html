    Q:

    ELI5: Why does it burn to quickly drink carbonated drinks?

    The_Moister_Oyster A:

    Something that has a large amount of CO2 dissolved in solution is essentially carbonic acid. H2O + CO2 => H2CO3 which dissolves into HCO3- and H+ which makes it acidic. Drinking said solution quickly means an increased exposure to this acid which is painful.

    Edit: Others are saying that the carbon dioxide bubbles coming out of solution while being drunk are abrasive to the throat and that is what causes the pain! Sorry about my first statement, I always thought that it was painful due to the acidity!
