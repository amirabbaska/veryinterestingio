• Physics If light is effected by gravity of black holes, does that mean light can orbit a black hole?

    NEW
    Q:

    So if the black hole was exactly the right mass and the light was at exactly the right passing trajectory could it be pulled into an orbit?

    Ameisen A:

    'Yes' and No.

    Yes in that in an idealized two-body system, you can have a photon orbit another body of sufficient mass (in this case, a black hole). A two-body system is required, in an idealized universe, otherwise the orbits would be incredibly chaotic and decay instantly. Idealized in that you also cannot have sporadic particle generation (such as that which results in Hawking Radiation) as the force of even those particles would be sufficient to disrupt the photon's orbit.

    No in that, as said, their orbits are far too chaotic to exist in the real universe in any situation.
