    drew1492 A:

    There is a distinction between the random, short-lived, ringing you mention and chronic, low level ringing. The latter is (edit: usually) the result of some degree of ear damage. The former is not, and happens to a large percent of people at least once or twice. It's thought to be caused by muscle spasm that results in temporary constriction of the ear canal.

    WikiWantsYourPics A:

    It's because you have certain specialised hair cells in your inner ear that don't just passively respond to sound, but also vibrate when they detect sound, so that faint sounds get amplified. This can sometimes cause positive feedback: the one set of hair cells vibrates a bit, then the next set responds to that, and the first set increases it even more until you're hearing a ringing in your ears.

    It goes away because your nervous system detects the problem and damps out the oscillation.
