• Why is it so hard to wake up early even though we've had the same amount of time sleeping?

    NEW
    Q:

    I went to bed at 11 and woke up at 7, and it was super difficult. But I ordinarily go to bed at around 12;30-1 am and wake up around 9. I am getting the same amount of hours of sleep but it is much harder to wake up at 7, as opposed to 9. Why is that?

    MackTen A:

    This is a great question for me for once! So basically, you can think of sleep in terms of three processes, S, C, and Z. Process S is your tiredness based on how long it's been since you last slept or had a full night's sleep. Process C is your Circadian Rhythm. Process Z is sleep inertia (the period of time, usually two hours following waking up, that you are groggy from sleeping). What's going on here is that even though your process S is well adjusted since you got enough sleep last night, your process C is still a bit off since your 23:30-24:30ish internal clock is used to still being asleep at 7.     Chemical1428 A:

    This is due to your circadian rhythm. It isn't feasible to adjust your circadian rhythm by more than an hour a day. So if your sleep schedule has a sudden change your circadian rhythm won't be able to catch up. This is primary factor in jet lag as well.

    Interesting note- Some people are more controlled by their circadian rhythm than others. The sleep schedule of night owls is almost entirely dictated by their circadian rhythms. The opposite is true of morning people, who tend to not have their sleep cycles controlled by their circadian rhythm. They instead, and much to their benefit in society, get tired when they are low on sleep (ie the end of the day).

    r3solv A:

    Could just be waking up during a rem cycle versus after one. If you go to bed at say, 815, and sleep for 8 hours, or go to bed at 915 and sleep for 7, you'd feel just as well rested because of the rem cycle. However if you went to bed at 845, and still woke up the same time 7 hours or so later, you'd feel more tired than you would just going to bed at 915. Even if you slept an extra 15-20 mins, you wake up in the middle of a rem cycle, which makes you feel tired. Often it's better to go to bed at specific times than guaranteeing a number of hours of sleep, depending when you need to wake, because of rem cycles. There's a calculator for it, not always very accurate, but gives you an idea.
