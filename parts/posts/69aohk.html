    Q:

    If your cells are always dying and being replaced, how come things like freckles and birth marks don't go away?

    TheFancySingularity A:

    The cells that make up your freckles/birthmarks split into basically identical copies of themselves through a process called Mitosis and since they do not move, the new daughter cells have the same pigment of the parent cell and take their place after the cell dies. Basically, the new cells inherit the parent cell's position.
