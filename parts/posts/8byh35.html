    Nooms88 A:

    Africans, as a whole, aren't. Middle and Long distance running is dominated by a very specific region is East Africa.

    The vast majority of the successful athletes there were born and raised at high altitude, giving them a significant advantage, over say a Dutch person. Added to that, genetically many of them are tall and thin, a useful body type for long distance running.

    Further more, success breads success. I did some high level middle distance running in the UK, our group of serious athletes would get maybe a dozen people at training, but most people played other sports. in Kenya in particular, running is essentially the national sport, partly down to how successful many Kenyans are at it, meaning that the talent pool at a young age is quite large.
