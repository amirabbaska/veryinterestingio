    Q:

    ELI5: How come during winter my feet are cold but sometimes sweat while I try to warm them up?

    dvorahtheexplorer A:

    Your feet are cold, but that doesn't mean your body is cold, and the only temperature your body cares about is your core body temperature. So you made your core warmer than it likes, so it sweats, even your feet.

    There is an interesting metabolic response to cold hands and feet being warmed called the Lewis Hunting Response (or Reaction). LHR can be used by outdoors people to mitigate against the effects of cold... I use LHR for winter kiteboarding with bare feet and hands - essentially immersion till they are freezing, then rewarming, then I am good to go.
