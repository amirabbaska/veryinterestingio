• Wendy's coined the phrase "fresh, never frozen", and supposedly refrigerate their meat. What are the negative effects of freezing beef that make this motto worthwhile?

    NEW
    Q:

    I'm assuming that with this motto that other fast food establishments (such as Burger King, McDonald's, White Castle, etc.) freeze their meat therefore making Wendy's "superior"? Just don't understand what freezing the meat could do health or taste-wise.

    CasusBellyBell A:

    Take a look here

    When it comes down to it, freezing anything with water in it (which is technically everything that needs to be frozen) changes the nature of the cells. Specifically, water expands when frozen - this shouldn't be a surprise to most people, but what should surprise most people is that things generally shrink when frozen not expand. Water is different, and it's some of these qualities that make water so important to life.

    Pack to the ELI5, since there's water in the cells of meat, freezing the meat tends to destroy the cells - either by rupturing them or by pulling the water out of the cell -dessicating it. It's one of the (several) reasons that cryogenically preserving people doesn't work - imagine that happening to your BRAIN CELLS!

    Even if only a few cells out of a bunch get destroyed, it changes the taste. And if air can reach the meat at the same time it'll dehydrate it quicker and cause more of a change to the flavor - check out freezer burn for some more info.

    edit - really cool freezing fact: some animals can survive deep cold by partially freezing. In the case of the wood frogs they use aptly named "cryoprotectants" to lower the freezing point of the cells in the body, reducing the formation of ice etc.etc. They also slow/suspend many bodily functions so that they don't need ...well... anything. Cool stuff!

    krystar78 A:

    Slow freezing cells creates big water crystals that can puncture cell walls. When thawed, the contents of cell walls leak out, leaving a shell and a mess on the floor. Macroscopically, this leads to mushiness. Taste, which is the contents of the cells, is greatly leaked out.

    Flash freezing creates smaller water crystals and drastically reduces adverse effects. All your seafood is flash frozen unless it's you pick the lobster at restaurant type.

    TheCSKlepto A:

    On top of the science mumbo-jumbo other have pointed out, making this claim in the US has a bit of wiggle room. I know this, because I worked in a grocery store in high school that also makes the "never frozen" claim. You don't have to call something 'frozen' as long as it's never stored below 0 degrees F. However, at what temp does water freeze? 32F. So as long as you store the meat/whatever low enough to actually freeze but not go below 0 you can make the "fresh, never frozen" claim.

    Now, this was early 2000s, so things may have changed in the 2 decades since but the grocery chain still has it in their ad campaign

    QuantumDischarge A:

    Frozen beef can last a lot longer than refrigerated. Patties can be formed and frozen for months at end, and with the right marketing, it can seem very unappetizing to eat "old" meat, even if it's perfectly safe. There can also be degradation of quality with having meat frozen and thawed.

    Saying your meat is "fresh, never frozen" makes people feel like the company uses high-quality ingredients, with more care in the product that they're selling. Is it true? Well... probably not.
