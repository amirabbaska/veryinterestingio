    ERRORMONSTER A:

    Lots of inaccurate answers here.

    Will a 1080p video going to your 4k tv look better than 1080p? Probably. Upscaling can guess, reasonably accurately, what the extra pixels would be if they were being sent in the video stream.

    Will it look as good as 4k native? No. You cannot paint a picture using 1/4 of your canvas. It just won't look the same.

    Upscaling will get you somewhere in the middle, using surrounding gradients to approximate the extra pixels.

    HeavyDT A:

    No even though Most 4k tv's coming out now have upscalers (or hardware like game consoles do) you can't add more detail to the image than there was originally. An upscaler uses an algorithm to fill in those extra pixels based off the lower resolution picture. It's much better than showing a raw lower resolution image on a 4k screen like 1080p content raw on a 4k screen would look terrible and horribly distorted for example but it won't give you the extra detail that the 4k display is capable of showing. Now whether or not it decreases the clarity depends on the upscaler as they aren't all built equal but it's possible because you are taking a much smaller image and trying to blow up up way bigger without distorting it.
