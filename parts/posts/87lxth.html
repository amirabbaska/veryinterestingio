    Q:

    Say we scale down an aircraft proportionately (say, all dimensions divided by a factor of 2). What happens to the lift, drag, wing efficiency, etc? What are the scaling laws here?

    cartoptauntaun A:

    N.B. - This answer is oversimplified for what OP wanted. Correct answer was given in the following comments

    Volume has a cubed relationship with length, surface area has a squared relationship.

    Weight reduces faster than surface area when scaling linear dimensions, which means smaller objects require less lift per unit area.

    In air disturbances become more severe at small sizes because of the loss of mass/inertia.

    These are general statements, but they should illustrate that a linear scaling does not hold at all. I think drag coefficient on wing profiles will stay constant but almost other variables change. see below
