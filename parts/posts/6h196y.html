    Hypothesis_Null A:

    Best I understand it, they were worried about chain-reaction fusion of low-weight atoms, like oxygen or nitrogen.

    We know now it's impossible. Not nearly dense enough, not enough pressure or energy, etc etc. But keep in mind, the Manhattan Project spanned across World War II, and ended before 1945.

    Fission [of heavy elements] was only discovered in 1938. They were literally discovering and making stuff up as they went. So the idea that the energies necessary weren't there wasn't something they could know until after they ran the numbers.
