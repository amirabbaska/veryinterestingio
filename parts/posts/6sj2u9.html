    What's the coldest flames can be?

    bvictorg A:

    If you're referring to flames that can be seen with the naked eye, it's usually any mixtures of alcohol and water, the "coldest" ones being formed by isopropyl alcohol with water mixtures and diethyl ether (150ish C). It should be noted, though, that combustion can occur even before that, at lower temperatures, but the flames can't be seen with the naked eye.

    For more info, see here: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cool_flame

    root42 A:

    I would say that this is the Draper Point: https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Draper_point

    At 798K all solid materials will glow red due to black body radiation. This is what you will usually see in a flame. There are other processes why you would see light at lower temperatures, e.g. ionization or excitation by electric fields, but that is usually not happening in flames.
