• What is the procedure for when a demolition fails?

    NEW
    Q:

    When a building is demolished and the initial explosives go off but fail to collapse the structure, what happens next?

    Are there secondary explosives? Surely it's not safe to approach the building once those first explosives have been detonated.

    onion13 A:

    Controlled implosions aren't just about bringing a building down...its about bringing it down so the debris goes where you want it to.

    The actual amount of explosive force needed to bring a structure down is actually low (because of gravity) So if there is a screw up in the implosion process, the building is still coming down...just in a really unsafe way.

    Fenrir101 A:

    The explosives going off on their own is not a worry, it takes certain very controlled conditions for modern demolition explosives to be detonated. If the detonation sequence goes off badly enough that there are large parts of the building still standing then traditional but riskier and slower demolition tactics will just need to be used (wrecking balls and the like).

    But the demolitions company will want to recover the unused explosives because governments tend to get a bit touchy about lost high explosives.

    SapperBomb A:

    If their is a failure of the initial detonation a soak time is applied to make sure there is not a slow burn in one of the detonators and then someone walks down to start checking the circuit

They ended up tearing it down with a wrecking ball over a week or so. I can't remember how long they waited between the failed detonation and finishing the deconstruction, though.
