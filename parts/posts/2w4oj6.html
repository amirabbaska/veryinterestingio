    carmooch A:

    You are mistaking the literal measurement of teaspoon with the home utensil. Unless you are using a prepackaged portion, spoonfuls from the sugar jar at home are almost always significantly more than the standardised measurement for a teaspoon which is roughly 5 grams.

    Coca-Cola contains 10.6 grams of sugar per 100mL (literally taken from the information on the can).

    A commercially available brand of Iced Coffee contains 9.5 grams of sugar per 100mL.

    So 3 teaspoons of sugar in 200mL of tea is actually 7.5 grams per 100mL which isn't very far behind a can of Coca-Cola at all.

    If we are talking 3 actual teaspoons heaped from the sugar jar, then you are likely adding more sugar than your average can of Coca-Cola.
