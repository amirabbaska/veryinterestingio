    crackersoncrackers A:

    Your skin is constantly losing moisture. It's called transepidermal water loss.

    Sebum is naturally produced by your skin to counteract rapid evaporation, protect you from germs, etc. It's basically your natural moisturizer and waterproofer. Without sebum your skin will become brittle and dry. Your saliva is wiping away your sebum and the enzymes in your saliva are causing further damage to your skin by slightly digesting it.

    So you're already losing moisture anyway, and you've licked away your only natural defense against moisture loss.

    You need an emollient and/or a humectant to fix chapped lips. Saliva is neither. Neither is water! Most people have tried straight water as a cure before if they didn't have a chapstick handy, but it evaporates quickly and just leaves you feeling worse.

    An emollient will keep moisture trapped in. A humectant will attract moisture from the its environment (the air in this case) and hold onto it so that you are being supplied with moisture. An example of an emollient is petrolatum (Vaseline) and an example of a humectant is glycerin. One of the most unpleasant parts of chapped lips is the stiffness of the skin, and a humectant will go the extra mile to restore softness.

    A good chapstick also lubricates so that you don't chap your lips even more as you talk, eat, etc.
