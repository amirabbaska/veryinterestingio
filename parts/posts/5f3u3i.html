    myztry A:

    The endocrine system is our body's chemically based control system.

    It works by sending chemicals through our blood to make changes down to the very smallest molecular level. It can be slow to react as these chemicals travel all over our body but only as fast as our blood can carry them.

    When the chemical gets to where it is going it might just tell part of the body to make another chemical to change something else. That thing that needs changing might be the one that started it creating a feedback loop.

    It is all very complicated and it's a wonder it works with any reliability at all. So when your chest or lungs feel uncomfortable it may be your kidney gland not telling your body to swap potassium for salt.

    It may get there. It might be temporary discomfort until the feedback loop fixes the imbalance. Or it may not.
