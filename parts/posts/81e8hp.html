    Cleanthehouse A:

    I can't say exactly which brainwaves are different, but it has to do with how well your thalamus is working. There is a lot of evidence that the thalamus the thalumus cortex regulate consciousness, and coma patients have damage to that part of the brain. General anesthetics slow the metabolism of the thalamus too.

    Source

    I couldn't find the full article online for free, but this is basically what the part about comas said: "The most consistent effect produced by anesthetics at LOC is a reduction of thalamic metabolism, suggesting that the thalamocortical system is the major system that is essential for conscious activity. Post-mortem analysis of coma patients revealed suppression or destruction of thalamocortical functions, and electrical stimulation of the central thalamus restored a degree of behavioral responsiveness in a patient who had been minimally conscious for 6 years."
