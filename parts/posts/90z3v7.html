    horror_unfolds A:

    In short, your body sucks more at things the older you get. Your liver isn't as efficient when it comes to metabolizing alcohol because you have less enzymes. In addition to that, your body composition changes the older you get. Older people tend to have a higher fat content, which makes it more difficult to process alcohol.

    nobeardpete A:

    Generally speaking, everything gets worse as you get older. When you're in your early twenties, you can eat total crap and not gain weight. You can drink hard with only a mild hangover. Be generally lazy but then be physically active in spurts, not suffer for it. Crash your bike, fall off the roof, generally knock yourself around without serious injury. By the time you're in your fourties, you're gonna suffer a much worse hangover when you drink. You'll put on a lot of weight much more easily when you eat too much. You'll be stiff and sore for days after you finally start going back to the gym again. And instead of walking away from epic wipeouts with just a few scratches, you'll get injuries from activities like "slept in a funny position", and "picked up a box the wrong way." Basically, as soon as you're done growing, your body immediately starts to slowly fall apart.
