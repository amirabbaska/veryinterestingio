    Q:

    ELI5: When giving mouth-to-mouth, how does the other person receive oxygen instead of CO2?

    RhynoD A:

    You don't use even most of the oxygen in the air you inhale. There is more than enough to sustain a person who is already unconscious. This is especially true when you take a deep breath and immediately breath it out into them. Regardless, even a reduced amount of oxygen is better than the zero amount they're getting from the stale air in their lungs that aren't moving.

    MJMurcott A:

    There are two elements here

    Firstly the air you breathe in has about 20% oxygen and 16% when you breathe it out meaning that there is still a lot of oxygen in the air you breathe out and you can force that air quite deep into someone's lungs by doing mouth to mouth.

    Secondly CO2 levels in air you breathe out will be fairly high, but your body system actually uses the level of CO2 in the system to regulate breathing. i.e. the more CO2 there is in your system the more often you breathe which might be quite useful in this case.
