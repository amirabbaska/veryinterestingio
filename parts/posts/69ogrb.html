    BeautyAndGlamour A:

    Nope! In order to see something, light must interact with it and reflect into our eyes. However light (photons), being uncharged particles, is generally kind of terrible at interacting with atoms. Having a 1 atom thick wall would give the light just a single chance to interact and reflect. Since the odds are so low for this, the plank would be almost invisible.

    bruno-bg A:

    It depends on several things(material, light, angle, etc), but yes, it could be seen. To pick one example, Graphene can be seen. Graphene is a 1-atom thick arrangement of carbon atoms, and it absorbs about 2.3% to 2.6% percent of visible light, and Wikipedia even has a photo.

    More than that, you could probably test your idea to the size too. It does not matter to your question if it's a 1mx1m or 1cmx1cm sheet, but graphene could also probably hold up to a 1mx1m sheet (hanging, not a plank standing by itself).

    The Nobel announcement illustrated this by saying that a 1 square meter graphene hammock would support a 4 kg cat but would weigh only as much as one of the cat's whiskers, at 0.77 mg (about 0.001% of the weight of 1 m2 of paper).

    You can actually make graphene at home and test this. Check https://www.scientificamerican.com/slideshow/diy-graphene-how-to-make-carbon-layers-with-sticky-tape/ or https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LwmxSjydPEE
