    DustRainbow A:

    I just visited the European Astronaut formation centre in Cologne recently and they mentioned cleaning the ISS as a regular, planned activity. Excess dust is hazardous in such environments and the task of cleaning is taken quite seriously.

    Don't quote me on it but I remember something like a full day a week goes to cleaning duties.

    FlyingSpacefrog A:

    I think it was on Skylab Mir where they had some malfunctioning electronics that they were going to fix. When they removed a panel to access where the wires were they found a large ball of water floating there filled with mold or algae or something. It wasn’t that anyone had purposefully dumped out a bunch of water, just that water vapor had condensed there and started an ecosystem.

    This led to them being more careful with water recyclers. ISS has machines to condense water vapor out of the air and filter it for reuse. I’m sure they do regular checks for buildup of water or filth in poorly ventilated areas.
