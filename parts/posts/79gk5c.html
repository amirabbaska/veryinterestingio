    I’ve been wondering this for awhile now and haven’t quite found an explanation for it, how is it that all of these particles are universally the same no matter where you measure it?

    thelamestofall A:

    Because the fundamental components of nature, as far as we understand it, aren't particles: they're fields. There is the electron field, the up-quark field, etc and those fields permeate the whole universe. When one of those fields is excited, this excitation is perceived as a particle.
