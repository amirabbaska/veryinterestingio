    ArchAcoustics A:

    It's important to remember that 0dB is just an arbitrary point which represents something you just barely can't hear. It's not actually zero. 0+0 = 3dB. A million speakers playing a 0dB sound would theoretically play a sound of about 60dB.

    nivvis A:

    I'm disappointed no one has told them how many speakers they would need. I think that more impressively describes the logarithmic nature of sound power.

    The scale is logarithmic, where 10 more units is equivalent to multiplying by 10. So in order to achieve 100 dB, assuming the speakers would constructively interfere*, you would need 10^10 speakers. Thats 10,000,000,000.

    *Sound travels in waves. The peaks and troughs of the waves have to arrive aligned to the listener (in phase) for them to act cooperatively.

    ETrevin0 A:

    If you want to know how loud 100 1 decibel speakers will be, you first need to find the intensity of 1 decibel using dB=10*log(I/Io) where Io=1x10^-12 W/m^2, then multiply that intensity by 100, and then convert that intensity back to decibels using the same equation.

    For 1-1 decibel speaker

    I = Io 10^(dB/10) = (1x10^-12 W/m^2 )10^(1/10)

    I = 1.2589x10^-12 W/m^2

    For 100-1 decibel speakers

    I = 100 * 1.2589x10^-12 W/m^2 = 1.2589x10^-10 W/m^2

    dB=10*log(1.2589x10^-10 W/m^2 / 1.2589x10^-12 W/m^2 )=20 decibels

    Hence, 100-1 decibel speakers will have a loudness of 20 decibels.
