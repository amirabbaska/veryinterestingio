    knnl A:

    The control center of emotions, called 'lymbic system', has a degree of control over the autonomous nervous system.

    Crying is controlled by a branch of the autonomous nervous system - the parasympathetic.

    When you feel sad, the parasympathetic nerve that controls the lacrimal gland is stimulated.

    So, basically, the brain process that makes you feel sad is directly linked to the mechanism that stimulates the lacrimal gland to produce tears.
