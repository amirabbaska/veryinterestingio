• In cartoons, certain clothing patterns such as checkered or plaid, it seems like it's a static pattern that the characters move around on. Why is this, and how is it done?

    NEW
    Q:

    ELI5:In cartoons, certain clothing patterns such as checkered or plaid, it seems like it's a static pattern that the characters move around on. Why is this, and how is it done?

    Aureo_Speedwagon A:

    On a technical level, their shirt is actually transparent, and there is a layer with the picture of the pattern behind the layer the character is drawn on. The character and their shirt moves, but the background layer does not, resulting in what you've described.

    The reason for doing this is that its a cheaper/faster/easier way to animate clothing with those types of patterns. The alternative is redrawing the whole thing each time the character moves slightly.

    In whatever animation software they use, the shirt layer is transparent and above a motionless layer of plaid underneath it. It's done because it's cheap, lazy and easy compared to actually animating a plaid textured shirt.
