    Q:

    Whenever I think of eating some sour candy like a warhead or sour patch kids, just the thought of eating it triggers some sort of response from my mouth. Why does this happen?

    Everything you hear, see, taste, touch, smell, is all happening in your brain. Remembering a taste is no different than remembering a smell or a sound or a sight or a touch. You're literally recalling the sensory perception in your brain where it happened in the first place.

    GenXCub A:

    Conditioning. Have you heard of "Pavlov's dogs?" Ivan Pavlov conducted an experiment where he would feed dogs, but only after a bell rings. Eventually, the sound of the bell made the dogs salivate even if they weren't given food. They associated the bell with food and their mouths anticipated that.

    This is also why you feel the need to go to the bathroom even more when you're near a bathroom.

    And also why your mouth waters when you think of certain foods.

    Also have you ever felt jaw pain when eating or even thinking of eating sour candy?
