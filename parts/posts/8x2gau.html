    MnstrPoppa A:

    Food tastes too salty because our tongues tell us there’s too much salt for this to be good for our bellies. Water from the ocean has a whole bunch of salt in it, even more than salty food. If there was as much salt in your food as there is in ocean water, the food would be too salty, it would taste yucky, and it would make your belly ache.

    “There is a lot more salt in salt water than we get in salty foods. The problem is osmotic pressure and chemical gradients within the body. Essentially water follows sodium. When we take in sodium it goes into out blood stream. When we take in a lot, our blood has more sodium then the fluid in our cells do.     PrionBacon A:

    Ocean salt water is really, really salty. There is about 35 grams of salt per 1 liter of salt water. 1 gram of salt contains about 0.4 grams of sodium. So 1 liter of salt water has 14 grams of sodium. Your daily sodium requirement is about 2 grams (2000 mg).

    You would reach your daily sodium intake drinking 1 L/14 grams x 2 grams per day = 142 mL or 4.8 fluid oz of salt water. This is about half a cup of salt water.

    Edit: Should be daily 2 grams of sodium not 1 gram.
