    Q:

    How much radiation dose would you receive if you touched Chernobyl's Elephant's Foot?

    RDS-37 A:

    Two-time Chernobyl visitor here (proof-photo from inside the turbine hall: http://imgur.com/gallery/O1zqy). While my group tried to get access to to the foot, we were not successful. However, we spoke to dosimetrists who had access to the area. According to them, the gamma exposure rate on contact with the foot was ~600-1000R/hr depending on the spot. These measurements were taken in the last couple of years, so little has changed.

    If you were to run into the room, touch it just long enough to get a selfie and then leave, you'd get somewhere under 10 Rad (if you assume a mean gamma energy of 1MeV ). This is a dose that would faintly show up in bloodwork, but would not cause noticeable acute effects.

    Based on the linear-no-threshold model using data from BEIR VII (ERR=~.6/Sv), this experience would raise your lifetime fatal cancer risk from ~22% (male, US) to ~23.3%. So yeah, I'd totally do it.

    Edited to change "lifetime cancer risk" to "lifetime fatal cancer risk"
