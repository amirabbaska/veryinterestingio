• Astronomy How come we are able to detect planets thousands (millions?) of light years away from us, but we have yet to determine with 100% certainty whether or not there is a ninth planet in our solar system?

    NEW
    Q:

    How come we are able to detect planets thousands (millions?) of light years away from us, but we have yet to determine with 100% certainty whether or not there is a ninth planet in our solar system?

    Rannasha A:

    Our main method to detect planets orbiting distant stars is by observing the star when the planet moves in front of it. This briefly makes the star appear dimmer as the planet blocks part of the light of the star. If this happens on a regular interval, we assume that this dimming is caused by a planet and its periodic orbit.

    An additional planet in our solar system can't be detected this way, since such an object would never come between the Earth and the Sun. Since this hypothetical planet would be far away from the Sun, it would only receive and reflect a small amount of light, making it very hard to detect. Detecting a very dim speck of light amidst a sky full of objects that are much brighter, without any idea about where to start looking, is worse than finding a needle in a haystack.

    lehyde A:

    In general it is easier to find a thing of which millions exist than to proof the non-existence of a specific thing. For one, proving non-existence is generally not so easy because you have to check every possible place very carefully. If you fail to check a single place, you can't claim non-existence. Everything has to be done correctly. In contrast when you want to find just any instance of a common thing, you can be much more sloppy and still succeed. Our telescopes aren't perfect but they're good enough to find at least some exo-planets. We can't detect most of them, but that's ok. It's enough if our telescopes work at least sometimes. This would not be enough in the case of proving the non-existence of a ninth planet.

    So you see: one task is fundamentally easier than the other.

    Think about how much easier it is to find just any store that sells lemon drops than to prove that there are absolutely no lemon drops hidden in your apartment right now.
