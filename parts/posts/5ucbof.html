    Q:

    ELI5: Why are there more visible air bubbles in tap water when it is cold outside?

    corntamale A:

    Gasses are more soluble in a liquid like water when it is really cold, and its solubility goes down as temperature increases. In hot water, a lot of those dissolved gas bubbles have left the water, whereas in cold water, the gas bubbles stay dissolved in the water easily. Thats why you see more bubbles in cold water than in hot water.

    bgnatedg A:

    Gasses have higher solubility at colder temperatures because the gas molecules have less energy and are less able to escape the forces holding them in the solution. At higher temperatures the gas molecules have higher energy of motion (kinetic energy) and are able to more easily escape the solution. This lowers the solubility of a gas at higher temperatures. Interestingly, this is the exact opposite effect seen with the solubility of salts which have higher solubility at higher temperatures.
