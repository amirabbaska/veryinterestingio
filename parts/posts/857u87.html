    Q:

    I've been fascinated by ball lightning for a long time. Has a reasonable theory emerged as to what causes it or is it still a mystery?

    FTLSquid A:

    Another redditor linked this article for a similar question. It's certainly a fascinating possible explanation!

    In a few words, the final step leader before it the lighting contacts the ground accelerates electrons to relativistic speeds. As they strike the ground, they release radiation in the microwave frequency. These microwaves ionizes the air around them, forming a ball of plasma. In rare scenarios, the microwaves can be trapped in the plasma ball as a standing wave which can persist and provide energy to 'fuel' the plasma ball.

    The article explains it a lot better than I could, and is fairly accessible. It's definitely worth a read!
