    ELI5: Why have our bodies arrived at 98.6F as the "normal" body temperature?

    Sell200AprilAt142 A:

    It's a "sweet spot" in organic chemistry and biology where a number of useful things coincide...

    • water is liquid
    • fats are liquid/solid in useful combination
    • a wealth of proteins and catalysts are able to facilitate our metabolism
    • it's a balance between being high enough to keep fungal infections at bay (which adversely affect cold blooded creatures) without being TOO high and putting a too high requirement on food consumption to fuel it
