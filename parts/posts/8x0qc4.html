    Beefenstein A:

    I'm no expert, but I've read that sperm don't swim to move forward so much as swim to maintain their place in the moving seminal payload. Therefore sperm->egg travel is not actually in any way a force in natural selection and the 'best' sperm don't 'swim' any faster or otherwise better to get to the egg.

    Intrasexual competition and intersexual mate choice are done on the level of individuals within the species; competition between gametes is not something I am aware of.
