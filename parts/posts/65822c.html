    ELI5: The correct use of 'whom'

    dundizzle A:

    If you can replace the word with “he”’ or “’she,” use who. If you can replace it with “him” or “her,” use whom. Or even easier, answer a question with those words.

    Who would like some ice cream? He would like some ice cream.

    To whom is that letter addressed? The letter is addressed to her.

    Who would like some ice cream? Would he like some ice cream?

    To whom is that letter addressed? Is that letter addressed to her?

    Who/whom ate my sandwich?

    Try substituting “she” and “her”: 

    She ate my sandwich. Her ate my sandwich. “She” works and “her” doesn’t. That means the word you want is who.

    Who/whom should I talk to about labeling food in the refrigerator?

    Try substituting “he” and “him”: 

    I should talk to he. I should talk to him. “Him” works, so the word you need is whom.

    Beyond ELI5, but the technical definition:

    Who should be used to refer to the subject of a sentence

    Whom should be used to refer to the object of a verb or preposition.

    TheRockefellers A:

    "Who" is a subject. It's the thing in the sentence that's doing the verb. "Who ran away?" The "who" is the one doing the running.

    "Whom" is an object. It's a thing that's having something done to it. "To whom do I deliver the message?" I'm the subject here, and I'm doing the delivering. "Whom" is the person who's having the delivering done to them.

    He/she/we/who. Him/her/us/whom.

    DaraelDraconis A:

    Imagine replacing it with a third-person pronoun, as if you were talking about a known person. You might have to change the order of words, especially when the phrase you're wondering about is a question, but it'll still show you which word is right.

    Where you'd use he or she, it's who. Where you'd use him or her, it's whom. For his-or-her and his-or-hers it's "whose", but that's kinda beside the point.

    Examples of the process:

    "[?] are they"? -> "He is ..." so "Who are they"?

    "[?] did you go with?" -> "You went with her" so "Whom did you go with?" (but this sounds a little clunky, and most people would accept who. If you rephrased it in a pickier way, as "with whom did you go?", practically nobody would accept who)

    We can go into the technical terminology if you like, but this is ELI5 so I thought I'd start with the answer that people know.
