    jayhigher A:

    u/Ender37 is wrong. The surface of a curling rink is not smooth, but is pebbled. This effect is achieved by spraying drops of water onto the ice that freeze into little bumps. The pebbled surface actually decreases friction for the stone because there is a smaller area of contact between the stone and the ice. The sweeping comes into play as the stone is traveling down the ice. Sweeping the ice in front to the stone briefly and slightly raises the temperature of the ice, which further lowers the friction. More sweeping means the the stone will travel further and curl less.
