    dill0nfd A:

    This is a great question. As long as there is no change in acceleration between the Sun and Earth, the gravitation field "anticipates" where the Sun will be and the Earth is attracted to the Sun's instantaneous position. If there is any 3rd-order motion (changing acceleration) the motion will not be "anticipated" and the Earth will be attracted to the position the Sun would have been had there been no changing acceleration. This discrepancy creates a "lag" which is actually the source of gravitation waves. In this case a grav wave will travel between the Sun and the Earth at the speed of light to account for the discrepancy. Source
