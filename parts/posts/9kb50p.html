    WeRtheBork A:

    Perhaps in some cases but habitat fragmentation is a major problem with conservation efforts. Animals and plants as well as their associated ecosystem services don't do well in many but fragmented habitats compared to an equal area of nonfragmented ecosystems.

    It is unlikely that it isn't natural selection that makes some animals better with roads but rather learned behavior from parents or experience.
