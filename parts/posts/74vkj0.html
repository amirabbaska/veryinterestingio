    A:

    Actually, it's a natural aging process when the skins antioxidant defenses begins to decline. As they decline, the natural oils become oxidized. Fatty acids from the body's glands then react to the oxygen to form a unsaturated aldehyde (organic compound) called nonenal, which is responsible for that funky smell. It isn't water soluble, so it remains on the skin even after washing and the smell can be quite prominent.
