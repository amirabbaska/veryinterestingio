    Quicker and easier to grow.

    Green peppers are the youngest of the four, and are therefore picked sooner and can be replanted quicker.

    Less resources are used, so they're cheaper.

    ExTrafficGuy A:

    I grow peppers in my back garden. The green ones are just unripe red ones. Peppers will bare fruit quickly and abundantly in the right conditions but need to be left on the vine longer to ripen. Which is why the green ones are cheaper. They can be harvested sooner.

    Most capsicums will turn green, to orange, to red as they ripen. Even jalapenos and other chilies. The green ones usually aren't as sweet.
