    EntropyOx A:

    Microwaves induce rotation of water/fat molecules at the atomic level, which generates heat. If your food is frozen the rotation doesn't occur very well. The portion of the food touching the plate thaws first, allowing the free molecules to move. So most of the heat is generated right where the food touches the plate. Hence the plate gets hotter than the food.

    The material of the plate or possible coating might conduct heat very well allowing that plate to absorb more heat from this process, which will also make your plate feel much hotter.
