• Why must a country "declare war" before attacking?

    NEW
    Q:

    Is it just a moral thing to do? wouldn't they take the advantage by attacking without notification?

    jlitwinka A:

    It's part of an agreed treaty from the Hauge convention

    The Contracting Powers recognize that hostilities between themselves must not commence without previous and explicit warning, in the form either of a reasoned declaration of war or of an ultimatum with conditional declaration of war.

    And was adopted by the United Nations after its founding. It serves multiple purposes

    1) All native and allied citizens are being informed to leave the country

    2) It informs citizens of said country, thus limiting loss of civilian life

    3) It informs allied and neighboring countries what is commencing so they can either not get caught in the cross fire or join in if they have an agreement with one of the nations

    4) Is considered morally right in many eyes, the goal of most modern wars isn't the decimation of a country, but a specific action or actions. A formal declaration of war would include a list of demands, with the hope being a coup will take place to meet those demands.

    Gnonthgol A:

    Yes, you would be able to take much more advantage if you can organize a surprise attack. But this is a hard thing to do with a big army group as the movements will be spotted long in advance. Most wars do not just suddenly start one day. It is usually a result of escalation of a conflict. The best result for all countries is if their conflicts can get resolved before it escalates to war. So the last step before invasion is usually a threat of war or even a direct declaration in the hopes that the country will yield to the demands before the war even starts. It is also a declaration to other nations that you currently consider the other country an enemy which does have some implications for trade and diplomacy.

    GlyphGryph A:

    Most nations in the modern world do not actually declare war before attacking.

    Declaring war is generally something you'd want to do if you want the other guy to capitulate or surrender rather than fight back, or you want allies and local industry to be aware you are about to engage in a military conflict and to recalibrate themselves appropriately.
