    Nodeemus A:

    From a nurse practitioner: due the way the nerves of your throats are placed, sometimes when something is stuck low in your esophagus, just above your stomach, it will feel like it is actually stuck much higher in your throat.

    She told this to a patient as we went through a procedure that involved swallowing barium, a thick, pasty substance. the patient kept saying was getting caught in their throat. Since it was a fluorography exam (x-ray), we could actually see it was not in the throat at all.

    I never followed up on the research, but it has always made sense to me.

    dkf295 A:

    A few possibilities.

    1. The pill actually is lodged. Fairly uncommon but some people have difficulties swallowing pills.

    2. The pill rubbed/scratched against the back of your throat and you're feeling that, not the pill.

    3. Psychosomatic. There's no pill but the thought/idea/fear of the pill getting lodged is causing the sensation.
