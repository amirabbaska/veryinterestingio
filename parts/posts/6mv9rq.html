    The US around that time switched from a system where precious metals decided the value of the coin. We then switched to a Fiat system were currency value is decided by the government and the worlds trust in the US currency.

    Essentially the US went from the Gold Standard to a Fiat System.

    NF_ A:

    In 1934, the United States tried to make itself stronger by hoarding silver. This cause the value of silver to raise to the point of other countries not being able to export (inflation). Countries started to abandon silver as a monetary system and the United States got stuck with a stock pile of under valued silver. In the 1960s silver started to have industrial use and the United States sold their silver and stopped minting silver coins because they would lose money on it.
