• Why do a lot of basement storage rooms/laundry rooms have a string to turn on the light but all other rooms use a light switch?

    NEW
    Q:

    ELI5: Why do a lot of basement storage rooms/laundry rooms have a string to turn on the light but all other rooms use a light switch?

    goramfed A:

    Most cellars were concrete, very hard to run electrical through the wall buy simple to drill in the top and insert a bulb fixture. A string works without any additional wires. Switch requires wires ran to the switch point.

    Edit: yes there are plenty of other reasons. And you might have a pull string in a modern house. I don't care, I gave a simple explanation for concrete cellars like my grandma had for canned food.

    woolmaster430 A:

    Its easier to to install a fixture with a draw string than to wire to a switch. There are fixtures with the draw string already set up and because basements are usually less likely to be seen by company, the aesthetics are less important.

    account_not_valid A:

    1. It's cheaper to install than running a line to a switch.

    2. It's safer in rooms where water is present or that could potentially have water on the floor. Touching a poorly insulated switch with wet hands or standing in a pool of water could kill you.
