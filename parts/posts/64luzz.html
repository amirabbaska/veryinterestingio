    ELI5: How does your body decide when to wake up?

    reddit_user_70942239 A:

    Mostly cues from our environment which influence what are called our circadian rhythms. This is something like our body's "sleep schedule" which can be thrown off if our environmental cues change (like traveling to a different time zone) or if we become accustomed to being awake at different times (staying up too late on the weekend and waking up feeling like a zombie). There is also an area in our brain which can keep track of time independently from these cues and rhythms, however I don't know much about what it's called or how it works
