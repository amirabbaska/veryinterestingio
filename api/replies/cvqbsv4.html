  • EvanDaniel

    As I pointed out elsewhere, if you do the math, copper at room temperature has a vapor pressure of about 6E-53 atmospheres. In other words, it doesn't evaporate until it gets hot. So saying it "has a vapor pressure" at room temperature, while technically accurate, is probably going to give people a bad intuition for what actually happens. At that pressure, a single atom leaving is an exceedingly rare event!
